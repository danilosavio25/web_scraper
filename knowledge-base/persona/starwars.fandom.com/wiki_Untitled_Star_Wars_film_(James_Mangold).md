## Development[]

### Origins[]

In the early [1970s](/wiki/1970 "1970"), *[Star Wars](/wiki/Star_Wars "Star Wars")* creator [George Lucas](/wiki/George_Lucas "George Lucas") once envisioned the [*Star Wars* saga](/wiki/Star_Wars_saga "Star Wars saga") consisting of nine films, the first which would focus on the origins of the [Jedi Knights](/wiki/Jedi_Knight "Jedi Knight") and how they were initiated and trained. [Original trilogy](/wiki/Original_trilogy "Original trilogy") producer [Gary Kurtz](/wiki/Gary_Kurtz "Gary Kurtz") was briefed about Lucas' plans, but by [1980](/wiki/1980 "1980"), Lucas changed his mind and decided to wrap up the series with the [1983](/wiki/1983 "1983") film [*Star Wars*: Episode VI *Return of the Jedi*](/wiki/Star_Wars:_Episode_VI_Return_of_the_Jedi "Star Wars: Episode VI Return of the Jedi"), much to Kurtz's dismay as he revealed to [TheForce.net](/wiki/TheForce.net "TheForce.net") in light of the prequel trilogy's mixed beginning with the [1999](/wiki/1999 "1999") film [*Star Wars*: Episode I *The Phantom Menace*](/wiki/Star_Wars:_Episode_I_The_Phantom_Menace "Star Wars: Episode I The Phantom Menace").[[4]](#cite_note-TFN_Gary_Kurtz's_Interview-4) Published between [2012](/wiki/2012 "2012") and [2014](/wiki/2014 "2014"), the *[Star Wars: Dawn of the Jedi](/wiki/Star_Wars:_Dawn_of_the_Jedi "Star Wars: Dawn of the Jedi")* [comic](/wiki/Star_Wars_comic_books "Star Wars comic books") series by [John Ostrander](/wiki/John_Ostrander "John Ostrander") and [Jan Duursema](/wiki/Jan_Duursema "Jan Duursema") explored the origins of the [Je'daii](/wiki/Jedi/Legends "Jedi/Legends") in the [*Star Wars* Legends](/wiki/Star_Wars_Legends "Star Wars Legends") continuity.[[5]](#cite_note-CBR_Interview-5)

On [February 6](/wiki/February_6 "February 6"), [2018](/wiki/2018 "2018"), [*Game of Thrones*](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Game_of_Thrones_(TV_series) "wikipedia:Game of Thrones (TV series)") showrunners [David Benioff](/wiki/David_Benioff "David Benioff") and [D.B. Weiss](/wiki/D.B._Weiss "D.B. Weiss") were announced by [StarWars.com](/wiki/StarWars.com "StarWars.com") to produce and write a [*Star Wars* film series](/wiki/Untitled_Star_Wars_film_series "Untitled Star Wars film series"),[[6]](#cite_note-Benioff&Weiss-6) which reportedly focused on the time period in which the [Jedi](/wiki/Jedi "Jedi") came to exist.[[7]](#cite_note-VarietysClaims-7) Benioff and Weiss reluctantly walked away from their project on [October 28](/wiki/October_28 "October 28"), [2019](/wiki/2019 "2019") due to scheduling conflicts with [Netflix](/wiki/Netflix "Netflix") despite their plans for a [2022](/wiki/2022 "2022") release after a hiatus following [*Star Wars*: Episode IX *The Rise of Skywalker*](/wiki/Star_Wars:_Episode_IX_The_Rise_of_Skywalker "Star Wars: Episode IX The Rise of Skywalker"). [Lucasfilm](/wiki/Lucasfilm "Lucasfilm") president [Kathleen Kennedy](/wiki/Kathleen_Kennedy "Kathleen Kennedy") confirmed her interest in waiting for the duo to return at some point.[[8]](#cite_note-DeadlinesExitNews-8) At some point around 2019, [David S. Goyer](/wiki/David_S._Goyer "David S. Goyer") was signed on to write a film regarding the Jedi's origins 25,000 [years](/wiki/Standard_year "Standard year") before *The Phantom Menace*, alongside a [separate project](/wiki/Untitled_Jabba_the_Hutt_film "Untitled Jabba the Hutt film") under director [Guillermo del Toro](/wiki/Guillermo_del_Toro "Guillermo del Toro"). However, Goyer left his work for Lucasfilm and the latter project became shelved.[[2]](#cite_note-DelToroMovie-2)

### Pre-production[]

After the Disney purchase of Lucasfilm, filmmaker [James Mangold](/wiki/James_Mangold "James Mangold") was attached to making a [Boba Fett](/wiki/Boba_Fett "Boba Fett") film in the style of [Sergio Leone](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sergio_Leone "wikipedia:Sergio Leone"). [This project](/wiki/Untitled_Boba_Fett_film "Untitled Boba Fett film") eventually morphed into the series *[Star Wars: The Book of Boba Fett](/wiki/Star_Wars:_The_Book_of_Boba_Fett "Star Wars: The Book of Boba Fett")*.[[9]](#cite_note-) Mangold was then hired by Lucasfilm to direct and co-write [*Indiana Jones and the Dial of Destiny*](https://indianajones.fandom.com/wiki/Indiana_Jones_and_the_Dial_of_Destiny "w:c:indianajones:Indiana Jones and the Dial of Destiny"), the final *[Indiana Jones](/wiki/Indiana_Jones "Indiana Jones")* film. On [April 7](/wiki/April_7 "April 7"), [2023](/wiki/2023 "2023"), it was confirmed during [Celebration Europe](/wiki/Celebration_Europe_(2023) "Celebration Europe (2023)") that Mangold would direct a *Star Wars* film centered on the origins of the Jedi.[[1]](#cite_note-New_movies-1) After the panel, Mangold revealed to the [*Variety*](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Variety_(magazine) "wikipedia:Variety (magazine)") magazine that his film would take place during the dawn of the Jedi and flesh out the exact origins of [The Force](/wiki/The_Force "The Force"), like how it was formed and discovered by the [first Jedi](/wiki/Prime_Jedi "Prime Jedi"). Having been drawn at the prospect of making a *Star Wars* film in the vein of a [religious](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Religion "wikipedia:Religion") [epic](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Epic_film "wikipedia:Epic film") like [Cecil B. DeMille's](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cecil_B._DeMille "wikipedia:Cecil B. DeMille") [1956](/wiki/1950s#1956 "1950s") film [*The Ten Commandments*](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/The_Ten_Commandments_(1956_film) "wikipedia:The Ten Commandments (1956 film)") or [William Wyler's](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/William_Wyler "wikipedia:William Wyler") [1959](/wiki/1950s#1959 "1950s") film [*Ben-Hur*](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ben-Hur_(1959_film) "wikipedia:Ben-Hur (1959 film)"), Mangold discussed these references with Kennedy to convince her of the potential to explore the concept of the Force due to having evolved into a "kind of religious legend" due to encompassing across the *Star Wars* saga.[[10]](#cite_note-MangoldsStoryPlans-10) A [*The Hollywood Reporter*](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/The_Hollywood_Reporter "wikipedia:The Hollywood Reporter") article stated that the title of the film is *Dawn of the Jedi*.[[11]](#cite_note-THR_3BP-11)

On [February 27](/wiki/February_27 "February 27"), [2025](/wiki/2025 "2025"), Lucasfilm president [Kathleen Kennedy](/wiki/Kathleen_Kennedy "Kathleen Kennedy") revealed to *Deadline* that Mangold was currently working on a script, and was delayed by directing [*A Complete Unknown*](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/A_Complete_Unknown "wikipedia:A Complete Unknown") and awards season due to the film's success.[[12]](#cite_note-KK_Q&A-12)
