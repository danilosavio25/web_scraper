The premise of **WookieeProject *Knights of the Old Republic*** is coordinating the efforts of [Wookieepedians](/wiki/Wookieepedia "Wookieepedia") maintaining articles related to the *[Star Wars: Knights of the Old Republic](/wiki/Star_Wars:_Knights_of_the_Old_Republic "Star Wars: Knights of the Old Republic")* (KotOR) [metaseries](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Metaseries "wikipedia:Metaseries"), which includes two [video](/wiki/Star_Wars:_Knights_of_the_Old_Republic "Star Wars: Knights of the Old Republic") [games](/wiki/Star_Wars:_Knights_of_the_Old_Republic_II:_The_Sith_Lords "Star Wars: Knights of the Old Republic II: The Sith Lords"), [miniatures and role-playing scenarios](/wiki/Knights_of_the_Old_Republic_(Star_Wars_Miniatures) "Knights of the Old Republic (Star Wars Miniatures)"), a [roleplaying campaign guide](/wiki/Knights_of_the_Old_Republic_Campaign_Guide "Knights of the Old Republic Campaign Guide"), a [50-issue comic series](/wiki/Star_Wars:_Knights_of_the_Old_Republic_(comic_series) "Star Wars: Knights of the Old Republic (comic series)"), a [five-issue miniseries](/wiki/Star_Wars:_Knights_of_the_Old_Republic:_War "Star Wars: Knights of the Old Republic: War"), and an announced [game remake](/wiki/Star_Wars:_Knights_of_the_Old_Republic_%E2%80%93_Remake "Star Wars: Knights of the Old Republic – Remake"). If you would like to contribute to the project, feel free to take a look at what we've done or ask questions in the forum. If you decide that this is the place for you and that you'd like to help us out, then please sign your name using three tildes at the bottom of the page under the "Participants" section.

## Current WookieeProject Focus[]

**"Maintenance and Promotion"** — currently the project is involved in maintaining current articles and promoting articles to [Featured](/wiki/Wookieepedia:Featured_articles "Wookieepedia:Featured articles"), [Good](/wiki/Wookieepedia:Good_articles "Wookieepedia:Good articles"), and [Comprehensive](/wiki/Wookieepedia:Comprehensive_articles "Wookieepedia:Comprehensive articles") status. However, this does not mean that we aren't writing new articles as the *Knights of the Old Republic* metaseries develops, or improving existing articles. All project participants are encouraged to edit any and all articles that catch their eye.

## Article guidelines[]

Because a large percentage of our information comes from the video games, *[Star Wars: Knights of the Old Republic](/wiki/Star_Wars:_Knights_of_the_Old_Republic "Star Wars: Knights of the Old Republic")* and *[Star Wars: Knights of the Old Republic II: The Sith Lords](/wiki/Star_Wars:_Knights_of_the_Old_Republic_II:_The_Sith_Lords "Star Wars: Knights of the Old Republic II: The Sith Lords")*, there are certain factors that writers and editors must be aware of. The following guidelines will explain these issues in greater detail. If anyone has any questions or comments, please feel free to start a new thread in our forum.

1. Make sure all articles follow the [Manual of Style](/wiki/Wookieepedia:Manual_of_Style "Wookieepedia:Manual of Style") and [Layout Guide](/wiki/Wookieepedia:Layout_Guide "Wookieepedia:Layout Guide"). For more information, please see [policies on Wookieepedia](/wiki/Category:Policies_on_Wookieepedia "Category:Policies on Wookieepedia").
2. Articles are written with a neutral [point of view](/wiki/Wookieepedia:Manual_of_Style#Perspective "Wookieepedia:Manual of Style"), without adding [original research and speculation](/wiki/Wookieepedia:Attribution "Wookieepedia:Attribution").
3. Make sure all information follows [canon](/wiki/Canon "Canon"). This has been determined as always being the light side choice in regards to the video games. Any dark side missions or player choices are to be placed in the **Behind the scenes** section of an article.
4. Please be aware that quite often it will be necessary to add the {{[GameLS](/wiki/Template:GameLS "Template:GameLS")}}, {{[Gamemechanics](/wiki/Template:Gamemechanics "Template:Gamemechanics")}}, or {{[GameLSmechanics](/wiki/Template:GameLSmechanics "Template:GameLSmechanics")}} templates to sections of an article. Although all LS player choices are canon, not all character interactions have been confirmed. The [KOTOR strategy guide](/wiki/Star_Wars:_Knights_of_the_Old_Republic:_Prima%27s_Official_Strategy_Guide "Star Wars: Knights of the Old Republic: Prima's Official Strategy Guide") has confirmed the order in which Revan visits the planets in-game. Articles that are affected by the variation in the order should use the {{[K1orderHeader](/wiki/Template:K1orderHeader "Template:K1orderHeader")}} or {{[K1order](/wiki/Template:K1order "Template:K1order")}} template.
5. Do not add "fate unknown" to articles.
6. All information in articles must be sourced with either reference tags or self-sourcing paragraphs. For more information, please see [Wookieepedia:Sourcing](/wiki/Wookieepedia:Sourcing "Wookieepedia:Sourcing"). Do not assume that any sourcing already present is correct. Double check all references yourself, or if unable to do so, add the {{[Citation](/wiki/Template:Citation "Template:Citation")}} template.
7. Information from comic books and novels must be cited by individual issue or novel. Please do not cite the entire story arc and/or trade paperback.
8. When citing specific dialog or cut content, it is recommended to use the StrRefs from the game dialog files. Please see the [Dialog.tlk files](#Dialog.tlk_files) section for more details.
9. Please take care in citing specific dates in the Old Republic era. The games are not sources for dates. Please see this [forum topic](/wiki/Forum:Citing_dates_in_the_Old_Republic_era "Forum:Citing dates in the Old Republic era") for more details.

## Tasks[]

The primary goal of this WookieeProject is to bring articles related to *Knights of the Old Republic* to [Featured article](/wiki/Wookieepedia:Featured_articles "Wookieepedia:Featured articles"), [Good article](/wiki/Wookieepedia:Good_articles "Wookieepedia:Good articles"), and [Comprehensive article](/wiki/Wookieepedia:Comprehensive_articles "Wookieepedia:Comprehensive articles") status. While it is understood that this will not be possible for every article, we wish for all articles to be complete and reliable sources of information.

Below, you will find links to the Master lists of articles that fall under the KotOR era. The master lists will be updated regularly, as new information is released. If you happen to find an article that is not listed, but you feel that it should be added to our project, please add it to the list or start a new thread in the forum. Once an article from one of these lists has been brought up to standard, please strike it so that other members will be aware of what is already finished.

## Continuity notes and common article issues[]

### Chronicles of the Old Republic[]

There have been some questions regarding the canonicity of the *[Chronicles of the Old Republic](/wiki/Chronicles_of_the_Old_Republic "Chronicles of the Old Republic")*. In the [Holocron continuity database questions](http://forums.starwars.com/thread.jspa?threadID=152583&start=525) [Leland Chee](/wiki/Leland_Chee "Leland Chee") states that *Chronicles* is considered **C-canon**, and that it had been written specifically as a lead in to *Star Wars: Knights of the Old Republic II: The Sith Lords*, which was released in Winter 2004/2005. What this means for us is that the information found in *Chronicles* is superior only to the *KotOR I* video game and the *KotOR Strategy Guide*. However, keep in mind that all of the other media that has been released since then, is superior to *Chronicles*. Any continuity errors that are found in *Chronicles*, or anywhere else for that matter, are to be relegated to Bts. The most recent C-canon source or appearance is always the correct choice.

### Dialog.tlk files[]

In game engines derived from BioWare's Infinity and Aurora engines (including KOTOR and TSL's Odyssey engine), all strings used in the game are stored in a file named dialog.tlk in the root game directory, uncompressed and unencrypted. These files can be used to search for exact quotes and/or correct them. There is no way to identify the *source* of a particular quote based on dialog.tlk alone. You will still need the game for that. If you don't have the games, or have non-English versions, dialog.tlk files from both games can be downloaded from [here](http://www.starwarsknights.com/tools.php#de).

#### Opening[]

Dialog.tlk files are technically binary files, but as they contain unmodified text, they can be opened in any text editor. Windows Notepad has issues with opening large files, so you can use another editor, such as [Notepad++](http://notepad-plus.sourceforge.net), instead. The file begins with binary data, and then the text follows. You can search for the text you need. However, this method may not be very user friendly.

Alternatively, you can use [TalkEd](http://starwarsknights.com/mtools/TalkEd104b.rar) (rar, 223 KB), a dialog.tlk editor. It was developed for Windows, but works fine in Wine. Another is the [FindRefs](http://www.starwarsknights.com/tools.php#gmt) program which can be used to search all dialog quickly by including TLK references when using. Not all lines in the dialog.tlk file will show up when specific words are searched but all dialog that is accessible in-game are.

### Fanon watch[]

Many KOTOR articles are prone to fanon. It especially concerns Darths (such as Revan, Darth Malak, or Darth Traya), Meetra Surik, and, to a lesser extent, the Jedi Masters. Here are some things you should keep in mind [This](http://forum.swtimeline.ru/index.php?s=&showtopic=304&view=findpost&p=4106) is all the NEC has to say about KOTOR. If someone makes a dubious claim and cites the NEC and it's not there, remove it. T3, HK and G0-T0 have NEGD pages. It is there that the Exile is confirmed to be female.

Nothing is said about lightsaber forms in the first game or the comics. It is unknown who used which form. In *The Sith Lords*, the only differences between forms are in gameplay mechanics, not aesthetics. It is impossible to distinguish forms any of the characters used based on the visuals; the engine always uses the same animations. The Exile always learns the forms in the order they're canonically numbered: [Form I](/wiki/Form_I "Form I") (Shii-Cho), [Form II](/wiki/Form_II "Form II") (Makashi), [Form III](/wiki/Form_III "Form III") (Soresu), [Form IV](/wiki/Form_IV "Form IV") (Ataru), [Form V](/wiki/Form_V "Form V") (Shien), and either [Form VI](/wiki/Form_VI "Form VI") (Niman) or [Form VII](/wiki/Form_VII "Form VII") (Juyo)\*. Forms I – III are learned as the player levels up, regardless of the class. Forms IV – VII are not available to the "Jedi Master" prestige class, and are taught by the three Jedi Masters always in the same order, regardless of the order the Masters are visited, so any form can be taught by any Master. Canonically, it is not possible to say which class the Exile may have been based on the forms. In the CSWE entry for Kavar, it says "After Vaklu was defeated, Kavar chose to teach the Exile new lightsaber combat techniques before setting out for Dantooine to rebuild the Jedi Order." However, a prestige class has not been identified for the Exile, and it is the prestige class that will determine which forms she used.

(\*)The final form learned depends on the player's prestige class: a "Jedi Watchman" learns Niman, while a "Jedi Weapon Master" learns Juyo. The Form VII variant Vaapad does not exist at this point; any references to Vaapad in *[Darth Bane: Path of Destruction](/wiki/Darth_Bane:_Path_of_Destruction "Darth Bane: Path of Destruction")* are erroneous, and [Drew Karpyshyn](/wiki/Drew_Karpyshyn "Drew Karpyshyn") has said that readers should pretend it says Juyo.

Double identity or suspected identity theories should be always treated as speculation and constrained to Bts at best, unless explicitly confirmed by John Jackson Miller or another LFL official.

### *The Old Republic* continuity[]

The continuing development of *[Star Wars: The Old Republic](/wiki/Star_Wars:_The_Old_Republic "Star Wars: The Old Republic")* is going to introduce new pieces of information in regards to the KotOR era. This may come in the form of ancestral connections, new information about events or individuals, or expanded details. While TOR will not fall under our project, we must stay aware of these changes. Currently, there is very little new information, so it will be listed in this section. When the game is released, no doubt much more information will be introduced. To this end, [WP:TOR](/wiki/Wookieepedia:WookieeProject_The_Old_Republic "Wookieepedia:WookieeProject The Old Republic") have set up [a forum](/wiki/Forum:WP:KotOR_information_requests "Forum:WP:KotOR information requests") where WP:KOTOR members can request information from *The Old Republic* media.

* [Satele Shan](/wiki/Satele_Shan "Satele Shan") is confirmed as the descendant of one of the main characters of *Star Wars: Knights of the Old Republic*. This ancestor has been confirmed to be Bastila Shan. It has been confirmed in-universe that she is also Revan's descendant. It is also confirmed he and Bastila were married, and had a [kid](/wiki/Vaner_Shan "Vaner Shan") together.

* It has been confirmed that after the events of the original *KOTOR*, Revan went to fight the [Sith Empire](/wiki/Sith_Empire_(Post%E2%80%93Great_Hyperspace_War) "Sith Empire (Post–Great Hyperspace War)"). Any reference to an "unknown threat" or "True Sith" should be changed to reflect this. Note that the term "True Sith" is not actually a canon proper noun while doing so.
