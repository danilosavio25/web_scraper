**The Ronin** was the alias used by a [male](/wiki/Gender "Gender") [human](/wiki/Human "Human") former [Sith](/wiki/Sith "Sith") who wandered the far edge of the [Outer Rim Territories](/wiki/Outer_Rim_Territories "Outer Rim Territories") in the aftermath of the [Sith rebellion](/wiki/Sith_rebellion "Sith rebellion"). He would later be nicknamed as **Grim**. The Ronin had begun the rebellion as the [Dark Lord of the Sith](/wiki/Dark_Lord_of_the_Sith "Dark Lord of the Sith") and led the Sith armies in battle after they broke away from the [Jedi clans](/wiki/Jedi_clan "Jedi clan"), seeking to control their own destinies and end the cycle of never-ending wars between rival [feudal](/wiki/Feudalism "Feudalism") [lords](/wiki/Lord_(Empire) "Lord (Empire)") and their [Jedi knights](/wiki/Jedi_knight_(Empire) "Jedi knight (Empire)").

The lords formed alliances as they sent their [Jedi](/wiki/Jedi "Jedi") to battle the Dark Lord, [his witch](/wiki/Unidentified_witch "Unidentified witch"), their forces, and the [Sith demons](/wiki/Sith_demon "Sith demon") that the witch resurrected after their [deaths](/wiki/Death "Death"). However, the Dark Lord grew disenchanted with what the Sith became during their rebellion and turned on his followers, ending the rebellion. The Dark Lord renounced the Sith, shed his former identity, and became the Ronin. After the rebellion failed, the former rival lords led a [revitalized](/wiki/Imperial_unification "Imperial unification") [Empire](/wiki/Empire_(Rei%27izu) "Empire (Rei'izu)").

Accompanied by the faithful [astromech droid](/wiki/Astromech_droid "Astromech droid") [R5-D56](/wiki/R5-D56 "R5-D56"), the Ronin wandered through the Outer Rim in a self-imposed [exile](/wiki/Exile_(punishment) "Exile (punishment)") and did not speak about his past. Pledging allegiance to no clan, he made it his life's mission to hunt and eliminate other Sith, collecting their [kyber crystals](/wiki/Kyber_crystal "Kyber crystal") to prevent them from causing any more evil. He was both guided and taunted by the disembodied voice of his daughter [Mirahi](/wiki/Mirahi "Mirahi") in his head, whose identity was unknown to him and to whom he sometimes responded aloud.

Twenty [years](/wiki/Standard_year "Standard year") after his rebellion, he came upon a [village](/wiki/Unidentified_village_(Genbara) "Unidentified village (Genbara)") on the [planet](/wiki/Planet "Planet") [Genbara](/wiki/Genbara "Genbara") that was being [attacked](/wiki/Skirmish_of_unidentified_village_(Genbara) "Skirmish of unidentified village (Genbara)") by [Bandit Troopers](/wiki/Bandit_Troopers "Bandit Troopers") led by [Kouru](/wiki/Kouru "Kouru"), who claimed the title of Dark Lord of the Sith. Their [lightsaber duel](/wiki/Lightsaber_duel "Lightsaber duel") ended when the Ronin killed her; however, she awoke from her death as a Sith demon. His path brought him into contact with [the Traveler](/wiki/The_Traveler "The Traveler") and the crew of the *[Poor Crow](/wiki/Poor_Crow "Poor Crow")*, who sought his help in confronting the witch and uncovering the secrets of the planet [Rei'izu](/wiki/Rei%27izu "Rei'izu"), the former capital of the Empire who had seemingly vanished from [the galaxy](/wiki/The_galaxy "The galaxy").

His travels led him to the planet [Dekien](/wiki/Dekien "Dekien") in search of a shard of the [kyber mirror](/wiki/Kyber_mirror_of_Shinsui_Temple "Kyber mirror of Shinsui Temple") of [Shinsui Temple](/wiki/Shinsui_Temple "Shinsui Temple"), to the [Imperial Dreadnought](/wiki/Imperial_Dreadnought "Imperial Dreadnought") *[Reverent](/wiki/Reverent "Reverent")* where he escaped capture by Lord [Hanrai](/wiki/Hanrai "Hanrai"), and ultimately to Rei'izu, where he confronted Mirahi. Choosing reconciliation over conflict, he joined forces with her against the Jedi, banding with the crew of the *Poor Crow*.

Sometime later, the Ronin and R5-D56 found themselves on the planet [Olcatz](/wiki/Olcatz "Olcatz"). There, they were pursued by a Jedi known as [the Grand Master](/wiki/The_Grand_Master "The Grand Master"), who desired revenge on the Ronin due to a prior incident in which the Jedi was maimed and disfigured by Grim. The two dueled, but the former Sith managed to escape. The Grand Master and his followers, the [Crusaders](/wiki/Crusaders_(The_Grand_Master) "Crusaders (The Grand Master)"), placed the planet under [lockdown](/wiki/Lockdown_of_Olcatz "Lockdown of Olcatz") while the Ronin joined forces with the [Anzellan](/wiki/Anzellan "Anzellan") [Que-Dama](/wiki/Que-Dama "Que-Dama") and his crew to strike back against the Jedi. In the ensuing [battle](/wiki/Battle_on_Tkoku_Island "Battle on Tkoku Island"), the Ronin aided in fighting off the Crusaders, eventually killing the Grand Master in a duel.

## Biography[]

### Early years[]

:   "*I examined the records, as many as I could find. They differed by their teller. All together, they showed me a man of tremendous talent. Ambitious. Protective. Loyal, deeply so, both to his lord and to his brethren—by which I mean those clansmen adopted beside him.*"
:   ―Lord Hanrai, referring to the Ronin[[1]](#cite_note-Ronin-1)

 [![The Ronin at the foot of a mountain](data:image/gif;base64,R0lGODlhAQABAIABAAAAAP///yH5BAEAAAEALAAAAAABAAEAQAICTAEAOw%3D%3D)](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/starwars/images/3/30/RoninMountain.png/revision/latest?cb=20251007000526) [![The Ronin at the foot of a mountain](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/starwars/images/3/30/RoninMountain.png/revision/latest/scale-to-width-down/180?cb=20251007000526)](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/starwars/images/3/30/RoninMountain.png/revision/latest?cb=20251007000526) 

The Ronin at the foot of a mountain

 

As a boy, the man who later came to be known as "the Ronin" had a deep-seated anger he carried throughout his life, and kindness had rarely been afforded to him in his youth. He at one point became a [Jedi apprentice](/wiki/Jedi_apprentice_(Empire) "Jedi apprentice (Empire)") to a [lord](/wiki/Lord_(Empire) "Lord (Empire)"), an occasion upon which he learned the board game [shogi](/wiki/Shogi "Shogi"). At the time, it was fashionable for a [Jedi master](/wiki/Jedi_master_(Empire) "Jedi master (Empire)") to use the game to train their most promising apprentices how to manage a battlefield. The boy, however, was not interested in shogi, only developing an appreciation for it years later, when he was no longer a [Jedi](/wiki/Jedi "Jedi").[[1]](#cite_note-Ronin-1) Due to his exceptional performance for a child taken into the Jedi clans, he was bequeathed the power and responsibility of a [lightsaber](/wiki/Lightsaber "Lightsaber"), as well as power over people, his [Jedi guardians](/wiki/Jedi_guardian_(Empire) "Jedi guardian (Empire)").[[4]](#cite_note-Newsletter2-4)

At some point, a [lord](/wiki/Unidentified_lord_(wounded_heart) "Unidentified lord (wounded heart)") fired a [blaster](/wiki/Blaster "Blaster") bolt at the Ronin; although he dodged, it struck the left side of his face and left his [jaw](/wiki/Mouth "Mouth") misshapen and scarred. Thereafter, the Ronin wore a [metal](/wiki/Metal "Metal") [prosthetic](/wiki/Cybernetics "Cybernetics") that supported his jaw from [ear](/wiki/Ear "Ear") to ear, and he took the lord's [blaster](/wiki/Wakizashi_blaster "Wakizashi blaster") to carry on his own [belt](/wiki/Belt "Belt").[[1]](#cite_note-Ronin-1) Still a [Padawan](/wiki/Padawan "Padawan"), the young Ronin had long, but shorter hair than later in his life, and wore an apron, along with his by then still functional [lightsaber](/wiki/The_Ronin%27s_lightsaber "The Ronin's lightsaber") hanging from the belt, as opposed to using a [scabbard auxiliary](/wiki/Scabbard_auxiliary "Scabbard auxiliary"). By this time, his lightsaber had no guard either.[[5]](#cite_note-Vol3ConceptArt-5)

According to Jedi records, he was deeply loyal to both his lord and his adopted clansmen. When he was knighted, his clansmen became his guardians,[[1]](#cite_note-Ronin-1) one of which was the woman who became known as the witch, whom he fell in love with.[[4]](#cite_note-Newsletter2-4) During a battle, he faced a choice between saving his lord or saving his guardians, eventually allowing his lord to die, which was considered a cowardly act among the Jedi, as outliving one's lord was seen as dishonorable and unacceptable for a knight within the Empire. As a consequence, the [Empire](/wiki/Empire_(Rei%27izu) "Empire (Rei'izu)") sentenced him and his guardians to [death](/wiki/Death "Death"), declaring them rebels for disobeying Imperial orders.[[1]](#cite_note-Ronin-1)

### Dark Lord of the Sith[]

:   "*Not long ago, there was a certain Jedi. A knight, who rose from the ranks of the children welcomed into one of our esteemed clans. He was blessed, you could say. Favored by the gods, if you'd rather. Or simply very good at following orders as his masters imagined he should. Yet this was the very man who so famously turned on the Jedi. I've often wondered why.*"
:   ―Hanrai, speaking about the Ronin's own past to the Ronin[[1]](#cite_note-Ronin-1)

 [![The Ronin was a former Jedi who broke off from the Jedi clans and created the Sith.](data:image/gif;base64,R0lGODlhAQABAIABAAAAAP///yH5BAEAAAEALAAAAAABAAEAQAICTAEAOw%3D%3D)](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/starwars/images/3/36/Sith_-_SW_Visions.jpg/revision/latest?cb=20221018221534) [![The Ronin was a former Jedi who broke off from the Jedi clans and created the Sith.](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/starwars/images/3/36/Sith_-_SW_Visions.jpg/revision/latest/scale-to-width-down/250?cb=20221018221534)](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/starwars/images/3/36/Sith_-_SW_Visions.jpg/revision/latest?cb=20221018221534) 

The Ronin was a former Jedi who broke off from the Jedi clans and created the Sith.

 

Prior to the [unification](/wiki/Imperial_unification "Imperial unification") of the Empire, [Jedi knights](/wiki/Jedi_knight_(Empire) "Jedi knight (Empire)") had served various constantly warring [feudal](/wiki/Feudalism "Feudalism") [lords](/wiki/Lord_(Empire) "Lord (Empire)"). The [Sith](/wiki/Sith "Sith") arose as a splinter faction of the [Jedi clans](/wiki/Jedi_clan "Jedi clan") seeking self-determination and an end to the constant wars plaguing [the galaxy](/wiki/The_galaxy "The galaxy"). Together with a [Sith witch](/wiki/Unidentified_witch "Unidentified witch"), the Ronin led the [Sith rebellion](/wiki/Sith_rebellion "Sith rebellion") against the Jedi as the [Dark Lord of the Sith](/wiki/Dark_Lord_of_the_Sith "Dark Lord of the Sith"). The witch would resurrect the bodies of the dead before they could join [the Force](/wiki/The_Force "The Force") to create [Sith demons](/wiki/Sith_demon "Sith demon") that continued to fight for the Sith.[[1]](#cite_note-Ronin-1)

Later, during a [skirmish](/wiki/Unidentified_skirmish_(the_Grand_Master) "Unidentified skirmish (the Grand Master)"), the Ronin fought a Jedi who would later call himself [the Grand Master](/wiki/The_Grand_Master "The Grand Master"), knocking him down onto the ground and cutting off his legs and lower jaw. The Ronin blasted him for his obsession with judgment, claiming it was a denial of the balance needed between [light](/wiki/Light_side_of_the_Force "Light side of the Force") and [dark](/wiki/Dark_side_of_the_Force "Dark side of the Force") sides. The Ronin saw the young man as closer to darkness than the Sith themselves.[[6]](#cite_note-Payback-6)

As the Dark Lord of the Sith, he devised [lightsaber auxiliary](/wiki/Lightsaber_auxiliary "Lightsaber auxiliary") technology that extended the capabilities of lightsabers, and [holographic blueprints](/wiki/Holographic_blueprint "Holographic blueprint") for his designs were distributed amongst the Sith cells. After the young warrior [Kouru](/wiki/Kouru "Kouru") built her lightsaber, she received the dark lord's blueprints to construct a [parasol auxiliary](/wiki/Parasol_auxiliary "Parasol auxiliary"), and [her Sith master](/wiki/Kouru%27s_master "Kouru's master") made a fan auxiliary. The Dark Lord and the Sith witch had been in love, and they [conceived](/wiki/Reproduction "Reproduction") a [daughter](/wiki/Mirahi "Mirahi") on Rei'izu, unknown to the Dark Lord.[[1]](#cite_note-Ronin-1)

During the [great muster](/wiki/Great_muster "Great muster") on [Rei'izu](/wiki/Rei%27izu "Rei'izu"), the Dark Lord looked into the divine [kyber mirror of Shinsui Temple](/wiki/Kyber_mirror_of_Shinsui_Temple "Kyber mirror of Shinsui Temple") and saw a [vision](/wiki/Force_vision "Force vision") of horrors and warfare. He became disenchanted with the rebellion he had led and turned his lightsaber against the Sith who had followed him. The Sith rebellion ended after the Ronin's change of heart. At the culmination of the rebellion, the Empire was [reunited](/wiki/Imperial_unification "Imperial unification") through alliances amongst the rival feudal lords, and the [Jedi](/wiki/Jedi "Jedi") came to be the [Emperor's](/wiki/Unidentified_Emperor "Unidentified Emperor") most faithful servants. The Sith became scarce in the galaxy as they were hunted down by Jedi Knights and agents of the Empire, as well as by the Ronin himself.[[1]](#cite_note-Ronin-1)

### A new companion[]

:   "*I would like to challenge you to a duel.*"
:   ―The Ronin, facing Okinaa[[7]](#cite_note-VisionsTO1-7)

 [![The Ronin duels Okinaa.](data:image/gif;base64,R0lGODlhAQABAIABAAAAAP///yH5BAEAAAEALAAAAAABAAEAQAICTAEAOw%3D%3D)](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/starwars/images/9/99/OkinaaRoninDuel-TO1.png/revision/latest?cb=20240531062606) [![The Ronin duels Okinaa.](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/starwars/images/9/99/OkinaaRoninDuel-TO1.png/revision/latest/scale-to-width-down/250?cb=20240531062606)](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/starwars/images/9/99/OkinaaRoninDuel-TO1.png/revision/latest?cb=20240531062606) 

The Ronin duels Okinaa.

 

At some point, the Ronin found himself on a [planet](/wiki/Unidentified_planet_(Okinaa) "Unidentified planet (Okinaa)") where [Zabrak](/wiki/Zabrak "Zabrak") [Sith Master](/wiki/Sith_Master "Sith Master") [Okinaa](/wiki/Okinaa "Okinaa") and his [astromech droid](/wiki/Astromech_droid "Astromech droid") [R5-D56](/wiki/R5-D56 "R5-D56") were. The Ronin then challenged Okinaa to a [duel](/wiki/Lightsaber_duel "Lightsaber duel"), but Okinaa refused, as they believed one of them would die. However, when the Ronin removed the hat that covered his face, Okinaa recognized him from a prior premonitory [dream](/wiki/Dream "Dream") they had and, accepting their fate, agreed to the [duel](/wiki/Lightsaber_combat "Lightsaber combat") and told R5-D56 to ask the Ronin for help if something happened to them.[[7]](#cite_note-VisionsTO1-7)

Okinaa ordered R5-D56 to witness their duel, and suggested to the Ronin that they fight elsewhere, as they were in a [holy place](/wiki/Unidentified_holy_place "Unidentified holy place"). The Ronin refused Okinaa's suggestion and attacked, beginning the duel. In response, Okinaa [leaped](/wiki/Force_jump "Force jump") down the side of the cliff, moving the duel away from the holy place. As the Ronin pursued them, Okinaa taunted the Ronin, refusing to draw their [lightsaber](/wiki/Okinaa%27s_lightsaber "Okinaa's lightsaber"). Eventually, the Ronin forced Okinaa to ignite their [lightsaber](/wiki/Sith_lightsaber "Sith lightsaber"), and the two exchanged several blows before Okinaa leapt to a [tree](/wiki/Tree "Tree") in the middle of a nearby body of [water](/wiki/Water "Water") and again taunted the Ronin, who had waded into the water. Okinaa jumped down to attack the Ronin, who landed a fatal strike that split Okinaa's mask.[[7]](#cite_note-VisionsTO1-7)

Before dying, Okinaa warned the Ronin that their fate was the fate of Sith, and said a farewell to R5-D56. As the Ronin dragged Okinaa's body out of the water, a distressed R5-D56 tapped their body with his arms. At [night](/wiki/Standard_day "Standard day"), the Ronin lit a [fire](/wiki/Fire "Fire") to burn Okinaa's body. After putting on Okinaa's [hat](/wiki/Hat "Hat") and waving goodbye to their body, R5-D56 accepted the Ronin's offer to come with him,[[7]](#cite_note-VisionsTO1-7) and the two traveled away together.[[1]](#cite_note-Ronin-1)

### Hot spring and sake[]

:   "*Hey. Didn't you forget something?*"  
    "*Forget?*"  
    "*Yes. Don't you have to kill me…? Master Jedi.*"
:   ―The Ronin and Shogun[[3]](#cite_note-Visions_1-3)

[Years](/wiki/Standard_year "Standard year") after the end of the war,[[8]](#cite_note-V1Date-8) while hiking on[[3]](#cite_note-Visions_1-3) [Mount Hakamori](/wiki/Mount_Hakamori "Mount Hakamori"),[[9]](#cite_note-FirstPic-9) a snowy [mountain](/wiki/Mountain "Mountain") on an [astronomical object](/wiki/Unidentified_astronomical_object_(Shogun) "Unidentified astronomical object (Shogun)") a group of [thieves](/wiki/Thief "Thief") attempted to steal the R5-D56 from the Ronin. An individual formerly called [Shogun](/wiki/Shogun "Shogun") witnessed the incident, and left before they began fighting to avoid being struck by a stray [blaster](/wiki/Blaster "Blaster") bolt and later learned that all but [one](/wiki/Unidentified_thief_(the_Ronin) "Unidentified thief (the Ronin)") of the bandits had been killed.[[3]](#cite_note-Visions_1-3)

That night, while the the Ronin was bathed in a secret [hot spring](/wiki/Unidentified_hot_spring "Unidentified hot spring"), Shogun visited the location, surprised to see the Ronin, but remarking that it was nice to see someone else there. Shogun joined the Ronin in the hot spring and commented that it would be even nicer if they had some [sake](/wiki/Sake "Sake"). In response, the Ronin offered to share his sake with Shogun, which Shogun accepted. Shogun then informed the Ronin that the hot spring was made to heal the battle wounds of knights and offered to give him a massage for a discount. The Ronin declined the offer, explaining that he deserved his pain due to the many individuals he had killed in the war.[[3]](#cite_note-Visions_1-3)

 [![The Ronin saw a vision of himself dueling Shogun.](data:image/gif;base64,R0lGODlhAQABAIABAAAAAP///yH5BAEAAAEALAAAAAABAAEAQAICTAEAOw%3D%3D)](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/starwars/images/c/cd/RoninShogunDuel-Visions1.png/revision/latest?cb=20241005194747) [![The Ronin saw a vision of himself dueling Shogun.](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/starwars/images/c/cd/RoninShogunDuel-Visions1.png/revision/latest/scale-to-width-down/250?cb=20241005194747)](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/starwars/images/c/cd/RoninShogunDuel-Visions1.png/revision/latest?cb=20241005194747) 

The Ronin saw a vision of himself dueling Shogun.

 

Shogun then recounted their own experience in the war, describing it as "horrible" and telling the Ronin of their vision of the [Father](/wiki/Father_(Shogun) "Father (Shogun)") and how they were still searching for the [Temple of Elujoji](/wiki/Temple_of_Elujoji "Temple of Elujoji") on the [planet](/wiki/Planet "Planet") [Sukalan](/wiki/Sukalan "Sukalan"). The Ronin told Shogun what he knew of the temple, having heard from his [teacher](/wiki/The_Ronin%27s_teacher "The Ronin's teacher") that "the truth of [the galaxy](/wiki/The_galaxy "The galaxy")" was there and that it was the only place where one could purify their [soul](/wiki/Soul "Soul"), but he did not know its location. In response, Shogun joked that the sake and hot spring were good enough to purify them. The two then smiled and laughed after noticing that R5-D56 was [gambling](/wiki/Gambling "Gambling") with a group of [Ewoks](/wiki/Ewok "Ewok") nearby. Shogun said they would have liked to take the Ronin to a nearby [casino](/wiki/Unidentified_casino_(Shogun) "Unidentified casino (Shogun)"), but that it was closed due to a hassle, and told him they had heard about a fight between a group of thieves and an individual with a [red](/wiki/Color "Color") lightsaber.[[3]](#cite_note-Visions_1-3)

Just then, the [sun](/wiki/Star "Star") rose, and the Ronin announced his departure. Shogun walked with him for some distance and then offered to buy a [drink](/wiki/Foodstuff "Foodstuff") for the Ronin at a [station](/wiki/Unidentified_station "Unidentified station") below, but the Ronin declined, explaining that someone was picking them up. Just as they began to walk away from each other, the Ronin asked if Shogun had forgotten something. When Shogun questioned him, the Ronin asked if they were going to kill him and revealed that he was aware that Shogun was a Jedi. The Ronin then experienced a vision of him dueling Shogun, in which Shogun quickly defeated him and destroyed his lightsaber. After the vision ended, Shogun told the Ronin that they had not forgotten anything and then said a farewell before walking away with their [droid](/wiki/Droid "Droid"). After they left, the Ronin realized that had he had his [hands](/wiki/Limb "Limb") on his lightsaber and actually fought Shogun, he would have died.[[3]](#cite_note-Visions_1-3)

### Just a passerby[]

:   "*It will ward off evil spirits. Take great care of it.*"
:   ―Ronin, giving the village chief Kouru's kyber crystal[[1]](#cite_note-Ronin-1)

Two [decades](/wiki/Standard_decade "Standard decade") after the Sith rebellion, the Ronin wandered the [Outer Rim Territories](/wiki/Outer_Rim_Territories "Outer Rim Territories") with his droid companion, R5-D56. He also acquired a mysterious voice whispering in his mind, urging him towards danger. He would later learn that it was the voice of his daughter, Mirahi. The Ronin made it his life's mission to hunt down other Sith, collecting their [kyber crystals](/wiki/Kyber_crystal "Kyber crystal") as to prevent them from causing further harm,[[1]](#cite_note-Ronin-1) despite the fact that possessing kybers had been made illegal by the Empire.[[10]](#cite_note-CWK-10)

Eventually, the duo came to the planet [Genbara](/wiki/Genbara "Genbara"), where, after two months, they ran out of credits while wandering in the [spring](/wiki/Spring "Spring") through the countryside. A local [woodcutter](/wiki/Unidentified_woodcutter "Unidentified woodcutter") told them of a small lean-to they could spend the night at, and offered directions to the [mountains](/wiki/Unidentified_mountain_range "Unidentified mountain range"), noting that only one out of four villages that lay in the valley beyond the mountains still remained, allegedly due to angering a [spirit](/wiki/Ghost_(folklore) "Ghost (folklore)") that did not take kindly to settlers.[[1]](#cite_note-Ronin-1)

After following a road up the mountains past a ridge,[[1]](#cite_note-Ronin-1) the duo and stopped at [Tono's Teahouse](/wiki/Tono%27s_Teahouse "Tono's Teahouse") just outside the [village](/wiki/Unidentified_village_(Genbara) "Unidentified village (Genbara)"). He repaired the [shopkeeper's](/wiki/Unidentified_teahouse_shopkeep "Unidentified teahouse shopkeep") [power droid](/wiki/Power_droid "Power droid") in exchange for food and [tea](/wiki/Tea "Tea"), when [Bandit Troopers](/wiki/Bandit_Troopers "Bandit Troopers") led by Kouru, a self-styled Dark Lord of the Sith, [attacked](/wiki/Skirmish_of_unidentified_village_(Genbara) "Skirmish of unidentified village (Genbara)") the village. Kouru's bandits rounded up the village's residents in a central area when a group of [bounty hunters](/wiki/Bounty_hunter "Bounty hunter") who had been hired to protect the village counter-attacked. They began to turn the tide of the battle when Kouru emerged, killing a [Trandoshan guard](/wiki/Unidentified_Trandoshan_guard "Unidentified Trandoshan guard") with her [lightsaber parasol](/wiki/Lightsaber_parasol "Lightsaber parasol").[[2]](#cite_note-The_Duel-2)

### The road to Osou[]

:   ―The Ronin, asking Mirahi, the voice in his head[[1]](#cite_note-Ronin-1)

 [![The Ronin and R5 walk away from the village](data:image/gif;base64,R0lGODlhAQABAIABAAAAAP///yH5BAEAAAEALAAAAAABAAEAQAICTAEAOw%3D%3D)](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/starwars/images/5/5c/RoadToOsou.png/revision/latest?cb=20251004130017) [![The Ronin and R5 walk away from the village](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/starwars/images/5/5c/RoadToOsou.png/revision/latest/scale-to-width-down/300?cb=20251004130017)](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/starwars/images/5/5c/RoadToOsou.png/revision/latest?cb=20251004130017)

The next day, as the Ronin and R5 walked towards [Osou spaceport](/wiki/Osou_spaceport "Osou spaceport") along the road away from the village, they approached a crossroads near a crashed silver [starship](/wiki/Starship "Starship") at the end of the valley. Beside a copse of pink-blossomed trees, they met a being who was practicing playing the [flute](/wiki/Flute "Flute"). The individual, who wore a [vulpine](/wiki/Fox "Fox") mask, joined the due on their trek, and asked him his name, to which he did not reply. In his stead, R5 answered "Ronin" in [binary](/wiki/Binary "Binary"). To match his alias, they told the Ronin to call them "[the Traveler](/wiki/The_Traveler "The Traveler")."[[1]](#cite_note-Ronin-1)

As the road to the spaceport became busier over time, The Traveler and the Ronin joined an impromptu caravan of treaded [wagons](/wiki/Wagon "Wagon"), [landspeeders](/wiki/Landspeeder "Landspeeder"), and other travelers on foot making their way to the spaceport from nearby farming villages. The trio heard news from the villagers regarding the Empire, and rumors about the population of the [little red moon](/wiki/Little_red_moon_over_Buna "Little red moon over Buna") over [Buna](/wiki/Buna "Buna") vanishing as they did in the days of the Sith rebellion. The return of the Sith demons frightened the villagers and concerned the Ronin.[[1]](#cite_note-Ronin-1)

As he reached town, the Ronin found several posters, one of them a wanted poster for himself for the crimes of banditry, extortion, and disturbance of the peace in the countryside. The Traveler informed him that the Gran bounty hunter who had helped defend the village from Kouru was in a local [cantina](/wiki/Cantina "Cantina") telling tales of the Sith who had attacked the village. Having his status as a Sith revealed, the Ronin fled, telling R5 to keep an eye on the Traveler. He was hunted down by local [Imperial troops](/wiki/Imperial_troop "Imperial troop"), but was [intercepted](/wiki/Skirmish_at_the_Osou_spaceport "Skirmish at the Osou spaceport") by a resurrected Kouru, narrowly escaping her. During a fight with Kouru in the dockyard, the Ronin jumped onto the open ramp of an ascending ship where R5 was waiting for him. Kouru tried to follow but was pushed down by the Traveler using the [black current of the Force](/wiki/Black_current_of_the_Force "Black current of the Force").[[1]](#cite_note-Ronin-1)

Aboard the [light freighter](/wiki/Light_freighter "Light freighter") *[Poor Crow](/wiki/Poor_Crow "Poor Crow")*, the Ronin used his mechanical knowledge, as well as the Force, to disable the ship's [gravity generator](/wiki/Gravity_generator "Gravity generator"). He threatened to disable more systems as he interrogated the Traveler. The interrogation was quickly interrupted by the bounty hunter [Chie](/wiki/Chie "Chie"), who attacked the Ronin with an [electrostaff](/wiki/Electrostaff "Electrostaff") and the ship's [captain](/wiki/Captain "Captain") [Ekiya](/wiki/Ekiya "Ekiya"). The fight deescalated and the group sat down together to drink tea and discuss what they wanted from each other. The Traveler explained that the crew was [hunting](/wiki/Hunt_for_the_Sith_witch "Hunt for the Sith witch") the Sith witch who was resurrecting the dead and that they had tracked down the Ronin due to his expertise of hunting Sith. Together they set course for the planet [Dekien](/wiki/Dekien "Dekien") in [search](/wiki/Catastrophe_in_the_Seikara_Caverns "Catastrophe in the Seikara Caverns") of a shard of the kyber mirror of the Shinsui Temple, which the Ronin had previously hidden in the [Seikara Caverns](/wiki/Seikara_Caverns "Seikara Caverns").[[1]](#cite_note-Ronin-1)

On route to Dekien the Ronin ate and slept to regain his strength and then at the urging of the Traveler sparred against Chie to prepare him for their upcoming adventure. While sparring, Chie surprised the Ronin with her skill and ferocity, and she interrogated him about his past as a Sith, revealing her distaste for the Jedi's loyalty to their lords rather than the people and the Sith's hypocrisy in conquering planets in the name of liberation. After a long match, the Ronin disarmed her, but her words had provoked a violent anger in him such that he was unknowingly levitating many of the cargo containers in the room with the Force.[[1]](#cite_note-Ronin-1)

### Settling in[]

The Traveler interrupted their session, and as they left the room, Chie revealed to them that she did not trust the Ronin as he stayed behind, the anger ringing in his ears. Eventually Ekiya came to check on the cargo crates that the Ronin had disturbed, and the Ronin realized that they were various religious artifacts containing kyber crystals. She interrogated the Ronin on why he and the Sith had betrayed the Jedi and revealed that she had been a citizen of the lost planet Rei'izu, pressed into the Sith army and taken off world to fight on the front lines shortly before the planet disappeared. The Ronin attempted to apologize, but realized there was no excuse for his past actions and that his numerous sins were catching up to him.[[1]](#cite_note-Ronin-1)

The Ronin built a new lightsaber as a replacement for the one he lost to Kouru and sparred Chie again, revealing that his new lightsaber's hilt extended into a staff. The Ronin passed time with the Traveler, who told the Ronin stories about how the crew had come together and repeatedly cheated in their games of [shogi](/wiki/Shogi "Shogi").[[1]](#cite_note-Ronin-1)

### Facing Hanrai[]

:   "*I want the measure of you, Sith. Meet me as you are. No distractions.*"
:   ―Hanrai, to the Ronin[[1]](#cite_note-Ronin-1)

Within the caves, the Ronin plunged himself and the Traveler into an underground river, following the current to a graveyard. The Traveler revealed their face to the Ronin, in order to properly glare at him, and the Ronin pried for more information about their past, to which the Traveler delayed. Eventually they came across the grotto where the Ronin had left the shard of the mirror of Shinsui Temple. When they realized that the shard was no longer there, the two fled, but they were intercepted by Hanrai before they could reach the exit. Hanrai addressed the Traveler and challenged them to a duel.[[1]](#cite_note-Ronin-1)

When the Traveler protested, Hanrai drew and attacked anyway, and the Ronin drew his own blade to defend the Traveler. The Ronin found an even match in Hanrai, who attacked with the strength of a wave and deftly defended against all of the Ronin's own attacks. The Ronin was briefly distracted when Chie dragged the Traveler off a ledge. He attempted to use the Force to soften their fall but was interrupted by Hanrai, who attacked him and revealed that Chie had been secretly working for him. However, their fight was interrupted when the ceiling of the cavern burst open, torn with the Force by two of Hanrai's entourage who had been killed by Kouru and were now resurrected Sith demons. The Ronin stopped fighting and was caught in a trance staring at the undead, the unnatural consequences of what he had helped unleash on the galaxy, causing his mind to fall into disarray.[[1]](#cite_note-Ronin-1)

In conversation with the Traveler, the Ronin revealed the reason for his first visit to Dekien, and speculated on the Sith witch's plans. As the Ronin grabbed the shard and looked into it, a vision seemingly transported him to a different realm.[[1]](#cite_note-Ronin-1)

### Into the mirror[]

:   "*Would you have me dead?*"  
    "*What would that fix?*"  
    "*Why did you ask me to come back?*"  
    "*I didn't.*"
:   ―The Ronin and the Sith witch[[1]](#cite_note-Ronin-1)

 [![The Ronin (pictured) was shown visions of the past when glancing upon the mirror of Shinsui Temple.](data:image/gif;base64,R0lGODlhAQABAIABAAAAAP///yH5BAEAAAEALAAAAAABAAEAQAICTAEAOw%3D%3D)](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/starwars/images/0/03/Ronin-WhilcePortacio.jpg/revision/latest?cb=20251112121955) [![The Ronin (pictured) was shown visions of the past when glancing upon the mirror of Shinsui Temple.](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/starwars/images/0/03/Ronin-WhilcePortacio.jpg/revision/latest/scale-to-width-down/250?cb=20251112121955)](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/starwars/images/0/03/Ronin-WhilcePortacio.jpg/revision/latest?cb=20251112121955) 

The Ronin (pictured) was shown visions of the past when glancing upon the mirror of Shinsui Temple.

 

In a void where light and darkness merged and replaced the natural sky, the Ronin became aware of the Sith witch before him—a figure he had not seen since leaving Rei'izu. She appeared exactly as she had then, wearing a simple dark [kimono](/wiki/Kimono "Kimono") and trousers with her long hair tied back. The environment around them shifted into a clear vision of the snowcapped Shinsui Temple complex, overlooking the distant city of [Yojou](/wiki/Yojou "Yojou"), Rei'izu's ancient capital. The Ronin recognized it as a reconstruction of the day the Sith rebellion reached its peak. Inside the temple, he and the witch stood before the kyber mirror, an artifact said to reveal truth or grant dominion to those who looked into it.[[1]](#cite_note-Ronin-1)

In this vision of the past, the witch began to pray before the mirror, remaining motionless for days as the Ronin managed their forces in preparation for an impending Imperial counterattack. Weary and uncertain, he eventually approached the mirror himself. When his hand touched its surface, he was overwhelmed by a vision showing the endless cycle of conflict throughout history—how each victory gave rise to new wars, and how their own rebellion repeated that pattern. Unable to accept what he saw, the Ronin struck the mirror with his fist, shattering it into thousands of pieces. The destruction marked the end of the mirror's power and the collapse of what he and the witch had built together.[[1]](#cite_note-Ronin-1)

When the vision dissolved, the two were again alone in the void. The witch accused him of destroying them both through his actions, while the Ronin admitted that he had acted out of despair. When he asked why she had brought him back, she denied doing so and told him that if he was to return, he would do so in her name. The void around them faded, leaving only darkness before the Ronin regained his sight.[[1]](#cite_note-Ronin-1)

Back from his vision, the Ronin moved to the cockpit, where he announced he knew the way to Rei'izu. Ekiya inputted the coordinates he gave her into the [navigation computer](/wiki/Navigation_computer "Navigation computer"), who rejected the numbers after three separate attempts. The Ronin, believing the Sith witch only gave him permission to do it, tried to enter the same coordinates, and this time, the system did not reject them. As a [Jedi Dreadnought](/wiki/Imperial_Dreadnought "Imperial Dreadnought") above deployed small [starfighters](/wiki/Starfighter "Starfighter") and engaged its [tractor beam](/wiki/Tractor_beam "Tractor beam") to immobilize the *Crow*, the ship narrowly escaped by jumping into [hyperspace](/wiki/Hyperspace "Hyperspace"). The journey was short, and they jumped out in the planetary [orbit](/wiki/Orbit "Orbit") of Rei'izu.[[1]](#cite_note-Ronin-1)

While the planet was seemingly lit, [Rei'izu's sun](/wiki/Rei%27izu%27s_sun "Rei'izu's sun") was nowhere the crew could see. The *Crow*'s systems displayed contradictory data, some confirming their position around Rei'izu, and others showing there was no celestial body at their location. It also flashed lights indicating damage to the ship caused by the jump. The Ronin led the crew to Yojou, the old capital city of the first Empire, where they landed, and walked towards [pilgrims' ward](/wiki/Pilgrims%27_ward "Pilgrims' ward"), beyond the [Moga River](/wiki/Moga_River "Moga River").[[1]](#cite_note-Ronin-1)

During the walk, Mirahi possessed Kouru's body, and told the Ronin to meet her past the ward and the chasm, at the end of the true pilgrim's path. After hours of walking, certain technology such as the speeder and [chronometer](/wiki/Chronometer "Chronometer") they used stopped working as they approached Shinsui Temple, at end of the pilgrims' ward. R5-D56 also started showing signs of malfunction. By then, [shades](/wiki/Shade_(Rei%27izu) "Shade (Rei'izu)") of Sith demons amassed around the crew and mounted an attack. The Ronin cut through one of the demons, who melted into thin air. As more followed, Chie decided to take an impaired R5 and the apprentices back to safety.[[1]](#cite_note-Ronin-1)

### Facing his daughter[]

:   "*Aren't you curious? Ask me who I am.  
    I wouldn't presume to know.*"
:   ―Mirahi and the Ronin[[1]](#cite_note-Ronin-1)

Kouru told him a woman awaited him inside the temple. He entered the inner chamber, where he saw his daughter, Mirahi, whose identity was still unknown to him. In a vision, she showed him scenes from her upbringing. She was taken in by the Traveler, then known as Idzuna, who cared for and tutored her. She showed him how, wanting to stop the Jedi and reshape the galaxy, she quarreled with Idzuna, accidentally killing him in the process, then bringing him back as a demon. Mirahi then shared her ultimate goal, which was not to kill the Ronin, but to strenghthen and enlist him as a lord of demons, working under her.[[1]](#cite_note-Ronin-1)

Pondering her proposal, the Ronin became lost in thought, speaking to the ghosts of Kouru, Hanrai, the Traveler, and the witch. After deliberating, he offered his lightsaber to Mirahi, who took it in gratitude. As he opened his arms, his daughter ignited the lightsaber and pierced it through his gut. Shivering, the Ronin embraced his daughter and lurched backwards, causing both to tumble off of the mirror and back into the real world. Rei'izu then sudden burst back into life, as Mirahi worked with Chie, Ekiya, and the Jedi apprentices to ensure they were all safely evacuated from the temple before anyone could inquire them about the gravely injured Ronin and his red lightsaber.[[1]](#cite_note-Ronin-1)

### A new beginning[]

:   "*Look at it this way, you've already done the brave thing. You chose not to die. Go on, now. Live.*"
:   ―The ghost of the Traveler[[1]](#cite_note-Ronin-1)

The following day, the Ronin woke up from his injuries after his daughter stabbed him. It was on this occasion that she told him her name, Mirahi. Sometime later, the Ronin, still on his respirator prosthetic, sat down at a [teahouse](/wiki/Unidentified_teashop "Unidentified teashop") in Rei'izu's [pilgrims' ward](/wiki/Pilgrims%27_ward "Pilgrims' ward") with the [teashop owner](/wiki/Unidentified_teashop_owner "Unidentified teashop owner") and a [friend](/wiki/Unidentified_teashop_owner%27s_friend "Unidentified teashop owner's friend") as they typically did before the Sith takeover of the planet. While two decades had passed since then, from the perspective of the locals, it felt as if only one week had passed, and the Jedi apprentices that had accompanied the Ronin were credited for undoing the decades-long Sith curse the planet and its residents had been under.[[1]](#cite_note-Ronin-1)

Chie and R5-D56 met up with the Ronin at the teashop, who was repairing a droid, amidst a general planetary effort to rebuild Rei'izu, either due to the Sith takeover, or the maintenance needs that arose from twenty years of disrepair. The Ronin had refused more bacta treatment than what was strictly necessary for survival, so that he could focus on helping to rebuild the planet.[[1]](#cite_note-Ronin-1)

After being tipped off by Chie that Imperials were headed to Rei'izu, and that the Ronin might attract unwanted attention to himself, he took up on her offer to leave the planet aboard the *Poor Crow*. Upon climbing aboard the ship, he spoke to the ghosts of Kouru and the Traveller, the latter of which let him know that his daughter Mirahi was also onboard. Mirahi, who had kept a sliver of the kyber mirror with herself, asked for his help in using it to fashion a lightsaber for her so that both of them could fight the Empire and the Jedi. Echoing the witch's wish to honor her name through honoring their daughter, the Ronin obliged, and began to teach Mirahi by disassembling his own lightsaber hilt first.[[1]](#cite_note-Ronin-1)

:   ―The Ronin, to the Grand Master[[6]](#cite_note-Payback-6)

During the clash, the Jedi cut off the Twi'lek's lightsaber-equipped arm prosthetic, and Force pushed her away, rendering her unconscious. In the ensuing duel between the Grand Master and the Ronin, the latter warrior used the Force to push three small [kanzashi sabers](/wiki/Kanzashi_saber "Kanzashi saber") previously belonging to Aneé-san's arm prosthetic into the Jedi's face, destroying his jaw implant. The Grand Master then attempted to pierce the Ronin's gut, which was initially blocked with the Force. The Ronin then gave in, and the blade impaled him.[[6]](#cite_note-Payback-6)

 [![The Ronin delivers the final blow against the Grand Master](data:image/gif;base64,R0lGODlhAQABAIABAAAAAP///yH5BAEAAAEALAAAAAABAAEAQAICTAEAOw%3D%3D)](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/starwars/images/7/7d/GrandMasterFinalBlow.png/revision/latest?cb=20251101061347) [![The Ronin delivers the final blow against the Grand Master](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/starwars/images/7/7d/GrandMasterFinalBlow.png/revision/latest/scale-to-width-down/300?cb=20251101061347)](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/starwars/images/7/7d/GrandMasterFinalBlow.png/revision/latest?cb=20251101061347) 

The Ronin delivers the final blow against the Grand Master

 

The Ronin then pretended to be defeated, and seized the opportunity to grab the Grand Master's arms while an awoken Aneé-san struck the man with a lightsaber attached to her missing right lower leg. Her blow damaged his eyes, making him uncapable of discerning colors. In confusion, the Grand Master picked up Aneé-san's saber instead of his own, and when he got up to attack the Ronin, the former Sith slashed him in the torso, killing his foe.[[6]](#cite_note-Payback-6)

Some time afterward, while the Ewoks celebrated their victory over the Crusaders, the Ronin got ready to leave the island, carrying R5-D56 on his back. Que-Dama offered him a boat and invited him to come again in the future. They then saw the Rodian bounty hunter Do N-Yuk ferrying the Grand Master's prosthetics, claiming that they were worth more than the bounty on the Ronin. The former Sith then noticed that Aneé-san still kept his kyber crystals in the[[6]](#cite_note-Payback-6) hairsticks[[16]](#cite_note-PaybackFilmmakerFocus-16) in her [lekku](/wiki/Lekku "Lekku"). When she refused to give them up, the two again engaged in a lightsaber duel.[[6]](#cite_note-Payback-6)

## Personality and traits[]

### Identity and physical traits[]

:   "*Sir …  
    “That seems an inappropriate thing for you to call me.  
    I don't know your name. No one knows your name.*"
:   ―Yuehiro, to the Ronin[[1]](#cite_note-Ronin-1)

 [![The Ronin had several deep scars on his chest](data:image/gif;base64,R0lGODlhAQABAIABAAAAAP///yH5BAEAAAEALAAAAAABAAEAQAICTAEAOw%3D%3D)](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/starwars/images/8/80/MuscularRonin.png/revision/latest?cb=20250926143806) [![The Ronin had several deep scars on his chest](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/starwars/images/8/80/MuscularRonin.png/revision/latest/scale-to-width-down/250?cb=20250926143806)](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/starwars/images/8/80/MuscularRonin.png/revision/latest?cb=20250926143806) 

The Ronin had several deep scars on his chest

 

His identity and origin was a mystery to all except for R5-D56, though the droid did not disclose it either.[[17]](#cite_note-MeetTheHeroes-17) His taciturn and serious demeanor earned him the nickname of "Grim" —short for "grimace"— among the part of the crew of the *Poor Crow*, while others, such as the Sullustan shopkeep on Genbara and the Traveller, referred to him as "Master Ronin."[[1]](#cite_note-Ronin-1)

The Ronin was a tall,[[1]](#cite_note-Ronin-1) muscular human,[[3]](#cite_note-Visions_1-3) with a broad frame.[[1]](#cite_note-Ronin-1) He possessed a pale skin and long, jet black hair. Two dark scars crossed both his cheeks, from the nose bridge to the jaw.[[11]](#cite_note-The_DuelAD-11) Several deep laceration scars were etched on his torso, as well as one on the top left-hand side of his forehead[[3]](#cite_note-Visions_1-3) and on his right shoulder, the latter wound being inflicted by the Grand Master.[[6]](#cite_note-Payback-6) He had several pins on his shoulders, chest, and within a gash on the left side of his forehead.[[3]](#cite_note-Visions_1-3) Because of his previous injury, and lack of adequate treatment,[[18]](#cite_note-ResilienceSquadron-18) his jaw occasionally throbbed with pain, such as when the weather became damp, or when he was reminded of the lord who shot his jaw in the first place.[[1]](#cite_note-Ronin-1)

### Persona and relationships[]

:   "*Such humility. Surely you must be a Jedi knight.*"
:   ―Genbara village chief, to the Ronin[[1]](#cite_note-Ronin-1)

A man of [prayer](/wiki/Prayer "Prayer"), he was seen as humble and gracious by his peers. He was prone to quiet contemplation and was described by his companion droid R5-D56 as "senile and eccentric."[[1]](#cite_note-Ronin-1) He had a strong sense of right and wrong,[[17]](#cite_note-MeetTheHeroes-17) being loyal only to his own code of honor and R5-D56.[[14]](#cite_note-V3ProductionBrief-14) He carried a deep-seated guilt for his past actions during the Sith rebellion, which haunted him and made him reject proper self care, and hesitate to form attachments, believing himself to be responsible for death and disaster.[[1]](#cite_note-Ronin-1)

The Traveler noted a dramatic flair in him and a capacity for "multiple homicide" when cornered. According to lord Hanrai, records on the Ronin's past as a Jedi revealed him to be talented, ambitious, protective, and loyal. Kouru regarded him as judicious in his movements, sly and attentive,[[1]](#cite_note-Ronin-1) though at times cowardly, such as when in their duel he attempted to hurl a droid at her from behind.[[2]](#cite_note-The_Duel-2) While he initially tried to hide his Sith side during his passage through the village on Genbara, his fighting style, considered foul, revealed his true nature.[[19]](#cite_note-Filmmaker_Focus-19)

Ever since R5-D56 joined the Ronin in his travels,[[7]](#cite_note-VisionsTO1-7) they developed a close relationship, in which R5 often chided his companion when he was being inappropriate,[[1]](#cite_note-Ronin-1) and provided care for the human.[[18]](#cite_note-ResilienceSquadron-18) The Ronin respected the droid, at one point referring to him as "master,"[[3]](#cite_note-Visions_1-3) and showed concern when learning about his temporary destruction on Olcatz at the hands of the Grand Master.[[6]](#cite_note-Payback-6)

## Powers and abilities[]

:   "*It's a shame I'm not a Jedi. You might have had a chance.*"
:   ―The Ronin, to Kouru[[1]](#cite_note-Ronin-1)

 [![The Ronin blocks a lightsaber strike using the Force](data:image/gif;base64,R0lGODlhAQABAIABAAAAAP///yH5BAEAAAEALAAAAAABAAEAQAICTAEAOw%3D%3D)](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/starwars/images/c/c3/The_Duel.jpg/revision/latest?cb=20210922143500) [![The Ronin blocks a lightsaber strike using the Force](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/starwars/images/c/c3/The_Duel.jpg/revision/latest/scale-to-width-down/300?cb=20210922143500)](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/starwars/images/c/c3/The_Duel.jpg/revision/latest?cb=20210922143500) 

The Ronin blocks a lightsaber strike using the Force

 

Though no longer in the prime of his health in the decades after his rebellion, the Ronin was a capable lightsaber duelist and skilled with the Force, both black current and white flare. At the time of his duel against Kouru on Genbara, the Ronin believed his [sensitivity to the Force](/wiki/Force-sensitive "Force-sensitive") had dulled with lack of practice. Despite this, he could still perform feats such as catching Kouru's lightsaber blade between his palms using the Force and moving at high speeds to avoid a blaster bolt.[[1]](#cite_note-Ronin-1)

The Ronin had long been skilled as a mechanic, with the Force amplifying his affinity for the workings of electrical and mechanical systems including droids and vehicles. He believed himself to be better at understanding intricate electronics than the complexity of [sentient](/wiki/Sentience "Sentience") beings. He used his talent for repairs to earn credits, food, and lodging during his travels. Likewise, his mechanical aptitude made him skilled at designing the Sith lightsaber auxiliaries and at breaking starships, as demonstrated when he disabled the *Poor Crow*'s gravity generator and when he single-handedly destroyed the Jedi Dreadnought *Reverent* using the Force.[[1]](#cite_note-Ronin-1)

## Equipment[]

:   "*Oh, the woes of an old warrior boldly flaunting at least one remarkably illegal weapon.*"
:   ―The Traveler[[1]](#cite_note-Ronin-1)

 [![The Ronin and his weapons](data:image/gif;base64,R0lGODlhAQABAIABAAAAAP///yH5BAEAAAEALAAAAAABAAEAQAICTAEAOw%3D%3D)](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/starwars/images/a/a2/RoninArtOfVisions.png/revision/latest?cb=20251006114922) [![The Ronin and his weapons](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/starwars/images/a/a2/RoninArtOfVisions.png/revision/latest/scale-to-width-down/250?cb=20251006114922)](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/starwars/images/a/a2/RoninArtOfVisions.png/revision/latest?cb=20251006114922) 

The Ronin and his weapons

 

Despite Imperial edict, the Ronin illegally carried two lightsabers, [one](/wiki/The_Ronin%27s_lightsaber "The Ronin's lightsaber") of which had an elegant, old hilt resembling a Jedi scion's inheritance and was sheathed in a [scabbard auxiliary](/wiki/Scabbard_auxiliary "Scabbard auxiliary") constructed from other lightsaber hilts, [durasteel](/wiki/Durasteel "Durasteel") and component parts that had a cruder appearance. This primary lightsaber[[1]](#cite_note-Ronin-1) broke at some point after his Padawan years,[[5]](#cite_note-Vol3ConceptArt-5) its blade locked in a perpetual cycle that required the specialized scabbard to contain it, a problem Kouru later fixed after stealing it.[[1]](#cite_note-Ronin-1)

The second was a lightsaber hidden within the scabbard of the primary blade, serving as a backup and to trick his opponents. When the Ronin's main saber was stolen by Kouru, he refashioned the auxiliary into a new weapon with a hilt that could extend into a staff.[[1]](#cite_note-Ronin-1) He also carried the long-barrelled[[2]](#cite_note-The_Duel-2) wakizashi blaster.[[20]](#cite_note-ComicsPreview-20) that had injured his jaw, an old but well-maintained weapon, which he kept on his waist. He also wore a wrist cuff that served as a both as communication device linked to R5-D56,[[1]](#cite_note-Ronin-1) and an activation trigger for weapons systems.[[6]](#cite_note-Payback-6)

After the Sith rebellion, the Ronin used a prosthetic jaw support due to the prior blaster injury, and later a [respirator](/wiki/Breath_mask "Breath mask") after his duel with Hanrai weakened his [lungs](/wiki/Lung "Lung"). He typically wore tattered dark hooded robes with a long white belt, a gray sash at his waist, four gray-colored shoulder accouterments, and an inner lining of cloak where kyber crystals were sewn into the seam[[1]](#cite_note-Ronin-1) of his [kimono](/wiki/Kimono "Kimono").[[21]](#cite_note-The_Duel_Episode_Gallery-21) He sported a metallic-looking hair clip that created a topknot, until it was destroyed in his duel with the Grand Master.[[6]](#cite_note-Payback-6) He also walked on sandals while wearing socks[[1]](#cite_note-Ronin-1) or [zori sandals](/wiki/Zori_sandals "Zori sandals") over his dark [boots](/wiki/Boot "Boot").[[5]](#cite_note-Vol3ConceptArt-5) On occasion, he wore black gloves.[[2]](#cite_note-The_Duel-2) When he first encountered Okinaa, the Ronin wore a tall, pointy hat that covered his face, with five long slits to allow the wearer to see.[[7]](#cite_note-VisionsTO1-7)

## Behind the scenes[]

### Conception and naming[]

:   "*Very early on we saw Takashi Okazaki's design for Ronin and his droid and were utterly blown away -- black and white, with a flourish of red for his lightsaber. We knew we were onto something with* Visions*.*"
:   ―Josh Rimes[[17]](#cite_note-MeetTheHeroes-17)

 [![The lightsaber wielding Ronin](data:image/gif;base64,R0lGODlhAQABAIABAAAAAP///yH5BAEAAAEALAAAAAABAAEAQAICTAEAOw%3D%3D)](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/starwars/images/1/11/HasbroRoninArt.png/revision/latest?cb=20250930144146) [![The lightsaber wielding Ronin](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/starwars/images/1/11/HasbroRoninArt.png/revision/latest/scale-to-width-down/200?cb=20250930144146)](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/starwars/images/1/11/HasbroRoninArt.png/revision/latest?cb=20250930144146) 

The lightsaber wielding Ronin

 

The Ronin first appeared in "[The Duel](/wiki/The_Duel_(short_film) "The Duel (short film)")," a non-[canon](/wiki/Canon "Canon") short film released as part of the [first volume](/wiki/Star_Wars:_Visions_Volume_1 "Star Wars: Visions Volume 1") of the *[Star Wars: Visions](/wiki/Star_Wars:_Visions "Star Wars: Visions")* series, produced by [Kamikaze Douga](/wiki/Kamikaze_Douga "Kamikaze Douga") and released on [Disney+](/wiki/Disney%2B "Disney+")[[2]](#cite_note-The_Duel-2) on [September 22](/wiki/September_22 "September 22"), [2021](/wiki/2021 "2021").[[22]](#cite_note-Date-22) Before the short's release, he was first pictured in a video uploaded to the official *[Star Wars](/wiki/Star_Wars "Star Wars")* [YouTube](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/YouTube "wikipedia:YouTube") channel on [July 3](/wiki/July_3 "July 3"), 2021.[[23]](#cite_note-Special_Look-23) He was voiced by [Masaki Terasoma](/wiki/Masaki_Terasoma "Masaki Terasoma") in the [Japanese](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Japanese_language "wikipedia:Japanese language") version, and [Brian Tee](/wiki/Brian_Tee "Brian Tee") in the [English](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/English_language "wikipedia:English language") dub.[[2]](#cite_note-The_Duel-2) Terasoma and Tee later reprised their roles for the[[6]](#cite_note-Payback-6) sequel[[24]](#cite_note-PaybackEpisodeGuide-24) non-canon short film "[The Duel: Payback](/wiki/The_Duel:_Payback "The Duel: Payback")," released as part of the [third volume](/wiki/Star_Wars:_Visions_Volume_3 "Star Wars: Visions Volume 3") of *Star Wars: Visions* on [Disney+](/wiki/Disney%2B "Disney+") on [October 29](/wiki/October_29 "October 29"), [2025](/wiki/2025 "2025"). The second short film was produced by the studios Kamikaze Douga and [ANIMA](/wiki/ANIMA_(company) "ANIMA (company)").[[25]](#cite_note-Vol3Cast-25)

While not named in "The Duel",[[2]](#cite_note-The_Duel-2) in supplementary material the character has been referred to as "Ronin"[[25]](#cite_note-Vol3Cast-25) or "the Ronin"[[26]](#cite_note-StrikesBack-26) (Japanese: 浪人[[27]](#cite_note-JpName1-27) or ローニン).[[28]](#cite_note-JpName2-28) In real life, [rōnin](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/R%C5%8Dnin "wikipedia:Rōnin") were vagrant, masterless [samurai](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Samurai "wikipedia:Samurai") warriors in feudal [Japan](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Japan "wikipedia:Japan").[[29]](#cite_note-Britannica-29) Sketches from 2020 revealed several potential names for the character, including "ダース・サンピン" (Darth Sanpin,[[30]](#cite_note-CGW1En-30) from 三一, meaning "low-ranking samurai"),[[31]](#cite_note-Sanpin-31) "ダース・スローニン" (Darth Suronin,[[30]](#cite_note-CGW1En-30) from 素浪人, a "poor or lowly rōnin"),[[32]](#cite_note-Suronin-32) and "ダース・ゲドー" (Darth Gedou,[[30]](#cite_note-CGW1En-30) from 外道, meaning "heretic", or "devil").[[33]](#cite_note-Gedou-33)

According to *Visions* executive producer [Josh Rimes](/wiki/Josh_Rimes "Josh Rimes"), [Lucasfilm](/wiki/Lucasfilm "Lucasfilm") was "blown away" by the designs of the Ronin,[[17]](#cite_note-MeetTheHeroes-17) being later chosen to be the main character for a spin-off novel.[[34]](#cite_note-LaunchEvent-34) The Ronin's background and subsequent exploits beyond what is shown in the first short film were expanded upon in the *[Ronin: A Visions Novel](/wiki/Ronin:_A_Visions_Novel "Ronin: A Visions Novel")*, written by [Emma Mieko Candon](/wiki/Emma_Mieko_Candon "Emma Mieko Candon")[[1]](#cite_note-Ronin-1) and published on [October 12](/wiki/October_12 "October 12"), 2021,[[35]](#cite_note-Penguin-Hardcover-35) The Ronin was voiced by narrator [Joel de la Fuente](/wiki/Joel_de_la_Fuente "Joel de la Fuente") in the [unabridged audiobook adaptation](/wiki/Ronin:_A_Visions_Novel_(audiobook) "Ronin: A Visions Novel (audiobook)") published on the same day as the novel.[[36]](#cite_note-Audio1-36)

### Characterization[]

:   "*I was very drawn into how the character was hiding his Sith-side and how people were surprised when they found out.*"
:   ―Takanobu Mizuno[[19]](#cite_note-Filmmaker_Focus-19)

 [![Toshiro Mifune's character in Yojimbo (1961) served as inspiration for the Ronin](data:image/gif;base64,R0lGODlhAQABAIABAAAAAP///yH5BAEAAAEALAAAAAABAAEAQAICTAEAOw%3D%3D)](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/starwars/images/3/3f/Toshiro-Mifune-as-Yojimbo.jpg/revision/latest?cb=20250926152148) [![Toshiro Mifune's character in Yojimbo (1961) served as inspiration for the Ronin](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/starwars/images/3/3f/Toshiro-Mifune-as-Yojimbo.jpg/revision/latest/scale-to-width-down/300?cb=20250926152148)](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/starwars/images/3/3f/Toshiro-Mifune-as-Yojimbo.jpg/revision/latest?cb=20250926152148) 

Toshiro Mifune's character in *Yojimbo* (1961) served as inspiration for the Ronin

 

Designed by [Takashi Okazaki](/wiki/Takashi_Okazaki "Takashi Okazaki")[[37]](#cite_note-Designer-37) based on the dangerous "Man With No Name" rogue archetype common to [samurai](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Samurai_cinema "wikipedia:Samurai cinema") and [Western](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Western_(genre) "wikipedia:Western (genre)") genres, according to Okazaki, the Ronin was inspired by the actor [Toshiro Mifune](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Toshiro_Mifune "wikipedia:Toshiro Mifune")'s "gangster atmosphere and voluminous silhouette" in *[Yojimbo](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Yojimbo "wikipedia:Yojimbo")*,[[38]](#cite_note-ArtOfVisions-38) a [1961](/wiki/1960s "1960s") Japanese film directed by [Akira Kurosawa](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Akira_Kurosawa "wikipedia:Akira Kurosawa").[[39]](#cite_note-RT-39) In his duel with Kouru, the Kamikaze Douga production team thought it would be interesting to explore the idea what would happen if two Sith met, explaining that the Ronin wants to fight her not to save the village,[[40]](#cite_note-RealSound-40) or due to a sense of justice, but that he is merely on a journey to collect crystals, and allows himself some fighting if it does not get in the way of that goal.[[41]](#cite_note-IGNjp-41)

The two Sith then carry on a cowardly fight, contrary to the [bushidō](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bushido "wikipedia:Bushido") (the ethical code of the samurai).[[40]](#cite_note-RealSound-40) According to "The Duel" director [Takanobu Mizuno](/wiki/Takanobu_Mizuno "Takanobu Mizuno"), a scene cut from the short in which the Ronin tries to trick Kouru by striking scarecrow droids would have emphasized the "evil against evil" theme by portraying Ronin's fighting style as more cunning and less honorable. The sequence was removed to reduce the film's runtime and production workload, though a remnant of the idea appears in the finished short when the Ronin uses the Force to strike the Sith from behind with a broken droid.[[42]](#cite_note-Backstory-42) Mizuno was also drawn to how the Ronin initially hid his Sith side, and the reaction from the villagers when truth came to light.[[19]](#cite_note-Filmmaker_Focus-19)

### Sexuality and identity[]

:   "*Among those guardians was the woman who became known as the witch, whom he loved and led the rebellion beside. Yet by the time we meet him, he's forsaken all these bonds, denying himself even the possibility of emotional intimacy. All the same, when that intimacy is unambiguously offered to him, he is nigh incapable of resisting it.*"
:   ―Emma Mieko Candon[[4]](#cite_note-Newsletter2-4)

 [![The Ronin smiles at Aneé-san](data:image/gif;base64,R0lGODlhAQABAIABAAAAAP///yH5BAEAAAEALAAAAAABAAEAQAICTAEAOw%3D%3D)](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/starwars/images/d/db/RoninSmile.png/revision/latest?cb=20251112034333) [![The Ronin smiles at Aneé-san](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/starwars/images/d/db/RoninSmile.png/revision/latest/scale-to-width-down/300?cb=20251112034333)](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/starwars/images/d/db/RoninSmile.png/revision/latest?cb=20251112034333) 

The Ronin smiles at Aneé-san

 

For the novel, Candon stated that she conceived the Ronin as a [queer](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Queer "wikipedia:Queer") character from an early stage of writing, explaining that this decision reflected both her own identity as a queer writer and her interest in exploring the "Big Grim Swordsman" archetype through a queer lens. She felt that the Ronin's personal history—appearing to conform to Jedi norms while privately experiencing friction with those expectations—aligned naturally with this thematic approach.[[4]](#cite_note-Newsletter2-4)

Candon further described the Ronin as a character defined by his capacity for deep connection, but by the time the events of the novel occur, the Ronin has severed all former ties and denies himself emotional intimacy. However, Candon noted that when intimacy is openly offered to him, he struggles to refuse it, humorously referring to him as "a giant [pansexual](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pansexuality "wikipedia:Pansexuality") disaster."[[4]](#cite_note-Newsletter2-4)

In her portrayal of the character, Candon deliberately refrained from depicting him as the "lone killer samurai badass" archetype, as she felt it would downplay the violence in it, as well as the psychological scarring inherent in being the perpetrator of acts of aggression.[[18]](#cite_note-ResilienceSquadron-18) The author saw parallels between the Ronin and [Anakin Skywalker](/wiki/Anakin_Skywalker "Anakin Skywalker") in that both turn against the Jedi[[43]](#cite_note-KitsuneNetwork-43) and are emotionally manipulated by mentor figures.[[44]](#cite_note-AnakinTweet-44) When characterizing the Ronin, the author saw him as a type of Anakin who regrets his fall to the dark side of the Force,[[45]](#cite_note-FTFF-45) and goes into a self-imposed exile, much like [Obi-Wan Kenobi](/wiki/Obi-Wan_Kenobi "Obi-Wan Kenobi") did.[[46]](#cite_note-RuPalp-46)

### Physical condition[]

:   "*So here's a guy who's been bumming around, hunting down Sith, not treating himself right, getting through these battles by the skin of his teeth. Arguably any of them could have killed him at any time. He did make it. But if he suddenly gets pulled into a much bigger problem, he's going to have to do more of that honoring of his body… to actually stand toe to toe with it.*"
:   ―Emma Mieko Candon[[18]](#cite_note-ResilienceSquadron-18)

 [![An injured and unconscious Ronin is taken to a bacta hot spring](data:image/gif;base64,R0lGODlhAQABAIABAAAAAP///yH5BAEAAAEALAAAAAABAAEAQAICTAEAOw%3D%3D)](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/starwars/images/e/ea/EwoksCarryTheRonin.png/revision/latest?cb=20251106213608) [![An injured and unconscious Ronin is taken to a bacta hot spring](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/starwars/images/e/ea/EwoksCarryTheRonin.png/revision/latest/scale-to-width-down/300?cb=20251106213608)](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/starwars/images/e/ea/EwoksCarryTheRonin.png/revision/latest?cb=20251106213608) 

An injured and unconscious Ronin is taken to a bacta hot spring

 

The Ronin was also developed with inspiration from author Emma Mieko Candon's experiences with injury and recovery, in particular in her work with veterans at a lab in university where trauma was researched, as well as from her own poor health following a bout of [viral meningitis](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Viral_meningitis "wikipedia:Viral meningitis"), whose treatment with [steroids](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Steroid "wikipedia:Steroid") scarred her lungs and caused [avascular necrosis](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Avascular_necrosis "wikipedia:Avascular necrosis") of her hipbone, requiring a prosthetic replacement.[[18]](#cite_note-ResilienceSquadron-18)

While not identified as such in the short film, Kamikaze Douga intended for the apparatus on his jaw to be a prosthetic, which Candon developed a backstory for, noting that, while bacta could have been used, the Ronin did not feel like he deserved proper treatment for the injury. She observed that, since he generally does not take good care of his health, R5 acts in a caregiver role in their relationship.[[18]](#cite_note-ResilienceSquadron-18) In the novel, the character is often referred to as "old man," despite the author noting he was likely in his late forties during the events in the book, reflecting his precarious health.[[47]](#cite_note-OldMan-47)
