**Behavioral modification biochips**, also known as **inhibitor chips**, **control chips**, and **behavioral inhibitor biochips**, were a type of organic bio-chip capable of dictating or responding to the thoughts of its host. [Kaminoan](/wiki/Kaminoan "Kaminoan") [cloners](/wiki/Cloning "Cloning") implanted them within each and every [clone trooper](/wiki/Clone_trooper "Clone trooper") in the [Grand Army of the Republic](/wiki/Grand_Army_of_the_Republic "Grand Army of the Republic") at the third stage of their embryonic development. When they were activated, the [troopers](/wiki/Trooper "Trooper") responded to take any order without question. This allowed the [Supreme Chancellor](/wiki/Supreme_Chancellor "Supreme Chancellor") [Palpatine](/wiki/Darth_Sidious "Darth Sidious"), secretly the [Sith Lord](/wiki/Sith_Lord "Sith Lord") called [Darth](/wiki/Darth "Darth") Sidious, to initiate [Clone Protocol 66](/wiki/Order_66 "Order 66"), which wiped out nearly all [Jedi](/wiki/Jedi "Jedi") marked for termination, and any soldier that did not comply with the order, would be executed for treason against the [Galactic Republic](/wiki/Galactic_Republic "Galactic Republic"). The [Galactic Empire](/wiki/Galactic_Empire "Galactic Empire") later employed these devices to keep in line its [enslaved](/wiki/Slavery "Slavery") [Wookiee](/wiki/Wookiee "Wookiee") population on [Kashyyyk](/wiki/Kashyyyk "Kashyyyk").

## Description[]

### Purpose[]

:   "*We modified their genetic structure to make them less independent than the original host. They are totally obedient, taking any order without question.*"
:   ―Lama Su, to [Jedi Knight](/wiki/Jedi_Knight "Jedi Knight") [Obi-Wan Kenobi](/wiki/Obi-Wan_Kenobi "Obi-Wan Kenobi")[[6]](#cite_note-Ep._II-6)

Behavioral modification biochips[[7]](#cite_note-HC10-7) were implanted in the [clone troopers](/wiki/Clone_trooper "Clone trooper") of the [Grand Army of the Republic](/wiki/Grand_Army_of_the_Republic "Grand Army of the Republic") on the suggestion of [Jedi Master](/wiki/Jedi_Master "Jedi Master") [Sifo-Dyas](/wiki/Sifo-Dyas "Sifo-Dyas"), who initially ordered the clone [army](/wiki/Army "Army"), as a contingency against the [clones](/wiki/Cloning "Cloning") being given orders by rogue [Jedi](/wiki/Jedi "Jedi").[[2]](#cite_note-Conspiracy-2) According to [Doctor](/wiki/Doctor "Doctor") [Nala Se](/wiki/Nala_Se "Nala Se"), it was not uncommon for clones of many different kinds to be implanted with inhibitor chips.[[3]](#cite_note-Fugitive-3) However, after the [Sith](/wiki/Sith "Sith") hijacked the project, the chips were tooled to another purpose: to force the clones to comply with the [execution](/wiki/Death_penalty "Death penalty") of [Order 66](/wiki/Order_66 "Order 66"), which wiped out nearly all Jedi.[[5]](#cite_note-ROTS-5) The control chips were extremely effective at taking over the thoughts of those who had been implanted with them, erasing all existing biases and beliefs in the clone [troopers](/wiki/Trooper "Trooper") to force them to [eliminate](/wiki/Death "Death") the Jedi without question.[[8]](#cite_note-TU-8) The clones would believe their Jedi [commanding officers](/wiki/Commanding_officer "Commanding officer") were [traitors](/wiki/Treason "Treason")[[9]](#cite_note-Victory_and_Death-9) even if they had loyally served under them for [years](/wiki/Standard_year "Standard year"). The chips implanted inside the clone troopers could be activated by voice command, with [Palpatine](/wiki/Darth_Sidious "Darth Sidious") only needing to say "Execute Order 66" to do so.[[5]](#cite_note-ROTS-5)

Clone troopers under the sway of their control chips would prioritize carrying out Order 66 above all else, including their own lives. Under this directive, any and all Jedi leadership marked for termination, and any soldier that did not comply with the order, would be executed for treason against the [Republic](/wiki/Galactic_Republic "Galactic Republic").[[10]](#cite_note-Shattered-10) Although they could still make rational decisions, they could not be reasoned with regarding the order, and would unhesitatingly go after even their own brothers if they realized that other clones were not obeying the order.[[9]](#cite_note-Victory_and_Death-9) The [brainwashed](/wiki/Brain "Brain")[[11]](#cite_note-Journeys:_Beginnings-11) clones were also aware of Palpatine's true identity as the [Sith Lord](/wiki/Sith_Lord "Sith Lord") [Darth](/wiki/Darth "Darth") Sidious, with clone [officers](/wiki/Officer "Officer") who received the order immediately addressing him as "Lord Sidious"[[10]](#cite_note-Shattered-10) or "my lord,"[[5]](#cite_note-ROTS-5) and clone troopers who had been completely unaware of the [Supreme Chancellor](/wiki/Supreme_Chancellor "Supreme Chancellor")'s double identity beforehand referring to him by his Sith name.[[9]](#cite_note-Victory_and_Death-9) Pre-activation, the chips also had the side effect of giving the clones [nightmares](/wiki/Dream "Dream") relating to carrying out the command, which were described as a "mission" by some dying clones.[[2]](#cite_note-Conspiracy-2) Clones under the sway of the chips occasionally used the phrase "Good soldiers follow orders."[[8]](#cite_note-TU-8)

Commander CT-7567 "Rex" struggles against the influence of his inhibitor chip as he tries to resist Order 66.

 

It was possible in rare cases[[12]](#cite_note-Kanan_5-12) for some clones to resist the will of the chips and hesitate from executing the order, but the act was extremely difficult, and only removing the chip entirely could free a clone from the compulsion to carry out Order 66. Clones whose chips were removed after activation still remembered what they had done under its influence.[[10]](#cite_note-Shattered-10) Even without its removal, the chips started to wear off and become less effective over time.[[13]](#cite_note-BB_What's_Next-13)

Although extremely rare, a few clones were immune to the chips,[[14]](#cite_note-Battle_Scars-14) with the odds being particularly high for [genetically defective](/wiki/Genetically_defective_clone "Genetically defective clone") or modified clones. Even if such clones were not immune, their mutations could still interfere with their chips' performance. In such cases, chips could be amplified to make clones totally loyal.[[15]](#cite_note-Aftermath-15) Immunity, however, was not absolute: it was possible for the chip to be fully activated and induce brainwashing as long as it remained present, through means such as head injuries.[[14]](#cite_note-Battle_Scars-14)

The chips were later installed in [enslaved](/wiki/Slavery "Slavery") [Wookiees](/wiki/Wookiee "Wookiee") by the [Galactic Empire](/wiki/Galactic_Empire "Galactic Empire") to keep them in line. Imperial Advisor [Gallius Rax](/wiki/Gallius_Rax "Gallius Rax") also had biochips installed in [Rebel](/wiki/Alliance_to_Restore_the_Republic "Alliance to Restore the Republic") prisoners kept at [Ashmead's Lock](/wiki/Ashmead%27s_Lock "Ashmead's Lock") on [Kashyyyk](/wiki/Kashyyyk "Kashyyyk") as part of an assassination plan against officials of the [New Republic](/wiki/New_Republic "New Republic").[[1]](#cite_note-Life_Debt-1) When the chips were discovered, they malfunctioned in a planned degradation before Republic [slicers](/wiki/Slicer "Slicer") could learn much about them.[[16]](#cite_note-Empire's_End-16)

### Structure[]

:   "*I believe this is an organic chip built out of cells.*"
:   ―[AZI-3](/wiki/AZI-345211896246498721347 "AZI-345211896246498721347")[[3]](#cite_note-Fugitive-3)

 [![The chips were organic and implanted into the clones of the Republic Army.](data:image/gif;base64,R0lGODlhAQABAIABAAAAAP///yH5BAEAAAEALAAAAAABAAEAQAICTAEAOw%3D%3D)](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/starwars/images/9/91/Chip_scanner.png/revision/latest?cb=20220109191537) [![The chips were organic and implanted into the clones of the Republic Army.](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/starwars/images/9/91/Chip_scanner.png/revision/latest/scale-to-width-down/260?cb=20220109191537)](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/starwars/images/9/91/Chip_scanner.png/revision/latest?cb=20220109191537) 

The chips were organic and implanted into the clones of the Republic Army.

 

The biochips were, as the name indicated, made out of biological material, specifically genetically engineered cells. The [DNA](/wiki/DNA "DNA") of the chips' cells was different from that of the clones they were implanted in.[[3]](#cite_note-Fugitive-3) They were microscopic in size with an amorphous shape and a punctured, fibrous texture, resembling muscle tissue. The microscopic size of the microchips combined with their biological nature made them extremely difficult to detect with standard medical equipment: it took a [Level 5 atomic brain scan](/wiki/Level_5_atomic_brain_scan "Level 5 atomic brain scan") to find the chip inside the head of trooper [Tup](/wiki/Tup "Tup"), after lower-level scans failed to register any anomaly.[[2]](#cite_note-Conspiracy-2) [Ahsoka Tano](/wiki/Ahsoka_Tano "Ahsoka Tano") was later unable to detect [Clone Commander](/wiki/Clone_Commander "Clone Commander") [Rex](/wiki/Rex "Rex")'s chip with the standard medical facilities onboard a [*Venator*-class Star Destroyer](/wiki/Venator-class_Star_Destroyer "Venator-class Star Destroyer"), relying instead on [the Force](/wiki/The_Force "The Force") to locate it.[[10]](#cite_note-Shattered-10)

The inhibitor chip was located ninety degrees from the right orbital floor, below the parietal and temporal intersection, in the brain of the genetically defective [clone commando](/wiki/Clone_commando "Clone commando") [Wrecker](/wiki/Wrecker "Wrecker"). However, as a mutated clone, he was structurally different in certain ways from a regular [Jango Fett](/wiki/Jango_Fett "Jango Fett") clone.[[14]](#cite_note-Battle_Scars-14) Clones who had had their chips removed sported scars on their right temples.[[17]](#cite_note-The_Lost_Commanders-17)

The chips were durable, with malfunctions being rare:[[18]](#cite_note-Inhibitor_chip_DB-18) out of the millions of clones that served in the [Grand Army of the Republic](/wiki/Grand_Army_of_the_Republic "Grand Army of the Republic") over the course of the [Clone Wars](/wiki/Clone_Wars "Clone Wars"),[[19]](#cite_note-MIA-19) Tup[[8]](#cite_note-TU-8) was one of only a few who suffered from chip malfunctions and the resulting erratic behavior.[[18]](#cite_note-Inhibitor_chip_DB-18) However, a chip malfunction could also prove lethal, as after Tup's chip malfunctioned, it broke down and began to rot,[[3]](#cite_note-Fugitive-3) and his health began to deteriorate rapidly, culminating in his death shortly after the chip was removed.[[2]](#cite_note-Conspiracy-2) Damage to the inhibitor chip could result in [headaches](/wiki/Headache "Headache"): Tup complained of one just before his chip went haywire,[[8]](#cite_note-TU-8) and Wrecker began suffering headaches connected to his chip[[20]](#cite_note-Decommissioned-20) after a series of head injuries sustained post-Order 66.[[21]](#cite_note-Replacements-21)

## History[]

### Creation of the clone army[]

:   "*You must remember the chip is a safeguard against betrayal from rogue Jedi. Master Sifo-Dyas was the only Jedi who knew of this. But with his passing, we alone know of this secret, and we must keep it that way.*"
:   ―The disguised Count Dooku, to Lama Su[[2]](#cite_note-Conspiracy-2)

Prior to the [Battle](/wiki/Battle_of_Naboo "Battle of Naboo") of [Naboo](/wiki/Naboo "Naboo"), former [Jedi Council](/wiki/Jedi_High_Council "Jedi High Council") member Master Sifo-Dyas contacted the [Kaminoans](/wiki/Kaminoan "Kaminoan") to order a clone [army](/wiki/Army "Army") for the [Republic](/wiki/Galactic_Republic "Galactic Republic").[[6]](#cite_note-Ep._II-6) He provided them with the inhibitor chip to act as a safeguard against betrayal from rogue Jedi giving orders to the clones.[[2]](#cite_note-Conspiracy-2) However, Sith [Lords](/wiki/Lord "Lord") Darth Sidious and his new [apprentice](/wiki/Sith_apprentice "Sith apprentice"), [Darth Tyranus](/wiki/Dooku "Dooku"), had Sifo-Dyas [eliminated](/wiki/Mission_to_Oba_Diah_(Sifo-Dyas) "Mission to Oba Diah (Sifo-Dyas)")[[22]](#cite_note-The_Lost_One-22) and took over his project, keeping it a secret from the Jedi. They retooled the chips for Order 66, programming them to turn the clones against the Jedi upon hearing an activation phrase. Kaminoans like [Lama Su](/wiki/Lama_Su "Lama Su") and Nala Se seemed to believe the chip was still only in case of rogue Jedi.[[2]](#cite_note-Conspiracy-2)

### Conspiracy[]

:   "*I already know this report's going to fall on deaf ears, but I owe it to Fives to record what I saw. I'm not sure I believe it myself, but there's a possibility that the inhibitor chips the Kaminoans put inside of us have a purpose that we don't yet fully understand.*"
:   ―Clone Captain Rex's confidential grievance report on ARC trooper Fives' suspicions about the "inhibitor" chips[[10]](#cite_note-Shattered-10)

 [![Tup prepares to shoot Tiplar due to his malfunctioning control chip.](data:image/gif;base64,R0lGODlhAQABAIABAAAAAP///yH5BAEAAAEALAAAAAABAAEAQAICTAEAOw%3D%3D)](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/starwars/images/3/3b/PrematureExecution-TU.png/revision/latest?cb=20150408070427) [![Tup prepares to shoot Tiplar due to his malfunctioning control chip.](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/starwars/images/3/3b/PrematureExecution-TU.png/revision/latest/scale-to-width-down/275?cb=20150408070427)](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/starwars/images/3/3b/PrematureExecution-TU.png/revision/latest?cb=20150408070427) 

Tup prepares to shoot Tiplar due to his malfunctioning control chip.

 

During the Clone Wars, the control chip conspiracy was nearly uncovered when the Order 66 function of the chip in clone trooper Tup was prematurely activated, causing him to execute [Jedi General](/wiki/Jedi_General "Jedi General") [Tiplar](/wiki/Tiplar "Tiplar") during the [Battle of Ringo Vinda](/wiki/Battle_of_Ringo_Vinda "Battle of Ringo Vinda").[[8]](#cite_note-TU-8) Though the Jedi discovered the existence of the chips, they never learned of their true purpose: [Chief Medical Scientist](/wiki/Chief_Medical_Scientist "Chief Medical Scientist") Nala Se claimed the chips were there to make the clones less aggressive than their [clone template](/wiki/Clone_template "Clone template"), Jango Fett, and to repress any insanity they may have inherited from him. In addition, [ARC trooper](/wiki/Advanced_Recon_Commando "Advanced Recon Commando") [Fives](/wiki/Fives "Fives"), who had been investigating the matter,[[3]](#cite_note-Fugitive-3) was killed by Clone [Commander](/wiki/Commander "Commander") [Fox](/wiki/Fox "Fox") of the [Coruscant Guard](/wiki/Coruscant_Guard "Coruscant Guard"). Afterwards, the official story was that Tup and Fives had both been infected with a rare [Ringo Vindan](/wiki/Ringo_Vinda "Ringo Vinda") [parasite](/wiki/Parasite "Parasite") which caused their chips to break down.[[4]](#cite_note-Orders-4)

Though Fives had warned that the conspiracy went to the highest level and that Supreme Chancellor Sheev Palpatine had tried to stop him from revealing what he knew, [Anakin Skywalker](/wiki/Anakin_Skywalker "Anakin Skywalker") stubbornly refused to believe that Palpatine was capable of what Fives was describing. Fives died in the arms of his friend [Clone Captain](/wiki/Clone_Captain "Clone Captain") Rex, whom he warned of the chips' existence and that the conspiracy was bigger than any of them. As he died, Fives' last words were that the "mission" and the "nightmares" were finally over. Due to Fives having been covertly drugged into a state of incoherency prior to his death, making him seem insane,[[4]](#cite_note-Orders-4) Rex mostly did not believe his claims. However, he could not completely dismiss the possibility that the so-called "inhibitor" chips had a hidden purpose. Uncertain as to whether he believed it or not, out of loyalty to his friend, Rex filed a confidential grievance report in which he outlined the theory, although he was aware it was likely to be ignored.[[10]](#cite_note-Shattered-10) The [Jedi High Council](/wiki/Jedi_High_Council "Jedi High Council") eventually [discovered](/wiki/Mission_to_Oba_Diah_(Clone_Wars) "Mission to Oba Diah (Clone Wars)") the Sith involvement in the clones' creation, but chose to keep it a secret, never making a connection between the discovery and the "inhibitor" chips.[[22]](#cite_note-The_Lost_One-22)

[501st Legion](/wiki/501st_Legion "501st Legion") [medic](/wiki/Medic "Medic") [Kix](/wiki/Kix "Kix"), who had encountered Fives while he was on the run and whom Fives had told he was being framed, eventually grew suspicious of the official explanation for Fives and Tup's behavior.[[4]](#cite_note-Orders-4)[[23]](#cite_note-Crimson_Corsair-23) At some point after the [Battle of Anaxes](/wiki/Battle_of_Anaxes "Battle of Anaxes"),[[24]](#cite_note-Kix_note-24) Kix, doing his own investigating, found out more than Fives had, including not only the true purpose of the chips but that Palpatine was himself a Sith Lord. However, he drew the attention of the Sith and was subsequently captured by [Separatist](/wiki/Confederacy_of_Independent_Systems "Confederacy of Independent Systems") forces sent by [Count](/wiki/Count "Count") Dooku. His subsequent denials about sharing any of his knowledge failed to convince his captors, who placed him in a [cryo-cycle stasis pod](/wiki/Cryo-cycle_stasis_pod "Cryo-cycle stasis pod") for transit to [Serenno](/wiki/Serenno "Serenno") so Dooku could personally interrogate him. However, the [cruiser](/wiki/Cruiser "Cruiser") Kix was being transported on, the *[Obrexta III](/wiki/Obrexta_III "Obrexta III")*, was [attacked](/wiki/Attack_in_an_unidentified_sector "Attack in an unidentified sector") by Republic forces en route, subsequently crashing into the remote [planet](/wiki/Planet "Planet") [Ponemah](/wiki/Ponemah_Terminal "Ponemah Terminal").[[23]](#cite_note-Crimson_Corsair-23) [Ponder](/wiki/Ponder "Ponder"), a clone who lost his leg after his transport got shot down by Separatists during the [Battle on Metalorn](/wiki/Battle_on_Metalorn_(Ponder) "Battle on Metalorn (Ponder)") and was regulated to being a cook for the other clones on Kamino before eventually meeting the [Keshiri](/wiki/Keshiri "Keshiri") woman [Sohi](/wiki/Sohi "Sohi") and falling in love, had his inhibitor chip removed.[[25]](#cite_note-Sanctuary-25)

### Order 66[]

:   "*It was at the end, the end of the war. Our fellow soldiers, the clones, the ones we Jedi fought side by side with, suddenly turned and betrayed us. I watched them kill my master. She fought beside them for years and they gunned her down in a second! Then they came for me. Later they said they had chips in their heads that made them do it. Said they had no choice.*"
:   ―Kanan Jarrus describing what happened during Order 66 to his Padawan, Ezra Bridger[[17]](#cite_note-The_Lost_Commanders-17)

When Sidious executed Order 66, the chips worked as intended, with the overwhelming majority of the clone army brainwashed into obedience, resulting in the downfall of the [Jedi Order](/wiki/Jedi_Order "Jedi Order") and eventually, the end of the Clone Wars as a whole.[[5]](#cite_note-ROTS-5) The few exceptions included clones who had managed to remove their chips[[10]](#cite_note-Shattered-10) and the rare clones who were somehow immune.[[14]](#cite_note-Battle_Scars-14)

#### Aftermath of Order 66[]

After Rex left Saleucami,[[29]](#cite_note-Cut_and_Run-29) he began to fight the Empire[[20]](#cite_note-Decommissioned-20) and help those amongst his brothers who were outside Imperial control.[[31]](#cite_note-Rex-31)

#### Occupation of Kashyyyk[]

Once the Galactic Empire [gained](/wiki/Proclamation_of_the_New_Order "Proclamation of the New Order") control of [the galaxy](/wiki/The_galaxy "The galaxy"), it enslaved the Wookiee population of [Kashyyyk](/wiki/Kashyyyk "Kashyyyk"). Enslaved Wookiees were implanted with inhibitor chips which could be used to cause them extreme pain should they rebel against their captors. The chip could be activated in a rebelling Wookiee, causing pain until the Wookiee either fell back in line or died from the pain. As the Empire became more savvy, a common tactic was to instead subject family or clan members to pain, as the Wookiees would often be willing to sacrifice their own lives but couldn't bear to see the suffering of clan or family members. During the [liberation of Kashyyyk](/wiki/Liberation_of_Kashyyyk "Liberation of Kashyyyk"), a strike team led by [Han Solo](/wiki/Han_Solo "Han Solo") disabled the field controlling the inhibitor chips, effectively rendering them useless and allowing the Wookiees to strike freely, though this was met with an [orbital bombardment](/wiki/Orbital_bombardment "Orbital bombardment") by [Star Destroyers](/wiki/Star_Destroyer "Star Destroyer") from the Galactic Empire.[[1]](#cite_note-Life_Debt-1)

Ultimately, the Imperial occupiers were defeated when a small [New Republic](/wiki/New_Republic "New Republic") fleet led by [Admiral](/wiki/Admiral "Admiral") [Ackbar](/wiki/Gial_Ackbar "Gial Ackbar") and [Captain](/wiki/Captain "Captain") [Wedge Antilles](/wiki/Wedge_Antilles "Wedge Antilles") entered the Kashyyyk system and forced their surrender.[[1]](#cite_note-Life_Debt-1) Wookiee [Chewbacca](/wiki/Chewbacca "Chewbacca")'s son [Lumpawaroo](/wiki/Lumpawaroo "Lumpawaroo") likened the inhibitor chips to a song which sounded like the thrumming wings of a swarm of [drriw-tcha blood-worm flies](/wiki/Drriw-tcha_blood-worm_fly "Drriw-tcha blood-worm fly").[[16]](#cite_note-Empire's_End-16)

Rax also installed inorganic bio-chips in the [brain](/wiki/Brain "Brain") stems of a hundred [Rebel Alliance](/wiki/Alliance_to_Restore_the_Republic "Alliance to Restore the Republic") [prisoners](/wiki/Prisoner "Prisoner") at [Ashmead's Lock](/wiki/Ashmead%27s_Lock "Ashmead's Lock") [prison](/wiki/Prison "Prison") on Kashyyyk. These prisoners were part of a secret contingency plan to [assassinate](/wiki/Assassin "Assassin") New Republic government officials and [military](/wiki/New_Republic_Defense_Force "New Republic Defense Force") [commanders](/wiki/Commander "Commander") during the [Liberation Day](/wiki/Liberation_Day_(New_Republic) "Liberation Day (New Republic)") celebrations on [Chandrila](/wiki/Chandrila "Chandrila")[[1]](#cite_note-Life_Debt-1) in [5 ABY](/wiki/5_ABY "5 ABY").[[43]](#cite_note-Life_Debt_Date-43) Rax and his undercover [agent](/wiki/Agent "Agent") [Windom Traducier](/wiki/Windom_Traducier "Windom Traducier") used a transponder installed at the [Hanna City opera house](/wiki/Hanna_City_opera_house "Hanna City opera house") to transmit signals which activated the agents.[[1]](#cite_note-Life_Debt-1)

Prisoners installed with the bio-chips included [Brentin Lore Wexley](/wiki/Brentin_Lore_Wexley "Brentin Lore Wexley"), [Jonda Jae-Talwar](/wiki/Jonda_Jae-Talwar "Jonda Jae-Talwar"), and [Esdo](/wiki/Esdo "Esdo"). The unwilling assassins [killed](/wiki/Attack_on_Chandrila "Attack on Chandrila") several New Republic officials and commanders, including [Hostis Ij](/wiki/Hostis_Ij "Hostis Ij"), but failed to kill [Chancellor](/wiki/Chancellor_(New_Republic) "Chancellor (New Republic)") [Mon Mothma](/wiki/Mon_Mothma "Mon Mothma"). The New Republic authorities managed to subdue the prisoners. Investigations by the [New Republic Security Bureau](/wiki/New_Republic_Security_Bureau "New Republic Security Bureau") and the [slicer](/wiki/Slicer "Slicer") [Conder Kyl](/wiki/Conder_Kyl "Conder Kyl") uncovered the chips.[[1]](#cite_note-Life_Debt-1) However, they malfunctioned in some kind of planned degradation before the New Republic could conduct further investigations.[[16]](#cite_note-Empire's_End-16)

Around fifty years after the end of the Clone Wars, the wreckage of the *Obrexta III* was discovered on Ponemah after an ionic storm recharged the ship's [distress beacon](/wiki/Distress_beacon "Distress beacon"). Over the [decades](/wiki/Standard_decade "Standard decade"), the ship had acquired a mythical reputation as having carried a treasure of great value to Count Dooku. Since Dooku had been the wealthiest man in [the galaxy](/wiki/The_galaxy "The galaxy") prior to his death, the wreck attracted many bands of [pirates](/wiki/Pirate "Pirate") willing to venture into Ponemah's dangerous [Sea of Sand](/wiki/Sea_of_Sand "Sea of Sand") to find the *Obrexta III* and claim its alleged valuables. The [Hunt for Dooku's Treasure](/wiki/Hunt_for_Dooku%27s_Treasure "Hunt for Dooku's Treasure") was eventually won by the [crew](/wiki/Sidon_Ithano%27s_pirate_crew "Sidon Ithano's pirate crew") of [Sidon Ithano](/wiki/Sidon_Ithano "Sidon Ithano").[[23]](#cite_note-Crimson_Corsair-23)

However, they instead discovered Kix, who had been kept alive inside his stasis pod for all that time. Ithano had the rest of his crew escape with the delirious clone, staying behind to what seemed his death as a giant [sand worm](/wiki/Sand_worm "Sand worm") destroyed the wreckage. However, he survived and rejoined his crew with the *Obrexta III*'s [memory core](/wiki/Memory_bank "Memory bank"), which contained the location of every Separatist base in the galaxy, including some that remained undisturbed. Kix told his story to the pirates, including his doomed attempt to reveal the truth of the chips to the Republic and Jedi. ## Behind the scenes[]

 [![Concept art of a chip](data:image/gif;base64,R0lGODlhAQABAIABAAAAAP///yH5BAEAAAEALAAAAAABAAEAQAICTAEAOw%3D%3D)](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/starwars/images/6/64/Inhibitor-Chip.jpg/revision/latest?cb=20140309215219) [![Concept art of a chip](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/starwars/images/6/64/Inhibitor-Chip.jpg/revision/latest/scale-to-width-down/250?cb=20140309215219)](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/starwars/images/6/64/Inhibitor-Chip.jpg/revision/latest?cb=20140309215219) 

Concept art of a chip

 

Control chips first appeared in the *[Star Wars: The Clone Wars](/wiki/Star_Wars:_The_Clone_Wars "Star Wars: The Clone Wars")* episode "[Conspiracy](/wiki/Conspiracy "Conspiracy")," the first of a four episode arc written by [Katie Lucas](/wiki/Katie_Lucas "Katie Lucas") and released as part of [*The Lost Missions*](/wiki/The_Clone_Wars:_The_Lost_Missions "The Clone Wars: The Lost Missions") season. Originally identified as inhibitor chips,[[2]](#cite_note-Conspiracy-2) they were referred to as control chips in the *[Star Wars Rebels](/wiki/Star_Wars_Rebels "Star Wars Rebels")* episode "[The Lost Commanders](/wiki/The_Lost_Commanders "The Lost Commanders"),"[[17]](#cite_note-The_Lost_Commanders-17) and first identified as behavioral modification biochips in the [tenth issue](/wiki/Star_Wars_Helmet_Collection_10 "Star Wars Helmet Collection 10") of the magazine *[Star Wars Helmet Collection](/wiki/Star_Wars_Helmet_Collection "Star Wars Helmet Collection")*.[[7]](#cite_note-HC10-7)
