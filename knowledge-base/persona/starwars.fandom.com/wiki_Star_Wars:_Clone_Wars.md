***Star Wars: Clone Wars*** is an animated television micro-series that chronicles the [Clone Wars](/wiki/Clone_Wars/Legends "Clone Wars/Legends") between the [Galactic Republic](/wiki/Galactic_Republic/Legends "Galactic Republic/Legends") and the [Confederacy of Independent Systems](/wiki/Confederacy_of_Independent_Systems/Legends "Confederacy of Independent Systems/Legends"). Produced by [Cartoon Network](/wiki/Cartoon_Network "Cartoon Network") Studios, the series originally aired from [2003](/wiki/2003 "2003") to [2005](/wiki/2005 "2005"), and was intended to serve as a bridge between the films [*Star Wars*: Episode II *Attack of the Clones*](/wiki/Star_Wars:_Episode_II_Attack_of_the_Clones "Star Wars: Episode II Attack of the Clones") and [*Star Wars*: Episode III *Revenge of the Sith*](/wiki/Star_Wars:_Episode_III_Revenge_of_the_Sith "Star Wars: Episode III Revenge of the Sith"). The series consists of twenty-five chapters; Seasons 1 and 2 (Volume I) are made up of three-minute installments, while Season 3 (Volume II) is made up of twelve-to-fifteen minute installments. The success of *Star Wars: Clone Wars* led to production of the similarly titled *[Star Wars: The Clone Wars](/wiki/Star_Wars:_The_Clone_Wars "Star Wars: The Clone Wars")*.

## Development[]

 [![CloneWarsPoster](data:image/gif;base64,R0lGODlhAQABAIABAAAAAP///yH5BAEAAAEALAAAAAABAAEAQAICTAEAOw%3D%3D)](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/starwars/images/9/9e/CloneWarsPoster.jpg/revision/latest?cb=20100610100944) [![CloneWarsPoster](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/starwars/images/9/9e/CloneWarsPoster.jpg/revision/latest/scale-to-width-down/200?cb=20100610100944)](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/starwars/images/9/9e/CloneWarsPoster.jpg/revision/latest?cb=20100610100944) 



 

In the early 2000s, [Hasbro](/wiki/Hasbro "Hasbro") asked [Lucasfilm](/wiki/Lucasfilm "Lucasfilm") to develop something between [prequel films](/wiki/Prequel_trilogy "Prequel trilogy"),[[7]](#cite_note-Stormtrooper-7) which they hoped would boost action figure sales and help promote the upcoming [*Star Wars*: Episode III *Revenge of the Sith*](/wiki/Star_Wars:_Episode_III_Revenge_of_the_Sith "Star Wars: Episode III Revenge of the Sith").[[8]](#cite_note-8)[[9]](#cite_note-9) [Cartoon Network](/wiki/Cartoon_Network "Cartoon Network")—who had previously worked with Hasbro on their [*Transformers*](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Transformers "wikipedia:Transformers") series—suggested [Genndy Tartakovsky](/wiki/Genndy_Tartakovsky "Genndy Tartakovsky"), who was finishing work on the animated series [*Samurai Jack*](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Samurai_Jack "wikipedia:Samurai Jack"). Lucasfilm met with Tartakovsky,[[7]](#cite_note-Stormtrooper-7) who agreed to the project if the shorts could be extended from the original proposed 1-minute runtime up to 3-to-5 minutes.[[10]](#cite_note-blastr-10)

 [![Clone Wars opening logo](data:image/gif;base64,R0lGODlhAQABAIABAAAAAP///yH5BAEAAAEALAAAAAABAAEAQAICTAEAOw%3D%3D)](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/starwars/images/d/d2/CW_Title.png/revision/latest?cb=20181130221322) [![Clone Wars opening logo](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/starwars/images/d/d2/CW_Title.png/revision/latest/scale-to-width-down/200?cb=20181130221322)](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/starwars/images/d/d2/CW_Title.png/revision/latest?cb=20181130221322) 

*Clone Wars* opening logo

 

Tartakovsky pitched the show as having "a [*Band of Brothers*](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Band_of_Brothers "wikipedia:Band of Brothers")-feel to it—where it's episodes of different battles and strategies during the Clone Wars."[[11]](#cite_note-11) After Lucasfilm and Cartoon Network approved the idea, Tartakovsky produced twenty 3-minute episodes.[[12]](#cite_note-SWArchive-Three-Minute_Epics:_A_Look_at_Star_Wars:_Clone_Wars-12) The series was produced using mostly [traditional animation](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Traditional_animation "wikipedia:Traditional animation") techniques.[[13]](#cite_note-13) For example, Tartakovsky animated C-3PO's eyes in a way that pays homage to the animation style of [Nelvana](/wiki/Nelvana "Nelvana"), who produced the [*Droids*](/wiki/Star_Wars:_Droids:_The_Adventures_of_R2-D2_and_C-3PO "Star Wars: Droids: The Adventures of R2-D2 and C-3PO") and [*Ewoks*](/wiki/Ewoks_(television_series) "Ewoks (television series)") series and *[The Star Wars Holiday Special](/wiki/The_Star_Wars_Holiday_Special "The Star Wars Holiday Special")*'s [animated segment](/wiki/The_Story_of_the_Faithful_Wookiee "The Story of the Faithful Wookiee")—all of which he had grown up with.[[14]](#cite_note-14)

The series's success led to the greenlight of a third season consisting of five 12- to 15-minute episodes.[[15]](#cite_note-SWArchive-The_Clone_Wars_Micro-Series_Continues!-15) Cartoon Network held a poll to determine which of three new Jedi should be introduced in the series. The three choices were [Roron Corobb](/wiki/Roron_Corobb/Legends "Roron Corobb/Legends"), [Voolvif Monn](/wiki/Voolvif_Monn "Voolvif Monn"), and [Foul Moudama](/wiki/Foul_Moudama "Foul Moudama"). Voolvif Monn won the vote and was introduced in "[Chapter 20](/wiki/Chapter_20_(Star_Wars:_Clone_Wars) "Chapter 20 (Star Wars: Clone Wars)")." The other two Jedi were later introduced anyway, both of whom played parts larger than Monn's.[[16]](#cite_note-poll-16)[[17]](#cite_note-SWArchive-Gentle_Giant_Toons_Out_the_Original_Trilogy-17)

## Release and reception[]

*Star Wars: Clone Wars* ran on Cartoon Network from [November 7](/wiki/November_7 "November 7"), 2003 to [March 25](/wiki/March_25 "March 25"), [2005](/wiki/2005 "2005"). The episodes were released simultaneously on television and online for members of the [Hyperspace fan club](/wiki/Hyperspace:_The_Official_Star_Wars_Fan_Club "Hyperspace: The Official Star Wars Fan Club"). They were available the next day on cartoonnetwork.com and on [StarWars.com](/wiki/StarWars.com "StarWars.com") for non-Hyperspace members.[[18]](#cite_note-Clone_Wars_Home_on_Hyperspace-18) In an act of cross-promotion, Cartoon Network ran a *Star Wars* contest involving the [2004 DVD release](/wiki/Star_Wars_Trilogy_(DVD) "Star Wars Trilogy (DVD)") of the [original trilogy](/wiki/Original_trilogy "Original trilogy").[[19]](#cite_note-19)

[20th Century Fox](/wiki/20th_Century_Studios "20th Century Studios") released the series on [home video](/wiki/Star_Wars_home_video_releases "Star Wars home video releases") in two phases in 2005; The [first DVD](/wiki/Star_Wars:_Clone_Wars_Volume_One "Star Wars: Clone Wars Volume One") was released on [March 22](/wiki/March_22 "March 22"), 2005 (one day after the third and final season began airing) and contained Chapters 1 through 20.[[20]](#cite_note-SWArchive-Star_Wars:_Clone_Wars_Volume_1_Coming_to_DVD-20) The [second DVD](/wiki/Star_Wars:_Clone_Wars_Volume_Two "Star Wars: Clone Wars Volume Two") was released on [December 6](/wiki/December_6 "December 6"), 2005 (the same day as the "family pack" DVD re-release of the original trilogy) and contained Chapters 21 through 25.[[21]](#cite_note-SWArchive-Star_Wars:_Clone_Wars_Volume_2_Coming_to_DVD-21) Chapters 1 and 8 were also included on a "Bonus Lightsaber Action DVD" that was packaged with Hasbro's Obi-Wan Kenobi, Anakin Skywalker and Darth Vader toy lightsabers.[[22]](#cite_note-22)

In [March](/wiki/March "March"), [2021](/wiki/2021 "2021"), it was announced on [Disney's](/wiki/The_Walt_Disney_Company "The Walt Disney Company") website D23.com that the entire series would be available on [Disney+](/wiki/Disney%2B "Disney+") starting April 2.[[23]](#cite_note-Disney+-23)

## Adaptations and tie-in media[]

A [PhotoComic adaptation](/wiki/Star_Wars:_Clone_Wars_Cine-Manga "Star Wars: Clone Wars Cine-Manga") of the entire series was released on [May 21](/wiki/May_21 "May 21"), 2008.[[24]](#cite_note-SWArchive-Upcoming_Dark_Horse_Titles_for_Spring_2008-24) [Dark Horse](/wiki/Dark_Horse_Comics "Dark Horse Comics") released a [comic book series](/wiki/Star_Wars:_Clone_Wars_Adventures_(graphic_novels) "Star Wars: Clone Wars Adventures (graphic novels)") based on the artwork and atmosphere of the show, in some cases with direct plot tie-ins. Hasbro released three waves of action figures—one for each season. The first two were initially available exclusively at [Target](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Target_Corporation "wikipedia:Target Corporation"), and then later at [StarWarsShop.com](/wiki/StarWars.com "StarWars.com"), whereas the final wave was released at multiple retailers.[[25]](#cite_note-Wave_1_Figures-25)[[26]](#cite_note-Wave_2_Figures-26)[[27]](#cite_note-SWShop-27)[[28]](#cite_note-Wave_3_Figures-28) In July 2021, following the Disney+ release of the show, StarWars.com announced that Hasbro would be producing *Clone Wars* figures for its Black Series and Vintage Collection lines.[[29]](#cite_note-New_Figures-29)

## Reception ‡[]

*Clone Wars* was one of the first online series to win an Emmy Award, due to the fact that it was released on television and the internet simultaneously.[[18]](#cite_note-Clone_Wars_Home_on_Hyperspace-18) Seasons one and two won an Emmy award for "Outstanding Animated Program" in 2004,[[30]](#cite_note-SWArchive-Clone_Wars_Micro-Series_Wins_Emmy_Award-30) and season three won in 2005 for the same category.[[31]](#cite_note-SWArchive-Clone_Wars_Volume_2_Wins_Emmy_Award-31) Background key designer [Justin Thompson](/wiki/Justin_Thompson "Justin Thompson") also won in "Outstanding Individual Achievement in Animation" for his work on season three.[[32]](#cite_note-SWArchive-Clone_Wars_Volume_2_Wins_Juried_Emmy_Award-32) The series was also a nominee and winner, respectively, of the 32nd and 33rd Annie Awards for "Best Animated Television Production."[[33]](#cite_note-33)[[34]](#cite_note-34)
