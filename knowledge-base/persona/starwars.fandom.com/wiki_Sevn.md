**Sevn** was a [human](/wiki/Human "Human") [female](/wiki/Gender "Gender"), former [mercenary](/wiki/Mercenary "Mercenary"), turned [bounty hunter](/wiki/Bounty_hunter "Bounty hunter"), who lived during the [Republic Era](/wiki/Republic_Era "Republic Era"). In her youth, she worked as a mercenary for the [Basham](/wiki/Basham "Basham") but later defected the group and became a bounty hunter. Sevn and her bounty hunter partner, the [droid](/wiki/Droid "Droid") [IV-A4](/wiki/IV-A4 "IV-A4") were [assigned](/wiki/Job_on_Genya "Job on Genya") a [bounty](/wiki/Bounty "Bounty") by [Jin-Sim Canbelon](/wiki/Jin-Sim_Canbelon "Jin-Sim Canbelon") of [Zonus Corporation](/wiki/Zonus_Corporation "Zonus Corporation"). However, she soon rejected the bounty and confronted Canbelon demanding him to free his [slaves](/wiki/Slavery "Slavery"). After a duel with Canbelon, Sevn and IV-A4 freed Canbelon's slaves.

## Biography[]

 [![Sevn piloting her ship](data:image/gif;base64,R0lGODlhAQABAIABAAAAAP///yH5BAEAAAEALAAAAAABAAEAQAICTAEAOw%3D%3D)](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/starwars/images/4/4a/Sevn-TheBountyHunters-Celebration.png/revision/latest?cb=20250426210430) [![Sevn piloting her ship](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/starwars/images/4/4a/Sevn-TheBountyHunters-Celebration.png/revision/latest/scale-to-width-down/250?cb=20250426210430)](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/starwars/images/4/4a/Sevn-TheBountyHunters-Celebration.png/revision/latest?cb=20250426210430) 

Sevn piloting her ship

 

Sevn was a [human](/wiki/Human "Human")[[1]](#cite_note-The_Bounty_Hunters-1) [female](/wiki/Gender "Gender")[[2]](#cite_note-TBH_Filmmaker_Focus-2) from [Mita](/wiki/Mita "Mita") who lived during the [Republic Era](/wiki/Republic_Era "Republic Era"). She was orphaned during a war and later kidnapped by the [mercenary](/wiki/Mercenary "Mercenary") group the [Basham](/wiki/Basham "Basham"). The Basham raised her and gave her the name Sevn. They trained her as a [soldier](/wiki/Soldier "Soldier") and she eventually became one of their best. However, at the age of seventeen, Sevn betrayed and defected the Basham. Sevn's defection prompted the Basham to send agents after her on multiple occasions. Eventually, Sevn was caught in a [burning](/wiki/Fire "Fire") building after Basham agents tracked her down, but she was rescued by [a Jedi](/wiki/Unidentified_Jedi_(Sevn) "Unidentified Jedi (Sevn)"), who [killed](/wiki/Death "Death") the agents with her [green](/wiki/Color "Color") [lightsaber](/wiki/Lightsaber "Lightsaber").[[1]](#cite_note-The_Bounty_Hunters-1)

Some [time](/wiki/Time "Time") later, Sevn formed a partnership with the [droid](/wiki/Droid "Droid") [IV-A4](/wiki/IV-A4 "IV-A4"). She and IV-A4 were [assigned](/wiki/Job_on_Genya "Job on Genya") by [Jin-Sim Canbelon](/wiki/Jin-Sim_Canbelon "Jin-Sim Canbelon"), the [CEO](/wiki/Chief_Executive_Officer "Chief Executive Officer") of [Zonus Corporation](/wiki/Zonus_Corporation "Zonus Corporation"), to seek out a [bounty](/wiki/Bounty "Bounty") on a [Twi'lek](/wiki/Twi%27lek "Twi'lek") on the [planet](/wiki/Planet "Planet") [Genya](/wiki/Genya "Genya"). Sevn and IV-A4 used a [speeder bike](/wiki/Speeder_bike "Speeder bike") to travel to a building, where they met [Eno](/wiki/Eno_(Twi%27lek) "Eno (Twi'lek)") and [several others](/wiki/Unidentified_insurgent_group_(Genya) "Unidentified insurgent group (Genya)"), whose family members were [enslaved](/wiki/Slavery "Slavery") by Canbelon. Sevn originally planned to follow through on the bounty and bring the captured Eno back with her and IV-A4 to Canbelon. However, after the [stunncuffed](/wiki/Stuncuffs "Stuncuffs") Eno dropped a [holoprojector](/wiki/Holoprojector "Holoprojector") of her and her [sister](/wiki/Karin_(Twi%27lek) "Karin (Twi'lek)"), Sevn had a change of heart. IV-A4 requested that they completed their task, but Sevn rejected his request and broke into a rage and opened fire with multiple [blasters](/wiki/Blaster "Blaster") on the building, before departing on his own.[[1]](#cite_note-The_Bounty_Hunters-1)

Sevn traveled back to Zonus Corporation with her captives in stuncuffs. She confronted Canbelon requesting her bounty, while IV-A4 appeared to depart the planet in their [gunship](/wiki/Gunship "Gunship") the *[Sand Bird](/wiki/Sand_Bird "Sand Bird")*. Sevn demanded that Canbelon released all of his slaves, while she brought her captives to Canbelon. Canbelon refused her request, citing that for security reasons, employees were not allowed to interact with outside parties. In response, Sevn unlocked her captives' binders, and they all opened fire on Canbelon's two massive [battle droids](/wiki/Unidentified_battle_droid_model_(Zonus_Corporation) "Unidentified battle droid model (Zonus Corporation)"). Sevn quickly destroyed the droids, which led to a duel between her and Canbelon. During their duel, Canbelon revealed his massive golden [droideka](/wiki/Unidentified_droideka_(Zonus_Corporation) "Unidentified droideka (Zonus Corporation)"), as he tried to subdue Sevn. However, IV-A4 soon returned, and helped Sevn destroy the droideka, while Sevn's mask was also destroyed in the process. The bounty hunter duo captured Canbelon and subsequently freed his slaves. After Canbelon's capture, Sevn and IV-A4 departed from Genya on the *Sand Bird*.[[1]](#cite_note-The_Bounty_Hunters-1)

## Personality and traits[]

Sevn had red and black [hair](/wiki/Hair "Hair") tied back in a ponytail, one blue and one brown [eye](/wiki/Eye "Eye"), as well light skin. She had a partnership with her droid IV-A4,[[1]](#cite_note-The_Bounty_Hunters-1) however, their relationship became strained while they performed a bounty for the disreputable businessman Jin-Sim Canbelon.[[3]](#cite_note-TBH_episode_guide-3) Sevn was caring and she did not like seeing others suffer, as she abandoned the bounty on the Twi'lek Eno, and instead freed Canbelon's slaves.[[1]](#cite_note-The_Bounty_Hunters-1)

## Skills and abilities[]

Sevn was a trained as a soldier and became one of the Basham's best. She was skilled in both combat and subversion.[[1]](#cite_note-The_Bounty_Hunters-1)

## Equipment[]

Sevn was armed with a [blaster pistol](/wiki/Blaster_pistol "Blaster pistol") that she kept in a tan [holster](/wiki/Holster "Holster"). She wore a black and red [crop top](/wiki/Clothing "Clothing"), brown [gloves](/wiki/Glove "Glove"), black pants and black [boots](/wiki/Boot "Boot"). She had tan padding on her right knee down to her ankle. Sevn also wore a tan mask and she had a dark [cloak](/wiki/Cloak "Cloak").[[1]](#cite_note-The_Bounty_Hunters-1)

## Behind the scenes[]

:   "*There are many iconic duos in Star Wars, and I think that the duo of Sevn and IV-A4 will be deeply etched in the minds of fans.*"
:   ―Junichi Yamamoto[[4]](#cite_note-First_Look-4)

Sevn appeared in "[The Bounty Hunters](/wiki/The_Bounty_Hunters "The Bounty Hunters")", a non-[canon](/wiki/Canon "Canon") short film directed by [Junichi Yamamoto](/wiki/Junichi_Yamamoto "Junichi Yamamoto") that was part of the [third volume](/wiki/Star_Wars:_Visions_Volume_3 "Star Wars: Visions Volume 3") of the *[Star Wars: Visions](/wiki/Star_Wars:_Visions "Star Wars: Visions")* series. The animated short, produced by [WIT Studio](/wiki/WIT_Studio "WIT Studio"), was released on [Disney+](/wiki/Disney%2B "Disney+") on [October 29](/wiki/October_29 "October 29"), [2025](/wiki/2025 "2025"). The character was first introduced at the *Star Wars: Visions* panel at [Celebration Japan](/wiki/Celebration_Japan_(2025) "Celebration Japan (2025)") on [April 19](/wiki/April_19 "April 19"), 2025.[[5]](#cite_note-Day_3-5) The spelling of her name was confirmed in a [StarWars.com](/wiki/StarWars.com "StarWars.com") article published on [April 25](/wiki/April_25 "April 25") of that year. The same article used he/him pronouns when referring to Sevn.[[4]](#cite_note-First_Look-4) However, Yamamoto used the Japanese pronoun [彼女](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/%E5%BD%BC%E5%A5%B3 "wiktionary:彼女"), meaning "she" or "her", to refer to Sevn at the panel.[[5]](#cite_note-Day_3-5)

Sevn was voiced by [Ai Fairouz](/wiki/Ai_Fairouz "Ai Fairouz") in the original [Japanese](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Japanese_language "wikipedia:Japanese language") version and by [Anna Sawai](/wiki/Anna_Sawai "Anna Sawai") in the English dub.[[6]](#cite_note-Getty-6) In the Trivia Gallery for "The Bounty Hunters," it was revealed that the filmmakers were inspired by the style and themes of *[Star Wars: The Mandalorian](/wiki/Star_Wars:_The_Mandalorian "Star Wars: The Mandalorian")*. Like [Din Djarin](/wiki/Din_Djarin "Din Djarin"), Sevn was compelled to take a stand and protect the innocent, instead of honoring a bounty.[[7]](#cite_note-TBH_trivia-7)
