## Publisher's summary[]

***Hunter, Wrecker, Tech, and Omega gamble on a mission to help rebuild Pabu in this thrilling adventure for The Bad Batch.***

*A good soldier knows that life is all about change — whether it's on or off the battlefield. Surviving, living, means adaptation. Hunter is well acquainted with this lesson. He's on the run from the Empire, Echo's off on a mission with Captain Rex, and Crosshair is…still Crosshair, but amidst all the change, Hunter still has Tech, Wrecker, and Omega.*

*And it seems that his small family might have finally found a safe place to land, far from the increasingly vigilant eyes of the Empire: Pabu. But their potential new island home is in desperate need of resources if there is any hope for the fledgling community to recover from a devastating sea wave. That's where Phee Genoa, self-proclaimed liberator of treasures, comes in, with a couple of jobs she swears will get them the funds they need. Despite Hunter's concern with Phee’s precarious plans, the rest of the crew is fine following her lead.*

*Things go wrong almost immediately, as Phee's droid blows the crew's cover at a high-stakes auction, and they barely make it out with the relic they'd been paid to acquire. Hunter insists they finish their first mission and deliver the relic before taking on more work, but Phee and the others push forward with a second job: ferrying a couple on the run, one of whom is due to give birth at any moment. Hunter worries what they're risking, especially when their mysterious new passengers cling to lies and secrets that trace back to an Imperial Security Bureau officer hot on their trail.*

*As Hunter tries to get the crew back on a stable, safe path far, far away from anything to do with the Empire's watchdogs, their overlapping missions only invite more danger and chaos. On the verge of failing both their desperate passengers and their community on Pabu, the Batch must remember that the only way they succeed, the only way they survive to fight another day, is by trusting each other.*

## Development[]

 [![According to author Lamar Giles, the battle droids seen throughout Sanctuary are BX-series droid commandos.](data:image/gif;base64,R0lGODlhAQABAIABAAAAAP///yH5BAEAAAEALAAAAAABAAEAQAICTAEAOw%3D%3D)](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/starwars/images/4/44/BX-seriesDroidCommando-TCW.png/revision/latest?cb=20241008053254) [![According to author Lamar Giles, the battle droids seen throughout Sanctuary are BX-series droid commandos.](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/starwars/images/4/44/BX-seriesDroidCommando-TCW.png/revision/latest/scale-to-width-down/250?cb=20241008053254)](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/starwars/images/4/44/BX-seriesDroidCommando-TCW.png/revision/latest?cb=20241008053254) 

According to author Lamar Giles, the battle droids seen throughout *Sanctuary* are BX-series droid commandos.

 

The novel and its content was officially revealed on [February 5](/wiki/February_5 "February 5"), 2025, after its title was already revealed on [December 21](/wiki/December_21 "December 21"), [2024](/wiki/2024 "2024") by a listing on [Penguin Random House](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Penguin_Random_House "wikipedia:Penguin Random House")'s UK website.[[5]](#cite_note-Penguin_UK-5) According to editor [Tom Hoeler](/wiki/Tom_Hoeler "Tom Hoeler"), the writing process for the novel took about sixteen months for author [Lamar Giles](/wiki/Lamar_Giles "Lamar Giles"),[[6]](#cite_note-Tom_Timeline-6) which Hoeler stated was a standard development timeline for a modern *[Star Wars](/wiki/Star_Wars "Star Wars")* novel.[[7]](#cite_note-Tom_Timeline2-7)

Giles confirmed after the book's release that he intended for all the [battle droids](/wiki/Battle_droid "Battle droid") featured throughout to be [BX-series droid commandos](/wiki/BX-series_droid_commando "BX-series droid commando")[[8]](#cite_note-CommandoDroid2-8)[[9]](#cite_note-CommandoDroid3-9)[[10]](#cite_note-CommandoDroid4-10)[[11]](#cite_note-CommandoDroid5-11) because it would make sense for [Cellia Moten](/wiki/Cellia_Moten "Cellia Moten") to own such expensive [droids](/wiki/Droid "Droid").[[12]](#cite_note-CommandoDroid1-12) In Giles' mind, Moten had multiple BX units in her possession to always ensure one was part of her personal entourage, with a new BX being rotated into the entourage if the currently assigned unit was destroyed.[[8]](#cite_note-CommandoDroid2-8) Discussing the battle droids with a fan made Giles consider a short story detailing how the BX units adapted to their new locale[[9]](#cite_note-CommandoDroid3-9) and reconsider how he approached droid details in his *[Star Wars](/wiki/Star_Wars "Star Wars")* works, something he was grateful for.[[11]](#cite_note-CommandoDroid5-11) Indeed, the battle droids in *Sanctuary* are described as [black](/wiki/Color "Color")-plated,[[13]](#cite_note-Sanctuary-13) which matches how the BX-series is visually depicted.[[14]](#cite_note-TCW-14)
