**Hunter**, formerly designated **CT-9901**, was a veteran [Clone Sergeant](/wiki/Clone_Sergeant "Clone Sergeant") who served as the [commanding officer](/wiki/Commanding_officer "Commanding officer") of [Clone Force 99](/wiki/Clone_Force_99 "Clone Force 99") in the [Grand Army of the Republic](/wiki/Grand_Army_of_the_Republic "Grand Army of the Republic") during the [Clone Wars](/wiki/Clone_Wars "Clone Wars"), fought between the [Galactic Republic](/wiki/Galactic_Republic "Galactic Republic") and the [Confederacy of Independent Systems](/wiki/Confederacy_of_Independent_Systems "Confederacy of Independent Systems"). [He](/wiki/Gender "Gender") was genetically altered to have heightened senses and commanded his comrades who had their own unique [mutations](/wiki/Genetically_defective_clone "Genetically defective clone"), in what they called the "Bad Batch." Other members of the [squad](/wiki/Squad "Squad") included [Wrecker](/wiki/Wrecker "Wrecker"), [Tech](/wiki/Tech "Tech"), and [Crosshair](/wiki/Crosshair "Crosshair"), and later, [Echo](/wiki/Echo "Echo").

In [19 BBY](/wiki/19_BBY "19 BBY"), Hunter and Clone Force 99 were recalled from an [insurrection on Yalbec Prime](/wiki/Insurrection_on_Yalbec_Prime "Insurrection on Yalbec Prime") to the planet [Anaxes](/wiki/Anaxes "Anaxes") by [Clone Marshal Commander](/wiki/Clone_Marshal_Commander "Clone Marshal Commander") [Cody](/wiki/Cody "Cody"), who along with [Clone Captain](/wiki/Clone_Captain "Clone Captain") [Rex](/wiki/Rex "Rex") was planning an infiltration of a Separatist [Cyber Center](/wiki/Cyber_Center "Cyber Center") to discover why Republic forces were strategically outmaneuvered in the [battle](/wiki/Battle_of_Anaxes "Battle of Anaxes") there. In the mission, Hunter demonstrated his [squad](/wiki/Squad "Squad")'s aggressive methods, leading full frontal charges and earning the respect of Captain Rex. After successfully infiltrating the Cyber Center, Rex and Tech discovered that their strategies had been compromised by Echo, a long-lost [Advanced Recon Commando](/wiki/Advanced_Recon_Commando "Advanced Recon Commando"). Clone Force 99 joined Rex and [Anakin Skywalker](/wiki/Anakin_Skywalker "Anakin Skywalker")'s [mission](/wiki/Mission_to_Skako_Minor_(Clone_Wars) "Mission to Skako Minor (Clone Wars)") to find Echo on [Skako Minor](/wiki/Skako_Minor "Skako Minor"). After rescuing Echo and facilitating the demise of Separatist [Admiral](/wiki/Admiral "Admiral") [Trench](/wiki/Trench "Trench"), Hunter extended an offer to the [cybernetically](/wiki/Cyborg "Cyborg")-enhanced clone to join his crew, which Echo accepted.

Most of the members' mutations and the ordeal Echo suffered prior to joining caused their [inhibitor chips](/wiki/Behavioral_modification_biochip "Behavioral modification biochip") to fail, meaning Clone Force 99 was able to disobey [Order 66](/wiki/Order_66 "Order 66"). With the [Proclamation of the New Order](/wiki/Proclamation_of_the_New_Order "Proclamation of the New Order") and the rise of the [Galactic Empire](/wiki/Galactic_Empire "Galactic Empire") from the shell of the Republic, Clone Force 99—with the exception of Crosshair, whose chip had functioned properly—defected with a child named [Omega](/wiki/Omega "Omega"), and [Admiral](/wiki/Admiral "Admiral") [Wilhuff Tarkin](/wiki/Wilhuff_Tarkin "Wilhuff Tarkin") ordered that they be hunted down. He would go on to lead Clone Force 99 as a mercenary outfit under the employ of [Trandoshan](/wiki/Trandoshan "Trandoshan") broker [Ciddarin Scaleback](/wiki/Ciddarin_Scaleback "Ciddarin Scaleback"). After settling down later in life on [Pabu](/wiki/Pabu_island "Pabu island"), he would serve as a father figure to Omega.

One of the millions of [clones](/wiki/Cloning "Cloning") of the [human](/wiki/Human "Human") [male](/wiki/Gender "Gender") [bounty hunter](/wiki/Bounty_hunter "Bounty hunter") [Jango Fett](/wiki/Jango_Fett "Jango Fett") grown on the [planet](/wiki/Planet "Planet") [Kamino](/wiki/Kamino "Kamino"),[[7]](#cite_note-Databank_Clone_Troopers-7) Hunter—designated CT-9901[[8]](#cite_note-Shatterpoint-8)—was the leader of the experimental unit [Clone Force 99](/wiki/Clone_Force_99 "Clone Force 99"), also known by its members as "The Bad Batch." The elite clone [squad](/wiki/Squad "Squad") consisted of [clone commandos](/wiki/Clone_commando "Clone commando") with desirable genetic mutations, with Hunter's mutations giving him enhanced senses. He was able to feel electromagnetic frequencies from anywhere on a planet. Hunter served as a [Clone Sergeant](/wiki/Clone_Sergeant "Clone Sergeant"), commanding the sharpshooter [Crosshair](/wiki/Crosshair "Crosshair"), the brute [Wrecker](/wiki/Wrecker "Wrecker"), and the technology genius [Tech](/wiki/Tech "Tech"). The squad flew in an [Omicron-class attack shuttle](/wiki/Omicron-class_attack_shuttle "Omicron-class attack shuttle") called the [*Marauder*](/wiki/Marauder_(Omicron-class) "Marauder (Omicron-class)"). Clone Force 99 was named after the [Maintenance duty clone](/wiki/Maintenance_duty_clone "Maintenance duty clone") [99](/wiki/Clone_99 "Clone 99"), who was [killed](/wiki/Death "Death") in the [Battle of Kamino](/wiki/Battle_of_Kamino "Battle of Kamino").[[2]](#cite_note-The_Bad_Batch-2)

Throughout the [Clone Wars](/wiki/Clone_Wars "Clone Wars") between the [Galactic Republic](/wiki/Galactic_Republic "Galactic Republic") and the [Confederacy of Independent Systems](/wiki/Confederacy_of_Independent_Systems "Confederacy of Independent Systems"), Clone Force 99 had a 100% percent success rate in their missions, but had a reputation for their unorthodox methods and disobedience.[[2]](#cite_note-The_Bad_Batch-2) Hunter lost count of the amount of missions they had completed, feeling that the action blurred together.[[9]](#cite_note-A_Distant_Echo-9) The Force was known throughout the [Grand Army of the Republic](/wiki/Grand_Army_of_the_Republic "Grand Army of the Republic") to [clone troopers](/wiki/Clone_trooper "Clone trooper") such as [Jesse](/wiki/Jesse "Jesse") and [Kix](/wiki/Kix "Kix"), although [Clone Captain](/wiki/Clone_Captain "Clone Captain") [Rex](/wiki/Rex "Rex") had not heard of them. At some point in the war, Hunter met [Clone Commander](/wiki/Clone_Commander "Clone Commander") [Cody](/wiki/Cody "Cody"), and Clone Force 99 was given at least one suicide mission by him,[[2]](#cite_note-The_Bad_Batch-2) but the members could not articulate who their regular superior officer was.[[9]](#cite_note-A_Distant_Echo-9) The members of Clone Force 99 also met and bonded with the [family](/wiki/Lawquane_family "Lawquane family") of the clone deserter [Cut Lawquane](/wiki/Cut_Lawquane "Cut Lawquane") on [Saleucami](/wiki/Saleucami "Saleucami") during the war.[[10]](#cite_note-Cut_and_Run-10)

#### Mirror match[]

Sometime between [21 BBY](/wiki/21_BBY "21 BBY") and [19 BBY](/wiki/19_BBY "19 BBY"),[[12]](#cite_note-Hylanth_Date-12) Hunter and the rest of Clone Force 99 were present during an [attack](/wiki/Attack_on_a_Republic_fleet "Attack on a Republic fleet") on a Republic [fleet](/wiki/Fleet "Fleet"). During the battle, Wrecker stumbled upon a [Wookiee doll](/wiki/Viiveenn%27s_doll "Viiveenn's doll"), getting himself distracted. Unbeknownst to the squad, the [doll](/wiki/Doll "Doll") was owned by the [Wookiee](/wiki/Wookiee "Wookiee") [Viiveenn](/wiki/Viiveenn "Viiveenn"), who just left the chaotic scene with [Jedi Master](/wiki/Jedi_Master "Jedi Master") [Obi-Wan Kenobi](/wiki/Obi-Wan_Kenobi "Obi-Wan Kenobi").

Tech reminded Wrecker that they were still in the middle of a battle. Wrecker and Crosshair then got into an argument about whether or not they should bring the lost doll with them. At the [ramp](/wiki/Entry_ramp "Entry ramp") of the *Marauder*, Hunter interrupted the three clones, telling them to get a move on for their next assignment, a [mission](/wiki/Mission_to_Hylanth "Mission to Hylanth") to the [Outer Rim](/wiki/Outer_Rim_Territories "Outer Rim Territories") [moon](/wiki/Moon "Moon") [Hylanth](/wiki/Hylanth "Hylanth").[[11]](#cite_note-Hyperspace_Stories_10-11)

Clone Force 99 arrives on Hylanth

 

After the *Marauder* landed on the moon, the squad got out of their ship and was surrounded by forests. They were sent out due to [battle droids](/wiki/Battle_droid "Battle droid"), which made Crosshair question the whole mission, stating that even regular clone troopers could handle it. Tech then added that the [droids](/wiki/Droid "Droid") were modified, though who was responsible for the modifications remained unknown, making Crosshair slightly more optimistic about their mission as he walked through the forest. Wrecker said that Crosshair just needed some cheering up and attempted to cheer him up, teasing the [marksman](/wiki/Sniper "Sniper") with the Wookiee doll he had found earlier. The pair argued, though Hunter interrupted the argument, telling them both to be quiet. Hunter noticed a movement, though it was too fast for him to get a lock on.[[11]](#cite_note-Hyperspace_Stories_10-11)

As the squad walked further into the forest, both Hunter and Crosshair felt that something was wrong with their mission. A [hologram](/wiki/Hologram "Hologram") then appeared out of nowhere, greeting the clones before introducing himself, [Doctor](/wiki/Doctor "Doctor") [Krail](/wiki/Krail "Krail"). The doctor hoped that the Republic would send them, to which Hunter asked why Krail hoped for them to come specifically. Krail proceeded to compare the clones to battle droids but was interrupted by Wrecker, saying that they were nothing alike. The doctor agreed, calling the clones special and better than the rest, adding that it was the same as his battle droids. Being very confident, Wrecker challenged Krail to send out his special droids, which the doctor already sent earlier.[[11]](#cite_note-Hyperspace_Stories_10-11)

In 19 [BBY](/wiki/%27ABY-BBY%27_dating_system "'ABY-BBY' dating system"),[[13]](#cite_note-Timelines-13) Clone Force 99 was fighting an [Insurrection on Yalbec Prime](/wiki/Insurrection_on_Yalbec_Prime "Insurrection on Yalbec Prime"), where Wrecker cut off the [Yalbec](/wiki/Yalbec "Yalbec") [queen's](/wiki/Unidentified_Yalbec_queen "Unidentified Yalbec queen") [stinger](/wiki/Yalbec_queen_stinger "Yalbec queen stinger"), causing the males of the species to try to [mate](/wiki/Reproduction "Reproduction") with them. During the insurrection, Commander Cody transmitted a request for Clone Force 99 to arrive on [Anaxes](/wiki/Anaxes "Anaxes"). The Republic was losing the [Battle of Anaxes](/wiki/Battle_of_Anaxes "Battle of Anaxes"), and to turn the tide, Captain Rex and Cody plotted an attack with a small squad on a Separatist [Cyber Center](/wiki/Cyber_Center "Cyber Center"), with Cody selecting The Bad Batch for the mission. The squad nearly crashed the *Marauder* as it flew at high speed into [Fort Anaxes](/wiki/Fort_Anaxes "Fort Anaxes").

Hunter exited the [starship](/wiki/Starship "Starship") first and apologized for their tardiness, explaining their encounter with the Yalbecs as an "unforeseen complication." The sergeant vouched for the flaws and abilities of his brothers before inquiring about the nature of their mission, boarding a [Low Altitude Assault Transport/infantry](/wiki/Low_Altitude_Assault_Transport/infantry "Low Altitude Assault Transport/infantry") gunship and getting briefed en route to their destination.[[2]](#cite_note-The_Bad_Batch-2)

##### Infiltrating Separatist bases[]

:   "*Everyone, find cover. We'll hold this position and let them come to us."  
    "I don't think so, Captain. That's not our style. We prefer going to them. Bad Batch! Plan 82, "Shockwave!"*"
:   ―Rex and Hunter after being shot down[[2]](#cite_note-The_Bad_Batch-2)

The gunship was shot down by [DSD1 dwarf spider droids](/wiki/DSD1_dwarf_spider_droid "DSD1 dwarf spider droid"), crashing in a canyon. Cody was trapped within the wreckage, and when Rex tried to help his friend Hunter stopped him and ordered Wrecker to help. Wrecker used his immense strength to lift the entire gunship and pull Cody out. Afterwards the clones saw an [army](/wiki/Army "Army") of [B1-series battle droid](/wiki/B1-series_battle_droid "B1-series battle droid") advancing towards them, and Rex advocated making a stand there. Hunter disagreed, as Clone Force 99 had their own methods and preferred taking the fight to the enemy. Hunter called out for [Plan 82](/wiki/Plan_82 "Plan 82"), shockwave, where the unit worked as a team, with Wrecker providing cover with a large piece of the gunship, and Tech calculating positions for Hunter to throw [Electro Magnetic Pulse grenades](/wiki/Electromagnetic_pulse_grenade "Electromagnetic pulse grenade") at, which were shot by Crosshair to disable a wide field of droids. As more spider droids advanced Hunter gave the order to fan out, fighting droids with his [DC-17 hand blaster](/wiki/DC-17_repeater_hand_blaster "DC-17 repeater hand blaster") and [vibro-knife](/wiki/Vibroknife "Vibroknife"). With the droids eliminated Rex complemented Hunter on the squad's work and suggested that they move out from their compromised position.[[2]](#cite_note-The_Bad_Batch-2)

At a camp, Jesse inquired as to what Hunter's genetic enhancement was, with Kix joking that it was ability to put up with the other three. Tech explained Hunter's abilities to sense electromagnetic frequencies and stated that unlike maps, Hunter was never wrong. With Cody incapacitated, Rex took command of the mission, escalating tensions between Clone Force 99 and Rex's men to the point of a near brawl. Hunter had to step in to get Wrecker to put down Kix, and chastised the men for their actions and focused on the mission, agreeing to follow Rex's lead and complete the mission his way. When the clones arrived at a Separatist [outpost](/wiki/Anaxes_Separatist_Security_Outpost "Anaxes Separatist Security Outpost") that was easier to attack than evade, Hunter asked for Rex's orders, expecting an infiltration mission, but the captain agreed to lead a frontal assault in Bad Batch style, which excited Hunter. The clones broke into the Center and rode an elevator up to the command tower, eliminating many droids along the way. Hunter complemented Rex, that he was not bad for a "Reg"; a term the Bad Batch used for the rank-and-file clones.[[2]](#cite_note-The_Bad_Batch-2)

From the outpost the clones spotted the Cyber Center, and Tech discovered a [platoon](/wiki/Platoon "Platoon") of droids heading there. Hunter correctly theorized that their destruction at the crash site had been noticed. To infiltrate the outpost the clones Hunter, Tech, and Wrecker stole [speeder bikes](/wiki/Speeder_bike "Speeder bike") to flank the Separatists from the rear while the other clones attacked the entrance. After Wrecker broke down the back door, the three clones entered the base and gunned down the B1 battle droids inside. Inside the base's command center, Hunter attacked two [T-series](/wiki/T-series_military_strategic_analysis_and_tactics_droid "T-series military strategic analysis and tactics droid") [tactical droids](/wiki/Tactical_droid "Tactical droid") with his vibro-knife, leaving Tech to analyze the Separatist computers while he and Wrecker joined the assault at the entrance. On returning to Fort Anaxes, Rex revealed that the Separatists tactical advantage was caused by [CT-1409](/wiki/Echo "Echo"), an [Advanced Recon Commando](/wiki/Advanced_Recon_Commando "Advanced Recon Commando") nicknamed "Echo" who was long believed to be [dead](/wiki/Battle_of_Lola_Sayu "Battle of Lola Sayu").[[2]](#cite_note-The_Bad_Batch-2)

At the base, plans for a [mission](/wiki/Mission_to_Skako_Minor_(Clone_Wars) "Mission to Skako Minor (Clone Wars)") to the source of Echo's signals on the planet [Skako Minor](/wiki/Skako_Minor "Skako Minor") were made. Hunter offered Clone Force 99 to the mission, if only to allow an "I told you so." Skywalker and Rex left so the [Jedi](/wiki/Jedi "Jedi") could contact his secret [wife](/wiki/Marriage "Marriage") [Padmé Amidala](/wiki/Padm%C3%A9_Amidala "Padmé Amidala"), while Hunter returned to wait at the *Marauder* with the Bad Batch. On the way to Skako Minor Rex and Skywalker asked about the Force's nature, with Hunter unsure of the amount of missions they had undertaken or who they regularly reported to.[[9]](#cite_note-A_Distant_Echo-9)

:   ―Anakin Skywalker to Hunter[[14]](#cite_note-On_the_Wings_of_Keeradaks-14)

More D1 battle droids arrived and were fought off by Hunter, Skywalker, Wrecker, and Crosshair. The Jedi ordered Hunter to continue the defense while he checked on Tech and Rex. The three clones were pushed into the room where Echo was held and closed the door, with Hunter and Crosshair using welders to seal it. As more droids surrounded the entrance Crosshair explained the amount of droids entering, with Hunter stating they could hold them off as long as necessary. The sergeant sealed another door leading into the room as the Separatists began piercing the first one with a [decimator](/wiki/S/D_Decimator_droid "S/D Decimator droid") droid. Rex and Tech were able to unplug Echo from the machinery, in which time the lost clone identified a roof exhaust vent through which they could escape. Echo unlocked the vent using his cybernetic attachments, and Hunter exclaimed that the Decimator had nearly breached through the second door. To reach the vent Wrecker threw Hunter upwards first, who complained that a warning would have been appreciated.[[14]](#cite_note-On_the_Wings_of_Keeradaks-14)

Wrecker threw the other members of the squad into the vent and threw [thermal detonators](/wiki/Thermal_detonator "Thermal detonator") to destroy the decimator. As the squad navigated the exhaust pipes Echo explained that he had been given access to [Techno Union](/wiki/Techno_Union "Techno Union") databases, including building schematics. Echo led the group to a large outdoor pipe at a great height, where Hunter tried to assure the acrophobic Wrecker that they were near safety. Tech used a recording of the keeradaks to summon the creatures to their location, where the clones and Skywalker jumped on the keeradaks to escape. Hunter and Tech were the last to jump, riding on the same keeradak. Hunter took the role of steering the creature while Tech fired back at the D1 droids, who pursued them in flight.[[14]](#cite_note-On_the_Wings_of_Keeradaks-14)

As a large force of droids arrived at the village the inhabitants and clones united to fight them off, with Hunter dispatching several with his vibro-knife. After many D1 droids were defeated several [Octuptarra tri-droids](/wiki/Octuptarra_tri-droid "Octuptarra tri-droid") wrought havoc on the villagers. ##### Liberating Anaxes[]

:   "*And the regs think we take risks.*"
:   ―Hunter after learning Echo's plan[[15]](#cite_note-Unfinished_Business-15)

After returning to Fort Anaxes, Echo formulated a plan to restore the planet to Republic control. Jedi General [Mace Windu](/wiki/Mace_Windu "Mace Windu") would lead a force to retake the planet's [assembly complex](/wiki/Anaxes_assembly_complex "Anaxes assembly complex"), while Clone Force 99 along with Skywalker and Rex would escort Echo to Separatist [Admiral](/wiki/Admiral "Admiral") [Trench](/wiki/Trench "Trench")'s flagship, *[Invulnerable](/wiki/Invulnerable "Invulnerable")*, where the clone could give false strategies to the Separatists. At the *Marauder*, Hunter asked Tech if they were ready to begin, which Tech answered in the affirmative. Aboard the ship Hunter inquired about Echo's plan, which was to transmit a code identifying the vessel to the flagship as a droid shuttle. Hunter commented that the plan was risky, even by Bad Batch standards.[[15]](#cite_note-Unfinished_Business-15)

Inside the ship, Echo engaged with the interface in the comm vault to complete the plan. Echo sent a message to Trench to send all droid forces to the assembly complex, which Hunter was suspicious of as that would outnumber the forces led by Windu and Kenobi. True to his loyalties, Echo transmitted a signal to deactivate all of the droids at the assembly complex, turning the various battlefronts on Anaxes in the Republic's favor. Hunter was glad to learn that Echo had not been compromised by the Separatists. As Echo discovered and helped Windu disarm a bomb hidden in the assembly complex, Trench's droids discovered the signal originating within their ship and blocked it, knocking out Echo. Hunter realized that meant Trench knew their location, and the clones began to fight their way out of the vault against a horde of B2 super battle droids and B1 battle droids.[[15]](#cite_note-Unfinished_Business-15)

As the war drew to a close, Hunter and Clone Force 99 were stationed on the planet [Kaller](/wiki/Kaller "Kaller"), where Republic forces led by Jedi General [Depa Billaba](/wiki/Depa_Billaba "Depa Billaba") were [pinned down](/wiki/Battle_of_Kaller "Battle of Kaller") by the droid army. Billaba's [Padawan](/wiki/Padawan "Padawan") [Caleb Dume](/wiki/Kanan_Jarrus "Kanan Jarrus") was sent to find reinforcements and recruited Clone Force 99, who rolled a boulder down a hill onto the oncoming battle droids. Hunter and Clone Force 99 then emerged from the [forest](/wiki/Forest "Forest"), with Hunter using his vibro-knife and blaster. Hunter then ordered Crosshair to deal with the [Armored Assault Tanks](/wiki/Armored_Assault_Tank_Mk_I "Armored Assault Tank Mk I"), which he did by launching a cable between them, which allowed Wrecker to then push them off a nearby cliff. Clone Force 99 dealt with the remaining battle droids and returned to the Republic trenches, where Hunter suggested that Billaba launch a counterattack. Billaba's subordinate commander [CC-10/994](/wiki/Grey_(clone_trooper) "Grey (clone trooper)"), nicknamed "Grey" responded indignantly, reminding Hunter that the general was in command, but Billaba agreed and followed Hunter's advice.[[5]](#cite_note-Aftermath-5)

While Billaba's clones advanced Dume introduced his master to Clone Force 99, where she praised the force's abilities. She then inquired about the reinforcements that were expected, but Hunter explained that they were directed to the capital and that Clone Force 99 was their only provision. Tech then revealed that communications were anticipating the potential end of the war, as Jedi Master Kenobi was [engaging](/wiki/Battle_of_Utapau "Battle of Utapau") Separatist General [Grievous](/wiki/Grievous "Grievous") on [Utapau](/wiki/Utapau "Utapau"), where Republic victory could result in the collapse of the Confederate command structure. The clones knew they still had to continue fighting on Kaller, and Hunter asked Billaba if she had orders or if they could apply their demonstrated expertise. Billaba asked Dume, who enthusiastically asked if he could join Clone Force 99, although Hunter asked him if he was prepared to move quickly along with them.[[5]](#cite_note-Aftermath-5)

The squad as soon summoned to a mass rally where they watched the [Proclamation of the New Order](/wiki/Proclamation_of_the_New_Order "Proclamation of the New Order") delivered by Palpatine, which transformed the Republic into the [Galactic Empire](/wiki/Galactic_Empire "Galactic Empire"). The girl introduced herself as [Omega](/wiki/Omega "Omega"), who was fascinated by the squad's exploits, addressing each member of the squad by name. As Hunter inquired the reason for her presence on Kamino, Nala Se interrupted, introducing the girl as her medical assistant. As the two parted the squad's presence, Hunter remarked that recent events have grown more and more strange.[[5]](#cite_note-Aftermath-5)

Unfortunately, a trio of shock troopers quickly notice Omega's absence, so Hunter claimed ignorance. When the troopers confirmed with the operations center that Omega was still in the detention block, the girl came crashing down from the vent before deactivating the ray shield. After stunning the shock troopers, Hunter stated they needed to find Crosshair, though Tech noted they wouldn't get far without their gear. Hunter was a skilled fighter

 

Hunter was the nickname of a clone commando whose serial number remained a secret, not being told to others.[[21]](#cite_note-Clone_Tales-21) A little gruff, but not unfriendly, Hunter habitually expressed himself in straight words,[[2]](#cite_note-The_Bad_Batch-2) although, at times, he did show a sense of humor.[[14]](#cite_note-On_the_Wings_of_Keeradaks-14) Hunter was described as strong and stoic.[[3]](#cite_note-HunterDB-3) Hunter's appearance, unlike that of other members of Clone Force 99, closely resembled that of regular clone troopers and of his progenitor [Jango Fett](/wiki/Jango_Fett "Jango Fett").[[22]](#cite_note-Cosplay-22) Other clone troopers, such as Jesse, could not identify his distinctions from a regular clone trooper by comparison to the rest of Clone Force 99.[[2]](#cite_note-The_Bad_Batch-2)

The entire left side of Hunter's face was tattooed with half of a skull. He had longish [dark brown](/wiki/Color "Color") [hair](/wiki/Hair "Hair")[[2]](#cite_note-The_Bad_Batch-2) that later started to gray,[[4]](#cite_note-Cavalry-4) and he wore a red bandana at most times with a small white skull symbol.[[2]](#cite_note-The_Bad_Batch-2) He was 1.8 [meters](/wiki/Meter "Meter") (5 [feet](/wiki/Foot "Foot"), 11 inches) tall,[[1]](#cite_note-CWCE_JtB-1) with brown [eyes](/wiki/Eye "Eye") and tan skin.[[2]](#cite_note-The_Bad_Batch-2) During the battle of Anaxes, Hunter played a role as a mediator between the regular clones, such as Jesse and Rex, and the members of Clone Force 99. The sergeant had to deescalate rising tensions that resulted in physical actions, reminding the clones that they were on the same side.[[2]](#cite_note-The_Bad_Batch-2)[[14]](#cite_note-On_the_Wings_of_Keeradaks-14) Indeed, for all of his and his squad's talk about "regs,"[[2]](#cite_note-The_Bad_Batch-2) Hunter did demonstrate a care for his fellow clone troopers. During the Imperial Era, upon learning that [Sohi](/wiki/Sohi "Sohi") was a former member of the Confederacy of Independent Systems who had helped leak clone troop movements, Hunter was enraged to have a "clone killer" on the *Marauder* and approached her in a fury, only being held back by Wrecker, to demand she leave their ship.[[23]](#cite_note-Sanctuary-23)

When Order 66 was initiated, Hunter was reluctant to follow it, and tried to keep the young Padawan Caleb Dume safe from the other clones. He was initially annoyed by the young enhanced clone Omega, warning her to stay away from them since the squad was "nothing but trouble." However, Hunter took a liking to her and insisted on returning to Kamino to retrieve her even though it placed his squad at great risk, saying she was one of them.[[5]](#cite_note-Aftermath-5) Hunter would then develop a strong bond with Omega, becoming very protective and acting as a parental guardian towards her.[[24]](#cite_note-Reunion-24)

Hunter demonstrated a strong sense of morality and knowledge of right and wrong, to the point of disobeying orders he felt were unjust. When Crosshair, one of Clone Force 99's members, turned on his own team and berated them for letting Caleb Dume live, Hunter responded without hesitation that he did what he felt was right. Similarly, he refused to eliminate the "insurgents" that Tarkin sent them to eliminate on Onderon, because they were civilians and refugees, not soldiers.[[5]](#cite_note-Aftermath-5) While he had been willing to overlook the war's politics during his mission to Raxus,[[25]](#cite_note-Common_Ground-25) Hunter was none-too-pleased to be sharing a space aboard the *Marauder* with someone who had hypothetically fought clones upon learning of Sohi's loyalties. Learning of her role in killing his brethren threw him into a rage that he was only able to calm by kicking and throwing [Ponder](/wiki/Ponder "Ponder"), whom he regarded as a traitor to their kind for protecting a "clone killer."[[23]](#cite_note-Sanctuary-23)

## Skills and abilities[]

:   "*Well, maps can be wrong. Hunter never is.*"
:   ―Tech[[2]](#cite_note-The_Bad_Batch-2)

Hunter's mutations granted him enhanced senses that allowed him to perceive electromagnetic frequencies, which meant while maps of enemy-controlled regions may be wrong, he rarely was. His mutation was especially useful during the Clone Wars to track droids' electromagnetic fields. Hunter had specialist training and was a superior [hand-to-hand combatant](/wiki/Unarmed_combat "Unarmed combat").[[1]](#cite_note-CWCE_JtB-1) He was an agile fighter, able to perform flips and kicks while coordinating his blaster-fire and strikes with his weapons. He was also skilled as a leader.[[2]](#cite_note-The_Bad_Batch-2) Hunter was incredibly skilled with a number of weapons, but his favorite was his [vibro-knife](/wiki/Vibroknife "Vibroknife").[[26]](#cite_note-audio_description-26)

 [![Hunter's armor and vibro-knife](data:image/gif;base64,R0lGODlhAQABAIABAAAAAP///yH5BAEAAAEALAAAAAABAAEAQAICTAEAOw%3D%3D)](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/starwars/images/8/81/Hunter_AG.png/revision/latest?cb=20200406133934) [![Hunter's armor and vibro-knife](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/starwars/images/8/81/Hunter_AG.png/revision/latest/scale-to-width-down/140?cb=20200406133934)](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/starwars/images/8/81/Hunter_AG.png/revision/latest?cb=20200406133934) 

Hunter's armor and vibro-knife

 

In combat, Hunter regularly utilized a [DC-17 hand blaster](/wiki/DC-17_repeater_hand_blaster "DC-17 repeater hand blaster"),[[2]](#cite_note-The_Bad_Batch-2) a [DC-17m Interchangeable Weapon System](/wiki/DC-17m_Interchangeable_Weapon_System "DC-17m Interchangeable Weapon System"),[[9]](#cite_note-A_Distant_Echo-9) a vibro-knife,[[26]](#cite_note-audio_description-26) and during a [mission](/wiki/Mission_to_a_droid_decommissioning_facility "Mission to a droid decommissioning facility") in [Corellia](/wiki/Corellia "Corellia") he carried another [DC-17 hand blaster](/wiki/DC-17_repeater_hand_blaster "DC-17 repeater hand blaster"). He also demonstrated familiarity with weapons employed by enemy combatants, in particular, the [E-5 blaster rifle](/wiki/E-5_blaster_rifle "E-5 blaster rifle") and the [DC-15a blaster carbine](/wiki/DC-15A_blaster_carbine "DC-15A blaster carbine"). Although combat-proficient with [blasters](/wiki/Blaster "Blaster"), the sergeant preferred to engage opponents with his knife at close quarters. As an experimental [clone commando](/wiki/Clone_commando "Clone commando"), he wore a set of modified [*Katarn*-class Commando Armor](/wiki/Katarn-class_commando_armor "Katarn-class commando armor").[[2]](#cite_note-The_Bad_Batch-2) Hunter also had a [Hush-98 comlink](/wiki/Hush-98_comlink "Hush-98 comlink") and wore a [green](/wiki/Color "Color") [tunic](/wiki/Clothing "Clothing") to blend himself amongst civilians.[[17]](#cite_note-Cornered-17)

A few months following the [bombardment](/wiki/Attack_on_Kamino "Attack on Kamino") on Kamino, Hunter alongside the rest of the squad had a new paint job for their armors. Hunter had a lot more color variety on his armor with stripes of orange and blue, further disassociating his past life as a soldier.[[27]](#cite_note-Spoils_of_War-27)

## Behind the scenes[]

 [![Concept design of Hunter](data:image/gif;base64,R0lGODlhAQABAIABAAAAAP///yH5BAEAAAEALAAAAAABAAEAQAICTAEAOw%3D%3D)](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/starwars/images/0/0a/Hunter_Bad_Batch.png/revision/latest?cb=20150430003902) [![Concept design of Hunter](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/starwars/images/0/0a/Hunter_Bad_Batch.png/revision/latest/scale-to-width-down/205?cb=20150430003902)](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/starwars/images/0/0a/Hunter_Bad_Batch.png/revision/latest?cb=20150430003902) 

Concept design of Hunter

 

[George Lucas](/wiki/George_Lucas "George Lucas") envisioned the Bad Batch as a version of [*The Dirty Dozen*](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/The_Dirty_Dozen "wikipedia:The Dirty Dozen") with clones. [Dave Filoni](/wiki/Dave_Filoni "Dave Filoni") sketched their appearances, which [Brent Friedman](/wiki/Brent_Friedman "Brent Friedman") and [Matt Michnovetz](/wiki/Matt_Michnovetz "Matt Michnovetz") used to imagine the Bad Batch's voices and personalities. Matt and Brent worked with Dave to flesh out the characters and their story, where Hunter was inspired by the character of [Billy Sole](https://avp.fandom.com/wiki/Billy_Sole "w:c:avp:Billy Sole") from the [1987](/wiki/1987 "1987") film *[Predator](https://avp.fandom.com/wiki/Predator_(film) "w:c:avp:Predator (film)")*.[[28]](#cite_note-Bad_Batch_Panel-28)

[Clone Force 99](/wiki/Clone_Force_99 "Clone Force 99") was supposed to be introduced in a four-episode arc of *[Star Wars: The Clone Wars](/wiki/Star_Wars:_The_Clone_Wars "Star Wars: The Clone Wars")* season seven before it got cancelled. The story reel was initially released during [Celebration Anaheim](/wiki/Celebration_Anaheim "Celebration Anaheim"), and later on [StarWars.com](/wiki/StarWars.com "StarWars.com") for everyone to see. The arc would later be finished for the [final season](/wiki/The_Clone_Wars:_Season_Seven "The Clone Wars: Season Seven") of *Star Wars: The Clone Wars*.

 [![Hunter, preparing to engage the beasts with Echo in the unfinished episodes on Kashyyyk](data:image/gif;base64,R0lGODlhAQABAIABAAAAAP///yH5BAEAAAEALAAAAAABAAEAQAICTAEAOw%3D%3D)](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/starwars/images/7/76/Hunter_on_kashyyyk.png/revision/latest?cb=20170608192647) [![Hunter, preparing to engage the beasts with Echo in the unfinished episodes on Kashyyyk](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/starwars/images/7/76/Hunter_on_kashyyyk.png/revision/latest/scale-to-width-down/250?cb=20170608192647)](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/starwars/images/7/76/Hunter_on_kashyyyk.png/revision/latest?cb=20170608192647) 

Hunter, preparing to engage the beasts with Echo in the unfinished episodes on Kashyyyk

 

Hunter was to appear in a four-episode arc written by Michnovetz[[29]](#cite_note-BFriedman_Yoda_Kashyyyk_tweet-29) which starred the Bad Batch which now included "Echo", alongside [Yoda](/wiki/Yoda "Yoda") on [Kashyyyk](/wiki/Kashyyyk "Kashyyyk"). At the "Untold Clone Wars" panel at [Celebration Anaheim](/wiki/Celebration_Anaheim_(2015) "Celebration Anaheim (2015)"), Dave Filoni talked about the plot of the arc. He revealed that the characters would ride on "giant ape lizards" that were holy to the [Wookiees](/wiki/Wookiee "Wookiee"). In one scene [Tarfful](/wiki/Tarfful "Tarfful") would ask the tree spirits for permission to go into battle against the enemy, which was a [Trandoshan](/wiki/Trandoshan "Trandoshan") and the Separatist Alliance. To get the enemy out of some of their cities they would have to burn some of the trees. The clones had no problem with it, but it was upsetting to the Wookiees. A part of the arc would be about the clones and the Wookiees finding a common ground to fight the Separatists. A story-reel clip was shown at the panel, which showed the Bad Batch and other Wookiees riding on the "ape lizards" called [mylayas](/wiki/Mylaya "Mylaya") and battling a new creature called [netcasters](/wiki/Kinrath "Kinrath")[[30]](#cite_note-Tribe-30) that was originally based on [kinraths](/wiki/Kinrath/Legends "Kinrath/Legends") from the *[Star Wars: Knights of the Old Republic](/wiki/Star_Wars:_Knights_of_the_Old_Republic "Star Wars: Knights of the Old Republic")* game.[[31]](#cite_note-Untold_Clone_Wars_Panel-31)[[32]](#cite_note-Trivia_Gallery-32)

A few elements from the unfinished arc would later be reworked and appeared in "[Tribe](/wiki/Tribe "Tribe")", the sixth episode of *[Star Wars: The Bad Batch](/wiki/Star_Wars:_The_Bad_Batch "Star Wars: The Bad Batch")*'s [second season](/wiki/The_Bad_Batch_Season_2 "The Bad Batch Season 2"). One example of the rework was the complete behavioral change for the netcasters. The creature seemed intelligent and neutral, unless provoked.[[30]](#cite_note-Tribe-30)

In The Light Side comic strip of [*Star Wars Insider* 222](/wiki/Star_Wars_Insider_222 "Star Wars Insider 222"), a human attended the [Lothal County Fair](/wiki/Lothal_County_Fair "Lothal County Fair") and got their face painted like Hunter's by [Sabine Wren](/wiki/Sabine_Wren "Sabine Wren"). The individual was on their phone as [C1-10P](/wiki/C1-10P "C1-10P"), painted to look like [Darth Maul](/wiki/Maul "Maul"), chased someone dressed as Obi-Wan Kenobi.[[33]](#cite_note-222_Launchpad-33)
