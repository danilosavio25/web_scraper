**Reproduction** was a biological process in which living beings produced more living beings. Among some [species](/wiki/Species "Species"), beings reproduced sexually, with the biological [parents](/wiki/Parent "Parent") passing on their [genetic](/wiki/Gene "Gene") material via fertilization, with one parent giving birth following a period of pregnancy in the womb or hatching eggs externally, though individuals engaged in sexual activities for pleasure too. New life could also be produced asexually through [cloning](/wiki/Cloning "Cloning") and [magick](/wiki/Magick "Magick"). The [woman](/wiki/Gender "Gender") [Shmi Skywalker](/wiki/Shmi_Skywalker_Lars "Shmi Skywalker Lars"), carried and gave birth to the boy [Anakin Skywalker](/wiki/Anakin_Skywalker "Anakin Skywalker") without a partner, a phenomenon that was atypical of their species as [humans](/wiki/Human "Human"), and Anakin was born with extraordinary [potential](/wiki/Midi-chlorian "Midi-chlorian") in connecting with [the Force](/wiki/The_Force "The Force"), a mystical energy field of [the galaxy](/wiki/The_galaxy "The galaxy").

## Reproductive practices

### Conception and delivery

:   "*My sister just had a baby, her first. That's the only reason I hesitated before accepting your invitation. It's put me in a mind to think about the future in a different sort of way than I usually do.*"  
    "*My mother told me that she had a similar shift in her thinking after I was born. And I imagine that I will, too, however my child comes to me.*"  
    "*Sola has no interest in a partner. It's normal enough on Naboo. I think I would like what my parents have, though. Or some version of it.*"
:   ―Senator Padmé Amidala and Queen Breha Organa, discussing reproductive options[[2]](#cite_note-Queen's_Shadow-2)

Among some [species](/wiki/Species "Species"), such as [humans](/wiki/Human "Human"), reproduction often involved a pair of beings conceiving a [child](/wiki/Youngling "Youngling") together as biological [parents](/wiki/Parent "Parent"),[[3]](#cite_note-Leia,_Princess_of_Alderaan-3) with one person fulfilling the role of mother,[[4]](#cite_note-Destiny-4)[[5]](#cite_note-Episode_I-5) who carried the child and gave birth;[[5]](#cite_note-Episode_I-5) and the other that of father.[[4]](#cite_note-Destiny-4)[[5]](#cite_note-Episode_I-5) For instance, [Lyra](/wiki/Lyra_Erso "Lyra Erso") and [Galen Erso](/wiki/Galen_Walton_Erso "Galen Walton Erso") were lovers and [spouses](/wiki/Marriage "Marriage") who conceived a child, [Jyn](/wiki/Jyn_Erso "Jyn Erso"), and Lyra delivered Jyn on the [planet](/wiki/Planet "Planet") [Vallt](/wiki/Vallt "Vallt").[[6]](#cite_note-Catalyst-6) [Senator](/wiki/Senator "Senator") [Padmé Amidala](/wiki/Padm%C3%A9_Amidala "Padmé Amidala") of the planet [Naboo](/wiki/Naboo "Naboo") became pregnant during the [Clone Wars](/wiki/Clone_Wars "Clone Wars") with her [husband](/wiki/Gender "Gender"), [Jedi Knight](/wiki/Jedi_Knight "Jedi Knight") [Anakin Skywalker](/wiki/Anakin_Skywalker "Anakin Skywalker"), as the father. She gave birth to [twins](/wiki/Twin "Twin"), [Luke Skywalker](/wiki/Luke_Skywalker "Luke Skywalker") and [Leia Organa](/wiki/Leia_Skywalker_Organa_Solo "Leia Skywalker Organa Solo").[[7]](#cite_note-Episode_III-7) Organa later became pregnant with her own husband, [Han Solo](/wiki/Han_Solo "Han Solo"): Early on in their [marriage](/wiki/Marriage "Marriage"), the two went on a [sublight](/wiki/Sublight_engine "Sublight engine") run together, which began with a great amount of bickering, but eventually lead to "far more enjoyable pursuits." Given the timing, Organa felt fairly sure that their son [Ben](/wiki/Ben_Solo "Ben Solo") had been conceived during the journey.[[8]](#cite_note-Bloodline-8) During her pregnancy, Organa worked with a [doctor](/wiki/Harter_Kalonia "Harter Kalonia") to ensure her health, with the doctor recommending that Organa incorporate [jogan fruit](/wiki/Jogan_fruit "Jogan fruit") into her [diet](/wiki/Diet "Diet").[[9]](#cite_note-Empire's_End-9)

Beings could also choose to have a child without a sexual partner, such as Amidala's sister, [Sola Naberrie](/wiki/Sola_Naberrie "Sola Naberrie"), who delivered a daughter, [Ryoo Naberrie](/wiki/Ryoo_Naberrie "Ryoo Naberrie").[[2]](#cite_note-Queen's_Shadow-2)

Some [humanoid](/wiki/Humanoid "Humanoid") species were capable of conceiving [hybrid](/wiki/Hybrid "Hybrid") offspring with members of other species. [Shaeeah Lawquane](/wiki/Shaeeah_Lawquane "Shaeeah Lawquane"), [Jek Lawquane](/wiki/Jek_Lawquane "Jek Lawquane"),[[10]](#cite_note-The_Deserter-10) and [Jacen Syndulla](/wiki/Jacen_Syndulla "Jacen Syndulla") all had human fathers and [Twi'lek](/wiki/Twi%27lek "Twi'lek") mothers.[[11]](#cite_note-Farewell-11) Humans could also reproduce with [Theelins](/wiki/Theelin "Theelin"),[[12]](#cite_note-SWCE_UE-12) [Mirialans](/wiki/Mirialan "Mirialan"),[[13]](#cite_note-Force_Collector-13) and [Espirions](/wiki/Espirion_(species) "Espirion (species)"); the latter resulted in a hybrid species on Alderaan, [Alder-Espirions](/wiki/Alder-Espirion "Alder-Espirion").[[14]](#cite_note-Leia_4-14) Pairings of the [Quarrens](/wiki/Quarren "Quarren") and [Mon Calamari](/wiki/Mon_Calamari "Mon Calamari") species could also produce hybrids.[[15]](#cite_note-A_Recipe_for_Death-15)

Conception did not guarantee that the pregnancy would result in a birth. On the desolate planet [Parnassos](/wiki/Parnassos "Parnassos"), atmospheric impurities or poor [nutrition](/wiki/Diet "Diet") due to scarce resources among the warrior clan [Scyre](/wiki/Scyre "Scyre") led to decreases in conceptions, and most pregnancies never made it to term. Resource limitations also led to childbearing restrictions in the [city](/wiki/City "City") [Arratu Station](/wiki/Arratu_Station "Arratu Station").[[16]](#cite_note-Phasma-16)

Pregnant humans could feel their unborn child kick in the womb during pregnancy.[[17]](#cite_note-Starros_1-17)

[Sentient](/wiki/Sentience "Sentience") beings called midwives[[6]](#cite_note-Catalyst-6) or [midwife droids](/wiki/Midwife_droid "Midwife droid") could assist with births, which might take place in a maternity ward[[18]](#cite_note-Darth_Vader_2020_5-18) or birthing chamber.[[19]](#cite_note-Aftermath:_Empire's_End-19) By the time of the Clone Wars, a rumor on Vallt held that the human women of [Coruscant](/wiki/Coruscant "Coruscant") no longer carried or delivered their own progeny and hired others to do it for them. However, during his imprisonment and subsequent questioning as to whether he was the father of Lyra's child, Coruscant resident Galen Erso disputed this.[[6]](#cite_note-Catalyst-6)

[Death](/wiki/Death "Death") could occur during childbirth. [Queen](/wiki/Queen "Queen") [Breha](/wiki/Breha_Organa "Breha Organa") and Senator [Bail Organa](/wiki/Bail_Prestor_Organa "Bail Prestor Organa") of the planet [Alderaan](/wiki/Alderaan "Alderaan") adopted Leia Organa shortly after her birth,[[7]](#cite_note-Episode_III-7) in part because Breha could not safely carry a pregnancy due to the loss of her [heart](/wiki/Heart "Heart") and [lungs](/wiki/Lung "Lung"), which were replaced with [pulmonodes](/wiki/Pulmonodes "Pulmonodes"). Bail and Breha told Leia that both of her biological parents had died, her birth father in one of the last battles of the Clone Wars and her badly-injured birth mother shortly after her birth;[[3]](#cite_note-Leia,_Princess_of_Alderaan-3) however, only the latter was true. The other twin, Luke, was adopted by the couple [Owen Lars](/wiki/Owen_Lars "Owen Lars") and [Beru Whitesun Lars](/wiki/Beru_Whitesun_Lars "Beru Whitesun Lars").[[7]](#cite_note-Episode_III-7)

The [Mon Calamari](/wiki/Mon_Calamari "Mon Calamari") spawned their offspring, producing many more in their lifetimes than humans.[[20]](#cite_note-Rogue_One_novel-20) The [Orishen](/wiki/Orishen "Orishen") species reproduced through two parents; each parent birthed one of their two children, and then the parents died, ensuring that the number of Orishen never grew.[[21]](#cite_note-Aftermath:_Life_Debt-21) Some species, such as the [Neimoidians](/wiki/Neimoidian "Neimoidian"), were born as grubs and forced to compete over a limited [food](/wiki/Foodstuff "Foodstuff") supply.[[22]](#cite_note-AbEverything-22) Others hatched from [eggs](/wiki/Egg "Egg"), such as the [Geonosians](/wiki/Geonosian "Geonosian"), who all came from a single hive [queen](/wiki/Queen "Queen").[[23]](#cite_note-Databank:_Queen_Karina_the_Great-23) Members of the [Vintian](/wiki/Vintian "Vintian") species went into mating smelts several [years](/wiki/Standard_year "Standard year") apart.[[24]](#cite_note-Into_the_Dark-24)

### Other ways of creating life

#### The Force

The human [twin](/wiki/Twin "Twin") children [Mae-ho](/wiki/Mae-ho_Aniseya "Mae-ho Aniseya") and [Verosha Aniseya](/wiki/Verosha_Aniseya "Verosha Aniseya") had no father; they were created by [Aniseya](/wiki/Aniseya "Aniseya"), the leader of a [coven](/wiki/Brendok_coven "Brendok coven") of [witches](/wiki/Witch "Witch"),[[4]](#cite_note-Destiny-4) through a powerful [Force vergence](/wiki/Vergence "Vergence") on the [planet](/wiki/Planet "Planet") [Brendok](/wiki/Brendok "Brendok").[[34]](#cite_note-The_Acolyte-34) The two were then carried until their birth by Aniseya's partner [Koril](/wiki/Koril "Koril"). Aniseya and Koril raised the sisters together as their mothers.[[4]](#cite_note-Destiny-4)

[Shmi Skywalker](/wiki/Shmi_Skywalker_Lars "Shmi Skywalker Lars"), a human female, carried and gave birth to Anakin Skywalker. However, he did not have a biological father, and Shmi did not know how she came to have a child by herself. [Jedi Master](/wiki/Jedi_Master "Jedi Master") [Qui-Gon Jinn](/wiki/Qui-Gon_Jinn "Qui-Gon Jinn") suspected that Anakin may have somehow been conceived within Shmi by the [midi-chlorians](/wiki/Midi-chlorian "Midi-chlorian") that connected all life to [the Force](/wiki/The_Force "The Force").[[5]](#cite_note-Episode_I-5) Anakin was later told by [Darth Sidious](/wiki/Darth_Sidious "Darth Sidious") that a [Dark Lord of the Sith](/wiki/Dark_Lord_of_the_Sith "Dark Lord of the Sith"), [Darth Plagueis](/wiki/Darth_Plagueis "Darth Plagueis"), could use the [dark side](/wiki/Dark_side_of_the_Force "Dark side of the Force") to influence midi-chlorians to create life or prevent death.[[7]](#cite_note-Episode_III-7)

[Cloning](/wiki/Cloning "Cloning") was a process in which the [DNA](/wiki/DNA "DNA") of one lifeform was copied to produce genetically identical individuals. Prior to the [Invasion of Naboo](/wiki/Invasion_of_Naboo "Invasion of Naboo"), the [Kaminoans](/wiki/Kaminoan "Kaminoan") cloned the human [bounty hunter](/wiki/Bounty_hunter "Bounty hunter") [Jango Fett](/wiki/Jango_Fett "Jango Fett"), resulting in the eventual creation of the [Grand Army](/wiki/Grand_Army_of_the_Republic "Grand Army of the Republic") of the [Republic](/wiki/Galactic_Republic "Galactic Republic").[[35]](#cite_note-Episode_II-35) The clones were grown in hatcheries on the planet [Kamino](/wiki/Kamino "Kamino"), and were genetically altered to accelerate their growth rate[[36]](#cite_note-AotC_story_gallery-36) and to be more compliant to orders.[[35]](#cite_note-Episode_II-35) To further ensure their compliance with [Order 66](/wiki/Order_66 "Order 66"), their [brains](/wiki/Brain "Brain") contained a [behavioral modification biochip](/wiki/Behavioral_modification_biochip "Behavioral modification biochip").[[37]](#cite_note-Orders-37)

#### Magick

On [Dathomir](/wiki/Dathomir "Dathomir"), the [Dathomirian](/wiki/Dathomirian "Dathomirian") [witch](/wiki/Witch "Witch") [Falta](/wiki/Falta "Falta") used [magick](/wiki/Magick "Magick") to create for herself a daughter, [Yenna](/wiki/Yenna "Yenna"), within a pod that served as the opposite of a [burial pod](/wiki/Burial_pod "Burial pod"). Falta refused to tell [Mother](/wiki/Mother_(title) "Mother (title)") [Talzin](/wiki/Talzin "Talzin"), leader of the [Nightsisters](/wiki/Nightsisters "Nightsisters"), the secrets of her daughter's creation.[[38]](#cite_note-Bug-38)

Sentient beings could be interested in having sexual or romantic relationships with only a gender besides their own, with multiple genders,[[3]](#cite_note-Leia,_Princess_of_Alderaan-3) or with only their own gender;[[40]](#cite_note-Aftermath-40) they could also have no interest in sex or romance.[[41]](#cite_note-Galaxy's_Edge:_Black_Spire-41) Interests related to other sentient species also varied, such as some humanoids finding only other humanoids appealing.[[3]](#cite_note-Leia,_Princess_of_Alderaan-3) Some individuals, such as the [pilot](/wiki/Pilot "Pilot") [Leox Gyasi](/wiki/Leox_Gyasi "Leox Gyasi"), did not experience sexual desires, but chose to perform sexual activities at times in spite of their lack of drive to do so.[[24]](#cite_note-Into_the_Dark-24)

Both because of the commitment it took to be a Jedi and the [Jedi Order](/wiki/Jedi_Order "Jedi Order") requiring its members to avoid attachments, its members were not allowed to marry,[[35]](#cite_note-Episode_II-35) and celibacy was a common practice.[[24]](#cite_note-Into_the_Dark-24) However, interpretations of the [Jedi Code](/wiki/Jedi_Code "Jedi Code") varied on the matter of casual sex without attachments.[[42]](#cite_note-Master_&_Apprentice-42) During the [High Republic Era](/wiki/High_Republic_Era "High Republic Era"), relationships involving Jedi were less rare, especially amongst Padawans. This included relationships between Jedi, such as the relationship between [Avar Kriss](/wiki/Avar_Kriss "Avar Kriss") and [Elzar Mann](/wiki/Elzar_Mann "Elzar Mann"). While commonly limited to Padawans, some Jedi would continue having relationships as they grew older, this was notably seen with Elzar Mann's relationship with the coordinator of the [Republic Fair](/wiki/Republic_Fair "Republic Fair"), [Samera Ra-oon](/wiki/Samera_Ra-oon "Samera Ra-oon").[[43]](#cite_note-The_High_Republic:_The_Rising_Storm-43) During the [time](/wiki/Time "Time") before the [Clone Wars](/wiki/Clone_Wars "Clone Wars"), the Jedi [Rael Averross](/wiki/Rael_Averross "Rael Averross") was rather flexible in his views concerning the Jedi Code, and was known to have casual sex with women he knew, but without forming attachments. On [Pijal](/wiki/Pijal "Pijal"), he had a casual sexual relationship with [Selbie](/wiki/Selbie "Selbie"), the local innkeeper.[[42]](#cite_note-Master_&_Apprentice-42)

Imperial [loyalty officers](/wiki/Loyalty_officer "Loyalty officer") such as [Sinjir Rath Velus](/wiki/Sinjir_Rath_Velus "Sinjir Rath Velus") would learn the identities of their subjects' sexual companions in order to more effectively conduct interrogations.[[21]](#cite_note-Aftermath:_Life_Debt-21) The euphemistic phrase "off-base, recreation" was logged as a location when [Imperial officers](/wiki/Imperial_officer "Imperial officer") were in actuality seeking sexual companions in [cantinas](/wiki/Cantina "Cantina") or visiting extramarital lovers. Popular locations for these activities included the [Octagon](/wiki/Octagon_district "Octagon district") in Ryloth's capital city, [Lessu](/wiki/Lessu_City "Lessu City"), and the [underworld](/wiki/Coruscant_Underworld "Coruscant Underworld")[[45]](#cite_note-Lords_of_the_Sith-45) at the city-covered world [Coruscant](/wiki/Coruscant "Coruscant").[[46]](#cite_note-Coruscant_DB-46)

Individuals who engaged in sexual activity for payment were called [prostitutes](/wiki/Prostitute "Prostitute").[[44]](#cite_note-Lost_Stars-44) Buildings where prostitution took place were called [brothels](/wiki/Brothel "Brothel").[[47]](#cite_note-Andor_1-47)

The act of forcing an individual to have sex against their will was termed rape: The Imperial [Lieutenant](/wiki/Lieutenant "Lieutenant") [Krole](/wiki/Krole "Krole") attempted to sexually assault [Bix Caleen](/wiki/Bix_Caleen "Bix Caleen") while doing overseeing audits on the [planet](/wiki/Planet "Planet") [Mina-Rau](/wiki/Mina-Rau "Mina-Rau"). In the process of trying to force himself on Caleen, Krole was [killed](/wiki/Death "Death") by her with a [wrench](/wiki/Wrench "Wrench").[[48]](#cite_note-Harvest-48)

## Genetics

:   "*How are you here?*"  
    "*Same as you. My father spent several intimate minutes with my mother, and drowned neither me nor my sister when we were born.*"
:   ―[Wyl Lark](/wiki/Wyl_Lark "Wyl Lark") and [Palal Seedia](/wiki/Palal_Seedia "Palal Seedia")[[49]](#cite_note-Victory's_Price-49)

It was possible for a [child](/wiki/Youngling "Youngling") to inherit their biological [parents](/wiki/Parent "Parent")' physical features. As a teenager, [Leia Organa](/wiki/Leia_Skywalker_Organa_Solo "Leia Skywalker Organa Solo") looked striking similar to her biological mother, [Padmé Amidala](/wiki/Padm%C3%A9_Amidala "Padmé Amidala"), and when she wore the [jubilation dress](/wiki/Jubilation_dress "Jubilation dress") that Amidala had once worn it greatly shocked [Quarsh Panaka](/wiki/Quarsh_Panaka "Quarsh Panaka"), an old friend and ally of Amidala's. Despite always knowing that she was adopted, and therefore knew any shared traits were just a coincidence, as a little girl Leia used to look in the [mirror](/wiki/Mirror "Mirror") to see if she shared any traits with her adoptive parents such as Breha Organa's wisdom and beauty or the kindness in Bail Organa's [eyes](/wiki/Eye "Eye").[[3]](#cite_note-Leia,_Princess_of_Alderaan-3) Organa's own son [Ben Solo](/wiki/Ben_Solo "Ben Solo") had his [father's](/wiki/Han_Solo "Han Solo") pronounced [nose](/wiki/Nose "Nose") and lopsided smile, and his mother's piercing [brown](/wiki/Color "Color") eyes. Organa, in turn, was sure that this feature was inherited from her own birth mother.[[50]](#cite_note-Family_at_War-50)

Other characteristics could also be passed on from a parent to their offspring: For example, the [human](/wiki/Human "Human") [Force-sensitive](/wiki/Force-sensitive "Force-sensitive") Leia Organa was described as wise, discerning and kindhearted, like her birth mother; and passionate, fearless and forthright, like her biological father [Anakin Skywalker](/wiki/Anakin_Skywalker "Anakin Skywalker").[[1]](#cite_note-Kenobi_Part_VI-1) [Beru Lars](/wiki/Beru_Whitesun_Lars "Beru Whitesun Lars") after dinner said to [Owen](/wiki/Owen_Lars "Owen Lars"), "Luke's just not a [farmer](/wiki/Farmer "Farmer"), Owen. He has too much of his father in him." To which Owen replied, "That's what I'm afraid of."[[51]](#cite_note-Episode_IV-51) Ben Solo's inherent [Force](/wiki/The_Force "The Force") ability came from his mother, whereas he gained his amazing reflexes and piloting skills from his father.[[52]](#cite_note-TROS_VD-52) He also hailed from a bloodline of expert [pilots](/wiki/Pilot "Pilot") that included not only his father, but also his maternal grandfather.[[53]](#cite_note-TLJ_Cross_Sections-53) [Snoke](/wiki/Snoke "Snoke") also claimed that Solo had 'too much' of his 'father's [heart](/wiki/Heart "Heart") in him which was why he was unable to fully commit to the dark side of the Force.[[54]](#cite_note-Episode_VIII-54)

The [saying](/wiki/List_of_phrases_and_slang "List of phrases and slang") "the [jogan](/wiki/Jogan_fruit "Jogan fruit") doesn't roll far from the vine" referred to a child taking after their parents.[[55]](#cite_note-Scum_and_Villainy-55)

## Behind the scenes

Although the [2002](/wiki/2002 "2002") film [*Star Wars*: Episode II *Attack of the Clones*](/wiki/Star_Wars:_Episode_II_Attack_of_the_Clones "Star Wars: Episode II Attack of the Clones") established that the [Jedi Order](/wiki/Jedi_Order "Jedi Order") members couldn't marry or have children,[[35]](#cite_note-Episode_II-35) *[Star Wars](/wiki/Star_Wars "Star Wars")* creator [George Lucas](/wiki/George_Lucas "George Lucas") explained that despite their monastic culture, the Jedi were permitted to have sexual intercourse as long they didn't form attachments or possessive relationships.[[56]](#cite_note-Jedi_Sex_Life-56)

The *Star Wars* films have consistently refrained from depicting any sexual content on-screen, whether implied or explicit. The live-action TV series *[Star Wars: Andor](/wiki/Star_Wars:_Andor "Star Wars: Andor")* is notable for pushing creative boundaries by depicting scenes that hint at impending sexual activity or that it has just occurred,[[57]](#cite_note-Hollywood_Reporter-57) in the [Season 1](/wiki/Andor_Season_1 "Andor Season 1") episodes "[That Would Be Me](/wiki/That_Would_Be_Me "That Would Be Me")"[[58]](#cite_note-That_Would_Be_Me-58) and "[Announcement](/wiki/Announcement "Announcement"),"[[59]](#cite_note-Announcement-59) and the [Season 2](/wiki/Andor_Season_2 "Andor Season 2") episode "[I Have Friends Everywhere](/wiki/I_Have_Friends_Everywhere "I Have Friends Everywhere")."[[60]](#cite_note-Friends_Everywhere-60) The Season 2 episode "[Harvest](/wiki/Harvest "Harvest")" further pushes the envelope by having an [Imperial officer](/wiki/Krole "Krole") attempt to assault [Bix Caleen](/wiki/Bix_Caleen "Bix Caleen"), a fugitive seeking refuge in a [farming community](/wiki/Mina-Rau "Mina-Rau"), as [Imperial](/wiki/Galactic_Empire "Galactic Empire") forces conduct a sweep for undocumented laborers; with Caleen explicitly referring to the encounter as attempted rape.[[48]](#cite_note-Harvest-48) While this shocked many fans, *Andor* creator [Tony Gilroy](/wiki/Tony_Gilroy "Tony Gilroy") believed that incorporating these scenes was essential, as he felt that avoiding the topic of sexual assault in a war narrative would lack authenticity.[[57]](#cite_note-Hollywood_Reporter-57)
