**Beilert Valance**, also known as **Valance the Hunter**, was a [human](/wiki/Human "Human") [male](/wiki/Gender "Gender") [bounty hunter](/wiki/Bounty_hunter "Bounty hunter"). Born into [slavery](/wiki/Slavery "Slavery") on [Chorin](/wiki/Chorin "Chorin"), Valance became a [miner](/wiki/Miner "Miner") like [his father](/wiki/Beilert_Valance%27s_father "Beilert Valance's father"). He grew up idealizing the [Galactic Empire](/wiki/Galactic_Empire "Galactic Empire"), which he later left his [homeworld](/wiki/Homeworld "Homeworld") to join, under designation **Cadet 404-913**.

Valance served alongside [Han Solo](/wiki/Han_Solo "Han Solo") as a [cadet](/wiki/Cadet "Cadet") at [Carida Academy](/wiki/Carida_Academy "Carida Academy"), the Imperial [Naval](/wiki/Imperial_Navy "Imperial Navy") [Academy](/wiki/Imperial_Academy "Imperial Academy") on the [planet](/wiki/Planet "Planet") [Carida](/wiki/Carida "Carida"). He was involved in at least two altercations with Solo, and both ended up crashing during an exercise due to their conflict. While covering [bombers](/wiki/Bomber "Bomber") during a [battle](/wiki/Mission_on_Qhulosk "Mission on Qhulosk") on [Qhulosk](/wiki/Qhulosk "Qhulosk"), Valance was shot down, leaving him with several permanent injuries. After being rescued by his [fellow cadets](/wiki/Carida_Squadron "Carida Squadron"), Valance enlisted for the infantry and was deployed to [Mimban](/wiki/Mimban "Mimban"), where he was severely injured in the ensuing [battle](/wiki/Mimban_Campaign "Mimban Campaign"). The Empire repaired Valance's body with extensive [cybernetics](/wiki/Cybernetics "Cybernetics"), but he was discharged from service. Valance became a [bounty](/wiki/Bounty "Bounty") [hunter](/wiki/Hunting "Hunting") after failing to protect his homeworld from [raiders](/wiki/Pirate "Pirate"). He was mentored by [Nakano Lash](/wiki/Nakano_Lash "Nakano Lash"), who betrayed him during a [mission](/wiki/Mission_to_Corellia_(Nakano_Lash%27s_crew) "Mission to Corellia (Nakano Lash's crew)") on [Corellia](/wiki/Corellia "Corellia"). Valance later resorted to breaking the [Bounty Hunter Code](/wiki/Bounty_Hunter_Code "Bounty Hunter Code") by stealing [bounties](/wiki/Bounty "Bounty") from other hunters.

The [Hidden Hand](/wiki/Hidden_Hand "Hidden Hand") crime syndicate eventually tracked down and hired Valance to [hunt](/wiki/Hunt_for_Vader "Hunt for Vader") down [Darth Vader](/wiki/Anakin_Skywalker "Anakin Skywalker"). Leading the [team](/wiki/Beilert_Valance%27s_crew "Beilert Valance's crew") of bounty hunters, which included [Dengar](/wiki/Dengar "Dengar"), [Chio Fain](/wiki/Chio_Fain "Chio Fain"), [Honnah](/wiki/Honnah "Honnah"), and [Urrr'k](/wiki/Gita "Gita"). Valance eventually faced Vader on [Heva](/wiki/Heva "Heva"), but most of his team was [killed](/wiki/Death "Death") by Vader, and the cyborg himself was betrayed by Dengar. After capturing him, Vader offered to leave the bounty hunter alive if he worked with the Empire to destroy the Hidden Hand. Valance, however, made a deal with a group of [Rebel Alliance](/wiki/Alliance_to_Restore_the_Republic "Alliance to Restore the Republic") operatives. After leading the Empire to the Hidden Hand's [main base](/wiki/Hidden_Hand_asteroid_base "Hidden Hand asteroid base"), Valance escaped, using his payment for the initial job to purchase weapons for the Rebellion, in exchange for the rebels rescuing his people from Chorin.

Valance later took on a job from his old friend Han Solo, who had become a rebel, which involved [delivering](/wiki/Mission_to_Home_One "Mission to Home One") the [engineer](/wiki/Engineer "Engineer") [Vharn](/wiki/Vharn "Vharn") and the schematics for a [shield generator](/wiki/Deflector_shield_generator "Deflector shield generator") to the Rebellion. During the mission, he killed Vharn after learning of his treachery. After delivering the schematics to the Rebellion personally, he requested that they spend half the [credits](/wiki/Galactic_Credit_Standard "Galactic Credit Standard") to ransom Vharn's family.

Around [3 ABY](/wiki/3_ABY "3 ABY"), Lash resurfaced with a bounty on her head, attracting the attention of several bounty hunters. When Valance learned about Lash's resurgence, he began tracking her down. He followed Lash's coordinates to the planet [Galmerah](/wiki/Galmerah "Galmerah"), where he engaged in a fight with the bounty hunter [Bossk](/wiki/Bossk%27wassak%27Cradossk "Bossk'wassak'Cradossk"). His path led him to [Ruusan](/wiki/Ruusan "Ruusan"), where he encountered the body of [Thisspiasian](/wiki/Thisspiasian "Thisspiasian") bounty hunter [Ooris Bynar](/wiki/Ooris_Bynar "Ooris Bynar"), and later Lash herself in orbit. There he fought [Boba Fett](/wiki/Boba_Fett "Boba Fett"), before losing. Fett then left the crumbling ship, as did Valance, who regretfully left Lash for dead while taking responsibility for a [Cadeliah](/wiki/Cadeliah "Cadeliah"), who was under Lash's protection. Soon after, Valance handed the child off to his old love [Yuralla Vega](/wiki/Yuralla_Vega "Yuralla Vega") at the [rebel outpost](/wiki/Rebel_outpost_(Lowik) "Rebel outpost (Lowik)") on [Lowik](/wiki/Lowik "Lowik") after paying [Zuckuss](/wiki/Zuckuss "Zuckuss") and [4-LOM](/wiki/4-LOM "4-LOM") off of his trail.

Feeling he owed Han Solo for saving him all those years before, Valance was soon called back into action to rescue Rebels from the [Spirit of Jedha](/wiki/Spirit_of_Jedha "Spirit of Jedha") who were captured by the [Ohnaka Gang](/wiki/Ohnaka_Gang "Ohnaka Gang"). Fighting the pirates, he was soon reunited with Dengar. The two soon heard about the bounty placed on Boba Fett for the loss of the frozen Han Solo, and pursued this opportunity together. This search took them to [Nar Shaddaa](/wiki/Nar_Shaddaa "Nar Shaddaa") and [Canto Bight](/wiki/Canto_Bight "Canto Bight"), where they fought [Crimson Dawn](/wiki/Crimson_Dawn "Crimson Dawn") and the assassin [Deathstick](/wiki/Deathstick "Deathstick"). The two hunters next targeted the [Auction for Han Solo](/wiki/Auction_for_Han_Solo "Auction for Han Solo"). Valance and Dengar fought Boba Fett on [Jekara](/wiki/Jekara "Jekara"), but Valance left Dengar on the icy world while accompanying Fett into space to reclaim Solo from the *[Executor](/wiki/Executor "Executor")*. On board the [Super Star Destroyer](/wiki/Super_Star_Destroyer "Super Star Destroyer"), Valance helped Fett find the frozen smuggler, but Fett blew the cyborg up with a [thermal detonator](/wiki/Thermal_detonator "Thermal detonator"), leaving him for dead while escaping to claim Solo for himself. Escaping his fate through sheer willpower, Valance made his way to the destroyer's hangar to meet up with T'onga on the [*Edgehawk*](/wiki/Edgehawk_(starship) "Edgehawk (starship)"). Just before escaping and boarding, Valance was captured by Darth Vader and repaired to once again work for the Empire.

### Early life[]

:   ―Valance's father[[9]](#cite_note-Target_Vader_2-9)

 [![Valance meets Tarl Sokoli as a child.](data:image/gif;base64,R0lGODlhAQABAIABAAAAAP///yH5BAEAAAEALAAAAAABAAEAQAICTAEAOw%3D%3D)](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/starwars/images/2/28/ValanceMeetsTarlSokoli-BountyHunters18.jpg/revision/latest?cb=20211227070211) [![Valance meets Tarl Sokoli as a child.](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/starwars/images/2/28/ValanceMeetsTarlSokoli-BountyHunters18.jpg/revision/latest/scale-to-width-down/210?cb=20211227070211)](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/starwars/images/2/28/ValanceMeetsTarlSokoli-BountyHunters18.jpg/revision/latest?cb=20211227070211) 

Valance meets Tarl Sokoli as a child.

 

Beilert Valance was [born](/wiki/Reproduction "Reproduction") into a [mining](/wiki/Mining "Mining") [family](/wiki/Valance_family "Valance family") which had dug the hills of their [homeworld](/wiki/Homeworld "Homeworld") of [Chorin](/wiki/Chorin "Chorin") for [centuries](/wiki/Standard_century "Standard century") as [slaves](/wiki/Slavery "Slavery") until the [Galactic Empire](/wiki/Galactic_Empire "Galactic Empire") [liberated](/wiki/Liberation_of_Chorin "Liberation of Chorin") them. As a child, Valance spoke with [his father](/wiki/Beilert_Valance%27s_father "Beilert Valance's father") about his heritage, asking if he would follow in his father's and [grandfather's](/wiki/Beilert_Valance%27s_grandfather "Beilert Valance's grandfather") footsteps as a [miner](/wiki/Miner "Miner"). His father informed him that, because of the Empire's efforts, their people were no longer slaves, and Valance was free to venture into the stars.[[9]](#cite_note-Target_Vader_2-9) As a child, Valance once attended an Imperial [parade](/wiki/Parade "Parade"), where he stumbled into [Captain](/wiki/Captain "Captain") [Tarl Sokoli](/wiki/Tarl_Sokoli "Tarl Sokoli"). Sokoli gave a hungry Valance his final ration bar, and inspired him to look past the mines and consider joining the Imperial military.[[13]](#cite_note-Bounty_Hunters_18-13) Valance later worked in the mines and soon decided to join the Empire. Before he left,[[1]](#cite_note-Target_Vader_5-1) [Yuralla Vega](/wiki/Yuralla_Vega "Yuralla Vega"),[[8]](#cite_note-Bounty_Hunters_3-8) his lover, gave him a red [gem](/wiki/Gemstone "Gemstone") to remember her by. Valance's father told him to work as hard as he did in the mines.[[1]](#cite_note-Target_Vader_5-1)

Valance in swamp trooper gear

 

Valance eventually became a cadet for the Empire at [Carida Academy](/wiki/Carida_Academy "Carida Academy"), the Imperial [Naval](/wiki/Imperial_Navy "Imperial Navy") [Academy](/wiki/Imperial_Academy "Imperial Academy") on the [planet](/wiki/Planet "Planet") [Carida](/wiki/Carida "Carida")[[6]](#cite_note-Imperial_Cadet_1-6) in the [star system](/wiki/Star_system "Star system") of the [same name](/wiki/Carida_system "Carida system").[[18]](#cite_note-Point_of_No_Return-18) At the Imperial Academy, the [Imperial cadets](/wiki/Imperial_cadet "Imperial cadet") were referred to by their assigned [operating numbers](/wiki/Operating_number "Operating number") instead of their names, with Valance being known by the digits "404-913." While [he](/wiki/Gender "Gender") seemingly won the love of the Empire, due to his belief in beating others into submission to show he was the best, one of Valance's fellow cadets, [Kanina Nico](/wiki/Kanina_Nico "Kanina Nico"), particularly hated him, even more than she hated everyone else.[[6]](#cite_note-Imperial_Cadet_1-6)

Valance came to dislike fellow cadet [Han Solo](/wiki/Han_Solo "Han Solo"), as he believed Solo was holding him back and viewed him as merely a [Corellian](/wiki/Corellian "Corellian") [scrumrat](/wiki/Scrumrat "Scrumrat"). At one point during training, Solo spoke out of turn to their [training officer](/wiki/Training_officer "Training officer") [Lieutenant](/wiki/Lieutenant "Lieutenant") [Triosa Broog](/wiki/Triosa_Broog "Triosa Broog"). She responded by ordering the cadets to run up a [mountain](/wiki/Mountain "Mountain") in full [swamp trooper](/wiki/Swamp_trooper "Swamp trooper") gear. In response to this, several cadets attacked Solo. Afterwards, Valance insulted Solo, claiming scrumrats like him did not belong in the [navy](/wiki/Navy "Navy"). Before Valance could cause any serious physical harm, cadet Nico cut in and told him to let the other cadet go.[[6]](#cite_note-Imperial_Cadet_1-6)

#### Causing trouble[]

Valance and the rest of the squad were assigned to [Qhulosk](/wiki/Qhulosk "Qhulosk"). Before the [mission on Qhulosk](/wiki/Mission_on_Qhulosk "Mission on Qhulosk"), he argued with Nakan about Solo's fitness to fly into battle. Once the attack began, Valance took the lead. Solo strafed some cannons, but was pursued by three [Qhuloskian fighters](/wiki/Qhuloskian_fighter "Qhuloskian fighter"). Valance dropped in behind them, destroying them and saving Solo. He then took the point and led an attack on the shield generators that were protecting the [Qhuloskian](/wiki/Qhuloskian "Qhuloskian") army. After a successful attack, Valance's TIE fighter was hit by enemy fire and heavily damaged. A pair of Qhuloskian fighters got behind him, but Solo convinced Valance to turn off his engines so he would drop out of the line of fire and give Solo a clear shot at them. He restarted his engines, but was unable to maneuver his fighter. Before Solo could assist him, his fighter was hit by more enemy fire and he crash-landed, unable to eject.[[12]](#cite_note-Imperial_Cadet_4-12)

Valance got out of his crashed [starfighter](/wiki/Starfighter "Starfighter") and set up a defensive perimeter. When Solo and the rest of the squad approached him, he fired on them, thinking they were enemies. Tamu applied first aid to his wounds. Valance expressed disbelief that the Empire would send his squadmates to rescue him, and Solo informed him they did it on their own. While Valance could not believe it, Solo told him that Valance was like all [soldiers](/wiki/Soldier "Soldier"), expendable. Solo got Valance on the back of his speeder bike, and Valance begrudgingly thanked him for the rescue.[[21]](#cite_note-Imperial_Cadet_5-21)

On their way back to base, they ran into a large group of Qhuloskian soldiers. Valance fired upon them, but the squad was quickly surrounded. They were saved by a group of [Ghular](/wiki/Ghular "Ghular") slaves. After learning Nico's planet was next to be conquered by the Empire, Solo concocted a plan to make it look like she [died](/wiki/Death "Death") in combat. Valance agreed to go along with the falsehood but warned her she'd be wanted by the Empire. When Valance got back to base and was sent to [medical bay](/wiki/Medical_bay "Medical bay"), he learned the Empire would not replace his eye or leg and he would lose his flight privileges. He was upset, but Tamu guessed he would pay for them himself and re-enlist as a soldier so he could continue to serve.[[21]](#cite_note-Imperial_Cadet_5-21)

### Serving in the infantry[]

Valance did indeed serve in the infantry. During one battle in the [Mid Rim](/wiki/Mid_Rim_Territories "Mid Rim Territories"), serving as a [stormtrooper](/wiki/Stormtrooper "Stormtrooper"), Valance was pinned behind a trench alongside three of his comrades. When one of them asked him if his death was worth it, Valance recounted how the Empire had saved his family, implying that his death was worth it for that. Four enemy soldiers arrived to kill the stormtroopers, only for them to be killed by [Darth Vader](/wiki/Anakin_Skywalker "Anakin Skywalker") as he brought reinforcements with him.[[22]](#cite_note-Target_Vader_3-22)

During an assignment to [Mimban](/wiki/Mimban "Mimban"), Valance served as a swamp trooper and was pinned down in a trench with several other troopers. One attempted to climb out, only to have his head vaporized by enemy fire. When Valance failed to muster air support through his [comlink](/wiki/Comlink "Comlink"), the other soldiers claimed the Empire did not care about them. Determined to complete his mission, Valance led them out of the trench. He was suddenly hit by enemy fire, injuring him gravely as he was swept away by an explosion. As his body burned, he told his comrades to "keep going."[[1]](#cite_note-Target_Vader_5-1)

 [![Valance is discharged from service.](data:image/gif;base64,R0lGODlhAQABAIABAAAAAP///yH5BAEAAAEALAAAAAABAAEAQAICTAEAOw%3D%3D)](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/starwars/images/1/13/ValanceDischarged-TargetVader5.jpg/revision/latest?cb=20191204140442) [![Valance is discharged from service.](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/starwars/images/1/13/ValanceDischarged-TargetVader5.jpg/revision/latest/scale-to-width-down/220?cb=20191204140442)](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/starwars/images/1/13/ValanceDischarged-TargetVader5.jpg/revision/latest?cb=20191204140442)

At the Imperial medical facility, [Captain](/wiki/Captain "Captain") Valance received [cybernetic](/wiki/Cybernetics "Cybernetics") arms and legs, as well as having the left side of his face cybernetically enhanced. Although Valance wanted to return to battle, his old instructor, Yurib Nakan made sure that he was discharged, wishing for him to not be sent to his death. Valance was shocked at this news, but Nakan told him that he would get to go home, something which most soldiers in the Empire could never do.[[1]](#cite_note-Target_Vader_5-1)

Valance returned to Chorin in a [*Zeta*-class Heavy Cargo Shuttle](/wiki/Zeta-class_Heavy_Cargo_Shuttle "Zeta-class Heavy Cargo Shuttle"). Upon his arrival, he discovered his home in ruins and a graveyard of miners beside it. Kneeling before his father's grave, Valance said he was "sorry." Approaching the ruins of the village, he found Vega among the people. She told him the Empire had taken everything from the mines and left the people defenseless to raiders, which they were not able to stop. Valance told her he would make the raiders pay. He found where they had been, but the raiders were already gone. He was determined to keep going, and became a [bounty hunter](/wiki/Bounty_hunter "Bounty hunter").[[1]](#cite_note-Target_Vader_5-1)

The cyborg approached the troopers and attacked them, while lamenting how he was a pawn of the Empire and the loss of his "humanity"[[23]](#cite_note-Bounty_Hunters_2-23) as a result of the injuries he sustained on Mimban.[[1]](#cite_note-Target_Vader_5-1)

#### Failed job[]

:   "*After this mission is over, you'll need more than beskar armor to protect you!*"
:   ―Valance, to Boba Fett[[8]](#cite_note-Bounty_Hunters_3-8)

Following the event, Valance was mentored in bounty hunting by Lash. Prior to embarking on a [job](/wiki/Mission_to_Corellia_(Nakano_Lash%27s_crew) "Mission to Corellia (Nakano Lash's crew)"), Valance, Lash, and fellow hunters [Bossk](/wiki/Bossk%27wassak%27Cradossk "Bossk'wassak'Cradossk") and [T'ongor](/wiki/T%27ongor "T'ongor") were stationed aboard Lash's [starship](/wiki/Starship "Starship"), the *[Starskimmer](/wiki/Starskimmer "Starskimmer")*. Valance used the ship's long-range communicator to contact Vega on Chorin via [Receiver Station 356-39](/wiki/Receiver_Station_356-39 "Receiver Station 356-39"), but closed the connection before they could meet. He was then taunted by Bossk, who stated that she would not accept Valance due to his cybernetics. After getting into a scuffle with the [Trandoshan](/wiki/Trandoshan "Trandoshan"), the two were interrupted by Lash and were briefed on the job, which was to assist the son of [Mourner's Wail Syndicate](/wiki/Mourner%27s_Wail_Syndicate "Mourner's Wail Syndicate")'s leader, [Khamus](/wiki/Khamus "Khamus"), in the assassination of a member of the [Unbroken Clan](/wiki/Unbroken_Clan "Unbroken Clan")[[8]](#cite_note-Bounty_Hunters_3-8) on the [Core Worlds](/wiki/Core_Worlds "Core Worlds") planet[[27]](#cite_note-Corellia-27) [Corellia](/wiki/Corellia "Corellia").[[28]](#cite_note-Bounty_Hunters_1-28) The [team](/wiki/Nakano_Lash%27s_crew "Nakano Lash's crew") also hired the revered bounty hunter [Boba Fett](/wiki/Boba_Fett "Boba Fett") to help them.[[8]](#cite_note-Bounty_Hunters_3-8)

During the mission, Valance moved in to assist Fett in eliminating the Unbroken Clan's members. He criticized Fett for not following the team's original plan, which involved a discreet approach. While exchanging fire with one of the clanmates, Valance's face was struck by [blaster](/wiki/Blaster "Blaster") fire, melting the skin off the left side of his face and exposing his cybernetics. Suddenly, Lash betrayed her team and killed Khamus. T'ongor alerted Valance and Fett to her actions over his [wrist comm](/wiki/Wrist_comm "Wrist comm") and told them to flee, but was killed afterward.[[28]](#cite_note-Bounty_Hunters_1-28)

Valance worked with a number of bounty hunters over time, including [CT-720](/wiki/CT-720 "CT-720"), [BT-823](/wiki/BT-823 "BT-823"), [Kolivo](/wiki/Kolivo "Kolivo"), and a Nautolan, as well as [Cavic](/wiki/Cavic "Cavic"), whom he considered an ally. As they landed inside a ship after flying through an [asteroid field](/wiki/Asteroid_field "Asteroid field"), Cavic explained to Valance that he knew exactly where the Empire's reaches extended, and set up outposts across other asteroid fields. During the performance of a hit on [Abregado-rae](/wiki/Abregado-rae "Abregado-rae"), Valance was part of a job with the [Caviness Thorn](/wiki/Caviness_Thorn "Caviness Thorn") Crew. In order to prove himself, Thorn forced Valance to make his way through the spaceport and face countless [Pyke Syndicate](/wiki/Pyke_Syndicate "Pyke Syndicate") enforcers. After a brawl, Valance took out all of the [Pykes](/wiki/Pyke "Pyke") and moved on to join the crew for the hit. Scanning the area for the target, whom Valance heard would be paying the dockmaster, he saw the target: Han Solo. Valance was taken aback by this, and fumbled his shot to spare his old compatriot's life. Soon a fight ensued between Thorn's crew and Solo and [Chewbacca](/wiki/Chewbacca "Chewbacca"). Valance stepped in to take out Thorn, but fell over the balustrade next to Solo. Solo was surprised to see Valance as part of a crew trying to kill him, and felt betrayed. Still in shock, Valance couldn't get the words out and wished to apologize to Solo.[[30]](#cite_note-Bounty_Hunters_12-30)

However, Valance was shot down and held captive during the job, and Fetya escaped empty handed.[[9]](#cite_note-Target_Vader_2-9) Valance also made a habit of stealing [bounties](/wiki/Bounty "Bounty"), which put him at odds with the [Bounty Hunters' Guild](/wiki/Bounty_Hunters%27_Guild "Bounty Hunters' Guild"). As the other bounty hunters Valance had lured confronted him, he killed them all in the middle of the crowd, further flouting the [Bounty Hunter Code](/wiki/Bounty_Hunter_Code "Bounty Hunter Code").[[14]](#cite_note-Target_Vader_1-14)

#### Skirmish on Heva[]

:   "*Vader is as much machine as I am, Chio.*"
:   ―Valance to Chio Fain[[22]](#cite_note-Target_Vader_3-22)

 [![Valance engages Vader's forces in the air.](data:image/gif;base64,R0lGODlhAQABAIABAAAAAP///yH5BAEAAAEALAAAAAABAAEAQAICTAEAOw%3D%3D)](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/starwars/images/3/3b/ValanceHevaBattle-TargetVader3.jpg/revision/latest?cb=20191204143035) [![Valance engages Vader's forces in the air.](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/starwars/images/3/3b/ValanceHevaBattle-TargetVader3.jpg/revision/latest/scale-to-width-down/250?cb=20191204143035)](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/starwars/images/3/3b/ValanceHevaBattle-TargetVader3.jpg/revision/latest?cb=20191204143035)

Valance and the crew arrived on the world [Heva](/wiki/Heva "Heva"), where they planned to use an [electro-pulse](/wiki/Electro-pulse "Electro-pulse") device on Vader in order to immobilize him. They purchased a number of [bot-drones](/wiki/Bot-drone "Bot-drone") from the [arms dealer](/wiki/Arms_dealer "Arms dealer") [Wef](/wiki/Wef "Wef"), right as Vader promptly arrived in the Star Destroyer *[Formidable](/wiki/Formidable "Formidable")*. The team quickly moved to their designated positions. While Dengar and Honnah remained on the ground, Valance flew the *Broken Wing* with Urrr'k and Fain on board and engaged the star destroyer with the bot-drones escorting them. He ordered Urrr'k to man the guns and shoot down the TIE fighters chasing them, while Fain remotely controlled the [drones](/wiki/Drone "Drone"). He distracted Vader and the other fighters while giving the drones enough time to create a formation and destroy the *Formidable*. The *Broken Wing* was heavily damaged in the engagement, forcing Valance to make a crash landing. He and the others quickly escaped the starship as Vader and his forces surrounded them. Using the dust cloud created by the crash landing as an opportunity, Valance and his team trained their weapons on Vader.[[22]](#cite_note-Target_Vader_3-22)

Valance prepared the electro-pulse device he had acquired earlier, ordering the rest of the team to stand by and await his signal. However, Fain grew impatient and began firing at Vader. He deflected his shots, catching the rest of the team off guard and injuring Valance in the process. Urrr'k quickly arrived to check on him, revealing her true identity as the rebel spy Gita. Valance ordered her to cover him while Fain and Honnah were killed by Vader. He dashed towards the [Sith Lord](/wiki/Sith_Lord "Sith Lord") to activate his electro-pulse device. However, he failed to place the device on Vader, and was promptly electrified by Dengar, who revealed his true client to be the Empire. His betrayal led to Valance's capture.[[31]](#cite_note-Target_Vader_4-31)

#### Return to Chorin[]

:   "*The Empire* abandoned *Chorin. But… so did I.*"
:   ―Valance to Darth Vader[[1]](#cite_note-Target_Vader_5-1)

Valance was transported to the Star Destroyer *[Vengeance](/wiki/Vengeance "Vengeance")* and was interrogated by [interrogator droids](/wiki/IT-O_interrogation_droid "IT-O interrogation droid") for many days, dreaming of his past life on Chorin and the battle on Mimban, repeating the words "keep going" in his sleep. Vader entered his interrogation room and ordered everyone else to leave. Valance woke up, stating that he had Vader right where he wanted him. He was [tortured](/wiki/Torture "Torture") and interrogated about the locations of the Hidden Hand outposts, but stated that the outposts were already destroyed. Later, Valance escaped by severing his arm, and copied the data on the outposts by connecting his arm to the terminal. Using the noise produced by the limb, he attracted and incapacitated the stormtroopers outside the room, disguising as one of them in the process. However, he discovered that the *Broken Wing* was also brought on board, and was caught by Vader. Vader accompanied him on the starship and traveled to his homeworld, Chorin.[[1]](#cite_note-Target_Vader_5-1)

 [![Valance returns to Chorin with Vader.](data:image/gif;base64,R0lGODlhAQABAIABAAAAAP///yH5BAEAAAEALAAAAAABAAEAQAICTAEAOw%3D%3D)](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/starwars/images/8/89/ValanceChorinReturn-TargetVader5.jpg/revision/latest?cb=20191204142211) [![Valance returns to Chorin with Vader.](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/starwars/images/8/89/ValanceChorinReturn-TargetVader5.jpg/revision/latest/scale-to-width-down/250?cb=20191204142211)](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/starwars/images/8/89/ValanceChorinReturn-TargetVader5.jpg/revision/latest?cb=20191204142211) 

Valance returns to Chorin with Vader.

 

While on the planet, Valance lamented the attack on his village, stating that while the Empire abandoned the planet, he also abandoned his people. He told the story of how he returned to kill the raiders that laid waste to his home, but was too late as they had already left. He explained to Vader that the Hidden Hand was created as a result of these events. Vader stated that his people were still safe for the time being, and invited him back to the Empire. Valance later traveled to a [rebel outpost](/wiki/Rebel_outpost_(Lowik) "Rebel outpost (Lowik)") on [Lowik](/wiki/Lowik "Lowik"), where he met Gita and her commanding officer. He stated that he needed to meet the Hidden Hand while the Rebellion required weapons from them. He connected his arm to the terminal and displayed the location of a Hidden Hand outpost, claiming he knew where both he and the Rebellion could fulfill their goals.[[1]](#cite_note-Target_Vader_5-1)

As Valance approached the [Hidden Hand's main base](/wiki/Hidden_Hand_asteroid_base "Hidden Hand asteroid base") inside an asteroid field, he was captured, but not before placing a beacon that attracted the Empire's attention. He was brought before Gwi, who revealed himself to be the criminal syndicate's true leader. Furious over the failure of his mission to kill Vader, Gwi immediately began thrashing Valance. The cyborg explained that only he knew about the Hidden Hand's hideout as he deduced its position thanks to his old friend Cavic's advice, and that the rest of his team was killed on Heva. Valance stated that he tracked him down as revenge for his home's destruction. As Gwi prepared to fire his blaster, Vader and his forces, who tracked the beacon, arrived and assaulted the base. In the ensuing chaos, Valance quickly overpowered Gwi, aiming his own blaster at him. However, he was stopped by Vader as he [lifted](/wiki/Telekinesis "Telekinesis") Gwi in the air, stating that the [Emperor](/wiki/Darth_Sidious "Darth Sidious") wished to interrogate him personally. As Vader and forces took Gwi away, Valance took this opportunity to escape back to Lowik.[[29]](#cite_note-Target_Vader_6-29)

Valance reunites with Vega on Lowik.

 

Back on Lowik, Gita asked Valance if he could stay and help the Rebellion alongside his people, who migrated from Chorin and escaped certain doom from the Empire. He refused, returning the gem to Vega that she gave him back when he left Chorin to become a pilot. Valance then traveled to Coruscant to meet his old rival Dengar, who boasted about his earnings to a crowd in the [Galactic City](/wiki/Imperial_City "Imperial City"). Upon meeting him, he struck Dengar in the face. A member of the crowd pointed out that he was part of his team back when they hunted Vader down, and asked him where he spent his [credits](/wiki/Galactic_Credit_Standard "Galactic Credit Standard"). Valance clarified that his team was killed and Dengar had betrayed him. He explained that he used his credits on "something stupid." In reality, he used them to purchase supplies for the rebels on Lowik.[[29]](#cite_note-Target_Vader_6-29)

#### Task from an old friend[]

:   "*It's good to see you again…A lot has changed for* both *of us since we last fought together.*"
:   ―Han Solo, to Valance[[32]](#cite_note-EA-TSTES-32)

Valance was contacted by an old friend from his Imperial days, Han Solo, who was by then allied with the Rebellion. He traveled to [Rimmer's Rest](/wiki/Rimmer%27s_Rest "Rimmer's Rest") on [Nar Shaddaa](/wiki/Nar_Shaddaa "Nar Shaddaa") where he met Solo and the [engineer](/wiki/Engineer "Engineer") [Vharn](/wiki/Vharn "Vharn"). The [smuggler](/wiki/Smuggler "Smuggler") piqued Valance's interest for the job when he stated he could strike back at the Empire. Valance was tasked with [delivering](/wiki/Mission_to_Home_One "Mission to Home One") Vharn to the Rebellion, who possessed the schematics for a [shield generator](/wiki/Deflector_shield_generator "Deflector shield generator") that could withstand an [orbital bombardment](/wiki/Orbital_bombardment "Orbital bombardment"). After accepting the job, the cyborg and the engineer made their way to [Docking Bay 13](/wiki/Docking_Bay_13 "Docking Bay 13"), where they were attacked by a TIE fighter. Valance shot down the fighter after damaging its [stabilizers](/wiki/Stabilizer "Stabilizer") with his blaster, but were soon engaged by a platoon of Imperial forces. Distracting them with a [thermal detonator](/wiki/Thermal_detonator "Thermal detonator"), he eliminated the platoon, and the two made their way to Valance's starship, the *Broken Wing*, to escape into the Outer Rim.[[32]](#cite_note-EA-TSTES-32)

While in the Outer Rim, they were caught by a detachment of TIE fighters alongside a Star Destroyer, but escaped into [hyperspace](/wiki/Hyperspace "Hyperspace") through an opening in their formation. At the rendezvous point, Valance revealed Vharn's connection to the Empire and threatened him. Vharn pleaded with the cyborg, stating that the Empire held his family hostage. As Valance prepared to kill the traitor, Vharn requested that his treachery be kept a secret from Solo, and Valance agreed before killing him using his palm blaster in order to keep the vital information the engineer possessed safe from the Empire. The bounty hunter arrived on *[Home One](/wiki/Home_One "Home One")* to deliver the schematics to the Rebellion personally, informing them that Vharn had died during the mission. He requested that half the credits for the job be sent to ransom Vharn's family on [Sorgal 12](/wiki/Sorgal_12 "Sorgal 12") before leaving.[[32]](#cite_note-EA-TSTES-32)

#### Ruusan[]

:   "*Well it looks like Lash was here, leaving more death in her wake! Who is this guy?*"
:   ―T'onga[[36]](#cite_note-Bounty_Hunters_4-36)

Following Bynar to Ruusan, T'onga and Valance encountered the Thisspiasian bounty hunter's corpse where Lash and [Cadeliah](/wiki/Cadeliah "Cadeliah"), the heir of both the Unbroken Clan and Mourner's Wail Syndicate, had been hiding. Valance and T'onga entered a nearby village to ask where Lash had gone, and were met by defensive citizens and a [nexu](/wiki/Nexu "Nexu"). After battling the beast, a villager informed them that Lash was nearby, hiding in [Clone Wars](/wiki/Clone_Wars "Clone Wars")–era wreckage in [orbit](/wiki/Orbit "Orbit"). The two took the *Broken Wing* to dock with [Lash's ship](/wiki/Nakano_Lash%27s_720_light_freighter "Nakano Lash's 720 light freighter").[[36]](#cite_note-Bounty_Hunters_4-36)

On board, Valance betrayed T'onga's goal of eliminating their target, as the [restraining bolt](/wiki/Restraining_bolt "Restraining bolt") she planted on him was damaged by the nexu earlier. T'onga was shot in the chest and killed by Boba Fett, who followed them to Ruusan thanks to the confession of Bossk.[[36]](#cite_note-Bounty_Hunters_4-36) Valance lunged at Fett and brawled with the bounty hunter, before being electrified and temporarily disabled, allowing Fett to attack Lash and the young Cadeliah. They engaged in a short fight with Fett, where he stabbed Lash in the chest. As the ship began to disintegrate and Fett fled the scene, Valance had to leave her to die after desiring to, yet failing to save his former mentor. Valance was the sole protector of Cadeliah, with whom he escaped on the *Broken Wing* just as the Unbroken Clan arrived to destroy Lash once and for all.[[37]](#cite_note-Bounty_Hunters_5-37)

#### On the run[]

To get repairs on his injured cybernetics, Valance brought Cadeliah to the [Spur Orbiting Market](/wiki/Spur_Orbiting_Market "Spur Orbiting Market"). While stopping for food, he and the girl barely escaped an ambush from an [Arcona bounty hunter](/wiki/Unidentified_Arcona_bounty_hunter "Unidentified Arcona bounty hunter") before making it to [Slade's Repairs](/wiki/Slade%27s_Repairs "Slade's Repairs"), where Valance could get his palm blasters repaired and improved. Unknown to him, the Unbroken Clan's general [Vukorah](/wiki/Vukorah "Vukorah") has enlisted the help of famed bounty hunting duo [Zuckuss](/wiki/Zuckuss "Zuckuss") and [4-LOM](/wiki/4-LOM "4-LOM") to hunt him down. The two quickly reached the repair shop, and shot down the technician finishing work on Valance. After a brief brawl with the two, Valance had dismembered 4-LOM's right arm, and sliced open Zuckuss's regulator, narrowly escaping with Cadeliah once more. Zuckuss soon revealed to his compatriot, however, that he placed a tracking device on the *Broken Wing*. #### Prelude to the War[]

Prior to the [War of the Bounty Hunters](/wiki/War_of_the_Bounty_Hunters "War of the Bounty Hunters"), one of Dengar's contacts on Nar Shaddaa had confirmed a sighting of Boba Fett, so Valance and the Corellian bounty hunter made their way to the smuggler's moon.[[43]](#cite_note-Bounty_Hunters_11-43) While traveling through the nearby [Y'Toub System](/wiki/Y%27Toub_system "Y'Toub system"), they were attacked by the *Mist Hunter*, Zuckuss and 4-LOM still being on the hunt for Cadeliah. Dengar wanted to surrender, to Valance's disagreement, so he threatened to kill the cyborg. Flying into an [asteroid field](/wiki/Asteroid_field "Asteroid field") full of [exogorths](/wiki/Exogorth "Exogorth"), Valance believed he had lost his pursuers. Suddenly, the [Gand](/wiki/Gand "Gand")'s blasts landed and damaged Valance's ship's thrusters. Having had enough, Dengar knocked out Valance, taking control of the vessel and striking a deal with the two insectoid hunters. He gave them Boba Fett's location, much to Valance's dismay.[[30]](#cite_note-Bounty_Hunters_12-30)

#### Battle at Bestine[]

:   "*AAAAHHHHH! He's a monster!*"  
    "*I am…WHAT THE EMPIRE MADE ME!*"
:   ―A shoretrooper and Beilert Valance[[59]](#cite_note-Bounty_Hunters_30-59)

Following this, Valance and Haydenn were summoned to the *Executor* along with the Revengers. When Valance asked Haydenn what Vader wanted with them, she reminded him to address her as *lieutenant* when they were in public and to address Vader as *lord*. Once inside the [turbolift](/wiki/Turbolift "Turbolift"), Haydenn attempted to fix Valance's collar. When Valance claimed Vader wouldn't notice, Haydenn said he never missed any detail. Once they reached Vader, he chastised them for being early. When Valance demanded to know why they were there, Vader used the Force to fix his collar, saying that he expected those who served him to be held to a certain standard. Haydenn told Vader that Valance had the team operating at peak efficiency. To this, Vader said they would get a chance to prove their loyalty and advised Valance to channel his energies in a less self-destructive manner. When Valance asked what their mission was, Piett explained that they had been selected to protect a medicine shipment to [Bestine IV](/wiki/Bestine_IV "Bestine IV") from Crimson Dawn. Vader warned them not to disappoint him as he would be watching before they left for their mission.[[60]](#cite_note-BH_28-60)

After boarding the transport ship, Valance explained the plan to the Revengers as they approached Bestine. Once they landed, Valance told one of the stormtroopers stationed there to have the dock's perimeter patrolled. As Valance and the Revengers waited, the transport was ambushed by the crew of the *Edgehawk*. While guarding the ship's controls, Valance was attacked by a local [insurgent](/wiki/Unidentified_Bestine_IV_insurgent "Unidentified Bestine IV insurgent"), who he promptly killed as he heard a familiar voice. T'onga then peaked out from behind a crate and expressed shock at seeing Valance still working with the Empire.[[61]](#cite_note-BH_29-61) Valance likewise expressed shock at T'onga working with Crimson Dawn. When T'onga pointed out that this kind of mission wasn't like him, he explained that he had no choice due to Vader threatening to kill Yura, who was stationed at the rebel base where he left Cadeliah. T'onga told Valance that Cadeliah had been taken by Crimson Dawn and had chosen to stay with Qi'ra. She then reluctantly informed Valance that the rebel base had been bombed by the Empire, meaning Yura had likely been killed. Realizing the Empire must have learned of her location from his extracted memories, Valance collapsed in grief as T'onga comforted him.[[62]](#cite_note-BH_30-62)

With nothing left to keep him in the Empire's service, Valance exited the ship and began killing the surrounding [shoretroopers](/wiki/Coastal_defender_stormtrooper "Coastal defender stormtrooper"), saving T'onga's crew in the process. As the Revengers expressed confusion over Valance's actions, Valance opened the supplies and discovered that they were explosives rather than medicine. The crews were then met by a pair of TIE fighters, and Valance used his knowledge from his Imperial training to throw a projectile at one of the TIEs' weak spots and cause it to crash. After 4-LOM used the *Edgehawk* to destroy the other TIE, Valance contacted Haydenn and accused her of withholding Yura's fate from him. Haydenn attempted to apologize and explain that her feelings for him were genuine, but Valance stopped her and told her he was going to complete the mission before breaking the connection. When T'onga suggested leaving before more Imperials arrived, Valance said he was going to use himself as bait as a *Lambda*-class shuttle landed in front of them. Vader then stepped out of the ship along with Haydenn as he drew his lightsaber.[[62]](#cite_note-BH_30-62)

Charging into battle, Valance told Vader that he regretted ever serving the Empire. As they fought, Vader took note of Valance's power and told him that he was a waste of potential. Once he was beaten into submission, Valance said that the planet's people would still be free of the Empire. But Vader retorted that Valance had doomed them as the Sith Lord's forces bombed the locals, as well as Vader's own troops. Valance chastised Vader's lack of care for even his own people, which Vader ignored. As Vader prepared to strike Valance down, Haydenn insisted on finishing the job herself to make up for her previous failures, which Vader allowed. Valance tried to convince Haydenn to leave the Empire with him and start anew with someone she loved. However, Haydenn responded that her first love was the Empire before blasting Valance in the face and sending him falling over the edge of the cliff. Later, as the Empire left the planet, Valance was found by Losha and Zuckuss. Saying there was nothing left for him there, Valance left with their crew aboard the *Edgehawk*, mulling over his loss of Yura and guilt over ever having loved Haydenn.[[11]](#cite_note-BH_31-11)

Beilert Valance was a human male[[3]](#cite_note-Databank-3) with [brown](/wiki/Color "Color") [hair](/wiki/Hair "Hair"), blue [eyes](/wiki/Eye "Eye") and light skin. As a child, Valance believed the Empire had saved his family. His father had convinced him that he was destined for a career far greater than a miner.[[9]](#cite_note-Target_Vader_2-9) Growing up, he developed a relationship with Vega, and was saddened to leave her behind to become a pilot for the Empire.[[1]](#cite_note-Target_Vader_5-1) At the flight academy, Valance believed he could prove he was the best by beating others into submission, as demonstrated by his fight with Solo, which made him well suited for service in the [Imperial Military](/wiki/Imperial_Military "Imperial Military"). He also held a distaste for Solo for his past as a scrumrat.[[6]](#cite_note-Imperial_Cadet_1-6) As an adult, Valance measured 1.8 [meters](/wiki/Meter "Meter").[[5]](#cite_note-Altaya_Bounty_Hunters-5)

During training exercises, he acted as the de facto leader, and would be furious when his orders were disobeyed.[[19]](#cite_note-Imperial_Cadet_2-19) At the mission on Qhulosk, Valance repeatedly ordered his team to reduce unnecessary chatter and focus entirely on the mission at hand. He suffered damage to his TIE, but survived after listening to Solo's instructions and softened up to him, saying he was not a waste of life before he was shot down by Qhuloskian fighters.[[12]](#cite_note-Imperial_Cadet_4-12) After he was rescued by his team, Valance thanked Solo, finally abandoning his hard-heartedness towards him.[[21]](#cite_note-Imperial_Cadet_5-21)

While serving in the infantry,[[22]](#cite_note-Target_Vader_3-22) Valance was ready to give up his life for the Empire,[[8]](#cite_note-Bounty_Hunters_3-8) as evidenced in a situation where he and a few stormtroopers were surrounded by enemy soldiers.[[22]](#cite_note-Target_Vader_3-22) During an assignment on Mimban, Valance bravely led his comrades out of their trench and told them to keep moving forward, even if it nearly cost him his life.[[1]](#cite_note-Target_Vader_5-1)

Despite suffering extensive injuries at Mimban, Valance believed he could continue fighting, even after Nakan discharged him from service out of good faith.[[1]](#cite_note-Target_Vader_5-1) This led to Valance losing faith in the Empire.[[8]](#cite_note-Bounty_Hunters_3-8) Traveling back to his homeworld Chorin, Valance was devastated to find his home ravaged by raiders, and learned from Vega that the Empire had stripped the mines of its resources and abandoned the people. Determined to find and eliminate the raiders, he left and traveled down a new path.[[1]](#cite_note-Target_Vader_5-1) During this time, he struggled to settle in [the galaxy](/wiki/The_galaxy "The galaxy")[[8]](#cite_note-Bounty_Hunters_3-8) and was distraught about being a "pawn" for the Empire. This led him to violently attack a group of stormtroopers on Eriadu, where he met Lash and her crew and started a new career as a bounty hunter.[[23]](#cite_note-Bounty_Hunters_2-23) His distaste for the Empire lasted well into his bounty-hunting career, evidenced by his violence against stormtroopers, and trying to relate to a cadet named Venn, in whom he saw himself aboard the *Executor*.[[49]](#cite_note-Bounty_Hunters_17-49)

### Bounty hunter[]

:   "*Why don't you leave the morality to those of us better qualified, eh?*"  
    "*Whatever you say…* soldier*.*"
:   ―A rebel soldier and Valance[[32]](#cite_note-EA-TSTES-32)

 [![As a bounty hunter, Valance was ruthless.](data:image/gif;base64,R0lGODlhAQABAIABAAAAAP///yH5BAEAAAEALAAAAAABAAEAQAICTAEAOw%3D%3D)](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/starwars/images/b/b0/BGRTsShootout-TargetVader1.jpg/revision/latest?cb=20191202174429) [![As a bounty hunter, Valance was ruthless.](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/starwars/images/b/b0/BGRTsShootout-TargetVader1.jpg/revision/latest/scale-to-width-down/250?cb=20191202174429)](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/starwars/images/b/b0/BGRTsShootout-TargetVader1.jpg/revision/latest?cb=20191202174429) 

As a bounty hunter, Valance was ruthless.

 

As a bounty hunter, Valance did not hesitate to eliminate anyone who attempted to silence him, such as in the shootout at BG-RT's Tavern and Spirits, which left several bounty hunters dead.[[14]](#cite_note-Target_Vader_1-14) It was around this time that Valance realized that both rebels and Imperials were only soldiers waiting to be killed, the only difference being which side of the battlefield the blaster fire came from.[[32]](#cite_note-EA-TSTES-32) Valance kept [food](/wiki/Foodstuff "Foodstuff") and energy capsules with him as a reminder of the food he once enjoyed before becoming a cyborg.[[8]](#cite_note-Bounty_Hunters_3-8)

However, the cyborg also had a softer side, such as when he assisted the Rebellion in exchange for granting the people of Chorin a haven on Lowik. He spent the credits earned from the job to assassinate Vader on acquiring munitions for them, and returned the gemstone to Vega, saying she needed it more than he did by then.[[29]](#cite_note-Target_Vader_6-29) During a job from his old friend Han Solo, Valance killed Vharn, whom he was supposed to protect, after learning of his treachery, but honored the engineer's wish to free his family when he requested the Rebellion that they spend half the credits for the job to ransom the family on Sorgal 12.[[32]](#cite_note-EA-TSTES-32) Valance also showed his softer side in taking care of Cadeliah from his old master Nakano Lash after her passing,[[37]](#cite_note-Bounty_Hunters_5-37) and struggled immensely to get her to safety on Lowik.[[39]](#cite_note-Bounty_Hunters_7-39)

Owing a debt to the Rebellion,[[39]](#cite_note-Bounty_Hunters_7-39) and to Han Solo,[[21]](#cite_note-Imperial_Cadet_5-21) Valance once more risked his life to rescue several rebels aboard the *Spirit of Jedha* from the Ohnaka Gang.[[42]](#cite_note-Bounty_Hunters_9-42) He butted heads with Skragg and found kinship with Blanch Sproull in conversation about truly believing in the values of the Rebellion. Valance once again showed his dedication to doing what was right in the galaxy, even if it meant getting his hands dirty.[[41]](#cite_note-Bounty_Hunters_10-41) From there, Valance made even with Dengar and they set off together to find Han Solo in Boba Fett's possession.[[43]](#cite_note-Bounty_Hunters_11-43) Valance fought hard against and with both other hunters and the Empire trying and failing to reclaim Han Solo.[[47]](#cite_note-Bounty_Hunters_15-47)[[44]](#cite_note-Bounty_Hunters_16-44) He was eventually betrayed by Fett, who took the frozen smuggler for himself.[[48]](#cite_note-War_of_the_Bounty_Hunters_5-48)

### Working with other individuals[]

:   "*Buddy, I got two rules. First, always know who you're working for. Second? I work* alone*.*"
:   ―Valance, to Gwi[[14]](#cite_note-Target_Vader_1-14)

During their mission on Corellia, Valance held a rivalry with Fett[[28]](#cite_note-Bounty_Hunters_1-28) and disliked Bossk;[[8]](#cite_note-Bounty_Hunters_3-8) Fett due to his arrogance,[[28]](#cite_note-Bounty_Hunters_1-28) and Bossk due to him abandoning the mission.[[23]](#cite_note-Bounty_Hunters_2-23) In his later years, Valance mainly worked in isolation, completing bounties for the Bounty Hunters' Guild stolen from other hunters.[[14]](#cite_note-Target_Vader_1-14) When working for the Hidden Hand with a team, Valance showed little concern for them, even when Honnah and Fain lost their lives while fighting Vader.[[31]](#cite_note-Target_Vader_4-31) However, Valance also trusted Gita with the safety of his people,[[29]](#cite_note-Target_Vader_6-29) despite learning about her true identity[[31]](#cite_note-Target_Vader_4-31) and motivation.[[1]](#cite_note-Target_Vader_5-1)

Valance displayed a stern attitude towards his droid Nine-Four-El whenever he would remind the cyborg of details such as bounty terms[[28]](#cite_note-Bounty_Hunters_1-28) and property regulations.[[23]](#cite_note-Bounty_Hunters_2-23) Upon discovering the droid's lifeless body, however, Valance showed a little sorrow, stating that while he hated Nine-Four-El for his stupidity, it was "his stupid droid."[[8]](#cite_note-Bounty_Hunters_3-8) When Lash supposedly betrayed her team, Valance was shocked upon learning about this and[[28]](#cite_note-Bounty_Hunters_1-28) searched for her in order to learn the reason for her actions,[[23]](#cite_note-Bounty_Hunters_2-23) unlike other hunters who wished to kill Lash either out of revenge or profit from the bounty on the Nautolan's head.[[28]](#cite_note-Bounty_Hunters_1-28)

When serving under Darth Vader, Valance was forced to work with a team of other individuals, including Ochi, the Revengers, and some assassins, fighting against the growing threat of Crimson Dawn. Valance and Ochi had something of a rivalrous back and forth, at one point both acknowledging their statuses as pawns of Vader.[[15]](#cite_note-DV_2020_19-15)

### Yuralla Vega[]

:   "*I have something for you. Something to remember us by.* [Hands a gem to Beilert Valance] *Be safe Beilert…*"
:   ―Yuralla Vega, to Beilert Valance[[1]](#cite_note-Target_Vader_5-1)

As a young man on Chorin, Valance had fallen in love with Yuralla Vega.[[1]](#cite_note-Target_Vader_5-1) Valance explained to her his intention to join the Imperial Military and leave Chorin. He was sad to leave her, yet was confident in attaining higher status and returning. As he left, Vega gave him a fire ruby to remember her by. Valance would clutch this gem as he was being operated on on the battlefield of Mimban, after he'd been scorched in a ground assault. The [2-1B surgical droid](/wiki/2-1B_surgical_droid "2-1B surgical droid") and an officer around the operating table remarked how it was baffling that Valance was still alive, as he held on through his love for Vega and the thought of returning to her.[[38]](#cite_note-Bounty_Hunters_6-38)

Valance returned to Chorin years later, but he was a changed man.[[39]](#cite_note-Bounty_Hunters_7-39) He'd lost all of his limbs on Mimban,[[1]](#cite_note-Target_Vader_5-1) and his eye on Qhulosk.[[21]](#cite_note-Imperial_Cadet_5-21) After saving the citizens of Chorin and relocating them to a rebel outpost on Lowik,[[29]](#cite_note-Target_Vader_6-29) he decided to leave Vega again, giving her back the fire ruby he had held onto for all those years. She was betrayed by this notion, and was enraged by his breaking their promise. Valance believed that she looked at him differently after he'd lost most of his body, and could not bear to stay and have her not really love him. She threw the gem back at him, and left before he could offer any explanation.[[39]](#cite_note-Bounty_Hunters_7-39)

Later, Valance was at his wit's end as he was protecting the heir of a criminal empire, Cadeliah, and decided to bring her to the only person he could trust: Vega.[[38]](#cite_note-Bounty_Hunters_6-38) He arrived very injured, and his old flame helped him inside the rebel outpost to be operated on. Valance had an ever-changing relationship with Han Solo. The two young men first met at the Imperial Academy on Carida, where they had a very competitive relationship that lead them to fight on at least one occasion[[19]](#cite_note-Imperial_Cadet_2-19) Their training as wingmen culminating in a battle on Qhulosk, where Valance's TIE was shot down, left for dead in an area that would soon be bombed by Imperial leadership. Not willing to let a teammate die, Solo and his fellow cadets set out on speeder bikes to rescue their squad mate.[[12]](#cite_note-Imperial_Cadet_4-12) The cadets reached Valance, who was surrounded by Qhuloskian soldiers. Sneaking by their enemies, the Imperials made a deal with some Ghulars, slaves of the Qhuloskians, in order to escape the battlefield. Solo had risked his own life and saved Valance's, although injuries from this battle lead to the first batch of cybernetics Valance would receive.[[21]](#cite_note-Imperial_Cadet_5-21)

Over time, Valance would learn to appreciate what Solo did for him, eventually feeling that he owed the Corellian. In one particular instance on Abregado-Rae, Valance ran into Solo and Chewbacca on a hit job, not knowing his former wingman was the target. When Solo realized this, Valance didn't have time to explain why he was targeting the smuggler, and Solo felt betrayed by the friend he saved, leaving Valance with regret.[[30]](#cite_note-Bounty_Hunters_12-30) In another instance, Valance, at Solo's request, aided the Rebellion in transporting a young engineer carrying a datacard to his designated rendezvous point. The two were continually ambushed by the Empire, and the engineer was revealed to be a traitor. Nonetheless, Valance released the engineer, gave his credits to the man and his family, and followed through on his debt to Solo by delivering the datacard to the Rebel Alliance.[[32]](#cite_note-EA-TSTES-32)

As Solo was frozen in [carbonite](/wiki/Carbonite "Carbonite"), Valance struggled immensely intending to rescue the smuggler from first Boba Fett,[[46]](#cite_note-Bounty_Hunters_14-46) later Crimson Dawn, and then the Empire,[[48]](#cite_note-War_of_the_Bounty_Hunters_5-48) but was betrayed by Fett on board the *Executor* in the end. During this time, Valance went through hell and back trying to save his old fellow cadet, resulting in what was almost his death.[[49]](#cite_note-Bounty_Hunters_17-49)

While at the flight academy, Valance proved himself to be the top cadet.[[19]](#cite_note-Imperial_Cadet_2-19) Aside from his piloting skills, Valance was also trained in using blasters, utilizing one during his rescue on Qhulosk.[[21]](#cite_note-Imperial_Cadet_5-21) As a cyborg, Valance could take on several opponents at once using his palm blaster and a combination of other weaponry.[[14]](#cite_note-Target_Vader_1-14) With his knowledge from his academy days, he was able to maneuver through Imperial forces on his starship, the *Broken Wing*.[[22]](#cite_note-Target_Vader_3-22)

Valance was skilled in [hand-to-hand combat](/wiki/Unarmed_combat "Unarmed combat"), defeating opponents such as his former employer Gwi[[29]](#cite_note-Target_Vader_6-29) and the bounty hunter Bossk.[[8]](#cite_note-Bounty_Hunters_3-8) When his weapons failed him, he improvised in combat by using nearby objects to incapacitate his enemies.[[8]](#cite_note-Bounty_Hunters_3-8)[[28]](#cite_note-Bounty_Hunters_1-28) He was also able to use his knowledge of TIE fighters to shoot one down by damaging its stabilizers during an assignment from Han Solo.[[32]](#cite_note-EA-TSTES-32) While infiltrating the *Executor* with Boba Fett, Valance used his Imperial knowledge to guide the bounty hunter to the frozen Han Solo, where he was betrayed.[[48]](#cite_note-War_of_the_Bounty_Hunters_5-48)

## Equipment[]

### Cybernetics[]

:   "*You are…droid?*"  
    "*Not where it counts.*"
:   ―A KX-series security droid and Beilert Valance[[48]](#cite_note-War_of_the_Bounty_Hunters_5-48)

 [![A diagram of Valance's extensive cybernetics](data:image/gif;base64,R0lGODlhAQABAIABAAAAAP///yH5BAEAAAEALAAAAAABAAEAQAICTAEAOw%3D%3D)](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/starwars/images/1/1b/ValanceCybernetics-BountyHunters3.png/revision/latest?cb=20200925180929) [![A diagram of Valance's extensive cybernetics](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/starwars/images/1/1b/ValanceCybernetics-BountyHunters3.png/revision/latest/scale-to-width-down/250?cb=20200925180929)](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/starwars/images/1/1b/ValanceCybernetics-BountyHunters3.png/revision/latest?cb=20200925180929) 

A diagram of Valance's extensive cybernetics

 

After losing his left eye on Qhulosk, Valance was given a [cybernetic](/wiki/Cybernetics "Cybernetics") eye as a replacement.[[21]](#cite_note-Imperial_Cadet_5-21) Sustaining severe injuries in Mimban, he received extensive cybernetics at the medical facility,[[1]](#cite_note-Target_Vader_5-1) which covered the majority of his body and internal organs. [Synthflesh](/wiki/Synthflesh "Synthflesh") was used to cover his cybernetics in order to avoid revulsion at his cyborg appearance from others. Valance's cheaper Imperial cybernetics were upgraded greatly after encountering Nakano Lash and her team. A [durasteel](/wiki/Durasteel "Durasteel") skeleton made from scrapped [battle droid](/wiki/Battle_droid "Battle droid") technology covered his internal organs, replacing the older parts installed by Imperial [medics](/wiki/Medic "Medic"). His heart, which received severe damage at Mimban, was covered with ryboarse-based synthetic cybernetics to strengthen it.[[8]](#cite_note-Bounty_Hunters_3-8)

A built-in respirator supported his lungs, which enabled him to survive for more than ten [minutes](/wiki/Standard_minute "Standard minute") in a vacuum. His right arm had space for various weapon upgrades, including a [vibroblade](/wiki/Vibroblade "Vibroblade"), grappling hook, or a concussion disc launcher, while his left arm was equipped with a palm blaster, a weapon that could be fired from his hand. The legs were piston-powered, allowing Valance to leap at much greater distances than a normal human. His feet contained [magnetic](/wiki/Magnet "Magnet") pods that could attach to metal surfaces and assist Valance in scaling them, or stabilize him in zero [gravity](/wiki/Gravity "Gravity"). His synthetic organs filtered out any [lactic acids](/wiki/Lactic_acid "Lactic acid") and [poisons](/wiki/Poison "Poison") from the system in order to increase his stamina.[[8]](#cite_note-Bounty_Hunters_3-8)

Valance's [brain](/wiki/Brain "Brain")'s nervous system was replaced with cybernetic ports connected to electrostatic wiring. His [ears](/wiki/Ear "Ear") had built-in receivers that could tap into unencrypted transmissions within a short distance. Valance's cybernetics were also equipped with a failsafe that placed Valance in hibernation should he face a critical systems failure. The right eye was an advanced cybernetic [photoreceptor](/wiki/Photoreceptor "Photoreceptor") that could switch between [infrared](/wiki/Infrared "Infrared"), [night](/wiki/Night_vision "Night vision"), [ultraviolet](/wiki/Ultraviolet "Ultraviolet"), and magnetic vision. The eye could be used for targeting or telescopic zooming, and had both a built-in [camera](/wiki/Camera "Camera") and a [holographic](/wiki/Hologram "Hologram") projector to capture and display footage, respectively. The eye could also display a diagnostic screen which monitored Valance's body and cybernetic systems.[[8]](#cite_note-Bounty_Hunters_3-8)

Valance's body and cybernetics were powered by a power cell. After recovering from losing consciousness and regaining stability, Valance's internal power cells were able to recharge.[[39]](#cite_note-Bounty_Hunters_7-39)

When Valance was once more in service to Darth Vader, his heart was removed and placed in a vat aboard the *Executor*. This greatly disturbed Valance, and Vader would continue to heavily abuse Valance's cybernetic systems.[[13]](#cite_note-Bounty_Hunters_18-13)

## Behind the scenes[]

 [![Beilert Valance originally appeared in the Star Wars Legends continuity.](data:image/gif;base64,R0lGODlhAQABAIABAAAAAP///yH5BAEAAAEALAAAAAABAAEAQAICTAEAOw%3D%3D)](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/starwars/images/b/ba/Termivalance.jpg/revision/latest?cb=20090902195718) [![Beilert Valance originally appeared in the Star Wars Legends continuity.](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/starwars/images/b/ba/Termivalance.jpg/revision/latest/scale-to-width-down/200?cb=20090902195718)](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/starwars/images/b/ba/Termivalance.jpg/revision/latest?cb=20090902195718) 

Beilert Valance originally appeared in the *Star Wars* Legends continuity.

 

Beilert Valance first appeared in *[Star Wars](/wiki/Star_Wars "Star Wars")* [canon](/wiki/Canon "Canon") in the [first issue](/wiki/Solo:_A_Star_Wars_Story_Adaptation_1 "Solo: A Star Wars Story Adaptation 1") of the [comic book](/wiki/Star_Wars_comic_books "Star Wars comic books") [adaptation](/wiki/Solo:_A_Star_Wars_Story_Adaptation "Solo: A Star Wars Story Adaptation") of *[Solo: A Star Wars Story](/wiki/Solo:_A_Star_Wars_Story "Solo: A Star Wars Story")*, which was written by [Robbie Thompson](/wiki/Robbie_Thompson "Robbie Thompson"), illustrated by [Will Sliney](/wiki/Will_Sliney "Will Sliney") and published by [Marvel Comics](/wiki/Marvel_Comics "Marvel Comics")[[68]](#cite_note-Solo-1-68) on [October 10](/wiki/October_10 "October 10"), [2018](/wiki/2018 "2018").[[69]](#cite_note-Marvel-Solo1-69) The character went unnamed until the release of the *[Star Wars: Han Solo - Imperial Cadet](/wiki/Star_Wars:_Han_Solo_-_Imperial_Cadet "Star Wars: Han Solo - Imperial Cadet")* comic miniseries, which was written by Thompson, illustrated by [Leonard Kirk](/wiki/Leonard_Kirk "Leonard Kirk"), and published by [Marvel Comics](/wiki/Marvel_Comics "Marvel Comics"). Initially introduced as Cadet 404-913 in the [first issue](/wiki/Han_Solo_-_Imperial_Cadet_1 "Han Solo - Imperial Cadet 1"),[[6]](#cite_note-Imperial_Cadet_1-6) he was later identified as Valance in the [third issue](/wiki/Han_Solo_-_Imperial_Cadet_3 "Han Solo - Imperial Cadet 3")[[20]](#cite_note-Imperial_Cadet_3-20) and further identified as Beilert in the [fourth](/wiki/Han_Solo_-_Imperial_Cadet_4 "Han Solo - Imperial Cadet 4").[[12]](#cite_note-Imperial_Cadet_4-12)

The character of [Beilert Valance](/wiki/Beilert_Valance/Legends "Beilert Valance/Legends") originated in the [*Star Wars* Legends](/wiki/Star_Wars_Legends "Star Wars Legends") continuity, first appearing in [*Star Wars* (1977) 16](/wiki/Star_Wars_(1977)_16 "Star Wars (1977) 16")—a [comic book](/wiki/Star_Wars_(1977) "Star Wars (1977)") written by [Archie Goodwin](/wiki/Archie_Goodwin "Archie Goodwin")[[70]](#cite_note-Star_Wars_16-70) and published on [July 25](/wiki/July_25 "July 25"), [1978](/wiki/1978 "1978").[[71]](#cite_note-Marvel_SW_16-71) The name "Beilert" was established in "[The Hunt Within: Valance's Tale: Part 1](/wiki/The_Hunt_Within:_Valance%27s_Tale:_Part_1 "The Hunt Within: Valance's Tale: Part 1")," a [2004](/wiki/2004 "2004") scenario for the [*Star Wars* Miniatures](/wiki/Star_Wars_Miniatures "Star Wars Miniatures") [roleplaying game](/wiki/Roleplaying_game "Roleplaying game") by [Jason Fry](/wiki/Jason_Fry "Jason Fry").[[72]](#cite_note-The_Hunt_Within-72) According to Fry, Goodwin likely took the name "Valance" from the title outlaw character in the classic [John Ford](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/John_Ford "wikipedia:John Ford") western film [*The Man Who Shot Liberty Valance*](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/The_Man_Who_Shot_Liberty_Valance "wikipedia:The Man Who Shot Liberty Valance"); accordingly, Fry devised the name "Beilert" as a near-anagram of "Liberty."[[73]](#cite_note-The_Hunt_Within:_Valance's_Tale:_Conclusion-73)

After being reintroduced to *Star Wars* canon, Valance became a major character in the *[Star Wars: Bounty Hunters](/wiki/Star_Wars:_Bounty_Hunters "Star Wars: Bounty Hunters")* comic series written by [Ethan Sacks](/wiki/Ethan_Sacks "Ethan Sacks").[[28]](#cite_note-Bounty_Hunters_1-28) Fans of Valance called themselves the "ValanceNation,"[[74]](#cite_note-Sacks_Fans-74) whom Sacks reflected endured a great deal of torture thanks to all he put Valance through in *Bounty Hunters*.[[75]](#cite_note-Sacks_Thanksgiving-75) Sacks stated his favorite thing about writing him was how the character recovered from the trauma inflicted upon him.[[76]](#cite_note-Valance_troubled_life-76) Sacks played coy with fans as to whether Valance would survive the series, joking that [*Bounty Hunters* 42](/wiki/Bounty_Hunters_42 "Bounty Hunters 42") was his "last adventure"[[77]](#cite_note-Last_adventure-77) and remarking that, if a fan did not open the issue, Valance would be [simultaneously dead and alive](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Schr%C3%B6dinger%27s_cat "wikipedia:Schrödinger's cat").[[78]](#cite_note-Schrödinger's_Valance-78) With the end of the series, Sacks joked that Valance's odds of survival were suddenly much higher all because he was no longer writing his journey.[[79]](#cite_note-Charge_of_fate-79)
