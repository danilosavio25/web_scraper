### Timeline

[[Source]](https://starwars.fandom.com/wiki/Template:TelevisionSeries)

:   "Skeleton Crew *tells the story of four kids searching for their home planet after getting lost in the Star Wars galaxy, and the strange aliens and dangerous places they encounter on their adventure.*"
:   ―StarWars.com[[9]](#cite_note-SW.com-9)

***Star Wars: Skeleton Crew*** is a television series that premiered on [Disney+](/wiki/Disney%2B "Disney+") on [December 2](/wiki/December_2 "December 2"), [2024](/wiki/2024 "2024"). Starring [Jude Law](/wiki/Jude_Law "Jude Law") as [Jod Na Nawood](/wiki/Jod_Na_Nawood "Jod Na Nawood") and focusing on four children in an adventure through the *[Star Wars](/wiki/Star_Wars "Star Wars")* [galaxy](/wiki/The_galaxy "The galaxy") during the [New Republic Era](/wiki/New_Republic_Era "New Republic Era"), the live-action series was created and written by [Jon Watts](/wiki/Jon_Watts "Jon Watts") and [Christopher Ford](/wiki/Christopher_Ford "Christopher Ford"). *Skeleton Crew* features [Wim](/wiki/Wim "Wim") ([Ravi Cabot-Conyers](/wiki/Ravi_Cabot-Conyers "Ravi Cabot-Conyers")), [Fern](/wiki/Fern "Fern") ([Ryan Kiera Armstrong](/wiki/Ryan_Kiera_Armstrong "Ryan Kiera Armstrong")), [KB](/wiki/KB "KB") ([Kyriana Kratter](/wiki/Kyriana_Kratter "Kyriana Kratter")), [Neel](/wiki/Neel "Neel") ([Robert Timothy Smith](/wiki/Robert_Timothy_Smith "Robert Timothy Smith")), as well as the droid [SM-33](/wiki/SM-33 "SM-33") ([Nick Frost](/wiki/Nick_Frost "Nick Frost")) and the pirates [Vane](/wiki/Vane "Vane"), [Brutus](/wiki/Brutus "Brutus"), [Chaelt](/wiki/Chaelt "Chaelt"), [Gunter](/wiki/Gunter "Gunter"), and [Pax](/wiki/Pax_(pirate) "Pax (pirate)"). It was announced on [May 17](/wiki/May_17 "May 17"), [2022](/wiki/2022 "2022") under the code name ***Grammar Rodeo*** and its episodes were directed by Watts, [David Lowery](/wiki/David_Lowery "David Lowery"), the Daniels ([Daniel Kwan](/wiki/Daniel_Kwan "Daniel Kwan") and [Daniel Scheinert](/wiki/Daniel_Scheinert "Daniel Scheinert")), [Jake Schreier](/wiki/Jake_Schreier "Jake Schreier"), [Bryce Dallas Howard](/wiki/Bryce_Dallas_Howard "Bryce Dallas Howard"), and [Lee Isaac Chung](/wiki/Lee_Isaac_Chung "Lee Isaac Chung"). The series is heavily influenced by the [1985](/wiki/1985 "1985") adventure film [*The Goonies*](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/The_Goonies "wikipedia:The Goonies").

## Official description[]

*'*Star Wars*: Skeleton Crew'* follows the journey of four kids who make a mysterious discovery on their seemingly safe home planet, then get lost in a strange and dangerous galaxy. Finding their way home—and meeting unlikely allies and enemies—will be a greater adventure than they ever imagined.*[[10]](#cite_note-ILM-10)*

## Development[]

:   "*It's the story of their journey trying to find their way home. It stars four kids, but it is not a kids show.*"
:   ―Jon Watts[[11]](#cite_note-Showcase-11)

On [May 17](/wiki/May_17 "May 17"), [2022](/wiki/2022 "2022") [*Vanity Fair*](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Vanity_Fair_(magazine) "wikipedia:Vanity Fair (magazine)") reported that a *[Star Wars](/wiki/Star_Wars "Star Wars")* series was in development for [Disney+](/wiki/Disney%2B "Disney+"), with [*Spider-Man: No Way Home*](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Spider-Man:_No_Way_Home "wikipedia:Spider-Man: No Way Home") director [Jon Watts](/wiki/Jon_Watts "Jon Watts") serving as creator and executive producer. [*Spider-Man: Homecoming*](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Spider-Man:_Homecoming "wikipedia:Spider-Man: Homecoming") writer [Christopher Ford](/wiki/Christopher_Ford "Christopher Ford") will serve as writer.[[4]](#cite_note-Vanity_Fair-4) Later that month, during [Celebration Anaheim](/wiki/Celebration_Anaheim_(2022) "Celebration Anaheim (2022)"), the name of the show and further details were revealed, including the casting of [Jude Law](/wiki/Jude_Law "Jude Law").[[11]](#cite_note-Showcase-11) [Jon Favreau](/wiki/Jon_Favreau "Jon Favreau") and [Dave Filoni](/wiki/Dave_Filoni "Dave Filoni"), the creators of the shows [*The Mandalorian*](/wiki/Star_Wars:_The_Mandalorian "Star Wars: The Mandalorian") and *[Ahsoka](/wiki/Star_Wars:_Ahsoka "Star Wars: Ahsoka")*, which take place around the same time frame,[[11]](#cite_note-Showcase-11) are serving as executive producers.[[2]](#cite_note-Twitter-2) While filming *Spider-Man: No Way Home*, Favreau asked Watts if he was interested in directing *Star Wars*. Instead, Watts pitched his idea for a new series. Favreau called Filoni from [Atlanta](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Atlanta "wikipedia:Atlanta"), and they joined a video call, where Watts pitched his idea.[[12]](#cite_note-Pitch-12) The series is heavily influenced by the [1985](/wiki/1985 "1985") children adventure film [*The Goonies*](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/The_Goonies "wikipedia:The Goonies") and other adventure stories of the era like [*E.T. the Extra-Terrestrial*](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/E.T._the_Extra-Terrestrial "wikipedia:E.T. the Extra-Terrestrial"), [Lucasfilm](/wiki/Lucasfilm "Lucasfilm")'s *[Indiana Jones](/wiki/Indiana_Jones_(franchise) "Indiana Jones (franchise)")* films and [LucasArts](/wiki/LucasArts "LucasArts")'s video game [*The Secret of Monkey Island*](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/The_Secret_of_Monkey_Island "wikipedia:The Secret of Monkey Island").[[13]](#cite_note-swcom_creators_interview-13) It also feature references to the 1985 [*Star Wars* Legends](/wiki/Star_Wars_Legends "Star Wars Legends") film *[Ewoks: The Battle for Endor](/wiki/Ewoks:_The_Battle_for_Endor "Ewoks: The Battle for Endor")*[[14]](#cite_note-Teek-14) and the [1978](/wiki/1978 "1978") *[The Star Wars Holiday Special](/wiki/The_Star_Wars_Holiday_Special "The Star Wars Holiday Special")*.[[15]](#cite_note-Circus_holo-15)

The series soundtrack was composed by [Mick Giacchino](/wiki/Mick_Giacchino "Mick Giacchino").[[7]](#cite_note-Giacchino-7) The [soundtrack album](/wiki/Star_Wars:_Skeleton_Crew_(Original_Soundtrack) "Star Wars: Skeleton Crew (Original Soundtrack)") was later released on [January 17](/wiki/January_17 "January 17"), [2025](/wiki/2025 "2025").[[16]](#cite_note-Spotify-16)

## Release and reception[]

The series' first official trailer was released on [August 10](/wiki/August_10 "August 10"), [2024](/wiki/2024 "2024").[[17]](#cite_note-Official_Trailer_1-17) *Skeleton Crew* was announced on [May 26](/wiki/May_26 "May 26"), [2022](/wiki/2022 "2022") for a release in [2023](/wiki/2023 "2023"),[[2]](#cite_note-Twitter-2) before later being moved to [2024](/wiki/2024 "2024").[[18]](#cite_note-Disney-18) On August 10, 2024, it was announced that the series would be released on [Disney+](/wiki/Disney%2B "Disney+") on [December 3](/wiki/December_3 "December 3"), 2024.[[17]](#cite_note-Official_Trailer_1-17) On [November 27](/wiki/November_27 "November 27") that year, the series' release date was moved earlier to [December 2](/wiki/December_2 "December 2"), at 18:00 [PT](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pacific_Time_Zone "wikipedia:Pacific Time Zone"), with a two-episode premiere. They would be followed by a new episode every Tuesday until the final episode, episode 8, on [January 14](/wiki/January_14 "January 14"), [2025](/wiki/2025 "2025").[[3]](#cite_note-Early-3)

A [Huttese](/wiki/Huttese "Huttese") variation of the song "[Major Tom (Coming Home)](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Major_Tom_(Coming_Home) "wikipedia:Major Tom (Coming Home)")" by [Peter Schilling](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Peter_Schilling "wikipedia:Peter Schilling") ([1983](/wiki/1983 "1983")) was used in the second trailer from [November 1](/wiki/November_1 "November 1"),[[19]](#cite_note-Official_Trailer_2-19) and later received a full lyric video with the title "[Youngee Wim (Bunky Dunko)](/wiki/Youngee_Wim_(Bunky_Dunko) "Youngee Wim (Bunky Dunko)")."[[20]](#cite_note-Youngee_Wim-20)

### Accolades[]

On [November 18](/wiki/November_18 "November 18"), [2025](/wiki/2025 "2025"), the series was nominated for 17 [Emmy Awards](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Emmy_Awards "wikipedia:Emmy Awards") including "Outstanding Young Teen Series," "Outstanding Lead Performer in a Preschool, Children's or Young Teen Program" ([Jude Law](/wiki/Jude_Law "Jude Law")), "Outstanding Younger Performer in a Preschool, Children's or Young Teen Program" ([Ravi Cabot-Conyers](/wiki/Ravi_Cabot-Conyers "Ravi Cabot-Conyers")) and "Outstanding Writing for a Young Teen Series" ("[This Could Be a Real Adventure](/wiki/This_Could_Be_a_Real_Adventure "This Could Be a Real Adventure")").[[21]](#cite_note-Emmy_Awards_Nov_2025-21)[[22]](#cite_note-SWNN_Nov_2025-22)
