***Star Wars Resistance*** is an animated television series that premiered on [October 7](/wiki/October_7 "October 7"), [2018](/wiki/2018 "2018"), and concluded on [January 26](/wiki/January_26 "January 26"), [2020](/wiki/2020 "2020"), having aired on [Disney Channel](/wiki/Disney_Channel "Disney Channel") and [Disney XD](/wiki/Disney_XD "Disney XD"). The series was created by [Dave Filoni](/wiki/Dave_Filoni "Dave Filoni") and executive produced by [Athena Yvette Portillo](/wiki/Athena_Yvette_Portillo "Athena Yvette Portillo"), [Justin Ridge](/wiki/Justin_Ridge "Justin Ridge"), and [Brandon Auman](/wiki/Brandon_Auman "Brandon Auman"). It stars [Christopher Sean](/wiki/Christopher_Sean "Christopher Sean"), [Josh Brener](/wiki/Josh_Brener "Josh Brener"), [Suzie McGrath](/wiki/Suzie_McGrath "Suzie McGrath"), [Scott Lawrence](/wiki/Scott_Lawrence "Scott Lawrence"), and [Justin Ridge](/wiki/Justin_Ridge "Justin Ridge"), with special appearances from [Oscar Isaac](/wiki/Oscar_Isaac "Oscar Isaac") as [Poe Dameron](/wiki/Poe_Dameron "Poe Dameron") and [Gwendoline Christie](/wiki/Gwendoline_Christie "Gwendoline Christie") as [Captain](/wiki/Captain "Captain") [Phasma](/wiki/Phasma "Phasma").

## Plot summary[]

:   "*We wanted to give a slice of life in a part of the galaxy that wasn't about the Jedi. How do people who are maybe not as connected with the Force, or at least they don't think they are, how did they respond to these threats? What do we do in the meantime when there isn't really a war going on?*"
:   ―Justin Ridge[[14]](#cite_note-io9-14)

The series is set between the events of [*Star Wars*: Episode VI *Return of the Jedi*](/wiki/Star_Wars:_Episode_VI_Return_of_the_Jedi "Star Wars: Episode VI Return of the Jedi") and [*Star Wars*: Episode VII *The Force Awakens*](/wiki/Star_Wars:_Episode_VII_The_Force_Awakens "Star Wars: Episode VII The Force Awakens"), in a time when the [Resistance](/wiki/Resistance "Resistance") is still learning about the [growing threat](/wiki/Cold_War "Cold War") of the [First Order](/wiki/First_Order "First Order"). It centers on the character of [Kazuda Xiono](/wiki/Kazuda_Xiono "Kazuda Xiono"), a young [pilot](/wiki/Pilot "Pilot") in [General](/wiki/General "General") [Leia Organa's](/wiki/Leia_Skywalker_Organa_Solo "Leia Skywalker Organa Solo") [Resistance](/wiki/Resistance "Resistance") who is tasked with spying on the growing threat of the First Order.[[1]](#cite_note-New_Animated_Series-1)

[Slashfilm](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Slashfilm "wikipedia:Slashfilm") reported November 12, 2018 that a more specific timeline was revealed in episode 1.6 of [Resistance Rewind](/wiki/Resistance_Rewind "Resistance Rewind"). In that episode, executive producer Justin Ridge let slip that *Star Wars Resistance* takes place six months before the events of *The Force Awakens*, revealing: "Because this series takes place six months before The Force Awakens we have a lot of connective tissue to what we see in the movie. So eventually our events in the show will parallel what happens in the movie."[[15]](#cite_note-SWYouTube-#1.6:_Whispers_of_the_First_Order-15)

## Development[]

:   "*So right now, Resistance is a bit different, in that I'm not on that day-to-day. I created and set it in motion, but I've given that to other people to raise up, people that I've worked with a long time, who I think deserve the opportunity to tell stories and I think it's exciting, and I give them my thoughts and notes – I was just doing that this morning, like George would give me. So that's fun.*"
:   ―Dave Filoni on his involvement with the series[[16]](#cite_note-Filoni_Not_To_Work_Day-To-Day-16)

*Star Wars Resistance* was created by [Dave Filoni](/wiki/Dave_Filoni "Dave Filoni"), head of [Lucasfilm Animation](/wiki/Lucasfilm_Animation "Lucasfilm Animation") who led the creative team on *[Star Wars: The Clone Wars](/wiki/Star_Wars:_The_Clone_Wars "Star Wars: The Clone Wars")* and *[Star Wars Rebels](/wiki/Star_Wars_Rebels "Star Wars Rebels")*. The inspiration for the series was Filoni's interest in [World War II](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/World_War_II "wikipedia:World War II") aircraft and fighter pilots, with his grandfather's time as a pilot during the war being a particular influence on him. In the announcement of the series, Filoni noted that the series would capture the sense of excitement around high-speed racing, which *Star Wars* had depicted in many stories over the years.[[1]](#cite_note-New_Animated_Series-1) Executive producer [Justin Ridge](/wiki/Justin_Ridge "Justin Ridge") elaborated: "It was a concept Dave came up with: he wanted to have a show that went more into 'How is the First Order gathering materials? What is the First Order doing before *The Force Awakens*?' It grew from that initial seed."[[17]](#cite_note-Setting_up_TFA-17) For the show's setting, Filoni was also inspired by the sitcom [*Cheers*](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cheers "wikipedia:Cheers"); executive producer [Athena Yvette Portillo](/wiki/Athena_Yvette_Portillo "Athena Yvette Portillo") explained this to [StarWars.com](/wiki/StarWars.com "StarWars.com"): "He said, 'I want a show that takes place in one set that you go to constantly. You get to know the patrons, you get to know the people, and you introduce new characters…and it's all about their way of life.'"[[13]](#cite_note-Final_Season-13)

*Resistance* begins six months before the [Battle of Starkiller Base](/wiki/Battle_of_Starkiller_Base "Battle of Starkiller Base"), and eventually crosses over with the events of [*Star Wars*: Episode VII *The Force Awakens*](/wiki/Star_Wars:_Episode_VII_The_Force_Awakens "Star Wars: Episode VII The Force Awakens").[[18]](#cite_note-Ep_7_Crossover-18) When the series began development, the creative team was unsure of the details of [*Star Wars*: Episode VIII *The Last Jedi*](/wiki/Star_Wars:_Episode_VIII_The_Last_Jedi "Star Wars: Episode VIII The Last Jedi") and [*Star Wars*: Episode IX *The Rise of Skywalker*](/wiki/Star_Wars:_Episode_IX_The_Rise_of_Skywalker "Star Wars: Episode IX The Rise of Skywalker"), so they had to be careful to avoid any possible continuity conflicts with those films.[[19]](#cite_note-Ep_9_Story_Gap-19) The show was planned to last only two seasons, so as to bridge the gap between the two films.[[20]](#cite_note-20)[[21]](#cite_note-21)

In [September](/wiki/September "September") [2016](/wiki/2016 "2016"), [Disney Television](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Disney%E2%80%93ABC_Television_Group "wikipedia:Disney–ABC Television Group") president [Ben Sherwood](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ben_Sherwood "wikipedia:Ben Sherwood") revealed that he had been working with Lucasfilm on a new animated series.[[22]](#cite_note-22) When the [third season](/wiki/Star_Wars_Rebels_Season_Three "Star Wars Rebels Season Three") of *Star Wars Rebels* debuted, it was announced that Filoni had stepped down as Supervising Director, in lieu of helping to develop "future animation development."[[23]](#cite_note-Big_Shiny_Robot-23) [Pablo Hidalgo](/wiki/Pablo_Hidalgo "Pablo Hidalgo") revealed that Filoni and crew were working on the new series as of [September](/wiki/September "September") [2017](/wiki/2017 "2017").[[24]](#cite_note-Details_surface-24) In [February](/wiki/February "February") [2018](/wiki/2018 "2018"), it was reported that [Lucasfilm](/wiki/Lucasfilm "Lucasfilm") filed a trademark for the name *Star Wars Resistance*[[25]](#cite_note-Filings-25)[[26]](#cite_note-Trademark-26) (rumors of such a series had stretched back to [2014](/wiki/2014 "2014")).[[27]](#cite_note-Rebels_Follow-Up-27) The series was officially announced on [April 26](/wiki/April_26 "April 26"), 2018 for a debut in the fall of that year.[[1]](#cite_note-New_Animated_Series-1) Despite creating the show, Filoni confirmed in an [August 12](/wiki/August_12 "August 12") interview that he would not be working as directly in a day-to-day capacity on *Resistance* as he had on previous projects, given his work on the [revival](/wiki/The_Clone_Wars:_Season_Seven "The Clone Wars: Season Seven") of *The Clone Wars*, but he would still provide notes and direction to the story team, a role Filoni compared to [George Lucas](/wiki/George_Lucas "George Lucas")' input on *The Clone Wars*.[[16]](#cite_note-Filoni_Not_To_Work_Day-To-Day-16) The show's animations were produced by [Polygon Pictures](/wiki/Polygon_Pictures "Polygon Pictures").[[28]](#cite_note-Polygon_Pictures-28) They are rendered in [CG](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Computer_animation "wikipedia:Computer animation") and based on the [anime](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Anime "wikipedia:Anime") animation style,[[29]](#cite_note-Anime_inspired-29)[[1]](#cite_note-New_Animated_Series-1) with major inspirations being [*Robotech*](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Robotech "wikipedia:Robotech"), [*Lupin the Third*](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Lupin_the_Third "wikipedia:Lupin the Third") and the films of [Hayao Miyazaki](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hayao_Miyazaki "wikipedia:Hayao Miyazaki").[[30]](#cite_note-Alive_and_Animated-30) Many of the show's designs are based on unused [original trilogy](/wiki/Original_trilogy "Original trilogy") concept sketches from artists such as [Joe Johnston](/wiki/Joe_Johnston "Joe Johnston"), [Ralph McQuarrie](/wiki/Ralph_McQuarrie "Ralph McQuarrie"), and [Nilo Rodis-Jamero](/wiki/Nilo_Rodis-Jamero "Nilo Rodis-Jamero").[[31]](#cite_note-SW-Designing_Star_Wars:_Star_Wars_Resistance-31) During production, the animated series was called "Zero."[[32]](#cite_note-Moore-32)

## Release ‡[]

*Star Wars Resistance* premiered on [October 7](/wiki/October_7 "October 7"), 2018.[[3]](#cite_note-Trailer-3) In the days leading up to the premiere, the show had a presence at the 2018 [New York Comic Con](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/New_York_Comic_Con "wikipedia:New York Comic Con"); a life-sized model of the *[Fireball](/wiki/Fireball "Fireball")* was present,[[33]](#cite_note-NYCC_Fireball-33) and [Hasbro](/wiki/Hasbro "Hasbro") released a [toy line](/wiki/Star_Wars_Resistance_(toy_line) "Star Wars Resistance (toy line)") based on the series.[[34]](#cite_note-NYCC_Hasbro-34)

On [August 4](/wiki/August_4 "August 4"), 2019, it was announced that the second season would premiere on [October 6](/wiki/October_6 "October 6"), 2019 when the Season 2 trailer was released. It was also revealed to be the final season of the show.[[2]](#cite_note-Season_2_trailer-2)

### Home video[]

The first season was released on [DVD](/wiki/Star_Wars_Resistance:_Complete_Season_One "Star Wars Resistance: Complete Season One") on [August 20](/wiki/August_20 "August 20"), 2019.[[35]](#cite_note-SW-Star_Wars_Resistance_Season_One_Coming_to_DVD_on_August_20-35)

Both seasons are available on the [Disney+](/wiki/Disney%2B "Disney+") streaming service. The first season was available at launch on [November 12](/wiki/November_12 "November 12"), 2019,[[36]](#cite_note-Disney+-36) and the second season became available on [February 25](/wiki/February_25 "February 25"), [2020](/wiki/2020 "2020").[[37]](#cite_note-37)

## Release and reception[]

In [2019](/wiki/2019 "2019"), the first season was nominated for an [Emmy](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Emmy_Award "wikipedia:Emmy Award") for "Outstanding Children's Programming,"[[38]](#cite_note-38) and won a [Saturn](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Saturn_Award "wikipedia:Saturn Award") for "Best Animated Television Series."[[39]](#cite_note-39) In [2020](/wiki/2020 "2020"), the second season was also nominated for an Emmy for "Outstanding Children's Program."[[40]](#cite_note-SW-The_Mandalorian_Nominated_for_15_Emmys-40)
