This **notability policy** describes criteria for determining the notability of articles on Wookieepedia, as accepted and approved by community consensus. Except for fan projects, which are covered on their own page at [Wookieepedia:Notability policy/Fan projects](/wiki/Wookieepedia:Notability_policy/Fan_projects "Wookieepedia:Notability policy/Fan projects"), any type of article not discussed below does not have fixed rules for determining notability; in such cases, notability is determined on a case-by-case basis.

## In-universe articles

### Aliases, nicknames, and pseudonyms

Articles on aliases, nicknames, and pseudonyms shall be merged with their main subjects (examples: [Arica](/wiki/Arica "Arica") is merged with [Mara Jade Skywalker](/wiki/Mara_Jade_Skywalker "Mara Jade Skywalker"); [The Negotiator](/wiki/The_Negotiator "The Negotiator") is merged with [Obi-Wan Kenobi](/wiki/Obi-Wan_Kenobi "Obi-Wan Kenobi"); *[Sunfighter Franchise](/wiki/Sunfighter_Franchise "Sunfighter Franchise")* is merged with [*Millennium Falcon*](/wiki/Millennium_Falcon/Legends "Millennium Falcon/Legends"))

* Exceptions are granted to aliases shared by multiple individuals (examples: [Fulcrum](/wiki/Fulcrum "Fulcrum"), [Blackhole](/wiki/Blackhole "Blackhole"), [Madame Atuarre's Roving Performers](/wiki/Madame_Atuarre%27s_Roving_Performers "Madame Atuarre's Roving Performers")) and subjects known only by an alias, nickname, or pseudonym (examples: [Jon Antilles](/wiki/Jon_Antilles "Jon Antilles"), [The Contessa](/wiki/The_Contessa "The Contessa"), [Rookie One](/wiki/Rookie_One "Rookie One")). Squadron [callsigns](/wiki/Callsign "Callsign") do not count as nicknames for the purposes of determining notability.

:   → See [Wookieepedia:Mofferences/November 7, 2020](/wiki/Wookieepedia:Mofferences/November_7,_2020 "Wookieepedia:Mofferences/November 7, 2020"), [Forum:CT:Codifying squadron callsign notability](/wiki/Forum:CT:Codifying_squadron_callsign_notability "Forum:CT:Codifying squadron callsign notability")

### Battle droids, clone troopers, and stormtroopers

Articles may only be granted to unidentified battle droids, clone troopers, and stormtroopers who play a significant plot role or who have significant dialogue. Simple exclamations (example: "Run for it!") and affirmations (examples: "Roger, roger."; "Yes, sir.") are not considered significant, whereas having more than one line of dialogue is considered notable.

:   → See [Wookieepedia:Mofferences/March 6, 2016](/wiki/Wookieepedia:Mofferences/March_6,_2016 "Wookieepedia:Mofferences/March 6, 2016") and [Forum:CT:"Unidentified" Notability policy amendment](/wiki/Forum:CT:%22Unidentified%22_Notability_policy_amendment "Forum:CT:\"Unidentified\" Notability policy amendment")

### Centuries and decades

Articles shall be granted to centuries and decades that are both explicitly named in source material and have at least one event assigned to them that does not have an explicitly identified date.

Examples:

* The [690s BBY](/wiki/690s_BBY "690s BBY"), [9400s BBY](/wiki/9400s_BBY "9400s BBY"), and [10,970s BBY](/wiki/10,970s_BBY "10,970s BBY") (all examples from *[The Essential Guide to Warfare](/wiki/The_Essential_Guide_to_Warfare "The Essential Guide to Warfare")* unless specified otherwise) shall all receive articles.
* "*The decade before the Battle of Yavin*," "*the century that began in 1100 BBY*," and "*the last century before the Battle of Yavin*" shall not receive articles.
* An event is specified to have happened in [7786 BBY](/wiki/7786_BBY "7786 BBY") ([source](https://www.starwars.com/news/the-essential-guide-to-warfare-authors-cut-part-4-the-rise-of-the-republic-needs-author-entry)), but other, unrelated events are also stated to have occurred in the [7700s BBY](/wiki/7700s_BBY "7700s BBY"); therefore, the latter shall receive an article.
* The term "3900s BBY" is exclusively used with respect to the timeframe of the [Mandalorian Wars](/wiki/Mandalorian_Wars "Mandalorian Wars"), and the conflict is also specifically stated to have taken place between [3976 BBY](/wiki/3976_BBY "3976 BBY") and [3960 BBY](/wiki/3960_BBY "3960 BBY"); therefore, the 3900s BBY shall not receive an article.

:   → See [Wookieepedia:Mofferences/February 27, 2021](/wiki/Wookieepedia:Mofferences/February_27,_2021 "Wookieepedia:Mofferences/February 27, 2021")

### Information from future products

In-universe articles on topics from future *Star Wars* products may only be created once the information is released through official outlets, such as [StarWars.com](/wiki/StarWars.com "StarWars.com") press releases or exclusive feature stories in outlets like [*Entertainment Weekly*](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Entertainment_Weekly "wikipedia:Entertainment Weekly") and [*Vanity Fair*](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Vanity_Fair_(magazine) "wikipedia:Vanity Fair (magazine)"), where Lucasfilm or an official licensee is expressly providing the information. This specifically disallows information that has been [leaked](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/News_leak "wikipedia:News leak") through unofficial channels.

:   → See [Forum:CT:In-universe information from future products#Vote 1](/wiki/Forum:CT:In-universe_information_from_future_products#Vote_1 "Forum:CT:In-universe information from future products")

### LEGO articles

[LEGO](/wiki/LEGO "LEGO") subpages are to be used only for subjects that were created for LEGO media and were subsequently mentioned in canonical media (example: [R0-GR](/wiki/R0-GR "R0-GR"), [Freemakers](/wiki/Freemakers "Freemakers"), [Pace Freemaker](/wiki/Pace_Freemaker "Pace Freemaker"), [Graballa](/wiki/Graballa "Graballa")). Characters and subjects that originated outside LEGO media will not be eligible for a LEGO subpage (example: [Lightsaber](/wiki/Lightsaber "Lightsaber"), [Blaster](/wiki/Blaster "Blaster"), [Luke Skywalker](/wiki/Luke_Skywalker "Luke Skywalker")).

:   → See [Forum:CT:LEGO Star Wars articles](/wiki/Forum:CT:LEGO_Star_Wars_articles "Forum:CT:LEGO Star Wars articles")

### New canon subjects

New canon subjects that share a name with a *Star Wars* Legends counterpart may be assumed to be the same as that Legends counterpart, particularly if the canon subject is not visually identified in canon source material. For example: [Mosep Binneed](/wiki/Mosep_Binneed "Mosep Binneed"), who appears in the canon short story "[The Ride](/wiki/The_Ride "The Ride")" but is not pictured, may be assumed to be the same [Mosep Binneed](/wiki/Mosep_Binneed/Legends "Mosep Binneed/Legends") visually identified by Legends source material.

This does not apply to:

* Individual starships that are visually indistinguishable. For example: most individual [*Imperial*-class Star Destroyers](/wiki/Imperial-class_Star_Destroyer "Imperial-class Star Destroyer") are visually indistinguishable. As such, the *[Adjudicator](/wiki/Adjudicator "Adjudicator")* should not be assumed to appear in [*The Empire Strikes Back*](/wiki/Star_Wars:_Episode_V_The_Empire_Strikes_Back "Star Wars: Episode V The Empire Strikes Back") or [*Return of the Jedi*](/wiki/Star_Wars:_Episode_VI_Return_of_the_Jedi "Star Wars: Episode VI Return of the Jedi") in canon, despite the Legends [*Adjudicator*](/wiki/Liberator_(Imperial_I-class) "Liberator (Imperial I-class)") appearing in these pieces of media.
* Appearances of [sectors](/wiki/Sector "Sector") or galactic regions such as the [Outer Rim Territories](/wiki/Outer_Rim_Territories "Outer Rim Territories").

:   → See [Forum:CT:Legends names in Canon](/wiki/Forum:CT:Legends_names_in_Canon "Forum:CT:Legends names in Canon")
:   → See [Forum:CT:Addition to new canon subjects notability policy regarding astro material](/wiki/Forum:CT:Addition_to_new_canon_subjects_notability_policy_regarding_astro_material "Forum:CT:Addition to new canon subjects notability policy regarding astro material")
:   → See [Forum:CT:Notability policy: Identical starships modification](/wiki/Forum:CT:Notability_policy:_Identical_starships_modification "Forum:CT:Notability policy: Identical starships modification")

### Planets

Planet articles may only be created based on maps from *[The Essential Atlas](/wiki/The_Essential_Atlas "The Essential Atlas")* if the *Atlas* or another source explicitly identifies the article subject as a planet. According to author [Jason Fry](/wiki/Jason_Fry "Jason Fry"), *The Essential Atlas* lists star systems rather than planets, so galactic locations highlighted on *Atlas* maps are not to be interpreted as planet names without aforementioned source confirmation.

:   → See [Forum:CT:Notability policy: Atlas planets](/wiki/Forum:CT:Notability_policy:_Atlas_planets "Forum:CT:Notability policy: Atlas planets")

### Sentient species

If an individual is of a unique and unnamed sentient species that does not appear elsewhere, that species should not receive an article; instead, the character's physical appearance should be described in the individual's article. Generic background characters should not receive species articles. However, if two or more characters are of the same unidentified species, that species may receive an article. Exceptions can be made for single-member species that are particularly notable.

A species must have one of the following to necessitate an article:

* A canonical name.
* Multiple members. For example: [Yoda's species](/wiki/Yoda%27s_species/Legends "Yoda's species/Legends") or [Eero Iridian's species](/wiki/Eero_Iridian%27s_species "Eero Iridian's species").
* Significant development/behind the scenes information, such as inspiration. For example: [Unidentified red pachydermoid species](/wiki/Unidentified_red_pachydermoid_species "Unidentified red pachydermoid species").
  + A species may only receive an article based on this clause if no corresponding character article exists for that species.
* A homeworld that has been specifically mentioned or appeared, and has been specifically identified as the species' homeworld.
* Significant backstory or accomplishments that have been directly attributed to the species. For example: [Those who came before](/wiki/Those_who_came_before "Those who came before").
* Significant description and identification as a unique species in dialogue. For example: [The Warlord's species](/wiki/The_Warlord%27s_species "The Warlord's species").
* For species in the current [canon](/wiki/Canon "Canon"), a [*Star Wars* Legends](/wiki/Star_Wars_Legends "Star Wars Legends") counterpart of matching visual appearance or description. Legends names for the species must still be used by a [canon source](/wiki/Wookieepedia:Canon_policy "Wookieepedia:Canon policy") before the name is considered canon.

For the following policies, there must also be a significant distinction indicating that the subject may not be the same as an existing species.

* An appearance in a live-action film or live-action series.
* An appearance in a 3D animated or stop motion animated project.
* An appearance in a 3D video game.

:   → See [Wookieepedia:Mofferences/January 26, 2014](/wiki/Wookieepedia:Mofferences/January_26,_2014 "Wookieepedia:Mofferences/January 26, 2014"), [Wookieepedia:Mofferences/March 6, 2016](/wiki/Wookieepedia:Mofferences/March_6,_2016 "Wookieepedia:Mofferences/March 6, 2016"), [Forum:CT:Notability policy amendment for recanonized species](/wiki/Forum:CT:Notability_policy_amendment_for_recanonized_species "Forum:CT:Notability policy amendment for recanonized species"), [Forum:CT:Live-action sentient species notability](/wiki/Forum:CT:Live-action_sentient_species_notability "Forum:CT:Live-action sentient species notability"), [Forum:CT:Various sentient species notability policy changes and updates](/wiki/Forum:CT:Various_sentient_species_notability_policy_changes_and_updates "Forum:CT:Various sentient species notability policy changes and updates")

### Star systems

Star systems that *[The Essential Atlas](/wiki/The_Essential_Atlas "The Essential Atlas")* and/or the [*Star Wars: The Essential Atlas* Online Companion](/wiki/Star_Wars:_The_Essential_Atlas_Online_Companion "Star Wars: The Essential Atlas Online Companion") have named after nebulae; star clusters; rogue planets; or any other non-standard, non-planetary astronomical location shall not have articles. Exceptions to this rule are systems named after space stations (example: [Azzameen Station system](/wiki/Azzameen_Station_system/Legends "Azzameen Station system/Legends")) and any star system that is clearly shown to exist in a source outside of *Atlas* material (example: [Red Nebula system](/wiki/Red_Nebula_system "Red Nebula system")).

:   → See [Forum:CT:Atlas Star Systems: Deviating from our Present Course](/wiki/Forum:CT:Atlas_Star_Systems:_Deviating_from_our_Present_Course "Forum:CT:Atlas Star Systems: Deviating from our Present Course")

### Wookieepedia is not a dictionary

[Shortcut](/wiki/Wookieepedia:Shortcut "Wookieepedia:Shortcut"):  
WP:DICT

Wookieepedia exists to catalog the *Star Wars* universe, but only within reason. We do not serve to list every item that has ever existed in *Star Wars* if a simple real-world dictionary definition would suffice. The [Trash compactor archives](/wiki/Category:Trash_compactor_archive "Category:Trash compactor archive") are littered with examples of overzealous article creation, and while we generally appreciate such enthusiasm, articles with an obvious real-world equivalent should generally only be created if they fall into a few major categories:

* The item possesses a distinct otherworldly quality. For example, we have **[shirts](/wiki/Shirt "Shirt")** on Earth, but our shirts don't go 'boop' and change color.
* The item's distinct lack of an otherworldly quality creates a unique incongruity. For example, in a universe with [bacta](/wiki/Bacta/Legends "Bacta/Legends") and [laser scalpels](/wiki/Laser_scalpel "Laser scalpel"), why does a child have **[braces](/wiki/Braces "Braces")**? And in a galaxy full of [blasters](/wiki/Blaster/Legends "Blaster/Legends"), it's interesting when one lands on a planet dominated by **[swords](/wiki/Sword/Legends "Sword/Legends")**.

Outside of such areas, items with real-world equivalents will normally be deleted upon creation via the speedy deletion process unless a compelling argument can rapidly be presented for their preservation.

## Real-world articles

### Film re-release products

Articles for film re-release products should only be created if the product contains new content, such as content changes or behind-the-scenes features. Any film re-release product without new content should be covered on the main article for that film.

:   → See [Forum:CT:Assorted policy shenanigans pt. 2](/wiki/Forum:CT:Assorted_policy_shenanigans_pt._2 "Forum:CT:Assorted policy shenanigans pt. 2")

### Future products

Articles about future *Star Wars* products may only be created if the products have been revealed by an official source. Examples of official sources include Lucasfilm, publishers, or mainstream media sites.

Internet marketplace and listing sites may only be used as independent confirmation if the listing is directly maintained by an official source. Sites that provide directly maintained listings include [Disney Books](https://books.disney.com), [Edelweiss+](https://www.edelweiss.plus), and [Penguin Random House](https://www.penguinrandomhouse.com/). Sites such as [Amazon](https://www.amazon.com) may only be used as independent confirmation if the listing was created by an official source (or a verified proxy) and not an independent third-party seller.

Listings by third-party sellers may only be used to confirm rare or limited-release products that cannot be otherwise identified by official sources (example: [*Star Wars Galactic Adventures*](/wiki/Star_Wars_Galactic_Adventures_(2016) "Star Wars Galactic Adventures (2016)")). In this case, the third-party listing must clearly demonstrate that the product is officially licensed, such as with identifiable close-up images.

### Canceled products

Canceled products may only receive articles if they are confirmed by official sources, such as Lucasfilm or mainstream media (example: *[Battle of the Sith Lords](/wiki/Battle_of_the_Sith_Lords "Battle of the Sith Lords")*). Authorial comments or portfolio content (example: concept art) may also be used as confirmation provided the source clearly demonstrates that the product was officially in development with Lucasfilm. Internet marketplace and listing sites may only be used as confirmation of canceled products if they fit the above listed criteria for future products.

If the canceled product is untitled, there must be substantive information available in order to necessitate an article. An article may not be created if the only known information is the product's author, publisher, release date, or [ISBN](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/International_Standard_Book_Number "wikipedia:International Standard Book Number"). If the product does not meet these criteria, it should instead be documented on the publisher's article or other relevant pages.

:   → See [Forum:CT:Amendment to the future/canceled products notability policy](/wiki/Forum:CT:Amendment_to_the_future/canceled_products_notability_policy "Forum:CT:Amendment to the future/canceled products notability policy")

### Merchandise

Wookieepedia does not maintain articles for general merchandise, which is defined as any officially-licensed [Lucasfilm](/wiki/Lucasfilm "Lucasfilm") *Star Wars* product that has no canonical value with respect to the franchise's [in-universe](/wiki/In-universe "In-universe") continuities. Merchandise items may be more appropriate for the [*Star Wars* Merchandise Wiki](https://swmerchandise.fandom.com/wiki/ "w:c:swmerchandise").

* Exceptions include [video games](/wiki/Star_Wars_video_games "Star Wars video games") and [board games](/wiki/Category:Real-world_board_games "Category:Real-world board games"). Wookieepedia covers all officially-licensed *Star Wars* video games and board games, even those without any clear canonical value, such as *[Star Wars Chess](/wiki/Star_Wars_Chess "Star Wars Chess")* and *[Star Wars Pinball](/wiki/Star_Wars_Pinball "Star Wars Pinball")*.

:   → See [Wookieepedia:Mofferences/March 4, 2018](/wiki/Wookieepedia:Mofferences/March_4,_2018 "Wookieepedia:Mofferences/March 4, 2018"), [Forum:CT:Notability of board games](/wiki/Forum:CT:Notability_of_board_games "Forum:CT:Notability of board games")

### Toys and toy lines

Toys such as action figures and [LEGOs](/wiki/LEGO "LEGO") should not receive articles for individual figures or sets but should instead be documented in articles for individual toy lines. These toy lines should be listed in the Sources section of in-universe articles. Toy lines should be listed in the Sources list using their respective citation templates, with each toy or set listed as a separate entry.

:   → See [Forum:CT:LEGOs](/wiki/Forum:CT:LEGOs "Forum:CT:LEGOs") and [Forum:CT:LEGOs, redux](/wiki/Forum:CT:LEGOs,_redux "Forum:CT:LEGOs, redux")

### Musical themes and motifs

Musical themes and motifs may only be granted articles if they meet at least one of the following criteria:

* They appear multiple times within a piece of media spread out between multiple scenes, or in multiple pieces of media, and are connected to a single subject (Example: [Shmi's motif](/wiki/Shmi%27s_motif "Shmi's motif")),
* They are specifically named, such as in the footer notes of a soundtrack booklet, sheet music book, or other official source (Example: "[The Arena](/wiki/The_Arena "The Arena")") (Note that some tracks also have pages in sheet music books but should still only receive articles if they represent a single composition), or
* They are composed as a concert suite that is included in official media (Example: "[Duel of the Fates](/wiki/Duel_of_the_Fates "Duel of the Fates")").

Note that articles are not to be granted to individual tracks on soundtracks. If a track shares a name with a musical theme, such as "[The Imperial March](/wiki/The_Imperial_March "The Imperial March")" on the [soundtrack](/wiki/Star_Wars:_Episode_V_The_Empire_Strikes_Back_Original_Soundtrack "Star Wars: Episode V The Empire Strikes Back Original Soundtrack") for [*Star Wars*: Episode V *The Empire Strikes Back*](/wiki/Star_Wars:_Episode_V_The_Empire_Strikes_Back "Star Wars: Episode V The Empire Strikes Back"), the article is based on the musical piece, not the track on the album. Motifs that are not specific to *Star Wars* but that are included in *Star Wars* music also do not receive articles, and mentions of these motifs should instead link to their Wikipedia article (Example: "[Dies irae](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dies_irae "wikipedia:Dies irae")").

:   → See [Forum:CT:Notability amendment for musical themes](/wiki/Forum:CT:Notability_amendment_for_musical_themes "Forum:CT:Notability amendment for musical themes")

### Non-copyrighted products

Articles for products or web articles explicitly labeled as not endorsed, authorized, licensed by, or affiliated with Lucasfilm should not be created unless they otherwise qualify under the [notability guidelines for fan projects](/wiki/Wookieepedia:Notability_policy/Fan_projects "Wookieepedia:Notability policy/Fan projects"). Examples of products that do not qualify for individual articles include unlicensed books such as [*George Lucas: A Life*](/wiki/Forum:TC:George_Lucas:_A_Life "Forum:TC:George Lucas: A Life") and [*A Galaxy Far, Far Away: Exploring Star Wars Comics*](/wiki/Forum:TC:A_Galaxy_Far,_Far_Away:_Exploring_Star_Wars_Comics "Forum:TC:A Galaxy Far, Far Away: Exploring Star Wars Comics"), which instead utilize {{[BookCite](/wiki/Template:BookCite "Template:BookCite")}} to document their content.

:   → See [Forum:CT:Non-copyrighted projects](/wiki/Forum:CT:Non-copyrighted_projects "Forum:CT:Non-copyrighted projects"), [Forum:CT:Notability - non-copyrighted tweak](/wiki/Forum:CT:Notability_-_non-copyrighted_tweak "Forum:CT:Notability - non-copyrighted tweak"), [Forum:CT:Policies and "guidelines" review](/wiki/Forum:CT:Policies_and_%22guidelines%22_review "Forum:CT:Policies and \"guidelines\" review")

### Individuals with an eponymously named subject

Articles may be created for real-world individuals for whom in-universe subjects were named in tribute if there is a known story expanding on the event of the naming.

:   → See [Forum:CT:Named-after individuals](/wiki/Forum:CT:Named-after_individuals "Forum:CT:Named-after individuals")

### Devices and services

Wookieepedia does not maintain articles for third-party devices and services, including phones, tablets, computers, operating systems, video game consoles and peripherals, as well as traditional and digital distribution platforms.

:   → See [Forum:CT:Device and console notability](/wiki/Forum:CT:Device_and_console_notability "Forum:CT:Device and console notability")
