***Star Wars* Legends**, formerly known as the **Expanded Universe** (abbreviated **EU**), encompasses every one of the licensed and background stories of the *[Star Wars](/wiki/Star_Wars "Star Wars")* universe, outside of the original six *Star Wars* films produced by [George Lucas](/wiki/George_Lucas "George Lucas") and certain other material created before [April 25](/wiki/April_25 "April 25"), [2014](/wiki/2014 "2014"), excluding the animated series *[Star Wars: The Clone Wars](/wiki/Star_Wars:_The_Clone_Wars "Star Wars: The Clone Wars")*. It is derived from and includes most official *Star Wars* [books](/wiki/Category:Real-world_books "Category:Real-world books"), [comic books](/wiki/Category:Comics "Category:Comics"), [video games](/wiki/Category:Computer_and_video_games "Category:Computer and video games"), [spin-off films](/wiki/Category:Television_films "Category:Television films"), [television series](/wiki/Category:Television_series "Category:Television series"), toys, and other media created before that date. This material expands and continues the stories told in the films, [taking place](/wiki/Timeline_of_galactic_history/Legends "Timeline of galactic history/Legends") anywhere from over 36,000 years before [*The Phantom Menace*](/wiki/Star_Wars:_Episode_I_The_Phantom_Menace "Star Wars: Episode I The Phantom Menace") to 136 years after [*Return of the Jedi*](/wiki/Star_Wars:_Episode_VI_Return_of_the_Jedi "Star Wars: Episode VI Return of the Jedi").

On April 25, 2014, [Lucasfilm](/wiki/Lucasfilm "Lucasfilm") announced that in preparation for the upcoming [sequel trilogy](/wiki/Sequel_trilogy "Sequel trilogy"), the Expanded Universe would be rebranded as Legends and no longer adhered to; past tales of the Expanded Universe would be printed under the *Star Wars* Legends banner, and a new continuity would be established that consisted only of the original six films, *Star Wars: The Clone Wars*, and all future material from that point onward. Though the majority of the Expanded Universe media was not brought into the new canon, it still remains a resource for future *Star Wars* materials to reference, thus bringing these older elements into the new continuity.[[2]](#cite_note-Legends-2)[[3]](#cite_note-Heddle-3) Today, some of the only Legends products that are still being released are the video game *[Star Wars: The Old Republic](/wiki/Star_Wars:_The_Old_Republic "Star Wars: The Old Republic")*, along with short stories published on the *[Star Wars](/wiki/Star_Wars "Star Wars")* blog.

The Expanded Universe had a continuity with few wrinkles. The general rule was that nothing in the Expanded Universe was allowed to contradict any other part of the Expanded Universe or the films. For the most part, [retcons](/wiki/Retroactive_continuity "Retroactive continuity") were created to fix contradictions when any discrepancies arose.

The Expanded Universe is actually older than the films themselves, as the [novelization of the original film](/wiki/Star_Wars:_A_New_Hope_(novelization) "Star Wars: A New Hope (novelization)") was published six months before the film was released. In [in-universe](/wiki/In-universe "In-universe") chronology, the earliest works are the [*Dawn of the Jedi*](/wiki/Star_Wars:_Dawn_of_the_Jedi "Star Wars: Dawn of the Jedi") comics, which are set millennia before the films, while the latest are the [*Legacy*](/wiki/Star_Wars:_Legacy "Star Wars: Legacy") comics, which are set about one hundred and thirty years after *Return of the Jedi*.

## History

:   "*The premise of all the comic books, novels, games, and other spin-off works is that they all work chronologically, that the continuity forms one unbroken story. […] Since our movies have their own internal continuity, we maintain that in the spin-off works. Technically, George Lucas has been doing continuity all along by mapping out the nine films. But it wasn't until 1991, when Timothy Zahn wrote the novel* Heir to the Empire*, the first* Star Wars *best-seller, which was the beginning of what we call the* Star Wars *renaissance, that continuity became an issue.*"
:   ―Lucasfilm continuity editor [Allan Kausch](/wiki/Allan_Kausch "Allan Kausch"), 1996[[4]](#cite_note-The_Secrets_of_Star_Wars:_Shadows_of_the_Empire-4)

### Early years

The early development of the Expanded Universe was sporadic and unrefined, in large part because, at this time, there was so little [canon](/wiki/Canon "Canon") material for the creators to use as reference.

The Expanded Universe is generally considered to have begun with [Alan Dean Foster](/wiki/Alan_Dean_Foster "Alan Dean Foster")'s February 1978 *Star Wars* spin-off novel, *[Splinter of the Mind's Eye](/wiki/Splinter_of_the_Mind%27s_Eye "Splinter of the Mind's Eye")*, although technically it began in October 1977 with the story "[The Keeper's World](/wiki/The_Keeper%27s_World "The Keeper's World")" in [Marvel Comics](/wiki/Marvel_Comics "Marvel Comics")' *[Pizzazz](/wiki/Pizzazz "Pizzazz")* magazine. *Splinter* drew inspiration primarily from an early draft of the *Star Wars* script. Although [George Lucas](/wiki/George_Lucas "George Lucas")'s name is on the cover of the original *Star Wars* novelization, Alan Dean Foster [ghostwrote](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ghostwriter "wikipedia:Ghostwriter") it. Foster was given a copy of the working script and a tour of the production.[[5]](#cite_note-legendaryauthors-5)

Much of the early Legends material from the early 1980s contained analogies to the real world, which belied the impression that the *Star Wars* universe had no connection to Earth or our own time.

### Development

:   "*Over the years, many artists and designers have contributed to the articulation of the various universes of Lucasfilm. Taking their cues from the minimal words of description on a script page, these talented men and women have sketched, drawn and/or modeled creatures of magnificent breadth, unimaginable terror, and mind-boggling eccentricity. Some of these creatures have made it into film, while others, because of the way stories unravel, have not (so far). But this does not mean they do not exist. For once something is created, no matter what the context, it takes on a life of its own.*"
:   ―Foreword written by George Lucas in *[Monsters and Aliens from George Lucas](/wiki/Monsters_and_Aliens_from_George_Lucas "Monsters and Aliens from George Lucas")*[[6]](#cite_note-Monsters_and_Aliens_from_George_Lucas-6)

 [![ introduced a new threat called the  to the saga.](data:image/gif;base64,R0lGODlhAQABAIABAAAAAP///yH5BAEAAAEALAAAAAABAAEAQAICTAEAOw%3D%3D)](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/starwars/images/a/ab/NJO-VectorPrime.png/revision/latest?cb=20200304021120) [![ introduced a new threat called the  to the saga.](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/starwars/images/a/ab/NJO-VectorPrime.png/revision/latest/scale-to-width-down/150?cb=20200304021120)](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/starwars/images/a/ab/NJO-VectorPrime.png/revision/latest?cb=20200304021120) 

[*Vector Prime*](/wiki/The_New_Jedi_Order:_Vector_Prime "The New Jedi Order: Vector Prime") introduced a new threat called the [Yuuzhan Vong](/wiki/Yuuzhan_Vong "Yuuzhan Vong") to the saga.

 

A turning point was reached when [West End Games](/wiki/West_End_Games "West End Games") began publishing the *[Star Wars: The Roleplaying Game](/wiki/Star_Wars:_The_Roleplaying_Game "Star Wars: The Roleplaying Game")* in [1987](/wiki/1987 "1987"). In order for players of the [roleplaying game](/wiki/Roleplaying_game "Roleplaying game") to create new adventures, West End Games needed to provide supplemental material describing the *Star Wars* universe in previously unknown detail and to make it self-consistent and coherent. As an example, the [Aurebesh](/wiki/Aurebesh/Legends "Aurebesh/Legends") alphabet was originally a random piece of set dressing used in *Return of the Jedi*. [Stephen Crane](/wiki/Stephen_Crane "Stephen Crane") copied those symbols and turned them into a complete and workable alphabet which would later be used in the [prequel trilogy](/wiki/Prequel_trilogy "Prequel trilogy"). Developing and extrapolating from details like this in a consistent fashion turned West End Games' *Star Wars* products into a de facto reference library for other developers of the EU.

Around the same time, [Dark Horse Comics](/wiki/Dark_Horse_Comics "Dark Horse Comics") acquired the *Star Wars* license previously owned by Marvel and used it to launch a number of ambitious sequels to the [original trilogy](/wiki/Original_trilogy "Original trilogy"), which began with the popular *[Star Wars: Dark Empire](/wiki/Star_Wars:_Dark_Empire "Star Wars: Dark Empire")* series.

At the same time as *Dark Empire*'s release in the early 1990s, [Bantam Spectra](/wiki/Bantam_Spectra "Bantam Spectra") published Timothy Zahn's [*The Thrawn Trilogy*](/wiki/Star_Wars:_The_Thrawn_Trilogy "Star Wars: The Thrawn Trilogy"). Widely publicized as the "sequels which were never made," Zahn's novels reignited *Star Wars* fandom and sparked a revolution in *Star Wars* literature.

All this development began to feed back and reference itself and create cross-connections. West End Games produced roleplaying supplements based upon Dark Horse's comics and Zahn's novels. Novelists and comic creators used West End Games' supplements as reference material. Sequels to the novels were being published as comics and vice versa, and the scope of the Expanded Universe grew at a prodigious rate.

At that point, the bulk of the Expanded Universe has detailed the *Star Wars* universe after the end of *Return of the Jedi*, as numerous topics, including the rise of the Galactic Empire, the personal histories of [Anakin Skywalker](/wiki/Anakin_Skywalker/Legends "Anakin Skywalker/Legends") and Emperor [Palpatine](/wiki/Palpatine/Legends "Palpatine/Legends"), and the [Clone Wars](/wiki/Clone_Wars/Legends "Clone Wars/Legends") had been declared off-limits by George Lucas prior to the development of his prequel trilogy and related material.

It was decided in the late '90s that using the [Empire](/wiki/Galactic_Empire/Legends "Galactic Empire/Legends") as the [villains](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Villain "wikipedia:Villain") had become repetitive and monotonous. A new threat, the [Yuuzhan Vong](/wiki/Yuuzhan_Vong "Yuuzhan Vong"), was introduced in the [New Jedi Order](/wiki/Star_Wars:_The_New_Jedi_Order "Star Wars: The New Jedi Order") series. Specifically, the Yuuzhan Vong first appeared in the first New Jedi Order book, *[The New Jedi Order: Vector Prime](/wiki/The_New_Jedi_Order:_Vector_Prime "The New Jedi Order: Vector Prime")*.

### Legends and the Prequels

Prior to the release of [*The Phantom Menace*](/wiki/Star_Wars:_Episode_I_The_Phantom_Menace "Star Wars: Episode I The Phantom Menace"), Lucasfilm specifically prohibited development of the decades prior to *A New Hope* in the Expanded Universe. The release of Episode I, however, opened an entire new era of the series to explore.

 [![Heir to the Empire Legends cover](data:image/gif;base64,R0lGODlhAQABAIABAAAAAP///yH5BAEAAAEALAAAAAABAAEAQAICTAEAOw%3D%3D)](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/starwars/images/8/83/HTTE-Legends.png/revision/latest?cb=20140812233828) [![Heir to the Empire Legends cover](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/starwars/images/8/83/HTTE-Legends.png/revision/latest/scale-to-width-down/150?cb=20140812233828)](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/starwars/images/8/83/HTTE-Legends.png/revision/latest?cb=20140812233828) 

*Heir to the Empire* Legends cover

 

Since *The Phantom Menace* was set in a time of peace, most material that built on *The Phantom Menace* was set either before or during the film, rather than after.

[*Attack of the Clones*](/wiki/Star_Wars:_Episode_II_Attack_of_the_Clones "Star Wars: Episode II Attack of the Clones"), on the other hand, finally delved into the long-anticipated Clone Wars conflict first mentioned in *A New Hope*. *Attack of the Clones*' release led to a massive emergence of new content in Legends, most notable among them are the comic book series, [Star Wars: Republic](/wiki/Star_Wars:_Republic "Star Wars: Republic"), and a TV micro-series called *[Star Wars: Clone Wars](/wiki/Star_Wars:_Clone_Wars "Star Wars: Clone Wars")*, both of which built up to the release of [*Revenge of the Sith*](/wiki/Star_Wars:_Episode_III_Revenge_of_the_Sith "Star Wars: Episode III Revenge of the Sith"). In *Star Wars: Clone Wars*, produced and directed by [Genndy Tartakovsky](/wiki/Genndy_Tartakovsky "Genndy Tartakovsky"), many battles of the titular war are shown, with the Force's power displayed in an exaggerated fashion reminiscent of Tartakovsky's previous work. The second season of the series concludes by introducing [General](/wiki/General/Legends "General/Legends") [Grievous](/wiki/Grievous/Legends "Grievous/Legends"), one of the main antagonists in the upcoming *Revenge of the Sith*. The third and final season is set in the days leading up until the very beginning of *Revenge of the Sith*. Meanwhile, *Star Wars: Republic* supplemented the show, filling in various empty spaces *Clone Wars* left while providing more context for all three prequel movies.

Following the release of *Revenge of the Sith*, events between the two trilogies were elaborated upon, such as the [Great Jedi Purge](/wiki/Great_Jedi_Purge/Legends "Great Jedi Purge/Legends"). Notable content released in this period that was set between Episodes III and IV include *[Star Wars: Dark Times](/wiki/Star_Wars:_Dark_Times "Star Wars: Dark Times")*, a sequel comic series to *Republic* that follows in the aftermath of Episode III, as well as the multimedia project *[Star Wars: The Force Unleashed](/wiki/Star_Wars:_The_Force_Unleashed "Star Wars: The Force Unleashed")*, which starred Darth Vader's secret apprentice, [Galen Marek](/wiki/Galen_Marek "Galen Marek"), and explored the early days of the [Rebel Alliance](/wiki/Alliance_to_Restore_the_Republic "Alliance to Restore the Republic").

### Legends

On [April 25](/wiki/April_25 "April 25"), [2014](/wiki/2014 "2014"), [Lucasfilm](/wiki/Lucasfilm "Lucasfilm") announced that the Expanded Universe was being reorganized under the new non-canon "*Star Wars* Legends" banner to make way for a new line of continuity, led by principal projects *[Star Wars Rebels](/wiki/Star_Wars_Rebels "Star Wars Rebels")* and the *Star Wars* [sequel trilogy](/wiki/Sequel_trilogy "Sequel trilogy"), to take shape. Certain previously published Expanded Universe material remained in print as Legends stories.[[2]](#cite_note-Legends-2) The first novels reprinted under the Legends banner included *[Heir to the Empire](/wiki/Heir_to_the_Empire "Heir to the Empire")*, [*The Han Solo Adventures*](/wiki/The_Han_Solo_Adventures_(omnibus) "The Han Solo Adventures (omnibus)"), [*The Lando Calrissian Adventures*](/wiki/The_Lando_Calrissian_Adventures_(omnibus) "The Lando Calrissian Adventures (omnibus)"), [*Crucible*](/wiki/Crucible_(novel) "Crucible (novel)"), [*Kenobi*](/wiki/Kenobi_(novel) "Kenobi (novel)"), *[Razor's Edge](/wiki/Razor%27s_Edge "Razor's Edge")*, *[Death Troopers](/wiki/Death_Troopers "Death Troopers")*,[[7]](#cite_note-Insider_150:_Launch_Pad-7) *[Fate of the Jedi: Outcast](/wiki/Fate_of_the_Jedi:_Outcast "Fate of the Jedi: Outcast")*, *[Maul: Lockdown](/wiki/Maul:_Lockdown "Maul: Lockdown")*, [*Lost Tribe of the Sith: The Collected Stories*](/wiki/Star_Wars:_Lost_Tribe_of_the_Sith:_The_Collected_Stories "Star Wars: Lost Tribe of the Sith: The Collected Stories"), and the *[Star Wars: Lives & Adventures](/wiki/Star_Wars:_Lives_%26_Adventures "Star Wars: Lives & Adventures")* compendium.

While new material released on or after April 25, 2014 is generally considered canon, a certain amount of new material has continued to be released under the *Star Wars* Legends brand. This has included such material as the concluding comics in the [*Star Wars* comic series](/wiki/Star_Wars_(2013) "Star Wars (2013)") by Dark Horse Comics and the final issues of the series [*Star Wars: Legacy* Volume 2](/wiki/Star_Wars:_Legacy_Volume_2 "Star Wars: Legacy Volume 2")[[8]](#cite_note-Legacy2-8) the *[Star Wars: Rebel Heist](/wiki/Star_Wars:_Rebel_Heist "Star Wars: Rebel Heist")* miniseries; *[Star Wars: Darth Maul—Son of Dathomir](/wiki/Star_Wars:_Darth_Maul%E2%80%94Son_of_Dathomir "Star Wars: Darth Maul—Son of Dathomir")* miniseries (which applies to both Legends and Canon continuities[[9]](#cite_note-SoD_status-9)); comic strips published in the concluding volumes of *[Star Wars Comic UK](/wiki/Star_Wars_Comic_UK "Star Wars Comic UK")*; early supplements for [Fantasy Flight Games](/wiki/Fantasy_Flight_Games "Fantasy Flight Games")' roleplaying system and *[Star Wars: Imperial Handbook: A Commander's Guide](/wiki/Star_Wars:_Imperial_Handbook:_A_Commander%27s_Guide "Star Wars: Imperial Handbook: A Commander's Guide")*,[[10]](#cite_note-ImperialHandbook-10) and tie-in short stories for the video game *[Star Wars: The Old Republic](/wiki/Star_Wars:_The_Old_Republic "Star Wars: The Old Republic")*, which were published online. Most of those, however, ceased publication by the end of 2014.

In [2015](/wiki/2015 "2015"), the novella *[SkyeWalkers: A Clone Wars Story](/wiki/SkyeWalkers:_A_Clone_Wars_Story "SkyeWalkers: A Clone Wars Story")* was released on [StarWars.com](/wiki/StarWars.com "StarWars.com").

On [May 29](/wiki/May_29 "May 29"), [2019](/wiki/2019 "2019"), the original [*Star Wars* series](/wiki/Star_Wars_(1977) "Star Wars (1977)") by Marvel Comics—which is set in the Legends continuity—received a [one-shot continuation issue](/wiki/Star_Wars_(1977)_108 "Star Wars (1977) 108") in commemoration of the company's 80th anniversary.[[11]](#cite_note-108_comic-11)

As of [2024](/wiki/2024 "2024"), the only new Legends media being continually released is the video game *[Star Wars: The Old Republic](/wiki/Star_Wars:_The_Old_Republic "Star Wars: The Old Republic")* with its constant updates and expansions.

## Story eras

:   ![Blue Glass Arrow](data:image/gif;base64,R0lGODlhAQABAIABAAAAAP///yH5BAEAAAEALAAAAAABAAEAQAICTAEAOw%3D%3D) *See also: [List of publishing eras](/wiki/List_of_publishing_eras "List of publishing eras")*

* **Before the Republic (37,000 BBY – 25,000 BBY)**

In the era before the Galactic Republic, the Je'daii Order first discovers the Force on the planet Tython, and works to better understand the mystical energy. They struggle to retain balance in the Force, and come into conflict with the marauding [Rakata](/wiki/Rakata/Legends "Rakata/Legends") species.

* **The Old Republic (25,000 BBY – 1,000 BBY)**

The Old Republic was the government that united the *Star Wars* galaxy under the rule of the [Galactic Senate](/wiki/Galactic_Senate/Legends "Galactic Senate/Legends"). In this era, the Jedi are numerous, and serve as guardians of peace and justice. The *Tales of the Jedi* comics series takes place in this era, chronicling the immense wars fought by the Jedi of old, and the ancient Sith.

 [![The light spacetrooper is one of the many Legends characters of the Galactic Empire.](data:image/gif;base64,R0lGODlhAQABAIABAAAAAP///yH5BAEAAAEALAAAAAABAAEAQAICTAEAOw%3D%3D)](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/starwars/images/f/f1/Spacetrooper.png/revision/latest?cb=20160804213339) [![The light spacetrooper is one of the many Legends characters of the Galactic Empire.](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/starwars/images/f/f1/Spacetrooper.png/revision/latest/scale-to-width-down/100?cb=20160804213339)](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/starwars/images/f/f1/Spacetrooper.png/revision/latest?cb=20160804213339) 

The light spacetrooper is one of the many Legends characters of the Galactic Empire.

 

* **The Rise of the Empire (1,000 BBY – 0 BBY)**

After the seemingly final defeat of the Sith, the Republic enters a state of complacency. In the waning years of the Republic, the senate was rife with corruption and scandal, and saddled with a bureaucracy so immense that effective governing was nearly impossible. The ambitious Senator Palpatine caused himself to be elected Supreme Chancellor, and promised to reunite the galaxy under a New Order. The prequel trilogy takes place during this era.

* **The Rebellion (0 BBY – 5 ABY)**

An outcry of resistance begins to spread across the galaxy in protest to the new Empire's tyranny. Cells of Rebellion fight back, and the [Galactic Civil War](/wiki/Galactic_Civil_War/Legends "Galactic Civil War/Legends") begins. This era begins with the Rebel victory that secured the Death Star plans, and ends a year after the death of the Emperor high over the forest moon of Endor. The Rebellion starts to reform itself into a body of government, first as the Alliance of Free Planets, and later the New Republic. The original trilogy takes place during this era.

* **The New Republic (5 ABY – 25 ABY)**

Having defeated the Empire at the [Battle of Endor](/wiki/Battle_of_Endor/Legends "Battle of Endor/Legends"), the [Rebel Alliance](/wiki/Alliance_to_Restore_the_Republic/Legends "Alliance to Restore the Republic/Legends") must now transform itself from a militant resistance force into a functioning galactic government. As Imperial territory is reclaimed, the [New Republic](/wiki/New_Republic/Legends "New Republic/Legends") suffers growing pains, having to fend off insurrections, Imperial loyalists, and wayward warlords. Also, Luke Skywalker, the last of the Jedi, begins training apprentices, rebuilding the Jedi order.

* **The New Jedi Order (25 ABY – 36 ABY)**

The Jedi Knights are now a hundred strong. The New Republic has signed a peace treaty with what little remains of the Empire. The galaxy is finally enjoying a peaceful respite from decades of war. It's at this time that a horrible alien menace invades the Republic from beyond known space. The [Yuuzhan Vong](/wiki/Yuuzhan_Vong "Yuuzhan Vong") lay waste to entire worlds in their scourge, as depicted in the novels of [*The New Jedi Order*](/wiki/Star_Wars:_The_New_Jedi_Order "Star Wars: The New Jedi Order"). Five years later the galaxy goes through the events of *[Star Wars: The Dark Nest Trilogy](/wiki/Star_Wars:_The_Dark_Nest_Trilogy "Star Wars: The Dark Nest Trilogy")*. The novels detail how Luke Skywalker and his New Jedi Order confront the mysterious insectoid Killiks, who are a hive-minded species intent on conquering the galaxy.

* **Legacy (40 ABY – 140+ ABY)**

Having reached peace with the Yuuzhan Vong, the newly formed [Galactic Federation of Free Alliances](/wiki/Galactic_Federation_of_Free_Alliances "Galactic Federation of Free Alliances") struggles to keep itself working as a single government. But many threats from inside are joined by a danger that comes from the remains of the [dark side](/wiki/Dark_side_of_the_Force/Legends "Dark side of the Force/Legends"). The new Jedi order created by Luke Skywalker faces a new era as the heirs of the Skywalker legacy grow up. [Jacen Solo](/wiki/Darth_Caedus "Darth Caedus"), perhaps the wisest of that new order, now [Ben Skywalker](/wiki/Ben_Skywalker "Ben Skywalker")'s master, falls to the Dark Side and attempts to create a new empire from the squabbling systems that form the GA. In the [*Fate of the Jedi*](/wiki/Star_Wars:_Fate_of_the_Jedi "Star Wars: Fate of the Jedi") series, Luke and Ben travel the galaxy to determine the causes of Jacen's descent into evil, while [Abeloth](/wiki/Abeloth "Abeloth") and the [Keshiri](/wiki/Lost_Tribe_of_Sith "Lost Tribe of Sith") Sith attempt to take over the galaxy. The Legacy era continues hundreds of years later in a series of comics that debuted in June 2006 entitled *[Star Wars: Legacy](/wiki/Star_Wars:_Legacy "Star Wars: Legacy")*.

## Works

:   "*The analogy is that every piece of published* Star Wars *fiction is a window into the 'real'* Star Wars *universe. Some windows are a bit foggier than others. Some are decidedly abstract. But each contains a nugget of truth to them. Like the great Jedi Knight Obi-Wan Kenobi said, 'many of the truths we cling to depend greatly on our own point of view.'*"

* ***[The Star Wars Holiday Special](/wiki/The_Star_Wars_Holiday_Special "The Star Wars Holiday Special")*** ([1978](/wiki/1978 "1978")) was a two-hour [television](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Television "wikipedia:Television") special portraying [Chewbacca's](/wiki/Chewbacca/Legends "Chewbacca/Legends") return to his home planet of [Kashyyyk](/wiki/Kashyyyk/Legends "Kashyyyk/Legends") to celebrate [Life Day](/wiki/Life_Day/Legends "Life Day/Legends") with his family. Along with the stars of the original 1977 movie, such TV and music stars as [Beatrice Arthur](/wiki/Bea_Arthur "Bea Arthur"), [Art Carney](/wiki/Art_Carney "Art Carney") and [Jefferson Starship](/wiki/Jefferson_Starship "Jefferson Starship") appeared in plot-related skits and musical numbers. The content was considered canonical within the continuity of the Expanded Universe, but the special is reviled by some fans and virtually disowned by George Lucas, though other fans enjoy its nostalgic sweetness and naively misguided creativity; an online petition for its video release has gotten press in [*New York Newsday*](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Newsday "wikipedia:Newsday") and other media outlets. The *Holiday Special* features the first appearance of [bounty hunter](/wiki/Bounty_hunter/Legends "Bounty hunter/Legends") [Boba Fett](/wiki/Boba_Fett/Legends "Boba Fett/Legends"), in an 11-minute animated sequence, and the first reference to Kashyyyk. The general look of the Kashyyyk sets from the Holiday Special formed the basis for the settings used in [*Revenge of the Sith*](/wiki/Star_Wars:_Episode_III_Revenge_of_the_Sith "Star Wars: Episode III Revenge of the Sith") (2005).
* ***[Caravan of Courage: An Ewok Adventure](/wiki/Caravan_of_Courage:_An_Ewok_Adventure "Caravan of Courage: An Ewok Adventure")*** ([1984](/wiki/1984 "1984")) was the first of two films featuring the [Ewoks](/wiki/Ewok/Legends "Ewok/Legends") from *Return of the Jedi*. In *Caravan of Courage*, the Ewoks help two children rescue their parents from a giant known as [Gorax](/wiki/Gorax/Legends "Gorax/Legends"). This and the next film are notable for having their stories written by Lucas himself, one of his few contributions to non-theatrical *Star Wars* productions, other than his obvious sanctioning of them.
* ***[Ewoks: The Battle for Endor](/wiki/Ewoks:_The_Battle_for_Endor "Ewoks: The Battle for Endor")*** ([1985](/wiki/1985 "1985")). In this second Ewok film, [Wicket](/wiki/Wicket_Wystri_Warrick/Legends "Wicket Wystri Warrick/Legends"), [Cindel](/wiki/Cindel_Towani "Cindel Towani"), and the Ewoks ally with a hermit named [Noa](/wiki/Noa_Briqualon "Noa Briqualon") to defeat [Marauders](/wiki/Sanyassan_Marauders "Sanyassan Marauders") who attacked their village.
* ***[Star Wars: Droids: The Adventures of R2-D2 and C-3PO](/wiki/Star_Wars:_Droids:_The_Adventures_of_R2-D2_and_C-3PO "Star Wars: Droids: The Adventures of R2-D2 and C-3PO")*** (1985–[1986](/wiki/1986 "1986")) was an animated series following the adventures of [C-3PO](/wiki/C-3PO/Legends "C-3PO/Legends") and [R2-D2](/wiki/R2-D2/Legends "R2-D2/Legends") between *Revenge of the Sith* and *A New Hope*. It featured [Anthony Daniels](/wiki/Anthony_Daniels "Anthony Daniels") as the voice of C-3PO.
* **[*Ewoks*](/wiki/Ewoks_(television_series) "Ewoks (television series)")** (1985–1987) was an animated series featuring the adventures of the Ewoks prior to *Return of the Jedi*.
* ***[Star Wars: Clone Wars](/wiki/Star_Wars:_Clone_Wars "Star Wars: Clone Wars")*** ([2003](/wiki/2003 "2003")–2005) aired on the [Cartoon Network](/wiki/Cartoon_Network "Cartoon Network") and depicted events between *Attack of the Clones* and *Revenge of the Sith*. The series introduced the characters of [General](/wiki/General/Legends "General/Legends") [Grievous](/wiki/Grievous/Legends "Grievous/Legends") and [Asajj Ventress](/wiki/Asajj_Ventress/Legends "Asajj Ventress/Legends") and received an [Emmy Award](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Emmy_Award "wikipedia:Emmy Award").

### Radio and audio drama

:   ![Blue Glass Arrow](data:image/gif;base64,R0lGODlhAQABAIABAAAAAP///yH5BAEAAAEALAAAAAABAAEAQAICTAEAOw%3D%3D) *See also: [Star Wars radio dramas](/wiki/Star_Wars_radio_dramas "Star Wars radio dramas") and [List of Legends audio productions](/wiki/List_of_Legends_audio_productions "List of Legends audio productions")*

 [![Promotional poster for ](data:image/gif;base64,R0lGODlhAQABAIABAAAAAP///yH5BAEAAAEALAAAAAABAAEAQAICTAEAOw%3D%3D)](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/starwars/images/c/cd/Esb-radio.png/revision/latest?cb=20101029221105) [![Promotional poster for ](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/starwars/images/c/cd/Esb-radio.png/revision/latest/scale-to-width-down/200?cb=20101029221105)](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/starwars/images/c/cd/Esb-radio.png/revision/latest?cb=20101029221105) 

Promotional poster for [*The Empire Strikes Back* radio drama](/wiki/The_Empire_Strikes_Back_(radio) "The Empire Strikes Back (radio)")

 

A [radio adaptation of *A New Hope*](/wiki/Star_Wars_(radio) "Star Wars (radio)") was first broadcast on [National Public Radio](/wiki/National_Public_Radio "National Public Radio") in [1981](/wiki/1981 "1981"). The adaptation was written by [science fiction](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Science_fiction "wikipedia:Science fiction") author [Brian Daley](/wiki/Brian_Daley "Brian Daley") and directed by [John Madden](/wiki/John_Madden "John Madden"), with post-production led by [Tom Voegeli](/wiki/Tom_Voegeli "Tom Voegeli"). Daley, Madden and Voegeli collaborated to adapt the second installment of the trilogy, *[The Empire Strikes Back](/wiki/The_Empire_Strikes_Back_(radio) "The Empire Strikes Back (radio)")*, in [1983](/wiki/1983 "1983"). Plans for a radio version of *Return of the Jedi* had to be scrapped due to federal cuts to NPR's funding.

The radio adaptations were notable for including background material that Lucas cut from the films, as well as original material created by Daley. [Mark Hamill](/wiki/Mark_Hamill "Mark Hamill"), Anthony Daniels, and [Billy Dee Williams](/wiki/Billy_Dee_Williams "Billy Dee Williams") reprised their roles as [Luke Skywalker](/wiki/Luke_Skywalker/Legends "Luke Skywalker/Legends"), C-3PO, and [Lando Calrissian](/wiki/Lando_Calrissian/Legends "Lando Calrissian/Legends"), respectively. For the sound mixing, Voegeli had access to all of [John Williams](/wiki/John_Williams "John Williams")'s original score and [Ben Burtt](/wiki/Ben_Burtt "Ben Burtt")'s sound effect library. He and his assistants recombined these elements of the film in new ways with the recordings of the voice actors.

In [1983](/wiki/1983 "1983"), Daley wrote an original *Star Wars* audio drama, *[Rebel Mission to Ord Mantell](/wiki/Rebel_Mission_to_Ord_Mantell "Rebel Mission to Ord Mantell")*. The drama was produced by [Buena Vista Records](/wiki/Buena_Vista_Records "Buena Vista Records"), which also made the *Star Wars* [Read-Along Adventures](/wiki/Read-Along_Adventures "Read-Along Adventures"). It was performed by most of the same cast as the Read-Along tapes and records.

For more than a decade, *Rebel Mission to Ord Mantell* was the only *Star Wars* audio drama not adapted from a feature film. Then, between [1994](/wiki/1994 "1994") and [1999](/wiki/1999 "1999") nearly a dozen audio dramas were released as [audio tapes and CDs](/wiki/List_of_Legends_audio_productions "List of Legends audio productions"). These dramas were adapted from comic books, short stories, and video games. They include: *[Dark Empire](/wiki/Dark_Empire_(audio_drama) "Dark Empire (audio drama)")* and its sequels (1994–1995), [*Tales of the Jedi*](/wiki/Tales_of_the_Jedi_(audio_drama) "Tales of the Jedi (audio drama)") and *[Dark Lords of the Sith](/wiki/Tales_of_the_Jedi:_Dark_Lords_of_the_Sith_(audio_drama) "Tales of the Jedi: Dark Lords of the Sith (audio drama)")* ([1995](/wiki/1995 "1995")), *[The Band's Tale](/wiki/We_Don%27t_Do_Weddings:_The_Band%27s_Tale_(audio_drama) "We Don't Do Weddings: The Band's Tale (audio drama)")* and *[The Lovers' Tale](/wiki/Nightlily:_The_Lovers%27_Tale_(audio_drama) "Nightlily: The Lovers' Tale (audio drama)")* (1995), the *[Dark Forces](/wiki/Dark_Forces:_Soldier_for_the_Empire_(audio_drama) "Dark Forces: Soldier for the Empire (audio drama)")* trilogy ([1998](/wiki/1998 "1998")), and *[Crimson Empire](/wiki/Crimson_Empire_(audio_drama) "Crimson Empire (audio drama)")* (1999). During this same period, [HighBridge Audio](/wiki/HighBridge_Audio "HighBridge Audio") picked up where NPR had left off, producing a [*Return of the Jedi* radio drama](/wiki/Return_of_the_Jedi_(radio) "Return of the Jedi (radio)") in [1996](/wiki/1996 "1996"), which NPR broadcast in November and December.

After this wave, no more Legends audio dramas were made, either adapting the [Prequel trilogy](/wiki/Prequel_trilogy "Prequel trilogy") or any other [era](/wiki/List_of_publishing_eras "List of publishing eras"). The next [full-cast *Star Wars* audio dramas](/wiki/List_of_canon_audio_productions "List of canon audio productions") were made within the [new canon](/wiki/Canon "Canon") in the 2020s.

*Star Wars*–based fiction predates the release of the first movie, with the 1976 [novelization of *A New Hope*](/wiki/Star_Wars:_A_New_Hope_(novelization) "Star Wars: A New Hope (novelization)") (ghostwritten by [Alan Dean Foster](/wiki/Alan_Dean_Foster "Alan Dean Foster") and credited to George Lucas). However, Foster's 1978 novel, *[Splinter of the Mind's Eye](/wiki/Splinter_of_the_Mind%27s_Eye "Splinter of the Mind's Eye")*, is thought of as the first Expanded Universe work to be released. In addition to filling in the time between the movies, this additional content greatly expanded the *Star Wars* timeline before and after the film series.

*Star Wars* fiction flourished during the time of the original series (1977–1983), but slowed to a trickle afterwards. In [1991](/wiki/1991 "1991"), however, [Timothy Zahn](/wiki/Timothy_Zahn "Timothy Zahn")'s celebrated *Thrawn Trilogy* debuted, sparking a new interest in the *Star Wars* universe. Since then, several hundred tie-in novels have been published by [Bantam Spectra](/wiki/Bantam_Spectra "Bantam Spectra") and [Del Rey](/wiki/Del_Rey "Del Rey").

Notable books in the series include [the *X-Wing* series](/wiki/Star_Wars:_X-Wing_(novel_series) "Star Wars: X-Wing (novel series)") by [Michael A. Stackpole](/wiki/Michael_A._Stackpole "Michael A. Stackpole") and [Aaron Allston](/wiki/Aaron_Allston "Aaron Allston"), the [*Jedi Academy trilogy*](/wiki/Star_Wars:_The_Jedi_Academy_Trilogy "Star Wars: The Jedi Academy Trilogy") and *Tales From...* series by [Kevin J. Anderson](/wiki/Kevin_J._Anderson "Kevin J. Anderson"), and the [*New Jedi Order*](/wiki/Star_Wars:_The_New_Jedi_Order "Star Wars: The New Jedi Order") series, by various authors. Another notable series of books is the [*Young Jedi Knights*](/wiki/Star_Wars:_Young_Jedi_Knights "Star Wars: Young Jedi Knights"), also by Kevin J. Anderson, which follow the adventures of [Jacen](/wiki/Darth_Caedus "Darth Caedus") and [Jaina Solo](/wiki/Jaina_Solo_Fel "Jaina Solo Fel") and their friends. The [*Legacy*](/wiki/Star_Wars:_Legacy_of_the_Force "Star Wars: Legacy of the Force") series is another important book series, which is written by Aaron Allston, Karen Traviss, and Troy Denning .

The first issue of the original Marvel series of Star Wars comic books

 

Marvel Comics published the [*Star Wars* comic-book series](/wiki/Star_Wars_(1977) "Star Wars (1977)") and adaptations from [1977](/wiki/1977 "1977") to 1986, and published the first original work in the Expanded Universe with the story *[The Keeper's World](/wiki/The_Keeper%27s_World "The Keeper's World")*. A wide variety of creators worked on this series, including [Archie Goodwin](/wiki/Archie_Goodwin "Archie Goodwin"), [Howard Chaykin](/wiki/Howard_Chaykin "Howard Chaykin"), [Al Williamson](/wiki/Al_Williamson "Al Williamson"), [Carmine Infantino](/wiki/Carmine_Infantino "Carmine Infantino"), [Gene Day](/wiki/Gene_Day "Gene Day"), [Walter Simonson](/wiki/Walter_Simonson "Walter Simonson"), [Michael Golden](/wiki/Michael_Golden "Michael Golden"), [Chris Claremont](/wiki/Chris_Claremont "Chris Claremont"), [Whilce Portacio](/wiki/Whilce_Portacio "Whilce Portacio"), [Mary Jo Duffy](/wiki/Mary_Jo_Duffy "Mary Jo Duffy"), and [Ron Frenz](/wiki/Ron_Frenz "Ron Frenz").

In the [1980s](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/1980s "wikipedia:1980s"), as part of its [Star Comics](/wiki/Star_Comics "Star Comics") line aimed at young children, Marvel published the short-lived series [*Ewoks*](/wiki/Star_Wars:_Ewoks_(1985) "Star Wars: Ewoks (1985)") and [*Droids*](/wiki/Star_Wars:_Droids_(1986) "Star Wars: Droids (1986)"), based on the two Saturday-morning cartoons of the same name.

*Star Wars* was also a daily [newspaper](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Newspaper "wikipedia:Newspaper") [comic strip](/wiki/Star_Wars_(comic_strip) "Star Wars (comic strip)") from [1979](/wiki/1979 "1979") to 1984. Among the creators were Goodwin, Williamson, and [Russ Manning](/wiki/Russ_Manning "Russ Manning").

In the late 1980s, Marvel announced it would publish a new *Star Wars* comic by [Tom Veitch](/wiki/Tom_Veitch "Tom Veitch") and [Cam Kennedy](/wiki/Cam_Kennedy "Cam Kennedy"). However, Dark Horse Comics published *Dark Empire* instead, and went on to publish a large number of original adventures set in the *Star Wars* universe. These include *Star Wars: Republic*, *Star Wars Empire*, *Star Wars Tales* and *Star Wars: Tales of the Jedi*. Dark Horse has also republished the Marvel series and newspaper strips in a collection entitled *[Classic Star Wars](/wiki/Classic_Star_Wars "Classic Star Wars")*. In addition, the company has reprinted several [Japanese](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Japan "wikipedia:Japan") [manga](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Manga "wikipedia:Manga") interpretations of the films, including *[Star Wars Manga: The Empire Strikes Back](/wiki/Star_Wars_Manga:_The_Empire_Strikes_Back "Star Wars Manga: The Empire Strikes Back")* by [Toshiki Kudo](/wiki/Toshiki_Kudo "Toshiki Kudo") and *[Star Wars Manga: Return of the Jedi](/wiki/Star_Wars_Manga:_Return_of_the_Jedi "Star Wars Manga: Return of the Jedi")* by [Shin-Ichi Hiromoto](/wiki/Shin-Ichi_Hiromoto "Shin-Ichi Hiromoto").

Since 1983, over 120 [video games](/wiki/Star_Wars_video_games "Star Wars video games") have been published bearing the *Star Wars* name, beginning with *Star Wars: The Empire Strikes Back* published for the [Atari 2600](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Atari_2600 "wikipedia:Atari 2600") by [Parker Brothers](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Parker_Brothers "wikipedia:Parker Brothers"). Other early titles include the [*Star Wars*](/wiki/Star_Wars_(1991_video_game) "Star Wars (1991 video game)") [Nintendo Entertainment System](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nintendo_Entertainment_System "wikipedia:Nintendo Entertainment System") game (published by [JVC](/wiki/JVC "JVC")) and three other titles for the Atari 2600.

[Atari](/wiki/Atari "Atari") produced [arcade games](/wiki/Arcade_game/Legends "Arcade game/Legends") based on the original trilogy, beginning with *Star Wars* and *The Empire Strikes Back*, which were both "flight sim" style games that utilized [vector graphics](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Vector_graphics "wikipedia:Vector graphics"). The third, *Return of the Jedi*, used more traditional [raster graphics](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Raster_graphics "wikipedia:Raster graphics").

*Star Wars* has also, not surprisingly, opened the way to a myriad of space-flight simulations that take the space wars of the saga more seriously, teaching the player to fly various *Star Wars* starfighters along the lines of more traditional "Modern Aircraft" flight simulators. The first among these were "X-Wing" and its two expansions, "B-Wing" and "Imperial Pursuit," dealing with the Rebellion's side of the war, taking place in the period right before, and up to, the destruction of the first Death Star. The second was "TIE Fighter," dealing with the Empire's starfighters at the time prior to Episode VI. Both games were released for DOS and Macintosh. "TIE Fighter" had an expansion disk, "Defender of the Empire." In addition, both the original "X-Wing" and "TIE Fighter" games saw two collector's edition releases (one for DOS and another for Windows 9x) which featured enhanced graphics quality and added missions. Newer simulators are also available, with *[Star Wars: X-Wing Alliance](/wiki/Star_Wars:_X-Wing_Alliance "Star Wars: X-Wing Alliance")* in the lead.

The first *Star Wars* first-person shooter, [*Dark Forces*](/wiki/Star_Wars:_Dark_Forces "Star Wars: Dark Forces"), was introduced by [LucasArts](/wiki/LucasArts "LucasArts") in February 1995. Telling the story of [Kyle Katarn](/wiki/Kyle_Katarn "Kyle Katarn"), Imperial soldier turned mercenary, the game featured a little over a dozen levels where the player explored various original and familiar settings. Featuring an original and interactive soundtrack by game composer [Clint Bajakian](/wiki/Clint_Bajakian "Clint Bajakian") using the [iMUSE](/wiki/IMUSE "IMUSE") sound system, along with state-of-the-art graphics, the game succeeded in capturing many gamers' imaginations. The [1997](/wiki/1997 "1997") sequel, *[Star Wars: Jedi Knight: Dark Forces II](/wiki/Star_Wars:_Jedi_Knight:_Dark_Forces_II "Star Wars: Jedi Knight: Dark Forces II")*, was notable for having a few cut scenes which were made up of live-action footage of certain Expanded Universe characters, such as Kyle Katarn. It had 2 other sequels named [Star Wars: Jedi Knight II: Jedi Outcast](/wiki/Star_Wars:_Jedi_Knight_II:_Jedi_Outcast "Star Wars: Jedi Knight II: Jedi Outcast") and [Star Wars: Jedi Knight: Jedi Academy](/wiki/Star_Wars:_Jedi_Knight:_Jedi_Academy "Star Wars: Jedi Knight: Jedi Academy"), that incorporated a new system of lightsaber combat and received re-releases years later.

[*Rogue Squadron*](/wiki/Rogue_Squadron/Legends "Rogue Squadron/Legends") was a cross-platform title on Nintendo 64 and PC which allowed the player to experience a more arcade-action version of the same gameplay in *X-Wing* and *TIE Fighter*, similar to the action in the Nintendo 64 title [*Shadows of the Empire*](/wiki/Star_Wars:_Shadows_of_the_Empire_(video_game) "Star Wars: Shadows of the Empire (video game)"). The game consisted of piloting several different *Star Wars* vehicles through missions on planet surfaces and in space. *Rogue Squadron* saw two sequels, both on the [Nintendo](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nintendo "wikipedia:Nintendo") [GameCube](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nintendo_GameCube "wikipedia:Nintendo GameCube") system.

[*Star Wars: Rebellion*](/wiki/Star_Wars:_Rebellion_(video_game) "Star Wars: Rebellion (video game)") allowed players to compete in the *Star Wars* universe on a larger scale, focusing more on the strategic aspect of handling (or defeating) a rebellion, with resource management and agent allocation, as well as large-scale conflicts between entire fleets of starships.

[*Knights of the Old Republic*](/wiki/Star_Wars:_Knights_of_the_Old_Republic "Star Wars: Knights of the Old Republic") by [BioWare](/wiki/BioWare "BioWare") and [*Knights of the Old Republic II: The Sith Lords*](/wiki/Star_Wars:_Knights_of_the_Old_Republic_II:_The_Sith_Lords "Star Wars: Knights of the Old Republic II: The Sith Lords") by [Obsidian Entertainment](/wiki/Obsidian_Entertainment "Obsidian Entertainment") are recent additions to the EU, and take place in the Old Republic era, right after the Mandalorian wars. ### Multimedia projects

* *[Star Wars: Shadows of the Empire](/wiki/Star_Wars:_Shadows_of_the_Empire "Star Wars: Shadows of the Empire")* ([1996](/wiki/1996 "1996")) was an ambitious multimedia project created by Lucasfilm. Dubbed "a film without a film," *Shadows of the Empire* told the story of the events between *The Empire Strikes Back* and *Return of the Jedi*, and introduced a new villain, the crime lord [Prince Xizor](/wiki/Xizor/Legends "Xizor/Legends"). Utilizing all previous types of media that have been used to present the Expanded Universe, the project included a novel written by [Steve Perry](/wiki/Steve_Perry "Steve Perry"), multiple comic book series, a soundtrack, a video game, concept art, action figures, and the like.
* [*Clone Wars*](/wiki/Clone_Wars_multimedia_project "Clone Wars multimedia project") (2003–2005). Using methods similar to the *Shadows of the Empire* project, Lucasfilm directed a widespread project to tell the stories of the Clone Wars. This project was made up of films, novels, video games, comics, action figures, and even its own animated series (described above).
* *The Force Unleashed* ([2008](/wiki/2008 "2008")). Originally set for [2007](/wiki/2007 "2007"), production was postponed for a year. Set between the two trilogies and during the [Great Jedi Purge](/wiki/Great_Jedi_Purge/Legends "Great Jedi Purge/Legends"), it focuses on the adventures of Darth Vader's secret [apprentice](/wiki/Sith_apprentice/Legends "Sith apprentice/Legends"), [Galen Marek](/wiki/Galen_Marek "Galen Marek"). It's been referred to as "the next chapter in the *Star Wars* saga." Like its predecessors, it includes novels, comics, a game, roleplaying-game resources, and more.

## Continuity and canonicity

:   [![icon](data:image/gif;base64,R0lGODlhAQABAIABAAAAAP///yH5BAEAAAEALAAAAAABAAEAQAICTAEAOw%3D%3D)](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/starwars/images/0/04/Blue_Glass_Arrow.svg/revision/latest?cb=20090726033133) Main article: [Canon](/wiki/Canon "Canon")

 [![Star Wars - 1976 first printing](data:image/gif;base64,R0lGODlhAQABAIABAAAAAP///yH5BAEAAAEALAAAAAABAAEAQAICTAEAOw%3D%3D)](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/starwars/images/3/3a/FromtheAdventuresofLukeSkywalker.png/revision/latest?cb=20190926173352) [![Star Wars - 1976 first printing](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/starwars/images/3/3a/FromtheAdventuresofLukeSkywalker.png/revision/latest/scale-to-width-down/150?cb=20190926173352)](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/starwars/images/3/3a/FromtheAdventuresofLukeSkywalker.png/revision/latest?cb=20190926173352) 

*Star Wars* - 1976 first printing

 

:   "*If you don't believe in the EU version of the story or didn't like it, then perhaps this new revelation that* […] *suits you better. Again a great many of the truths we cling to depend largely on our own point of view.* […] *In the end I have to say this: many die hard EU fans pick and choose the stories they think are canon based on what they like and what they don't like. They read a novel they like it and it's 'in,' they don't like it, and it's 'out.'* […] *Much effort goes into trying to word things or shoot things so that the existing EU can remain, if only at times from a certain point of view.*"
:   ―[Dave Filoni](/wiki/Dave_Filoni "Dave Filoni"), director of the *Star Wars: The Clone Wars* TV series[[15]](#cite_note-TFN-15)

The Expanded Universe was intended to be a continuation, and an expansion, on the six *Star Wars* theatrical films produced by George Lucas from 1977 to 2005. All Legends material, combined with that presented in the films, was meant to function as a complete story. However, in order to allow this story to function as a whole, it was kept in an order of continuity. Lucasfilm held this of such high importance that a team's sole job at Lucasfilm was maintaining continuity between Lucas's films and the Legends, which was created by many other authors and artists, many times out of order, and with many different ideas. Lucas, while supporting the works of the EU, nevertheless told the stories he wished to in his films, which sometimes contradicted material previously seen in the EU. When asked in an interview his general opinion on the EU, he replied:

:   "*I don't read that stuff. I haven't read any of the novels. I don't know anything about that world. That's a different world than my world. But I do try to keep it consistent. The way I do it now is they have a Star Wars Encyclopedia. So if I come up with a name or something else, I look it up and see if it has already been used. When I said [other people] could make their own Star Wars stories, we decided that, like Star Trek, we would have two universes: My universe and then this other one. They try to make their universe as consistent with mine as possible, but obviously they get enthusiastic and want to go off in other directions.*"
:   ―George Lucas, from an interview in [Starlog #337](/wiki/Starlog_(magazine) "Starlog (magazine)")[[16]](#cite_note-Starlog-16)

During his time heading Lucasfilm, Lucas retained ultimate creative control over the *Star Wars* universe. For example, the "death" of central characters and similar changes in the status quo were required to first pass his screening before authors were given the go-ahead. In addition, Lucasfilm Licensing devoted considerable effort to ensure continuity between the works of various authors across multiple companies. Nothing in the Expanded Universe was supposed to contradict the films or any other part of the Expanded Universe. Upon occasion, Lucas's new films, reedited original-trilogy films, or statements contradicted existing EU material, and several [retcons](/wiki/Retroactive_continuity "Retroactive continuity") were used to fix these inconsistencies.

Some purists rejected the Expanded Universe as [apocrypha](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/apocrypha "wikipedia:apocrypha"), believing that only the events in the film series are part of the "real" *Star Wars* universe. (Palpatine's clones, for example, seem to contradict the "chosen one" theory.) This line of thought was supported to the extent that some Expanded Universe material released before Lucas's prequel films drew conclusions that Lucas later overturned. However, elements of the Expanded Universe were adopted by Lucas for use in the films. For example, the name of planet [Coruscant](/wiki/Coruscant/Legends "Coruscant/Legends") first appeared in [Timothy Zahn](/wiki/Timothy_Zahn "Timothy Zahn")'s novel *[Heir to the Empire](/wiki/Heir_to_the_Empire "Heir to the Empire")* before being used in the prequel trilogy (with a different pronunciation)—though the planet itself, under a different name, had existed in a previous version of the *Return of the Jedi* script. Also, the Twi'lek Jedi [Aayla Secura](/wiki/Aayla_Secura/Legends "Aayla Secura/Legends") originally appeared in the ongoing Dark Horse comics series *Republic*; apparently Lucas saw the cover which featured her and liked the look of her character so much that he included her in the Jedi battle at the end of [*Attack of the Clones*](/wiki/Star_Wars:_Episode_II_Attack_of_the_Clones "Star Wars: Episode II Attack of the Clones"), played by Lucasfilm employee [Amy Allen](/wiki/Amy_Allen "Amy Allen"), and her demise is later shown in [*Revenge of the Sith*](/wiki/Star_Wars:_Episode_III_Revenge_of_the_Sith "Star Wars: Episode III Revenge of the Sith"). These examples sometimes ended up confusing the issue, having blurred the lines between the Expanded Universe and "his world."

This was a hotly debated issue among *Star Wars* fans. Superficially, one allure of the films is that they are organized numerically and logically whereas the Expanded Universe was published out of chronological sequence and occasionally contained minor contradictions and in some cases even major discrepancies, despite the best efforts of [Lucas Licensing](/wiki/Lucas_Licensing "Lucas Licensing"). On the other hand, the Expanded Universe provided depth to the world of *Star Wars*. Some readers accused the EU sources of being excessively self-referential, to an extent that misrepresents the *Star Wars* universe (e.g., *EU minimalism*, the creeping reduction of technological abilities and physical scope in EU sources). Some other fans found that the Expanded Universe convention destroyed many of the good dramatic elements of the movies by explaining things in a way they find unfavorable. These critics felt that writing a new story within the context of the EU handcuffs the author.

In theory, the films were the absolute canon and everything else official was part of the Expanded Universe that, while generally valid, could not contradict anything in the movies.
Wherever an EU source contradicted movie canon, the EU source was invalid on that specific point, though the rest of the source was still considered part of the continuity.
Despite the unpopularity of works like the [*Jedi Prince*](/wiki/Adventures_of_a_Jedi_Prince "Adventures of a Jedi Prince") series, they were considered just as canonical as popular works like *[Star Wars: Shadows of the Empire](/wiki/Star_Wars:_Shadows_of_the_Empire "Star Wars: Shadows of the Empire")*, to the degree that they were not contradicted by material from a higher "canon tier," later work, or some other statement from an official source declaring them to be non-canon.

However, this sometimes did not appear to be true in practice. For example, [*Prophets of the Dark Side*](/wiki/Prophets_of_the_Dark_Side_(novel) "Prophets of the Dark Side (novel)") featured [the wedding](/wiki/Wedding_of_Han_Solo_and_Leia_Organa/Legends "Wedding of Han Solo and Leia Organa/Legends") of [Han Solo](/wiki/Han_Solo/Legends "Han Solo/Legends") and [Princess](/wiki/Princess/Legends "Princess/Legends") [Leia](/wiki/Leia_Organa_Solo/Legends "Leia Organa Solo/Legends"), but [Dave Wolverton](/wiki/Dave_Wolverton "Dave Wolverton") ignored this and featured the same event in his novel *[The Courtship of Princess Leia](/wiki/The_Courtship_of_Princess_Leia "The Courtship of Princess Leia")*, which was released a few years later. According to the rules of the Expanded Universe, both versions were within continuity, though it was the wedding in Dave Wolverton's book that is most often referenced. Fans tried to fix this problem by suggesting that, since the scene in *Prophets of the Dark Side* concludes just as Han and Leia are walking down the aisle, the event was disrupted and postponed until the time of *The Courtship of Princess Leia*; the authors of *Prophets of the Dark Side* confirmed that they had planned to write another series of novels which would begin with the wedding's disruption, but their contract was cancelled before they could do so.

There were also minor disputes about what was, and what was not, part of the Expanded Universe. For example, the spin-off films: *Caravan of Courage: An Ewok Adventure* and *Ewoks: The Battle for Endor* were written by George Lucas and are films, but neither of them is one of the six main films in the series, so they were usually considered a part of the Expanded Universe. Both films were later officially confirmed as part of the continuity of *Star Wars* Legends and no longer canon.

The [original Star Wars Databank](/wiki/Databank_(original) "Databank (original)") entries distinguished movie information and EU information, providing them in separate tabs.

As of April 25, 2014, no material originating purely from an Expanded Universe source is considered canon. In order to be considered canon, the material must have appeared within one of the *Star Wars* films from the original trilogy or prequel trilogy, the *[Star Wars: The Clone Wars](/wiki/Star_Wars:_The_Clone_Wars "Star Wars: The Clone Wars")* television series or its [associated film](/wiki/Star_Wars:_The_Clone_Wars_(film) "Star Wars: The Clone Wars (film)"), or most material released on or after April 25, 2014. While such material often features material originating from the Expanded Universe, only the details featured within the new material are considered canon. For example, while "[spicebrew](/wiki/Spicebrew/Legends "Spicebrew/Legends")" appears in both material from the old Expanded Universe and the new canon continuity, one cannot assume that the drink is part of the mixture that makes a [Sonic Servodriver](/wiki/Sonic_Servodriver "Sonic Servodriver"), these details being provided only in a Legends source.

### Official levels of canon

The [Holocron continuity database](/wiki/Holocron_continuity_database "Holocron continuity database") was an internal database maintained by Lucas Licensing for the express purpose of trying to maintain continuity. This database was sorted into five levels that reflected LFL's canon and continuity policies: G, T, C, S, and N. All levels with the exception of N formed an overall continuity that was considered by Lucasfilm to be the "true" *Star Wars* canon prior to the rebranding of the Expanded Universe to Legends in April of 2014.

* **G** (George Lucas) canon was absolute canon. This category included the six films, some of the deleted scenes from the films, the novelizations of the films, the [radio dramas](/wiki/Star_Wars_(radio) "Star Wars (radio)") based on the films, the film scripts, and any material found in any other source (published or not) that comes directly from George Lucas himself. G canon overruled all other forms of canon when there was a contradiction.
* **T**[[17]](#cite_note-17) canon referred to the canon level comprising only the television show *Star Wars: The Clone Wars*.
* **C** (continuity) canon referred to the main body of EU work, and was the next most authoritative level of canon. All material published under the *Star Wars* label but not falling into G, S, or N was C canon, and was considered authoritative as long as not contradicted by G canon. Games were a special case as generally only the stories would be C-canon while things like stats and gameplay were N-canon. If the video game had several possible endings or if the player could choose the gender or the species of the main character, only one of each is considered C-canon. C-canon elements have appeared in the movies, thus making them G-canon. These included [Coruscant](/wiki/Coruscant/Legends "Coruscant/Legends") (both its name and the concept of it being an [ecumenopolis](/wiki/Ecumenopolis/Legends "Ecumenopolis/Legends")), [swoop bikes](/wiki/Swoop/Legends "Swoop/Legends"), [Aayla Secura](/wiki/Aayla_Secura/Legends "Aayla Secura/Legends"), [Double Bladed Lightsabers](/wiki/Double-bladed_lightsaber/Legends "Double-bladed lightsaber/Legends"), [YT-2400 freighters](/wiki/YT-2400_light_freighter/Legends "YT-2400 light freighter/Legends"), and [Action VI transports](/wiki/Action_VI_bulk_transport/Legends "Action VI bulk transport/Legends").
* **S** (secondary) canon referred to older, less accurate, or less coherent EU works, which would not ordinarily fit in the main continuity of G and C canon. Unless referenced by a G- or C-level source, the story itself is considered non-continuity, but the non-contradicting elements were still a canon part of the *Star Wars* universe. For example, this included *The Star Wars Holiday Special*, the Marvel comics, the popular online roleplaying game *Star Wars Galaxies*, and certain elements of a few N-canon stories.
* **N** continuity material is also known as "non-canon" or "non-continuity" material. What-if stories (such as those published under the [Infinities](/wiki/Infinities "Infinities") label), game stats, "comic" material such as *[Angry Birds Star Wars](/wiki/Angry_Birds_Star_Wars "Angry Birds Star Wars")* or [*Jedi Academy*](/wiki/Jedi_Academy_(novel) "Jedi Academy (novel)") and anything else that was directly contradicted by higher canon and could not at all fit into continuity was placed into this category. N-canon was the only level that was not at all considered canon by Lucasfilm.

### Lucas's use of Legends content

#### Legends content in the films

:   [![icon](data:image/gif;base64,R0lGODlhAQABAIABAAAAAP///yH5BAEAAAEALAAAAAABAAEAQAICTAEAOw%3D%3D)](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/starwars/images/0/04/Blue_Glass_Arrow.svg/revision/latest?cb=20090726033133) Main article: [List of Legends elements in the films](/wiki/List_of_Legends_elements_in_the_films "List of Legends elements in the films")

C-canon elements from licensed creators have appeared in Lucas's films. Most of these are brief, cameo appearances, almost taking the form of [Easter eggs](/wiki/Easter_egg_(virtual) "Easter egg (virtual)") (which may have been added by animators or others under Lucas, rather than specifically dictated), but others are more substantial. While Lucas viewed Legends as separate from his story, he took inspiration from it or borrowed certain elements and characters for his films.

* The name of the Wookiee home planet [Kashyyyk](/wiki/Kashyyyk/Legends "Kashyyyk/Legends") was taken from Legends, although Lucas himself invented the species and the planet. Concept artists viewed *The Star Wars Holiday Special* multiple times while designing the [Kashyyyk](/wiki/Kashyyyk/Legends "Kashyyyk/Legends") environment for *Revenge of the Sith*.[[18]](#cite_note-18)
* George Lucas adopted the name "[Coruscant](/wiki/Coruscant "Coruscant")" for the capital planet of the Galactic Republic, previously known as Had Abbadon in his drafts.
* Fascinated by her depiction in cover art for a comic by [Jan Duursema](/wiki/Jan_Duursema "Jan Duursema"), Lucas adopted the character [Aayla Secura](/wiki/Aayla_Secura/Legends "Aayla Secura/Legends") for [*Star Wars:* Episode II *Attack of the Clones*](/wiki/Star_Wars:_Episode_II_Attack_of_the_Clones "Star Wars: Episode II Attack of the Clones").[[19]](#cite_note-19) He also adopted the Expanded Universe name "Twi'lek" for Secura's species, as evidenced by a remark of his which is documented in an Episode III Set Diary entry.[[20]](#cite_note-20)
* The Legends character [Tsui Choi](/wiki/Tsui_Choi/Legends "Tsui Choi/Legends") was at one point slated to appear in *Revenge of the Sith*.[[21]](#cite_note-Missing-21)

#### Lucas' involvement with *Star Wars* Legends

In [August](/wiki/August "August") [2009](/wiki/2009 "2009"), [Mixnmojo](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/The_International_House_of_Mojo "wikipedia:The International House of Mojo") made an interview with Mary Bihr, [LucasArts](/wiki/LucasArts "LucasArts")' Vice President of Global Publishing back in 2009. It's worth to highlight the following part from the interview:[[23]](#cite_note-Mixnmojo-23)

:   Mixnmojo: "*How much of a role does George Lucas have in the day-to-day activities of LucasArts? Tim Schafer once described him 'coming to visit' the team during the making of The Secret of Monkey Island in 1990, and offering his advice on the title. Was he involved in the decision to revisit it*?"
:   Mary Bihr: "*Definitely. George is involved at a high level with everything we do. He approves and gives feedback on all projects, concepts, and stories.*"

Lucas has often worked very closely with Legends creators:

* Lucas met with [Roy Thomas](/wiki/Roy_Thomas "Roy Thomas") to help plan the early storylines for Marvel's *Star Wars*, and personally approved the direction Thomas planned to take the series.
* Lucas selected [Archie Goodwin](/wiki/Archie_Goodwin "Archie Goodwin") to become a writer for the *Star Wars* comic strip.
* Lucas was involved in the writing of the script's first draft for *The Star Wars Holiday Special* with the [Smith-Hemion productions](/wiki/Smith-Hemion_Productions "Smith-Hemion Productions").[[24]](#cite_note-24)
* Lucas wrote the stories for, executive produced, and directed pick-ups and re-shoots for both of the *Ewok* films from the mid-eighties, *Caravan of Courage* and *The Battle for Endor*.
* Lucas was involved with the creation of the [Star Tours](/wiki/Star_Tours_(real-world) "Star Tours (real-world)") theme park attraction.
* Kevin J. Anderson developed the process of Jedi training for the [*Jedi Academy trilogy*](/wiki/Star_Wars:_The_Jedi_Academy_Trilogy "Star Wars: The Jedi Academy Trilogy") based on Lucas' input and memos.[[25]](#cite_note-25)
* When asked about it, Lucas approved [Kevin J. Anderson'](/wiki/Kevin_J._Anderson "Kevin J. Anderson")s idea of killing off the character of [Crix Madine](/wiki/Crix_Madine/Legends "Crix Madine/Legends") in the novel Darksaber.[[26]](#cite_note-26)
* Lucas helped [Tom Veitch](/wiki/Tom_Veitch "Tom Veitch") and Kevin J. Anderson to develop aspects of the Jedi and Sith for the [*Tales of the Jedi*](/wiki/Star_Wars:_Tales_of_the_Jedi_(comic_series) "Star Wars: Tales of the Jedi (comic series)") comics by answering questionnaires and approving/vetoing ideas.[[27]](#cite_note-27)
* When [Terry Brooks](/wiki/Terry_Brooks "Terry Brooks") was writing the [novelization of *The Phantom Menace*](/wiki/Star_Wars:_Episode_I_The_Phantom_Menace_(novelization) "Star Wars: Episode I The Phantom Menace (novelization)"), Lucas informed him of the extensive history of the [Sith](/wiki/Sith/Legends "Sith/Legends") and Jedi before that time period, so he could include it in his book. For example, the character of [Darth Bane](/wiki/Darth_Bane/Legends "Darth Bane/Legends") is an original creation of Lucas', and although he did not include information on the character in his films, he informed Terry Brooks of the character to incorporate into the novelization. Lucas also gave Brooks other extensive bits of info of what went on during *The Phantom Menace*.
* Lucas wrote the prologue for [Matthew Stover](/wiki/Matthew_Stover "Matthew Stover")'s novel [*Shatterpoint*](/wiki/Shatterpoint_(novel) "Shatterpoint (novel)").
* Lucas gave [Genndy Tartakovsky](/wiki/Genndy_Tartakovsky "Genndy Tartakovsky") information on specific events during the Clone Wars, which Tartakovsky used in part of the series *[Star Wars: Clone Wars](/wiki/Star_Wars:_Clone_Wars "Star Wars: Clone Wars")*.
* [James Luceno](/wiki/James_Luceno "James Luceno") based his book *[Labyrinth of Evil](/wiki/Labyrinth_of_Evil "Labyrinth of Evil")* on the background Lucas informed him of, of what happened right before *Revenge of the Sith*. Lucas also allowed him to explore the backstory of [Sifo-Dyas](/wiki/Sifo-Dyas/Legends "Sifo-Dyas/Legends") and the mystery behind [Kamino's](/wiki/Kamino/Legends "Kamino/Legends") erasure from the [Jedi Archives](/wiki/Jedi_Archives/Legends "Jedi Archives/Legends"), two plot points he originally intended to reveal in *Revenge of the Sith*.
* Lucas decided that [Delta Squad](/wiki/Delta_Squad/Legends "Delta Squad/Legends") should have colored armor in *Star Wars: Republic Commando*, to match Episode III.
* Lucas instructed [John Ostrander](/wiki/John_Ostrander "John Ostrander") on the fate of [Quinlan Vos](/wiki/Quinlan_Vos/Legends "Quinlan Vos/Legends") in [*Republic* 83](/wiki/Republic_83 "Republic 83").
* Lucas decreed that there could be no more Wookiee [Jedi](/wiki/Jedi/Legends "Jedi/Legends") in the Expanded Universe. Notably, [Obsidian Entertainment](/wiki/Obsidian_Entertainment "Obsidian Entertainment") was forbidden to make [Hanharr](/wiki/Hanharr "Hanharr") a [Dark Jedi](/wiki/Dark_Jedi/Legends "Dark Jedi/Legends") because of this restriction.
* Lucas decreed that, following Episode III, Palpatine has only minor concern over the remaining Jedi.
* Lucas owns the original cover art of *[Tag & Bink Were Here](/wiki/Tag_%26_Bink_Were_Here "Tag & Bink Were Here")*.
* Lucas gave his direct input and guidance to the 2007 multimedia project *Star Wars: The Force Unleashed*.
* Lucas established that [Darth Plagueis](/wiki/Darth_Plagueis/Legends "Darth Plagueis/Legends") is a [Muun](/wiki/Muun/Legends "Muun/Legends").[[28]](#cite_note-28)

#### Lucas/Legends contradictions

:   "*[George Lucas] and I always thought the Expanded Universe was just that. It was an expanded universe. Basically it's stories that are really fun and really exciting, but they're a view on Star Wars, not necessarily canon to him. That was the way it was from the day I walked into Lucasfilm with him all through Clone Wars, everything we worked on, he felt the Clone Wars series and his movies were what was actually the reality of it all, the canon, then there was everything else. [...]*"
:   ―Dave Filoni about George Lucas and the Expanded Universe[[29]](#cite_note-29)

On the other hand, Lucas and authors of the Expanded Universe mostly worked independently from each other. This resulted in the Expanded Universe often deviating from his vision. Examples of inconsistencies include:

* Opposed to the thousand years old struggle between Jedi and Sith depicted in the Expanded Universe, George Lucas stated that there was never a war between them.[[30]](#cite_note-30)
* George Lucas stated that events of the Expanded Universe following *Return of the Jedi* "isn't at all what I would have done with it", stating that the "Emperor doesn't get cloned and Luke doesn't get married".[[31]](#cite_note-31)
* The deaths of Obi-Wan, [Yoda](/wiki/Yoda/Legends "Yoda/Legends"), and Anakin Skywalker in the original trilogy made it appear that dead Jedi typically disappeared and reappeared as [Force ghosts](/wiki/Force_ghost/Legends "Force ghost/Legends"). *Revenge of the Sith* revealed that this is in fact a very rare ability only a few Jedi have ever mastered. The 2007 *[Star Wars: Legacy of the Force](/wiki/Star_Wars:_Legacy_of_the_Force "Star Wars: Legacy of the Force")* novel [*Sacrifice*](/wiki/Legacy_of_the_Force:_Sacrifice "Legacy of the Force: Sacrifice") further reveals that a Jedi can choose to become one with the Force or to have the body left behind. While the C-canon presented several Sith spirits, George Lucas stated that preserving identity after death only can be achieved via the [light side of the Force](/wiki/Light_side_of_the_Force "Light side of the Force").
* Boba Fett's origins originally named him as [Jaster Mereel](/wiki/Jaster_Mereel "Jaster Mereel"), a Journeyman Protector exiled from [Concord Dawn](/wiki/Concord_Dawn/Legends "Concord Dawn/Legends"). It was later revealed that Jaster Mereel was merely an alias Fett was using when he was exiled. The real Jaster, whose name Boba used as an alias, was [retconned](/wiki/Retroactive_continuity "Retroactive continuity") into a separate character. In the Expanded Universe, Boba Fett and his father, Jango presented as Mandalorians, whereas, according to Lucas, they were in fact, not Mandalorians, just wearing Mandalorian armor.
* The portrayal of Mandalorian culture and the planet Mandalore is significantly different in the Expanded Universe and in the 2008 Clone Wars Tv show.
* The Clone Wars as described in Zahn's Thrawn trilogy were, at least in part, a struggle between the Old Republic and an army of insane clones grown and controlled by a number of "clonemasters." *Attack of the Clones*, on the other hand, revealed that the Clone Wars were fought between the Old Republic (using clones) and a (single) Separatist movement (using droids). When writing the prequel trilogy, Lucas changed the dates he had originally given Zahn for the Clone Wars, so Zahn's estimate was at least a decade off. This inconsistency was easily retconned, however, since it was the [Noghri](/wiki/Noghri/Legends "Noghri/Legends") who gave the former date, and this species was using their own unique dating system.
* In *Revenge of the Sith*, Anakin is outraged that he was admitted to the Jedi Council but not given the rank of master. He says that such an occurrence had never happened in the history of the order. However, it had been established that during the time of *The Phantom Menace*, [Ki-Adi-Mundi](/wiki/Ki-Adi-Mundi/Legends "Ki-Adi-Mundi/Legends") was a council member though he was only a knight. This may be less an inconsistency than mere hyperbole if Anakin knew that it was a rare but not unheard-of occurrence, or it may indicate that his knowledge of the order's history was incomplete.
* The backstory of General Grievous presented in the Expanded Universe is significantly different from George Lucas' vision of the character.
* [R4-P17](/wiki/R4-P17/Legends "R4-P17/Legends"), the droid in Obi-Wan's Jedi Starfighter in *Attack of the Clones*, is at first an incorrect designation, as it has the dome of an R2 unit. The R4's dome is more conical. However, this was retconned by saying that R4-P17's old R4 body was damaged, and its remains were placed in an R2 body.
