[Parallels in the Duel on the Invisible Hand and the Duel on the Death Star II](/f/p/4400000000003951142)

Something I noticed while reading the Revenge of the Sith novelization is how the Duel on the Invisible Hand between Anakin and Count Dooku parallels Luke‚Äôs fight with Darth Vader aboard the Death Star II in Return of the Jedi.

![Fandom Image](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/6b438878-74b2-4059-bd8f-e7e27cdcc44f/scale-to-width/755)

Sidious presides over each fight from a throne-like chair (the ROTS novelization makes clear that while Sidious is play-acting the captured Chancellor, he‚Äôs still in control of the situation) as his current and prospective pupils duke it out.

Anakin and Luke both emerge victorious, but only after tapping tapping into their hate and anger of their Sith opponent. Sidious then encourages both Skywalkers to execute his fallen apprentices (and take their place, even if Sidious is not ready to say the quiet part out loud to Anakin).

![Fandom Image](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/ff97fe01-2cd7-4613-8343-f26317a1dc2d/scale-to-width/755)

I love these similar ‚ÄúJedi Traps‚Äù that Sidious sets-up because it conveys so much about Sidious as well as the Skywalkers.

George Lucas did a lot between the prequels and originals to intentionally draw parallels between Anakin and Luke (e.g., Anakin destroying the Trade Federation command ship in The Phantom Menace and Luke destroying the Death Star in A New Hope, Anakin and Luke being defeated and dismembered by Dooku and Vader in Attack of the Clones and The Empire Strikes Back respectively).

[Cue by Cue: The Music of John Williams - #2.37 ESB (1980) - 10m3 Chewie Chokes Lando](/f/p/4400000000003951196)

Hello and welcome to this week's installment of Cue by Cue.

Today we're going to be listening to one of my favorite cues of this score, ***10m3*** **Chewie Chokes Lando**.

This cue is 114 bars long and was orchestrated by Herb Spencer.

Here are the tops of the sketch and the orchestrated score:

![Fandom Image](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/e93f9cbd-671a-4b45-a61d-dcc29336d386/scale-to-width/755)

![Fandom Image](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/c698fc2c-9b57-4a62-af49-bad6aa94bebe/scale-to-width/755)

Now let's listen to the cue together:

<https://youtu.be/JsF0v93LkqA>

At 0:00 the cue begins with some string notes playing Bespin Dirge 1. Notably, when this theme appeared in ***9m6-10m1*** **Carbon Freeze** it was very militaristic for Vader giving orders, and its variant at the start of 10m2 Luke Pursues the Captives was very sad and funeral-like. Here it's back to being militaristic, but more upbeat as Lando almost immediately gives an order to his guards to take the stormtroopers prisoner and release the rebels.

Some plucky woodwinds as he collects all the guns, before the Dirge returns at 0:17 as the troops start marching again. This continues as Lando removes Chewie's handcuffs, building into some tense music as the choking begins at 0:30.The tension is mostly held with atonal strings and brass, with some accentuation from staccato and piano, as well as some pizzicato strings.

This continues until 1:01 when the strings return and sound like they're about to start playing Bespin Dirge 1 again, but bounce around it until the brass kicks in at 1:06 with Bespin Dirge 2 for the shot of Boba Fett.

At 1:16 the strings return again - it seems they come back slightly before the shot change, perhaps they missed their sync mark. It's definitely not a sync issue on my part nor a video edit issue, there's no way to get this mark to sync without messing up every other mark before or after it. The strings are quickly joined by the love theme at 1:20, which plays in a heroic mode as the rebels sprint to try and rescue Han from the Slave I.

The love theme changes modes to be sadder and almost despairful at 1:50 as Leia sadly watches the Slave I depart.

The love theme finally ends with a crash at 2:06, immediately cutting back into the Vader/Luke lightsaber fight. Some brief atonal percussion music before some piano accentuates Luke's lightsaber falling at 2:15.

This is immediately followed by an imposing brass rendition of the Imperial March as Luke rolls down the stairs and Vader jumps after him. I love how the trumpet descends in each accompanying note here, it really accentuates the feeling of total dominance, the Dark Side is winning...

Woodwinds take up the next verse at 2:25, before some ascending brass from 2:31 leading to Luke's fall into the freezing chamber at 2:36.

Three transitional horn notes at 2:41 are followed by a heroic rendition of the Force theme starting at 2:47 as Luke escapes the chamber. The Force theme climaxes as Vader is hit with the steam at 2:57, and immediately leads into an even more heroic rendition of Yoda's theme as Luke reclaims his lightsaber and fights back. This continues through the rest of the battle culminating in a super bombastic brass bit for Luke's flip at 3:17. One last Yoda's theme bit at 3:22, and then some lighthearted Luke theme as Luke walks off at 3:29. Thus, the cue ends...

Only one minor visual edit appears to have been made where they inserted a 1.5s closeup shot of Luke at 2:51, this causes the end of the cue to be out of sync with the final cut. This didn't actually require any music edits in the final film however, as this entire section of the cue was dropped.

In the final theatrical cut:

0:00-0:59 =¬†***10m3*** **Chewie Chokes Lando** 0:00-0:59

0:59-1:09 =¬†***10m3*** **Chewie Chokes Lando** 1:00-1:10

1:09-1:17 =¬†***10m3*** **Chewie Chokes Lando** 1:06-1:15

1:17-1:32 =¬†***10m3*** **Chewie Chokes Lando** 1:18-1:33

1:32-1:46 =¬†***10m3*** **Chewie Chokes Lando** 1:50-2:04

1:46-1:48 =¬†***10m3*** **Chewie Chokes Lando** 2:06-2:08

1:48-end = silence

George Lucas must've decided he really did not like the music edits made to the stem here for the theatrical cut because for the Special Edition release he had it totally scrapped and redone for this cue:

0:00-2:08 = ***10m3*** **Chewie Chokes Lando** 0:00-2:08

2:08-end = silence

In my opinion this is one of the most underrated Special Edition changes. The original analog edits for this cue really sound terrible, and I don't like at all how the cue climaxes and fades out before the Slave I even escapes in the theatrical cut. I think the intended timing featured in the Special Edition works much much better. I will say though I think the decision to dial out all of the music for the Luke/Vader fight was definitely the right one. As brilliant as the music is, and it is some of the best music in the entire score, it really does not fit the mood of the scene at all. Having heroic Force theme and Yoda's theme play as Luke is struggling to survive and run away from Vader really doesn't fit, as cool as it sounds.

Since I don't have a recording log I'm unsure what day this cue was recorded, but based on the take numbers written in the sketch it was likely recorded in early January. I'm also unsure how many takes were recorded, but according to the sketch the performance edit uses takes 168 and 169.

This cue has been officially released on four different albums:

1) In 1980 on RSO Records' OST album

2) In 1993 on Arista Records' 4-CD Anthology box set

3) In 1997 on RCA Victor's 2-CD Special Edition set

4) In 2018 on Walt Disney Records' Remastered album (remastered OST rebuilt from scratch from the session masters)

More specifically, ***10m3*** **Chewie Chokes Lando**can be heard:

From 0:00-3:17 of track 6 "Departure of Boba Fett" on the 1980/2018 albums

From 7:33-end of disc 4 track 17 "Carbon Freeze/Luke Pursues the Captives/Departure of Boba Fett" on the 1993 album

From 8:12-end of disc 2 track 6 "Carbon Freeze/Darth Vader's Trap/Departure of Boba Fett" on the 1997 album.

Only the 1993 and 1997 releases have the clean ending, and none of the releases have the clean opening. The 1993 and 1997 releases have the film mix, every other release is remixed. The 1993 release also accidentally flips the left and right channels.

The 1980/2018 releases are both cutdown, snipping out 0:00-0:16, 1:01-1:17 and 3:27-end:

![Fandom Image](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/797b433d-7b2e-4865-acbe-af66c88da0fb/scale-to-width/755)

Additionally, most releases of this cue play at the wrong pitch/speed, although this can easily be corrected in an audio editor:

* 1980 set has to be sped up by 0.300 before the microedit and by 0.060 after the microedit. Why these speeds are different I'm not entirely sure.
* 1993 set has to be slowed down by -0.190 to match film
* 1997 set¬†has to be sped up by 0.570 to match film
* 2018 set has to be slowed down by -0.650 before the microedit and by -0.425 after the microedit. Why these speeds are different I'm not entirely sure.

For my edit I used a speed corrected copy of the 1993 set, with a clean opening taken from the Radio Drama (ESB episode 9, 13:04). Thankfully it contained this cue with a clean opening without any SFX or dialogue, no AI required.
