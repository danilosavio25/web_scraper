The **Jedi Archives**, also known as the **Jedi Temple Library**, was the [Jedi Order](/wiki/Jedi_Order "Jedi Order")'s repository of knowledge on [Coruscant](/wiki/Coruscant "Coruscant"), rumored to hold the sum total of all galactic knowledge. They were overseen by the [Council of First Knowledge](/wiki/Council_of_First_Knowledge "Council of First Knowledge"). The **Archives** housed incredible amounts of data which were stored electronically and holographically, making them very possibly the single largest source of information in the galaxy. It was here that [Jedi](/wiki/Jedi "Jedi") of nearly all ages would come to learn new information, whether it be for personal curiosity, studying for tests, or preparing for assignments. The Archives could also be used to search for specific members of the Order. Files, or profiles, were kept of each member, whether alive, [dead](/wiki/Death "Death"), or if they left the order. These files included colon-labeled information giving the persons' name, age, species, home planet, and clan. Similar profiles were kept of known criminals, making studies for more dangerous missions all the more informative. Besides standard [data tapes](/wiki/Data-tape "Data-tape") and holobooks, the Archives contained [holocrons](/wiki/Holocron "Holocron"), which are polyhedral-shaped devices that could store phenomenal amounts of data. These were housed in a vault prohibited to non-Jedi.

Located in the Jedi Temple on Coruscant, the Jedi Archives was the largest repository of information in the galaxy.

 

Established in the [Jedi Temple](/wiki/Imperial_Palace "Imperial Palace") on [Coruscant](/wiki/Coruscant "Coruscant") by the [Jedi Order](/wiki/Jedi_Order "Jedi Order") early in the [Temple's](/wiki/Jedi_temple "Jedi temple") history, the [Jedi](/wiki/Jedi "Jedi") Archives held the few of the [holobooks](/wiki/Holobook "Holobook") and artifacts that were [evacuated](/wiki/Evacuation_of_Ossus "Evacuation of Ossus") from the [Great Jedi Library](/wiki/Great_Jedi_Library "Great Jedi Library") of [Ossus](/wiki/Ossus "Ossus"). The Archives,[[8]](#cite_note-NoP-8) also known as the Jedi Temple [Library](/wiki/Library "Library"),[[9]](#cite_note-TSWB-9) contained historical records dating back thousands of [years](/wiki/Standard_year "Standard year"); maps of the entire [galaxy](/wiki/The_galaxy "The galaxy"); scientific, mathematical and astronomical journals; [engineering](/wiki/Engineer "Engineer") and technology documents; and Jedi records on the [Sith](/wiki/Sith "Sith"). It detailed the geography and cultures of various [planets](/wiki/Planet "Planet") and [species](/wiki/Species "Species") across the [galaxy](/wiki/Galaxy "Galaxy"), as well as their zoology and botany. Secrets about how the Jedi used [the Force](/wiki/The_Force "The Force") and biographies of numerous Jedi and their identification details were also housed in the Archives.[[8]](#cite_note-NoP-8)

Built near the top of the Temple [ziggurat](/wiki/Ziggurat "Ziggurat"), the Archives was a two-story cruciform chamber centered around a large rotunda. Four wings branched out from the rotunda and held tens of millions of books. The central reference desk was located at the heart of the Archives.[[8]](#cite_note-NoP-8)

Obi-Wan Kenobi unsuccessfully searched the Jedi Archives for information on the mysterious world of Kamino.

 

The [Chief Librarian](/wiki/Chief_Librarian "Chief Librarian"), assisted by associate librarians and [JN-66](/wiki/JN-66_analysis_droid "JN-66 analysis droid") and [SP-4 analysis droids](/wiki/SP-4_analysis_droid "SP-4 analysis droid"), administered the Archives. During the [Clone Wars](/wiki/Clone_Wars "Clone Wars"), it was overseen by [Jocasta Nu](/wiki/Jocasta_Nu "Jocasta Nu"), who assisted Jedi in finding the information they needed and also controlled access to the restricted [Holocron Vault](/wiki/Holocron_Vault "Holocron Vault") within the library. Before the [Battle](/wiki/First_Battle_of_Geonosis "First Battle of Geonosis") of [Geonosis](/wiki/Geonosis "Geonosis"), [Jedi Knight](/wiki/Jedi_Knight "Jedi Knight") [Obi-Wan Kenobi](/wiki/Obi-Wan_Kenobi "Obi-Wan Kenobi") tried to find information on the planet [Kamino](/wiki/Kamino "Kamino") to find the [bounty hunter](/wiki/Bounty_hunter "Bounty hunter") [Jango Fett](/wiki/Jango_Fett "Jango Fett"). He and Nu discovered that there was no information on the system. Kenobi and [Yoda](/wiki/Yoda "Yoda") deduced that a Jedi had erased the information on Kamino.[[2]](#cite_note-AOTC-2)

 [![After the Jedi Order fell, the Galactic Empire took control of the Jedi Archives along with the rest of the Temple.](data:image/gif;base64,R0lGODlhAQABAIABAAAAAP///yH5BAEAAAEALAAAAAABAAEAQAICTAEAOw%3D%3D)](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/starwars/images/8/8e/VaderVSGrandInquisitor-2017DarthVader6.png/revision/latest?cb=20190718233018) [![After the Jedi Order fell, the Galactic Empire took control of the Jedi Archives along with the rest of the Temple.](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/starwars/images/8/8e/VaderVSGrandInquisitor-2017DarthVader6.png/revision/latest/scale-to-width-down/250?cb=20190718233018)](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/starwars/images/8/8e/VaderVSGrandInquisitor-2017DarthVader6.png/revision/latest?cb=20190718233018) 

After the Jedi Order fell, the Galactic Empire took control of the Jedi Archives along with the rest of the Temple.

 

At some point during the [Great Jedi Purge](/wiki/Great_Jedi_Purge "Great Jedi Purge"), Jedi Master [Cere Junda](/wiki/Cere_Junda "Cere Junda") witnessed the Jedi Archives burning while she was on the run.[[10]](#cite_note-Battle_Scars-10) After the [rise](/wiki/Proclamation_of_the_New_Order "Proclamation of the New Order") of the [Galactic Empire](/wiki/Galactic_Empire "Galactic Empire"), [Emperor](/wiki/Galactic_Emperor "Galactic Emperor") [Palpatine](/wiki/Darth_Sidious "Darth Sidious") allowed [the Grand Inquisitor](/wiki/The_Grand_Inquisitor "The Grand Inquisitor") to study the records about the Jedi and their [combat](/wiki/Lightsaber_combat "Lightsaber combat") forms. It was at this [time](/wiki/Time "Time") Nu, who survived the [fall](/wiki/Operation:_Knightfall "Operation: Knightfall") of the Temple, [returned](/wiki/Hunt_for_Jocasta_Nu "Hunt for Jocasta Nu") to the Temple.[[11]](#cite_note-Dying_Light_I-11) Unnoticed by the [Grand Inquisitor](/wiki/Grand_Inquisitor "Grand Inquisitor"), Nu entered a secret vault within the Archives, where she retrieved a [list](/wiki/Kyber_memory_crystal "Kyber memory crystal") of every known [Force-sensitive](/wiki/Force-sensitive "Force-sensitive") [children](/wiki/Youngling "Youngling"), as of just before the [purge](/wiki/Great_Jedi_Purge "Great Jedi Purge"). Though intent on leaving without conflict, upon sighting the [Inquisitor](/wiki/Inquisitorius "Inquisitorius") again, Nu accosted him with [her](/wiki/Jocasta_Nu%27s_lightsaber "Jocasta Nu's lightsaber") [lightsaber](/wiki/Lightsaber "Lightsaber"), offended that he dared to read her [books](/wiki/Book "Book") only to toss them aside. After a brief [lightsaber duel](/wiki/Lightsaber_duel "Lightsaber duel") Nu was bested, but as the Inquisitor prepared to deliver the final blow, he was interrupted by [Darth Vader](/wiki/Anakin_Skywalker "Anakin Skywalker").[[12]](#cite_note-Dying_Light_II-12) On orders from Emperor Palpatine, Vader demanded her alive. In the confrontation that followed, Nu [Force pushed](/wiki/Force_push "Force push") the two and fled. Retreating to a [computer](/wiki/Computer "Computer") in the library, Nu subsequently purged all the archive files. As Vader continued his pursuit, Nu retrieved a [weapon](/wiki/Lightsaber_rifle "Lightsaber rifle") stored in the archives that was capable of fighting Vader.[[11]](#cite_note-Dying_Light_I-11)

Later, the Emperor converted the Temple into his Palace.[[13]](#cite_note-Rise_of_the_Old_Masters-13)[[14]](#cite_note-AbEverything-14) The [Imperial Security Bureau](/wiki/Imperial_Security_Bureau "Imperial Security Bureau") took control of the remaining secrets within the Archives, removing all Jedi-related material and destroying much of their history in the process within a year of the library's seizure.[[8]](#cite_note-NoP-8) The Jedi Archives would be restored in some form later, documenting various individuals important to recent [galactic history](/wiki/Timeline_of_galactic_history "Timeline of galactic history"), such as [Luke Skywalker](/wiki/Luke_Skywalker "Luke Skywalker"), in holocrons,[[15]](#cite_note-Luke_Temple_Archives-15) much like the Sith Information Guide.[[16]](#cite_note-Luke_Sith_Guide-16)

## Behind the scenes[]

 [![The Long Room in Trinity College in Dublin, Ireland](data:image/gif;base64,R0lGODlhAQABAIABAAAAAP///yH5BAEAAAEALAAAAAABAAEAQAICTAEAOw%3D%3D)](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/starwars/images/e/ed/Trinity-college-library-dub.jpg/revision/latest?cb=20120707133623) [![The Long Room in Trinity College in Dublin, Ireland](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/starwars/images/e/ed/Trinity-college-library-dub.jpg/revision/latest/scale-to-width-down/200?cb=20120707133623)](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/starwars/images/e/ed/Trinity-college-library-dub.jpg/revision/latest?cb=20120707133623) 

The Long Room in Trinity College in Dublin, Ireland

 

The Jedi Archives first appeared in the [2002](/wiki/2002 "2002") film [*Star Wars*: Episode II *Attack of the Clones*](/wiki/Star_Wars:_Episode_II_Attack_of_the_Clones "Star Wars: Episode II Attack of the Clones"), the second installment of the *[Star Wars](/wiki/Star_Wars "Star Wars")* [prequel trilogy](/wiki/Prequel_trilogy "Prequel trilogy").[[2]](#cite_note-AOTC-2) The Archives are actually an almost exact digital reconstruction of the [Long Room](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Library_of_Trinity_College_Dublin#Long_Room "wikipedia:Library of Trinity College Dublin") of [Trinity College](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Trinity_College_Dublin "wikipedia:Trinity College Dublin") in [Dublin](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dublin "wikipedia:Dublin"), [Ireland](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ireland "wikipedia:Ireland"). Its imitation extends to the busts of the Lost Twenty, which, in [Trinity Library](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Library_of_Trinity_College_Dublin "wikipedia:Library of Trinity College Dublin"), are busts of alumni, including writer [Jonathan Swift](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Jonathan_Swift "wikipedia:Jonathan Swift").[[17]](#cite_note-Irish-17)

According to [Pablo Helman](/wiki/Pablo_Helman "Pablo Helman"), during the scene where Kenobi speaks to Nu, the room includes busts of himself, [George Lucas](/wiki/George_Lucas "George Lucas"), [Rob Coleman](/wiki/Rob_Coleman "Rob Coleman"), and [John Knoll](/wiki/John_Knoll "John Knoll").[[18]](#cite_note-Commentary-18)
