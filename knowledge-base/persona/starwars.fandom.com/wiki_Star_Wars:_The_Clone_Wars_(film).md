***Star Wars: The Clone Wars*** is a [2008](/wiki/2008 "2008") animated feature film directed by [Dave Filoni](/wiki/Dave_Filoni "Dave Filoni") and [executive produced](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Executive_producer "wikipedia:Executive producer") by [George Lucas](/wiki/George_Lucas "George Lucas") as well as the first Star Wars animated film made. The plot focuses on a struggle between the [Galactic Republic](/wiki/Galactic_Republic "Galactic Republic") and the [Confederacy of Independent Systems](/wiki/Confederacy_of_Independent_Systems "Confederacy of Independent Systems")—each vying for [Jabba the Hutt's](/wiki/Jabba_Desilijic_Tiure "Jabba Desilijic Tiure") permission to use [Hutt Space](/wiki/Hutt_Space "Hutt Space")'s [trade routes](/wiki/Hyperspace_route "Hyperspace route"). In an attempt to gain Jabba's favor, [Sith Lord](/wiki/Sith_Lord "Sith Lord") and Separatist leader [Count](/wiki/Count "Count") [Dooku](/wiki/Dooku "Dooku") kidnaps Jabba's son [Rotta](/wiki/Rotta "Rotta"), in hopes of framing the Republic's [Jedi Order](/wiki/Jedi_Order "Jedi Order") as the true captors.

The film is set during the three-year time period between the films [*Star Wars*: Episode II *Attack of the Clones*](/wiki/Star_Wars:_Episode_II_Attack_of_the_Clones "Star Wars: Episode II Attack of the Clones") (2002) and [*Star Wars*: Episode III *Revenge of the Sith*](/wiki/Star_Wars:_Episode_III_Revenge_of_the_Sith "Star Wars: Episode III Revenge of the Sith") (2005). *The Clone Wars* served as an introduction to the [animated series of the same name](/wiki/Star_Wars:_The_Clone_Wars "Star Wars: The Clone Wars") that premiered on [Cartoon Network](/wiki/Cartoon_Network "Cartoon Network") on [October 3](/wiki/October_3 "October 3"), 2008. Lucas was inspired to make the movie while reviewing footage from the series and described the film as "almost an afterthought." The film was released [August 15](/wiki/August_15 "August 15"), 2008.

The film was a box office success, and grossed $68.3 million worldwide against an $8.5 million budget,[[6]](#cite_note-TCWOCE-6) and earned $14.6 million during its opening weekend.

The film chronologically takes place between the [Season One](/wiki/The_Clone_Wars:_Season_One "The Clone Wars: Season One") episode "[The Hidden Enemy](/wiki/The_Hidden_Enemy "The Hidden Enemy")" and [Season Three](/wiki/The_Clone_Wars:_Season_Three "The Clone Wars: Season Three") episode "[Clone Cadets](/wiki/Clone_Cadets "Clone Cadets")."

## Plot summary[]

The film begins with a narrator explaining the state of the [Clone Wars](/wiki/Clone_Wars "Clone Wars"). The Separatists control the majority of the [hyperlanes](/wiki/Hyperspace_route "Hyperspace route"), leaving Republic forces stranded in different parts of the [Outer Rim](/wiki/Outer_Rim_Territories "Outer Rim Territories"). An [octopus-like craft](/wiki/Trident-class_assault_ship "Trident-class assault ship") heads for [Tatooine](/wiki/Tatooine "Tatooine") to capture [Jabba](/wiki/Jabba_Desilijic_Tiure "Jabba Desilijic Tiure") the [Hutt](/wiki/Hutt "Hutt")'s son [Rotta](/wiki/Rotta "Rotta") as part of a plan to make the Hutts join the [Clone Wars](/wiki/Clone_Wars "Clone Wars").

Meanwhile, a fierce battle is taking place on the crystalline [planet](/wiki/Planet "Planet") of [Christophsis](/wiki/Christophsis "Christophsis") between the Republic's very limited [clone army](/wiki/Grand_Army_of_the_Republic "Grand Army of the Republic") and the [Retail Caucus](/wiki/Retail_Caucus "Retail Caucus") forces. With the help of [Obi-Wan Kenobi](/wiki/Obi-Wan_Kenobi "Obi-Wan Kenobi"), [Anakin Skywalker](/wiki/Anakin_Skywalker "Anakin Skywalker"), [Rex](/wiki/Rex "Rex"), and [Cody](/wiki/Cody "Cody"), the clones steadily advance on the Separatists' forces, gaining the Republic an early victory. It doesn't last long, though, as the droid army under General [Loathsom](/wiki/Whorm_Loathsom "Whorm Loathsom") soon returns for more bloodshed. With no communications or the ability to fly in reinforcements, the fate of the few remaining clone soldiers are in the hands of Obi-Wan and Anakin. A shuttle soon comes with an important delivery; thinking it's reinforcements, the Jedi are suprised to find just a young [Padawan](/wiki/Padawan "Padawan") named [Ahsoka Tano](/wiki/Ahsoka_Tano "Ahsoka Tano"), who insists that she has been sent by Master [Yoda](/wiki/Yoda "Yoda") to serve as Anakin's Padawan.

The battle resumes, with [Separatist](/wiki/Confederacy_of_Independent_Systems "Confederacy of Independent Systems") forces advancing behind an expanding shield which the Republic's artillery is unable to penetrate. Tasked with taking down the [deflector](/wiki/Deflector_shield "Deflector shield") shield, Anakin and Ahsoka succeed in penetrating the enemy lines, using all their stealth, cunning and ability to improvise, while Obi-Wan stalls for time by holding a fake surrender negotiation with Loathsom.

Soon after the [final victory](/wiki/Battle_of_Christophsis "Battle of Christophsis") for the Republic on [Christophsis](/wiki/Christophsis "Christophsis"), Master Yoda arrives with the urgent message that a mysterious group of renegades has kidnapped Jabba's son, and it's up to Anakin, Ahsoka, Rex, and the clones to rescue him and bring him back home safely. Obi-Wan flies to [Tatooine](/wiki/Tatooine "Tatooine") to assure Jabba that Rotta will be returned safely and to secure a promise by the Hutts to use their trading routes for safe passage within the [Outer Rim](/wiki/Outer_Rim_Territories "Outer Rim Territories").

After briefing in a Republic flagship, Anakin's forces descend through the clouds of the jungle planet of [Teth](/wiki/Teth "Teth") for one of the many natural stone pillars which litter the landscape. Under heavy fire, Anakin, Ahsoka and Rex storm the [monastery](/wiki/B%27omarr_Order_Monastery "B'omarr Order Monastery") atop the pillar and find Rotta, who has gotten sick, only to be caught in an elaborate trap: Count [Dooku](/wiki/Dooku "Dooku") has staged the kidnapping himself in order to blacklist the [Jedi](/wiki/Jedi "Jedi") among the Hutts. First he has the Jedi retrieve the young [Hutt](/wiki/Hutt "Hutt") to secure fake evidence and then [Asajj Ventress](/wiki/Asajj_Ventress "Asajj Ventress"), as the overseer of the operation, is charged with either retrieving or killing the young Huttlet.

While quarreling all the while about the proper procedure, yet slowly gaining respect of one another, Anakin and Ahsoka manage to escape the trap along with [R2-D2](/wiki/R2-D2 "R2-D2") and hijack a derelict [transport](/wiki/Twilight "Twilight"), which they use to travel to Tatooine. Ahsoka uses medicine onboard to treat the sick Huttlet. Obi-Wan, alerted by Anakin, arrives just in time to relieve Rex and the rest of his forces and engages Ventress in combat where he manages to defeat her, though Ventress flees in the face of capture.

In the meantime, [Senator](/wiki/Senator "Senator") [Amidala](/wiki/Padm%C3%A9_Amidala "Padmé Amidala") learns of Anakin's mission. Worried about him, she decides to contact Jabba's uncle, [Ziro the Hutt](/wiki/Ziro_Desilijic_Tiure "Ziro Desilijic Tiure"), who lives in a shady part of [Coruscant](/wiki/Coruscant "Coruscant"). The Hutt strangely refuses to cooperate, and soon Padmé finds out why: Ziro has actually conspired with Dooku to engineer the downfall of his nephew to seize the power over the Hutt clans for himself. Padmé is discovered and detained, but a chance call by [C-3PO](/wiki/C-3PO "C-3PO") enables her to summon help, and Ziro is soon arrested by the [Coruscant Guard](/wiki/Coruscant_Guard "Coruscant Guard").

Upon their arrival on Tatooine, Anakin and Ahsoka are attacked by [MagnaGuards](/wiki/IG-100_MagnaGuard "IG-100 MagnaGuard") and shot down. Faced with a long way across desert sands and relentless opponents, Anakin devises a ruse; when Dooku corners Anakin, the pack he is carrying is revealed to contain merely rocks. Ahsoka and R2 travel with Rotta to Jabba's palace unmolested, but just as they near it, Ahsoka is ambushed by three MagnaGuards, whom she narrowly defeats. But as Anakin and Ahsoka enter the palace one after another, they are threatened with execution by Jabba's men even though Rotta is safely returned. Padmé calls just in time to convince Jabba of his uncle's duplicity, while Anakin and Ahsoka are triumphantly retrieved by Obi-Wan, Yoda, Cody and the clones. Dooku tells Sidious about his failure but Sidious tells Dooku that the tide of war still turned in their favor according with Sidious' master plan as Jabba allowed the Republic to use the Hutt Cartel hyperspace lanes.

## Development[]

*Star Wars: The Clone Wars* was designed to serve as both a stand-alone story and a lead-in to the weekly [animated TV series of the same name](/wiki/Star_Wars:_The_Clone_Wars "Star Wars: The Clone Wars").[[7]](#cite_note-OfficialBlog-Report_from_Wonder-Con:_Lucasfilm_Presentation_Part_3-7) [George Lucas](/wiki/George_Lucas "George Lucas") had the idea for a film after viewing some of the already completed footage on the big screen.[[5]](#cite_note-CWQA-5) [Warner Bros.](/wiki/Warner_Bros. "Warner Bros.") had tracked the series' development from the beginning, and Lucas decided on a theatrical launch after viewing some footage,[[8]](#cite_note-aswv-8) and deciding, "This is so beautiful, why don't we just go and use the crew and make a feature?" Lucas described the film was "almost an afterthought."[[9]](#cite_note-EW_Lucas_Q&A-9) [Howard Roffman](/wiki/Howard_Roffman "Howard Roffman"), president of [Lucas Licensing](/wiki/Lucas_Licensing "Lucas Licensing"), said of the decision, "Sometimes George works in strange ways."[[10]](#cite_note-NYT0701-10) Producer [Catherine Winder](/wiki/Catherine_Winder "Catherine Winder") said the sudden decision added to an already large challenge of establishing a show "of this sophistication and complexity,"[[11]](#cite_note-Georgia-11) but she felt it was a good way to start the series, and she felt budgetary constraints forced the production team to think outside the box in a positive way.[[11]](#cite_note-Georgia-11) One of the film's main plot threads—the kidnapping of [Jabba the Hutt's son](/wiki/Rotta "Rotta")—was inspired by the film [*Shogun's Shadow*](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Shogun%27s_Shadow "wikipedia:Shogun's Shadow").[[12]](#cite_note-12)

The main part of the film was composed of three episodes with similar titles originally meant to form a trilogy: "Castle of Deception," "Castle of Doom"[[13]](#cite_note-pablo-13) and "Castle of Salvation,"[[14]](#cite_note-bullockint-14) while a standalone episode "The New Padawan," which was developed later in the production as a flashback episode during the series, became the opening sequence of the movie, eventually leading to the introduction of [Ahsoka Tano](/wiki/Ahsoka_Tano "Ahsoka Tano").[[15]](#cite_note-db-15)

[Lucasfilm Animation](/wiki/Lucasfilm_Animation "Lucasfilm Animation") used [Autodesk](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Autodesk "wikipedia:Autodesk") [software](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Software "wikipedia:Software") to animate the film. The [Maya](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Maya_(software) "wikipedia:Maya (software)") [3D modeling](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/3D_modeler "wikipedia:3D modeler") program was used to create highly detailed worlds, characters and creatures.[[16]](#cite_note-16) The film's animation style was designed to pay homage to the stylized looks of both [Japanese](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Japan "wikipedia:Japan") [anime](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Anime "wikipedia:Anime") and [manga](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Manga "wikipedia:Manga"), and the [supermarionation](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Supermarionation "wikipedia:Supermarionation") of the [British](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/England "wikipedia:England") [1960s](/wiki/1960s "1960s") series [*Thunderbirds*](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Thunderbirds "wikipedia:Thunderbirds"). At a [Cartoon Network](/wiki/Cartoon_Network "Cartoon Network")-hosted discussion, Lucas said did not want the *Clone Wars* film or television series to look like such movies as [*Beowulf*](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Beowulf_(2007_film) "wikipedia:Beowulf (2007 film)") because he wanted a stylized look rather than a realistic one, and he did not want it to look like the popular [Pixar](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pixar "wikipedia:Pixar") films, such as [*The Incredibles*](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/The_Incredibles "wikipedia:The Incredibles") because he wanted the film and characters to have a unique style.[[17]](#cite_note-Broadcasting_&_Cable-17) Lucas also decided to create the animated film and series from a live-action perspective, which Winder said set it apart from other CGI films because it "meant using long camera shots, aggressive lighting techniques, and relying on editing instead of storyboards."[[11]](#cite_note-Georgia-11) Animators also reviewed designs from the [original 2003 *Clone Wars* series](/wiki/Star_Wars:_Clone_Wars "Star Wars: Clone Wars") when creating the animation style for the film and the new series.[[18]](#cite_note-18)

The film's music was composed by [Kevin Kiner](/wiki/Kevin_Kiner "Kevin Kiner").[[19]](#cite_note-soundtrack-19) Steward Lee is a storyboard artist.[[8]](#cite_note-aswv-8) When it was decided to produce the film, Lucasfilm contacted all the major film *Star Wars* actors to see who was interested in voicing their characters for *The Clone Wars*.[[20]](#cite_note-20) In the end, [Anthony Daniels](/wiki/Anthony_Daniels "Anthony Daniels"), [Matthew Wood](/wiki/Matthew_Wood "Matthew Wood"), [Christopher Lee](/wiki/Christopher_Lee "Christopher Lee") and [Samuel L. Jackson](/wiki/Samuel_L._Jackson "Samuel L. Jackson") returned to voice their respective characters.[[21]](#cite_note-21) Several voice actors also reprised their roles from the [2003 series](/wiki/Star_Wars:_Clone_Wars "Star Wars: Clone Wars").

Warner Bros. Animation, [Industrial Light & Magic](/wiki/Industrial_Light_%26_Magic "Industrial Light & Magic"), Production I.G, [Polygon Pictures](/wiki/Polygon_Pictures "Polygon Pictures"), and CGCG contributed some animation for the film.

## Release and reception[]

:   "*This is a breakthrough project -- returning Star Wars to the big screen in a completely new way while beginning an exciting new chapter in George Lucas' legendary saga. We immediately felt that it would be a fantastic theatrical event and are thrilled to be bringing it to moviegoers.*"
:   ―Dan Fellman, Warner Bros. Pictures President of Domestic Distribution[[3]](#cite_note-ann-3)

At Hasbro's fall 2007 analyst event, it was announced that *Star Wars: The Clone Wars* would have a "theatrical launch" on [August 8](/wiki/August_8 "August 8"), 2008, with it beginning to be shown on TV during that fall.[[22]](#cite_note-22) Although Lucasfilm had previously announced that it would produce an animated feature film in 2008,[[23]](#cite_note-23) when initially asked for confirmation, an LFL representative replied that "It's one of the many things being discussed but we have no decisions yet. For us it's all about finding a creative way to launch a creative TV series."[[24]](#cite_note-24)

At ToyFare 2008, a LEGO official announced that an animated feature film would be released in the UK in September 2008,[[25]](#cite_note-25) and would be made up of the first three episodes of the series, with regular episodes airing later on television on an at-the-time unrevealed channel.[[26]](#cite_note-26) It was officially announced on [February 12](/wiki/February_12 "February 12"), 2008 that the feature film would be released in theaters on [August 15](/wiki/August_15 "August 15"), 2008, with the TV series debuting in the fall.[[3]](#cite_note-ann-3) Warner Bros. showed a preview of the film in Las Vegas, Nevada on March 13, 2008.[[27]](#cite_note-27) Lucas attended the event and held a question and answer session.[[5]](#cite_note-CWQA-5) The first theatrical trailer debuted on May 8, 2008 on five separate [Turner Network](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Turner_Broadcasting_System "wikipedia:Turner Broadcasting System") channels.[[28]](#cite_note-SWArchive-The_Trailer_&_Poster_for_Star_Wars:_The_Clone_Wars-28) A second trailer was released in June 2008.[[29]](#cite_note-SW-The_Clone_Wars:_Theatrical_Trailer_#2-29)

The film premiered at [Grauman's Egyptian Theatre](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Grauman%27s_Egyptian_Theatre "wikipedia:Grauman's Egyptian Theatre") on [August 10](/wiki/August_10 "August 10"), 2008,[[30]](#cite_note-SWArchive-Star_Wars:_The_Clone_Wars_To_Premiere_at_Egyptian_Theatre-30) and was later screened on [August 14](/wiki/August_14 "August 14"), 2008 at [Los Angeles's](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Los_Angeles,_California "wikipedia:Los Angeles, California") [Nokia Theater](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nokia_Theater "wikipedia:Nokia Theater") accompanying a Lucasfilm VIP party with special guests John Knoll and Dave Filoni. SpikeTV producer Terry Minogue speculated that his channel would debut the film on television at some point after its theatrical release.[[31]](#cite_note-31) The film had its US broadcast premiere on Cartoon Network on November 20, 2010.[[32]](#cite_note-SWArchive-See_Where_The_Clone_Wars_Began_This_Saturday_on_Cartoon_Network-32)

*Star Wars: The Clone Wars* merchandise was first released on [July 26](/wiki/July_26 "July 26"), 2008. [Hasbro](/wiki/Hasbro "Hasbro") released several 3 3/4-inch *Clone Wars* [action figures](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Action_figure "wikipedia:Action figure"), an electronic clone trooper helmet, a customizable lightsaber, and an electronic [AT-TE](/wiki/All_Terrain_Tactical_Enforcer "All Terrain Tactical Enforcer").[[33]](#cite_note-OfficialBlog-Hasbro's_Clone_Wars_figures_at_Toy_Fair-33) [Toys "R" Us](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Toys_%22R%22_Us "wikipedia:Toys \"R\" Us") mounted digital clocks in all 585 of its stores that counted down to the release of the *Clone Wars* toys, and more than 225 of the stores opened at midnight for the debut of the *Star Wars* products. Two of the Toys "R" Us flagship outlets in [Mission Bay](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mission_Bay,_San_Diego,_California "wikipedia:Mission Bay, San Diego, California"), [San Diego](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/San_Diego,_California "wikipedia:San Diego, California"), [California](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/California "wikipedia:California") and [Times Square](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Times_Square "wikipedia:Times Square") in [Manhattan](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Manhattan "wikipedia:Manhattan"), [New York City](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/New_York_City "wikipedia:New York City") held costume and trivia contests on July 26 and gave away limited-edition *Star Wars* toys with every purchase. A section of the Toys "R" Us [website](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Website "wikipedia:Website") was also dedicated to *The Clone Wars*.[[10]](#cite_note-NYT0701-10)

Due to the Lucas's sudden decision to make a *Clone Wars* film after viewing footage of the television show, Lucas Licensing did not have time to enter into agreements with previous *Star Wars* marketing partners like [Pepsi](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pepsi "wikipedia:Pepsi"), [Burger King](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Burger_King "wikipedia:Burger King") and [Kellogg's](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kellogg_Company "wikipedia:Kellogg Company"), with which the [Lucasfilm](/wiki/Lucasfilm "Lucasfilm") licensing company had a 10-year marketing plan for the other films; when questioned by [*The New York Times*](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/The_New_York_Times "wikipedia:The New York Times") for a *Star Wars* merchandising in July 2006, a Pepsi spokesperson was unaware a new *Star Wars* film was even being released. [Target](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Target_Corporation "wikipedia:Target Corporation") and [KB Toys](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/KB_Toys "wikipedia:KB Toys") also devoted shelf space for *Clone Wars* toys, but did not hold midnight releases or pursue the branding opportunities Toys "R" Us did. The [McDonald's](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/McDonald%27s "wikipedia:McDonald's") [fast food restaurant](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fast_food_restaurant "wikipedia:Fast food restaurant") chain held its first ever [Happy Meal](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Happy_Meal "wikipedia:Happy Meal") promotion for a *Star Wars* movie starting on August 15. For four weeks, 18 exclusive toys came in specially designed Happy Meal boxes.[[10]](#cite_note-NYT0701-10)

[DK Publishing](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/DK_Publishing "wikipedia:DK Publishing") and [Penguin Group](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Penguin_Group "wikipedia:Penguin Group") released tie-in books, activities and other merchandise, including *[Star Wars: The Clone Wars: The Visual Guide](/wiki/Star_Wars:_The_Clone_Wars:_The_Visual_Guide "Star Wars: The Clone Wars: The Visual Guide")* (published by DK) and *Star Wars: The Clone Wars* (published by Puffin in the UK and by Grosset & Dunlap in the U.S). ### Home video[]

Warner Bros. also has the home video rights, and had planned a DVD release of the film and TV series as early as February 2008.[[8]](#cite_note-aswv-8) *Star Wars: The Clone Wars* was released on home video on [November 11](/wiki/November_11 "November 11"), 2008, as a single-disc DVD, a two-disc Special Edition DVD set, and a two-disc Blu-ray set. The single and double-disc standard-definition versions both include the widescreen film with Dolby Digital 5.1 Surround EX sound, and the feature-length audio commentary.

The single-disc DVD includes as a special feature only an audio commentary by Director [Dave Filoni](/wiki/Dave_Filoni "Dave Filoni"), Producer [Catherine Winder](/wiki/Catherine_Winder "Catherine Winder"), Writer [Henry Gilroy](/wiki/Henry_Gilroy "Henry Gilroy"), and Editor [Jason Tucker](/wiki/Jason_Tucker "Jason Tucker").

The two-disc editions include the above commentary plus:

* *The Clone Wars: The Untold Stories*: Preview stories, vehicles, planets, and battles from [Season One of The Clone Wars television series](/wiki/The_Clone_Wars:_Season_One "The Clone Wars: Season One").
* *The Voices of The Clone Wars*: Voiceover actors and animated characters in split-screen performances.
* *A New Score*: Composer Kevin Kiner establishing a new musical identity for the series.
* Gallery of Concept and Production Art
* *Webisodes*: Six making-of featurettes, as seen on StarWars.com
* *Deleted Scenes*: *Cargo Bay*, *Platform Droid Fight*, *[Rancor](/wiki/Rancor "Rancor") Pit*, and *Through the [Tanks](/wiki/NR-N99_Persuader-class_droid_enforcer "NR-N99 Persuader-class droid enforcer")*
* Theatrical and video game trailers
* Digital copy of the film on the second disc

The Blu-ray editions include all the above features in 1080p high definition (deleted Scenes and certain trailers are not in HD) on a single 50GB disc. Audio commentary is not present.

* *A Creative Conversation Video Commentary*: Director Dave Filoni, Producer Catherine Winder, Writer Henry Gilroy, and Editor Jason Tucker discuss bringing the *Star Wars* legend on the big screen again
* *Take the Hologram Memory Challenge*: Test your skill and memory and unlock three hi-def TV series sneak peeks
* Digital copy of the film on a second disc

## Reception ‡[]

:   "*As a fan of 'Star Wars' for so long, I always knew there would be debate. No matter what you do with 'Star Wars,' you're going to have a huge debate about it. I actually think its part of the fun of being a fan; having the big arguments over 'I like this' and 'I didn't like that' or 'this aspect fit with what I thought' but 'this aspect didn't.' I think one of the greatest things is that people are still talking about 'Star Wars.' 'Anakin has a Padawan? I've never heard of that before?' But now everybody is talking about the fact that Anakin has a Padawan and the movie introduced that idea.*"
:   ―David Filoni[[48]](#cite_note-Filoni_Reception-48)

*The Clone Wars* earned $68.28 million worldwide, including $35.16 million in domestic box office grosses and $33.12 million in foreign grosses.[[49]](#cite_note-49) The movie earned $14.6 million on 3,590 screens its opening weekend,[[50]](#cite_note-Box_Office_Weekend-50) including $6.23 million on opening day August 15.[[51]](#cite_note-Box_Office_Opening_Day-51) It was the third-highest earning film of the weekend, behind the [action](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Action_film "wikipedia:Action film") [comedy](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Comedy_film "wikipedia:Comedy film") [*Tropic Thunder*](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tropic_Thunder "wikipedia:Tropic Thunder") and [Batman](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Batman "wikipedia:Batman") [sequel](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sequel "wikipedia:Sequel") [*The Dark Knight*](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/The_Dark_Knight_(film) "wikipedia:The Dark Knight (film)"), which earned $26 million and $16.8 million, respectively.[[50]](#cite_note-Box_Office_Weekend-50) Dan Fellman, head of distribution for Warner Bros. Pictures, said the box office performance met expectations because two-thirds of the audience were families, and because the movie was meant to introduce the animated series. Fellman said, "It was targeted to a specific audience for specific reasons. We accomplished that mission, and it will continue in another medium."[[52]](#cite_note-Box_Office_Fellman-52) When *The Clone Wars* dropped to $5.7 million in the second week, *ContactMusic.com* described it as "the first bona fide *Star Wars* flop."[[53]](#cite_note-53)
