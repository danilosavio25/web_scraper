***Brotherhood*** is a [canon](/wiki/Canon "Canon") adult novel by [Mike Chen](/wiki/Mike_Chen_(author) "Mike Chen (author)"). Published by [Del Rey](/wiki/Del_Rey "Del Rey") on [May 10](/wiki/May_10 "May 10"), [2022](/wiki/2022 "2022"), it focuses on [Obi-Wan Kenobi](/wiki/Obi-Wan_Kenobi "Obi-Wan Kenobi") and [Anakin Skywalker](/wiki/Anakin_Skywalker "Anakin Skywalker") during the [Clone Wars](/wiki/Clone_Wars "Clone Wars"). An [unabridged audiobook](/wiki/Brotherhood_(audiobook) "Brotherhood (audiobook)") version of the novel was released simultaneously.

## Publisher's summary[]

***Obi-Wan Kenobi and Anakin Skywalker must stem the tide of the raging Clone Wars and forge a new bond as Jedi Knights.***

*The Clone Wars have begun. Battle lines are being drawn throughout the galaxy. With every world that joins the Separatists, the peace guarded by the Jedi Order is slipping through their fingers.*

*After an explosion devastates Cato Neimoidia, the jewel of the Trade Federation, the Republic is blamed and the fragile neutrality of the planet is threatened. The Jedi dispatch Obi-Wan Kenobi, one of the Order's most gifted diplomatic minds, to investigate the crime and maintain the balance that has begun to dangerously shift. As Obi-Wan investigates with the help of a heroic Neimoidian guard, he finds himself working against the Separatists who hope to draw the planet into their conspiracy—and senses the sinister hand of Asajj Ventress in the mists that cloak the planet.*

*Amid the brewing chaos, Anakin Skywalker rises to the rank of Jedi Knight. Despite the mandate that Obi-Wan travel alone–and his former master's insistence that he listen this time—Anakin's headstrong determination means nothing can stop him from crashing the party, and bringing along a promising but conflicted youngling.*

*Once a Padawan to Obi-Wan, Anakin now finds himself on equal—but uncertain—footing with the man who raised him. The lingering friction between them increases the danger for everyone around them. The two knights must learn a new way to work together—and they must learn quickly, to save Cato Neimoidia and its people from the fires of war. To overcome the threat they face they must grow beyond master and apprentice. They must stand together as brothers.*[[1]](#cite_note-Penguin-1)

## Development[]

*Brotherhood* was known as "Playground Fight" during production.[[3]](#cite_note-Production-3) It was announced by [StarWars.com](/wiki/StarWars.com "StarWars.com") on [October 7](/wiki/October_7 "October 7"), [2021](/wiki/2021 "2021"), along with other upcoming titles.[[4]](#cite_note-New_Books-4) During its development, Chen worked closely with fellow *[Star Wars](/wiki/Star_Wars "Star Wars")* author [E. K. Johnston](/wiki/E._K._Johnston "E. K. Johnston"), whose novel *[Queen's Hope](/wiki/Queen%27s_Hope "Queen's Hope")* is set slightly prior to *Brotherhood*. They decided to focus specifically on 2 clone battalions, the 555th and the 302nd. The two authors ensured their stories synced together and created ties between them.[[5]](#cite_note-Mike_Chen_Queen's_Hope-5) Chen wanted the novel to feel like an episode of *[Star Wars: The Clone Wars](/wiki/Star_Wars:_The_Clone_Wars "Star Wars: The Clone Wars")*.[[6]](#cite_note-TCW_Inspired-6)

Chen based the mannerisms, voice, and outfit of the character [Ruug Quarnom](/wiki/Ruug_Quarnom "Ruug Quarnom") on [Kira Nerys](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kira_Nerys "wikipedia:Kira Nerys") from *[Star Trek](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Star_Trek "wikipedia:Star Trek")*.[[7]](#cite_note-Kira_Nerys-7) The [Korgee beast](/wiki/Korgee_beast "Korgee beast") got its name from the [corgi](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Welsh_Corgi "wikipedia:Welsh Corgi").[[8]](#cite_note-Corgi_fur-8) Whenever a [battle droid](/wiki/Battle_droid "Battle droid") in the text is identified simply as a "battle droid" without any further context, Chen intended for the unit to be a [B1-series battle droid](/wiki/B1-series_battle_droid "B1-series battle droid").[[9]](#cite_note-Chen_B1_1-9)[[10]](#cite_note-Chen_B1_2-10)[[11]](#cite_note-Chen_B1_3-11)[[12]](#cite_note-Chen_B1_4-12) For that reason, [B2-series super battle droids](/wiki/B2-series_super_battle_droid "B2-series super battle droid") are outright identified as "super" battle droids.[[11]](#cite_note-Chen_B1_3-11) The training droids that Ketar Nor spares with are B1 units equipped with training ammunition, with Chen later reasoning they were using ion-based weaponry.[[12]](#cite_note-Chen_B1_4-12)

## Continuity[]

### Relation to previous stories[]

:   "*So, if you want to treat* Labyrinth of Evil *as 'that business,' nothing that happens in my book will negate that. They take place at two different points in the timeline, so they can totally co-exist. It's totally cool.*"
:   ―Mike Chen[[13]](#cite_note-Chen_interview-13)

The [mission to Cato Neimoidia](/wiki/Mission_to_Cato_Neimoidia_(Kenobi_and_Skywalker) "Mission to Cato Neimoidia (Kenobi and Skywalker)") featured in the novel is the same incident first mentioned in the [2005](/wiki/2005 "2005") film [*Star Wars*: Episode III *Revenge of the Sith*](/wiki/Star_Wars:_Episode_III_Revenge_of_the_Sith "Star Wars: Episode III Revenge of the Sith").[[14]](#cite_note-Cover_reveal-14) "[Anakin Skywalker](/wiki/Anakin_Skywalker_(reference_book) "Anakin Skywalker (reference book)")," a [2021](/wiki/2021 "2021") issue of [De Agostini](/wiki/De_Agostini "De Agostini")'s *[Star Wars Encyclopedia](/wiki/Star_Wars_Encyclopedia_(series) "Star Wars Encyclopedia (series)")* series, had previously stated that Kenobi and Skywalker undertook an [operation on Cato Neimoidia](/wiki/Assault_on_Cato_Neimoidia "Assault on Cato Neimoidia") shortly before the [Battle of Yerbana](/wiki/Battle_of_Yerbana "Battle of Yerbana"), which it claimed to be the event referenced in *Revenge of the Sith*.[[15]](#cite_note-Anakin-15) The encyclopedia's account roughly corresponds to the [*Star Wars* Legends](/wiki/Star_Wars_Legends "Star Wars Legends") continuity's depiction of the event from the [2005](/wiki/2005 "2005") novel *[Labyrinth of Evil](/wiki/Labyrinth_of_Evil "Labyrinth of Evil")*, written by [James Luceno](/wiki/James_Luceno "James Luceno").[[16]](#cite_note-Labyrinth_of_Evil-16) Due to his love of *Labyrinth of Evil* and because his book takes place much earlier in the timeline, author Mike Chen has stated fans are free to continue to view the events seen in *Labyrinth* as "that business," even stating the two stories can co-exist.[[13]](#cite_note-Chen_interview-13)

Another connection with a Legends novel comes with a tie to the [*Revenge of the Sith* novelization](/wiki/Star_Wars:_Revenge_of_the_Sith_(novelization) "Star Wars: Revenge of the Sith (novelization)") by [Matthew Stover](/wiki/Matthew_Stover "Matthew Stover"): while that novelization sees a mission to an [unidentified dead star system](/wiki/Unidentified_dead_star_system "Unidentified dead star system") from Anakin Skywalker's point of view, Chen included a re-canonization of the scene, but now from Kenobi's perspective.[[17]](#cite_note-Connection_to_ROTS-17) The image of Anakin and Obi-Wan running together on the inside cover of the book is sourced from the cover of the [2002](/wiki/2002 "2002") Legends [comic book](/wiki/Star_Wars_comic_books "Star Wars comic books") [*Practice Makes Perfect*](/wiki/Practice_Makes_Perfect_(comic_book) "Practice Makes Perfect (comic book)").[[18]](#cite_note-Practice_Makes_Perfect_comic-18)

With the personal mission to reference it as much as possible,[[19]](#cite_note-Sneak_in-19) Chen also included a number of references to the *[Star Wars: Clone Wars](/wiki/Star_Wars:_Clone_Wars "Star Wars: Clone Wars")* microseries.[[20]](#cite_note-CW_references-20) Chen considered that the microseries could be seen as representing a HoloNet-dramatized versions of actual events—meaning that while liberties were taken for audiences, the bones of what had occurred were still accurate. He felt this was partially due to the stylized way action is portrayed in the *Clone Wars* series feeling disconnected to the films. The author also noted that his personal approach helped him consolidate what had happened in the films to what had happened in the series effectively during the novel's planning process.[[21]](#cite_note-Email-21)

The phrase "Like fire across the galaxy" is from *Clone Wars*. Windu's role in the fighting on Dantooine and [Grievous](/wiki/Grievous "Grievous")'s battle with [Ki-Adi-Mundi](/wiki/Ki-Adi-Mundi "Ki-Adi-Mundi") were also referenced in the novel.[[20]](#cite_note-CW_references-20) Chen purposely alluded to the [duel](/wiki/Duel_on_Yavin_4 "Duel on Yavin 4") on [Yavin 4](/wiki/Yavin_4/Legends "Yavin 4/Legends") between Skywalker and Ventress shown in *Clone Wars*,[[22]](#cite_note-Mike_Chen_Twitter-22) with, in his words, "some caveats."[[20]](#cite_note-CW_references-20) The mention he included that agents of Count Dooku had been sent after Skywalker previously referenced the duel.[[23]](#cite_note-Inverse-23) Assuming that[[22]](#cite_note-Mike_Chen_Twitter-22) the *Clone Wars* duel occurred in a dark, rainy environment, Chen posited that Skywalker could have fought Ventress previously without realizing who she was or getting a good look at her.[[23]](#cite_note-Inverse-23) Skywalker also presumed he had bested the enemy.[[24]](#cite_note-Mike_Chen_one_of_minions-24) Still, he recognizes her [*Ginivex*-class fanblade starfighter](/wiki/Ginivex-class_fanblade_starfighter "Ginivex-class fanblade starfighter"), while Ventress is more than aware of who the Jedi is.[[25]](#cite_note-Mike_Chen_Twitter_2-25) The book did not go into more detail because it would have taken too much exposition.[[24]](#cite_note-Mike_Chen_one_of_minions-24)

### Contradictions and connections[]

 [![Anakin Skywalker's hair on the cover of Brotherhood is shorter than what is described in the novel.](data:image/gif;base64,R0lGODlhAQABAIABAAAAAP///yH5BAEAAAEALAAAAAABAAEAQAICTAEAOw%3D%3D)](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/starwars/images/d/d2/AnakinBrotherhoodCover.png/revision/latest?cb=20220510234759) [![Anakin Skywalker's hair on the cover of Brotherhood is shorter than what is described in the novel.](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/starwars/images/d/d2/AnakinBrotherhoodCover.png/revision/latest/scale-to-width-down/300?cb=20220510234759)](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/starwars/images/d/d2/AnakinBrotherhoodCover.png/revision/latest?cb=20220510234759) 

Anakin Skywalker's hair on the cover of *Brotherhood* is shorter than what is described in the novel.

 

The cover of *Brotherhood* depicts Kenobi and Skywalker in appearances[[26]](#cite_note-Brotherhood-26) derived from [*Star Wars*: Episode II *Attack of the Clones*](/wiki/Star_Wars:_Episode_II_Attack_of_the_Clones "Star Wars: Episode II Attack of the Clones"),[[27]](#cite_note-aotc-27) with Skywalker's design updated to reflect his gloved right hand. However, the novel itself also describes "his once-short hair" as having started to grow out, implying a bridge[[26]](#cite_note-Brotherhood-26) between his *Attack of the Clones* hair[[27]](#cite_note-aotc-27) and his *[Star Wars: The Clone Wars](/wiki/Star_Wars:_The_Clone_Wars "Star Wars: The Clone Wars")* style[[28]](#cite_note-Cat_and_Mouse-28) and meaning his hair would be longer than what is seen on the cover. In the novel, after [Padmé Amidala](/wiki/Padm%C3%A9_Amidala "Padmé Amidala") makes note of his growing hair, Skywalker muses that he will allow it to grow out even more,[[26]](#cite_note-Brotherhood-26) foreshadowing the yet longer hair to come by the time of *The Clone Wars*.[[28]](#cite_note-Cat_and_Mouse-28) Kenobi's hair in the novel is also noted to have grown out since *Attack of the Clones* despite its appearance on the cover.[[26]](#cite_note-Brotherhood-26)

The end of *Brotherhood* see Kenobi and Skywalker adopt appearances[[26]](#cite_note-Brotherhood-26) that match their *[Star Wars: The Clone Wars](/wiki/Star_Wars:_The_Clone_Wars "Star Wars: The Clone Wars")* designs,[[28]](#cite_note-Cat_and_Mouse-28) including Kenobi cutting his hair and parting it[[26]](#cite_note-Brotherhood-26) to match his *The Clone Wars* appearance[[28]](#cite_note-Cat_and_Mouse-28) and both looking over pieces of body armor,[[26]](#cite_note-Brotherhood-26) which both have donned by the events of *The Clone Wars*.[[28]](#cite_note-Cat_and_Mouse-28) Such suggests that all appearances that stray closer to their *Attack of the Clones* designs are before *Brotherhood*, while *The Clone Wars*-inspired designs come after the novel. However, the novel also claims itself as the first time Kenobi and Amidala have seen each other since the Battle of Geonosis.[[26]](#cite_note-Brotherhood-26) Such would suggest that the events of [*Hyperspace Stories* 1](/wiki/Hyperspace_Stories_1 "Hyperspace Stories 1")—in which Kenobi and Skywalker, who is a Jedi Knight judging by his lack of Padawan braid, are closer to their *Attack of the Clones* designs—must occur after the events of *Brotherhood* due to Kenobi and Amidala's interactions.[[29]](#cite_note-HS1-29)

Additionally, [*Hyperspace Stories* 5](/wiki/Hyperspace_Stories_5 "Hyperspace Stories 5") depicts Skywalker as familiar with Ventress, thereby placing it after *Brotherhood* despite using the *Attack of the Clones* inspired design.[[30]](#cite_note-HS5-30) "[Practice Makes Perfect](/wiki/Practice_Makes_Perfect_(episode) "Practice Makes Perfect (episode)")" depicts Kenobi with his longer hair and Skywalker with a shorter haircut than normal during [Ahsoka Tano](/wiki/Ahsoka_Tano "Ahsoka Tano")'s apprenticeship to Skywalker.[[31]](#cite_note-Practice_Makes_Perfect_Talkes-31) As such, Kenobi and Skywalker must adjust their clothing and hairstyles several times over the course of the Clone Wars. Indeed, the end of the book sees Skywalker remark that he only wants to try the armor as a trial period instead of committing to it full time[[26]](#cite_note-Brotherhood-26) as he has by the time of *The Clone Wars*.[[28]](#cite_note-Cat_and_Mouse-28) The claim that Kenobi and Amidala had not seen each other since the Battle of Geonosis[[26]](#cite_note-Brotherhood-26) would later be contradicted by *[Star Wars: My First Comic Reader 1](/wiki/Star_Wars:_My_First_Comic_Reader_1 "Star Wars: My First Comic Reader 1")*, which, judging by Skywalker's Padawan braid, is set during his apprenticeship to Kenobi.[[32]](#cite_note-Galactic_Adventures-32) As the events of *Brotherhood* pick up sometime after Skywalker's apprenticeship is over, Kenobi and Amidala cannot meet for the first time since Geonosis during the novel.
