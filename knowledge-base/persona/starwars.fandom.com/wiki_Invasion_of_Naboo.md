The **Invasion of Naboo**, also known as the **Naboo conflict**, the **Naboo Crisis**, the **siege of Naboo**, and the **occupation of Naboo** was a pivotal [planetary](/wiki/Planet "Planet") war on [Naboo](/wiki/Naboo "Naboo") initiated by the [Trade Federation](/wiki/Trade_Federation "Trade Federation") in [32 BBY](/wiki/32_BBY "32 BBY") during the [Great Peace](/wiki/Republic_Era "Republic Era"). The conflict began with the Trade Federation's weeklong **blockade of Naboo**, before escalating when Naboo's [capital](/wiki/Capital "Capital") [city](/wiki/City "City"), as well as its surrounding regions, were conquered by the [Federation Army's](/wiki/Trade_Federation_Droid_Army "Trade Federation Droid Army") [battle droids](/wiki/Battle_droid "Battle droid"). Both the [Royal House of Naboo](/wiki/Royal_House_of_Naboo "Royal House of Naboo") and the Federation were members of the [Galactic Republic](/wiki/Galactic_Republic "Galactic Republic"), which sparked a [political](/wiki/Politics "Politics") debacle within [Galactic Senate](/wiki/Galactic_Senate "Galactic Senate"). However, the Federation was secretly in league with the [Sith](/wiki/Sith "Sith") led by the [Sith Lord](/wiki/Sith_Lord "Sith Lord") [Darth Sidious](/wiki/Darth_Sidious "Darth Sidious"), who was Naboo's [senator](/wiki/Senator "Senator"), Sheev Palpatine. The [Dark Lord](/wiki/Dark_Lord_of_the_Sith "Dark Lord of the Sith") intended to use the crisis as a pretext to remove the [Supreme Chancellor](/wiki/Supreme_Chancellor "Supreme Chancellor") [Finis Valorum](/wiki/Finis_Valorum "Finis Valorum") from office and have himself elected in his place as Chancellor.

During the initial blockade, Valorum sent two [Jedi](/wiki/Jedi "Jedi"), [Qui-Gon Jinn](/wiki/Qui-Gon_Jinn "Qui-Gon Jinn") and [Obi-Wan Kenobi](/wiki/Obi-Wan_Kenobi "Obi-Wan Kenobi"), to negotiate with the Federation, but Sidious ordered that they be killed and that the Federation deploy their forces to Naboo. The Jedi escaped and rescued [Queen](/wiki/Monarch_of_Naboo "Monarch of Naboo") [Padmé Amidala](/wiki/Padm%C3%A9_Amidala "Padmé Amidala") from the Federation, making their way off-planet and landing on the [world](/wiki/Planet "Planet") of [Tatooine](/wiki/Tatooine "Tatooine") that was controlled by the [Hutt Clan](/wiki/Hutt_Clan "Hutt Clan") for refuge after their vessel was damaged while escaping the blockade. There, Jinn discovered a young slave named [Anakin Skywalker](/wiki/Anakin_Skywalker "Anakin Skywalker"), the [Chosen One](/wiki/Chosen_One "Chosen One") destined to bring balance to [the Force](/wiki/The_Force "The Force").

The group repaired their damaged vessel, however [Darth Maul](/wiki/Maul "Maul") revealed himself and fought a [duel](/wiki/Duel_on_Tatooine "Duel on Tatooine") with Jinn before escaping, and fleeing to the Republic capital world of [Coruscant](/wiki/Coruscant "Coruscant"), where Amidala pleaded for assistance before the Galactic Senate. The Republic's bureaucracy was unable to help Amidala, who called for a vote of no confidence in Chancellor Valorum, leading to an election in which Palpatine was a favorite to win. Knowing she would find no help on Coruscant, Amidala returned to Naboo with the Jedi and planned to defeat the Federation on her own.

Upon her return, Amidala met and forged an alliance with the [Gungans](/wiki/Gungan "Gungan") who lived in Naboo's oceans, and the Gungans agreed to join the fight against the Federation. In the [final battle](/wiki/Battle_of_Naboo "Battle of Naboo"), the Gungan army took on the Federation army as a diversion; a wing of starfighters attacked the droid control ship in orbit; and Amidala and the Jedi infiltrated the palace to capture [Nute Gunray](/wiki/Nute_Gunray "Nute Gunray"), the Federation viceroy. By the battle's end, Skywalker had destroyed the Federation control ship, the Gungans were victorious, Gunray was captured, and the Sith [Lord](/wiki/Lord "Lord") [Darth](/wiki/Darth "Darth") Maul was defeated—at the cost of Jinn's life.

The event would be remembered by the [Naboo](/wiki/Naboo_(people) "Naboo (people)") as the **Occupation** and by critics of the Republic as the "**debacle at Naboo**." Fought ten [years](/wiki/Standard_year "Standard year") prior to the [Clone Wars](/wiki/Clone_Wars "Clone Wars"), the invasion showcased major faults with the Republic to the galactic stage and, ultimately, laid the seeds for the war itself. Meanwhile, for Palpatine, the crisis was a success even though Maul was lost and the Federation had been defeated; with a strong sympathy vote, Palpatine was elected Supreme Chancellor. Over the years, Palpatine furthered his Sith plot to take over the Republic and destroy the Jedi, launching the Clone Wars that would allow him to form a [Galactic Empire](/wiki/Galactic_Empire "Galactic Empire") and [destroy](/wiki/Order_66 "Order 66") the [Jedi Order](/wiki/Jedi_Order "Jedi Order").

At least a [millennium](/wiki/Millennium "Millennium") before the Invasion, the [Jedi](/wiki/Jedi "Jedi") and [Sith](/wiki/Sith "Sith") were at [war](/wiki/Jedi-Sith_War "Jedi-Sith War"). The war ended in the Sith's [defeat](/wiki/Defeat_of_the_Sith "Defeat of the Sith"), as their thirst for power caused them to turn on each other, which allowed the Jedi to defeat and nearly destroy the weakened Sith. However, the Sith remained alive through one survivor, [Darth Bane](/wiki/Darth_Bane "Darth Bane"), who created the [Rule of Two](/wiki/Rule_of_Two "Rule of Two") in order for the Sith to [operate](/wiki/Grand_Plan "Grand Plan") in secret.[[8]](#cite_note-Sacrifice-8) Bane's tradition continued throughout the centuries.[[9]](#cite_note-Sith-DB-9) Eventually, [Darth Sidious](/wiki/Darth_Sidious "Darth Sidious"), [apprentice](/wiki/Sith_apprentice "Sith apprentice") to [Sith Lord](/wiki/Sith_Lord "Sith Lord") [Darth Plagueis](/wiki/Darth_Plagueis "Darth Plagueis"),[[10]](#cite_note-TarkinNovel-10) betrayed his master by killing him in his sleep, and subsequently succeeded him as the [Sith Master](/wiki/Sith_Master "Sith Master").[[11]](#cite_note-Episode_III-11) Sidious also took on an apprentice of his own, [Darth](/wiki/Darth "Darth") [Maul](/wiki/Maul "Maul").[[3]](#cite_note-Episode_I-3) The Sith were also aided by [Dooku](/wiki/Dooku "Dooku"),[[4]](#cite_note-The_Sith_Lord-4) a former Jedi Master[[12]](#cite_note-Jedi_Lost-12) who maintained good relations with the Order.[[4]](#cite_note-The_Sith_Lord-4)

[Wilhuff Tarkin](/wiki/Wilhuff_Tarkin "Wilhuff Tarkin")'s stonewalling of the attempts to investigate the [Eriadu Conference](/wiki/Eriadu_Conference "Eriadu Conference"),[[10]](#cite_note-TarkinNovel-10) during which the [Nebula Front](/wiki/Nebula_Front "Nebula Front") had [killed](/wiki/Death "Death") the previous leadership of the [Trade Federation](/wiki/Trade_Federation "Trade Federation"), which allowed the anti-[Galactic Republic](/wiki/Galactic_Republic "Galactic Republic") [Nute Gunray](/wiki/Nute_Gunray "Nute Gunray") to rise to be the [Viceroy of the Trade Federation](/wiki/Viceroy_of_the_Trade_Federation "Viceroy of the Trade Federation"),[[13]](#cite_note-Rise-13) helped to foment and hasten the [Naboo](/wiki/Naboo "Naboo") invasion.[[10]](#cite_note-TarkinNovel-10) All the same, thanks to a brief boost in popularity after surviving an assassination attempt at that conference, [Supreme Chancellor](/wiki/Supreme_Chancellor "Supreme Chancellor") [Finis Valorum](/wiki/Finis_Valorum "Finis Valorum") had enough political capital needed to pass taxation legislation through the [Galactic Senate](/wiki/Galactic_Senate "Galactic Senate").[[13]](#cite_note-Rise-13) Under [Prop 31-814D](/wiki/Prop_31-814D "Prop 31-814D"), the Senate made the [Free Trade Zones](/wiki/Free_Trade_Zone "Free Trade Zone") eligible for taxation,[[14]](#cite_note-Propaganda-14) which angered the Federation.[[13]](#cite_note-Rise-13) Around [33 BBY](/wiki/33_BBY "33 BBY"),[[15]](#cite_note-Jango_Date-15) the Federation began to threaten blockades over the new taxes it would need to pay.[[16]](#cite_note-Jango_4-16) In [32 BBY](/wiki/32_BBY "32 BBY"),[[17]](#cite_note-Peril_date-17) all while trade disputes were a major concern for the [Mid Rim Territories](/wiki/Mid_Rim_Territories "Mid Rim Territories"), the Federation would also put forth its own trade bill in the senate, which was repeatedly voted down and brought back up.[[1]](#cite_note-Queen's_Peril-1)

Angered by Prop 31-814D[[3]](#cite_note-Episode_I-3) and hoping to support its own bill as it was repeatedly debated,[[1]](#cite_note-Queen's_Peril-1) the Federation [blockaded](/wiki/Blockade "Blockade") the Mid Rim [planet](/wiki/Planet "Planet") Naboo[[3]](#cite_note-Episode_I-3) in 32 BBY.[[5]](#cite_note-Galactic_Atlas-5) Beyond the wider tax concerns,[[3]](#cite_note-Episode_I-3) Naboo was also in its own trade dispute with the Federation over [plasma](/wiki/Plasma "Plasma") mining.[[13]](#cite_note-Rise-13) The Federation and [Naboo](/wiki/Naboo_(people) "Naboo (people)") delegates had previously met to discuss an issue facing both groups, yet talks had broken down[[18]](#cite_note-Jango_1-18) around 33 BBY.[[15]](#cite_note-Jango_Date-15) Even still, there was more at play with the invasion; in actuality, the invasion was engineered by the shadowy Sidious, who the Federation had allied themselves with.[[3]](#cite_note-Episode_I-3) Sidious had been the one who suggested Naboo be the Federation's target, as, in his guise as Naboo [Senator](/wiki/Senator "Senator")[[7]](#cite_note-On_the_Front_Lines-7) Sheev[[10]](#cite_note-TarkinNovel-10) Palpatine, he planned to use his people's suffering in a bid for political sympathy,[[7]](#cite_note-On_the_Front_Lines-7) which would push him to be the [Supreme Chancellor](/wiki/Supreme_Chancellor "Supreme Chancellor") of the Galactic Republic, allowing him to further his own plans.[[3]](#cite_note-Episode_I-3) To that end, Sidious, in secret, had engineered various taxation bills to be presented to the Senate which he, as Palpatine, publicly opposed.[[1]](#cite_note-Queen's_Peril-1)

### Naboo ventures[]

In 32 BBY, that same [year](/wiki/Standard_year "Standard year"),[[17]](#cite_note-Peril_date-17) the Naboo had held one of their biannual elections, in which the incumbent [Queen](/wiki/Queen "Queen"), [Sanandrassa](/wiki/Sanandrassa "Sanandrassa"), was defeated by the heavily-favored candidate [Padmé Amidala](/wiki/Padm%C3%A9_Amidala "Padmé Amidala"). Sanandrassa had held to a policy of isolation which unwittingly assisted Sidious' plans by weakening Naboo and depleting its stocks of food, leaving its people in danger of starvation if the planet was cut off. Amidala had not publicly criticized these beliefs of Sanandrassa's while campaigning, but after her [coronation](/wiki/Coronation_of_Queen_Amidala "Coronation of Queen Amidala"), she began working to undo the policies. Eventually, Amidala organized a [summit](/wiki/Chommell_sector_summit "Chommell sector summit"), to which she invited the heads of state of every other Republic member world in the [Chommell sector](/wiki/Chommell_sector "Chommell sector"). When he was informed of the summit by Amidala herself, Palpatine realized his plans would have to be accelerated. Although many of the planets to which Amidala had extended invitations did not send [ambassadors](/wiki/Ambassador "Ambassador"), the summit was otherwise a success. However, the Trade Federation arrived the day the summit ended, with orbital sensors picking up unusual feedback that prompted Amidala's [chief guard](/wiki/Head_of_Security "Head of Security"), [Captain](/wiki/Captain "Captain") [Quarsh Panaka](/wiki/Quarsh_Panaka "Quarsh Panaka"), to have [Governor](/wiki/Governor "Governor") [Kelma](/wiki/Kelma "Kelma") of [Karlinus](/wiki/Karlinus "Karlinus"), the last guest to leave, expedite her departure.[[1]](#cite_note-Queen's_Peril-1)

Intending to force Amidala to sign a treaty, which could help their own proposed taxation bill and would place Naboo under their control,[[1]](#cite_note-Queen's_Peril-1) the Federation blockaded Naboo with a [fleet](/wiki/Fleet "Fleet") of[[19]](#cite_note-USW-19) [*Lucrehulk*-class Battleships](/wiki/Lucrehulk-class_battleship "Lucrehulk-class battleship"),[[20]](#cite_note-Battlefront_2-20) [warships](/wiki/Warship "Warship") illegally modified from the organization's [*Lucrehulk*-class LH-3210 cargo freighters](/wiki/Lucrehulk-class_LH-3210_cargo_freighter "Lucrehulk-class LH-3210 cargo freighter").[[19]](#cite_note-USW-19) As the blockade carried on for the next [week](/wiki/Standard_week "Standard week"),[[21]](#cite_note-Battles_that_Changed_the_Galaxy-21) it would leave the planet ill-supplied and starved.[[22]](#cite_note-SWE_Daultay_Dofine-22) Amidala worked closely with various officials to find allies to help them. On the seventh day of the blockade, an agricultural transport from Karlinus attempted to bring a shipment of grain to Naboo, but the ship war turned away and diverted to [Enarc](/wiki/Enarc "Enarc"), where the grain was placed into cold storage. When [Graf Zapalo](/wiki/Graf_Zapalo "Graf Zapalo") told Amidala that the planet would begin to suffer with its food supply due to the first yield of crops being turned into fertilizer, Amidala ordered the planet to begin rationing.[[1]](#cite_note-Queen's_Peril-1) Meanwhile, [Jedi Master](/wiki/Jedi_Master "Jedi Master") [Adi Gallia](/wiki/Adi_Gallia "Adi Gallia")—a intuitive and intelligent member of the [Jedi High Council](/wiki/Jedi_High_Council "Jedi High Council")[[23]](#cite_note-Pocket-23) who also maintained an extensive intelligence network[[24]](#cite_note-Force_Users-24)—took notice of the strange activity on Naboo, marking her as the first Jedi to become aware of the developing crisis.[[23]](#cite_note-Pocket-23)

In response to Queen Amidala's requests for help, Chancellor Valorum[[3]](#cite_note-Episode_I-3)—who had taken a great many political hits[[16]](#cite_note-Jango_4-16) in recent months,[[25]](#cite_note-Few_Months-25) such as the breakdown of Federation-Naboo talks,[[18]](#cite_note-Jango_1-18) the Federation's blockade threats, and[[16]](#cite_note-Jango_4-16) the resumption[[18]](#cite_note-Jango_1-18) of the [Anselmi–Nautolan war](/wiki/Anselmi%E2%80%93Nautolan_war "Anselmi–Nautolan war")[[16]](#cite_note-Jango_4-16)—dispatched two ambassadors to speak to both the Naboo and the Trade Federation, sending Jedi Master [Qui-Gon Jinn](/wiki/Qui-Gon_Jinn "Qui-Gon Jinn")[[3]](#cite_note-Episode_I-3)—who happened to be the former apprentice of Dooku[[4]](#cite_note-The_Sith_Lord-4)—and his [Padawan](/wiki/Padawan "Padawan") [Obi-Wan Kenobi](/wiki/Obi-Wan_Kenobi "Obi-Wan Kenobi").[[3]](#cite_note-Episode_I-3) Jinn had previously been reading up on the taxation bills going before the Senate that were the public cause of the situation, as he had sensed something amiss with them, but neither he nor Kenobi had been able to pinpoint exactly what the problem was.[[1]](#cite_note-Queen's_Peril-1) The Senate itself was totally consumed with debate over the blockade, creating the unintended effect of preventing the [Alvadorjian Senate bill](/wiki/Alvadorjian_Senate_bill "Alvadorjian Senate bill") from reaching the rotunda.[[26]](#cite_note-MOF-26)

The two Jedi were scheduled to meet with [Viceroy](/wiki/Viceroy "Viceroy") Gunray immediately after arriving at Naboo in the [starship](/wiki/Starship "Starship") *[Radiant VII](/wiki/Radiant_VII "Radiant VII")*. However, [TC-14](/wiki/TC-14 "TC-14"), the hostess [protocol droid](/wiki/Protocol_droid "Protocol droid") which they encountered first, promptly informed Gunray, his [lieutenant](/wiki/Lieutenant "Lieutenant"), [Settlement Officer](/wiki/Settlement_Officer "Settlement Officer") [Rune Haako](/wiki/Rune_Haako "Rune Haako"), and invasion force [Captain](/wiki/Captain "Captain") [Daultay Dofine](/wiki/Daultay_Dofine "Daultay Dofine"), that the [ambassadors](/wiki/Ambassador "Ambassador") from [Coruscant](/wiki/Coruscant "Coruscant") were [Jedi Knights](/wiki/Jedi_Knight "Jedi Knight"). Terrified at the prospect of facing Jedi, Gunray contacted [Darth](/wiki/Darth "Darth") Sidious and presented the situation. Sidious said the Chancellor should never have bought them into crisis and ordered Gunray to [kill](/wiki/Death "Death") the Jedi at once and begin landing his [troops](/wiki/Troop "Troop") on Naboo for an invasion, and when Gunray questioned the invasions' legality, Sidious replied that he would make it legal. Sidious also told Gunray to exclude Dofine from any future communications after the latter implied calling off the plan.[[3]](#cite_note-Episode_I-3)

Toxic [dioxis](/wiki/Dioxis "Dioxis") gas then flooded the ventilation system in the Jedi's waiting room, and Gunray dispatched a [squad](/wiki/Squad "Squad") of [battle droids](/wiki/Battle_droid "Battle droid") to verify that the Jedi had been destroyed. They had survived the gas, however, and [Qui-Gon Jinn](/wiki/Qui-Gon_Jinn "Qui-Gon Jinn") and [Obi-Wan Kenobi](/wiki/Obi-Wan_Kenobi "Obi-Wan Kenobi") decimated the [OOM command battle droid](/wiki/OOM_command_battle_droid "OOM command battle droid") and [OOM-series security droids](/wiki/OOM-series_security_droid "OOM-series security droid"). The two Jedi continued to cut through the [security droids](/wiki/Security_droid "Security droid") and made their way throughout the ship. Jinn then attempted to cut through the [blast doors](/wiki/Blast_door "Blast door") to the [bridge](/wiki/Bridge_(starship) "Bridge (starship)") where the Federation leadership was waiting. They nearly succeeded, but the arrival of two [shield-bearing](/wiki/Deflector_shield "Deflector shield") [droidekas](/wiki/Droideka "Droideka") forced them to flee the scene. The two Jedi made it to the ship's hangar where they observed thousands of [B1-series battle droids](/wiki/B1-series_battle_droid "B1-series battle droid") being loaded onto [landing craft](/wiki/Landing_craft "Landing craft"). Realizing that an invasion of Naboo was imminent, they stowed away on separate [droid invasion craft](/wiki/C-9979_landing_craft "C-9979 landing craft") to escape to the planet's surface. Kenobi's [transport](/wiki/Transport "Transport") was forced to crash-land on Naboo, although the Jedi was uninjured.[[3]](#cite_note-Episode_I-3)

After learning the Jedi had escaped, Gunray contacted invasion [commander](/wiki/Commander "Commander"), an OOM command battle droid named [OOM-9](/wiki/OOM-9 "OOM-9"), and warned him of the Jedi's presence among them and to not underestimate them.[[3]](#cite_note-Episode_I-3) OOM-9 had his droid troops and pilots take out communication transmitters, stopping the Naboo population from reporting the invasion or calling for aid, and occupy settlements.[[19]](#cite_note-USW-19)

In the capital city of Theed, a communications jam occurred as [Padmé Amidala](/wiki/Padm%C3%A9_Amidala "Padmé Amidala") was consulting with Senator Palpatine. The failure prompted [Theed](/wiki/Theed "Theed")'s [Governor](/wiki/Governor "Governor") [Sio Bibble](/wiki/Sio_Bibble "Sio Bibble") to say that it was an invasion, although Amidala reconfirmed her pledge of no fighting. But realizing an invasion was underway, she soon switched places with her decoy [Handmaiden](/wiki/Naboo_Royal_Handmaidens "Naboo Royal Handmaidens") [Sabé](/wiki/Sab%C3%A9 "Sabé").[[3]](#cite_note-Episode_I-3) Over the course of [minutes](/wiki/Standard_minute "Standard minute"),[[27]](#cite_note-Altaya_Naboo-27) the [Trade Federation military](/wiki/Trade_Federation_military "Trade Federation military") had taken Theed, subduing Naboo's small [Royal Naboo Security Forces](/wiki/Royal_Naboo_Security_Forces "Royal Naboo Security Forces") and leaving the planet completely under Federation control. Nute Gunray himself landed in Theed and assumed control from [Queen](/wiki/Monarch_of_Naboo "Monarch of Naboo") Amidala's own throne room. After Amidala refused to sign a treaty legalizing the occupation, she and her retinue were to be processed into [Camp Four](/wiki/Camp_Four "Camp Four").[[3]](#cite_note-Episode_I-3)

Meanwhile, Kenobi and Jinn had reached the Naboo [swamps](/wiki/Swamp "Swamp"). After running into local [Gungan](/wiki/Gungan "Gungan") [Jar Jar Binks](/wiki/Jar_Jar_Binks "Jar Jar Binks"), the Jedi went underwater and contacted [Boss](/wiki/Boss "Boss") [Rugor Nass](/wiki/Rugor_Nass "Rugor Nass"), leader of the Gungans, in the underwater [city](/wiki/City "City") of [Otoh Gunga](/wiki/Otoh_Gunga "Otoh Gunga"). Although the Gungan leader refused to get involved in the fighting on the surface, he did lend them a Gungan transport, which they used to travel to Theed with Jar Jar. There they found the [Queen](/wiki/Queen "Queen") and her entourage held hostage by battle droids. After a brief skirmish against the escorting droids, the Jedi implored the Queen to travel with them back to Coruscant. Not only would she be safer there, but she would be able to ask the Senate to condemn the invasion and take military action. She eventually agreed.[[3]](#cite_note-Episode_I-3)

However, getting past the Trade Federation blockade proved very difficult and the [Queen's ship](/wiki/Naboo_Royal_Starship "Naboo Royal Starship") was barely able to scrape by, its damaged shields needing repair by [astromech droid](/wiki/Astromech_droid "Astromech droid") [R2-D2](/wiki/R2-D2 "R2-D2") at the last possible moment. Since the [hyperdrive](/wiki/Hyperdrive "Hyperdrive") had begun to leak badly during the escape, the group decided to hide on [Tatooine](/wiki/Tatooine "Tatooine") in the [Outer Rim Territories](/wiki/Outer_Rim_Territories "Outer Rim Territories") until a replacement could be found.[[3]](#cite_note-Episode_I-3)

In the following [days](/wiki/Standard_day "Standard day"), the Trade Federation continued to take over the planet, eventually taking complete control. Following the subjugation of the planet, command transferred from the *[Saak'ak](/wiki/Saak%27ak "Saak'ak")* to the Droid Control Ship[[3]](#cite_note-Episode_I-3) *[Vuutun Palaa](/wiki/Vuutun_Palaa "Vuutun Palaa")*, which had been at the heart of the Federation's blockade.[[28]](#cite_note-Starfighters-28) After this was done the blockade dispersed, but the *Yuutun Palaa* stayed, and the commanders of the *Saak'ak* transferred to the control ship.[[3]](#cite_note-Episode_I-3)

After the duel on Tatooine, the Jedi and Queen Amidala arrived on Coruscant. They were met on the landing platform by Senator Palpatine and Supreme Chancellor [Finis Valorum](/wiki/Finis_Valorum "Finis Valorum"). Palpatine brought them to [his apartment](/wiki/Palpatine%27s_apartment "Palpatine's apartment").[[3]](#cite_note-Episode_I-3)

While in the apartment, Palpatine explained to the Queen how corrupt and inefficient the Republic had become and expressed scepticism about the possibility that the Senate could act on the invasion; he also underlined the fact that Chancellor Valorum had little real power and that the [bureaucrats](/wiki/Bureaucrat "Bureaucrat") were in fact the true rulers of the Republic by that point. Given the situation, Palpatine suggested that the Queen called a [vote of no confidence](/wiki/Vote_of_No_Confidence "Vote of No Confidence") on the Chancellor, in order to give the Republic a more effective government; or, in alternative, to submit a plea to the courts, which, however, were generally even slower than the Senate to decide. Amidala was unknowingly being manipulated and deceived by Palpatine.

Jinn, Kenobi, and Skywalker appeared before the [Jedi Council](/wiki/Jedi_High_Council "Jedi High Council"). Jinn reported his encounter with Maul, leaving the Council to consider the possibility that the Sith had returned after a thousand years. Skywalker was tested by the members of the Council, who recognized the boy to be a Force-sensitive, but expressed concern about young Skywalker's feelings of fear and loss.

In a special meeting of the Galactic Senate, Palpatine and Amidala spoke before the thousands of Galactic delegates. Their pleas were instantly accused of being lies and baseless accusations by the Senator of the Trade Federation, [Lott Dod](/wiki/Lott_Dod "Lott Dod"), who recommended appointing a commission to ascertain the truth; Dos's proposal was almost immediately supported by senator [Aks Moe](/wiki/Aks_Moe "Aks Moe") of [Malastare](/wiki/Malastare "Malastare"). Valorum tried to silence Dod and Moe, but he was interrupted by [Vice Chancellor](/wiki/Vice_Chair "Vice Chair") [Mas Amedda](/wiki/Mas_Amedda "Mas Amedda"). Palpatine used this opportunity to further manipulate Amidala into turning on Valorum, by stating that the [Bureaucrats](/wiki/Bureaucrat "Bureaucrat") were the true rulers of the Republic and on the payroll of the Trade Federation, as well as this was when Valorum's influence would reduce. After consulting with Amedda and his [aide](/wiki/Aide "Aide") [Sei Taria](/wiki/Sei_Taria "Sei Taria"), Valorum asked Amidala if she would defer her motion and allow the commission to proceed. The Queen responded by saying that she would not defer and that she had not been elected to see her people suffer and die while the Senate discussed the invasion in a committee.[[3]](#cite_note-Episode_I-3) Amidala had unknowingly fallen for Palpatine's manipulation and deception.

Exasperated by the Republic's inefficiency and corruption, Amidala shocked the Senate by moving for a [vote of no confidence](/wiki/Vote_of_No_Confidence "Vote of No Confidence") in the leadership of Chancellor Valorum, as it had been suggested to her by Palpatine himself. The proposal caused an uproar in the Senate, with many senators calling to proceed to the vote immediately, while Amedda vainly called for order.[[3]](#cite_note-Episode_I-3)

Within hours, Amidala's motion of no confidence was passed and Valorum removed from power. Palpatine was nominated as one of the candidates to succeed Valorum and promised Queen Amidala to clean up the rampant corruption; [Bail Antilles](/wiki/Bail_Antilles "Bail Antilles") and [Ainlee Teem](/wiki/Ainlee_Teem "Ainlee Teem") were nominated as well.[[3]](#cite_note-Episode_I-3)

Against Palpatine's suggestions, Amidala returned to Naboo, deciding that her fate belonged with that of her [people](/wiki/Naboo_(people) "Naboo (people)"). Soon after that, Qui-Gon Jinn and Obi-Wan Kenobi met again with the Jedi Council: the Council denied admittance to Anakin in the Jedi Order, citing his advanced age as the deciding factor, and ordered Kenobi and Jinn to return to Naboo with the Queen and find out what they could about her mysterious assailant to bring the existence of the Sith into light.[[3]](#cite_note-Episode_I-3)

Upon their arrival back on Naboo, Amidala and the Jedi formulated a plan to [capture](/wiki/Battle_of_Naboo "Battle of Naboo") Viceroy Gunray in Theed, thus taking back the planet. With the aid of Jar Jar Binks, an [alliance](/wiki/Boss_Meetup "Boss Meetup") was formed with the proud Boss Nass and the Gungans, and Captain [Panaka](/wiki/Quarsh_Panaka "Quarsh Panaka") organized together all Resistance cells.[[3]](#cite_note-Episode_I-3)

The plan was for the [Gungan Grand Army](/wiki/Gungan_Grand_Army "Gungan Grand Army") to engage the Droid Army of the Federation in open combat while the Queen and the Jedi quietly infiltrate the [palace](/wiki/Theed_Royal_Palace "Theed Royal Palace") in Theed with the remainder of the Royal Security forces to capture the Viceroy. The small number of pilots in the [Naboo security forces](/wiki/Naboo_Royal_Space_Fighter_Corps "Naboo Royal Space Fighter Corps") would then fly into space and attempt to destroy the only battleship still in [orbit](/wiki/Orbit "Orbit"): the Droid Control Ship, where all the battle droids were controlled from a single [computer](/wiki/Computer "Computer") [brain](/wiki/Brain "Brain").[[3]](#cite_note-Episode_I-3) Another battle would begin in Camp Four when [Mariek Panaka](/wiki/Mariek_Panaka "Mariek Panaka") and [Sergeant](/wiki/Sergeant "Sergeant") [Tonra](/wiki/Tonra "Tonra") led a prison uprising against [Overseer](/wiki/Overseer "Overseer") [Usan Ollin](/wiki/Usan_Ollin "Usan Ollin") and her battle droids.[[1]](#cite_note-Queen's_Peril-1)

The plan began as intended, but the entire dynamic of the situation changed with the arrival of Darth Maul. The two Jedi engaged the Sith Lord in [battle](/wiki/Lightsaber_duel "Lightsaber duel") while the queen led a raid into the throne room. Soon after, Amidala and Security Captain Panaka successfully captured both Gunray and Rune Haako, holding them at gunpoint and outlining their desire for a new treaty. Maul led Jinn and Kenobi through the hangar and into [Theed's power generator complex](/wiki/Theed_power_generator "Theed power generator"), where Maul managed to kick Kenobi, who fell several stories below. Jinn was then able to knock Maul back. Jinn jumped down after him, but the [Sith Lord](/wiki/Sith_Lord "Sith Lord") managed to get up. They moved down the catwalk near the refinery.[[3]](#cite_note-Episode_I-3)

 [![Amidala "discusses" a new treaty with Gunray.](data:image/gif;base64,R0lGODlhAQABAIABAAAAAP///yH5BAEAAAEALAAAAAABAAEAQAICTAEAOw%3D%3D)](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/starwars/images/4/4d/Gunray_captured.png/revision/latest?cb=20130120031407) [![Amidala "discusses" a new treaty with Gunray.](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/starwars/images/4/4d/Gunray_captured.png/revision/latest/scale-to-width-down/300?cb=20130120031407)](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/starwars/images/4/4d/Gunray_captured.png/revision/latest?cb=20130120031407) 

Amidala "discusses" a new treaty with Gunray.

 

Meanwhile, Kenobi was able to jump up on the catwalk Jinn and Maul were on, and ran to help Jinn. But Jinn, Kenobi and Maul were stuck behind the [laser](/wiki/Laser "Laser") shields, each cut off from the other. Maul waited impatiently looking in mocking contempt at Jinn, who was calmly [meditating](/wiki/Meditation "Meditation"), possibly as an attempt to strike the Sith Lord off-guard. The walls shut down, Kenobi ran, and Jinn jumped up and battled Maul once again. At the melting pit, Maul battled the Jedi and mortally wounded Jinn. He then had to face Kenobi. The wall once again shut down and Kenobi was left to face Maul, alone. Maul managed to kick Kenobi into the pit, but Kenobi grabbed hold onto a nozzle which was jutting out a little below Maul..[[3]](#cite_note-Episode_I-3)

The Sith Lord then kicked [Kenobi's lightsaber](/wiki/Obi-Wan_Kenobi%27s_first_lightsaber "Obi-Wan Kenobi's first lightsaber") into the bottomless shaft, past the still hanging Jedi. Calling his [Master's lightsaber](/wiki/Qui-Gon_Jinn%27s_lightsaber "Qui-Gon Jinn's lightsaber") to him, Kenobi jumped and cut through the Sith Lord's waist. Maul then fell into the shaft, presumed dead. Kenobi joined his dying Master who, in his final words, told him to train Skywalker, the fabled "[Chosen One](/wiki/Chosen_One "Chosen One")" destined to bring balance to the force. Jinn died in a distraught Kenobi's arms. With Maul presumed dead, Amidala's assassination failed, but the Droid Army, however, was still active, until the Droid Control Ship was miraculously destroyed by an unexpected participant in the day's events: young Anakin Skywalker.[[3]](#cite_note-Episode_I-3) The destruction of the control ship shut down every Federation droid, with even OOM-9 being temporarily inactive.[[7]](#cite_note-On_the_Front_Lines-7)

## Aftermath[]

### Effect on Naboo[]

 [![N-1 starfighters of Bravo Flight participate in the celebration parade.](data:image/gif;base64,R0lGODlhAQABAIABAAAAAP///yH5BAEAAAEALAAAAAABAAEAQAICTAEAOw%3D%3D)](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/starwars/images/4/42/BravoFlightOfficer-SWZ.png/revision/latest?cb=20210108154440) [![N-1 starfighters of Bravo Flight participate in the celebration parade.](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/starwars/images/4/42/BravoFlightOfficer-SWZ.png/revision/latest/scale-to-width-down/300?cb=20210108154440)](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/starwars/images/4/42/BravoFlightOfficer-SWZ.png/revision/latest?cb=20210108154440) 

N-1 starfighters of Bravo Flight participate in the celebration parade.

 

With the defeat of the Trade Federation and the deactivation of its mechanical army, the invasion was over. Peace was restored, as were positive relations between the Gungans and the people of Naboo. Soon after, Palpatine, who had been elected as new Supreme Chancellor in the meantime, arrived on Naboo with members of the Jedi High Council to oversee the arrest of Gunray and Haako. [Yoda](/wiki/Yoda "Yoda") promoted Kenobi to full Jedi Knight and assigned him to train Skywalker. That evening, Qui-Gon was [given](/wiki/Funeral_of_Qui-Gon_Jinn "Funeral of Qui-Gon Jinn") a [Jedi funeral](/wiki/Jedi_funeral "Jedi funeral"). The Council members believed that Kenobi had destroyed Darth Maul, but he had in fact survived and went into exile. The day after, the Gungans and Naboo celebrated with a [parade](/wiki/Parade "Parade") in Theed, complete with overflights by [N-1 starfighters](/wiki/N-1_starfighter "N-1 starfighter") of [Bravo Squadron](/wiki/Bravo_Squadron "Bravo Squadron"). Amidala and Boss Nass shared a symbolic globe as a Gungan band played [music](/wiki/Music "Music").[[3]](#cite_note-Episode_I-3)

In honor of the[[7]](#cite_note-On_the_Front_Lines-7) five hundred forty-three[[29]](#cite_note-AbEverything-29) Gungans who had been killed in the battle at the Great Grass Plains, Nass and Amidala vowed to work together, hoping for a new era made up of cooperation and openness, and forged a Naboo—Gungan pact. The subsequent treaty had immediate benefits, providing low-cost medicinal herbs to Theed, mechanical parts used in seawater pumps to Otoh Gunga's engineers, and new trade lines. Even though some Gungans went as far as to relocate to the surface settlements in order to sell their products, the planet's old sentiments made it so the exchange between both civilizations had all but ceased in quiet within a year of the invasion.[[7]](#cite_note-On_the_Front_Lines-7) Nevertheless, relations remained friendly,[[30]](#cite_note-Shadow_Warrior-30) with the Gungans even being given the right to vote in Naboo's elections.[[31]](#cite_note-Queen's_Shadow-31)

Although the Naboo had already been protective of the nature around them, treaties were established between the Gungans and Naboo in the aftermath of the invasion that further protected regions like the [Lake Country](/wiki/Lake_Country "Lake Country").[[31]](#cite_note-Queen's_Shadow-31) During the [Clone Wars](/wiki/Clone_Wars "Clone Wars"),[[30]](#cite_note-Shadow_Warrior-30) Battle of Naboo veteran[[3]](#cite_note-Episode_I-3) Roos Tarpals noted that he strongly believed in friendship between the Gungan and Naboo civilizations.[[30]](#cite_note-Shadow_Warrior-30) The people of Naboo came to simply remember the crisis as "the Occupation."[[31]](#cite_note-Queen's_Shadow-31)

### Galactic consequences[]

:   "*I guess you can kiss your trade franchise goodbye.*"
:   ―Captain Panaka, to Nute Gunray and Rune Haako[[3]](#cite_note-Episode_I-3)

Learning of his former apprentice's death was hard for Dooku, who confronted Sidious at [the Works](/wiki/The_Works "The Works") on Coruscant by saying he had "allowed" Maul to kill Jinn. Dooku was unknowingly followed by Jedi Master [Yaddle](/wiki/Yaddle "Yaddle"), whom he [dueled and killed](/wiki/Duel_in_the_Works "Duel in the Works") after deciding he was too far gone to return to the light.[[4]](#cite_note-The_Sith_Lord-4) Dooku subsequently replaced Maul as Sidious's apprentice and took on the name "Darth Tyrannus."[[32]](#cite_note-Episode_II-32)

The Federation and Valorum were faced with most blame for the invasion, and every subsequent anti-Republic speech called out the "debacle at Naboo."[[7]](#cite_note-On_the_Front_Lines-7) Following the invasion, the Federation's monopoly on shipping in the Outer Rim Territories was broken. This, combined with [Valorum Shipping](/wiki/Valorum_Shipping "Valorum Shipping")'s loss of prestige due to scandals and Chancellor Valorum's truncated term, allowed [Eriadu Mining and Shipping](/wiki/Eriadu_Mining_and_Shipping "Eriadu Mining and Shipping") to prosper.[[10]](#cite_note-TarkinNovel-10) The lost cost the company an inconceivable amount of money—a cost that grew as the company then needed to divert a great deal of funds towards Gunray's legal battle[[31]](#cite_note-Queen's_Shadow-31)—and led to it enduring a period of political exile.[[33]](#cite_note-Rise_and_Fall-33) However, ultimately, the ineffective Senate failed to make a major move against the Federation,[[21]](#cite_note-Battles_that_Changed_the_Galaxy-21) allowing it to recover from the Senate's minor sanctions[[34]](#cite_note-Ships_of_the_Republic_and_Separatists_One-34) and, despite no longer holding a monopoly,[[10]](#cite_note-TarkinNovel-10) keep its power over the Free Trade Routes.[[13]](#cite_note-Rise-13)

Upon his appointment to Chancellor, Palpatine had promised the galaxy that Gunray, his colleagues and the Federation would be penalized for their actions taken towards the Naboo.[[3]](#cite_note-Episode_I-3) However, while indicted for his crimes in the Republic, Gunray was eventually acquitted by the Supreme Court following four trials.[[32]](#cite_note-Episode_II-32) To add insult to the crisis that had occurred,[[13]](#cite_note-Rise-13) the Federation remained active with Gunray still acting as its leader.[[32]](#cite_note-Episode_II-32) After being acquitted, the Federation continued to exert their influence over the Free Trade Zones, and while the taxation issues were seemingly resolved following the Naboo invasion, the corporation had still continued to block regulations, laws, and taxes. It has also been speculated by historians and accountants that Gunray and the Federation had only sent a third of the taxes it owed to the Republic, after having tied the process up through bureaucracy.[[13]](#cite_note-Rise-13)

With the Federation's evasion of Republic laws, several worlds decided to create their own trade deals with the corporation, despite their previous oppositions towards them.[[13]](#cite_note-Rise-13) In fact, during the political exile it did endure, the Federation elected to forge ties with Outer Rim worlds, as well as other [megacorporations](/wiki/Megacorporation "Megacorporation"), setting the stage for a secessionist movement.[[33]](#cite_note-Rise_and_Fall-33) A short time prior to his final trial, Amidala's term as Queen expired. Although the Naboo people were willing to amend the Constitution, Amidala stepped down and former Queen [Réillata](/wiki/R%C3%A9illata "Réillata") was elected her successor. The new Queen offered Amidala a position as Senator to replace the outgoing [Oshadam](/wiki/Oshadam "Oshadam"), which she accepted.[[31]](#cite_note-Queen's_Shadow-31) Eventually, [Count](/wiki/Count "Count") [Dooku](/wiki/Dooku "Dooku") was apprenticed to Darth Sidious, but became the public face of the [Separatist Crisis](/wiki/Separatist_Crisis "Separatist Crisis"). Gunray continued to despise Amidala for her part in his defeat and sought revenge against her.[[32]](#cite_note-Episode_II-32)

The ascension of Palpatine to the post of Supreme Chancellor was part of his grand scheme[[3]](#cite_note-Episode_I-3) to put himself in [total control](/wiki/Galactic_Emperor "Galactic Emperor") of [the galaxy](/wiki/The_galaxy "The galaxy").[[11]](#cite_note-Episode_III-11) Thanks to the invasion, three major flaws in the Republic government—the lack of an [official military](/wiki/Republic_Military "Republic Military"), the weak central authority, and layers of bureaucracy—were all exposed on the full [galactic](/wiki/Galaxy "Galaxy") stage, but Palpatine was gifted a reputation of being a crusader for reforms to the Republic government.[[7]](#cite_note-On_the_Front_Lines-7) Although Maul was believed to have died, he had in fact survived,[[35]](#cite_note-Witches_of_the_Mist-35) but was crippled, and ended up going into hiding until he was found by his brother, [Savage Opress](/wiki/Savage_Opress "Savage Opress"). During all those years, Maul grew obsessed with the concept of having revenge on Kenobi, blaming the Jedi Padawan for his misfortune.[[36]](#cite_note-Brothers-36)

For the Sith, the Invasion of Naboo set into motion a series of events that would culminate into the Clone Wars, the termination of the Jedi Order, and the rise of the Galactic Empire. So much so that at the height of the Clone Wars a decade after, Count Dooku considered the Invasion of Naboo the true start of the war.[[30]](#cite_note-Shadow_Warrior-30) Despite the routing of Federation forces, the organization secretly left behind a [laboratory](/wiki/Trade_Federation_laboratory "Trade Federation laboratory") and a [hidden base](/wiki/Rish_Loo%27s_laboratory "Rish Loo's laboratory") that the Separatists would later come to utilize throughout the war.

## Behind the scenes[]

The Invasion of Naboo first appeared in the [1999](/wiki/1999 "1999") film [*Star Wars*: Episode I *The Phantom Menace*](/wiki/Star_Wars:_Episode_I_The_Phantom_Menace "Star Wars: Episode I The Phantom Menace").[[3]](#cite_note-Episode_I-3) The [Encyclopedia](/wiki/Encyclopedia_(StarWars.com) "Encyclopedia (StarWars.com)") entry for Daultay Dofine said the blockade of Naboo lasted for a [month](/wiki/Standard_month "Standard month").[[22]](#cite_note-SWE_Daultay_Dofine-22) However, the later novel *[Queen's Peril](/wiki/Queen%27s_Peril "Queen's Peril")* established that Naboo did not have the food stocks to last that long under the blockade.[[1]](#cite_note-Queen's_Peril-1) Indeed, the reference book *[Star Wars: Battles that Changed the Galaxy](/wiki/Star_Wars:_Battles_that_Changed_the_Galaxy "Star Wars: Battles that Changed the Galaxy")* established the blockade was in place only a week before the invasion began,[[21]](#cite_note-Battles_that_Changed_the_Galaxy-21) which was also implied in *Queen's Peril* when the chapter that sees the start of the invasion is the same which notes, before a time break mid-chapter, that seven days have passed since the blockade began.[[1]](#cite_note-Queen's_Peril-1)

*The Phantom Menace* does, however, establish that the blockade was withdrawn at some point before the main characters' return to Naboo, with Obi-Wan Kenobi and Ric Olié noting that the Droid Control Ship is the only battleship to appear on the Royal Starship's scope.[[3]](#cite_note-Episode_I-3) While *Battles that Changed the Galaxy* concurs with this,[[21]](#cite_note-Battles_that_Changed_the_Galaxy-21) several other sources have erroneously described the blockade as still being in place during this frame of time. Nute Gunray claims that the Royal Starship managed to get past the blockade in *Queen's Peril*,[[1]](#cite_note-Queen's_Peril-1) and several reference books,[[21]](#cite_note-Battles_that_Changed_the_Galaxy-21)[[37]](#cite_note-CVD-37)[[38]](#cite_note-Complete_Vehicles_New-38) including *Battles that Changed the Galaxy* despite its text,[[21]](#cite_note-Battles_that_Changed_the_Galaxy-21) feature images that depict several Federation battleships being involved in the battle.[[21]](#cite_note-Battles_that_Changed_the_Galaxy-21)[[37]](#cite_note-CVD-37)[[38]](#cite_note-Complete_Vehicles_New-38) This article assumes the film, and therefore the text in *Battles that Changed the Galaxy* that supports it, is correct.
