**Jedi Generals** were high-[ranking](/wiki/Military_rank "Military rank") [Jedi](/wiki/Jedi "Jedi") [military](/wiki/Republic_Military "Republic Military") [officers](/wiki/Officer "Officer") in the [Grand Army of the Republic](/wiki/Grand_Army_of_the_Republic "Grand Army of the Republic"). Originally informal, the title was officially [created](/wiki/Jedi_Military_Integration_Act "Jedi Military Integration Act") in [22 BBY](/wiki/22_BBY "22 BBY") shortly into the [Clone Wars](/wiki/Clone_Wars "Clone Wars"), being reserved for [Jedi Knights](/wiki/Jedi_Knight "Jedi Knight"), [Jedi Masters](/wiki/Jedi_Master "Jedi Master"), and the [Jedi High Council](/wiki/Jedi_High_Council "Jedi High Council")—the governing body of the [Jedi Order](/wiki/Jedi_Order "Jedi Order"). As [generals](/wiki/General "General"), the Jedi were responsible for leading the [Galactic Republic](/wiki/Galactic_Republic "Galactic Republic")'s [clone trooper](/wiki/Clone_trooper "Clone trooper") [army](/wiki/Army "Army") against the [forces](/wiki/Separatist_Droid_Army "Separatist Droid Army") of the [Confederacy of Independent Systems](/wiki/Confederacy_of_Independent_Systems "Confederacy of Independent Systems").

By [19 BBY](/wiki/19_BBY "19 BBY"), the Jedi were spread thin across [the galaxy](/wiki/The_galaxy "The galaxy"). As the war drew to a close, [Supreme Chancellor](/wiki/Supreme_Chancellor "Supreme Chancellor") [Sheev Palpatine](/wiki/Darth_Sidious "Darth Sidious") ordered the [clone](/wiki/Cloning "Cloning") army to initiate [Order 66](/wiki/Order_66 "Order 66")—a military protocol authorizing the [annihilation](/wiki/Great_Jedi_Purge "Great Jedi Purge") of the Jedi Order, which the clone [troopers](/wiki/Trooper "Trooper") were [programmed](/wiki/Behavioral_modification_biochip "Behavioral modification biochip") to obey without question. As a result, many unsuspecting Jedi generals were summarily [executed](/wiki/Death "Death") by their own [soldiers](/wiki/Soldier "Soldier"), although a few survivors managed to escape into [exile](/wiki/Exile_(punishment) "Exile (punishment)"), such as [Obi-Wan Kenobi](/wiki/Obi-Wan_Kenobi "Obi-Wan Kenobi") and [Grand Master](/wiki/Grand_Master "Grand Master") [Yoda](/wiki/Yoda "Yoda").

Having liquidated most of the Grand Army's Jedi [officers](/wiki/Commanding_officer "Commanding officer"), Palpatine—whose secret identity was [Darth](/wiki/Darth "Darth") Sidious, the [Dark Lord of the Sith](/wiki/Dark_Lord_of_the_Sith "Dark Lord of the Sith")—[declared](/wiki/Proclamation_of_the_New_Order "Proclamation of the New Order") himself [Emperor](/wiki/Galactic_Emperor "Galactic Emperor") of the [Galactic Empire](/wiki/Galactic_Empire "Galactic Empire"). [Anakin Skywalker](/wiki/Anakin_Skywalker "Anakin Skywalker"), himself a Jedi General who turned to the [dark side of the Force](/wiki/Dark_side_of_the_Force "Dark side of the Force"), became the Emperor's [apprentice](/wiki/Sith_apprentice "Sith apprentice") and enforcer, and assumed the identity of Darth Vader. Despite Sidious's victory, the legacy of the Jedi who led legions on behalf of the light would haunt his reign, the few survivors and their students eventually shattering his hold over the galaxy.

## Powers[]

 [![Jedi Generals provided leadership to the Grand Army of the Republic and coordinated strategy with the Supreme Chancellor.](data:image/gif;base64,R0lGODlhAQABAIABAAAAAP///yH5BAEAAAEALAAAAAABAAEAQAICTAEAOw%3D%3D)](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/starwars/images/5/52/GeonosisPlanning.png/revision/latest?cb=20120831044545) [![Jedi Generals provided leadership to the Grand Army of the Republic and coordinated strategy with the Supreme Chancellor.](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/starwars/images/5/52/GeonosisPlanning.png/revision/latest/scale-to-width-down/250?cb=20120831044545)](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/starwars/images/5/52/GeonosisPlanning.png/revision/latest?cb=20120831044545) 

Jedi Generals provided leadership to the Grand Army of the Republic and coordinated strategy with the Supreme Chancellor.

 

Under the [Jedi Military Integration Act](/wiki/Jedi_Military_Integration_Act "Jedi Military Integration Act"),[[7]](#cite_note-Brotherhood-7) the [rank](/wiki/Military_rank "Military rank") of [general](/wiki/General "General") provided the [Jedi](/wiki/Jedi "Jedi") with the authority to lead the [Grand Army of the Republic](/wiki/Grand_Army_of_the_Republic "Grand Army of the Republic"). A Jedi general was the [commanding officer](/wiki/Commanding_officer "Commanding officer") of a [battalion](/wiki/Battalion "Battalion") of [clone troopers](/wiki/Clone_trooper "Clone trooper")[[8]](#cite_note-CL-8)—elite [soldiers](/wiki/Soldier "Soldier") [cloned](/wiki/Cloning "Cloning") from the [template](/wiki/Clone_template "Clone template") of [Jango Fett](/wiki/Jango_Fett "Jango Fett") and conditioned to follow orders with absolute obedience.[[1]](#cite_note-Episode_II-1) As generals, the Jedi had various wartime responsibilities such as leading [troops](/wiki/Troop "Troop") on the battlefield or coordinating strategy with the [Supreme Chancellor](/wiki/Supreme_Chancellor "Supreme Chancellor") of the [Galactic Republic](/wiki/Galactic_Republic "Galactic Republic").[[3]](#cite_note-TCW_film-3) Jedi Generals also dealt with mundane administrative decisions regarding staffing, equipment and resupply, promotions and training, and even budget requests.[[9]](#cite_note-Collapse-9) As the governing body of the [Jedi Order](/wiki/Jedi_Order "Jedi Order"),[[10]](#cite_note-KothDB-10) the [Jedi High Council](/wiki/Jedi_High_Council "Jedi High Council") directed the Jedi in their role as [military](/wiki/Republic_Military "Republic Military") [leaders](/wiki/Commander "Commander"). However, the High Council could also make forays onto the [battlefield](/wiki/Ground_warfare "Ground warfare"), serving alongside their [troopers](/wiki/Trooper "Trooper") as [Jedi Master](/wiki/Jedi_Master "Jedi Master") [Mace Windu](/wiki/Mace_Windu "Mace Windu") demonstrated during the [campaign](/wiki/Battle_of_Ryloth "Battle of Ryloth") to liberate [Ryloth](/wiki/Ryloth "Ryloth").[[11]](#cite_note-WinduDB-11)[[12]](#cite_note-Liberty_on_Ryloth-12) Directly fighting the enemy was understood as an expression of bravery.[[13]](#cite_note-Star_Wars_Battlefront_II-13) Many Jedi expressed respect for their charges and a hope for them to grow, Master Koon repudiating their idea that Clones were expendable,[[14]](#cite_note-rm2-14) and Yoda telling Clones that though in body they looked alike, he saw them through the Force with unique traits.[[15]](#cite_note-Ambush-15)

The clone troopers were subject to the authority of their Jedi Generals.[[6]](#cite_note-Carnage_of_Krell-6) They were loyal to the Jedi and the Republic,[[16]](#cite_note-clonetroopers-16) although there were instances of disobedience among the rank-and-file. In such cases a Jedi could exercise their authority as a general by ordering the arrest and [court-martial](/wiki/Court-martial "Court-martial") of an offending clone. If the offense was [treason](/wiki/Treason "Treason"), it was within the general's authority to have the [prisoner](/wiki/Prisoner "Prisoner") summarily [executed](/wiki/Death_penalty "Death penalty"). The execution could be carried out either by a firing [squad](/wiki/Squad "Squad") or the general.[[6]](#cite_note-Carnage_of_Krell-6) On the other hand, a Jedi General could choose to let the normal gears of justice turn when dealing with those who were accused of treason.[[17]](#cite_note-The_Hidden_Enemy-17)

### Background and origins[]

#### A day to come[]

Jedi leading armies into battle, lightsabers swung in anger. You already made me a marshal when all I wanted to do was help those who were hurting. When all I wanted to do was* serve*.*"

Throughout its history, Jedi Order found itself obliged to wage war against a range of enemies, such as the [Sith](/wiki/Sith "Sith"),[[19]](#cite_note-The_High_Republic:_Light_of_the_Jedi-19) the [slaver empire of the Zygerrians](/wiki/Zygerrian_Slave_Empire "Zygerrian Slave Empire"),[[20]](#cite_note-Zygerrian-20) and Mandalore.[[21]](#cite_note-21) Afterwards, some members of the Jedi Order resolved to never again become warriors,[[22]](#cite_note-Rising_Storm-22) instead hoping to merely remain keepers of galactic peace despite their close ties to the Galactic Republic.[[1]](#cite_note-Episode_II-1) On the other hand, Jedi like Porter Engle and those serving with him on [Elphrona](/wiki/Elphrona "Elphrona") when the Nihil began to be a known threat considered the role of a Jedi as a warrior as natural as that of being a mentor, discovering new places, and mediator of disputes.[[19]](#cite_note-The_High_Republic:_Light_of_the_Jedi-19) As the [Nihil conflict](/wiki/Nihil_conflict "Nihil conflict") dragged on during the [High Republic Era](/wiki/High_Republic_Era "High Republic Era"), the Jedi grew increasingly close with the Republic at the government's urging[[23]](#cite_note-Out_of_the_Shadows-23) in the name of opposing the [Nihil](/wiki/Nihil "Nihil") [marauders](/wiki/Pirate "Pirate").[[24]](#cite_note-Wayseeker-24) Although the Jedi would fight alongside and lead the [Republic Defense Coalition](/wiki/Republic_Defense_Coalition "Republic Defense Coalition")[[25]](#cite_note-Tempest_Runner-25) and its [Galactic Republic troopers](/wiki/Republic_Defense_Coalition_troop "Republic Defense Coalition troop")[[26]](#cite_note-TOS1-26) in the name of destroying the Nihil threat, Jedi Master [Stellan Gios](/wiki/Stellan_Gios "Stellan Gios") made it clear to Supreme Chancellor [Lina Soh](/wiki/Lina_Soh "Lina Soh") that the Jedi were not going to become her soldiers.[[22]](#cite_note-Rising_Storm-22) All the same, the [Outer Rim Territories](/wiki/Outer_Rim_Territories "Outer Rim Territories") were effectively consumed by war over the course of the Nihil conflict, which many Jedi had trouble adjusting to.[[27]](#cite_note-Midnight_Horizon-27)

While the Nihil were eventually defeated with the [Battle of Eriadu](/wiki/Battle_of_Eriadu "Battle of Eriadu") and [mission to Planet X](/wiki/Mission_to_Planet_X_(Jedi_Order) "Mission to Planet X (Jedi Order)"),[[18]](#cite_note-The_Finale_1-18) the tightened bond between the Republic and Jedi, something that had been useful when fighting the Nihil, did not end with the conflict's conclusion; instead, the Republic seemed, in the opinion of Jedi Master [Vernestra Rwoh](/wiki/Vernestra_Rwoh "Vernestra Rwoh"), to be a demanding entity that wanted access to Jedi affairs and hoped turn the Order into a branch of Republic policy, instead of leaving it an independent follower of the [light side of the Force](/wiki/Light_side_of_the_Force "Light side of the Force").[[24]](#cite_note-Wayseeker-24) Jedi Master [Keeve Trennis](/wiki/Keeve_Trennis "Keeve Trennis"), too, feared for the future of the Jedi Order after the Nihil conflict;[[18]](#cite_note-The_Finale_1-18) having been named [marshal](/wiki/Marshal "Marshal") of the Republic's [Stormwall Defense Fleet](/wiki/Stormwall_Defense_Fleet "Stormwall Defense Fleet") and helped to lead what had been a war effort against the Nihil,[[28]](#cite_note-Tempest_Breaker-28) Trennis pondered if she had actually been serving "light and life," as she told her followers, as a marshal or if taking a part in the conflict had prevented her from saving those who could have been rescued. Fearful that, one day, the Jedi would uncover a threat worse than the Nihil and dive even deeper into a war mentality, Trennis decided to leave the Jedi, taking up the life of [Speaker](/wiki/Speaker "Speaker") of the [Beacon](/wiki/Beacon "Beacon") instead.[[18]](#cite_note-The_Finale_1-18)

#### Coming of the Clone Wars[]

:   "*No, I mean officially. Generals of the Grand Army of the Republic. Padawans will be commanders. The Jedi will hold true military standing in terms of strategy and resources. Our relationship with the Republic will now go far beyond peacekeeping and mediation.*"
:   ―Obi-Wan Kenobi, after the passage of the Jedi Military Integration Act[[7]](#cite_note-Brotherhood-7)

Over the following two [centuries](/wiki/Standard_century "Standard century"),[[29]](#cite_note-Timelines-29) the Jedi continued to see themselves as peacekeepers and worked with the Republic.[[1]](#cite_note-Episode_II-1) In that time, however, the Jedi had grown increasingly reliant on their traditions[[30]](#cite_note-Ahsoka-30) and seemed to grow more and more isolated from the galactic community, focusing more on the future of the Republic than more individual, planetary matters. Rather than maintain their High Republic Era-network of outposts, the Jedi largely kept themselves cloistered[[31]](#cite_note-Living_Force_Reveal-31) on the Republic [capital](/wiki/Capital "Capital") world of [Coruscant](/wiki/Coruscant "Coruscant") in their [main temple](/wiki/Imperial_Palace "Imperial Palace").[[32]](#cite_note-Living_Force-32) Citizens across the Outer Rim Territories held a distrust for the Jedi, whom they had come to regard as agents of the Republic first and foremost.[[33]](#cite_note-RiseSeps-33) Finally, it was the advent of the [Clone Wars](/wiki/Clone_Wars "Clone Wars") in [22 BBY](/wiki/22_BBY "22 BBY")[[34]](#cite_note-GA-34) that totally threatened the Jedi Order's position as "peacekeepers" as they found themselves called to fight for the Republic against the [breakaway](/wiki/Separatist_Crisis "Separatist Crisis") [Confederacy of Independent Systems](/wiki/Confederacy_of_Independent_Systems "Confederacy of Independent Systems").[[1]](#cite_note-Episode_II-1)

 [![Under the Jedi Military Integration Act, Jedi Knights and Masters served as generals of the Grand Army of the Republic.](data:image/gif;base64,R0lGODlhAQABAIABAAAAAP///yH5BAEAAAEALAAAAAABAAEAQAICTAEAOw%3D%3D)](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/starwars/images/7/7b/Battle-of-Kamino-SabyMenyhei.png/revision/latest?cb=20230418234345) [![Under the Jedi Military Integration Act, Jedi Knights and Masters served as generals of the Grand Army of the Republic.](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/starwars/images/7/7b/Battle-of-Kamino-SabyMenyhei.png/revision/latest/scale-to-width-down/300?cb=20230418234345)](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/starwars/images/7/7b/Battle-of-Kamino-SabyMenyhei.png/revision/latest?cb=20230418234345) 

Under the Jedi Military Integration Act, Jedi Knights and Masters served as generals of the Grand Army of the Republic.

 

At the [First Battle of Geonosis](/wiki/First_Battle_of_Geonosis "First Battle of Geonosis") that kicked off the war,[[1]](#cite_note-Episode_II-1) the Jedi did not hold a military rank in the newly-formed Grand [Army](/wiki/Army "Army") of the Republic. Despite this, high-ranking Jedi acted as *de facto* generals, commanding more than 80 [regiments](/wiki/Regiment "Regiment") of clone troopers. Under the Jedi High Council's skillful leadership, the clone army was able to achieve both air and ground supremacy against the [forces](/wiki/Separatist_Droid_Army "Separatist Droid Army") of the Confederacy of Independent Systems.[[8]](#cite_note-CL-8) Following their victory on [Geonosis](/wiki/Geonosis "Geonosis"),[[1]](#cite_note-Episode_II-1) the Jedi Order's role in the Republic's military forces remained in effect, with Jedi Masters and Jedi Knights acting as high ranking officers, but their position was remained informal. The term "general" emerged as an informal designation from the clones, who felt confused about the Jedi's role in military affairs.[[7]](#cite_note-Brotherhood-7) The title was respected by other Republic officials.[[35]](#cite_note-QH-35) Several [weeks](/wiki/Standard_week "Standard week") into the [Clone Wars](/wiki/Clone_Wars "Clone Wars"), the Jedi Military Integration Act was passed, which officially made Knights and Masters into holders of a formal rank, Jedi General, while Padawans were made into Jedi Commanders.[[7]](#cite_note-Brotherhood-7)

### Official generals[]

Throughout the Clone Wars, the Jedi generals commanded the loyalty of the clone troopers who followed them into battle across [the galaxy](/wiki/The_galaxy "The galaxy").[[16]](#cite_note-clonetroopers-16) These generals led their men to many victories, liberating worlds[[12]](#cite_note-Liberty_on_Ryloth-12) and taking care of their troopers, though not without losses.[[14]](#cite_note-rm2-14) General Jedi [Iskat Akaris](/wiki/Thirteenth_Sister "Thirteenth Sister") led a force of clone troopers into the [mission to Thule](/wiki/Mission_to_Thule "Mission to Thule"), which ended why many of her clones killed in action. The losses weighed heavily on her, but [Clone Captain](/wiki/Clone_Captain "Clone Captain") [Spider](/wiki/Spider "Spider") urged her not to apologize general for actions made in the heat of battle, reflecting that otherwise apologizing would be all a general ever did. Nonetheless, the mission helped to pave the way to her fall to the [dark side of the Force](/wiki/Dark_side_of_the_Force "Dark side of the Force").[[36]](#cite_note-Inquisitor-36)

 [![At the end of the Clone Wars, the Jedi Generals were executed by their clone troopers under Order 66.](data:image/gif;base64,R0lGODlhAQABAIABAAAAAP///yH5BAEAAAEALAAAAAABAAEAQAICTAEAOw%3D%3D)](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/starwars/images/6/68/Depa-troopers-Order-66.png/revision/latest?cb=20211231053511) [![At the end of the Clone Wars, the Jedi Generals were executed by their clone troopers under Order 66.](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/starwars/images/6/68/Depa-troopers-Order-66.png/revision/latest/scale-to-width-down/300?cb=20211231053511)](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/starwars/images/6/68/Depa-troopers-Order-66.png/revision/latest?cb=20211231053511) 

At the end of the Clone Wars, the Jedi Generals were executed by their clone troopers under Order 66.

 

For the war's end, however, the clones turned against the Jedi and executed them as enemies of the state in accordance with [Order 66](/wiki/Order_66 "Order 66")[[2]](#cite_note-Episode_III-2)—a military protocol[[37]](#cite_note-Conspiracy-37) that authorized the [liquidation](/wiki/Great_Jedi_Purge "Great Jedi Purge") of the entire Jedi Order.[[2]](#cite_note-Episode_III-2) Unknown to the clones, secret [behavioral modification biochips](/wiki/Behavioral_modification_biochip "Behavioral modification biochip") had been designed and integrated into them to compel them to follow Order 66 without hesitation as part of a Sith plot to destroy the Jedi[[38]](#cite_note-Orders-38) and gain control of the galaxy. Although the generals [Yoda](/wiki/Yoda "Yoda"), [Quinlan Vos](/wiki/Quinlan_Vos "Quinlan Vos"), [Oppo Rancisis](/wiki/Oppo_Rancisis "Oppo Rancisis"), and [Obi-Wan Kenobi](/wiki/Obi-Wan_Kenobi "Obi-Wan Kenobi") survived the initial purge, the [Sith Lord](/wiki/Sith_Lord "Sith Lord") [Darth Sidious](/wiki/Darth_Sidious "Darth Sidious") [proclaimed](/wiki/Proclamation_of_the_New_Order "Proclamation of the New Order") himself [Emperor](/wiki/Galactic_Emperor "Galactic Emperor") of the [Galactic Empire](/wiki/Galactic_Empire "Galactic Empire") in the wake of the Jedi Order's fall[[2]](#cite_note-Episode_III-2) in [19 BBY](/wiki/19_BBY "19 BBY").[[34]](#cite_note-GA-34)

### Legacy of service[]

:   "*General Kenobi, years ago you served my father in the Clone Wars.*"
:   ―Princess Leia Organa, to Obi-Wan Kenobi[[39]](#cite_note-Episode_IV-39)

After [decades](/wiki/Standard_decade "Standard decade") in [exile](/wiki/Exile_(punishment) "Exile (punishment)"), Kenobi was [sought out](/wiki/Secret_mission_to_Tatooine "Secret mission to Tatooine") by [Princess](/wiki/Princess "Princess") [Leia Organa](/wiki/Leia_Skywalker_Organa_Solo "Leia Skywalker Organa Solo"). Knowing of the former Jedi general's service in the Clone Wars through her adoptive [father](/wiki/Parent "Parent"), [Bail Organa](/wiki/Bail_Prestor_Organa "Bail Prestor Organa"), she delivered a message imploring General Kenobi to help the [Alliance to Restore the Republic](/wiki/Alliance_to_Restore_the_Republic "Alliance to Restore the Republic") in their [struggle](/wiki/Galactic_Civil_War "Galactic Civil War") against the Empire. Kenobi agreed that the time was right to openly fight the darkness again, but his quest was cut short when he gave his life fighting on the Death Star.[[40]](#cite_note-:0-40) Ultimately, both Kenobi[[39]](#cite_note-Episode_IV-39) and Yoda[[41]](#cite_note-Episode_V-41) aided the Alliance by serving as a mentor, in turn, to the [aspiring Jedi](/wiki/Jedi_training "Jedi training") [Luke Skywalker](/wiki/Luke_Skywalker "Luke Skywalker")[[39]](#cite_note-Episode_IV-39)[[41]](#cite_note-Episode_V-41)—son of the [fallen](/wiki/Dark_side_of_the_Force "Dark side of the Force") Jedi General [Anakin Skywalker](/wiki/Anakin_Skywalker "Anakin Skywalker"), who assumed a new identity as [Darth](/wiki/Darth "Darth") Vader, [apprentice](/wiki/Sith_apprentice "Sith apprentice") of the [Dark Lord of the Sith](/wiki/Dark_Lord_of_the_Sith "Dark Lord of the Sith").[[2]](#cite_note-Episode_III-2) Luke and Anakin Skywalker would eventually bring about Sidious's first fall from power, the son inspiring his father to hurl the Emperor to his death.[[42]](#cite_note-42)

While they did not hold the formal rank of general after the Clone Wars, adherents to the Jedi path such as Luke Skywalker and Rey continued to lead in the battles against the Sith and their puppet factions as their predecessors once did.[[43]](#cite_note-Star_Wars:_Episode_V_The_Empire_Strikes_Back-43)[[44]](#cite_note-Star_Wars:_Episode_IX_The_Rise_of_Skywalker-44) Similarly, though they likewise never formally held the general rank, Ahsoka Tano and Kanan Jarrus provided high-level leadership to the rebels against the Empire.[[45]](#cite_note-45) Master Skywalker, in the [book he wrote](/wiki/The_Secrets_of_the_Jedi "The Secrets of the Jedi") after meeting Rey, praised the Jedi who defended the Republic, calling them noble warriors and holding them as inspirations in the Jedi way.[[46]](#cite_note-Star_Wars:_The_Secrets_of_the_Jedi-46)

## Behind the scenes[]

The concept that Jedi served as generals was first mentioned in *[Star Wars:](/wiki/Star_Wars:_Episode_IV_A_New_Hope "Star Wars: Episode IV A New Hope")* [Episode IV *A New Hope*](/wiki/Star_Wars:_Episode_IV_A_New_Hope "Star Wars: Episode IV A New Hope"), the first Star Wars film, where Obi-Wan Kenobi is introduced as such by Leia's message.[[40]](#cite_note-:0-40) Jedi were first seen leading the Grand Army of the Republic in the [2002](/wiki/2002 "2002") film [*Star Wars*: Episode II *Attack of the Clones*](/wiki/Star_Wars:_Episode_II_Attack_of_the_Clones "Star Wars: Episode II Attack of the Clones") of the [prequel trilogy](/wiki/Prequel_trilogy "Prequel trilogy").[[1]](#cite_note-Episode_II-1) At the time, however, the Jedi only acted as *de facto* generals because the rank of Jedi General had not yet been established [in-universe](/wiki/In-universe "In-universe"), according to the [2016](/wiki/2016 "2016") [canon](/wiki/Canon "Canon") reference book [*Star Wars: Complete Locations*](/wiki/Star_Wars:_Complete_Locations_(2016) "Star Wars: Complete Locations (2016)").[[8]](#cite_note-CL-8) When High Republic Era Jedi Master Stellan Gios remarked that the Jedi should never become warriors in the novel *[The High Republic: The Rising Storm](/wiki/The_High_Republic:_The_Rising_Storm "The High Republic: The Rising Storm")*,[[22]](#cite_note-Rising_Storm-22) it was intended by author [Cavan Scott](/wiki/Cavan_Scott "Cavan Scott") as a reference of the Jedi Order's eventual fate of becoming generals.[[47]](#cite_note-Never_be_generals-47)
