[Vanguard Squadron](/wiki/Vanguard_Squadron "Vanguard Squadron") was comprised of non-binary, male, and female beings.

 

**Gender** referred to a myriad of identities and expressions. Various [species](/wiki/Species "Species") had sexes such as female and male that influenced individuals' characteristics, and on top of that, many individuals had **female**, **male**, and **non-binary** genders or were **genderless**, with some changing their identities over [time](/wiki/Time "Time"). Gender was a social construct among [sentient](/wiki/Sentience "Sentience") species, with specific roles associated with female and male members of certain communities. Many [droids](/wiki/Droid "Droid") had personality programming that corresponded to organic beings' notions of gender.

Based upon differences of sex,[[3]](#cite_note-Helmet_Collection_11-3) many members of [sentient](/wiki/Sentience "Sentience") [species](/wiki/Species "Species") used the designations "female" or "male" or had cultural equivalents for that binary pair of terms, such as "woman" and "man."[[4]](#cite_note-Alien_Archive-4) Multiple genders existed among [the galaxy](/wiki/The_galaxy "The galaxy")'s sentient beings,[[5]](#cite_note-Bloodline-5) including trans and non-binary individuals,[[6]](#cite_note-Terec_Ceret-6) and some beings were genderless.[[7]](#cite_note-Solo:_Expanded_Edition-7) Gendered terms such as "daughter" and "son" for a child and "mother" and "father" for a [parent](/wiki/Parent "Parent") were used.[[8]](#cite_note-THRCE-8) Individuals were referred to by various sets of personal pronouns, though some beings used feminine, masculine, and gender-neutral interchangeably without necessarily indicating their gender, including: "she/her," "he/him," "they/them," and "zhe/zher."[[9]](#cite_note-Aftermath:_Life_Debt-9) Although [droids](/wiki/Droid "Droid") were [artificially intelligent](/wiki/Artificial_intelligence "Artificial intelligence") mechanical creations rather than organic lifeforms,[[10]](#cite_note-Droidography-10) individual droids had gendered programming—such as masculine for the [astromech](/wiki/R2-series_astromech_droid "R2-series astromech droid") [R2-D2](/wiki/R2-D2 "R2-D2")[[11]](#cite_note-R2-D2_Deluxe-11)—and were referred to by "he," "she," and "it" pronouns.[[10]](#cite_note-Droidography-10) The [bounty droid](/wiki/Haxion_Brood_Bounty_Droid "Haxion Brood Bounty Droid") [MA-55H](/wiki/MA-55H "MA-55H") was referred to by singular "they/them" and "he/him" pronouns.[[12]](#cite_note-Survivor-12)

Varying from culture to culture, societal roles and expectations were sometimes defined by gender.[[4]](#cite_note-Alien_Archive-4) Gendered social roles could be reflected by individuals' [clothing](/wiki/Clothing "Clothing"),[[13]](#cite_note-TLJ_VD-13) which could convey culture-specific meanings that the wearer may not have intended.[[14]](#cite_note-Star_Wars:_How_Not_to_Get_Eaten_by_Ewoks_and_Other_Galactic_Survival_Skills-14) During an argument between [Han Solo](/wiki/Han_Solo "Han Solo") and [Princess](/wiki/Princess "Princess") [Leia Organa](/wiki/Leia_Skywalker_Organa_Solo "Leia Skywalker Organa Solo"), Solo claimed that the princess was having romantic feelings for him, and an annoyed Leia shot back: "I guess you don't know everything about women yet."[[15]](#cite_note-ESB-15) Decades later, when Solo suspected that [Finn](/wiki/Finn "Finn") was lying to [Rey](/wiki/Rey_Skywalker "Rey Skywalker") about his real identity, he warned Finn that women "always figure out the truth. Always."[[16]](#cite_note-TFA-16)

At least eleven genders existed throughout the [galaxy](/wiki/Galaxy "Galaxy"),[[17]](#cite_note-Gender_count-17) and some individuals of the [Sabetue](/wiki/Sabetue "Sabetue") species were genderless and could be identified by their pure [white](/wiki/Color "Color") skin.[[7]](#cite_note-Solo:_Expanded_Edition-7) Individuals could be a gender that differed from what was assumed based on their sex when they were born. Among the [clones](/wiki/Cloning "Cloning") of the [human](/wiki/Human "Human") male [Jango Fett](/wiki/Jango_Fett "Jango Fett"),[[18]](#cite_note-Episode_II-18) a specific [clone trooper](/wiki/Clone_trooper "Clone trooper") was named [Sister](/wiki/Sister "Sister") by her fellow clones to affirm that she belonged among her brothers; the [Jedi Knight](/wiki/Jedi_Knight "Jedi Knight") [Anakin Skywalker](/wiki/Anakin_Skywalker "Anakin Skywalker") observed that she had "transcended gender." ### Societal roles

#### Overview

Societal roles and expectations were sometimes defined by gender, varying from culture to culture. The [Tusken Raiders](/wiki/Tusken_Raider "Tusken Raider") of the [desert](/wiki/Desert "Desert") [planet](/wiki/Planet "Planet") [Tatooine](/wiki/Tatooine "Tatooine") formed clans or tribes; in many of these, the males served as warriors who protected the tribe and attacked trespassers, and females raised the children and protected the home. Tuskens completely clothed their bodies, but Tusken females were identifiable by their jeweled masks.[[4]](#cite_note-Alien_Archive-4) However, not all tribes adhered to such strict gender roles: in the [tribe](/wiki/Unidentified_Tusken_Raider_tribe "Unidentified Tusken Raider tribe") joined by the [human](/wiki/Human "Human") former [bounty hunter](/wiki/Bounty_hunter "Bounty hunter") [Boba Fett](/wiki/Boba_Fett "Boba Fett"), females and males wore similar garb and fought alongside each other.[[25]](#cite_note-Stranger_in_a_Strange_Land-25)

Warrior initiation rites included giving each individual a [bantha](/wiki/Bantha "Bantha") of their gender to care for, creating a lifelong bond between the bantha and the Tusken. Members of the [Gamorrean](/wiki/Gamorrean "Gamorrean") species lived in clans ruled by a [Clan Matron](/wiki/Clan_Matron "Clan Matron") who selected a male [Warlord](/wiki/Warlord "Warlord") to rule with her based on his combat skills and strength. The primary duty of the sows was farming while the boars warred against other clans. Boars may have been responsible for hunting,[[4]](#cite_note-Alien_Archive-4) but sows may have hunted in addition to farming.[[26]](#cite_note-USWNE-26)

Among the avian[[13]](#cite_note-TLJ_VD-13) and matriarchal [Lanai](/wiki/Lanai "Lanai") on the planet [Ahch-To](/wiki/Ahch-To "Ahch-To"), the female Caretakers passed down their titles and roles from [mother](/wiki/Parent "Parent") to daughter, and maintained the sacred structures of the [First Jedi Temple](/wiki/First_Jedi_Temple "First Jedi Temple").[[26]](#cite_note-USWNE-26) The male Visitors spent much of their time [fishing](/wiki/Fishing "Fishing") in [sea](/wiki/Ocean "Ocean"), but when they returned with the [month's](/wiki/Standard_month "Standard month") catch, they joined the Caretakers for several [days](/wiki/Standard_day "Standard day") of celebration.[[4]](#cite_note-Alien_Archive-4) Accounts are unclear regarding the species of the planet [Crul](/wiki/Crul "Crul"), though they agree that [Crolutes](/wiki/Crolute "Crolute") were [native](/wiki/Homeworld "Homeworld") to the planet. Some said that Crolutes were divided into "cows" and "bulls,"[[27]](#cite_note-Aliens_of_the_Galaxy-27) and others that Crolutes were an entirely male species with a counterpart female species, [Gilliands](/wiki/Gilliand "Gilliand").[[4]](#cite_note-Alien_Archive-4)[[28]](#cite_note-True_Love-28)

Some species had differing appearances in addition to differing roles for their genders. An example of this was the [Dathomirian](/wiki/Dathomirian "Dathomirian") species. On the planet [Dathomir](/wiki/Dathomir "Dathomir"), female [Nightsisters](/wiki/Nightsisters "Nightsisters") and male [Nightbrothers](/wiki/Nightbrothers "Nightbrothers") lived separately. The Nightsisters became witches who wielded [magick](/wiki/Magick "Magick") that was enhanced by the power of their planet. When they wished to reproduce, they would visit the Nightbrother village to select a suitable mate;[[26]](#cite_note-USWNE-26) the Nightbrothers were subservient to the Nightsisters. While the Nightsisters had pale or white skin that they often tattooed with subtle or monochrome designs, the Nightbrothers used bold tattoos to enhance their colorful skin and natural striping.[[29]](#cite_note-The_Visual_Encyclopedia-29) The government on [Devaron](/wiki/Devaron "Devaron") was matriarchal. [Devaronian](/wiki/Devaronian "Devaronian") males were not permitted to hold office as they were considered less serious-minded and more inclined to wander the stars than females, who would remain on the planet. Males were bald and had horns, but females had small bumps instead along with [hair](/wiki/Hair "Hair") on their heads.[[4]](#cite_note-Alien_Archive-4)

Some species, such as the [Paccosh](/wiki/Paccosh "Paccosh") of the [Unknown Regions](/wiki/Unknown_Regions "Unknown Regions"), held one gender in particularly high regard. The Chiss man [Mitth'raw'nuruodo](/wiki/Mitth%27raw%27nuruodo "Mitth'raw'nuruodo"), serving as a [Senior Captain](/wiki/Senior_Captain "Senior Captain") of the [Chiss Ascendancy](/wiki/Chiss_Ascendancy "Chiss Ascendancy"), insisted on bringing a Chiss woman with him to a [Paccosh asteroid mining station](/wiki/Unidentified_Paccosh_mining_station "Unidentified Paccosh mining station"), having surmised from the clothing and body positions of Paccosh corpses he encountered that that species held women in high regard.[[30]](#cite_note-Chaos_Rising-30)

#### Species-specific genders

:   "*In case you were unaware, Your Highness, Chalhuddans have five different genders and shift through them throughout their lives. Their native pronoun cases are rather complex—indicating not only current gender but two or three previous ones, and occasionally the gender they feel most likely to be next, but as our language has no equivalent words, 'you' or 'they' can be used in all cases.*"
:   ―C-3PO, to Princess Leia Organa[[31]](#cite_note-Leia,_Princess_of_Alderaan-31)

Some species as a whole would change their genders or choose new ones at different [times](/wiki/Time "Time") in their life. Individuals of the [Xidelphiad](/wiki/Xidelphiad "Xidelphiad") species had protogynic phases and entered a chrysalis when changing from female to male, a process that took [years](/wiki/Standard_year "Standard year") before they emerged.[[32]](#cite_note-Propaganda-32) [Dianogas](/wiki/Dianoga "Dianoga") were physically capable of multiple [reproductive](/wiki/Reproduction "Reproduction") roles and chose the gender they wished to be. Their most common gender was diangous; a female named [Omi](/wiki/Omi "Omi") met several other females and more who were diangous, but did not meet a dianoga who identified as male for many years.[[33]](#cite_note-The_Baptist-33) The [Chalhuddan](/wiki/Chalhuddan "Chalhuddan") species had five genders through which they shifted at different life stages. In their native language, their pronoun cases reflected their current gender, two or three of their previous genders, and sometimes the gender they felt most likely to be next. Due to the lack of equivalent pronouns in Galactic Basic, "you" and "they" were substituted.[[31]](#cite_note-Leia,_Princess_of_Alderaan-31)

During the [Imperial Era](/wiki/Imperial_Era "Imperial Era"), [Chiss](/wiki/Chiss "Chiss") individuals were rarely [Force-sensitive](/wiki/Force-sensitive "Force-sensitive"). Almost all of those with the [ability](/wiki/The_Force "The Force") of Third Sight were girls. Before their Third Sight faded with age into adulthood, Chiss girls were employed as [navigators](/wiki/Navigator "Navigator") called [ozyly-esehembo](/wiki/Sky-walker "Sky-walker") in [Cheunh](/wiki/Cheunh "Cheunh"), which translated as "sky-walker" in Basic.[[34]](#cite_note-Thrawn:_Alliances-34)

### LGBTQIA+ representation

#### Books and comics

 [![2021's The High Republic comic series introduced the trans non-binary Jedi Terec and Ceret.](data:image/gif;base64,R0lGODlhAQABAIABAAAAAP///yH5BAEAAAEALAAAAAABAAEAQAICTAEAOw%3D%3D)](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/starwars/images/1/1b/PrideHR6Garron.jpg/revision/latest?cb=20210721220251) [![2021's The High Republic comic series introduced the trans non-binary Jedi Terec and Ceret.](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/starwars/images/1/1b/PrideHR6Garron.jpg/revision/latest/scale-to-width-down/200?cb=20210721220251)](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/starwars/images/1/1b/PrideHR6Garron.jpg/revision/latest?cb=20210721220251) 

2021's *The High Republic* comic series introduced the trans non-binary Jedi Terec and Ceret.

 

Author [Claudia Gray](/wiki/Claudia_Gray "Claudia Gray") has mentioned on her [Tumblr](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tumblr "wikipedia:Tumblr") blog that there are brand new genders in the *Star Wars* galaxy.[[46]](#cite_note-Tumblr-46) Although her novels have not named new genders, she first indicated their existence in [2016](/wiki/2016 "2016") with Leia Organa's observation of four or more genders in her novel [*Bloodline*](/wiki/Bloodline_(novel) "Bloodline (novel)").[[5]](#cite_note-Bloodline-5) Gray introduced the multi-gendered Chalhuddans in the [2017](/wiki/2017 "2017") novel *[Leia, Princess of Alderaan](/wiki/Leia,_Princess_of_Alderaan "Leia, Princess of Alderaan")*.[[31]](#cite_note-Leia,_Princess_of_Alderaan-31)

Also in 2016, the novel *[Aftermath: Life Debt](/wiki/Aftermath:_Life_Debt "Aftermath: Life Debt")* by [Chuck Wendig](/wiki/Chuck_Wendig "Chuck Wendig") was the first *[Star Wars](/wiki/Star_Wars "Star Wars")* work to feature an explicitly [genderqueer](https://lgbtqia.fandom.com/wiki/Genderqueer "w:c:lgbtqia:Genderqueer") character who identified as neither male or female, the non-binary pirate Eleodie Maracavanya.[[47]](#cite_note-Wendig_Gender_Tweet-47) Following Maracavanya's introduction, zhe was followed by additional non-binary characters, such as a [Black Sun agent](/wiki/Unidentified_Black_Sun_agent "Unidentified Black Sun agent") in the 2016 novel [*Ahsoka*](/wiki/Ahsoka_(novel) "Ahsoka (novel)") by [E. K. Johnston](/wiki/E._K._Johnston "E. K. Johnston"),[[48]](#cite_note-EKJ_Nonbinary_Tweet-48) Taka Jamoreesa in the [2018](/wiki/2018 "2018") novel *[Last Shot](/wiki/Last_Shot "Last Shot")* by [Daniel José Older](/wiki/Daniel_Jos%C3%A9_Older "Daniel José Older"),[[49]](#cite_note-DJO_Nonbinary_Tweet-49) and Keo Venzee in the [2020](/wiki/2020 "2020") [video game](/wiki/Star_Wars_video_games "Star Wars video games") *[Star Wars: Squadrons](/wiki/Star_Wars:_Squadrons "Star Wars: Squadrons")*.[[50]](#cite_note-BT-K_Nonbinary_Statement-50) In honor of the [International Transgender Day of Visibility](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/International_Transgender_Day_of_Visibility "wikipedia:International Transgender Day of Visibility") in March [2021](/wiki/2021 "2021"), the *Star Wars* [Instagram](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Instagram "wikipedia:Instagram") account posted that Terec and Ceret, two Kotabi Jedi Knights from the [*Star Wars: The High Republic*](/wiki/Star_Wars:_The_High_Republic_(2021) "Star Wars: The High Republic (2021)") [comic book](/wiki/Star_Wars_comic_books "Star Wars comic books") series, are [trans](https://lgbtqia.fandom.com/wiki/Transgender "w:c:lgbtqia:Transgender") [non-binary](https://lgbtqia.fandom.com/wiki/Non-binary "w:c:lgbtqia:Non-binary").[[6]](#cite_note-Terec_Ceret-6)

#### Non-canon history

In the [2022](/wiki/2022 "2022") non-[canon](/wiki/Canon "Canon") video game *[LEGO Star Wars: The Skywalker Saga](/wiki/LEGO_Star_Wars:_The_Skywalker_Saga "LEGO Star Wars: The Skywalker Saga")*, a [gonk droid](/wiki/Energetic_GNK "Energetic GNK") is designated with "he/him" as well as "they/them" pronouns.[[51]](#cite_note-Gendergonk-51)

### Inconsistencies

In the [2005](/wiki/2005 "2005") film [*Star Wars*: Episode III *Revenge of the Sith*](/wiki/Star_Wars:_Episode_III_Revenge_of_the_Sith "Star Wars: Episode III Revenge of the Sith"), the performer under [Meena Tills](/wiki/Meena_Tills "Meena Tills")' guise was a man, causing action figure packaging and [Legends](/wiki/Star_Wars_Legends "Star Wars Legends") storytelling to claim that Tills was male. When it was decided that Tills would appear in the *[Star Wars: The Clone Wars](/wiki/Star_Wars:_The_Clone_Wars "Star Wars: The Clone Wars")* television series, it was agreed that Tills would be female to match her namesake.[[52]](#cite_note-Much_to_Learn-52)

Several characters in the multimedia project *[Star Wars: The High Republic](/wiki/Star_Wars:_The_High_Republic "Star Wars: The High Republic")* have had their genders inconsistently depicted, rather than intentionally portraying gender [transitions](https://lgbtqia.fandom.com/wiki/Transition "w:c:lgbtqia:Transition"). [Shai Tennem](/wiki/Shai_Tennem "Shai Tennem")'s first appearance in the short story "[Starlight: Go Together](/wiki/Starlight:_Go_Together "Starlight: Go Together")," written by [Charles Soule](/wiki/Charles_Soule "Charles Soule") and published in [*Star Wars Insider* 199](/wiki/Star_Wars_Insider_199 "Star Wars Insider 199"), portrayed Tennem with masculine pronouns;[[53]](#cite_note-Starlight_Go_Together-53) however, Soule's 2021 novel *[The High Republic: Light of the Jedi](/wiki/The_High_Republic:_Light_of_the_Jedi "The High Republic: Light of the Jedi")* referred to Tennem with feminine pronouns. [Estala Maru](/wiki/Estala_Maru "Estala Maru") is erroneously referred to as "her" in *Light of the Jedi*;[[54]](#cite_note-Light_of_the_Jedi-54) the *Insider* short story "[Starlight: First Duty](/wiki/Starlight:_First_Duty "Starlight: First Duty")," written by [Cavan Scott](/wiki/Cavan_Scott "Cavan Scott"), uses masculine pronouns,[[55]](#cite_note-First_Duty-55) and Maru is portrayed as male in *The High Republic* comic book series.[[56]](#cite_note-THR_1-56) Upon the initial publication of *Light of the Jedi*,[[54]](#cite_note-Light_of_the_Jedi-54) [Teri Rosason](/wiki/Teri_Rosason "Teri Rosason"),[[57]](#cite_note-The_Rising_Storm-57) whose first name was not revealed in the book, was first mentioned with masculine pronouns and [Adampo](/wiki/Adampo "Adampo") first appeared with feminine pronouns.[[54]](#cite_note-Light_of_the_Jedi-54) In *[The High Republic: Into the Dark](/wiki/The_High_Republic:_Into_the_Dark "The High Republic: Into the Dark")* by Claudia Gray, Rosason's first appearance stated the character was "a human woman of advanced years," and Adampo received masculine pronouns.[[58]](#cite_note-Into_the_Dark-58) E-book editions of *Light of the Jedi* were later corrected to reflect Rosason as female and Adampo as male.[[54]](#cite_note-Light_of_the_Jedi-54)
