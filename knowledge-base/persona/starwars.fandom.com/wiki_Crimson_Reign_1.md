***Crimson Reign* 1** is the first issue of the [canon](/wiki/Canon "Canon") [comic](/wiki/Star_Wars_comic_books "Star Wars comic books") miniseries *[Star Wars: Crimson Reign](/wiki/Star_Wars:_Crimson_Reign "Star Wars: Crimson Reign")*. The issue was written by [Charles Soule](/wiki/Charles_Soule "Charles Soule"), illustrated by [Steven Cummings](/wiki/Steven_Cummings "Steven Cummings") and [Guru-eFX](/wiki/Guru-eFX "Guru-eFX"), and published by [Marvel Comics](/wiki/Marvel_Comics "Marvel Comics") on [December 8](/wiki/December_8 "December 8"), [2021](/wiki/2021 "2021"). The issue contains the story **Part 1: "The Orphans"**.

## Plot summary[]

## After the Fall[]

(The galaxy is a prison, and all its inhabitants are prisoners, and the reason is one word. It is the name of our fear. It is the name of our chains. That word is Sith.)
In a secluded, castle-like chamber, two individuals activate a glowing orb atop a pedestal. The device is a unique [holocron](/wiki/Holocron "Holocron") that projects the face of **[the Archivist](/wiki/The_Archivist "The Archivist")**, a woman with long hair tendrils and one white eye. She explains that the record is accessible to both the Light and Dark sides of [the Force](/wiki/The_Force "The Force") before launching into the history of Lady [Qi'ra](/wiki/Qi%27ra "Qi'ra").

Qi’ra, a survivor of the [Corellian](/wiki/Corellian "Corellian") sewers, rose to lead [Crimson Dawn](/wiki/Crimson_Dawn "Crimson Dawn") following the death of its previous master, the former [Sith](/wiki/Sith "Sith") [Maul](/wiki/Maul "Maul"). In a grand hall, she addresses an assembled group of experts and mercenaries, including the **[Knights of Ren](/wiki/Knights_of_Ren "Knights of Ren")**, the assassin [Deathstick](/wiki/Deathstick "Deathstick"), [Ochi of Bestoon](/wiki/Ochi "Ochi"), and a strike team known as [Orphans](/wiki/Orphans "Orphans").

Qi’ra reveals the secret purpose of her organization: to destroy the Sith. She describes the galaxy not as a balanced range of powers, but as a pyramid topped by [Emperor Palpatine](/wiki/Emperor "Emperor") ([Darth Sidious](/wiki/Darth_Sidious "Darth Sidious")) and [Darth Vader](/wiki/Anakin_Skywalker "Anakin Skywalker"). She plans to exploit the Sith's habit of discarding those they use, promising her followers that by working together, they can kill the Sith and achieve their own diverse goals of profit, knowledge, or justice.

The campaign begins with deception. Qi’ra meets with [Lord Gyuti](/wiki/Gyuti "Gyuti") of the [Black Sun](/wiki/Black_Sun "Black Sun") [syndicate](/wiki/Syndicate "Syndicate"), falsely suggesting that the Empire is seeking a new criminal partner to replace the [Hutts](/wiki/Hutts "Hutts"). Her allies repeat this performance with the [Pykes](/wiki/Pyke_Syndicate "Pyke Syndicate") and other syndicates, sowing seeds of greed and competition.

Simultaneously, she deploys the Orphans - led by [Chanath Cha](/wiki/Chanath_Cha "Chanath Cha"), whose parents were murdered by Vader - to sabotage syndicate operations. On the planet [Dantooine](/wiki/Dantooine "Dantooine"), the team destroys a Black Sun narcotics lab, leaving no survivors and framing the attack to look like a message from a rival power. As the syndicates descend into a paranoid civil war, the disorder draws the ire of the Emperor, who summons Darth Vader to intervene.

As the war escalates, Qi’ra initiates the next phases of her plan:

* The Knights of Ren **are dispatched to infiltrate [Fortress Vader](/wiki/Fortress_Vader "Fortress Vader"), using secret codes provided by Qi’ra.**
* The Archivist **is tasked with searching for surviving [Jedi](/wiki/Jedi "Jedi"), such as [Yoda](/wiki/Yoda "Yoda"), to serve as potential allies or bait.**
* Qi'ra prepares to draw [Leia Organa](/wiki/Leia_Organa "Leia Organa") and the [Rebel Alliance](/wiki/Alliance_to_Restore_the_Republic "Alliance to Restore the Republic") into the conflict.

The story concludes with Qi'ra expressing confidence in her scheme, while the Archivist's narration provides a grim foreshadowing: despite Qi'ra's brilliance, the record is titled "The Fall of Crimson Dawn"—a tragedy in the making.

## Continuity[]

* One panel erroneously states that the [Black Sun smash-processing lab](/wiki/Black_Sun_smash-processing_lab "Black Sun smash-processing lab") was located on [Lothal](/wiki/Lothal "Lothal").
* The issue depicts [Deathstick](/wiki/Deathstick "Deathstick") as a [human](/wiki/Human "Human"), even though she was previously established to be a [Dathomirian](/wiki/Dathomirian "Dathomirian") [Zabrak](/wiki/Zabrak "Zabrak") by the [2015](/wiki/2015 "2015") video game *[Star Wars: Uprising](/wiki/Star_Wars:_Uprising "Star Wars: Uprising")*.[[3]](#cite_note-SWU-3)
* [Ochi](/wiki/Ochi "Ochi") is seen with the [Blade of Ochi of Bestoon](/wiki/Blade_of_Ochi_of_Bestoon "Blade of Ochi of Bestoon"). However *[Shadow of the Sith](/wiki/Shadow_of_the_Sith "Shadow of the Sith")* established that he didn't receive the blade until the [quest for Exegol](/wiki/Quest_for_Exegol "Quest for Exegol") in [21 ABY](/wiki/21_ABY "21 ABY"). Also, [*Star Wars*: Episode IX *The Rise of Skywalker*](/wiki/Star_Wars:_Episode_IX_The_Rise_of_Skywalker "Star Wars: Episode IX The Rise of Skywalker") established that the blade was inscribed with information about the [DS-2 Death Star II Mobile Battle Station](/wiki/DS-2_Death_Star_II_Mobile_Battle_Station "DS-2 Death Star II Mobile Battle Station")'s wreckage meaning that the blade must've been created after [Battle of Endor](/wiki/Battle_of_Endor "Battle of Endor").
