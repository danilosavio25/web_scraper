The **Battle of Naboo**, also known as the **Battle for Naboo** or **Liberation of Naboo**, was the final conflict of the [Trade Federation](/wiki/Trade_Federation "Trade Federation")'s [invasion](/wiki/Invasion_of_Naboo "Invasion of Naboo") of the [planet](/wiki/Planet "Planet") [Naboo](/wiki/Naboo "Naboo"). The battle featured four separate parts and was conceived by Queen [Padmé Amidala](/wiki/Padm%C3%A9_Amidala "Padmé Amidala") upon returning to her planet, after unsuccessfully pleading with the [Galactic Senate](/wiki/Galactic_Senate "Galactic Senate") for help. The plan would be to use the [Gungans](/wiki/Gungan "Gungan") and their Grand Army to lure the Trade Federation army away from [Theed](/wiki/Theed "Theed"), the capital city, so Amidala and her [security forces](/wiki/Royal_Naboo_Security_Forces "Royal Naboo Security Forces") could infiltrate the palace and capture [Trade Federation Viceroy](/wiki/Viceroy_of_the_Trade_Federation "Viceroy of the Trade Federation") [Nute Gunray](/wiki/Nute_Gunray "Nute Gunray"). Meanwhile, a team of starfighter pilots would enter orbit in an assault on the Federation command ship, the destruction of which would disable all Federation battle droids on the planet.

In what became known as the **Battle of the Great Grass Plains** or simply the **Battle of the Grassy Plain**, the Gungans lured the Federation army away from the city to distract them through a fight in the [Great Grass Plains](/wiki/Great_Grass_Plains "Great Grass Plains"), while the queen and her team made their way to the palace in the **Battle of Theed**, which was also known as **Second Battle of Theed** due to a [prior engagement](/wiki/Capture_of_Theed "Capture of Theed"). Once inside, and as the starfighter pilots made their way into orbit after being freed, the Naboo were confronted by the [Sith Lord](/wiki/Sith_Lord "Sith Lord") [Darth Maul](/wiki/Maul "Maul"). The queen's [Jedi](/wiki/Jedi "Jedi") protectors, [Qui-Gon Jinn](/wiki/Qui-Gon_Jinn "Qui-Gon Jinn") and [Obi-Wan Kenobi](/wiki/Obi-Wan_Kenobi "Obi-Wan Kenobi"), fought the Sith Lord throughout the palace while Amidala and her forces made their way to the Viceroy. Another front of the Theed battle opened up at [Camp Four](/wiki/Camp_Four "Camp Four"), where imprisoned Naboo, led by [Mariek Panaka](/wiki/Mariek_Panaka "Mariek Panaka"), rebelled against their droid captors.

By the battle's end, the Federation control ship had been destroyed by [Anakin Skywalker](/wiki/Anakin_Skywalker "Anakin Skywalker"), saving the Gungan army from destruction; Amidala and her security personnel captured Gunray; and Darth Maul was defeated and presumed dead after having killed Jinn in [lightsaber](/wiki/Lightsaber "Lightsaber") combat. The battle liberated Naboo from the Federation, led to Kenobi's promotion to [Jedi Knight](/wiki/Jedi_Knight "Jedi Knight"), and convinced the [Jedi Council](/wiki/Jedi_High_Council "Jedi High Council") to accept Skywalker for Jedi training.[[1]](#cite_note-Episode_I-1)

The Battle of Naboo was ultimately part of a much larger plot. The invasion itself had been orchestrated by Naboo's Senator [Sheev Palpatine](/wiki/Darth_Sidious "Darth Sidious"), who was secretly the Sith Lord Darth Sidious, in a successful effort to be elected [Supreme Chancellor](/wiki/Supreme_Chancellor "Supreme Chancellor"). He later engineered the [Clone Wars](/wiki/Clone_Wars "Clone Wars") to amass total control over the [Galactic Republic](/wiki/Galactic_Republic "Galactic Republic") so he could transform it into the [Galactic Empire](/wiki/Galactic_Empire "Galactic Empire") and destroy the [Jedi Order](/wiki/Jedi_Order "Jedi Order"). During the war, in which Palpatine achieved his goals, [Count](/wiki/Count "Count") [Dooku](/wiki/Dooku "Dooku") remarked that, despite ten years passing between them, the Clone Wars truly began during the Naboo Crisis.

Ten years before the Clone Wars, the [Trade Federation](/wiki/Trade_Federation "Trade Federation") was forced to [invade](/wiki/Invasion_of_Naboo "Invasion of Naboo") the [planet](/wiki/Planet "Planet") [Naboo](/wiki/Naboo "Naboo") by its hidden master, [Darth Sidious](/wiki/Darth_Sidious "Darth Sidious"). Over the course of the [Battle of Theed](/wiki/Capture_of_Theed "Capture of Theed"), the Federation failed to capture the planet's [Queen](/wiki/Queen "Queen"), [Padmé Amidala](/wiki/Padm%C3%A9_Amidala "Padmé Amidala"), despite the assistance of the [Sith Lord](/wiki/Sith_Lord "Sith Lord") [Darth Maul](/wiki/Maul "Maul"). She fled to [Coruscant](/wiki/Coruscant "Coruscant") to plead her case to the [Galactic Senate](/wiki/Galactic_Senate "Galactic Senate"), to no avail as many Senators were, as Palpatine put it, "on the Trade Federation's payroll."[[1]](#cite_note-Episode_I-1)

Deciding to take action herself, she returned to Naboo and, with the aid of the exiled [Gungan](/wiki/Gungan "Gungan") [Jar Jar Binks](/wiki/Jar_Jar_Binks "Jar Jar Binks"), the Queen went to the [Gungan Sacred Place](/wiki/Gungan_Sacred_Place "Gungan Sacred Place") to convince [Boss](/wiki/Boss "Boss") [Rugor Nass](/wiki/Rugor_Nass "Rugor Nass"), leader of the Gungans, to align with the [human](/wiki/Human "Human") [population](/wiki/Naboo_(people) "Naboo (people)") of Naboo. The Trade Federation had managed to drive the Gungans to their sacred place after invading their homes. Amidala humbled herself before the Gungans, [begging](/wiki/Boss_Meetup "Boss Meetup") them for their help, and Nass pledged to support to her, forging a historic pact between the Naboo and the Gungans. Once what remained of the [Royal Naboo Security Forces](/wiki/Royal_Naboo_Security_Forces "Royal Naboo Security Forces") had been summoned to their hiding place, the Queen, head of security [Captain](/wiki/Captain "Captain") [Panaka](/wiki/Quarsh_Panaka "Quarsh Panaka"), and Nass devised a plan to retake Naboo. By the time Amidala returned to the planet, only one [Droid Control Ship](/wiki/Lucrehulk-class_battleship "Lucrehulk-class battleship") was left in [orbit](/wiki/Orbit "Orbit").[[1]](#cite_note-Episode_I-1)

During the planning session, Nass made Binks a [Bombad General](/wiki/Bombad_General "Bombad General") for the upcoming battle. The plan for the Gungan Grand Army was to lure out the main bulk of the Federation's Droid Army onto the Great Grass Plains for the head-to-head assault, allowing Amidala and her strike force, along with the two Jedi, [Qui-Gon Jinn](/wiki/Qui-Gon_Jinn "Qui-Gon Jinn") and [Obi-Wan Kenobi](/wiki/Obi-Wan_Kenobi "Obi-Wan Kenobi") (with [Anakin Skywalker](/wiki/Anakin_Skywalker "Anakin Skywalker") tagging along), to attack capital city of Theed and capture the main hangar. Naboo pilots would then attack the Droid Control Ship in hopes of deactivating the Federation Army, and allowing Amidala to capture Viceroy [Nute Gunray](/wiki/Nute_Gunray "Nute Gunray"), and thus regain control of the planet.[[1]](#cite_note-Episode_I-1) Binks commanded a [regiment](/wiki/Binks%27_forces "Binks' forces") of [militiagungs](/wiki/Militiagung "Militiagung"), including Captain [Roos Tarpals](/wiki/Roos_Tarpals "Roos Tarpals") and [Oma Prumba](/wiki/Oma_Prumba "Oma Prumba").[[6]](#cite_note-On_the_Front_Lines-6)

## The battle[]

### Battle of the Great Grass Plains[]

:   "*Ouch time.*"
:   ―Captain Tarpals[[1]](#cite_note-Episode_I-1)

 [![The Gungan Grand Army moves into position.](data:image/gif;base64,R0lGODlhAQABAIABAAAAAP///yH5BAEAAAEALAAAAAABAAEAQAICTAEAOw%3D%3D)](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/starwars/images/0/04/Gunganarmy.jpg/revision/latest?cb=20110917124938) [![The Gungan Grand Army moves into position.](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/starwars/images/0/04/Gunganarmy.jpg/revision/latest/scale-to-width-down/257?cb=20110917124938)](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/starwars/images/0/04/Gunganarmy.jpg/revision/latest?cb=20110917124938) 

The Gungan Grand Army moves into position.

 

The Grand Army had moved into their position by midday, taking up much of the level part of the Great Grass Plains, 40 [kilometers](/wiki/Kilometer "Kilometer") from Theed, where Captain Tarpals ordered the activation of their [shield generators](/wiki/Deflector_shield_generator "Deflector shield generator"), set up on the backs of [fambaas](/wiki/Fambaa "Fambaa"). The army was encased in at least three enormous energy domes impervious to any bombardment. The Droid Army descended from the hills into the valley, in a formation of [Armored Assault Tanks](/wiki/Armored_Assault_Tank_Mk_I "Armored Assault Tank Mk I") escorting [Multi-Troop Transports](/wiki/Multi-Troop_Transport "Multi-Troop Transport"). Settled into position, facing the massive Grand Army, [OOM-9](/wiki/OOM-9 "OOM-9") ordered a preliminary bombardment, firing on the Gungans' [shields](/wiki/Deflector_shield "Deflector shield"), with his AATs, to little effect.[[1]](#cite_note-Episode_I-1)

After a while, OOM-9 ordered the other AATs to hold fire; the Gungans believed the battle to be won. The tanks fell back, and the Federation's MTTs unloaded thousands of [battle droids](/wiki/Battle_droid "Battle droid"), much to the horror of the Gungans. The [B1-series battle droids](/wiki/B1-series_battle_droid "B1-series battle droid") slowly marched through the Gungan shield,[[1]](#cite_note-Episode_I-1) which the Gungans had not expected to be possible.[[14]](#cite_note-Galaxy_at_War-14) The droids fired on the unprotected soldiers. Led by Captain Tarpals and Bombad General Binks, whose [kaadu](/wiki/Kaadu "Kaadu") tossed him off early in the battle, the Gungans fought with personal shields, [catapults](/wiki/Catapult "Catapult"), [boomas](/wiki/Booma "Booma"), [cestas](/wiki/Cesta "Cesta") and [atlatls](/wiki/Atlatl "Atlatl"), destroying dozens of battle droids. The Gungan energy catapults launched large [plasma](/wiki/Plasma "Plasma") boomas at the droid ranks, but they could not halt the persistent droid march. A squad of [droidekas](/wiki/Droideka "Droideka") rolled onto the battleground, further increasing Gungan casualties as the MTTs moved in to trap the Grand Army. At some point, a battle droid, having been severed at the waist, fell onto General Binks, eventually getting him off and he started stomping and kicking it, eventually accidentally getting his foot entangled with the severed wiring.[[1]](#cite_note-Episode_I-1)

That setback eventually came with an advantage when he took down some droids, including one destroyer droid, due to the wiring causing the droid to fire its blaster when he moved the entangled foot. Ultimately, the shield generator was destroyed by concentrated fire[[1]](#cite_note-Episode_I-1) from a team of droidekas.[[15]](#cite_note-SWE_Fambaa-15) With the whole the protective dome deactivating from that, OOM-9 immediately called upon all AATs, battle droids, [Single Trooper Aerial Platforms](/wiki/Single_Trooper_Aerial_Platform "Single Trooper Aerial Platform"), to advance. The guards charged the camp's battle droids, while Panaka knocked out [Overseer](/wiki/Overseer "Overseer") [Usan Ollin](/wiki/Usan_Ollin "Usan Ollin").[[7]](#cite_note-Queen's_Peril-7)

Before the battle, the prisoners of [camp three](/wiki/Camp_Three_(Naboo) "Camp Three (Naboo)") had also been aware that they would be able to rebel. [Gregar Typho](/wiki/Gregar_Typho "Gregar Typho") had taken charge of the Naboo imprisoned in the camp.[[7]](#cite_note-Queen's_Peril-7) Serving with distinction,[[16]](#cite_note-A-Z58-16) he lost an eye during the battle[[12]](#cite_note-Queen's_Shadow-12) as he fought Federation battle droids.[[16]](#cite_note-A-Z58-16)

## Aftermath[]

### Peace on Naboo, looming turmoil for the galaxy[]

:   "*Thinking on it, was we all just mooie-mooie lucky? Maybe we was. But mesa liken to think it was destiny.*"
:   ―Gungan militiagung Oma Prumba[[6]](#cite_note-On_the_Front_Lines-6)

After the battle was over, peace finally returned to Naboo.[[1]](#cite_note-Episode_I-1) The engagement was henceforth remembered as "the Battle of Naboo,"[[12]](#cite_note-Queen's_Shadow-12) a name coined by Sabé before the final fight against the Occupation even started.[[7]](#cite_note-Queen's_Peril-7) Viceroy Gunray was captured and forced to sign a treaty, officially ending the invasion. The Gungan people and the Naboo united as one world, and a celebration took place in Theed.[[1]](#cite_note-Episode_I-1) During the [Clone Wars](/wiki/Clone_Wars "Clone Wars"), Skywalker recounted his story to members of the [501st Legion](/wiki/501st_Legion "501st Legion"); the story later served as an inspiration for three [clone troopers](/wiki/Clone_trooper "Clone trooper") who, acting against orders, utilized Skywalker's tactics of taking out a ship's reactor from the inside when they blew up a Separatist supply ship during the [Battle of Umbara](/wiki/Battle_of_Umbara "Battle of Umbara").[[17]](#cite_note-TCW:POD-17)

Palpatine had been elected [Supreme Chancellor](/wiki/Supreme_Chancellor "Supreme Chancellor") in the meantime, replacing [Finis Valorum](/wiki/Finis_Valorum "Finis Valorum"). The next day, Palpatine arrived on Naboo with members of the [Jedi High Council](/wiki/Jedi_High_Council "Jedi High Council") to oversee the arrest of Gunray and Haako. [Yoda](/wiki/Yoda "Yoda") promoted Kenobi to full Jedi Knight and assigned him to train Skywalker. That evening, Qui-Gon was given a [Jedi funeral](/wiki/Jedi_funeral "Jedi funeral"). The day after, the Gungans and Naboo celebrated with a parade in Theed, complete with overflights by N-1 starfighters.[[1]](#cite_note-Episode_I-1) Palpatine continued his plans and led [the galaxy](/wiki/The_galaxy "The galaxy") into the Clone Wars.[[18]](#cite_note-Episode_III-18) During the conflict, [Count](/wiki/Count "Count") [Dooku](/wiki/Dooku "Dooku")[[19]](#cite_note-Shadow_Warrior-19)—Sidious's new apprentice and replacement for Maul[[20]](#cite_note-Son_of_Dathomir_1-20)—once reflected that the war had truly started with the Invasion of Naboo. Skywalker read into Dooku's words, demanding to know what role he had played in the Battle of Naboo, to which Dooku refused to answer, merely responding that the Sith Order controlled everything.[[19]](#cite_note-Shadow_Warrior-19)

### The survival of Maul[]

Despite what the Jedi and even Darth Sidious were led to believe, Darth Maul did not die on Naboo. Instead he survived through his all-consuming hatred for Obi-Wan Kenobi and managed to escape the planet. Knowing his master would never forgive his failure, Maul fled to the reclusive world of [Lotho Minor](/wiki/Lotho_Minor "Lotho Minor"), where, despite the strength of his rage, the pain he felt from his near-fatal wound drove him to insanity and made him lose his mind. The only thing he remembered was the reason why he had become what he was now: Kenobi. The only person who knew of Darth Maul's survival was [Mother](/wiki/Mother_(title) "Mother (title)") [Talzin](/wiki/Talzin "Talzin") of the [Nightsisters](/wiki/Nightsisters "Nightsisters"). At the height of the Clone Wars, after having incurred the wrath of [Count](/wiki/Count "Count") [Dooku](/wiki/Dooku "Dooku"), Talzin sent her new servant, [Savage Opress](/wiki/Savage_Opress "Savage Opress"), Maul's brother, to find him.[[5]](#cite_note-TCWB-5) Sidious would later learn of Maul's survival during the Clone Wars from Kenobi in person when debriefing him on the events on the [Skirmish on Florrum](/wiki/Skirmish_on_Florrum "Skirmish on Florrum").[[21]](#cite_note-Revival-21) Sidious later tracked Maul down to Mandalore and managed to subdue him, sparing him as he still had use for him[[22]](#cite_note-The_Lawless-22) to [track down](/wiki/Campaign_against_the_Shadow_Collective "Campaign against the Shadow Collective") Mother Talzin.[[20]](#cite_note-Son_of_Dathomir_1-20)

Maul eventually escaped from Sidious and went into hiding for several years after the fall of the Republic and the Jedi Order. At one point, he became the secret head of the [Crimson Dawn](/wiki/Crimson_Dawn "Crimson Dawn") crime syndicate.[[23]](#cite_note-Solo:_A_Star_Wars_Story-23) He was finally discovered on [Malachor](/wiki/Malachor "Malachor"), when the Jedi survivors [Ahsoka Tano](/wiki/Ahsoka_Tano "Ahsoka Tano") and [Kanan Jarrus](/wiki/Kanan_Jarrus "Kanan Jarrus"), as well as Kanan's apprentice [Ezra Bridger](/wiki/Ezra_Bridger "Ezra Bridger"), arrived on the planet seeking information about how to destroy the Sith.[[24]](#cite_note-Twilight_of_the_Apprentice-24) Maul would manipulate the Jedi (particularly Ezra) in assisting him for his own purposes, eventually leading to him discovering that Obi-Wan Kenobi was still alive. After learning that Obi-Wan was hiding on [Tatooine](/wiki/Tatooine "Tatooine"), Maul manipulated Ezra to draw Obi-Wan out and then confronted his old enemy on the desert planet. However, when Maul attempted to defeat his opponent in the same manner as he had done with Qui-Gon Jinn years before, Obi-Wan countered in anticipation with a fatal slash of [his lightsaber](/wiki/Obi-Wan_Kenobi%27s_third_lightsaber "Obi-Wan Kenobi's third lightsaber") and finally brought an end to Maul's threat to the galaxy.[[25]](#cite_note-Twin_Suns-25)

## Behind the scenes[]

The Battle of Naboo first appeared in the [1999](/wiki/1999 "1999") film [*Star Wars*: Episode I *The Phantom Menace*](/wiki/Star_Wars:_Episode_I_The_Phantom_Menace "Star Wars: Episode I The Phantom Menace"), the first installment of the *[Star Wars](/wiki/Star_Wars "Star Wars")* [prequel trilogy](/wiki/Prequel_trilogy "Prequel trilogy").[[1]](#cite_note-Episode_I-1)

Like the [Battle of Endor](/wiki/Battle_of_Endor "Battle of Endor") featured in the [1983](/wiki/1983 "1983") film [*Star Wars*: Episode VI *Return of the Jedi*](/wiki/Star_Wars:_Episode_VI_Return_of_the_Jedi "Star Wars: Episode VI Return of the Jedi"), the Great Grass Plains battle is inspired by the [Vietnam War](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Vietnam_War "wikipedia:Vietnam War"), in particular, the concept of a technologically inferior race besting a more advanced culture in battle. However, unlike Endor, Great Grass Plains is not won by the protagonists, and they are only saved due to luck, and outside intervention. According to Lucas during the audio commentary, Binks' antics were inspired by [Buster Keaton](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Buster_Keaton "wikipedia:Buster Keaton"). The battle also had Japanese themes to it.[[26]](#cite_note-tpmc-26) According to the 1999 reference book *[The Art of Star Wars Episode I: The Phantom Menace](/wiki/The_Art_of_Star_Wars_Episode_I:_The_Phantom_Menace "The Art of Star Wars Episode I: The Phantom Menace")*, the battle was originally perceived as taking place between Naboo/Gungan piloted airwhales and Federation "air bikes."[[27]](#cite_note-TAoEI-27)

*[Star Wars: The Making of Episode I The Phantom Menace](/wiki/Star_Wars:_The_Making_of_Episode_I_The_Phantom_Menace "Star Wars: The Making of Episode I The Phantom Menace")* described the production process of the special effects for the battle. First, the scene was mapped out by animatics. Then, FX artist [Dennis Muren](/wiki/Dennis_Muren "Dennis Muren") assembled pictures of Earth locations, and these images were distorted by [Industrial Light & Magic](/wiki/Industrial_Light_%26_Magic "Industrial Light & Magic") to appear alien. CGI images of the Trade Federation vehicles and Gungan armies were subsequently inserted into the shot. Animators received horse-riding instructions to illustrate the kaadu realistically. In addition, hundreds of effects such as laser blasts were rendered. Explosions were made on stage in real life, then scanned into the digital compositing system for alterations. The droids, at the suggestion of George Lucas, were made to be pigeon-like, with different actions randomly assigned to individual droids. On the other hand, the space sequence was produced through a combination of computer animation and motion-control model photography.[[28]](#cite_note-SW:TMoEITPM-28)

For the duel between Obi-Wan Kenobi and Qui-Gon Jinn against Darth Maul on the Naboo hangar, [John Williams](/wiki/John_Williams "John Williams") wrote and scored "[Duel of the Fates](/wiki/Duel_of_the_Fates "Duel of the Fates")," the [musical theme](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Motif_(music) "wikipedia:Motif (music)") ultimately featured as the main theatrical motif for the film.[[29]](#cite_note-Interview1-29) According to [Dave Filoni](/wiki/Dave_Filoni "Dave Filoni"), the use of the musical theme symbolically represents that the duel's outcome will decide the fate of Anakin Skywalker; Qui-Gon Jinn fights for Anakin's future as he knows that he's the father figure Anakin needs after convincing him to leave his mother on Tatooine. In the end, Obi-Wan Kenobi takes Anakin as his Padawan because he promised Qui-Gon to do it rather than for genuine care of Anakin, as while Kenobi comes to see Skywalker as a brother, he doesn't act like a father.[[30]](#cite_note-DotF_Importance-30)
