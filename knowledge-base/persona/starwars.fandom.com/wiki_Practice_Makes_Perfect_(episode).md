"**Practice Makes Perfect**" is the fifth episode of the anthology animated television series [*Star Wars: Tales of the Jedi*](/wiki/Star_Wars:_Tales_of_the_Jedi_(television_series) "Star Wars: Tales of the Jedi (television series)"). The episode was directed by [Saul Ruiz](/wiki/Saul_Ruiz "Saul Ruiz"), written by [Dave Filoni](/wiki/Dave_Filoni "Dave Filoni"), and aired on [October 26](/wiki/October_26 "October 26"), [2022](/wiki/2022 "2022").

## Official description[]

*A Jedi Padawan is given a seemingly impossible exercise.*

## Plot summary[]

At the [Jedi Temple](/wiki/Imperial_Palace "Imperial Palace") on [Coruscant](/wiki/Coruscant "Coruscant"), [Anakin Skywalker](/wiki/Anakin_Skywalker "Anakin Skywalker") is late. He runs through the halls to a [dojo](/wiki/Jedi_Temple_dojo "Jedi Temple dojo"), where his [Padawan](/wiki/Padawan "Padawan"), [Ahsoka Tano](/wiki/Ahsoka_Tano "Ahsoka Tano"), practices against training [remotes](/wiki/Remote "Remote") under the supervision of [Jedi Master](/wiki/Jedi_Master "Jedi Master") [Tera Sinube](/wiki/Tera_Sinube "Tera Sinube"). Other [Jedi](/wiki/Jedi "Jedi"), including [Obi-Wan Kenobi](/wiki/Obi-Wan_Kenobi "Obi-Wan Kenobi"), are also present, and Obi-Wan sarcastically tells Anakin that he's right on time, meaning late. After Anakin has arrived, Sinube activates a ray shield around the center of the room, encasing Ahsoka as a set of [new remotes](/wiki/Battle_droid_remote "Battle droid remote") designed to emulate [battle droids](/wiki/Battle_droid "Battle droid") surround her. Ahsoka is tested against the remotes, which she destroys quickly and easily.

Afterwards, in the hallway, Ahsoka is not pleased that Anakin doesn't seem to be impressed by her results, on top of being late. Anakin admits that she's right, he isn't impressed, because in his mind, the test is far too easy. He tells Ahsoka that he doesn't think she will reach her full potential if she's only up against inferior remotes, and promises to arrange her a proper test.

Later, Ahsoka arrives at the [military operations center](/wiki/Imperial_Inspectorate_HQ "Imperial Inspectorate HQ") aboard a [gunship](/wiki/Low_Altitude_Assault_Transport/infantry "Low Altitude Assault Transport/infantry"), only to be told by Anakin that she's late. He shows her into a large room where [Clone Captain](/wiki/Clone_Captain "Clone Captain") [Rex](/wiki/Rex "Rex") is waiting with several [clone troopers](/wiki/Clone_trooper "Clone trooper") from the [501st Legion](/wiki/501st_Legion "501st Legion"). Anakin explains to Ahsoka that he wants her to be good enough to hold out against Rex and the boys, because if she can do that, she can take anything the droids can throw at her. He adds that [training](/wiki/Jedi_training "Jedi training") against living opponents will be more difficult because they are less predictable than droids, so a key part of the training will be sensing who will fire first. The [clones](/wiki/Cloning "Cloning") form a circle around Ahsoka, and she ignites [her lightsaber](/wiki/Ahsoka_Tano%27s_lightsabers "Ahsoka Tano's lightsabers"). Ahsoka blocks the first [stun](/wiki/Stun "Stun") shots, but is eventually taken out.

She wakes up on the floor as Anakin reassures Rex that Ahsoka will be fine. Anakin tells her she's been unconscious for an [hour](/wiki/Standard_hour "Standard hour"), and that [Jesse](/wiki/Jesse "Jesse") wanted to apologize for how hard he stunned her, which he does from across the room. The respite does not last long, however, as Anakin has another round start. The training session goes on for some time, as Ahsoka is stunned again and again and Anakin insists that she continue. At one point, Rex says he's not sure how much more Ahsoka can take and wonders if they should stop. By then, Ahsoka is waking up faster, and the session continues for a while.

An older Ahsoka Tano continues her training

 

The training sessions continue on other days and in other places. Some [time](/wiki/Time "Time") later, after having built herself a [shoto lightsaber](/wiki/Shoto_lightsaber "Shoto lightsaber"), Ahsoka trains against the 501st, who now wear [Phase II armor](/wiki/Phase_II_clone_trooper_armor "Phase II clone trooper armor"), in the [hangar](/wiki/Hangar "Hangar") bay of a [Jedi cruiser](/wiki/Venator-class_Star_Destroyer "Venator-class Star Destroyer"), yet again as Anakin watches. Ahsoka held out against them for more than five [minutes](/wiki/Standard_minute "Standard minute") the last time, and employs acrobatic leaps and twists as she blocks more stun shots. The round ends with Rex stunning her.

On board the *[Tribunal](/wiki/Tribunal "Tribunal")*, Ahsoka and Rex walk down a hallway choked with smoke and sparks, signs of heavy damage. They stop outside a door. Rex puts his [helmet](/wiki/Helmet "Helmet") on and says he hopes all of that training Ahsoka did will pay off. He raises one of his [blaster pistols](/wiki/Blaster_pistol "Blaster pistol") to her back, as if holding her [prisoner](/wiki/Prisoner "Prisoner"), and she puts her hands up to her head, agreeing with him. The door opens and the two of them walk into the hangar beyond, where Jesse and troops of the [332nd Division](/wiki/332nd_Division "332nd Division") [wait](/wiki/Skirmish_aboard_the_Tribunal "Skirmish aboard the Tribunal").

## Continuity[]

The first part of the episode contains several contradictory timeline indications. Tano's test is overseen by Tera Sinube, whom she first met in "[Lightsaber Lost](/wiki/Lightsaber_Lost "Lightsaber Lost"),"[[4]](#cite_note-Lightsaber_Lost-4) which is dated to [21 BBY](/wiki/21_BBY "21 BBY").[[5]](#cite_note-LL_date-5) However, Jesse is wearing unpainted [Phase I clone trooper armor](/wiki/Phase_I_clone_trooper_armor "Phase I clone trooper armor"), despite having painted [armor](/wiki/Armor "Armor") in his first appearance, "[The Deserter](/wiki/The_Deserter "The Deserter"),"[[6]](#cite_note-The_Deserter-6) set before "Lightsaber Lost" in the official chronological episode order,[[7]](#cite_note-Chronological-7) as well as the comic "[501 Plus One](/wiki/501_Plus_One "501 Plus One"),"[[8]](#cite_note-501_Plus_One-8) which is set in 22 BBY.[[9]](#cite_note-Mimban_date-9) [Caleb Dume](/wiki/Kanan_Jarrus "Kanan Jarrus") is shown with a [Padawan braid](/wiki/Padawan_braid "Padawan braid") while following [Depa Billaba](/wiki/Depa_Billaba "Depa Billaba") around, despite not becoming her Padawan[[10]](#cite_note-Kanan_7-10) until around [19 BBY](/wiki/19_BBY "19 BBY").[[11]](#cite_note-Kanan_7_date-11)

Tano also wears a brand-new outfit during the episode's first part, different from her first[[12]](#cite_note-TCW_movie-12) or second Padawan outfits,[[13]](#cite_note-HOBS-13) the latter of which is shown in the second part of the episode during the training session in a [Star Destroyer](/wiki/Star_Destroyer "Star Destroyer") hangar. The size of Tano's [montrals](/wiki/Montral "Montral") and [lekku](/wiki/Lekku "Lekku") in the episode's first part more closely match the character design introduced in "[Heroes on Both Sides](/wiki/Heroes_on_Both_Sides "Heroes on Both Sides")," which included her second outfit.[[13]](#cite_note-HOBS-13) Obi-Wan Kenobi is depicted with long [hair](/wiki/Hair "Hair") identical to his [*Star Wars*: Episode II *Attack of the Clones*](/wiki/Star_Wars:_Episode_II_Attack_of_the_Clones "Star Wars: Episode II Attack of the Clones") hairstyle,[[14]](#cite_note-AOTC-14) despite having short hair throughout *[Star Wars: The Clone Wars](/wiki/Star_Wars:_The_Clone_Wars "Star Wars: The Clone Wars")*.[[12]](#cite_note-TCW_movie-12)[[15]](#cite_note-Hunt_for_Ziro-15) The novel [*Brotherhood*](/wiki/Brotherhood_(novel) "Brotherhood (novel)") depicts Kenobi having cut his hair after receiving the [Jedi Commander armor](/wiki/Jedi_Commander_armor "Jedi Commander armor") he wears in the episode.[[16]](#cite_note-Brotherhood-16) Anakin Skywalker's hair is also shorter than it was ever depicted on *The Clone Wars*.[[12]](#cite_note-TCW_movie-12) Therefore, Kenobi and Skywalker must have changed their hair styles several times over the course of the war.

When asked about these inconsistencies, [Lucasfilm Story Group](/wiki/Lucasfilm_Story_Group "Lucasfilm Story Group") member [Matt Martin](/wiki/Matt_Martin "Matt Martin") suggested that Billaba and Dume were not master and apprentice yet, and just happened to be spending time together,[[17]](#cite_note-Martin-17) also pointing out that [*Kanan* 7](/wiki/Kanan_7 "Kanan 7") established that Dume knew Billaba prior to being her Padawan.[[18]](#cite_note-Martin2-18) [Pablo Hidalgo](/wiki/Pablo_Hidalgo "Pablo Hidalgo") also mentioned that having a Padawan braid did not necessarily indicate an apprenticeship, and that *Kanan* 7 also featured Dume with a braid prior to being Billaba's Padawan.[[19]](#cite_note-Hidalgo-19) However "[Jedi Master's Quiz](/wiki/Jedi_Master%27s_Quiz "Jedi Master's Quiz")," a department in [*Star Wars Insider* 220](/wiki/Star_Wars_Insider_220 "Star Wars Insider 220"), stated that Dume was a Padawan during "Practice Makes Perfect."[[20]](#cite_note-Insider-20)

The scene where Rex and Tano confront the 332nd Division is continued in the *[Victory and Death](/wiki/Victory_and_Death "Victory and Death")* episode of the *[The Clone Wars](/wiki/Star_Wars:_The_Clone_Wars "Star Wars: The Clone Wars")*.[[21]](#cite_note-Victory_and_Death-21)
