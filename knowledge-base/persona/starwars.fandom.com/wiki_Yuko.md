At one point, he was present in [Mos Eisley](/wiki/Mos_Eisley "Mos Eisley") on [Tatooine](/wiki/Tatooine "Tatooine") with the [droid](/wiki/Droid "Droid") [BILY](/wiki/BILY "BILY").[[1]](#cite_note-Celebration-1)

## Behind the scenes[]

:   «*I wanted to depict a story about a spoiled child who can’t let go of their parents, learning to stand on their own and carve out their own future.*»
:   ―Masaki Tachibana[[3]](#cite_note-DTIMES-3)

Yuko appeared in "[Yuko's Treasure](/wiki/Yuko%27s_Treasure "Yuko's Treasure")", a non-[canon](/wiki/Canon "Canon") short film directed by [Masaki Tachibana](/wiki/Masaki_Tachibana "Masaki Tachibana") that released as part of the [third volume](/wiki/Star_Wars:_Visions_Volume_3 "Star Wars: Visions Volume 3") of the *[Star Wars: Visions](/wiki/Star_Wars:_Visions "Star Wars: Visions")* series. The animated short, produced by [Kinema Citrus Co.](/wiki/Kinema_Citrus_Co. "Kinema Citrus Co."), released on [Disney+](/wiki/Disney%2B "Disney+") on [October 29](/wiki/October_29 "October 29"), [2025](/wiki/2025 "2025"). Yuko was first introduced at the *Star Wars: Visions* panel at [Celebration Japan](/wiki/Celebration_Japan_(2025) "Celebration Japan (2025)") on [April 19](/wiki/April_19 "April 19"), 2025.[[1]](#cite_note-Celebration-1) Yuko is voiced by [Momoka Terasawa](/wiki/Momoka_Terasawa "Momoka Terasawa") in the original [Japanese](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Japanese_language "wikipedia:Japanese language") version and by Liam Karlsson in the English dub.[[2]](#cite_note-Getty-2)
