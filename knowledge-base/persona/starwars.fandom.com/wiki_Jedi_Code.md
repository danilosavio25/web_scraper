Chaos can spiral us into the unfathomable darkness. We all find ourselves staring into the abyss at times. Only those who find a way to rise above the darkness are deemed worthy to become Jedi.*"

The **Jedi Code**, known apocryphally as the **Jedi's Meditation**,[[5]](#cite_note-Guardians_of_the_Whills-5) was a set of rules and tenets in the [Jedi Order](/wiki/Jedi_Order "Jedi Order"), a [religious](/wiki/Religion "Religion") order that devoted to the ways of [the Force](/wiki/The_Force "The Force"). The Code evolved over the course of [centuries](/wiki/Standard_century "Standard century") and applied to all members of the Order, from [Jedi Initiates](/wiki/Jedi_Initiate "Jedi Initiate") and [Padawans](/wiki/Padawan "Padawan"), to [Jedi Knights](/wiki/Jedi_Knight "Jedi Knight") and [Jedi Masters](/wiki/Jedi_Master "Jedi Master"). Among the precepts of the Code was a rule forbidding a Jedi from [training](/wiki/Jedi_training "Jedi training") more than one [Padawan](/wiki/Padawan "Padawan") at any given [time](/wiki/Time "Time"). The Code also embodied the philosophical ideals of the Order, such as discipline, self control, a introspection, and was developed to help the Jedi maintain their devotion to the [light side of the Force](/wiki/Light_side_of_the_Force "Light side of the Force") by rejecting the temptations of the [dark side](/wiki/Dark_side_of_the_Force "Dark side of the Force").

During the [Imperial Era](/wiki/Imperial_Era "Imperial Era"), few adherents of the Jedi Code remained as a result of the [Great Jedi Purge](/wiki/Great_Jedi_Purge "Great Jedi Purge") that all but [eradicated](/wiki/Order_66 "Order 66") the Order. When the Order was rebuilt during the [New Republic Era](/wiki/New_Republic_Era "New Republic Era"), Jedi Master [Luke Skywalker](/wiki/Luke_Skywalker "Luke Skywalker") trained his apprentices, including his [nephew](/wiki/Skywalker_family "Skywalker family") [Ben Solo](/wiki/Ben_Solo "Ben Solo"), to adhere to the principles of the Jedi Code.

## Overview[]

### The Code[]

:   "*So, the Jedi are Force users united in our quest to understand the mysteries of the Force and to serve as guardians of peace and justice throughout the galaxy.* […] *we ground ourselves in a spiritual existence and give up individual attachments in order to focus entirely on greater concerns.*"  
    "*So, that means no sex.*"  
    "*Basically.*"
:   ―[Reath Silas](/wiki/Reath_Silas "Reath Silas") and [Affie Hollow](/wiki/Affie_Hollow "Affie Hollow")[[6]](#cite_note-The_High_Republic:_Into_the_Dark-6)

The [Jedi](/wiki/Jedi "Jedi") Code was a series of rules and tenets that governed the [Jedi Order](/wiki/Jedi_Order "Jedi Order")'s actions and embodied its beliefs.[[3]](#cite_note-TSWB-3) The Code taught its followers to not give in to feelings of anger toward other lifeforms, which would help them resist fear and prevent them from falling to the [dark side of the Force](/wiki/Dark_side_of_the_Force "Dark side of the Force").[[7]](#cite_note-UFC-7)

 [![The Jedi Code mandated that the Jedi could train only one apprentice at any given time.](data:image/gif;base64,R0lGODlhAQABAIABAAAAAP///yH5BAEAAAEALAAAAAABAAEAQAICTAEAOw%3D%3D)](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/starwars/images/c/c6/CalebDumeDepaBillabaTraining-Kanan1.png/revision/latest?cb=20160206054524) [![The Jedi Code mandated that the Jedi could train only one apprentice at any given time.](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/starwars/images/c/c6/CalebDumeDepaBillabaTraining-Kanan1.png/revision/latest/scale-to-width-down/250?cb=20160206054524)](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/starwars/images/c/c6/CalebDumeDepaBillabaTraining-Kanan1.png/revision/latest?cb=20160206054524) 

The Jedi Code mandated that the Jedi could train only one apprentice at any given time.

 

The Code forbade the Jedi from [killing](/wiki/Death "Death") unarmed opponents[[8]](#cite_note-Episode_III-8) as well as seeking revenge.[[9]](#cite_note-Episode_II-9)[[10]](#cite_note-Lair_of_Grievous-10) Amongst other dictates, the Jedi Code forbade [Jedi Knights](/wiki/Jedi_Knight "Jedi Knight") and [Jedi Masters](/wiki/Jedi_Master "Jedi Master") from [training](/wiki/Jedi_training "Jedi training") more than one [Padawan](/wiki/Padawan "Padawan") at a given [time](/wiki/Time "Time").[[11]](#cite_note-Episode_I-11)

Attachment was also forbidden by the Code.[[9]](#cite_note-Episode_II-9) [Grand Master](/wiki/Grand_Master "Grand Master") [Yoda](/wiki/Yoda "Yoda") stressed that attachment led to jealousy, greed, and ultimately the fear of loss, all of which were paths to the dark side.[[8]](#cite_note-Episode_III-8) [Ahsoka Tano](/wiki/Ahsoka_Tano "Ahsoka Tano") also regarded attachment as dangerous, and rejected [Grogu](/wiki/Din_Grogu "Din Grogu") as an apprentice due to the [foundling](/wiki/Foundling "Foundling")'s bond with the [Mandalorian](/wiki/Mandalorian "Mandalorian") [bounty hunter](/wiki/Bounty_hunter "Bounty hunter") [Din Djarin](/wiki/Din_Djarin "Din Djarin").[[12]](#cite_note-Chapter_13-12)

Notwithstanding the strictures against attachment, the Code emphasized compassion as an essential aspect to the Jedi way of life, teaching that all lives were precious, though outsider misunderstandings of this were not unusual.[[9]](#cite_note-Episode_II-9)[[13]](#cite_note-Disciple-13) While Jedi did not [marry](/wiki/Marriage "Marriage"),[[14]](#cite_note-AnakinSWE-14) the Code banned neither romantic feelings[[15]](#cite_note-Rise_of_Clovis-15) nor familial love.[[13]](#cite_note-Disciple-13)[[16]](#cite_note-The_Unknown-16) Nevertheless, giving into personal attachment was tantamount to forsaking the way of the Jedi,[[17]](#cite_note-BOBF_6-17) the teachings of which had little to do with typical family relationships.[[18]](#cite_note-Shadow_of_the_Sith-18)

### Mantra[]

 [![The members of the Jedi Order were raised to uphold the tenets of the Jedi Code since the earliest days of their training.](data:image/gif;base64,R0lGODlhAQABAIABAAAAAP///yH5BAEAAAEALAAAAAABAAEAQAICTAEAOw%3D%3D)](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/starwars/images/c/c2/YoungKenobiInClass-AORObiWan1.jpg/revision/latest?cb=20190414005435) [![The members of the Jedi Order were raised to uphold the tenets of the Jedi Code since the earliest days of their training.](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/starwars/images/c/c2/YoungKenobiInClass-AORObiWan1.jpg/revision/latest/scale-to-width-down/200?cb=20190414005435)](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/starwars/images/c/c2/YoungKenobiInClass-AORObiWan1.jpg/revision/latest?cb=20190414005435) 

The members of the Jedi Order were raised to uphold the tenets of the Jedi Code since the earliest days of their training.

 

A Jedi such as [Obi-Wan Kenobi](/wiki/Obi-Wan_Kenobi "Obi-Wan Kenobi"), trained in the ways of the [light side of the Force](/wiki/Light_side_of_the_Force "Light side of the Force"), could take comfort in the words of the mantra of the Jedi Code:

:   *There is no emotion, there is peace.*
:   *There is no ignorance, there is knowledge.*
:   *There is no passion, there is serenity.*
:   *There is no chaos, there is harmony.*
:   *There is no death, there is the Force.*[[13]](#cite_note-Disciple-13)

There also existed an alternate version of the Code, recited by [Jedi younglings](/wiki/Jedi_Initiate "Jedi Initiate") during their [Initiate Trials](/wiki/Initiate_Trials "Initiate Trials"), and by [Depa Billaba](/wiki/Depa_Billaba "Depa Billaba") during her full fitness re-assessment after waking up from her 6-month coma:

:   *Emotion, yet peace.*
:   *Ignorance, yet knowledge.*
:   *Passion, yet serenity.*
:   *Chaos, yet harmony.*
:   *Death, yet the Force.*[[19]](#cite_note-Kanan_7-19)

A further version of the Code, considered old[[20]](#cite_note-Vengeance_Path-20) by the year [382 BBY](/wiki/382_BBY "382 BBY"),[[21]](#cite_note-POV_Date-21) made note of how a Jedi acted only for knowledge and enlightenment, instead of looking for selfish desires like personal power or profit. While Jedi Master [Vildar Mac](/wiki/Vildar_Mac "Vildar Mac") and Jedi Knight [Oliviah Zeveron](/wiki/Oliviah_Zeveron "Oliviah Zeveron") knew the alternate Jedi mantra by heart, Padawan [Matthea Cathley](/wiki/Matthea_Cathley "Matthea Cathley") only knew it from history crystals. Despite its older status, the facts contained within the alternate mantra were still considered true for how a Jedi must act. The older mantra included the lines:

:   *A Jedi does not act for personal power or wealth*
:   *But only seeks knowledge and enlightenment.*[[20]](#cite_note-Vengeance_Path-20)

### Following the Code[]

#### Conquering the dark side[]

:   "*Part of me you are, yes. But power over me you have not. Through patience and training, it is I who control you.*"
:   ―Yoda, to the shadow of his soul[[22]](#cite_note-Destiny-22)

Jedi were taught to accept the inherent dark side within themselves and conquer it, and not let it conquer them. Fear of loss, anger, hate, jealousy, greed, and aggression—all of the dark side—had to be stripped from its influence over a Jedi through patience and training.[[22]](#cite_note-Destiny-22)

#### Love and attachments[]

:   "*Are you allowed to love? I thought that was forbidden for a Jedi.*"  
    "*Attachment is forbidden. Possession is forbidden. Compassion, which I would define as unconditional love, is central to a Jedi's life. So you might say that we are encouraged to love.*"
:   ―Padmé Amidala and Anakin Skywalker[[9]](#cite_note-Episode_II-9)

 [![Romantic love and attachment were antithetical to the Jedi way of life.](data:image/gif;base64,R0lGODlhAQABAIABAAAAAP///yH5BAEAAAEALAAAAAABAAEAQAICTAEAOw%3D%3D)](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/starwars/images/9/96/Anakin_contacts_Padme.png/revision/latest?cb=20210623025715) [![Romantic love and attachment were antithetical to the Jedi way of life.](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/starwars/images/9/96/Anakin_contacts_Padme.png/revision/latest/scale-to-width-down/250?cb=20210623025715)](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/starwars/images/9/96/Anakin_contacts_Padme.png/revision/latest?cb=20210623025715) 

Romantic love and attachment were antithetical to the Jedi way of life.

 

While the Jedi Code forbade possession and attachments, the Jedi were encouraged and trained to love in terms of compassion.[[9]](#cite_note-Episode_II-9) Indeed, Jedi Master [Zallah Macri](/wiki/Zallah_Macri "Zallah Macri"), while explained the code to her apprentice [Kevmo Zink](/wiki/Kevmo_Zink "Kevmo Zink"), simply noted that a Jedi was "allowed to like people and things," but they could not allow those attachments to consume them.[[23]](#cite_note-POD-23) Jedi Knight [Indeera Stokes](/wiki/Indeera_Stokes "Indeera Stokes") stated that love, experiencing and embracing joy, affection and even grief was part of the light side, however, a Jedi couldn't be a slave of these emotions.[[24]](#cite_note-The_High_Republic:_The_Rising_Storm-24)

Attachment was the inability to accept change as the fundamental characteristic of life; to accept death as the natural part of life; the inability to let go. Feeding into fear of loss and greed, leading to jealousy, attachment was selfish, a shadow of greed and thus a path of the dark side of the Force. Therefore, attachment was forbidden for a Jedi, who had to train themselves to let go of everything they were afraid to lose; to renounce all attachments. Thus, they could be compassionate and loving and caring, but not be possessive and grabbing and holding on to things, trying to keep them frozen in time, accepting the transitional nature of life. This allowed them to love the totality of life unconditionally without selectively choosing individual life-forms to become selfishly attached to.[[8]](#cite_note-Episode_III-8)[[25]](#cite_note-Archives_I-III-25) An example of a Jedi whose attachments led to the dark side was Anakin Skywalker, who became a Sith in service to Sidious in his obsessive quest to get the power to save his wife from death.[[8]](#cite_note-Episode_III-8)

#### Personal relationships[]

:   "*Falling in love—that's what the Jedi Code forbids. Getting laid? Not so much. Not if it's casual, like me and Selbie.*"
:   ―Rael Averross, to Qui-Gon Jinn, defending casual sexual relationships[[26]](#cite_note-Master_&_Apprentice-26)

 [![The bond between a master and an apprentice could be akin to that of a parent and a child.](data:image/gif;base64,R0lGODlhAQABAIABAAAAAP///yH5BAEAAAEALAAAAAABAAEAQAICTAEAOw%3D%3D)](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/starwars/images/b/bb/AnakinAndKenobiMeditating-AORObiWan1.jpg/revision/latest?cb=20190414010609) [![The bond between a master and an apprentice could be akin to that of a parent and a child.](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/starwars/images/b/bb/AnakinAndKenobiMeditating-AORObiWan1.jpg/revision/latest/scale-to-width-down/250?cb=20190414010609)](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/starwars/images/b/bb/AnakinAndKenobiMeditating-AORObiWan1.jpg/revision/latest?cb=20190414010609) 

The bond between a master and an apprentice could be akin to that of a parent and a child.

 

Members of the Jedi Order considered each other their family and sometimes were truly related by blood.[[16]](#cite_note-The_Unknown-16)[[27]](#cite_note-Gathering-27)[[28]](#cite_note-Episode_VIII-28) Jedi Masters developed strong, trusting and loving bonds with their apprentices whom they raised, being like a [parent](/wiki/Parent "Parent") to them.[[9]](#cite_note-Episode_II-9) However, they were not supposed to form attachments; for the greater good, they had to be able to let go of their apprentice, and to not sacrifice a thousand lives just to save one.[[29]](#cite_note-Jedi_Crash-29) The Jedi considered romantic feelings "natural" and as such, they did not outright prohibit them. According to Jedi Master Obi Wan Kenobi, however, they were not supposed to enter into a romantic relationship—it was essential for a Jedi Knight to make the right choice for the Order and not neglect their Jedi duties in the favor of their beloved, even if that would mean the end of the relationship.[[30]](#cite_note-Voyage-30)[[31]](#cite_note-Rise_Clovis-31)

Jedi Knight [Rael Averross](/wiki/Rael_Averross "Rael Averross") believed that the Jedi code permitted casual sexual relationships as long as the Jedi did not form attachments. Averross had such a relationship with [Selbie](/wiki/Selbie "Selbie"), an innkeeper on [Pijal](/wiki/Pijal "Pijal"), when he served as [Lord Regent](/wiki/Regent "Regent") of that planet. His friend and fellow Jedi [Qui-Gon Jinn](/wiki/Qui-Gon_Jinn "Qui-Gon Jinn") did not agree with this belief.[[26]](#cite_note-Master_&_Apprentice-26)

#### Emotions and serenity[]

:   "*How do I know the good side from the bad?*"  
    "*You will know when you are calm, at peace, passive. A Jedi uses the Force for knowledge and defense, never for attack.*"
:   ―Luke Skywalker and Yoda[[32]](#cite_note-Episode_V-32)

The Jedi were encouraged to rely on their instincts over their mind.[[11]](#cite_note-Episode_I-11) They held their emotions valuable[[33]](#cite_note-Kanan_1-33)[[34]](#cite_note-Episode_VI-34) but were also warned to be mindful of them, for they could cloud their judgment. A Jedi had to maintain a serene, quiet mind in order to stand on the light side instead of the dark—thus, they were able to keep the Force within them in balance.[[32]](#cite_note-Episode_V-32)

#### Peace with death[]

:   "*Master Yoda, you can't die.*"  
    "*Strong am I with the Force, but not that strong. Twilight is upon me, and soon night must fall. That is the way of things…the way of the Force.*"
:   ―Luke Skywalker and Yoda[[34]](#cite_note-Episode_VI-34)

 [![The Jedi Order required its members to overcome the fear of loss, as it could lead to the dark side of the Force.](data:image/gif;base64,R0lGODlhAQABAIABAAAAAP///yH5BAEAAAEALAAAAAABAAEAQAICTAEAOw%3D%3D)](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/starwars/images/9/9d/YodaCouncilsAnakin-ROTS.png/revision/latest?cb=20130202024305) [![The Jedi Order required its members to overcome the fear of loss, as it could lead to the dark side of the Force.](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/starwars/images/9/9d/YodaCouncilsAnakin-ROTS.png/revision/latest/scale-to-width-down/250?cb=20130202024305)](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/starwars/images/9/9d/YodaCouncilsAnakin-ROTS.png/revision/latest?cb=20130202024305) 

The Jedi Order required its members to overcome the fear of loss, as it could lead to the dark side of the Force.

 

The Jedi knew that the universe was far from static, and the way of the Force was that all life forms eventually must die. They had a strong faith in the Force and found comfort in knowing that upon dying, they, just like all things that ever lived, would be transformed into the [Cosmic Force](/wiki/Cosmic_Force "Cosmic Force"),[[35]](#cite_note-Too_Much-35) the wellspring from which the Living Force emanated,[[36]](#cite_note-Voices-36) becoming one with it.[[37]](#cite_note-Weapons_Factory-37) Therefore, they saw death as a natural part of life;[[8]](#cite_note-Episode_III-8) despite being saddened by it, they were advised to remember that one day they would all pass on,[[35]](#cite_note-Too_Much-35) and rejoice and celebrate those around them who passed away and become one with the Force, instead of grieving and missing them. The fear of losing the living to inevitable death was attachment, the shadow of greed.[[8]](#cite_note-Episode_III-8)

## History[]

:   "*Each member of the Jedi Order—from the youngest initiate to the wisest master—was devoted to following the Jedi Code: There is no emotion, there is peace. There is no ignorance, there is knowledge. There is no passion, there is serenity. There is no chaos, there is harmony. There is no death, there is the Force.*"
:   ―Luke Skywalker[[4]](#cite_note-Secrets_of_the_Jedi-4)

The evolution of the Jedi Code spanned [centuries](/wiki/Standard_century "Standard century")[[3]](#cite_note-TSWB-3) and was driven by the Jedi Order's goal of remaining true to the light side of the Force by rejecting all paths to the dark side.[[38]](#cite_note-The_Force-38) The so-called "[Prime Jedi](/wiki/Prime_Jedi "Prime Jedi")," the founder and first member of the [Jedi Order](/wiki/Jedi_Order "Jedi Order"),[[39]](#cite_note-TLJ-TVD-39) followed an early version of the Jedi Code[[40]](#cite_note-Art_Wars-40) as the first Jedi to ever use the Force,[[41]](#cite_note-SWCE_2023-41) thereby standing in history as the first figure to use the Force under an early Jedi Code.[[40]](#cite_note-Art_Wars-40) The modern Jedi Code was believed to have been written by Jedi Master [Odan-Urr](/wiki/Odan-Urr "Odan-Urr").[[1]](#cite_note-Nexus_of_Power-1) Believing that using the Force was dangerous to the overall [universe](/wiki/Universe "Universe") and having fallen in love with the [Padawan](/wiki/Padawan "Padawan") Kevmo Zink, [Marda Ro](/wiki/Marda_Ro "Marda Ro") of the [Path of the Open Hand](/wiki/Path_of_the_Open_Hand "Path of the Open Hand") believed she would be able to convince Zink to reject the ways of the Jedi so he could be with her and join the Path. Ultimately, however, Zink was killed by the [Great Leveler](/wiki/Great_Leveler "Great Leveler"), but Ro blamed the Jedi for his death by claiming they had brainwashed him into the wrong ideology.[[23]](#cite_note-POD-23)

 [![The Jedi High Council upheld the Jedi Code by denying Qui-Gon Jinn's request to train Anakin Skywalker while Obi-Wan Kenobi was still his Padawan.](data:image/gif;base64,R0lGODlhAQABAIABAAAAAP///yH5BAEAAAEALAAAAAABAAEAQAICTAEAOw%3D%3D)](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/starwars/images/9/91/Young_Anakin_before_the_Council.jpg/revision/latest?cb=20151002173801) [![The Jedi High Council upheld the Jedi Code by denying Qui-Gon Jinn's request to train Anakin Skywalker while Obi-Wan Kenobi was still his Padawan.](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/starwars/images/9/91/Young_Anakin_before_the_Council.jpg/revision/latest/scale-to-width-down/250?cb=20151002173801)](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/starwars/images/9/91/Young_Anakin_before_the_Council.jpg/revision/latest?cb=20151002173801) 

The Jedi High Council upheld the Jedi Code by denying Qui-Gon Jinn's request to train Anakin Skywalker while Obi-Wan Kenobi was still his Padawan.

 

Although the Jedi Code required adherence from all members of the Jedi Order, Jedi Master Qui-Gon Jinn was better known for his efforts to follow the will of the Force than the tenets of the Code.[[4]](#cite_note-Secrets_of_the_Jedi-4) Jinn's willingness to defy the [Jedi High Council](/wiki/Jedi_High_Council "Jedi High Council") put him at odds with the Order's leadership, as well as his Padawan, Obi-Wan Kenobi, who believed that his Master would have served on the High Council if he had followed the Code. During the [final years](/wiki/Republic_Era "Republic Era") of the [Galactic Republic](/wiki/Galactic_Republic "Galactic Republic"), Jinn offered to train the [Chosen One](/wiki/Chosen_One "Chosen One") [Anakin Skywalker](/wiki/Anakin_Skywalker "Anakin Skywalker"), but the High Council rejected his proposal, citing the Code's restriction against training multiple Padawans. By the end of the [Invasion of Naboo](/wiki/Invasion_of_Naboo "Invasion of Naboo"), however, the High Council permitted Kenobi to train Skywalker in place of Jinn, who was killed by the [Sith apprentice](/wiki/Sith_apprentice "Sith apprentice") [Darth Maul](/wiki/Maul "Maul").[[11]](#cite_note-Episode_I-11)

[Iskat Aris](/wiki/Thirteenth_Sister "Thirteenth Sister"), who became a Jedi Knight upon the advent of the Clone Wars, struggled greatly with the Jedi Code, particularly the tenet that "there is no death, there is the Force." Observing other Jedi around her, she felt that this was a farce, merely empty words that they did not actually mean. At one point, when tasked with teaching the code to younglings, she found herself unconsciously reciting portions of the code backwards, i.e. "There is no serenity, there is passion." She claimed this to be a test for the younglings, to see if they would point it out, but it in fact reflected her own feelings.[[42]](#cite_note-Red_Blade-42)

During the [Clone Wars](/wiki/Clone_Wars "Clone Wars"), [Republic officer](/wiki/Republic_officer "Republic officer") [Captain](/wiki/Captain "Captain") [Wilhuff Tarkin](/wiki/Wilhuff_Tarkin "Wilhuff Tarkin") believed that the Jedi Code prevented the Order from doing what was necessary to win the war against the [Confederacy of Independent Systems](/wiki/Confederacy_of_Independent_Systems "Confederacy of Independent Systems").[[43]](#cite_note-Counterattack-43) While [Jedi General](/wiki/Jedi_General "Jedi General") Obi-Wan Kenobi regarded the [officer](/wiki/Officer "Officer")'s views on the Code as simplistic, Tarkin felt that the Jedi should be relieved of their authority over the [Grand Army of the Republic](/wiki/Grand_Army_of_the_Republic "Grand Army of the Republic"), a notion that [Jedi Commander](/wiki/Jedi_Commander "Jedi Commander") Ahsoka Tano disagreed with.[[44]](#cite_note-Citadel_Rescue-44)

Shortly before the end of the [Clone](/wiki/Cloning "Cloning") Wars, Anakin Skywalker was tasked by Kenobi and the other members of the [Jedi High Council](/wiki/Jedi_High_Council "Jedi High Council") to [spy](/wiki/Spy "Spy") on his friend, [Supreme Chancellor](/wiki/Supreme_Chancellor "Supreme Chancellor") [Sheev Palpatine](/wiki/Darth_Sidious "Darth Sidious"), something which Skywalker felt was against the Jedi Code.[[8]](#cite_note-Episode_III-8)

 [![The Jedi Order was restored by Luke Skywalker, who adhered to the tenets of the Jedi Code.](data:image/gif;base64,R0lGODlhAQABAIABAAAAAP///yH5BAEAAAEALAAAAAABAAEAQAICTAEAOw%3D%3D)](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/starwars/images/e/e9/LukeTeachingJedi-TheRiseOfKyloRen3.jpg/revision/latest?cb=20250306232725) [![The Jedi Order was restored by Luke Skywalker, who adhered to the tenets of the Jedi Code.](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/starwars/images/e/e9/LukeTeachingJedi-TheRiseOfKyloRen3.jpg/revision/latest/scale-to-width-down/250?cb=20250306232725)](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/starwars/images/e/e9/LukeTeachingJedi-TheRiseOfKyloRen3.jpg/revision/latest?cb=20250306232725) 

The Jedi Order was restored by Luke Skywalker, who adhered to the tenets of the Jedi Code.

 

As part of his efforts to rebuild the Jedi Order, Jedi Master [Luke Skywalker](/wiki/Luke_Skywalker "Luke Skywalker") trained his apprentices to practice the ways of the Jedi.[[18]](#cite_note-Shadow_of_the_Sith-18) Due to his unorthodox training as a Jedi, Skywalker sought to provide his disciples with a traditional path into the Order.[[4]](#cite_note-Secrets_of_the_Jedi-4) As a Jedi, Skywalker conformed to the tenets of the Jedi Code and expected the same devotion from his pupils, including his [nephew](/wiki/Skywalker_family "Skywalker family") [Ben Solo](/wiki/Ben_Solo "Ben Solo").[[18]](#cite_note-Shadow_of_the_Sith-18) Having devoted his life to the Jedi way, Skywalker abstained from romantic relationships,[[45]](#cite_note-Skywalker:_A_Family_at_War-45) eschewed past friendships, and worked hard to restore the Order. Adherence to Jedi tenets brought satisfaction to his life, although Skywalker also felt a degree of guilt over the length of time that he spent away from his friends, notwithstanding his commitment to the Jedi Code's stance towards personal connections.[[18]](#cite_note-Shadow_of_the_Sith-18)

In [34 ABY](/wiki/34_ABY "34 ABY"), [Rey](/wiki/Rey_Skywalker "Rey Skywalker") heard the soft chanting of the Jedi Code emanating from the [tree library](/wiki/Tree_library "Tree library") that contained the [original Jedi texts](/wiki/Sacred_Jedi_texts "Sacred Jedi texts") on [Ahch-To](/wiki/Ahch-To "Ahch-To"). The chanting compelled her to enter the library, where she found the [texts](/wiki/Book "Book") and began learning more about the history of the Jedi Order from Luke Skywalker.[[28]](#cite_note-Episode_VIII-28)

## Behind the scenes[]

:   "*I, Luke Skywalker, do swear on my honor, and on the faith of the brotherhood of knights, to use the Force only for good, denying, turning always from the Dark Side; to dedicate my life to the cause of freedom, and justice. If I should fail of this vow, my life shall be forfeit, here and hereafter.*"
:   ―Luke Skywalker's recitation of the "Jedi Oath"[[46]](#cite_note-SW:TAS-46)

The Jedi Code was first mentioned in the [1999](/wiki/1999 "1999") film [*Star Wars*: Episode I *The Phantom Menace*](/wiki/Star_Wars:_Episode_I_The_Phantom_Menace "Star Wars: Episode I The Phantom Menace").[[11]](#cite_note-Episode_I-11) However, the Code's concept harkens back to early drafts for the [1980](/wiki/1980 "1980") film [*Star Wars*: Episode V *The Empire Strikes Back*](/wiki/Star_Wars:_Episode_V_The_Empire_Strikes_Back "Star Wars: Episode V The Empire Strikes Back"), in which [Luke Skywalker](/wiki/Luke_Skywalker "Luke Skywalker") takes the "Jedi Oath" before traveling to [Cloud City](/wiki/Cloud_City "Cloud City") in order to save his friends.[[46]](#cite_note-SW:TAS-46)

The "There is no emotion, there is peace" version of the Code first appeared in the [1987](/wiki/1987 "1987") [Star Wars: The Roleplaying Game](/wiki/Star_Wars:_The_Roleplaying_Game_(first_edition) "Star Wars: The Roleplaying Game (first edition)"). It was there accompanied by a set of practical rules, more akin to a code of conduct, such as not acting for personal gain or trying as hard as possible to preserve life (while acknowledging that killing may at times be necessary).[[47]](#cite_note-Replaying_gamr-47) While the "mantra" part of the code lived on in later material, the code of conduct passed into obscurity. The "Emotion, yet peace" version of the mantra first showed up in the [roleplaying game](/wiki/Roleplaying_game "Roleplaying game") sourcebook *[Tales of the Jedi Companion](/wiki/Tales_of_the_Jedi_Companion "Tales of the Jedi Companion")* from 1996.[[48]](#cite_note-Tales-48)

In some [Legends](/wiki/Star_Wars_Legends "Star Wars Legends") materials "attachment" was used in terms of fondness, affection, love or loving commitment, stating that the Jedi were forbidding these emotional bonds.[[49]](#cite_note-TJP_IRL-49)[[50]](#cite_note-No_Prisoners-50)[[51]](#cite_note-Deceived-51) By contrast, [George Lucas](/wiki/George_Lucas "George Lucas") explained that the Jedi were trained, allowed and expected to love people, even their enemies, the Sith, but they were not supposed to form attachments, because attachment led to the dark side of the Force. When one possessed or had some other attachment to something—be it pleasure, a person or experience—one became afraid to lose it. The fear of loss fed into greed; an attached person was selfish and unable to let go. This turned into anger, which would lead to hate, and hate would lead to suffering, mostly on the part of the one who was selfish, because then one would spend their lives being afraid rather than actually living. But compassion was caring and giving and thus it was love, but the opposite of attachment; it was everlasting joy, devoid of fear of loss and the pain of loss. Lucas said, "As long as you love other people and treat them kindly, you won't be afraid."[[52]](#cite_note-TIME-52)[[53]](#cite_note-:0-53)

George Lucas, identifying himself as Buddhist Methodist or Methodist Buddhist, stated that his philosophy depicted in his movies was influenced by the fact that he was from San Francisco, the "Zen Buddhism capital of the United States."[[53]](#cite_note-:0-53) When author [Tom Veitch](/wiki/Tom_Veitch "Tom Veitch") asked Lucas for guidance about the spiritual aspect of the Jedi when developing the Legends comic series [*Tales of the Jedi*](/wiki/Star_Wars:_Tales_of_the_Jedi_(comic_series) "Star Wars: Tales of the Jedi (comic series)"), Lucas told him "Look to Buddha."[[54]](#cite_note-Star_Wars_Galaxy_Magazine_13-54) In 2020, he indicated that the Jedi were "designed to be a Buddhist monk who happened to be very good at fighting."[[25]](#cite_note-Archives_I-III-25)
