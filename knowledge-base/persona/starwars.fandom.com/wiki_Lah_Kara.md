**Lah Kara** was a [Force-sensitive](/wiki/Force-sensitive "Force-sensitive") [human](/wiki/Human "Human") [female](/wiki/Gender "Gender") who was the daughter of the legendary [sabersmith](/wiki/Sabersmith "Sabersmith") [Lah Zhima](/wiki/Lah_Zhima "Lah Zhima"). The two lived together in their [home](/wiki/Kara%27s_House "Kara's House") on the [planet](/wiki/Planet "Planet") [Hy Izlan](/wiki/Hy_Izlan "Hy Izlan"), and when [Margrave](/wiki/Margrave "Margrave") [Juro](/wiki/Juro "Juro") attempted to revive the [Jedi Order](/wiki/Jedi_Order "Jedi Order"), Zhima created several [lightsabers](/wiki/Lightsaber "Lightsaber") for him. After Zhima finished creating them, Kara practiced using [one](/wiki/Lah_Kara%27s_lightsaber "Lah Kara's lightsaber") of the lightsabers, which had their [kyber crystals](/wiki/Kyber_crystal "Kyber crystal") tempered so that their blade length and [color](/wiki/Color "Color") would reflect the user's connection to [the Force](/wiki/The_Force "The Force"). When [Jedi Hunters](/wiki/Jedi_hunter "Jedi hunter") arrived to [capture](/wiki/Plot_to_kidnap_the_Sabersmith "Plot to kidnap the Sabersmith") Zhima, he tasked Kara with delivering the lightsabers to the Jedi waiting for them in the [Aerial Temple](/wiki/Hy_Izlan_Aerial_Temple "Hy Izlan Aerial Temple") above Hy Izlan. [One](/wiki/Unidentified_Jedi_Hunter_(Lah_Kara) "Unidentified Jedi Hunter (Lah Kara)") of the Jedi Hunters pursued Kara as she escaped on her [speeder bike](/wiki/Speeder_bike "Speeder bike"), but she [deflected](/wiki/Lightsaber_combat "Lightsaber combat") his [blaster](/wiki/Blaster "Blaster") fire while riding backwards through a [forest](/wiki/Forest "Forest"). When they reached a frozen [lake](/wiki/Water "Water"), she sliced the hunter's [speeder](/wiki/Speeder "Speeder") and escaped to a station, where she hired a [Boatman droid](/wiki/Unidentified_boatman_droid "Unidentified boatman droid") to take her to the Aerial Temple.

Upon arriving at the temple, Kara gave the lightsabers to who she believed were the Jedi awaiting them. However, all but one—[Ethan](/wiki/Ethan "Ethan")—revealed themselves to be [acolytes](/wiki/Sith_acolyte "Sith acolyte") of the [Sith](/wiki/Sith "Sith"). The Sith surrounded Kara and Ethan, but Juro soon revealed himself. Several of the Sith [attacked](/wiki/Lightsaber_duel "Lightsaber duel") Juro, while Kara and Ethan drew their lightsabers to fight back as well. Juro managed to kill most of the Sith, but he was knocked back when the acolyte [Roden](/wiki/Roden "Roden") attacked with [Force lightning](/wiki/Force_lightning "Force lightning"). Kara then broke away from fighting [Homen](/wiki/Homen "Homen")—a Jedi consumed by the acolytes' [darkness](/wiki/Dark_side_of_the_Force "Dark side of the Force")—to attack Roden, allowing Juro to [push](/wiki/Telekinesis "Telekinesis") the Sith onto a massive kyber crystal, disintegrating him, after which Homen stood down. Kara then asked Juro to help [save](/wiki/Search_for_Lah_Zhima "Search for Lah Zhima") her father, which he agreed to before inviting Kara to aid him in restoring the Jedi Order and join him as the ninth Jedi.

Around six [months](/wiki/Standard_month "Standard month") later, Kara and the other Jedi were aboard a [starship](/wiki/Unidentified_Jedi_starship "Unidentified Jedi starship") while being pursued by Jedi Hunters. Their ship was struck by [proton torpedos](/wiki/Proton_torpedo "Proton torpedo"), and Kara was blasted into [space](/wiki/Realspace "Realspace") and eventually drifted near an [abandoned ship](/wiki/Unidentified_abandoned_ship "Unidentified abandoned ship"). She was brought aboard by the [service droid](/wiki/Service_droid "Service droid") [Teto](/wiki/Teto "Teto"), who asked Kara to help rescue his [master](/wiki/Teto%27s_master "Teto's master"). Kara was eventually able to [use](/wiki/Force_power "Force power") the Force to move a fallen statue blocking off the room where he had been recovering in a [bacta tank](/wiki/Bacta_tank "Bacta tank"), but then learned his [life support](/wiki/Life_support "Life support") systems had already failed. They later investigated a noise from the ship's [hangar](/wiki/Hangar "Hangar"), where they found the same Jedi Hunters who had pursued Kara earlier. Kara lured them toward the ship's automated [sentry guns](/wiki/Sentry_gun "Sentry gun"), which defeated the hunter's subordinates, but was forced to retreat with Teto after dueling their leader [Unda](/wiki/Unda "Unda") and his [lieutenant](/wiki/Lieutenant "Lieutenant"), [Anda](/wiki/Anda "Anda"). Teto pushed Kara into an [escape pod](/wiki/Escape_pod "Escape pod") and jettisoned it despite her protests, after which he activated the ship's [self-destruct mechanism](/wiki/Self-destruct_mechanism "Self-destruct mechanism"). The other Jedi soon returned for Kara and brought her aboard their ship.

### Daughter of the sabersmith[]

Lah Kara was a [Force-sensitive](/wiki/Force-sensitive "Force-sensitive") [human](/wiki/Human "Human") [female](/wiki/Gender "Gender") who lived with her [father](/wiki/Parent "Parent"),[[2]](#cite_note-Ninth_Jedi-2) the legendary[[3]](#cite_note-Meet_the_Heroes-3) [sabersmith](/wiki/Sabersmith "Sabersmith") [Lah Zhima](/wiki/Lah_Zhima "Lah Zhima"), on[[2]](#cite_note-Ninth_Jedi-2) her [home planet](/wiki/Homeworld "Homeworld")[[1]](#cite_note-Why_We_Love-1) [Hy Izlan](/wiki/Hy_Izlan "Hy Izlan") during an [era of war](/wiki/Era_of_war "Era of war")[[2]](#cite_note-Ninth_Jedi-2) long after [35 ABY](/wiki/35_ABY "35 ABY").[[4]](#cite_note-Visions_Date-4) The two were familiar with Hy Izlan's ruler, [Margrave](/wiki/Margrave "Margrave") [Juro](/wiki/Juro "Juro"), with Kara having known him since she was a baby.[[2]](#cite_note-Ninth_Jedi-2) At one point as a [child](/wiki/Youngling "Youngling"), Kara tried [using](/wiki/Force_power "Force power") [the Force](/wiki/The_Force "The Force") to prevent[[5]](#cite_note-Child_of_Hope-5) a [stone](/wiki/Stone "Stone") idol from falling off a rock.[[6]](#cite_note-Child_of_Hope_AD-6) She was unable to do so, and fell backward onto the ground. Zhima, who was working nearby, called out encouragement to her, but she still believed she would not be able to succeed. Zhima hiked over to her and encouraged her to believe in herself, and Kara agreed to try again. At another point, Kara took history lessons, during which she leaned of[[5]](#cite_note-Child_of_Hope-5) the planet[[7]](#cite_note-Child_of_Hope_Focus-7) [Kaamui](/wiki/Kaamui "Kaamui") and a [war](/wiki/Unidentified_war "Unidentified war") that had destroyed it hundreds of [years](/wiki/Standard_year "Standard year") earlier.[[5]](#cite_note-Child_of_Hope-5)

Juro, also a [Jedi Master](/wiki/Jedi_Master "Jedi Master"), sought to restore the [Jedi Order](/wiki/Jedi_Order "Jedi Order") and began [mining](/wiki/Mining "Mining") for [kyber crystals](/wiki/Kyber_crystal "Kyber crystal") within his territory so that [lightsabers](/wiki/Lightsaber "Lightsaber"), the ancient [weapons](/wiki/Weapon "Weapon") of the [Jedi](/wiki/Jedi "Jedi"), could be forged. While Zhima worked on completing the lightsabers for Juro, Kara watched from a balcony as a line of [harpoon cannons](/wiki/Harpoon_cannon "Harpoon cannon") flung hooks[[2]](#cite_note-Ninth_Jedi-2) into [orbit](/wiki/Orbit "Orbit")[[8]](#cite_note-Ninth_Jedi_AD-8) to mine kyber [crystals](/wiki/Crystal "Crystal") from [asteroids](/wiki/Asteroid "Asteroid").[[3]](#cite_note-Meet_the_Heroes-3) When a hook crashed back down to the [planet](/wiki/Planet "Planet")'s surface, Kara used a rope to jump down to the ground, then got on a [speeder bike](/wiki/Speeder_bike "Speeder bike") with her droid [99-99](/wiki/99-99 "99-99") and headed toward where the hook had landed.[[2]](#cite_note-Ninth_Jedi-2)

 [![Kara and Zhima](data:image/gif;base64,R0lGODlhAQABAIABAAAAAP///yH5BAEAAAEALAAAAAABAAEAQAICTAEAOw%3D%3D)](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/starwars/images/4/41/KaraZhimaLightsaber-TheNinthJedi.png/revision/latest?cb=20250815001204) [![Kara and Zhima](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/starwars/images/4/41/KaraZhimaLightsaber-TheNinthJedi.png/revision/latest/scale-to-width-down/325?cb=20250815001204)](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/starwars/images/4/41/KaraZhimaLightsaber-TheNinthJedi.png/revision/latest?cb=20250815001204) 

Kara and Zhima

 

Kara later returned to her [house](/wiki/Kara%27s_House "Kara's House"), and after entering, removed her [helmet](/wiki/Helmet "Helmet") and [coat](/wiki/Jacket "Jacket") while greeting her father. She ran over to where Zhima was working and asked if he had finished the lightsabers, which he confirmed. Zhima then handed his daughter [one](/wiki/Lah_Kara%27s_lightsaber "Lah Kara's lightsaber") of the lightsabers, and she activated it to produce a long, clear blade. 99-99 fired several [blaster](/wiki/Blaster "Blaster") bolts at Kara, which she deflected. Kara then noticed she could change the length of the lightsaber's blade and experimented with altering its size while Zhima informed her that he had tempered the kyber crystals used in the lightsabers he had created so that their blade length and [color](/wiki/Color "Color") would vary depending on the user's connection to [the Force](/wiki/The_Force "The Force"). Kara lamented that the blade's lack of color indicated her connection was not strong enough, but Zhima told her she had a natural talent with a lightsaber and explained the importance of growing her connection with proper training. Kara wondered what color the blade would turn when used by a real Jedi and resumed practicing with the lightsaber.[[2]](#cite_note-Ninth_Jedi-2)

### Speeder bike escape[]

:   "*Old droid! Please. I need for you to take us to the Aerial Temple immediately!*"
:   ―Kara, to the Boatman droid[[2]](#cite_note-Ninth_Jedi-2)

 [![Kara fleeing the Jedi Hunters on her speeder bike](data:image/gif;base64,R0lGODlhAQABAIABAAAAAP///yH5BAEAAAEALAAAAAABAAEAQAICTAEAOw%3D%3D)](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/starwars/images/3/30/KaraSpeederBike-TheNinthJedi.jpg/revision/latest?cb=20250815001507) [![Kara fleeing the Jedi Hunters on her speeder bike](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/starwars/images/3/30/KaraSpeederBike-TheNinthJedi.jpg/revision/latest/scale-to-width-down/350?cb=20250815001507)](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/starwars/images/3/30/KaraSpeederBike-TheNinthJedi.jpg/revision/latest?cb=20250815001507) 

Kara fleeing the Jedi Hunters on her speeder bike

 

When a [starship](/wiki/Starship "Starship") passed overhead and landed nearby, Kara asked if it was the Jedi. Zhima responded it was unlikely, as they had been called to the [Aerial Temple](/wiki/Hy_Izlan_Aerial_Temple "Hy Izlan Aerial Temple") above Hy Izlan, and gathered the lightsabers, handing them to Kara and instructing her to take them to the temple. As two [Jedi Hunters](/wiki/Jedi_hunter "Jedi hunter") approached their home, Zhima hugged Kara, who commented that he was acting as if they would never see each other again. Kara rode away on her speeder bike with 99-99 as the Jedi Hunters [confronted](/wiki/Plot_to_kidnap_the_Sabersmith "Plot to kidnap the Sabersmith") Zhima, but before she got far, she heard an explosion from their home and turned to see her father being captured by the hunters. As one of the hunters,[[2]](#cite_note-Ninth_Jedi-2) [Bee-Toh](/wiki/Bee-Toh "Bee-Toh"),[[5]](#cite_note-Child_of_Hope-5) fired at her with a blaster, Zhima told her to escape. Kara did so, and was pursued by the other [hunter](/wiki/Unidentified_Jedi_Hunter_(Lah_Kara) "Unidentified Jedi Hunter (Lah Kara)") on another speeder bike.[[2]](#cite_note-Ninth_Jedi-2)

As they sped across the tundra, the Jedi Hunter fired at Kara, but she avoided the shots. The pursuit continued into a [forest](/wiki/Forest "Forest"), and the hunter briefly stopped shooting. However,[[2]](#cite_note-Ninth_Jedi-2) he[[8]](#cite_note-Ninth_Jedi_AD-8) soon resumed firing, and Kara turned her speeder around, riding it backwards as she ignited her lightsaber and [deflected](/wiki/Lightsaber_combat "Lightsaber combat") the blaster bolts. As they exited the forest, Kara turned back around and drove over a frozen [lake](/wiki/Water "Water"). In the middle of the lake, she leapt off her speeder bike and began to run back toward the hunter, standing in his path. When he reached her, she used her lightsaber[[2]](#cite_note-Ninth_Jedi-2)—which by then had a faint and pale green glow[[8]](#cite_note-Ninth_Jedi_AD-8)—to slice the hunter's speeder bike, causing him to crash. She then got back on her bike, eventually arriving at a station near the lake.[[2]](#cite_note-Ninth_Jedi-2)

At the station, she asked a [Boatman droid](/wiki/Unidentified_boatman_droid "Unidentified boatman droid") to take her to the Aerial Temple. Although the [Boatman droid](/wiki/Boatman_droid "Boatman droid") was initially reluctant,[[2]](#cite_note-Ninth_Jedi-2) he[[9]](#cite_note-Art_of_Visions-9) eventually agreed to take Kara when she paid him with a [coin](/wiki/Coin "Coin"). The droid began slowly walking toward a starship, prompting Kara to urge him to hurry. Aboard the ship, Kara sat behind the droid with 99-99 in her lap and whimpered as the ship shook while traveling to the Aerial Temple. When she looked out the [viewport](/wiki/Viewport "Viewport"), she saw the Jedi Hunters' ship flying away from the planet.[[2]](#cite_note-Ninth_Jedi-2)

### Duel in the Aerial Temple[]

:   "*Something doesn't feel right. I don't think we have a choice.*"  
    "*That color…*"
:   ―Ethan and Kara[[2]](#cite_note-Ninth_Jedi-2)

 [![Kara's lightsaber turned green as she fought Homen.](data:image/gif;base64,R0lGODlhAQABAIABAAAAAP///yH5BAEAAAEALAAAAAABAAEAQAICTAEAOw%3D%3D)](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/starwars/images/c/cb/KaraHomenDuel-TheNinthJedi.png/revision/latest?cb=20250815001725) [![Kara's lightsaber turned green as she fought Homen.](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/starwars/images/c/cb/KaraHomenDuel-TheNinthJedi.png/revision/latest/scale-to-width-down/325?cb=20250815001725)](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/starwars/images/c/cb/KaraHomenDuel-TheNinthJedi.png/revision/latest?cb=20250815001725) 

Kara's lightsaber turned green as she fought Homen.

 

When the ship landed at the Aerial Temple, Kara thanked the Boatman droid and leapt out, where she was met by a group of individuals she believed to be Jedi waiting for the lightsabers. However, only one of them, [Ethan](/wiki/Ethan "Ethan"), was a Jedi, while the others—[Hanbei](/wiki/Hanbei "Hanbei"), [Roden](/wiki/Roden "Roden"), [Toguro](/wiki/Toguro "Toguro"), [Niizo](/wiki/Niizo "Niizo"), [Hen Jin](/wiki/Hen_Jin "Hen Jin"), and [Homen](/wiki/Homen "Homen")—were actually [Sith acolytes](/wiki/Sith_acolyte "Sith acolyte") who had taken the place of the Jedi invited by Juro. Kara introduced herself as the daughter of Lah Zhima, and after explaining the sabersmith had been taken by Jedi Hunters, asked the group to use the lightsabers she had brought to save him. When Kara then asked where Juro was and revealed she knew him, Hanbei inquired if that meant she knew where the margrave could be. The Sith surrounded Kara and Ethan and ignited their lightsabers, each of which turned red. A [steward droid](/wiki/Steward_droid "Steward droid") who had been waiting with the group then arrived, and Juro emerged from it, revealing he had secretly been with them as they waited.[[2]](#cite_note-Ninth_Jedi-2)

The Sith turned their attention to Juro, and Hanbei charged him but was swiftly [killed](/wiki/Death "Death") by the margave. Roden and Toguro then also [engaged](/wiki/Lightsaber_duel "Lightsaber duel") Juro, while the other Sith remained near Kara and Ethan, with Niizo telling Kara she would never see her father again. Ethan then ignited his own [lightsaber](/wiki/Ethan%27s_lightsaber "Ethan's lightsaber"), causing Kara to do so as well and attack the three Sith. Ethan was knocked to the ground, and as Kara continued to fight, she fell back to[[2]](#cite_note-Ninth_Jedi-2) the temple's reception hall,[[9]](#cite_note-Art_of_Visions-9) where Juro was still fighting Roden and Toguro. After Juro killed Toguro, Niizo and Hen Jin attacked the margrave as well, leaving Homen to fight Kara, who was soon joined by Ethan. As she fought, her blade turned a brighter green.[[2]](#cite_note-Ninth_Jedi-2)

Juro managed to defeat all the Sith attacking him except Roden, but he was overwhelmed by the Sith's [Force lightning](/wiki/Force_lightning "Force lightning") and knocked down. Kara broke away from Homen to assist Juro, and as she battled Roden, the margrave used the Force to [push](/wiki/Telekinesis "Telekinesis") the Sith onto a massive kyber crystal, which disintegrated him. Noticing Homen and Ethan still fighting, Juro commanded Homen to stop. Homen then extinguished his [lightsaber](/wiki/Homen%27s_lightsaber "Homen's lightsaber") and apologized, explaining he had been consumed by the [darkness](/wiki/Dark_side_of_the_Force "Dark side of the Force") of the others.[[2]](#cite_note-Ninth_Jedi-2)

Juro then turned to Kara and apologized to her for being unable protect her father, and agreed to Kara's request to help her [save](/wiki/Search_for_Lah_Zhima "Search for Lah Zhima") Zhima. Juro then told Kara and Ethan that there were still Jedi wandering [the galaxy](/wiki/The_galaxy "The galaxy"), and asked Kara to join them as the ninth Jedi as they worked to reestablish the Jedi Order. [Juro's ship](/wiki/Juro%27s_ship "Juro's ship") arrived outside the temple, and Kara and the other Jedi boarded it before it jumped to [hyperspace](/wiki/Hyperspace "Hyperspace").[[2]](#cite_note-Ninth_Jedi-2)

### Adrift in space[]

:   "*No, I'm still out here!*"
:   ―Lah Kara[[5]](#cite_note-Child_of_Hope-5)

 [![Kara operating the Jedi starship's cannon](data:image/gif;base64,R0lGODlhAQABAIABAAAAAP///yH5BAEAAAEALAAAAAABAAEAQAICTAEAOw%3D%3D)](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/starwars/images/5/5e/HelmetKara-ChildOfHope.jpg/revision/latest?cb=20251224192845) [![Kara operating the Jedi starship's cannon](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/starwars/images/5/5e/HelmetKara-ChildOfHope.jpg/revision/latest/scale-to-width-down/350?cb=20251224192845)](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/starwars/images/5/5e/HelmetKara-ChildOfHope.jpg/revision/latest?cb=20251224192845) 

Kara operating the Jedi starship's cannon

 

Around six [months](/wiki/Standard_month "Standard month") later,[[10]](#cite_note-Directors_Commentary-10) Kara and her allies were being pursued by a [Jedi Hunter ship](/wiki/Unidentified_Jedi_Hunter_ship "Unidentified Jedi Hunter ship") while aboard another [starship](/wiki/Unidentified_Jedi_starship "Unidentified Jedi starship"). As the Jedi ship attempted to evade their pursuers, Kara returned fire with its single [cannon](/wiki/Cannon "Cannon"). The Jedi Hunter ship struck the Jedi ship with[[5]](#cite_note-Child_of_Hope-5) a [proton torpedo](/wiki/Proton_torpedo "Proton torpedo"),[[6]](#cite_note-Child_of_Hope_AD-6) breaching its hull and sucking Kara into [space](/wiki/Realspace "Realspace"). Moments after she was sucked out, Juro engaged the [hyperdrive](/wiki/Hyperdrive "Hyperdrive"), and Kara,[[5]](#cite_note-Child_of_Hope-5) who was sheathed in a protective [force field](/wiki/Force_field "Force field"),[[6]](#cite_note-Child_of_Hope_AD-6) cried out as the vessel jumped to hyperspace. The Jedi Hunter ship then jumped as well, knocking Kara back further. While she drifted through space, Kara remembered when her father encouraged her as she trained and when he was taken by the Jedi Hunters.[[5]](#cite_note-Child_of_Hope-5)

Eventually, Kara drifted into an [asteroid field](/wiki/Asteroid_field "Asteroid field"), where she was noticed by the [service droid](/wiki/Service_droid "Service droid") [Teto](/wiki/Teto "Teto") aboard an [abandoned ship](/wiki/Unidentified_abandoned_ship "Unidentified abandoned ship"). Teto pulled her aboard the ship and then began dragging her down one of its corridors. When she awoke, she screamed and kicked Teto away from her, then ran away from[[5]](#cite_note-Child_of_Hope-5) him.[[10]](#cite_note-Directors_Commentary-10) Although Teto called after her, Kara continued running while turning around and exclaiming in fear. Kara arrived in a corridor filled with [trees](/wiki/Tree "Tree") and turned around to see Teto follow her. She took a step back, triggering a [laser](/wiki/Laser "Laser") tripwire [sensor](/wiki/Sensor "Sensor"), which caused [sentry guns](/wiki/Sentry_gun "Sentry gun") to fire on her. Kara ran evading the blaster fire, then pulled out her lightsaber and flipped around to deflect the blasts.[[5]](#cite_note-Child_of_Hope-5)

Kara defending herself from the sentry guns

 

Teto watched Kara defend herself from the sentry guns, and when they stopped firing, Teto bowed to her and introduced himself. He asked her to help his [master](/wiki/Teto%27s_master "Teto's master") and pulled her down the corridor to another room, which featured a lake surrounded by barren trees. Kara finally stopped Teto and asked him to explain himself. The droid told Kara that he and his master had escaped the war on Kaamui, but they had lost control of the ship during an attack along the way, after which Teto's master fell ill and began recovering in a [bacta tank](/wiki/Bacta_tank "Bacta tank"). During a subsequent attack, the room he was in had become blocked off from the rest of the ship. Kara recalled learning of Kaamui, and as they talked, Teto and Kara entered a mansion inside the room, where he guided her to a large fallen statue blocking to the entrance to the room Teto's master was in. Teto expressed his worry for his master, and Kara comforted him, assuring him she would help.[[5]](#cite_note-Child_of_Hope-5)

Although Ethan was worried about Kara, Juro assured him that the Force was strong with her. Kara and Teto used a pole as a lever to attempt to move the statue, but they were unable and fell down the stairs in front of it. Teto caught her as she fell, and she told him he would be able to see his master again.[[5]](#cite_note-Child_of_Hope-5)

Teto thanked Kara for allowing him to see his master and promised her he would being her back to her friends. Kara hugged the droid, and her [stomach](/wiki/Stomach "Stomach") growled, to which Teto told Kara he would cook for her.[[5]](#cite_note-Child_of_Hope-5)

### Lessons and soup[]

:   "*Only someone who is truly strong has the ability to reveal their weakness to others. This is a lesson that Master once taught Teto. Kara knows she is weaker right now, but she can become strong. And isn't understanding that important to becoming a Jedi?*"
:   ―Teto, to Lah Kara[[5]](#cite_note-Child_of_Hope-5)

 [![Kara eating Teto's soup](data:image/gif;base64,R0lGODlhAQABAIABAAAAAP///yH5BAEAAAEALAAAAAABAAEAQAICTAEAOw%3D%3D)](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/starwars/images/f/f6/TetoKaraSoup-ChildOfHope.png/revision/latest?cb=20251224195350) [![Kara eating Teto's soup](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/starwars/images/f/f6/TetoKaraSoup-ChildOfHope.png/revision/latest/scale-to-width-down/350?cb=20251224195350)](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/starwars/images/f/f6/TetoKaraSoup-ChildOfHope.png/revision/latest?cb=20251224195350) 

Kara eating Teto's soup

 

In another room, Kara sat on a sofa as Teto made[[5]](#cite_note-Child_of_Hope-5) [soup](/wiki/Soup "Soup")[[6]](#cite_note-Child_of_Hope_AD-6) for her. When he finished, Kara used a spoon to taste the soup. She praised the soup's taste, and Teto exclaimed excitedly as she drank the rest directly from the container. Teto reflected that his master had also complimented him, then asked Kara why people needed to fight wars. Kara responded by telling him that her father had told her that the [galaxy](/wiki/Galaxy "Galaxy") was engulfed in fear and anger, but as long as people helped those in need, they would be guiding the galaxy to a path of good. Teto told Kara her father sounded wonderful and asked her to become a great Jedi so she could bring peace to the galaxy. Kara told Teto she believed she would not be able to, recalling that she had been too weak to save her father when the Jedi Hunters kidnapped him. Remembering a lesson his master had taught him, Teto told Kara that only truly strong individuals could admit their weakness and assured her that she could become stronger. Considering his words, Kara enthusiastically promised that she would become a strong Jedi.[[5]](#cite_note-Child_of_Hope-5)

Later, Kara examined a [holographic](/wiki/Hologram "Hologram") [map](/wiki/Map "Map") and observed that the ship appeared to be stuck in an asteroid field. Teto confirmed her observation and added that it was far from any transport routes, meaning there were no nearby ships to call for help. Kara suggested using a sign that would be visible from far away, but before they could consider the idea further, the ship shook and they both headed to the ship's [hangar](/wiki/Hangar "Hangar") to investigate. There, they saw the Jedi Hunter ship and covertly observed as the hunters discussed the large [bounty](/wiki/Bounty "Bounty") on placed on a single Jedi. Teto wondered if they were looking for Kara, and she noted that they appeared similar to the Jedi Hunters who had taken her father. Teto then promised to fight for Kara, and she thanked him.[[5]](#cite_note-Child_of_Hope-5)

### Fighting the hunters[]

:   "*It's pointless to resist.*"  
    "*Tell me where my father is!*"
:   ―Anda and Lah Kara[[5]](#cite_note-Child_of_Hope-5)

 [![Kara fighting Unda (center) and Anda (right)](data:image/gif;base64,R0lGODlhAQABAIABAAAAAP///yH5BAEAAAEALAAAAAABAAEAQAICTAEAOw%3D%3D)](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/starwars/images/d/df/KaraVsUndaAnda-ChildOfHope.png/revision/latest?cb=20251224201426) [![Kara fighting Unda (center) and Anda (right)](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/starwars/images/d/df/KaraVsUndaAnda-ChildOfHope.png/revision/latest/scale-to-width-down/350?cb=20251224201426)](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/starwars/images/d/df/KaraVsUndaAnda-ChildOfHope.png/revision/latest?cb=20251224201426) 

Kara fighting Unda (center) and Anda (right)

 

Elsewhere on the ship, Kara hid as Jedi Hunter troops searched the area. Kara leapt out of hiding and lead them to the corridor with the sentry guns, where she raised her hands and pretended to surrender. One trooper stepped forward and triggered the sentry guns, which blasted all of the troopers. Teto head butted the final one, and he and Kara celebrated their victory. However, the leader of the Jedi Hunters, [Anda](/wiki/Anda "Anda"), arrived with[[5]](#cite_note-Child_of_Hope-5) his [lieutenant](/wiki/Lieutenant "Lieutenant"),[[5]](#cite_note-Child_of_Hope-5) [Unda](/wiki/Unda "Unda"), with one of the pair using [kunai](/wiki/Kunai "Kunai") to destroy the sentry guns. Anda asked them to surrender, and in response, Kara demanded to know where her father was. Unda was confused, but Anda surmised that Kara was the daughter of the man captured by Bee-Toh.[[5]](#cite_note-Child_of_Hope-5)

Igniting her lightsaber, Kara repeated her demand, while Unda removed his [hat](/wiki/Hat "Hat") and stepped forward. Kara told Teto to stay back, and Unda threw his hat, which Kara slashed in two before being blinded by [flash grenades](/wiki/Flash_grenade "Flash grenade") thrown behind it. Unda leapt at her, and she recovered in time to block his [electrostaff](/wiki/Electrostaff "Electrostaff"). They began dueling, then stepped apart and paused. Anda stepped forward to join Unda, and the duel resumed, with Kara dodging and parrying electrostaff attacks from both Jedi Hunters. After flipping backward, she avoided another attack by Unda, only to be struck by Anda and lose her lightsaber, which rolled near Teto. The droid picked up the weapon and ran to help, but was struck by Anda, who smashed his leg. Kara cried out, and Teto told Kara to close her eyes before throwing a flash grenade he had picked up. The two Jedi Hunters were disoriented, allowing Kara and Teto to hide beneath a tree.[[5]](#cite_note-Child_of_Hope-5)

 [![Kara begs Teto to let her out of the escape pod](data:image/gif;base64,R0lGODlhAQABAIABAAAAAP///yH5BAEAAAEALAAAAAABAAEAQAICTAEAOw%3D%3D)](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/starwars/images/e/e4/KaraTetoEscapePod-ChildOfHope.png/revision/latest?cb=20251224203514) [![Kara begs Teto to let her out of the escape pod](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/starwars/images/e/e4/KaraTetoEscapePod-ChildOfHope.png/revision/latest/scale-to-width-down/350?cb=20251224203514)](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/starwars/images/e/e4/KaraTetoEscapePod-ChildOfHope.png/revision/latest?cb=20251224203514) 

Kara begs Teto to let her out of the escape pod

 

Kara checked on Teto, who responded he was okay. He then told Kara he had an idea, and she followed him as they ran back to the mansion, where they moved a heavy slab blocking a door and entered through it. Teto showed Kara to a hatch in the floor, then knocked her inside an [escape pod](/wiki/Escape_pod "Escape pod") and shut the hatch. Teto informed Kara the escape pod would still function with his power, and despite her protests, refused to let her out. As Kara continued to protest, Teto launched the pod, soon after which Unda arrived and reported Kara's escape. Teto returned to his master's room and activated the ship's [self-destruct mechanism](/wiki/Self-destruct_mechanism "Self-destruct mechanism"). As it counted down, Teto spoke out loud that Kara would become a ray of hope for the galaxy.[[5]](#cite_note-Child_of_Hope-5)

From her escape pod, Kara watched as the abandoned ship exploded. Soon after, the Jedi ship arrived in the asteroid field and brought her aboard. Sitting by a window, Kara confirmed to Juro she had encountered Jedi-hunting bounty hunters, and when Ethan asked how she had fought them, she responded that Teto had rescued her. Juro decided to let her rest, and he and the others left her alone, with only 99-99 staying by her side. The droid chirped to get Kara's attention, and she knelt in front of him to hug him while sobbing. She promised to become a strong Jedi and rescue her father.[[5]](#cite_note-Child_of_Hope-5)

## Personality and traits[]

:   "*You've grown up so much, Kara.*"  
    "*You're all strange, like this is the last time we'll see each other.*"
:   ―Zhima and Kara[[2]](#cite_note-Ninth_Jedi-2)

Lah Kara was a human female with gray [eyes](/wiki/Eye "Eye"), light skin, and brown [hair](/wiki/Hair "Hair") that she worn in a ponytail.[[2]](#cite_note-Ninth_Jedi-2) She was in awe of her father and his important job, and she felt that she would eventually have what it took to learn the ways of the Jedi. Kara had a loving relationship with Zhima and the two were everything to each other. She longed to become a hero and leave her planet.[[3]](#cite_note-Meet_the_Heroes-3) Kara was determined to find Zhima after he was kidnapped, and she felt that she needed to become stronger to do so. She wanted to help Teto and was protective of him.[[5]](#cite_note-Child_of_Hope-5) Kara and Teto both drew strength and inspiration from each other when the abandoned ship was raided by Jedi Hunters.[[7]](#cite_note-Child_of_Hope_Focus-7) Kara was positive and optimistic, but struggled to open up to her allies about what she went through.[[11]](#cite_note-Lovecraftian_Monster-11)

## Powers and abilities[]

:   "*Don't worry. The Force is strong with her, even in this moment.*"  
    "*If that's so, might she be more powerful than you?*"  
    "*She may well be. Even if that's true, she has yet to learn how to control it or herself.*"
:   ―Juro and Homen[[5]](#cite_note-Child_of_Hope-5)

Lah Kara was strong in the Force and had natural talent with a lightsaber,[[2]](#cite_note-Ninth_Jedi-2) needing only training and focus to become a true Jedi.[[9]](#cite_note-Art_of_Visions-9) She was able to deflect blaster bolts with her lightsaber while piloting her speeder bike backwards, and she dueled multiple Sith acolytes simultaneously.[[2]](#cite_note-Ninth_Jedi-2) She was capable of defending herself from the sentry guns on the abandoned ship, but needed Teto's help to escape the Jedi Hunters.[[5]](#cite_note-Child_of_Hope-5)

## Equipment[]

:   "*Interesting. So you can change the length on this one?*"
:   ―Kara, on her lightsaber[[2]](#cite_note-Ninth_Jedi-2)

Lah Kara wore a pink[[2]](#cite_note-Ninth_Jedi-2) [kimono](/wiki/Kimono "Kimono")[[6]](#cite_note-Child_of_Hope_AD-6) with a red [sash](/wiki/Sash "Sash") and white ribbons over a [top](/wiki/Clothing "Clothing") with brown sleeves, brown shorts, and brown [boots](/wiki/Boot "Boot"). She also wore a white[[2]](#cite_note-Ninth_Jedi-2) [poncho](/wiki/Poncho "Poncho")[[3]](#cite_note-Meet_the_Heroes-3) with blue stripes that she removed upon returning to her home. While riding her speeder bike, she wore a helmet. Kara used a lightsaber with a kyber crystal tempered to reflect the Force connection of its wielding. The lightsaber's blade was initially clear, but gradually became green.[[2]](#cite_note-Ninth_Jedi-2) While firing the Jedi ship's cannon, she wore a white helmet and a device with a force field that protected her from the [vacuum](/wiki/Vacuum "Vacuum") of space. As a child, Kara wore a yellow kimono with a red sash, blue pants, and brown boots.[[5]](#cite_note-Child_of_Hope-5)

### Milestones[]

She longs for more, wishes to become a hero and get off her tundra planet where great harpoon cannons farm for kyber crystals from the asteroids above. What's new, though, is that Kara has a stable, loving relationship to her father. He's everything to her and she's everything to him.*"

Lah Kara first appeared in "[The Ninth Jedi](/wiki/The_Ninth_Jedi "The Ninth Jedi")," a short film that was written and directed by [Kenji Kamiyama](/wiki/Kenji_Kamiyama "Kenji Kamiyama"), produced by [Production IG](/wiki/Production_IG "Production IG"), and released as part of the [first volume](/wiki/Star_Wars:_Visions_Volume_1 "Star Wars: Visions Volume 1") of the non-[canon](/wiki/Canon "Canon") *[Star Wars: Visions](/wiki/Star_Wars:_Visions "Star Wars: Visions")* series[[2]](#cite_note-Ninth_Jedi-2) on [September 22](/wiki/September_22 "September 22"), [2021](/wiki/2021 "2021").[[3]](#cite_note-Meet_the_Heroes-3) Prior to the short's release, Kara was introduced in a video uploaded to the official *[Star Wars](/wiki/Star_Wars "Star Wars")* [YouTube](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/YouTube "wikipedia:YouTube") channel on [July 3](/wiki/July_3 "July 3"), 2021, which revealed her name written in the [Japanese](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Japanese_language "wikipedia:Japanese language") [katakana](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Katakana "wikipedia:Katakana") script.[[12]](#cite_note-Special_Look-12) The English spelling of Kara was later confirmed in a [StarWars.com](/wiki/StarWars.com "StarWars.com") article released on [September 13](/wiki/September_13 "September 13"), 2021,[[3]](#cite_note-Meet_the_Heroes-3) while the English spelling of her full name was not confirmed until the release of the episode itself, which included it in the credits.[[2]](#cite_note-Ninth_Jedi-2)

Kara is voiced by [Chinatsu Akasaki](/wiki/Chinatsu_Akasaki "Chinatsu Akasaki") in the original Japanese and [Kimiko Glenn](/wiki/Kimiko_Glenn "Kimiko Glenn") in the English dub.[[13]](#cite_note-Cast-13) When imagining Kara, Kamiyama though of her as bright and cheerful. He thought Akasaki's voice fit the role perfectly and complimented her performance.[[14]](#cite_note-Special_Program-14)

Kara also appeared in "[The Ninth Jedi: Child of Hope](/wiki/The_Ninth_Jedi:_Child_of_Hope "The Ninth Jedi: Child of Hope")," a sequel to "The Ninth Jedi" directed by [Naoyoshi Shiotani](/wiki/Naoyoshi_Shiotani "Naoyoshi Shiotani") and released on [October 29](/wiki/October_29 "October 29"), 2025, as part of the [third volume](/wiki/Star_Wars:_Visions_Volume_3 "Star Wars: Visions Volume 3") of *Visions*.[[15]](#cite_note-First_Look-15) On [April 20](/wiki/April_20 "April 20"), [2025](/wiki/2025 "2025"), at [Celebration Japan](/wiki/Celebration_Japan_(2025) "Celebration Japan (2025)"), it was announced that a spin-off series titled "[*Star Wars: Visions Presents* - The Ninth Jedi](/wiki/Star_Wars:_Visions_Presents_-_The_Ninth_Jedi "Star Wars: Visions Presents - The Ninth Jedi")" will also air in [2026](/wiki/2026 "2026").[[16]](#cite_note-Day_3-16) Kamiyama revealed that the series will be about Kara's journey to find her father.[[16]](#cite_note-Day_3-16) While working on "The Ninth Jedi," Kamiyama imagined what might have happened to Kara and other characters before and after its events. He believed viewers could sense there was more to the world of the short than what was shown, which allowed its story to be expanded further.[[10]](#cite_note-Directors_Commentary-10)

### Inspirations[]

:   "*Starting with Luke Skywalker,* Star Wars *has been about a young person swept away by fate into adventure and a larger universe. I wanted to keep this aspect in my story.*"
:   ―Kenji Kamiyama[[9]](#cite_note-Art_of_Visions-9)

 [![Concept art by Tetsuya Nishio](data:image/gif;base64,R0lGODlhAQABAIABAAAAAP///yH5BAEAAAEALAAAAAABAAEAQAICTAEAOw%3D%3D)](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/starwars/images/a/af/KaraConcept-Slide4.jpg/revision/latest?cb=20250430224214) [![Concept art by Tetsuya Nishio](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/starwars/images/a/af/KaraConcept-Slide4.jpg/revision/latest/scale-to-width-down/300?cb=20250430224214)](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/starwars/images/a/af/KaraConcept-Slide4.jpg/revision/latest?cb=20250430224214) 

Concept art by Tetsuya Nishio

 

Kara is intended to be a classic *Star Wars* character, similar to[[9]](#cite_note-Art_of_Visions-9) the Jedi[[17]](#cite_note-Encyclopedia-17) [Rey](/wiki/Rey_Skywalker "Rey Skywalker") and [Luke Skywalker](/wiki/Luke_Skywalker "Luke Skywalker"). Kamiyama felt that *Star Wars* has always been about a young person swept away into an adventure by fate and wanted to keep this aspect in his story.[[9]](#cite_note-Art_of_Visions-9) Like Luke, Kara started as a "nobody" and then set out on an adventure.[[10]](#cite_note-Directors_Commentary-10) Kara also had similar hopes and dreams to Luke and Rey, longing for more and hoping to become a hero. However, unlike them, she had a stable and loving relation ship with her father.[[3]](#cite_note-Meet_the_Heroes-3) Also like Luke and Rey, Kara had a droid companion in the form of 99-99.[[9]](#cite_note-Art_of_Visions-9)

Character designs for "The Ninth Jedi" were done by [Tetsuya Nishio](/wiki/Tetsuya_Nishio "Tetsuya Nishio"), who created concept art of Kara.[[9]](#cite_note-Art_of_Visions-9) Two concept art pieces were dated to piece to [February](/wiki/February "February") 2021[[12]](#cite_note-Special_Look-12)[[18]](#cite_note-Focus-18), with one of those pieces being dated to the [25th](/wiki/February_25 "February 25") of that month.[[18]](#cite_note-Focus-18) Kamiyama and Nishio wanted to created designs that would be attractive to Western viewers, and so looked at popular series such as [*Naruto Shippuden*](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Naruto_(TV_series) "wikipedia:Naruto (TV series)") to create a similar look.[[9]](#cite_note-Art_of_Visions-9) Another inspiration for Kara's design was[[3]](#cite_note-Meet_the_Heroes-3) the [rebel](/wiki/Alliance_to_Restore_the_Republic "Alliance to Restore the Republic") [princess](/wiki/Princess "Princess")[[17]](#cite_note-Encyclopedia-17) [Leia Organa](/wiki/Leia_Skywalker_Organa_Solo "Leia Skywalker Organa Solo"). Kamiyama and his team were inspired by the speeder chase in[[3]](#cite_note-Meet_the_Heroes-3) the [1983](/wiki/1983 "1983") [original trilogy](/wiki/Original_trilogy "Original trilogy") film[[19]](#cite_note-Film_Years-19) [*Star Wars*: Episode VI *Return of the Jedi*](/wiki/Star_Wars:_Episode_VI_Return_of_the_Jedi "Star Wars: Episode VI Return of the Jedi"), and Kara wore a helmet and poncho similar to the garb worn by Leia in the action sequence.[[3]](#cite_note-Meet_the_Heroes-3)

Additional concept art of Kara was created for "Child of Hope," with at least two pieces created by Shiotani.[[20]](#cite_note-Child_of_Hope_Guide_Slide_4-20)[[21]](#cite_note-Child_of_Hope_Guide_Slide_11-21) Another piece of concept art posted to the official *Star Wars* [Twitter](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Twitter "wikipedia:Twitter") account was dated to [July 24](/wiki/July_24 "July 24"), [2024](/wiki/2024 "2024").[[22]](#cite_note-Child_of_Hope_Twitter_Concept-22)

### Child of Hope[]

:   "*From this point on, I think Kara's story will involve more and more fighting. That means not only fun moments, but also facing sadness and pain. I strongly felt that she is really on the verge of facing some serious situations, so I thought it would be wonderful for this bittersweet story to weave into whatever comes next for Kara.*"
:   ―Naoyoshi Shiotani[[28]](#cite_note-Sequel_Directors-28)

 [!["The Ninth Jedi: Child of Hope" focused on Kara's growth and motivations.](data:image/gif;base64,R0lGODlhAQABAIABAAAAAP///yH5BAEAAAEALAAAAAABAAEAQAICTAEAOw%3D%3D)](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/starwars/images/e/ef/TheNinthJediPoster-VisionsS3Premiere.png/revision/latest?cb=20251103025127) [!["The Ninth Jedi: Child of Hope" focused on Kara's growth and motivations.](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/starwars/images/e/ef/TheNinthJediPoster-VisionsS3Premiere.png/revision/latest/scale-to-width-down/250?cb=20251103025127)](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/starwars/images/e/ef/TheNinthJediPoster-VisionsS3Premiere.png/revision/latest?cb=20251103025127) 

"The Ninth Jedi: Child of Hope" focused on Kara's growth and motivations.

 

For "The Ninth Jedi: Child of Hope," Shiotani wanted to create a short, growth story that Kara would remember for the rest of her life, as he felt that Kara's journey to find her father would be a grand story.[[15]](#cite_note-First_Look-15) Because of the obstacles Kara will face in future stories, Shiotani wanted the short to focus on awakening her determination, growth, and resolve,[[10]](#cite_note-Directors_Commentary-10) and to show her motivations. Additionally, he wanted to show Kara take action on her own, as he felt she lacked initiative in "The Ninth Jedi".[[29]](#cite_note-Animate_Times-29)

After watching "The Ninth Jedi," Shiotani felt that Kara appeared to be at the start of her growth. He said "Child of Hope" showed the moment that Kara decided to become a strong Jedi after going through the events of the two shorts, and that Teto was a key part in bringing out that element of the story. Shiotani believes that Kara's future story will involve more and more fighting, including both sad and fun moments. Because she will face serious situations, he wanted "Child of Hope" to have a bittersweet story.[[28]](#cite_note-Sequel_Directors-28) The color palette and lighting schemes in the short were designed to reflect the narrative, and Shiotani felt they were particular evocative in the scene when Kara and Teto parted ways. The lighting gradually shifted from orange to deep red as their farewell approached, and when Kara was pushed into the escape pod by Teto, the color of their worlds changed, with Kara being in artificial light and Teto in natural light.[[27]](#cite_note-Key_Tradition-27)

### Bond with Teto[]

:   "*So, both Teto and Kara carry their own burdens, and through that shared struggle, their emotional distance gets smaller and their bond starts to grow. As they help each other, their relationship evolves into an irreplaceable friendship, and through that bond, Kara takes a decisive step forward to become a stronger Jedi.*"
:   ―Naoyoshi Shiotani[[10]](#cite_note-Directors_Commentary-10)

 [![Concept art of Kara and Teto for "The Ninth Jedi: Child of Hope"](data:image/gif;base64,R0lGODlhAQABAIABAAAAAP///yH5BAEAAAEALAAAAAABAAEAQAICTAEAOw%3D%3D)](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/starwars/images/3/39/KaraAndTetoConcept-Twitter.jpg/revision/latest?cb=20251103213930) [![Concept art of Kara and Teto for "The Ninth Jedi: Child of Hope"](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/starwars/images/3/39/KaraAndTetoConcept-Twitter.jpg/revision/latest/scale-to-width-down/250?cb=20251103213930)](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/starwars/images/3/39/KaraAndTetoConcept-Twitter.jpg/revision/latest?cb=20251103213930) 

Concept art of Kara and Teto for "The Ninth Jedi: Child of Hope"

 

In "The Ninth Jedi," Kara was younger and taken care of by the other characters. Shiotani wanted to reverse this, which is how he started the relationship between Kara and Teto. His reactions were designed to make him look younger than Kara,[[30]](#cite_note-Instagram_Interview-30) and he imagined him as a "kid brother" figure for her.[[7]](#cite_note-Child_of_Hope_Focus-7) Shiotani said Kara carried a trauma from not knowing where her father was, which she bottled up in a child-like way because of her positive and optimistic character.[[11]](#cite_note-Lovecraftian_Monster-11)

Shiotani considered the characters Kara had relationships with in the previous short to be independent adults, while she always had someone to protect her, which he thought made it hard for her to open up and hesitate to speak when she was troubled due to her consideration for others. Because of this, Shiotani wanted to focus on the bond between Kara and Teto, who according to Shiotani, was the only one she was able to confide in. Both Kara and Teto carried burdens, and their shared struggle allowed their bond to grow into an irreplaceable friendship, through which Kara took a step to become a stronger Jedi.[[10]](#cite_note-Directors_Commentary-10) Additionally, Shiotani wanted to naturally convey Teto's relationship with his master through his conversations with Kara.[[10]](#cite_note-Directors_Commentary-10)
