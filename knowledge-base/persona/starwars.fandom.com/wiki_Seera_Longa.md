## Biography[]

After having recently been [knighted](/wiki/Knighting_Ceremony "Knighting Ceremony"), Longa fought with their fellow Jedi during the [Invasion of Syrinx Prime](/wiki/Invasion_of_Syrinx_Prime "Invasion of Syrinx Prime"), assisting [Jedi Master](/wiki/Jedi_Master "Jedi Master") [Adi Gallia](/wiki/Adi_Gallia "Adi Gallia") in uncovering a prison camp of [Vekurans](/wiki/Vekuran "Vekuran") on [Syrinx Prime](/wiki/Syrinx_Prime "Syrinx Prime"). Afterward, Longa helped sabotage the Syrinxian weapons forge to force the Vekurans and [Syrinxians](/wiki/Syrinxian "Syrinxian") into peace talks.[[2]](#cite_note-Jedi_Knights_1-2)

She later accompanied Master [Yaddle](/wiki/Yaddle "Yaddle") during the peace talks between the [Trade Federation](/wiki/Trade_Federation "Trade Federation") and the Gillanter Corporation, where they rescued former jedi [Dooku](/wiki/Dooku "Dooku") after he had been captured by [Wookiee](/wiki/Wookiee "Wookiee") gangsters led by [Lonkus](/wiki/Lonkus "Lonkus").[[3]](#cite_note-Jedi_Knights_4-3)

## Powers and abilities[]

Seera was a skilled lightsaber duelist able to easily deflect blaster bolts at close range. Aside from her skills as a Jedi she was also a competent slicer.[[3]](#cite_note-Jedi_Knights_4-3)

## Equipment[]

Longa carried a blue-bladed [lightsaber](/wiki/Lightsaber "Lightsaber").[[2]](#cite_note-Jedi_Knights_1-2)

## Behind the scenes[]

Seera Longa first appeared in the [first issue](/wiki/Jedi_Knights_1 "Jedi Knights 1") of the [2025](/wiki/2025 "2025") [comic](/wiki/Star_Wars_comic_books "Star Wars comic books") series *[Star Wars: Jedi Knights](/wiki/Star_Wars:_Jedi_Knights "Star Wars: Jedi Knights")*. The issue was written by [Marc Guggenheim](/wiki/Marc_Guggenheim "Marc Guggenheim"), illustrated by [Madibek Musabekov](/wiki/Madibek_Musabekov "Madibek Musabekov"), and published by [Marvel Comics](/wiki/Marvel_Comics "Marvel Comics")[[2]](#cite_note-Jedi_Knights_1-2) on [March 5](/wiki/March_5 "March 5"), 2025.[[4]](#cite_note-JK_1_Release-4) Longa was created by Guggenheim and Musabekov for the series.[[5]](#cite_note-Marc-5) The publisher's summary for [*Jedi Knights* 5](/wiki/Jedi_Knights_5 "Jedi Knights 5") incorrectly describes Longa as a Jedi Master,[[6]](#cite_note-Marvel5-6) instead of a knight.[[2]](#cite_note-Jedi_Knights_1-2)
