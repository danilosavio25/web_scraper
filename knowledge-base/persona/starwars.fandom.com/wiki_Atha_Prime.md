 [![Atha Prime stands before Grand Master Yoda of the Jedi Order.](data:image/gif;base64,R0lGODlhAQABAIABAAAAAP///yH5BAEAAAEALAAAAAABAAEAQAICTAEAOw%3D%3D)](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/starwars/images/d/d1/HesReal-JediKnights2.png/revision/latest?cb=20250702184144) [![Atha Prime stands before Grand Master Yoda of the Jedi Order.](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/starwars/images/d/d1/HesReal-JediKnights2.png/revision/latest/scale-to-width-down/350?cb=20250702184144)](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/starwars/images/d/d1/HesReal-JediKnights2.png/revision/latest?cb=20250702184144) 

Atha Prime stands before Grand Master Yoda of the Jedi Order.

 

At some point prior to 32 BBY, Atha Prime became the sole resident of the remote [moon](/wiki/Moon "Moon") [Veeros](/wiki/Veeros "Veeros"), building his own private [palace](/wiki/Veeros_palace "Veeros palace") on the moon which he used as a base to conduct a variety of [bioengineering](/wiki/Bioengineering "Bioengineering") experiments. These included his bio-field operation, a massive agricultural program that produced fields of bioengineered crops; these would become one of Atha Prime's more "renowned" public experiments. Secretly, however, in the lower levels of his palace, Atha Prime had created an army of bio-engineered creatures known as "[Xerexi](/wiki/Xerexi "Xerexi")" which he grew out of vats.[[1]](#cite_note-Jedi_Knights_2-1)

By 32 BBY, the [Jedi Order](/wiki/Jedi_Order "Jedi Order") had caught wind of a nefarious experiment being conducted on Veeros and thus [Grand Master](/wiki/Grand_Master "Grand Master") Yoda and Jedi Knight Vetna Mooncrest were dispatched to confront Prime. While Prime initially had his [droid](/wiki/Droid "Droid") [majordomo](/wiki/Majordomo "Majordomo") [Blue-Tark](/wiki/Blue-Tark "Blue-Tark") greet and distract the [Jedi](/wiki/Jedi "Jedi") with his grand bio-fields, Yoda and Vetna quickly discovered the Xerexi vats, which prompted Prime to unleash the creatures upon them while he watched from a vantage point. When Yoda noticed Prime spectating, he used the [the Force](/wiki/The_Force "The Force") to catch up to him, Prime fought the elderly Jedi, managing to pin him to the ground before Yoda used his [lightsaber](/wiki/Lightsaber "Lightsaber") to damage Prime's [helmet](/wiki/Helmet "Helmet"), prompting the bio-engineer to bash Yoda against a wall and retreat aboard [his starship](/wiki/Atha_Prime%27s_starship "Atha Prime's starship"). Afterwards, Yoda contacted [Mace Windu](/wiki/Mace_Windu "Mace Windu") and several other Jedi to exterminate the remaining Xerexi on Veeros, unaware that Atha Prime had dozens more stored aboard his ship when he fled.[[1]](#cite_note-Jedi_Knights_2-1)

## Skills and abilities[]

:   "*The thing about scalpels, Master Jedi, the deepest cut leaves the biggest scar.*"
:   ―Atha Prime to Yoda[[1]](#cite_note-Jedi_Knights_2-1)

Atha Prime was a skilled scientist, able to design and breed the [Xerexi](/wiki/Xerexi "Xerexi"). Despite being a scientist Prime was also a remarkable fighter, wielding his club with incredible speed and power, able to quickly defeat a legendary fighter like [Grand Master](/wiki/Grand_Master "Grand Master") [Yoda](/wiki/Yoda "Yoda") to the lengths of impaling the Grand Master.[[1]](#cite_note-Jedi_Knights_2-1)

Concept art for [Atha Prime](/wiki/Atha_Prime/Legends "Atha Prime/Legends")'s original *[The Epic Continues](/wiki/The_Epic_Continues "The Epic Continues")* version.

 

Atha Prime first appeared in the current *[Star Wars](/wiki/Star_Wars "Star Wars")* [canon](/wiki/Canon "Canon") in a vision in the [first issue](/wiki/Jedi_Knights_1 "Jedi Knights 1") of the [2025](/wiki/2025 "2025") [comic](/wiki/Star_Wars_comic_books "Star Wars comic books") series *[Star Wars: Jedi Knights](/wiki/Star_Wars:_Jedi_Knights "Star Wars: Jedi Knights")*. The issue was written by [Marc Guggenheim](/wiki/Marc_Guggenheim "Marc Guggenheim"), illustrated by [Madibek Musabekov](/wiki/Madibek_Musabekov "Madibek Musabekov"), and published by [Marvel Comics](/wiki/Marvel_Comics "Marvel Comics")[[4]](#cite_note-Jedi_Knights_1-4) on [March 5](/wiki/March_5 "March 5"), 2025.[[5]](#cite_note-Marvel-5) Atha Prime's entrance into canon was revealed at New York Comic Con 2024.[[6]](#cite_note-Wild_Deep_Cut-6)

Within the [*Star Wars* Legends](/wiki/Star_Wars_Legends "Star Wars Legends") continuity, [Atha Prime](/wiki/Atha_Prime/Legends "Atha Prime/Legends") originated from the unproduced *[The Epic Continues](/wiki/The_Epic_Continues "The Epic Continues")* project, an original toyline planned by [Kenner](/wiki/Kenner_Products "Kenner Products") to keep producing *Star Wars* action figures after the release of the [1983](/wiki/1983 "1983") film [*Star Wars*: Episode VI *Return of the Jedi*](/wiki/Star_Wars:_Episode_VI_Return_of_the_Jedi "Star Wars: Episode VI Return of the Jedi"), the last installment of the *Star Wars* [original trilogy](/wiki/Original_trilogy "Original trilogy"). With no more films on the horizon back then, Kenner developed a story where Prime would become [the galaxy's](/wiki/The_galaxy/Legends "The galaxy/Legends") biggest threat following [Emperor](/wiki/Emperor/Legends "Emperor/Legends") [Palpatine's](/wiki/Palpatine/Legends "Palpatine/Legends") [death](/wiki/Death/Legends "Death/Legends"). The character would proceed to engage not only [Luke Skywalker](/wiki/Luke_Skywalker/Legends "Luke Skywalker/Legends"), [Han Solo](/wiki/Han_Solo/Legends "Han Solo/Legends"), and the [Alliance to Restore the Republic](/wiki/Alliance_to_Restore_the_Republic/Legends "Alliance to Restore the Republic/Legends"), but also a surviving [Grand Moff](/wiki/Grand_Moff/Legends "Grand Moff/Legends") [Wilhuff Tarkin](/wiki/Wilhuff_Tarkin/Legends "Wilhuff Tarkin/Legends") and his surviving [Imperial](/wiki/Galactic_Empire/Legends "Galactic Empire/Legends") forces following the [Battle of Endor](/wiki/Battle_of_Endor/Legends "Battle of Endor/Legends").[[7]](#cite_note-topt-7) Kenner based their concept art for Atha Prime on rejected concept art for the [Emperor's Royal Guard](/wiki/Emperor%27s_Royal_Guard "Emperor's Royal Guard").[[8]](#cite_note-sobar-8) Kenner's proposal was ultimately rejected by [Lucasfilm](/wiki/Lucasfilm "Lucasfilm") though, who while grateful for their efforts, did not feel comfortable starting an Expanded Universe set after *Return of the Jedi* back then.[[7]](#cite_note-topt-7)

Commenting to [ComicBook.com](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/ComicBook.com "wikipedia:ComicBook.com") on how they chose to canonize Atha Prime, Marvel senior editor [Mark Paniccia](/wiki/Mark_Paniccia "Mark Paniccia") stated that while developing the *Jedi Knights* series, Guggenheim discovered Prime's existence and the unrealized project he originated from, so when both saw Prime's original design, they felt "immediately sold" and deemed Prime a "cool character" to bring into canon with their comic book series as the "perfect book" to do it.[[3]](#cite_note-PrimesCanonization-3) [Mark Paniccia](/wiki/Mark_Paniccia "Mark Paniccia") shared a panels Atha Prime confronting[[9]](#cite_note-Mark1-9) and battling from [*Jedi Knights* 2](/wiki/Jedi_Knights_2 "Jedi Knights 2")[[10]](#cite_note-Mark2-10) on [December 11](/wiki/December_11 "December 11")[[9]](#cite_note-Mark1-9) and [December 18](/wiki/December_18 "December 18"), [2024](/wiki/2024 "2024").[[10]](#cite_note-Mark2-10)
