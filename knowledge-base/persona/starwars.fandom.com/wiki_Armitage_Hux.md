**Armitage Hux** was a [human](/wiki/Human "Human") [male](/wiki/Gender "Gender") who served as a [general](/wiki/General "General") in the [military forces](/wiki/First_Order_military "First Order military") of the [First Order](/wiki/First_Order "First Order") during the [New Republic Era](/wiki/New_Republic_Era "New Republic Era"). With the support of [Supreme Leader](/wiki/Supreme_Leader "Supreme Leader") [Snoke](/wiki/Snoke "Snoke"), Hux took command of [Starkiller Base](/wiki/Starkiller_Base "Starkiller Base") and promoted the development of [new technology](/wiki/Hyperspace_tracker "Hyperspace tracker") that allowed the [First Order fleet](/wiki/First_Order_Navy "First Order Navy") to [track](/wiki/Active_tracker "Active tracker") targets through [hyperspace](/wiki/Hyperspace "Hyperspace"). However, Snoke's [death](/wiki/Death "Death") ruined the young general's career trajectory, as did the [years](/wiki/Standard_year "Standard year") that Hux spent jockeying for power against his rival [Kylo Ren](/wiki/Ben_Solo "Ben Solo"), who seized control of the First Order as the new Supreme Leader.

[Born](/wiki/Reproduction "Reproduction") on the [planet](/wiki/Planet "Planet") [Arkanis](/wiki/Arkanis "Arkanis") around the [time](/wiki/Time "Time") of the [Battle of Yavin](/wiki/Battle_of_Yavin "Battle of Yavin"), Hux was the [illegitimate](/wiki/Marriage "Marriage") [son](/wiki/Hux_family "Hux family") of [Commandant](/wiki/Commandant "Commandant") [Brendol Hux](/wiki/Brendol_Hux "Brendol Hux") and a [kitchen woman](/wiki/Armitage_Hux%27s_mother "Armitage Hux's mother"). In the aftermath of the [Galactic Civil War](/wiki/Galactic_Civil_War "Galactic Civil War"), Armitage fled into the [Unknown Regions](/wiki/Unknown_Regions "Unknown Regions") together with his father, [Grand Admiral](/wiki/Grand_Admiral "Grand Admiral") [Rae Sloane](/wiki/Rae_Sloane "Rae Sloane"), and a group of [children](/wiki/Gallius_Rax%27s_child_soldiers "Gallius Rax's child soldiers") who would become some of the first of the First Order's new [stormtroopers](/wiki/First_Order_stormtrooper "First Order stormtrooper"). A ruthless [officer](/wiki/Officer "Officer") by the time of the [Cold War](/wiki/Cold_War "Cold War"), Hux had engineered the death of his father, overseen the [training](/wiki/Project_Resurrection "Project Resurrection") and [indoctrination](/wiki/Propaganda "Propaganda") of a [new generation](/wiki/First_Order_stormtrooper "First Order stormtrooper") of stormtroopers, executed [Admiral](/wiki/Admiral "Admiral") [Brooks](/wiki/Brooks_(First_Order) "Brooks (First Order)"), and commanded the firing of the Starkiller [superweapon](/wiki/Superweapon "Superweapon") to [destroy](/wiki/Hosnian_Cataclysm "Hosnian Cataclysm") the [New Republic](/wiki/New_Republic "New Republic") [capital](/wiki/Capital "Capital") of [Hosnian Prime](/wiki/Hosnian_Prime "Hosnian Prime"). During the [First Order-Resistance War](/wiki/First_Order-Resistance_War "First Order-Resistance War"), Hux first served as [commanding officer](/wiki/Commanding_officer "Commanding officer") of the [*Resurgent*-class Star Destroyer](/wiki/Resurgent-class_Star_Destroyer "Resurgent-class Star Destroyer") *[Finalizer](/wiki/Finalizer "Finalizer")*, followed by the [*Mega*-class Star Dreadnought](/wiki/Mega-class_Star_Dreadnought "Mega-class Star Dreadnought") *[Supremacy](/wiki/Supremacy "Supremacy")* until its [destruction](/wiki/Battle_of_Oetchi "Battle of Oetchi") shortly before the [Battle of Crait](/wiki/Battle_of_Crait "Battle of Crait").

Ren's accession to the [rank](/wiki/Military_rank "Military rank") of Supreme Leader diminished Hux's influence over the First Order. Though he served on the [First Order Supreme Council](/wiki/First_Order_Supreme_Council "First Order Supreme Council") alongside other [members](/wiki/First_Order_officer "First Order officer") of the [First Order High Command](/wiki/First_Order_High_Command "First Order High Command"), Hux had little independent authority without supervision, and was made to serve under [Allegiant General](/wiki/Allegiant_General "Allegiant General") [Enric Pryde](/wiki/Enric_Pryde "Enric Pryde") aboard his flagship, the [*Steadfast*](/wiki/Steadfast_(Resurgent-class) "Steadfast (Resurgent-class)"). Disillusioned with his place in Ren's regime, he supplied the [Resistance](/wiki/Resistance "Resistance") with information about the [Sith Eternal](/wiki/Sith_Eternal "Sith Eternal")'s fleet, the [Final Order](/wiki/Final_Order "Final Order"), on [Exegol](/wiki/Exegol "Exegol"), determined to see his rival lose the war. Ultimately, however, Pryde discovered Hux's role as a [double agent](/wiki/Spy "Spy") and summarily [executed](/wiki/Death_penalty "Death penalty") Hux for [treason](/wiki/Treason "Treason").

## Biography[]

### Early life[]

:   "*He has a child—a bastard boy, as I understand. Not born of his wife, Maratelle, but of some…kitchen woman.*"
:   ―Gallius Rax, on Armitage Hux's illegitimacy[[10]](#cite_note-LD-10)

 [![Armitage Hux was the bastard son of Commandant Brendol Hux and a kitchen woman.](data:image/gif;base64,R0lGODlhAQABAIABAAAAAP///yH5BAEAAAEALAAAAAABAAEAQAICTAEAOw%3D%3D)](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/starwars/images/4/4b/YoungArmitageHux-AORHux1.jpg/revision/latest?cb=20210623053938) [![Armitage Hux was the bastard son of Commandant Brendol Hux and a kitchen woman.](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/starwars/images/4/4b/YoungArmitageHux-AORHux1.jpg/revision/latest/scale-to-width-down/200?cb=20210623053938)](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/starwars/images/4/4b/YoungArmitageHux-AORHux1.jpg/revision/latest?cb=20210623053938) 

Armitage Hux was the bastard son of Commandant Brendol Hux and a kitchen woman.

 

Armitage Hux[[10]](#cite_note-LD-10) was [born](/wiki/Reproduction "Reproduction") on the [planet](/wiki/Planet "Planet") [Arkanis](/wiki/Arkanis "Arkanis")[[1]](#cite_note-CEUE-1) in [1 BBY](/wiki/1_BBY "1 BBY"),[[2]](#cite_note-Timelines-2) during the [reign](/wiki/Imperial_Era "Imperial Era") of the [Galactic Empire](/wiki/Galactic_Empire "Galactic Empire").[[12]](#cite_note-Visual_Dictionary-12) Hux was fathered by [Commandant](/wiki/Commandant "Commandant") [Brendol Hux](/wiki/Brendol_Hux "Brendol Hux"), though not with[[10]](#cite_note-LD-10) his[[5]](#cite_note-DB-5) father's [wife](/wiki/Marriage "Marriage") [Maratelle](/wiki/Maratelle "Maratelle"), but rather a [kitchen woman](/wiki/Armitage_Hux%27s_mother "Armitage Hux's mother"), making Hux the commandant's bastard son.[[10]](#cite_note-LD-10) Hux never knew his [mother](/wiki/Parent "Parent") and as a boy remained utterly friendless.[[13]](#cite_note-TROS_Junior_Novel-13) As a [youngling](/wiki/Youngling "Youngling"), Hux was the subject of gossip among the [Imperial officers](/wiki/Imperial_officer "Imperial officer")' [ranks](/wiki/Imperial_Officer_Corps "Imperial Officer Corps"), as they whispered among themselves about the existence of Brendol's bastard and the mother who bore him.[[10]](#cite_note-LD-10)

Brendol made no secret of the low opinion he had about his son, whom he regarded as a frail, weak-willed boy, though not entirely bereft of potential.[[10]](#cite_note-LD-10) [Grand Admiral](/wiki/Grand_Admiral "Grand Admiral") [Rae Sloane](/wiki/Rae_Sloane "Rae Sloane") suspected that Hux was regularly abused by his father, both psychologically and physically,[[9]](#cite_note-Empire's_End-9) suspicions which proved to be true. Hux found strength in his father's stories of how the Empire brought peace to a galaxy that had weathered the chaos of the [Clone Wars](/wiki/Clone_Wars "Clone Wars"), which comforted him even as Hux weathered his own abuse under his father.[[13]](#cite_note-TROS_Junior_Novel-13) Because of his Imperial heritage, Hux developed notions of grandeur and believed that he had a right to sit on the [throne](/wiki/Throne "Throne") that ruled [the galaxy](/wiki/The_galaxy "The galaxy").[[12]](#cite_note-Visual_Dictionary-12) At an early age, Hux promised to himself that he would prove his father's preconceptions of him wrong. Taking inspiration from the legacy of [Sheev Palpatine](/wiki/Darth_Sidious "Darth Sidious"), the [Naboo](/wiki/Naboo "Naboo") [senator](/wiki/Senator "Senator") who became the [Galactic Emperor](/wiki/Galactic_Emperor "Galactic Emperor"), Hux believed that he was also capable of gaining absolute power. As such, Hux envisioned himself as the galaxy's future ruler, a savior bringing peace to the chaos for which he held the New Republic responsible.[[13]](#cite_note-TROS_Junior_Novel-13)

The young Hux became acquainted with his father's fellow officers, such as [Enric Pryde](/wiki/Enric_Pryde "Enric Pryde"), who was a friend of both Brendol and Maratelle,[[14]](#cite_note-TROS_Visual_Dictionary-14) and [Admiral](/wiki/Admiral "Admiral") [Brooks](/wiki/Brooks_(First_Order) "Brooks (First Order)"), who openly ridiculed Hux's illegitimacy. Brooks once mocked Armitage after he accidentally dropped some refreshments, suggesting that the illegitimate son of a kitchen woman should at least be capable of serving drinks. As Brendol expressed his contempt for his son, calling him pathetic and utterly useless, Hux promised to clean the mess, to which Brooks suggested that he lick the floor. Hux looked to his father for help before acquiescing to the admiral's request. Brendol, however, watched Armitage with disgust and began to physically accost his son.[[11]](#cite_note-Age_of_Resistance-11)

### Fall of the Galactic Empire[]

It will be your life's work to take children like these savages and hammer their malleable minds into whatever shape you so require. They will be tools built for the work at hand. This is my gift to you, boy. One day your father will die. One day soon, I fear. And you will take his place.*"
:   ―Gallius Rax to Armitage Hux[[9]](#cite_note-Empire's_End-9)

The Galactic Empire fell at the Battle of Jakku, while Armitage and Brendol escaped into the Unknown Regions.

 

The Empire was in its death throes as a result of the [Rebel](/wiki/Alliance_to_Restore_the_Republic "Alliance to Restore the Republic") [victory](/wiki/Battle_of_Endor "Battle of Endor") at [Endor](/wiki/Endor "Endor"). In [5 ABY](/wiki/5_ABY "5 ABY"),[[15]](#cite_note-Life_Debt_date-15) as the [New Republic](/wiki/New_Republic "New Republic") laid siege to Arkanis, Armitage and his father were forced to evacuate their [homeworld](/wiki/Homeworld "Homeworld"). [Fleet Admiral](/wiki/Fleet_Admiral "Fleet Admiral") [Gallius Rax](/wiki/Gallius_Rax "Gallius Rax"),[[10]](#cite_note-LD-10) a [political](/wiki/Politics "Politics") protégé of the [late](/wiki/Death "Death") [Emperor](/wiki/Emperor "Emperor") Palpatine, became invested in the future of Armitage Hux because of his father's visionary ideas on [stormtrooper](/wiki/Stormtrooper "Stormtrooper") training.[[9]](#cite_note-Empire's_End-9) At Rax's request, Sloane enlisted the services of the [bounty hunter](/wiki/Bounty_hunter "Bounty hunter") [Mercurial Swift](/wiki/Mercurial_Swift "Mercurial Swift") to rescue the Huxes, ensuring that neither Brendol nor his son became [prisoners](/wiki/Prisoner "Prisoner") of the New Republic.[[10]](#cite_note-LD-10) Unbeknownst to Sloane, Rax was fulfilling the Emperor's [Contingency](/wiki/Contingency "Contingency"), a [clandestine operation](/wiki/Operation:_Cinder "Operation: Cinder") designed to destroy the Empire from within and ensure its rebirth in the [Unknown Regions](/wiki/Unknown_Regions "Unknown Regions") of [galactic](/wiki/Galaxy "Galaxy") [space](/wiki/Realspace "Realspace").

Following the [Attack on Chandrila](/wiki/Attack_on_Chandrila "Attack on Chandrila"), Armitage and Brendol then traveled to the planet [Jakku](/wiki/Jakku "Jakku") to rendezvous with the [Imperial Military](/wiki/Imperial_Military "Imperial Military"). In the [months](/wiki/Standard_month "Standard month") prior to the [Battle of Jakku](/wiki/Battle_of_Jakku "Battle of Jakku"), the Huxes remained close to Rax, who led the Imperial [show government](/wiki/Shadow_Council "Shadow Council") as the self-styled [Counselor to the Empire](/wiki/Counselor_to_the_Empire "Counselor to the Empire"). While Brendol trained twenty-four local orphans, Hux tried to keep his distance from his abusive father. By the end of their training, these [child soldiers](/wiki/Gallius_Rax%27s_child_soldiers "Gallius Rax's child soldiers") became Counselor Rax's personal [bodyguards](/wiki/Bodyguard "Bodyguard").[[9]](#cite_note-Empire's_End-9)

When the [New Republic Defense Force](/wiki/New_Republic_Defense_Force "New Republic Defense Force") invaded Jakku, a [transport](/wiki/Transport "Transport") ferried Armitage, his father, Counselor Rax, [Yupe Tashu](/wiki/Yupe_Tashu "Yupe Tashu") (the [Emperor's adviser](/wiki/Emperor%27s_adviser "Emperor's adviser")), and the child [soldiers](/wiki/Soldier "Soldier") to the [Jakku Observatory](/wiki/Jakku_Observatory "Jakku Observatory") in the [Plaintive Hand plateau](/wiki/Plaintive_Hand_plateau "Plaintive Hand plateau"). As part of the Contingency, Rax intended to use the [observatory](/wiki/Observatory "Observatory") to detonate Jakku's planetary core, wiping out both the New Republic and the Empire in a single stroke, before taking Armitage and Brendol to the Unknown Regions where they could create a new Empire. Traveling to the observatory, Armitage found himself alone in the transport hold with the child soldiers. The older children intimidated him, as they had been trained by his father to become ruthless killers. Although Rax had intended to use Brendol's training methods to build the army of the new Empire, the Counselor considered Armitage's role as Brendol's successor to be more important.[[9]](#cite_note-Empire's_End-9)

 [![Taking an interest in young Armitage, Grand Admiral Rae Sloane ordered Brendol to stop mistreating his son.](data:image/gif;base64,R0lGODlhAQABAIABAAAAAP///yH5BAEAAAEALAAAAAABAAEAQAICTAEAOw%3D%3D)](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/starwars/images/2/28/AdmiralSloane-BTCG.png/revision/latest?cb=20211113002413) [![Taking an interest in young Armitage, Grand Admiral Rae Sloane ordered Brendol to stop mistreating his son.](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/starwars/images/2/28/AdmiralSloane-BTCG.png/revision/latest/scale-to-width-down/200?cb=20211113002413)](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/starwars/images/2/28/AdmiralSloane-BTCG.png/revision/latest?cb=20211113002413) 

Taking an interest in young Armitage, Grand Admiral Rae Sloane ordered Brendol to stop mistreating his son.

 

The child soldiers were ultimately gifted to Armitage by Counselor Rax, realizing that Hux would carry on his father's work by training a new generation of soldiers bred for warfare. With that, Rax addressed his [troops](/wiki/Troop "Troop") personally, informing them that they would follow Hux's orders to the death and without question. Putting his new authority to the test, Hux ordered one of the boys to strike his comrade. After reaching the observatory, the Huxes were bidden by the Counselor to board his luxury [yacht](/wiki/Yacht "Yacht")—a [replica](/wiki/Imperialis_(Jakku) "Imperialis (Jakku)") of the Emperor's *[Imperialis](/wiki/Imperialis "Imperialis")*. Rax, however, was killed by Sloane before he could complete his plan to explode Jakku. Seizing the opportunity to mold the next Empire according to her ideals, Sloane accompanied Hux and Brendol on their sojourn to uncharted space.[[9]](#cite_note-Empire's_End-9)

During the journey, Sloane befriended Hux, who came to see the Grand Admiral as his protector from Brendol. Hux likewise protected Sloane from the child soldiers, who remained loyal only to himself in accordance with Rax's last wishes. Sloane berated Brendol, warning the commandant to cease hurting his son. However, she also wanted Hux to benefit from his father's experience and as such Brendol was ordered to educate his illegitimate son as he would a proper heir. For several months, the *Imperialis* traveled through the Unknown Regions, and in that time, Hux and his soldiers grew even more vicious under Brendol's tutelage. Even Sloane was taken aback by Hux's cruelty, which she considered to be an unusual trait for such a small boy. Their travails eventually culminated in their discovery of the Emperor's [Super Star Destroyer](/wiki/Super_Star_Destroyer "Super Star Destroyer"), the [*Eclipse*](/wiki/Eclipse_(Executor-class) "Eclipse (Executor-class)").[[9]](#cite_note-Empire's_End-9)

### New Republic Era[]

#### A general in the making[]

Armitage grew up ascending the ranks of the First Order, and became a general like his father before him.

 

Although the Empire [surrendered](/wiki/Imperial_Instruments_of_Surrender "Imperial Instruments of Surrender") to the New Republic, signing the [Galactic Concordance](/wiki/Galactic_Concordance "Galactic Concordance") that ended the [Galactic Civil War](/wiki/Galactic_Civil_War "Galactic Civil War"),[[9]](#cite_note-Empire's_End-9) the principles of the [New Order](/wiki/New_Order "New Order") survived through a new generation of adherents. Armitage Hux was part of that generation,[[14]](#cite_note-TROS_Visual_Dictionary-14) having grown up listening to stories about legendary Imperial leaders. As part of his education, Hux was raised to believe that the Empire delivered the galaxy from the chaos of the Clone Wars, whereas the New Republic lacked the strength to impose such order.[[12]](#cite_note-Visual_Dictionary-12)

At the same time, several individual elements of the Old Empire were combined to form a new regime, melding the Empire's political, military and [spiritual](/wiki/Religion "Religion") survivors under a movement that became known as the [First Order](/wiki/First_Order "First Order"). As the son of an [Imperial Academy](/wiki/Imperial_Academy "Imperial Academy") commandant,[[5]](#cite_note-DB-5) Hux belonged to the military branch that was the template of the [First Order military](/wiki/First_Order_military "First Order military").[[14]](#cite_note-TROS_Visual_Dictionary-14) He eventually gained the status of [general](/wiki/General "General"), becoming the youngest [First Order officer](/wiki/First_Order_officer "First Order officer") to hold that [military rank](/wiki/Military_rank "Military rank")[[13]](#cite_note-TROS_Junior_Novel-13) while also serving as a member of [First Order High Command](/wiki/First_Order_High_Command "First Order High Command").[[16]](#cite_note-BTA-16) However, Hux aspired to control both branches of the military—the [First Order Navy](/wiki/First_Order_Navy "First Order Navy") and the [First Order Army](/wiki/First_Order_Army "First Order Army")—as [Grand Marshal](/wiki/Grand_Marshal "Grand Marshal"), although Supreme Leader Snoke denied him this title.[[17]](#cite_note-TLJ_Visual_Dictionary-17)

Having spent his life in thrall to the idea of Imperial power, General Hux continued his father's legacy which included Brendol's [radical ideas](/wiki/Commandant%27s_Cadets "Commandant's Cadets") on training soldiers from birth. This approach produced a [new generation of stormtroopers](/wiki/First_Order_stormtrooper "First Order stormtrooper") that began with Brendol and continued through Hux, who also revived another Imperial ideal—achieving military superiority through technological terror.[[5]](#cite_note-DB-5)

At some point Hux ventured to [Parnassos](/wiki/Parnassos "Parnassos") to respond to the [distress signal](/wiki/Distress_signal "Distress signal") sent by Brendol from the crash site of [his ship](/wiki/Palpatine%27s_yacht "Palpatine's yacht"). Footage recovered from the shuttle that transported them to the *Absolution* was later recovered by [Resistance](/wiki/Resistance "Resistance") intelligence agents. #### An act of patricide[]

:   "*Cardinal, you're an idiot. My father knew it, and so do I. I know Phasma killed him, and I'm glad the old bastard is dead. We agreed on the right time for it to happen. I told her it had to be untraceable, and it shall remain so.*"

Fueled by ambition and spite, General Hux murdered his father with the help of Captain Phasma.

 

Over the course of his rise to power, the young general ruthlessly dispatched many enemies and potential foes before they became too dangerous a threat. This included the quiet elimination of Hux's father, Brendol, the elder General Hux.[[17]](#cite_note-TLJ_Visual_Dictionary-17) Knowing how his father truly felt about him, Hux resolved to kill his father, although this was not the only reason behind his patricidal actions.[[11]](#cite_note-Age_of_Resistance-11) Driven by ambition and a sense of destiny, he saw Brendol as an obstacle in his path to gaining greater power. To that end, he enlisted the help of Captain Phasma, and the two conspirators agreed that the old Imperial veteran had to be permanently removed so as to promote their own interests.[[18]](#cite_note-Phasma-18)

Although Hux had no reservations about committing patricide, he left the actual killing to his co-conspirator, whom he instructed to use an untraceable method, not wanting the murder to be traced back to himself. Phasma therefore utilized a toxin, taken from the bite of a [Parnassos beetle](/wiki/Parnassos_beetle "Parnassos beetle"), to [assassinate](/wiki/Assassin "Assassin") Brendol. Once the deed was done, Hux proceeded with formalities by addressing the members of the [First Order Stormtrooper Corps](/wiki/Stormtrooper_Corps_(First_Order) "Stormtrooper Corps (First Order)"), informing them that their founder had died. After concluding his speech, Hux wasted no time in positioning himself as his father's successor, and even took possession of Brendol's suite on the *Absolution*.[[18]](#cite_note-Phasma-18)

 [![Hux consolidated his influence over the First Order military by purging the soldiers whose loyalty he doubted, including Captain Cardinal.](data:image/gif;base64,R0lGODlhAQABAIABAAAAAP///yH5BAEAAAEALAAAAAABAAEAQAICTAEAOw%3D%3D)](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/starwars/images/d/d5/PhasmaFirstOrderPosterBarnes.jpg/revision/latest?cb=20170813031935) [![Hux consolidated his influence over the First Order military by purging the soldiers whose loyalty he doubted, including Captain Cardinal.](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/starwars/images/d/d5/PhasmaFirstOrderPosterBarnes.jpg/revision/latest/scale-to-width-down/250?cb=20170813031935)](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/starwars/images/d/d5/PhasmaFirstOrderPosterBarnes.jpg/revision/latest?cb=20170813031935) 

Hux consolidated his influence over the First Order military by purging the soldiers whose loyalty he doubted, including Captain Cardinal.

 

In the years following Brendol's demise, Hux remodeled the First Order [army](/wiki/Army "Army") according to his design, promoting Phasma to complete the older stormtrooper [cadets](/wiki/Cadet "Cadet")' training. Her methods emphasized loyalty, obedience and ruthlessness, qualities that met with Armitage's approval. Keeping with his habit of eliminating potential threats, Hux sidelined Captain [Cardinal](/wiki/Archex "Archex"), knowing that the latter was a loyal supporter of the general's late father. Cardinal eventually concluded that Phasma was the culprit behind his mentor's death, though he was unaware of Hux's role in it. He reported his findings to Hux, who revealed his own involvement in the conspiracy that assassinated his father. Though Cardinal was ultimately deferential to Hux due to the latter's authority, Hux remained skeptical about Cardinal's loyalty and expected Phasma to eliminate him as well.[[18]](#cite_note-Phasma-18)

As a precaution, Hux excluded Cardinal from a meeting of senior First Order officials. When Cardinal arrived with evidence of Phasma's crimes, Hux curtly dismissed the captain, telling him that his services were no longer required. Having spent a lifetime following protocol, Cardinal complied but later attempted to avenge Brendol by killing Phasma. However, he was bested by his fellow captain in single combat and grievously wounded. Cardinal only escaped with the help of Moradi. In the end, his idealized view of the First Order was shattered by the actions of Hux and Phasma.[[18]](#cite_note-Phasma-18) As the new leaders of [Project Resurrection](/wiki/Project_Resurrection "Project Resurrection"), Hux and Phasma recruited [Jinata Security](/wiki/Jinata_Security "Jinata Security") to help the First Order capture infants to mold into new stormtroopers.[[19]](#cite_note-USWNE-19)

#### The hunt for Poe Dameron[]

At some point prior to the cold war, Hux allegedly harbored a distaste for [First Order Security Bureau](/wiki/First_Order_Security_Bureau "First Order Security Bureau") agent [Terex](/wiki/Terex "Terex"),[[20]](#cite_note-Poe_Dameron_2-20) a belief largely founded on Terex's past as a criminal.[[21]](#cite_note-Poe_Dameron_10-21) Terex had begun a manhunt against Resistance agent Poe Dameron, an action harshly opposed by Hux and other First Order leaders.[[22]](#cite_note-Poe_Dameron_11-22)

While traveling through hyperspace, a shuttle carrying Hux and Ren crashed on a [terrestrial forested planet](/wiki/Unidentified_planet_(Bylsma) "Unidentified planet (Bylsma)"). They survived, however, due to Ren's command of [the Force](/wiki/The_Force "The Force"), although the dark warrior admitted that Hux's survival was entirely accidental on his part. After coming to the conclusion that the ship had been sabotaged, Hux got into an argument with Ren, taunting him by telling him that Ren's grandfather, [Darth Vader](/wiki/Anakin_Skywalker "Anakin Skywalker"), needed his mask to survive, whereas Ren used his mask to play dress up and hide the faces of his parents. This angered Ren, who activated his [lightsaber](/wiki/Kylo_Ren%27s_lightsaber "Kylo Ren's lightsaber") and threatened to kill Hux; after all, no one would find out. Hux pointed out that Supreme Leader [Snoke](/wiki/Snoke "Snoke") would find out and would punish Ren. Ren deactivated his lightsaber but commented that he didn't know why Snoke kept Hux alive.[[11]](#cite_note-Age_of_Resistance-11)

Suddenly, the two men spotted a leg of an animal. Hux told Ren it was bait right as a massive beast known as a [norwood](/wiki/Norwood "Norwood") came out of the shrubbery and surprised the two. Ren activated his lightsaber and told Hux to stand behind him, but Hux had already fled. Kylo then used the Force to hurt the norwood. Thinking the beast was dead, Ren got closer to it. Suddenly, the infuriated beast rose and struck Ren, knocking his helmet off his head and knocking Ren unconscious.[[11]](#cite_note-Age_of_Resistance-11)

 [![Marooned on a distant world, Hux was forced to work with his rival in order to survive.](data:image/gif;base64,R0lGODlhAQABAIABAAAAAP///yH5BAEAAAEALAAAAAABAAEAQAICTAEAOw%3D%3D)](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/starwars/images/a/a1/SavedByTheForce-AORHux1.jpg/revision/latest?cb=20190926002542) [![Marooned on a distant world, Hux was forced to work with his rival in order to survive.](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/starwars/images/a/a1/SavedByTheForce-AORHux1.jpg/revision/latest/scale-to-width-down/250?cb=20190926002542)](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/starwars/images/a/a1/SavedByTheForce-AORHux1.jpg/revision/latest?cb=20190926002542) 

Marooned on a distant world, Hux was forced to work with his rival in order to survive.

 

Hux, who'd been watching the event unfold while he was hiding in the trees, saw a [man](/wiki/Bylsma_(Alderaanian) "Bylsma (Alderaanian)") with a blaster in his hand draw near to the beasts. The man saw Hux hiding, pointed the blaster at him, and ordered him to come out. Hux did as he was ordered and stepped out. He marveled at how the man had tamed the norwoods. Hux, looking at the man's uniform, concluded that the man was a [Palace Guard](/wiki/Alderaan_Guard "Alderaan Guard"). The man told Hux that he had been off-world when the [Death Star](/wiki/Death_Star "Death Star") destroyed Alderaan. The man then ran off, far away as possible from the Empire, and came to the planet. Hux, after finding out the man's name, which was Bylsma, told him that the Empire had fallen years before. Bylsma was skeptical and asked Hux how could he know that Hux spoke the truth. Hux told him that the truth was Kylo Ren. He then informed Bylsma that Kylo Ren was Ben Solo, son of Leia Organa, Princess of Alderaan and that Ben's parents had won the war. Bylsma then asked about the Death Star. Hux told him that the Death Star and its [successor](/wiki/DS-2_Death_Star_II_Mobile_Battle_Station "DS-2 Death Star II Mobile Battle Station") had both been destroyed. Bylsma was surprised when he heard that the Empire had built a second Death Star. Hux answered by saying the Galactic Empire was never renowned for its creativity. Bylsma then stated that he had known Ben's grandfather and mother.[[11]](#cite_note-Age_of_Resistance-11)

As the men headed back to where Bylsma lived, Hux asked Bylsma why he was still on the planet. Bylsma replied by saying that he didn't know who'd receive his transmission. He didn't want to risk being found since he had already lost one world. After laying Ben on a bed, Hux, who was pretending to be caring, convinced Bylsma to let him use the communication array so that they could enjoy the peacetime that was won. Finally, Kylo woke up, with Hux telling him that the shuttle would arrive soon, thanks to Bylsma.[[11]](#cite_note-Age_of_Resistance-11)

Eventually, a First Order shuttle with Phasma and a small team of stormtroopers on board arrived on the planet. As the ship opened, Phasma and her stormtroopers shot Bylsma's norwoods dead. Hux, now revealing his true nature, ordered Phasma to leave Bylsma alive, stating that he wanted to use the planet for target practice when [Starkiller Base](/wiki/Starkiller_Base "Starkiller Base") became operational. The shuttle left, leaving behind a shocked and saddened Bylsma.[[11]](#cite_note-Age_of_Resistance-11)

[Thirty years](/wiki/34_ABY "34 ABY") after the Battle of Endor, Hux was the general who commanded Starkiller Base, the primary base of operations of the First Order, as well as the *Resurgent*-class [Star Destroyer](/wiki/Star_Destroyer "Star Destroyer") *[Finalizer](/wiki/Finalizer "Finalizer")* and answered to Supreme Leader Snoke. Deeming [Luke Skywalker](/wiki/Luke_Skywalker "Luke Skywalker"), the last Jedi, to be a threat to the First Order, Hux, along with Ren, were given the directive. Though the First Order possessed maps from the Empire that would lead them to the planet where Skywalker was hiding, they lacked a final piece. To that end, the *Finalizer* traveled to Jakku and dispatched a group of stormtroopers to [attack](/wiki/Attack_on_Tuanul "Attack on Tuanul") the settlement of [Tuanul](/wiki/Tuanul "Tuanul"), where Ren captured a Resistance pilot named [Poe Dameron](/wiki/Poe_Dameron "Poe Dameron"), who already acquired and hid the final piece of the map. After Ren interrogated Dameron, he informed Hux that the prisoner had entrusted the map to the [BB-series astromech droid](/wiki/BB-series_astromech_droid "BB-series astromech droid") [BB-8](/wiki/BB-8 "BB-8"). Hux dispatched stormtroopers back to Jakku to search for it and posted a bounty on the droid.[[8]](#cite_note-Episode_VII-8)

Subsequently, Dameron was freed by a stormtrooper and the pair attempted to escape on a [TIE/sf space superiority fighter](/wiki/TIE/sf_space_superiority_fighter "TIE/sf space superiority fighter"), disabling the *Finalizer*'s turbolasers. Hux ordered the ventral cannons to shoot down the fighter, causing it to crash-land on Jakku. Upon informing Ren of the incident, the dark knight deduced that the traitorous stormtrooper was FN-2187. Hux checked with Phasma, who noted that this was FN-2187's first offense.[[8]](#cite_note-Episode_VII-8)

Subsequently, Supreme Leader Snoke ordered BB-8 captured or destroyed if necessary. When Hux informed Ren, the latter questioned the competency of their stormtroopers, given that one of them committed high treason, and suggested that the First Order instead appropriate a clone army like the Galactic Republic and early Empire before them. Hux assured him that his stormtroopers were trained from birth to succeed and reminded Ren not to let his obsession with Skywalker interfere with their mission.

Placing more emphasis on spectacle than tactical sense, Hux ordered no fighter deployments in favor of annihilating the base and fleet from orbit with the dreadnought. He was, however, briefly distracted by an insulting transmission from Poe Dameron, who repeatedly referred to Hux by the mocking term "General Hugs" and implied that Organa wanted to send a message about Hux's mother. Hux responded that he would not negotiate with the Resistance, but Dameron responded as if he couldn't hear him. After a few more attempts which made Hux increasingly angry, he ultimately ordered the dreadnought to destroy the Resistance base, and afterwards, the main fleet.[[30]](#cite_note-Episode_VIII-30)

The dreadnought's captain, a veteran of the Old Empire with little patience for Hux, retorted that the ship was too small and too close and ordered the ship's fighters deployed in a manner which contradicted Hux's prior orders.[[30]](#cite_note-Episode_VIII-30)

Though the base was ultimately destroyed, the victory wound up being largely pyrrhic because it had been completely evacuated and the Resistance deployed bombers which annihilated the dreadnought, allowing the Resistance fleet to escape. Hux was then contacted and subjected to painful humiliation by Snoke, who slammed the general into the floor of the bridge with the Force for his failure to destroy the Resistance. Hux, though smarting and with a bleeding lip, replied that he had a plan to track the Resistance through hyperspace, a plan which he relayed in full to the Supreme Leader aboard his flagship, the [*Mega*-class Star Dreadnought](/wiki/Mega-class_Star_Dreadnought "Mega-class Star Dreadnought") *[Supremacy](/wiki/Supremacy "Supremacy")*.[[30]](#cite_note-Episode_VIII-30)

Under the new Supreme Leader's reign, Hux traveled to the First Order [supertanker](/wiki/Supertanker_fuel_depot "Supertanker fuel depot"), the *[Titan](/wiki/Titan "Titan")*, to meet with Commander [Pyre](/wiki/Pyre "Pyre") as Supreme Leader Ren wanted an update on the First Order's stock of refueling stations and hunt for the Resistance cells. Xiono, a native of Hosnian Prime who was aware of Hux's role in the destruction of the planet, almost attacked Hux for revenge as the General spoke in a disdainful manner to his subordinate. Upon arriving to the briefing area, Hux delivered more orders to Pyre regarding the attainment of valuable supertanker assets, notably to quell the [resistance cell](/wiki/Team_Colossus "Team Colossus") on the *Colossus*. During the later meeting, Hux became frustrated with the interruption to the meeting caused by Xiono's theft of the *Titan's* radiation deflector.[[34]](#cite_note-Station_to_Station-34)

At the order of Supreme Leader Kylo Ren, Hux also ordered the [Kendoh Gang](/wiki/Kendoh_Gang "Kendoh Gang") to get Resistance operative Finn when they alerted him that they'd spotted him, though Hux had initially refuted the validity of the gang's claims.[[32]](#cite_note-Allegiance_1-32)

In the [year](/wiki/35_ABY "35 ABY") after the Battle of Crait, a [mysterious message](/wiki/Palpatine%27s_broadcast "Palpatine's broadcast") swept out across the galaxy in the voice of Emperor Palpatine, the public identity of Darth Sidious, previously presumed dead at Endor decades prior. To Hux's dismay, Ren diverted his attention away from the Resistance to focus on the potential threat to his rule the phantom Emperor posed, which Hux didn't take seriously, dismissing Ren's actions as chasing a ghost. To track down Sidious, Ren required a [Sith wayfinder](/wiki/Sith_wayfinder "Sith wayfinder"), which he eventually located, despite Hux's smug disapproval, on the [volcanic](/wiki/Volcano "Volcano") planet of [Mustafar](/wiki/Mustafar "Mustafar"), once ruled by Ren's grandfather Darth Vader from his personal stronghold, [Fortress Vader](/wiki/Fortress_Vader "Fortress Vader").[[40]](#cite_note-TROS_Expanded_Edition-40)

Hux accompanied Ren along with [Allegiant General](/wiki/Allegiant_General "Allegiant General") [Enric Pryde](/wiki/Enric_Pryde "Enric Pryde"), who had become a rival of his since the Crait fiasco, to Mustafar, which Hux described as a "desolate hellscape." Once there, Ren and a squad of stormtroopers [fought](/wiki/Mission_to_Mustafar_(First_Order) "Mission to Mustafar (First Order)") against the [Alazmec of Winsit](/wiki/Alazmec_of_Winsit "Alazmec of Winsit"), a tenacious sect of [Sith cultists](/wiki/Sith_cultist "Sith cultist") devoted to protecting [Corvax Fen](/wiki/Corvax_Fen "Corvax Fen") and who venerated Vader as a [deity](/wiki/Deity "Deity") of the dark side. Hux and Pryde watched the fight from a safe distance. While watching, Pryde asked Hux if he didn't like seeing Ren fighting, Hux expressed his opinion that Ren had gone mad due prioritizing the Emperor's supposed return over the burning flames of rebellion across the galaxy, leading Pryde to remind Hux that there was someone behind the broadcast and that Leader Ren would answer to no one due to the sacrifices he made to gain his power.[[40]](#cite_note-TROS_Expanded_Edition-40)

Ren eventually made his way to Fortress Vader and recovered [Vader's wayfinder](/wiki/Vader%27s_wayfinder "Vader's wayfinder"), which led him to the ancient Sith world of [Exegol](/wiki/Exegol "Exegol"). Once there, Ren discovered that Darth Sidious' spirit had in some manner endured, and through the power and influence of his station as Emperor and Sith Lord, he had assembled a shadow Empire on the fringes of the galaxy. With the aid of the [Sith Eternal](/wiki/Sith_Eternal "Sith Eternal") from Exegol, Sidious had forged the [Final Order](/wiki/Final_Order "Final Order")—one of the largest fleets in history. Following an initial meeting with a hostile Ren, Sidious persuaded him to augment his forces with the Sith fleet and usher in ultimate galactic dominance as the new Emperor. In parallel to these events, Hux's triumphant First Order military had been tasked by Ren with destroying the remaining Resistance forces, which had continued fighting after Crait but had largely been forced underground by the repressive and expanding regime. In the course of these operations, a number of TIE fighters became involved in a pursuit of the *Millennium Falcon* as it collected intelligence from [Boolio](/wiki/Boolio "Boolio") on a spy embedded deep within the First Order that could lead the Resistance to the resurgent Sidious.[[4]](#cite_note-Episode_IX-4)

Shortly after the TIEs had chased the *Falcon* from the [Sinta Glacier Colony](/wiki/Sinta_Glacier_Colony "Sinta Glacier Colony"), the Supreme Leader and his Knights of Ren arrived, interrogated and ultimately killed Boolio[[40]](#cite_note-TROS_Expanded_Edition-40) before delivering his decapitated head to a conference of the [First Order Supreme Council](/wiki/First_Order_Supreme_Council "First Order Supreme Council"), including Pryde and Hux. Ren informed his subordinates that there was a traitor in their midst. Sensing apprehension among them, the Supreme Leader inquired if his freshly reassembled helmet made Hux uncomfortable; the General quietly replied that it did not and congratulated him for fixing it. A comment from General [Bellava Parnadee](/wiki/Bellava_Parnadee "Bellava Parnadee") that she liked the mask brought a short pause to the proceedings.[[4]](#cite_note-Episode_IX-4)

General [Domaric Quinn](/wiki/Domaric_Quinn "Domaric Quinn") spoke up, positing that Sidious and his followers seemed more like a cult than anything else. Pryde dismissed this concern, but Quinn continued, wondering aloud if the Sith fleet promised by the Dark Lord was a gift and what he might demand in return. Enraged, Ren used the Force to choke General Quinn and throw his body to the ceiling.[[4]](#cite_note-Episode_IX-4) As Quinn died,[[40]](#cite_note-TROS_Expanded_Edition-40) Ren announced to the remaining assembly that his mission was hunting Rey with the Knights of Ren while they focus on crushing any worlds that defy them.[[4]](#cite_note-Episode_IX-4)

Following a brief skirmish between First Order troops and Rey's Resistance party on Pasaana, Hux presented to Pryde a "valuable prisoner" taken during the fighting: Chewbacca, who is believed dead by his allies. The elder commander, unfazed by the roaring of the Wookiee, ordered immediate interrogation. Shortly after, Ren would return to the [*Steadfast*](/wiki/Steadfast_(Resurgent-class) "Steadfast (Resurgent-class)"), having failed to accomplish the task set by Darth Sidious.[[4]](#cite_note-Episode_IX-4)

The *Steadfast* eventually arrived at Kijimi shortly after the arrival of Rey's party, with Ren swiftly disembarking to aid his knights in hunting the scavenger and her allies. Through an old accomplice of Dameron, the fugitives were able to sneak aboard the vessel in order to liberate Chewbacca from First Order custody, Rey having discovered his survival through the Force. Although they managed to kill a number of stormtroopers, all members of the strike team save for Rey were eventually swarmed by overwhelming numbers and captured. Hux soon arrived to confront the prisoners and ordered their immediate execution. He chose to accompany the squad of troopers selected for the duty as they escorted Finn, Dameron, and Chewbacca to a sequestered section of the ship.[[4]](#cite_note-Episode_IX-4)

Moments before the sentence was to be carried out, Hux spoke up and claimed that he would like to join the firing squad. Accepting the blaster of one of the troopers present, the general swiftly spun around and killed the troopers before outing himself as the spy that Ren and Pryde had been hunting within their ranks. Offering his assistance to the bewildered Resistance agents, he quickly escorted them to the incarcerated *Falcon*, lingering behind them as they blasted their way through his own troops.[[4]](#cite_note-Episode_IX-4)

Stopping Finn before he could board the *Falcon*, Hux demanded that the former stormtrooper shoot him in the arm in order to verify his cover story that he had been forced to aid in their escape. Out of spite, Finn shot the general in the thigh. He then inquired as to why Hux would betray the First Order he had helped build; disdainfully, the general said that he didn't care if the Resistance won so much as that Ren needed to lose. The Resistance agents then completed their escape, rejoining Rey as she ended her latest duel with Ren, who had returned to the *Steadfast* after discovering she was aboard.[[4]](#cite_note-Episode_IX-4)

Hux had his injured leg treated with bacta gel, causing the wound to itch, and bandaged before reporting to Pryde on the bridge of the *Steadfast*. He provided Pryde with a false account of how the Resistance escaped in order to conceal his actions of treason and murder.[[40]](#cite_note-TROS_Expanded_Edition-40) He then planned on framing Ren as the spy who supplied the Resistance with information concerning the fleet on Exegol, believing that this was his opportunity to wrest power from his rival.[[13]](#cite_note-TROS_Junior_Novel-13)

Hux's attempt at deception did not succeed on Pryde.[[5]](#cite_note-DB-5) Instead, Hux inadvertently revealed his treachery to the Allegiant General,[[41]](#cite_note-TSWB-41) who turned around and requested to communicate with Supreme Leader Kylo Ren before snatching a blaster from a nearby stormtrooper, turning back and blasting Hux dead for betraying the First Order just so he could have vengeance against the Supreme Leader, sending Hux's body sliding down through the aisle behind him.[[4]](#cite_note-Episode_IX-4) The general's last thought was about Rey, whom he held responsible for the failure of his plans. Hux's efforts to undermine his rivals contributed to the First Order's defeat.

 

The last days in the life of General Armitage Hux were spent under the authority of Supreme Leader Kylo Ren and Allegiant General Enric Pryde, both of whom outranked the ambitious officer[[41]](#cite_note-TSWB-41) who sought to rule the galaxy through the First Order.[[13]](#cite_note-TROS_Junior_Novel-13) Pryde held Hux personally responsible for the loss of Starkiller Base; as such, he welcomed the First Order's alliance with the Sith Eternal, convinced that they needed their allies' warships to replace the resources that were lost in the wake of Starkiller's destruction.[[40]](#cite_note-TROS_Expanded_Edition-40)

General Hux's jealousy and insecurities caused him to take desperate actions with far-reaching consequences, resulting not only in his demise but also the downfall of the First Order.[[41]](#cite_note-TSWB-41) In the aftermath of Hux's death and Ren's subsequent [renunciation of the dark side](/wiki/Mission_to_Kef_Bir "Mission to Kef Bir"), Pryde traveled to Exegol to oversee the deployment of the Sith fleet, having sworn fealty and giving complete control of the First Order to the resurrected Emperor. However, the Final Order fleet was destroyed by the Resistance and its [allies](/wiki/Citizens%27_Fleet "Citizens' Fleet") during the [Battle of Exegol](/wiki/Battle_of_Exegol "Battle of Exegol").[[4]](#cite_note-Episode_IX-4) The First Order consequently lost its dominance over the galaxy[[41]](#cite_note-TSWB-41) as multiple worlds, including [Bespin](/wiki/Uprising_on_Cloud_City "Uprising on Cloud City"), [Jakku](/wiki/Uprising_on_Jakku "Uprising on Jakku"), and [Endor](/wiki/Uprising_on_Endor "Uprising on Endor"), [rebelled against military rule](/wiki/Uprising_against_the_First_Order "Uprising against the First Order").[[4]](#cite_note-Episode_IX-4)

## Personality and traits[]

:   "*You. My father. Snoke. Ren. You all underestimate me. You see me as something you can control. Something weak. I am not weak. I am patient. You all have the power to destroy people. But I will outlive you all. I will hold more power than any of you. Destroying people is nothing. I will have the power to destroy worlds.*"
:   ―Armitage Hux, to Brooks[[11]](#cite_note-Age_of_Resistance-11)

 [![Armitage Hux was a young, ambitious general who craved power and promoted technological innovation.](data:image/gif;base64,R0lGODlhAQABAIABAAAAAP///yH5BAEAAAEALAAAAAABAAEAQAICTAEAOw%3D%3D)](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/starwars/images/3/32/Armitage_Hux_TLJ.png/revision/latest?cb=20230813060930) [![Armitage Hux was a young, ambitious general who craved power and promoted technological innovation.](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/starwars/images/3/32/Armitage_Hux_TLJ.png/revision/latest/scale-to-width-down/200?cb=20230813060930)](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/starwars/images/3/32/Armitage_Hux_TLJ.png/revision/latest?cb=20230813060930) 

Armitage Hux was a young, ambitious general who craved power and promoted technological innovation.

 

General Armitage Hux[[14]](#cite_note-TROS_Visual_Dictionary-14) was a [human](/wiki/Human "Human") [male](/wiki/Gender "Gender") who stood at 1.85 [meters](/wiki/Meter "Meter")[[5]](#cite_note-DB-5) and weighed 75 [kilograms](/wiki/Kilogram "Kilogram").[[7]](#cite_note-TFA_head_to_head-7) He had red [hair](/wiki/Hair "Hair"), green [eyes](/wiki/Eye "Eye")[[8]](#cite_note-Episode_VII-8) and pale skin due to lack of exposure to sunlight.[[12]](#cite_note-Visual_Dictionary-12) His favorite beverage was bitter [tarine tea](/wiki/Tarine_tea "Tarine tea").[[17]](#cite_note-TLJ_Visual_Dictionary-17) He was a ruthless officer in the First Order[[5]](#cite_note-DB-5) as well as an adherent to the ideals of the Galactic Empire. Like other members of his generation, who served as the vanguard of First Order leadership, Hux was strikingly young,[[14]](#cite_note-TROS_Visual_Dictionary-14) in fact being the youngest general in its ranks,[[13]](#cite_note-TROS_Junior_Novel-13) but what he lacked in experience and wisdom, he made up for in his zeal.[[14]](#cite_note-TROS_Visual_Dictionary-14) Due to his youth, Hux's experiences in warfare were entirely theoretical. Though acknowledged for the thoroughness and complexity of his simulations, Hux earned little respect for his abilities as a warrior.[[12]](#cite_note-Visual_Dictionary-12)

As the son of a highly placed official in the old Imperial Academy, Hux was raised to celebrate his Imperial heritage. He grew up idolizing historical Imperials who became legends in the First Order. Hux was convinced that the Empire delivered the galaxy from the violence of the Clone Wars, and that the galaxy once again needed to be saved from itself. This responsibility fell to the First Order, as the New Republic, in Hux's view, was too weak to prevent the inevitable chaos.[[12]](#cite_note-Visual_Dictionary-12) Hux felt nothing except contempt for the New Republic, a regime that he regarded as corrupt and illegitimate, and treacherous for secretly supporting the Resistance, another organization that the general loathed.[[42]](#cite_note-TFA_novel-42)

In addition to his views on the New Republic, Hux did not view the First Order as a continuation of the Old Empire, but rather its successor. Despite his admiration for the Empire's accomplishments[[31]](#cite_note-TLJ_Expanded_Edition-31) and his desire to relive the glories of its past,[[5]](#cite_note-DB-5) Hux believed the Empire fell due to internal flaws, whereas he saw the First Order as a more refined and pure version of what the Empire sought to become, but never achieved. This viewpoint led Hux to look down on the older generation of Imperial veterans who founded the First Order. In Hux's mind, officers such as Captain Edrison Peavey belonged to a different time, and had outlived their glory days.[[31]](#cite_note-TLJ_Expanded_Edition-31)

 [![Driven by ambition and pride in his Imperial heritage, Hux believed it was his destiny to rule the galaxy as Supreme Leader of the First Order.](data:image/gif;base64,R0lGODlhAQABAIABAAAAAP///yH5BAEAAAEALAAAAAABAAEAQAICTAEAOw%3D%3D)](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/starwars/images/d/d9/General_Hux_FOCK.png/revision/latest?cb=20190408041502) [![Driven by ambition and pride in his Imperial heritage, Hux believed it was his destiny to rule the galaxy as Supreme Leader of the First Order.](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/starwars/images/d/d9/General_Hux_FOCK.png/revision/latest/scale-to-width-down/250?cb=20190408041502)](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/starwars/images/d/d9/General_Hux_FOCK.png/revision/latest?cb=20190408041502) 

Driven by ambition and pride in his Imperial heritage, Hux believed it was his destiny to rule the galaxy as Supreme Leader of the First Order.

 

Due to his imperious nature,[[12]](#cite_note-Visual_Dictionary-12) Hux sought far greater authority than that of his official rank as general[[17]](#cite_note-TLJ_Visual_Dictionary-17) and dreamed of becoming the man who would save the galaxy from chaos.[[13]](#cite_note-TROS_Junior_Novel-13) Though his ultimate ambition was to become Supreme Leader,[[31]](#cite_note-TLJ_Expanded_Edition-31) Hux was content to wield authority over both the armies and fleets of the First Order as Grand Marshal while Snoke reigned. Undaunted by Snoke's refusal to grant this title, the young general continued to court the Supreme Leader's favor in order to promote his military career,[[17]](#cite_note-TLJ_Visual_Dictionary-17) resulting in his elevation as commander of Starkiller Base. With his ruler's support, Hux proudly took the reigns of the Starkiller project, determined to become the face of the First Order regime.[[14]](#cite_note-TROS_Visual_Dictionary-14) Hux took great care to conceal his ambitions as best he could, having to occasionally stop himself from referring to himself as "Supreme Leader Hux," as he was well aware of the First Order's extensive surveillance system. In addition to the Supreme Leader's title, Hux coveted Snoke's flagship, the *Supremacy*, and would often transfer his command ship from the *Finalizer* to the gargantuan *Mega*-class Star Dreadnought.[[17]](#cite_note-TLJ_Visual_Dictionary-17) Hux respected Snoke for building the First Order out of a group of Imperial refugees exiled to the Unknown Regions, but in private he did not consider anyone other than himself as being capable of directing the galaxy's industries and nurturing their innovations, while commanding the citizens' respect.[[31]](#cite_note-TLJ_Expanded_Edition-31)

 [![Hux was particularly attentive to his appearance, from his uniform and posture to the exact detail of his coiffed hair.](data:image/gif;base64,R0lGODlhAQABAIABAAAAAP///yH5BAEAAAEALAAAAAABAAEAQAICTAEAOw%3D%3D)](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/starwars/images/c/ca/General_hux_-_SW_Card_Trader.png/revision/latest?cb=20251110021217) [![Hux was particularly attentive to his appearance, from his uniform and posture to the exact detail of his coiffed hair.](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/starwars/images/c/ca/General_hux_-_SW_Card_Trader.png/revision/latest/scale-to-width-down/200?cb=20251110021217)](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/starwars/images/c/ca/General_hux_-_SW_Card_Trader.png/revision/latest?cb=20251110021217) 

Hux was particularly attentive to his appearance, from his uniform and posture to the exact detail of his coiffed hair.

 

Hux was obsessed with his appearance; believing it was a vital part for maintaining discipline, he would always wear a [parade uniform](/wiki/First_Order_military_uniforms "First Order military uniforms") designed to broadcast his authority as general. Even his posture was affected by this belief, as Hux would often pose in a formal parade stance.[[12]](#cite_note-Visual_Dictionary-12) In addition to his [uniform](/wiki/Clothing "Clothing") and bearing, Hux's hair was coiffed to his exacting standards.[[14]](#cite_note-TROS_Visual_Dictionary-14) Hux carried an officers version of the [SE-44C](/wiki/SE-44C_blaster_pistol "SE-44C blaster pistol") with dark plaster as opposed to the regular white [blaster pistol](/wiki/Blaster_pistol "Blaster pistol").[[12]](#cite_note-Visual_Dictionary-12)

Hux had little understanding or patience for the mystical ways of the Force.[[12]](#cite_note-Visual_Dictionary-12) While he did not doubt the Force's existence,[[31]](#cite_note-TLJ_Expanded_Edition-31) having witnessed its power in action[[11]](#cite_note-Age_of_Resistance-11) and even experienced it in the form of a punishment, Hux nonetheless regarded it as the center of an obsolete religion belonging to a bygone era. The future, Hux believed, would be ruled through industry and innovation,[[31]](#cite_note-TLJ_Expanded_Edition-31) and as such he regarded himself as a man of science and technology. Notwithstanding his lack of faith in the Force, Hux believed in himself and considered it his destiny to sit on the throne that would rule the galaxy.[[12]](#cite_note-Visual_Dictionary-12) When accompanying Kylo Ren to Mustafar, Hux became desperate to leave the volcanic planet and even considered to join the Supreme Leader's fight with the Alazmec of Winsit to hurry things up, but knowing that his fighting skills weren't as good to those of Ren and considering himself to be too valuable to risk, Hux preferred allowing Ren to do all the dirty work.[[40]](#cite_note-TROS_Expanded_Edition-40)

General Hux and Kylo Ren were rivals for the approval of Supreme Leader Snoke.

 

A rival of Kylo Ren,[[5]](#cite_note-DB-5) Hux loathed the Supreme Leader's apprentice,[[17]](#cite_note-TLJ_Visual_Dictionary-17) whom he disdainfully referred to as "Snoke's loathsome creature."[[31]](#cite_note-TLJ_Expanded_Edition-31) As they vied for the approval of the Supreme Leader, a tense competition was formed between Hux and Ren; Hux viewed Ren as petulant and self-absorbed, while Ren viewed Hux as useless without access to the mystical side of the First Order that Hux cared little for.[[12]](#cite_note-Visual_Dictionary-12) Despite their mutual contempt for each other,[[31]](#cite_note-TLJ_Expanded_Edition-31) a part of Hux respected Ren and his Force abilities, although Ren had little, if anything, to say about Hux's fighting skills.[[42]](#cite_note-TFA_novel-42) Ren also regarded Hux as a coward, a view reinforced when Hux, on one occasion, turned and fled from a hostile animal, leaving his rival to defend himself while he hid under the cover of tall grass.[[11]](#cite_note-Age_of_Resistance-11) In loathing Ren, Hux took pleasure in his adversary's failures and Snoke's anger with his apprentice. The fact that Ren required rescuing from Starkiller Base after losing to a lightsaber novice particularly delighted the young general.[[17]](#cite_note-TLJ_Visual_Dictionary-17)

His hatred for Ren extended to his followers, the Knights of Ren, despising them for their unkempt appearance as well as their tendency to tread dirt into the polished halls of First Order capital ships.[[14]](#cite_note-TROS_Visual_Dictionary-14) By the end of the war, Hux considered Ren to be a mindless dog and blunt instrument in combat. Another one of Ren's traits Hux greatly disliked was the Supreme Leader's disordered hair, to the point Hux was glad that Ren repaired his mask so he would be able to no longer see what he deemed an indignant detail a good leader shouldn't have. Hux swore himself that the first thing he would do if once he could take the role of Supreme Leader would be ordering Ren to cut off his hair.[[40]](#cite_note-TROS_Expanded_Edition-40)

 [![Proud of the training methods pioneered by his father, Hux had total confidence in the loyalty and skills of First Order stormtroopers.](data:image/gif;base64,R0lGODlhAQABAIABAAAAAP///yH5BAEAAAEALAAAAAABAAEAQAICTAEAOw%3D%3D)](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/starwars/images/e/e5/First_Order_Military.jpg/revision/latest?cb=20171209095035) [![Proud of the training methods pioneered by his father, Hux had total confidence in the loyalty and skills of First Order stormtroopers.](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/starwars/images/e/e5/First_Order_Military.jpg/revision/latest/scale-to-width-down/250?cb=20171209095035)](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/starwars/images/e/e5/First_Order_Military.jpg/revision/latest?cb=20171209095035) 

Proud of the training methods pioneered by his father, Hux had total confidence in the loyalty and skills of First Order stormtroopers.

 

Hux was raised in the military branch of the First Order.[[14]](#cite_note-TROS_Visual_Dictionary-14) As a general, he inherited the stormtrooper training regimen pioneered by his father, Brendol, whose views on military training influenced the new generation of soldiers that came to serve the First Order. Hux had complete confidence in his father's idea that stormtroopers trained through vivid simulations made the most loyal troopers.[[12]](#cite_note-Visual_Dictionary-12) Openly proud to be the inheritor of his father's legacy, Hux refused to stand by while Ren criticized the stormtroopers that had been trained under two generations of the Hux family. Having invested a lifetime in their training, Hux considered his stormtroopers as examples of the perfect warrior—a soldier trained from birth and programmed for absolute loyalty. Though baffled by the treasonous actions of FN-2187, Hux believed that the renegade trooper was an abnormality whose isolated case had no bearing on the reliability of the other stormtroopers that obeyed their orders without question.[[42]](#cite_note-TFA_novel-42)

 [![A remorseless man, Hux became the destroyer of worlds when he commanded the destruction of the Hosnian system, annihilating billions with a single shot.](data:image/gif;base64,R0lGODlhAQABAIABAAAAAP///yH5BAEAAAEALAAAAAABAAEAQAICTAEAOw%3D%3D)](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/starwars/images/c/ca/Hux_Starkiller_Speech.png/revision/latest?cb=20170610004141) [![A remorseless man, Hux became the destroyer of worlds when he commanded the destruction of the Hosnian system, annihilating billions with a single shot.](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/starwars/images/c/ca/Hux_Starkiller_Speech.png/revision/latest/scale-to-width-down/250?cb=20170610004141)](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/starwars/images/c/ca/Hux_Starkiller_Speech.png/revision/latest?cb=20170610004141) 

A remorseless man, Hux became the destroyer of worlds when he commanded the destruction of the Hosnian system, annihilating billions with a single shot.

 

Ultimately, Hux was a man of no scruples who craved power above all. He was utterly remorseless,[[17]](#cite_note-TLJ_Visual_Dictionary-17) possessing a predisposition to the act of killing that existed within him since early childhood.[[9]](#cite_note-Empire's_End-9) As he rose to power, Hux dispatched many enemies and would-be foes, including his own father, the elder General Brendol Hux. As the commanding officer of Starkiller Base, the younger Hux was responsible for the deaths of billions.[[17]](#cite_note-TLJ_Visual_Dictionary-17) He took pride in the weapon that the First Order had built into a planet, marveling at its destructive power and was certain that the new galactic war would be won with the firing of a single shot.[[12]](#cite_note-Visual_Dictionary-12) Throughout his career, Hux utilized assassination and sabotage as methods for eliminating his enemies, both within the First Order and without, although he rarely carried out such tactics on his own, preferring to have his allies or subordinates do the work for him.[[17]](#cite_note-TLJ_Visual_Dictionary-17) However, Hux made an exception for Admiral Brooks. Having never forgotten the way Brooks treated him as a child, Hux shot the elder officer in the shoulder and proceeded to [torture](/wiki/Torture "Torture") him, ignoring the Brooks' pleas for mercy before finally executing the injured, unarmed man.[[11]](#cite_note-Age_of_Resistance-11)

 [![Hux's viciousness was rooted in his childhood, when he was abused by his father and mocked for his illegitimacy.](data:image/gif;base64,R0lGODlhAQABAIABAAAAAP///yH5BAEAAAEALAAAAAABAAEAQAICTAEAOw%3D%3D)](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/starwars/images/9/9b/ArmitageAndBrendol-AORHux1.jpg/revision/latest?cb=20200218103828) [![Hux's viciousness was rooted in his childhood, when he was abused by his father and mocked for his illegitimacy.](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/starwars/images/9/9b/ArmitageAndBrendol-AORHux1.jpg/revision/latest/scale-to-width-down/250?cb=20200218103828)](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/starwars/images/9/9b/ArmitageAndBrendol-AORHux1.jpg/revision/latest?cb=20200218103828) 

Hux's viciousness was rooted in his childhood, when he was abused by his father and mocked for his illegitimacy.

 

Beneath the surface of Hux's pride and ambition existed a burning shame that started with the abuse he endured under his father's care. Though he also enjoyed a life of privilege due to Brendol's elevated position in the early First Order, young Armitage was the object of Brendol's scorn and therefore lived in fear of his father. Brendol considered his son pathetic, weak, sniveling and utterly useless.[[11]](#cite_note-Age_of_Resistance-11) Hux likewise loathed his father and regarded Brendol as an "old bastard" whose death gave him a sense of relief.[[18]](#cite_note-Phasma-18) Nevertheless, Hux was proud of his ties to the Empire, being the son of an Imperial Academy commandant.[[12]](#cite_note-Visual_Dictionary-12) However, he was forced to live with the shame[[11]](#cite_note-Age_of_Resistance-11) of being his father's bastard son by a kitchen woman, rather than a legitimate heir by Brendol's lawful wife, Maratelle.[[10]](#cite_note-LD-10) For all of his imperiousness and sense of manifest destiny,[[12]](#cite_note-Visual_Dictionary-12) Hux never forgot his origins—he was the unloved, illegitimate son of a man who hated him—and he never forgave his abusers.[[11]](#cite_note-Age_of_Resistance-11) As an adult, Hux possessed a delicate ego that was easily wounded by mockery, such as Poe Dameron's deliberate mispronunciation of his name as "General Hugs."[[17]](#cite_note-TLJ_Visual_Dictionary-17)

 [![Hux continued to despise Ren while feigning loyalty to the Force-sensitive warrior.](data:image/gif;base64,R0lGODlhAQABAIABAAAAAP///yH5BAEAAAEALAAAAAABAAEAQAICTAEAOw%3D%3D)](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/starwars/images/2/2c/Hux_Force_choked.png/revision/latest?cb=20190324161251) [![Hux continued to despise Ren while feigning loyalty to the Force-sensitive warrior.](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/starwars/images/2/2c/Hux_Force_choked.png/revision/latest/scale-to-width-down/250?cb=20190324161251)](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/starwars/images/2/2c/Hux_Force_choked.png/revision/latest?cb=20190324161251) 

Hux continued to despise Ren while feigning loyalty to the Force-sensitive warrior.

 

Vaunting the technological might of Starkiller Base was the last time when Hux felt certain about anything. He watched helplessly as his fortunes changed for the worse, with Ren ascending to the rank of Supreme Leader in the wake of Snoke's unforeseen demise, and his other rival Allegiant General Enric Pryde gaining greater favor in the new regime at Hux's expense. His deviousness and years of jockeying for power worked against the general, as the new Supreme Leader considered Hux untrustworthy and entrusted him with nothing of importance. After Ren seized power, Hux lost much of the zeal he possessed under Snoke's reign, despite managing to survive under his rival's rule. By then, Hux no longer aspired to the title of Grand Marshal, as that role had been essentially taken by the Allegiant General.[[14]](#cite_note-TROS_Visual_Dictionary-14)

He continued to observe military protocol, but no longer did so in spirit—and although he was given a seat on the Supreme Council, Hux maintained an uncharacteristically silent presence,[[14]](#cite_note-TROS_Visual_Dictionary-14) being careful not to provoke Ren's temper[[4]](#cite_note-Episode_IX-4) as he had done in the past.[[11]](#cite_note-Age_of_Resistance-11) Without Snoke's protection, Hux began addressing Ren with the deference due to the latter's power and rank.[[4]](#cite_note-Episode_IX-4) In private, however, he strongly disapproved of Ren's leadership of the First Order as well as his handling of the war, and was convinced that Ren had gone mad for diverting his attention from the Resistance to investigate Darth Sidious' return.[[14]](#cite_note-TROS_Visual_Dictionary-14) In particular, he thought that Ren was turning the regime into his own personal military to carry out whatever crusade he cared about that day.[[13]](#cite_note-TROS_Junior_Novel-13)

 [![Hux used his position in the First Order to spy for the Resistance, determined to see Ren lose the war.](data:image/gif;base64,R0lGODlhAQABAIABAAAAAP///yH5BAEAAAEALAAAAAABAAEAQAICTAEAOw%3D%3D)](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/starwars/images/e/e2/Hux-with-stormtroopers.png/revision/latest?cb=20211202001737) [![Hux used his position in the First Order to spy for the Resistance, determined to see Ren lose the war.](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/starwars/images/e/e2/Hux-with-stormtroopers.png/revision/latest/scale-to-width-down/250?cb=20211202001737)](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/starwars/images/e/e2/Hux-with-stormtroopers.png/revision/latest?cb=20211202001737) 

Hux used his position in the First Order to spy for the Resistance, determined to see Ren lose the war.

 

Even as he watched his career trajectory veer wildly off course, Hux did not completely abandon his scheming nature.[[14]](#cite_note-TROS_Visual_Dictionary-14) In the end, the general who led the vanguard of the First Order's invasion of the galaxy committed treason by spying for the Resistance. However, he was not motivated by regret for his actions or a newfound belief in the Resistance's cause. Hux was remorseless to the end; all he had left was his rivalry with Ren, whose reign he sought to undermine until it ended in defeat.[[4]](#cite_note-Episode_IX-4) Hux thought his actions against Ren were the greatest act of loyalty he could carry out for the First Order, even if to others they were the acts of a traitor, and did so in the name of protecting everything he had worked to build from falling apart at Ren's hands.[[13]](#cite_note-TROS_Junior_Novel-13)

## Skills and abilities[]

:   "*Given you found his mother in a kitchen, you'd think your illegitimate son could at least serve a drink, Commandant Hux.*"  
    "*Unfortunately, I've yet to find anything Armitage isn't utterly useless at, Admiral Brooks.*"
:   ―Brooks and Brendol Hux[[11]](#cite_note-Age_of_Resistance-11)

 [![General Hux oversaw the completion of multiple First Order projects, from training a new stormtrooper generation to the creation of Starkiller Base.](data:image/gif;base64,R0lGODlhAQABAIABAAAAAP///yH5BAEAAAEALAAAAAABAAEAQAICTAEAOw%3D%3D)](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/starwars/images/e/eb/ArmitageHux-TFApromo.png/revision/latest?cb=20230221004911) [![General Hux oversaw the completion of multiple First Order projects, from training a new stormtrooper generation to the creation of Starkiller Base.](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/starwars/images/e/eb/ArmitageHux-TFApromo.png/revision/latest/scale-to-width-down/250?cb=20230221004911)](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/starwars/images/e/eb/ArmitageHux-TFApromo.png/revision/latest?cb=20230221004911) 

General Hux oversaw the completion of multiple First Order projects, from training a new stormtrooper generation to the creation of Starkiller Base.

 

As a child, Armitage Hux was considered weak and useless by his father, Brendol Hux, who found his son to be unreliable at virtually everything.[[11]](#cite_note-Age_of_Resistance-11) Unlike Hux's father, however, Gallius Rax saw Hux's usefulness to his plans for the future, in particular the next generation of soldiers, the leadership of which he entrusted to Hux.[[9]](#cite_note-Empire's_End-9) Supreme Leader Snoke also saw Hux as someone who was useful to his plans.[[11]](#cite_note-Age_of_Resistance-11) Therefore, he supported the general's career despite Kylo Ren's opposition to a "rabid cur" occupying a position of significance in the First Order.[[30]](#cite_note-Episode_VIII-30)

Hux was precise and methodical in his rise to power as well as his approach to warfare.[[17]](#cite_note-TLJ_Visual_Dictionary-17) He possessed a combination of patience and inner shame, both of which motivated his desire to gain greater power at the expense of his rivals, including his own father whom he had assassinated.[[11]](#cite_note-Age_of_Resistance-11) Possessing an appreciation for superior technology, Hux pioneered the invention of cutting-edge technologies such as the Starkiller Base superweapon and active hyperspace tracking.[[17]](#cite_note-TLJ_Visual_Dictionary-17) He was also familiar with the operating systems of a *Resurgent*-class Star Destroyer and Starkiller Base.[[8]](#cite_note-Episode_VII-8)

## Equipment[]

General Hux wore a charcoal grey general's uniform, black gloves, a polished officer's buckle, traditional flared-hip breeches, and insulated boots. He also wore a crested command cap emblazoned with the insignia of the First Order and a [gaberwool](/wiki/Gaberwool "Gaberwool") officer's greatcoat. Hux believed that a tidy appearance was essential to maintaining discipline. When addressing his men, he wore a parade uniform designed to broadcast his authority as a First Order General.[[12]](#cite_note-Visual_Dictionary-12)

After the Battle of Starkiller Base, Hux carried a customized version of a [SE-44C blaster pistol](/wiki/SE-44C_blaster_pistol "SE-44C blaster pistol") in a cross draw holster and a monomolecular dagger hidden in his sleeve.[[17]](#cite_note-TLJ_Visual_Dictionary-17)

## Behind the scenes[]

### Development[]

:   "*I think General Hux would just like a little bit more order in the universe. Whatever it takes is where he's at.*"
:   ―Domhnall Gleeson, on General Hux[[43]](#cite_note-People-43)

 [![Hux's speech to the First Order has been compared to Adolf Hitler addressing a Nuremberg Rally.](data:image/gif;base64,R0lGODlhAQABAIABAAAAAP///yH5BAEAAAEALAAAAAABAAEAQAICTAEAOw%3D%3D)](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/starwars/images/b/b8/FirstOrder.png/revision/latest?cb=20150811001647) [![Hux's speech to the First Order has been compared to Adolf Hitler addressing a Nuremberg Rally.](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/starwars/images/b/b8/FirstOrder.png/revision/latest/scale-to-width-down/280?cb=20150811001647)](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/starwars/images/b/b8/FirstOrder.png/revision/latest?cb=20150811001647) 

Hux's speech to the First Order has been compared to Adolf Hitler addressing a Nuremberg Rally.

 

The character of General Hux was created for the [2015](/wiki/2015 "2015") film [*Star Wars*: Episode VII *The Force Awakens*](/wiki/Star_Wars:_Episode_VII_The_Force_Awakens "Star Wars: Episode VII The Force Awakens"), in which he is portrayed by [Domhnall Gleeson](/wiki/Domhnall_Gleeson "Domhnall Gleeson").[[44]](#cite_note-Name_Confirmation-44) Gleeson was cast in the movie in [April](/wiki/April "April") [2014](/wiki/2014 "2014"), just one day before appearing in a publicity photo of the cast doing a table read of the script. He afterward rented a screening room in his hometown of [Dublin](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dublin "wikipedia:Dublin"), [Ireland](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ireland "wikipedia:Ireland"), and watched all three installments of the [original *Star Wars* trilogy](/wiki/Original_trilogy "Original trilogy") to prepare for shooting the new film[[45]](#cite_note-Independent_Article-45)—he has described arriving on the set of a *[Star Wars](/wiki/Star_Wars "Star Wars")* movie on his first day of shooting as a breathtaking, "pinch me" moment.[[46]](#cite_note-We_Got_This_Covered-46) Details of Gleeson's character, including the name General Hux, were first revealed to the public at the [San Diego Comic-Con](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/San_Diego_Comic-Con_International "wikipedia:San Diego Comic-Con International")[[47]](#cite_note-Cinema_Blend-47) in [July](/wiki/July "July") 2015.[[44]](#cite_note-Name_Confirmation-44)

While developing Hux's character, Gleeson and the film's production team posited that he was an obsessive character who does not get a lot of sleep. They created his look with that in mind: pale with dark eyes, and dead-set on his goals.[[48]](#cite_note-LA_Times_Hux-48) *The Force Awakens* director [J.J. Abrams](/wiki/J.J._Abrams "J.J. Abrams") has called Hux a merciless character,[[46]](#cite_note-We_Got_This_Covered-46) and Gleeson has described him as someone who enjoys having authority and holding onto it at all costs.[[48]](#cite_note-LA_Times_Hux-48) He has also stated that Hux "would just like a little bit more order in the universe" and would do whatever it takes to achieve that order. In the lead up to the film's release, he joked that Hux was obviously an evil character due to his British accent, a trait shared by numerous *Star Wars* villains.[[49]](#cite_note-People_Magazine_December_2015-49) Gleeson was excited to play such a character, as it was unlike any acting role he had previously done[[46]](#cite_note-We_Got_This_Covered-46) and allowed him to explore the notion of authority.[[48]](#cite_note-LA_Times_Hux-48) [StarWars.com](/wiki/StarWars.com "StarWars.com") then confirmed that Gleeson was to return in [*Star Wars*: Episode VIII *The Last Jedi*](/wiki/Star_Wars:_Episode_VIII_The_Last_Jedi "Star Wars: Episode VIII The Last Jedi").[[50]](#cite_note-Episode_VIII_now_filming-50)

 [![Star Wars Insider 192's parody of Hux's speech](data:image/gif;base64,R0lGODlhAQABAIABAAAAAP///yH5BAEAAAEALAAAAAABAAEAQAICTAEAOw%3D%3D)](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/starwars/images/e/ee/Insider-192-Hux-comic.png/revision/latest?cb=20220831215055) [![Star Wars Insider 192's parody of Hux's speech](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/starwars/images/e/ee/Insider-192-Hux-comic.png/revision/latest/scale-to-width-down/300?cb=20220831215055)](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/starwars/images/e/ee/Insider-192-Hux-comic.png/revision/latest?cb=20220831215055) 

*Star Wars Insider* 192's parody of Hux's speech

 

During filming of *The Force Awakens*, several different versions were shot of a scene where Hux addresses the troops of the First Order, as J.J. Abrams hoped to try it at various levels of intensity. He ultimately "wanted it big," and he and screenplay writer [Lawrence Kasdan](/wiki/Lawrence_Kasdan "Lawrence Kasdan") believed that it was Hux's major moment in the movie. Although the amount of stormtrooper extras present for the scene made Gleeson nervous, he also felt like it gave him a sense of the number of people his speech was intended to affect.[[51]](#cite_note-Yahoo_UK_Video-51) The speech has been compared to [Adolf Hitler](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Adolf_Hitler "wikipedia:Adolf Hitler") speaking at a [Nuremberg Rally](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nuremberg_rallies "wikipedia:Nuremberg rallies").[[51]](#cite_note-Yahoo_UK_Video-51)[[52]](#cite_note-Star_Wars_in_the_Classroom-52)[[53]](#cite_note-rogerebert.com_The_Force_Awakens-53) [*Star Wars Insider* 192](/wiki/Star_Wars_Insider_192 "Star Wars Insider 192") parodied Hux's speech by showing it annoying the stormtroopers.[[54]](#cite_note-192_Launchpad-54)

### Continuity[]

:   "*I'd imagined Zare catching sight of a red-haired baby in a hover-pram pushed by a sinister nanny droid, but that wound up feeling like a reference for a reference's sake, which I've come to believe undermines storytelling instead of supporting it.*"
:   ―Jason Fry, on writing *Servants of the Empire: The Secret Academy*[[55]](#cite_note-sw.com_Inside_the_Secret_Academy-55)

Prior to *The Force Awakens*'s release, a character named Brendol Hux was introduced in *[Servants of the Empire: The Secret Academy](/wiki/Servants_of_the_Empire:_The_Secret_Academy "Servants of the Empire: The Secret Academy")*, a young readers' book by [Jason Fry](/wiki/Jason_Fry "Jason Fry") that serves as a tie-in to the television program *[Star Wars Rebels](/wiki/Star_Wars_Rebels "Star Wars Rebels")*. In the book, Brendol Hux reveals his ambition to create an army of soldiers loyal to the Galactic Empire from birth.[[56]](#cite_note-The_Secret_Academy-56) A StarWars.com [Databank](/wiki/Databank_(2014-) "Databank (2014-)") entry later revealed that Brendol Hux is General Hux's father,[[5]](#cite_note-DB-5) and *[Star Wars: The Force Awakens: The Visual Dictionary](/wiki/Star_Wars:_The_Force_Awakens:_The_Visual_Dictionary "Star Wars: The Force Awakens: The Visual Dictionary")* went on to confirm that Hux's army of stormtroopers in the film—trained from infancy to be loyal to the First Order—are an extension of his father's group, the Commandant's Cadets.[[12]](#cite_note-Visual_Dictionary-12)

Fry originally conceived of the cadets to advance a plot line in his story, but he was struggling to find them a goal or a purpose that would eventually come into fruition. The idea to connect them with General Hux came in [November](/wiki/November "November") 2014 when he met with [Lucasfilm](/wiki/Lucasfilm "Lucasfilm") Ltd. about another project, *[Star Wars: The Force Awakens: Incredible Cross-Sections](/wiki/Star_Wars:_The_Force_Awakens:_Incredible_Cross-Sections "Star Wars: The Force Awakens: Incredible Cross-Sections")*, and was privy to concept art, stills, and a synopsis of the upcoming movie. He then proposed to [Pablo Hidalgo](/wiki/Pablo_Hidalgo "Pablo Hidalgo") of the [Lucasfilm Story Group](/wiki/Lucasfilm_Story_Group "Lucasfilm Story Group") that the idea of training children to be stormtroopers began with Hux's father in the era of *Star Wars Rebels*, and when Hidalgo ran it up the chain of command, the answer came back in the positive. Fry considered inserting a "red-haired baby" Hux into his book, being cared for by a [sinister nanny droid](/wiki/DDM-38 "DDM-38"), but he ultimately felt that it was a reference for the sake of a reference and cut it from the final draft.[[55]](#cite_note-sw.com_Inside_the_Secret_Academy-55)

Domhnall Gleeson's brother [Brian Gleeson](/wiki/Brian_Gleeson "Brian Gleeson")[[57]](#cite_note-Independent-57) played the character's father[[10]](#cite_note-LD-10) [Brendol Hux](/wiki/Brendol_Hux "Brendol Hux") in *[Star Wars: The Mandalorian](/wiki/Star_Wars:_The_Mandalorian "Star Wars: The Mandalorian")* episode *[Chapter 23: The Spies](/wiki/Chapter_23:_The_Spies "Chapter 23: The Spies")*.[[58]](#cite_note-Chapter_23:_The_Spies-58)

General Hux was introduced as a playable character of the same name in the 2015 mobile game *[Star Wars: Galaxy of Heroes](/wiki/Star_Wars:_Galaxy_of_Heroes "Star Wars: Galaxy of Heroes")* in 2019, which features characters and ships from both the canon and Legends continuities.[[59]](#cite_note-GoH-59)
