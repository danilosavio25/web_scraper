Darth Sidious coveted the secret of immortality, and desired to rule the galaxy forever.

 

**Immortality**, or **eternal life**, was a state in which a living being could live without ever [dying](/wiki/Death "Death"). [Darth Plagueis](/wiki/Darth_Plagueis "Darth Plagueis"), a [Sith Lord](/wiki/Sith_Lord "Sith Lord") in the [years](/wiki/Standard_year "Standard year") prior to the [Clone Wars](/wiki/Clone_Wars "Clone Wars"), lusted for immortality. According to his [apprentice](/wiki/Sith_apprentice "Sith apprentice"), [Darth Sidious](/wiki/Darth_Sidious "Darth Sidious"), Plagueis manipulated the [midi-chlorians](/wiki/Midi-chlorian "Midi-chlorian") to both create life and seek immortality. Those who followed the [light side of the Force](/wiki/Light_side_of_the_Force "Light side of the Force") and had the appropriate training could achieve a form of immortality by [retaining their consciousness](/wiki/Force_spirit "Force spirit") after their physical deaths. [Qui-Gon Jinn](/wiki/Qui-Gon_Jinn "Qui-Gon Jinn") rediscovered this ability and, in turn, taught [Yoda](/wiki/Yoda "Yoda") and his apprentice [Obi-Wan Kenobi](/wiki/Obi-Wan_Kenobi "Obi-Wan Kenobi") how to attain it. The redeemed [Anakin Skywalker](/wiki/Anakin_Skywalker "Anakin Skywalker") accomplished the state of immortality, as well as his children, [Luke Skywalker](/wiki/Luke_Skywalker "Luke Skywalker") and [Leia Skywalker Organa Solo](/wiki/Leia_Skywalker_Organa_Solo "Leia Skywalker Organa Solo"), years later.

[Darth](/wiki/Darth "Darth") Sidious was able to cheat his upcoming death by [transferring](/wiki/Transference "Transference") his spirit into a [clone](/wiki/Cloning "Cloning") body while falling into the [reactor](/wiki/Reactor "Reactor") core of the [second Death Star](/wiki/DS-2_Death_Star_II_Mobile_Battle_Station "DS-2 Death Star II Mobile Battle Station"). He later sought to prolong his existence by futilely manipulating his grandchild [Rey](/wiki/Rey_Skywalker "Rey Skywalker") into killing him so he could live further by possessing her body.

## Techniques[]

### Healing with the Force[]

:   [![icon](data:image/gif;base64,R0lGODlhAQABAIABAAAAAP///yH5BAEAAAEALAAAAAABAAEAQAICTAEAOw%3D%3D)](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/starwars/images/0/04/Blue_Glass_Arrow.svg/revision/latest?cb=20090726033133) Main article: [Force healing](/wiki/Force_healing "Force healing")

:   "*The dark side of the Force is a pathway to many abilities some consider to be unnatural.*"
:   ―Darth Sidious, to Anakin Skywalker[[1]](#cite_note-Episode_III-1)

 [![Anakin Skywalker pledged his loyalty to Darth Sidious, believing the Sith Lord held the key to conquering death.](data:image/gif;base64,R0lGODlhAQABAIABAAAAAP///yH5BAEAAAEALAAAAAABAAEAQAICTAEAOw%3D%3D)](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/starwars/images/4/42/SithTeachings-Convergence.png/revision/latest?cb=20201129074930) [![Anakin Skywalker pledged his loyalty to Darth Sidious, believing the Sith Lord held the key to conquering death.](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/starwars/images/4/42/SithTeachings-Convergence.png/revision/latest/scale-to-width-down/250?cb=20201129074930)](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/starwars/images/4/42/SithTeachings-Convergence.png/revision/latest?cb=20201129074930) 

Anakin Skywalker pledged his loyalty to Darth Sidious, believing the Sith Lord held the key to conquering death.

 

In [19 BBY](/wiki/19_BBY "19 BBY"), having completed his plan to turn [Anakin Skywalker](/wiki/Anakin_Skywalker "Anakin Skywalker") to the [dark side of the Force](/wiki/Dark_side_of_the_Force "Dark side of the Force"), [Darth Sidious](/wiki/Darth_Sidious "Darth Sidious") claimed his late master [Darth Plagueis](/wiki/Darth_Plagueis "Darth Plagueis") to have gained the ability to cheat [death](/wiki/Death "Death") and to preserve the lives of the ones he loved by influencing [midi-chlorians](/wiki/Midi-chlorian "Midi-chlorian"). Sidious also stated that his master was slain in his sleep after the knowledge was passed on to his [apprentice](/wiki/Sith_apprentice "Sith apprentice"). Finally, however, he retracted that statement and admitted he had not attained the deceased Plagueis' knowledge, insisting that it could be discovered if Skywalker and he worked together.[[1]](#cite_note-Episode_III-1)

While Sidious shared the [Tragedy of Darth Plagueis](/wiki/Tragedy_of_Darth_Plagueis_the_Wise "Tragedy of Darth Plagueis the Wise") with Skywalker, he asserted that the dark side of the Force could provide one with abilities deemed by some as contrary with nature.[[1]](#cite_note-Episode_III-1) He maintained that stance in [35 ABY](/wiki/35_ABY "35 ABY") shortly after the [broadcast](/wiki/Palpatine%27s_broadcast "Palpatine's broadcast") revealing his forthcoming revenge to [the galaxy](/wiki/The_galaxy "The galaxy").[[2]](#cite_note-Episode_IX-2)

In addition to Plagueis' skills, which allegedly enabled him to save people from death, there existed several methods which allowed one [Force-sensitive](/wiki/Force-sensitive "Force-sensitive") to use [Force drain](/wiki/Force_drain "Force drain") or [healing](/wiki/Force_healing "Force healing") to resuscitate another through [resurrection](/wiki/Resurrection "Resurrection"). The individuals wielding those skills included Anakin Skywalker—who resurrected [Ahsoka Tano](/wiki/Ahsoka_Tano "Ahsoka Tano") by extracting the [Daughter](/wiki/Daughter "Daughter")'s energy as she died[[3]](#cite_note-Altar_of_Mortis-3)—and Ben Solo, who resurrected [Rey](/wiki/Rey_Skywalker "Rey Skywalker"), his co-participant in the [dyad of the Force](/wiki/Force_dyad "Force dyad"), at the cost of his own life.[[2]](#cite_note-Episode_IX-2)

### Force spirits[]

:   [![icon](data:image/gif;base64,R0lGODlhAQABAIABAAAAAP///yH5BAEAAAEALAAAAAABAAEAQAICTAEAOw%3D%3D)](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/starwars/images/0/04/Blue_Glass_Arrow.svg/revision/latest?cb=20090726033133) Main article: [Force spirit](/wiki/Force_spirit "Force spirit")

:   "*You can't win, Darth. If you strike me down, I shall become more powerful than you can possibly imagine.*"
:   ―Obi-Wan Kenobi to Darth Vader, shortly prior to his transformation into a spirit[[4]](#cite_note-Episode_IV-4)

 [![Some Jedi achieved immortality as Force spirits, their consciousness preserved in the Force.](data:image/gif;base64,R0lGODlhAQABAIABAAAAAP///yH5BAEAAAEALAAAAAABAAEAQAICTAEAOw%3D%3D)](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/starwars/images/5/55/JediGhosts-ROTJ.png/revision/latest?cb=20201008104501) [![Some Jedi achieved immortality as Force spirits, their consciousness preserved in the Force.](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/starwars/images/5/55/JediGhosts-ROTJ.png/revision/latest/scale-to-width-down/250?cb=20201008104501)](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/starwars/images/5/55/JediGhosts-ROTJ.png/revision/latest?cb=20201008104501) 

Some Jedi achieved immortality as Force spirits, their consciousness preserved in the Force.

 

Every living being became one with [the Force](/wiki/The_Force "The Force") during the process of dying.[[1]](#cite_note-Episode_III-1) Once transformed from the [living](/wiki/Living_Force "Living Force") into the [cosmic Force](/wiki/Cosmic_Force "Cosmic Force"), the majority of them lost their consciousnesses and [sentience](/wiki/Sentience "Sentience"), with only a handful gaining the ability to preserve both their self-awareness and physical appearance, occasionally manifested in the form of bluish [Force spirits](/wiki/Force_spirit "Force spirit"). The Jedi did not recognize that possibility up to the final days of the Republic.[[5]](#cite_note-Sacrifice-5)

The notable individuals to master the technique included the [Force Priestesses](/wiki/Force_Priestesses "Force Priestesses")[[6]](#cite_note-Voices-6) and the [Shaman of the Whills](/wiki/Shaman_of_the_Whills "Shaman of the Whills").[[7]](#cite_note-Jinn_Gallery-7) The latter relayed the knowledge to Jedi Master [Qui-Gon Jinn](/wiki/Qui-Gon_Jinn "Qui-Gon Jinn"), but since his training was incomplete as of his death in [32 BBY](/wiki/32_BBY "32 BBY"), his body did not vanish.[[8]](#cite_note-Episode_I-8) The Jedi was initially capable of communicating as a voice only.[[6]](#cite_note-Voices-6) Even so, he completed his training *post mortem* by [9 BBY](/wiki/9_BBY "9 BBY"),[[9]](#cite_note-Kenobi_Date-9) at which point he showed himself to Obi-Wan Kenobi during the latter's exile on [Tatooine](/wiki/Tatooine "Tatooine").[[10]](#cite_note-Part_VI-10) In addition, during the [Clone Wars](/wiki/Clone_Wars "Clone Wars"), Jinn's spirit appeared in a vision to Anakin Skywalker.[[7]](#cite_note-Jinn_Gallery-7) Some, including Skywalker,[[5]](#cite_note-Sacrifice-5) came to believe that experience to be an actual spiritual manifestation of the late Jedi.[[11]](#cite_note-Ghosts_of_Mortis-11)

Foreseeing [Yoda](/wiki/Yoda "Yoda")'s relevant role in training the one who would preserve the balance of the Force, Jinn communicated with the [Jedi Master](/wiki/Jedi_Master "Jedi Master") during the final days of the Clone Wars and instructed him on his way to the [Wellspring of Life](/wiki/Wellspring_of_Life "Wellspring of Life"), where he was supposed to undergo a preliminary training supervised by the Force Priestesses. With Yoda's suitability proven, Jinn promised to conduct the training onward.[[5]](#cite_note-Sacrifice-5) Yoda himself passed on the knowledge to [Kenobi](/wiki/Obi-Wan_Kenobi "Obi-Wan Kenobi") and taught him to communicate with Jinn.[[1]](#cite_note-Episode_III-1) Both Yoda and Kenobi were soon among a handful of the last Jedi survivors and both preserved their consciousness in [0 ABY](/wiki/0_ABY "0 ABY") and [4 ABY](/wiki/4_ABY "4 ABY"), respectively.[[4]](#cite_note-Episode_IV-4)[[12]](#cite_note-Episode_VI-12) In 4 ABY, Skywalker, the [Chosen One](/wiki/Chosen_One "Chosen One"), who once fell to the dark side and became [Darth](/wiki/Darth "Darth") Vader, was redeemed and made immortal by Yoda and Kenobi for his final heroic deeds before the three of them appeared to his son Luke on [Endor](/wiki/Endor "Endor").[[12]](#cite_note-Episode_VI-12)

Luke became another Jedi to reach immortality, his corpse vanishing on the planet of [Ahch-To](/wiki/Ahch-To "Ahch-To") in [34 ABY](/wiki/34_ABY "34 ABY").[[13]](#cite_note-Episode_VIII-13) The following year, Skywalker was able to appear to Rey, a manifestation that coincided with his younger twin sister [Leia Skywalker Organa Solo](/wiki/Leia_Skywalker_Organa_Solo "Leia Skywalker Organa Solo")'s death, whose corpse did not disappear until her son Ben Solo's did.[[2]](#cite_note-Episode_IX-2)

The individuals whose bodies vanished also included the [Father](/wiki/Father_(Mortis) "Father (Mortis)")[[11]](#cite_note-Ghosts_of_Mortis-11) and [Ben](/wiki/Ben_Solo "Ben Solo"). The latter could communicate with living beings, and his co-participant in the [dyad of the Force](/wiki/Force_dyad "Force dyad"), Rey, could hear his voice while eyewitnessing his body's disappearance.[[14]](#cite_note-Episode_IX_novel-14)

 [![A ](data:image/gif;base64,R0lGODlhAQABAIABAAAAAP///yH5BAEAAAEALAAAAAABAAEAQAICTAEAOw%3D%3D)](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/starwars/images/f/f9/Nightsister_Zombie_SoH.png/revision/latest?cb=20190428041317) [![A ](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/starwars/images/f/f9/Nightsister_Zombie_SoH.png/revision/latest/scale-to-width-down/200?cb=20190428041317)](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/starwars/images/f/f9/Nightsister_Zombie_SoH.png/revision/latest?cb=20190428041317)

[Mother](/wiki/Mother_(title) "Mother (title)") [Talzin](/wiki/Talzin "Talzin") was blessed by an unnaturally long life due to magick.[[15]](#cite_note-TSWB-15) Via certain aspects of the dark side combined with their mystical magick, the [Nightsisters](/wiki/Nightsisters "Nightsisters") were known to manifest as greenish phantoms in their post-mortem existence. In [19 BBY](/wiki/19_BBY "19 BBY"), while [Asajj Ventress](/wiki/Asajj_Ventress "Asajj Ventress")' body was being buried on [Dathomir](/wiki/Dathomir "Dathomir"), they could be heard whispering and murmuring something to one another until she somehow returned to life.[[16]](#cite_note-Dark_Disciple-16) During the [Imperial Era](/wiki/Imperial_Era "Imperial Era"), [Maul](/wiki/Maul "Maul") allied with their spirits via his promise to provide them with physical bodies to serve as new vessels. In return, they consented to merge his mind with that of [Ezra Bridger](/wiki/Ezra_Bridger "Ezra Bridger"), and when the ritual was complete, the ghosts possessed his Master [Kanan Jarrus](/wiki/Kanan_Jarrus "Kanan Jarrus") and his comrade [Sabine Wren](/wiki/Sabine_Wren "Sabine Wren"), even though Bridger liberated them by destroying the altar, as they could only appear within a short distance from it.[[17]](#cite_note-Vision_and_Voices-17)

In addition, the Nightsisters could arrange a special ritual to resuscitate the dead in the form of [zombies](/wiki/Nightsister_Zombie "Nightsister Zombie").[[18]](#cite_note-Massacre-18)

After Jarrus died, his will was conveyed onward by a [Loth-wolf](/wiki/Loth-wolf "Loth-wolf") identifying with the name [Dume](/wiki/Dume "Dume").[[22]](#cite_note-DUME-22) Inside the [World Between Worlds](/wiki/World_Between_Worlds "World Between Worlds"), Ahsoka Tano stated that contacting dead beings through animals was not impossible.[[23]](#cite_note-A_World_between_Worlds-23)

### Clone body[]

:   "*And now the power of two restores the one true Emperor.*"
:   ―Darth Sidious, to Rey and Ben Solo[[13]](#cite_note-Episode_VIII-13)

 [![Sidious transferred his soul into a clone modeled on his original body.](data:image/gif;base64,R0lGODlhAQABAIABAAAAAP///yH5BAEAAAEALAAAAAABAAEAQAICTAEAOw%3D%3D)](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/starwars/images/f/f5/Sidious_in_a_clone_body.jpg/revision/latest?cb=20200324095123) [![Sidious transferred his soul into a clone modeled on his original body.](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/starwars/images/f/f5/Sidious_in_a_clone_body.jpg/revision/latest/scale-to-width-down/250?cb=20200324095123)](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/starwars/images/f/f5/Sidious_in_a_clone_body.jpg/revision/latest?cb=20200324095123) 

Sidious transferred his soul into a clone modeled on his original body.

 

[Doctor](/wiki/Doctor "Doctor") [Penn Pershing](/wiki/Penn_Pershing "Penn Pershing"), a sought after cloning [scientist](/wiki/Scientist "Scientist") of an [unidentified](/wiki/Penn_Pershing%27s_homeworld "Penn Pershing's homeworld") [planet](/wiki/Planet "Planet") who would eventually work for [Moff](/wiki/Moff "Moff") [Gideon](/wiki/Gideon "Gideon")'s [Imperial remnant](/wiki/Gideon%27s_Imperial_remnant "Gideon's Imperial remnant"), was interested in the prospects of using cloning to enable one's loved ones to cheat death, which was sparked to him by his [mother](/wiki/Penn_Pershing%27s_mother "Penn Pershing's mother")’s death, feeling that had he been able to clone organs, he could have prevented her passing from a [heart](/wiki/Heart "Heart") failure.[[24]](#cite_note-Chapter_19-24)

In fear of his death, Darth Sidious ordered an exact [clone](/wiki/Cloning "Cloning") of his body be engineered as a new vessel for his spirit. Thus, once thrown down a shaft onboard the [Death Star II](/wiki/DS-2_Death_Star_II_Mobile_Battle_Station "DS-2 Death Star II Mobile Battle Station") by Vader, he transmitted his awareness to the clone, leaving the original body falling down to certain destruction.[[14]](#cite_note-Episode_IX_novel-14)

 [![Sidious' cloned body after being energized by the power of a dyad in the Force](data:image/gif;base64,R0lGODlhAQABAIABAAAAAP///yH5BAEAAAEALAAAAAABAAEAQAICTAEAOw%3D%3D)](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/starwars/images/2/2e/DarthSidious-TROS-transparent.png/revision/latest?cb=20250213045037) [![Sidious' cloned body after being energized by the power of a dyad in the Force](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/starwars/images/2/2e/DarthSidious-TROS-transparent.png/revision/latest/scale-to-width-down/250?cb=20250213045037)](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/starwars/images/2/2e/DarthSidious-TROS-transparent.png/revision/latest?cb=20250213045037) 

Sidious' cloned body after being energized by the power of a dyad in the Force

 

Aware of the new body's imperfection, Sidious sought another vessel to host his spirit. When a clone of him parented a child, he enjoined that the girl be brought to him so he could pass on to her body. However, the hunter [Ochi](/wiki/Ochi "Ochi"), tasked with that mission, failed to locate her and died without attaining her location. Sidious renewed his plan in [35 ABY](/wiki/35_ABY "35 ABY"), futilely encouraging Rey to kill him so that his spirit could possess her body. Regardless, the clone body was ultimately restored to a powerful version through the extracting of the power Rey and Ben shared as a dyad in the Force.[[2]](#cite_note-Episode_IX-2)

Ultimately, Emperor Palpatine's vision of the galaxy eternally ruled by the Sith ended, when his clone body got destroyed by Rey so he was vanquished in body and soul, and his dream of immortality died with him.[[15]](#cite_note-TSWB-15)

### Others[]

The artifact known as the [Ring of Immortality](/wiki/Ring_of_Immortality "Ring of Immortality"),[[25]](#cite_note-DA3-25) one of the two [Rings of Vaale](/wiki/Rings_of_Vaale "Rings of Vaale") created by the [Architects of Vaale](/wiki/Architects_of_Vaale "Architects of Vaale"), was said to provide its wearer with immortality but at the cost of driving them to madness, yet its negative effects could be removed supposedly if the wearer also possessed the [Ring of Fortune](/wiki/Ring_of_Fortune "Ring of Fortune").[[26]](#cite_note-DA2-26)

According to legend, Exegol was once visited separately by [Darth Sanguis](/wiki/Darth_Sanguis "Darth Sanguis") and [Darth Noctyss](/wiki/Darth_Noctyss "Darth Noctyss") in their quests for immortality. However, Sanguis became immortal through a ritual which transformed him into a horrendous creature. When Noctyss met him and became aware of Sanguis' achievement, Sanguis tricked her by not revealing his true identity so she would kill him in the very same ritual. Noctyss did so and Sanguis was freed from his miserable existence, leaving a transformed Noctyss alone at Exegol.[[27]](#cite_note-A_Life_Immortal-27)

[Doctor](/wiki/Doctor "Doctor") [Cylo](/wiki/Cylo "Cylo"), one of the Galactic Empire's best scientists, resolved to use illegal alien parts smuggled to his [secret base](/wiki/Cylo%27s_base "Cylo's base") during his research to create new life forms with technological augmentations, creating a personality map that, completed with [memory banks](/wiki/Memory_bank "Memory bank") and plug-in calculation systems, allowed himself to become an immortal system with several upgraded bodies at his disposal to use whenever his previous one died.[[28]](#cite_note-Darth_Vader_(2015)_5-28) Cylo survived several deaths through this way until Darth Vader finally killed him in a [star](/wiki/Star "Star") of the [Kuat system](/wiki/Kuat_system "Kuat system").[[29]](#cite_note-Darth_Vader_(2015)_25-29) The [Corellian](/wiki/Corellian "Corellian") [bounty hunter](/wiki/Bounty_hunter "Bounty hunter") [Rothgar Deng](/wiki/Rothgar_Deng "Rothgar Deng") subjected himself to [cybernetic](/wiki/Cybernetics "Cybernetics") replacements and turned to [black market](/wiki/Black_market "Black market") surgical clinics to replace his damaged body parts in order to live and work forever.[[30]](#cite_note-IX_Visual_Dictionary-30)

The [starbird](/wiki/Starbird "Starbird") of legend was said to be immortal, able to renew itself in the heart of a [nova](/wiki/Nova "Nova") when it was believed to be dead.[[31]](#cite_note-sketchbook-31)

The [sarlacc](/wiki/Sarlacc "Sarlacc") of the [Great Pit of Carkoon](/wiki/Great_Pit_of_Carkoon "Great Pit of Carkoon") allegedly digested its prey for millennia, thus keeping its victims alive during all that time for it to slowly consume.[[32]](#cite_note-SWCT-32) [Protocol droid](/wiki/Protocol_droid "Protocol droid") [C-3PO](/wiki/C-3PO "C-3PO"), when told by the [Hutt](/wiki/Hutt "Hutt") [crime lord](/wiki/Crime_lord "Crime lord") [Jabba Desilijic Tiure](/wiki/Jabba_Desilijic_Tiure "Jabba Desilijic Tiure") about the planned execution of his rebel friends, informed them that, according to Jabba, anyone in the sarlacc's belly would find a new definition of pain and suffering as they got digested over a thousand years.[[12]](#cite_note-Episode_VI-12)
