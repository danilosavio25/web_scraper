:   "*When developing* Star Wars: Young Jedi Adventures*, the first* Star Wars *series created for preschoolers, the creative team never stopped thinking about how this show may be a youngling's first step into a* larger world*, and their first time experiencing the limitless potential of the* Star Wars *galaxy. The show's characters, tone, and the life lessons woven throughout each episode were written just for them, and our talented team was committed to honoring the cinematic legacy while staying true to the expectations of parents for the youngest of audiences.*"

***Star Wars: Young Jedi Adventures*** is an animated *[Star Wars](/wiki/Star_Wars "Star Wars")* series focused for preschoolers, grade schoolers, and their families. It is set during the [High Republic Era](/wiki/High_Republic_Era "High Republic Era").[[1]](#cite_note-Announcement-1) It released on [YouTube](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/YouTube "wikipedia:YouTube") with shorts on [March 27](/wiki/March_27 "March 27"), [2023](/wiki/2023 "2023")[[3]](#cite_note-Meet-3) and released on [Disney Junior](/wiki/Disney_Junior "Disney Junior"), Disney+, Disney Channel, and Disney XD on [May 4](/wiki/May_4 "May 4"), 2023.[[5]](#cite_note-Full_Cast-5) The [first season](/wiki/Young_Jedi_Adventures_Season_One "Young Jedi Adventures Season One") was released in three further batches on [August 2](/wiki/August_2 "August 2"), [November 8](/wiki/November_8 "November 8"), 2023 and [February 14](/wiki/February_14 "February 14"), [2024](/wiki/2024 "2024").[[12]](#cite_note-Explore-12)[[13]](#cite_note-New_YJA_episodes_Nov_2023-13)[[10]](#cite_note-YJA_Returns_Feb_2024-10)

In 2024, the series was renewed for a [second season](/wiki/Young_Jedi_Adventures_Season_Two "Young Jedi Adventures Season Two"), with shorts on August 2, the first half released on [August 14](/wiki/August_14 "August 14"),[[14]](#cite_note-Season_2_Announcement-14) and the second half on [March 19](/wiki/March_19 "March 19"), [2025](/wiki/2025 "2025").[[15]](#cite_note-Season_2_Resume-15) In 2025, the series was renewed for a [third season](/wiki/Young_Jedi_Adventures_Season_Three "Young Jedi Adventures Season Three"),[[2]](#cite_note-Celebration_2025-2) which released all seven episodes on [December 8](/wiki/December_8 "December 8"), 2025.[[6]](#cite_note-S3_Release-6)

## Official description[]

*Set during The High Republic era, the animated series follows Jedi younglings as they study the ways of the Force, explore the galaxy, help citizens and creatures in need, and learn valuable skills needed to become Jedi along the way.*[[16]](#cite_note-April_2023-16)

## Development[]

The show was pitched to [Lucasfilm](/wiki/Lucasfilm "Lucasfilm") by [Michael Olson](/wiki/Michael_Olson "Michael Olson") with development beginning in [2019](/wiki/2019 "2019").[[17]](#cite_note-SWI_224-17)

On [May 27](/wiki/May_27 "May 27"), [2022](/wiki/2022 "2022"), during [Celebration Anaheim](/wiki/Celebration_Anaheim_(2022) "Celebration Anaheim (2022)"), the series was announced. It was set during the High Republic era and would focus on a group of Jedi younglings training to be [Jedi Knights](/wiki/Jedi_Knight "Jedi Knight"). *Young Jedi Adventures* would be the first animated Star Wars series aimed at preschoolers, early grade schoolers, and their families.[[1]](#cite_note-Announcement-1)

On [February 9](/wiki/February_9 "February 9"), [2023](/wiki/2023 "2023"), [Jamaal Avery Jr.](/wiki/Jamaal_Avery_Jr. "Jamaal Avery Jr."), [Juliet Donenfeld](/wiki/Juliet_Donenfeld "Juliet Donenfeld"), [Dee Bradley Baker](/wiki/Dee_Bradley_Baker "Dee Bradley Baker"), [Emma Berman](/wiki/Emma_Berman "Emma Berman"), [Jonathan Lipow](/wiki/Jonathan_Lipow "Jonathan Lipow"), and [Piotr Michael](/wiki/Piotr_Michael "Piotr Michael") were confirmed as key cast members. [StarWars.com](/wiki/StarWars.com "StarWars.com") also announced that the series would premiere on [May 4](/wiki/May_4 "May 4"), 2023.[[5]](#cite_note-Full_Cast-5)

### Production[]

:   "*I'm going for something a little more specific because this is for children. So, it's got to be clear what's being said. It's almost like with a pre-lingual child or even a dog, where there's a specific intent with the whining or vocalization. If you're a parent, you can often read what that is. And that's kind of what you want with a character like Nubs.*"
:   ―Dee Bradley Baker describing how he voiced his character Nubs[[18]](#cite_note-Baker_Nubs_interview-18)

 [![Younglings design exploration by ](data:image/gif;base64,R0lGODlhAQABAIABAAAAAP///yH5BAEAAAEALAAAAAABAAEAQAICTAEAOw%3D%3D)](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/starwars/images/7/73/YounglingYJA-BetsyBauer.jpg/revision/latest?cb=20250125164227) [![Younglings design exploration by ](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/starwars/images/7/73/YounglingYJA-BetsyBauer.jpg/revision/latest/scale-to-width-down/300?cb=20250125164227)](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/starwars/images/7/73/YounglingYJA-BetsyBauer.jpg/revision/latest?cb=20250125164227) 

Younglings design exploration by [Betsy Bauer](/wiki/Betsy_Bauer "Betsy Bauer")

 

*Young Jedi Adventures* was produced by Lucasfilm in collaboration with [Wild Canary](/wiki/Wild_Canary "Wild Canary"),[[7]](#cite_note-Diba-7) as well as ICON Creative Studio[[8]](#cite_note-ICON-8) for Disney+, Disney Junior, and Disney Channel. The series' executive producers included [James Waugh](/wiki/James_Waugh "James Waugh"), [Jacqui Lopez](/wiki/Jacqui_Lopez "Jacqui Lopez"), and [Josh Rimes](/wiki/Josh_Rimes "Josh Rimes"). The show runner was Michael Olson, who also served as executive producer. [Elliot M. Bour](/wiki/Elliot_M._Bour "Elliot M. Bour") served as co-producer and supervising director. Other producers included Jeannine Hodson and [Lamont Magee](/wiki/Lamont_Magee "Lamont Magee").[[5]](#cite_note-Full_Cast-5) The animated series was initially produced under the name "Big Wheel."[[7]](#cite_note-Diba-7)

Lucasfilm continued their collaboration with Wild Canary to produce the series' [second season](/wiki/Young_Jedi_Adventures_Season_Two "Young Jedi Adventures Season Two"). Waugh, Lopez and Rimes continued as executive producers while Olson continued as show runner and executive producer. Bour continued as supervising director and co-executive producer. [Lamont Magee](/wiki/Lamont_Magee "Lamont Magee") joined as consulting producer while Icon Creative provided production services for the series.[[14]](#cite_note-Season_2_Announcement-14)

Actors recorded their lines for the series' episodes remotely, with screenwriters and directors stitching the disparate voice performances together. During an interview with [StarWars.com](/wiki/StarWars.com "StarWars.com"), [Dee Bradley Baker](/wiki/Dee_Bradley_Baker "Dee Bradley Baker") likened his character Nubs, who doesn't speak [Galactic Basic Standard](/wiki/Galactic_Basic_Standard "Galactic Basic Standard") (English), to a pre-lingual child or even a dog. Baker recorded multiple options for his characters' lines. Nubs' friends Kai Brightstar and Lys Solay also reinforced what their friend was saying during dialogues, with Baker likening their relationship to that between [Han Solo](/wiki/Han_Solo "Han Solo") and [Chewbacca](/wiki/Chewbacca "Chewbacca"). Due to the show's pre-school target audience, sub-titles for Nubs was ruled out by the showrunners.[[18]](#cite_note-Baker_Nubs_interview-18)

## Marketing ‡[]

A promotional image depicting several characters including [Yoda](/wiki/Yoda "Yoda"), [Kai Brightstar](/wiki/Kai_Brightstar "Kai Brightstar"), and [Nash Durango](/wiki/Nash_Durango "Nash Durango") was unveiled at the D23 Expo 2022 on [September 10](/wiki/September_10 "September 10"), 2022.[[19]](#cite_note-D23_Expo-19)

The series' first official trailer was released at [Celebration Europe](/wiki/Celebration_Europe_(2023) "Celebration Europe (2023)") on [April 10](/wiki/April_10 "April 10"), 2023.[[20]](#cite_note-Official_trailer-20)

On [May 18](/wiki/May_18 "May 18"), 2023, *[This Week! in Star Wars](/wiki/This_Week!_in_Star_Wars "This Week! in Star Wars")* host [Kristin Baver](/wiki/Kristin_Baver "Kristin Baver") interviewed cast members [Jamaal Avery Jr.](/wiki/Jamaal_Avery_Jr. "Jamaal Avery Jr.") and [Juliet Donenfeld](/wiki/Juliet_Donenfeld "Juliet Donenfeld"), who voiced [Kai Brightstar](/wiki/Kai_Brightstar "Kai Brightstar") and [Lys Solay](/wiki/Lys_Solay "Lys Solay") respectively.[[21]](#cite_note-Rise_of_the_Red_Blade-21)

On [July 13](/wiki/July_13 "July 13"), Baver interviewed [Emma Berman](/wiki/Emma_Berman "Emma Berman") and [Trey Murphy](/wiki/Trey_Murphy "Trey Murphy"), who voiced [Nash Durango](/wiki/Nash_Durango "Nash Durango") and [Taborr Val Dorn](/wiki/Cyrus_Vuundir "Cyrus Vuundir"), respectively. The video also announced the release of six further new episodes commencing [August 2](/wiki/August_2 "August 2"), 2023.[[22]](#cite_note-More_YJA_Episodes-22)

## Release and reception[]

Three promotional shorts were released on YouTube on [March 27](/wiki/March_27 "March 27"), 2023. Three additional shorts were released over the following weeks leading up to [April 26](/wiki/April_26 "April 26"), 2023. These six shorts were subsequently released on Disney+ on April 26.[[23]](#cite_note-Shorts_Key_Art-23)

On [April 8](/wiki/April_8 "April 8"), 2023, the first two episodes of the series were screened at the *Young Jedi Adventures* panel at Celebration Europe.[[4]](#cite_note-Panels-4)

The first seven episodes were released on Disney+ on May 4, 2023.[[24]](#cite_note-D23_May-24) The seven episodes were also released on a weekly basis on Disney Junior between May 4 and [June 9](/wiki/June_9 "June 9").[[25]](#cite_note-Futon-25)

On [July 13](/wiki/July_13 "July 13"), 2023, Lucasfilm, Disney+ and Disney Junior announced that six more episodes would be released simultaneously on Disney+ and Disney Junior on August 2. More episodes will be released in 2023, bringing the [first season](/wiki/Young_Jedi_Adventures_Season_One "Young Jedi Adventures Season One") to a total of 25 episodes.[[12]](#cite_note-Explore-12)

On [August 18](/wiki/August_18 "August 18"), 2023, [StarWars.com](/wiki/StarWars.com "StarWars.com") announced that a third batch of episodes would be released on Disney+ and Disney Junior on [November 8](/wiki/November_8 "November 8"), including a [Life Day](/wiki/Life_Day "Life Day") special set on the [Wookiee](/wiki/Wookiee "Wookiee") homeworld of [Kashyyyk](/wiki/Kashyyyk "Kashyyyk"). In addition, further Season One episodes are scheduled to be released in [2024](/wiki/2024 "2024").[[13]](#cite_note-New_YJA_episodes_Nov_2023-13)

On [January 11](/wiki/January_11 "January 11"), 2024, StarWars.com announced that a fourth batch of episodes would be released on Disney+ and Disney Junior on [February 14](/wiki/February_14 "February 14"). These upcoming episodes would include a wilderness training exercise on [Tenoo](/wiki/Tenoo "Tenoo"), trips to both [Black Spire Outpost](/wiki/Black_Spire_Outpost "Black Spire Outpost") on [Batuu](/wiki/Batuu "Batuu") and [Starlight Beacon](/wiki/Starlight_Beacon "Starlight Beacon"), and a climatic showdown with the antagonistic Taborr Val Dorn. [Marcus Scribner](/wiki/Marcus_Scribner "Marcus Scribner"), [Justine Lee](/wiki/Justine_Lee "Justine Lee") and [Liam O'Brien](/wiki/Liam_O%27Brien "Liam O'Brien") were also confirmed as new cast members, playing [Bell Zettifar](/wiki/Bell_Zettifar "Bell Zettifar"), [Lina Soh](/wiki/Lina_Soh "Lina Soh") and [Estala Maru](/wiki/Estala_Maru "Estala Maru") respectively. [Jecobi Swain](/wiki/Jecobi_Swain "Jecobi Swain") was also confirmed as voicing [Kai Brightstar](/wiki/Kai_Brightstar "Kai Brightstar") alongside [Jamaal Avery Jr.](/wiki/Jamaal_Avery_Jr. "Jamaal Avery Jr.")[[10]](#cite_note-YJA_Returns_Feb_2024-10)

On [June 14](/wiki/June_14 "June 14"), 2024, StarWars.com announced that the first half of the [second season](/wiki/Young_Jedi_Adventures_Season_Two "Young Jedi Adventures Season Two") would be released on Disney+ and Disney Junior on [August 14](/wiki/August_14 "August 14"), with shorts on [August 2](/wiki/August_2 "August 2"). The second half is expected to be released in [2025](/wiki/2025 "2025"). A tie-in a digital series called *[Star Wars: Fun with Nubs](/wiki/Star_Wars:_Fun_with_Nubs "Star Wars: Fun with Nubs")* was also released on June 14. Jedi [Padawan](/wiki/Padawan "Padawan") [Wes Vinik](/wiki/Wes_Vinik "Wes Vinik") portrayed by Gunnar Sizemore and the [astromech droid](/wiki/Astromech_droid "Astromech droid") [R0-M1](/wiki/R0-M1 "R0-M1") were announced as new characters.[[14]](#cite_note-Season_2_Announcement-14)

On [January 31](/wiki/January_31 "January 31"), 2025, StarWars.com announced that the second half of the second season would be released on Disney+ and Disney Junior on [March 19](/wiki/March_19 "March 19"). These upcoming episodes would have storylines involving the Jedi younglings joining forces with Prince [Cyrus Vuundir](/wiki/Cyrus_Vuundir "Cyrus Vuundir") to defend his homeworld from pirates, the younglings and [Nash Durango](/wiki/Nash_Durango "Nash Durango") teaming up to save Master [Yoda](/wiki/Yoda "Yoda"), and an adult [Pooba](/wiki/Pooba "Pooba") named [Zeephoz](/wiki/Zeepohz "Zeepohz"). In addition, further episodes of *Fun with Nubs* were released in February and March 2025.[[15]](#cite_note-Season_2_Resume-15)

A [third season](/wiki/Young_Jedi_Adventures_Season_Three "Young Jedi Adventures Season Three") was announced at [Celebration Japan](/wiki/Celebration_Japan_(2025) "Celebration Japan (2025)"),[[26]](#cite_note-Season_3_Coming-26) which released on December 8, 2025.[[6]](#cite_note-S3_Release-6) The droids [Beepers](/wiki/Beepers "Beepers"), [Dozer](/wiki/Dozer "Dozer"), [Gigi](/wiki/Gigi_(droid) "Gigi (droid)"), [Dotti](/wiki/Dotti "Dotti") and the droid builder and tinkerer [Rek Minuu](/wiki/Rek_Minuu "Rek Minuu") were introduced as new characters.[[26]](#cite_note-Season_3_Coming-26) Nubs also appeared at the Kids Corner during Celebration Japan.[[26]](#cite_note-Season_3_Coming-26) Season Three consists of seven episodes.[[6]](#cite_note-S3_Release-6)

## Reception ‡[]

On [December 16](/wiki/December_16 "December 16"), 2023, *Young Jedi Adventures* won two [Emmy Awards](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Emmy_Awards "wikipedia:Emmy Awards") for "Outstanding Editing for a Preschool Animated Program" and "Outstanding Sound Mixing and Sound Editing for a Preschool Animated Program." The series was also nominated for three other Emmy awards including "Outstanding Pre–School Animated Series," "Outstanding Music Direction and Composition for an Animated Program," and "Outstanding Main Title."[[27]](#cite_note-Emmys_Dec_2023-27)

On [November 18](/wiki/November_18 "November 18"), [2025](/wiki/2025 "2025"), the series was nominated for four Emmy Awards: "Outstanding Voice Performer in a Preschool Program" ([Dee Bradley Baker](/wiki/Dee_Bradley_Baker "Dee Bradley Baker"), "Outstanding Directing for a Preschool Animated Series" ("[The Battle of Tenoo](/wiki/The_Battle_of_Tenoo "The Battle of Tenoo")"), "Outstanding Editing for a Preschool Animated Series" ("The Battle of Tenoo") and "Outstanding Sound Mixing and Sound Editing for a Preschool Animated Program" ("The Battle of Tenoo").[[28]](#cite_note-Emmy_Awards_Nov_2025-28)[[29]](#cite_note-SWNN_Nov_2025-29)
