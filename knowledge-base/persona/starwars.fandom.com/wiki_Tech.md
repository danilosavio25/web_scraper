**Tech**, formerly designated **CT-9902**, was a [clone commando](/wiki/Clone_commando "Clone commando") who served in the [Grand Army of the Republic](/wiki/Grand_Army_of_the_Republic "Grand Army of the Republic") during the [Clone Wars](/wiki/Clone_Wars "Clone Wars") as a member of [Clone Force 99](/wiki/Clone_Force_99 "Clone Force 99"), also known as the "Bad Batch," and who later deserted the [Galactic Empire](/wiki/Galactic_Empire "Galactic Empire"). As a result of [his](/wiki/Gender "Gender") genetic [mutations](/wiki/Genetically_defective_clone "Genetically defective clone"), he served as the brains of his [squad](/wiki/Squad "Squad") and was a valued asset in this regard, using his high intellect and mastery of technology to ensure their operations were successful. Even so, he was no less skilled in combat than the other three members.

Tech deserted the Empire shortly after its [proclamation](/wiki/Proclamation_of_the_New_Order "Proclamation of the New Order"), along with the [human](/wiki/Human "Human") [female](/wiki/Gender "Gender") [clone](/wiki/Cloning "Cloning") [Omega](/wiki/Omega "Omega") and the other members of the Bad Batch, except for [Crosshair](/wiki/Crosshair "Crosshair"), who remained loyal to the Empire and soon started tracking his former squad. He and the Bad Batch became mercenaries for the [Mantellian](/wiki/Ord_Mantell "Ord Mantell") [Trandoshan](/wiki/Trandoshan "Trandoshan") [Ciddarin "Cid" Scaleback](/wiki/Ciddarin_Scaleback "Ciddarin Scaleback") but were forced to come back to [Kamino](/wiki/Kamino "Kamino") to rescue their fellow clone brother [Hunter](/wiki/Hunter "Hunter"), who had been captured by the Empire. Tech then witnessed the [destruction](/wiki/Attack_on_Kamino "Attack on Kamino") of the [Kaminoan](/wiki/Kaminoan "Kaminoan") [capital](/wiki/Capital "Capital") [Tipoca City](/wiki/Tipoca_City "Tipoca City") and was thought to have died during its fall. However, he and the Bad Batch survived and sought to avoid drawing the attention of the Empire, while continuing their mercenary missions.

After Clone Force 99 learned that the Empire had destroyed Tipoca City to gain control over its cloning technology and created the [Advanced Science Division](/wiki/Advanced_Science_Division "Advanced Science Division"), a clandestine scientist program experimenting on clones, Tech and his squad [infiltrated](/wiki/Bombing_of_Raven%27s_Peak "Bombing of Raven's Peak") [Raven's Peak](/wiki/Raven%27s_Peak "Raven's Peak") on [Eriadu](/wiki/Eriadu "Eriadu") during an [Imperial summit](/wiki/Imperial_summit_on_Eriadu "Imperial summit on Eriadu") to learn the whereabouts of Imperial [Doctor](/wiki/Doctor "Doctor") [Royce Hemlock](/wiki/Royce_Hemlock "Royce Hemlock") and locate their brother Crosshair. However, the clones were quickly overwhelmed and Tech sacrificed himself so his teammates could escape.

## Biography[]

### Early life[]

:   "*Your mutations were enhanced in this room. Experimental Unit 99 began right here.*"
:   ―Omega about Nala Se's private laboratory[[7]](#cite_note-Return_to_Kamino-7)

Tech—designated CT-9902[[8]](#cite_note-AMG-8)—was part of [Clone Force 99](/wiki/Clone_Force_99 "Clone Force 99"), an elite [squad](/wiki/Squad "Squad") of [clone commandos](/wiki/Clone_commando "Clone commando") with genetically engineered enhanced abilities,[[1]](#cite_note-CWCE_JtB-1) as its computer and weapons specialist.[[4]](#cite_note-Tech_Databank-4) While each of his squad-mates, [Hunter](/wiki/Hunter "Hunter"), [Wrecker](/wiki/Wrecker "Wrecker"), and [Crosshair](/wiki/Crosshair "Crosshair"), had their own unique abilities, Tech had a great intellect, which granted him an aptitude for science and incredible skills with technology, including engineering[[5]](#cite_note-Aftermath-5) and hacking.[[9]](#cite_note-The_Bad_Batch-9) His appearance differed from that of normal clones of [Jango Fett](/wiki/Jango_Fett "Jango Fett")[[10]](#cite_note-Tech_old-10) in that he appeared younger, leaner, and had lighter-skin and hair than his genetic template.[[9]](#cite_note-The_Bad_Batch-9) Like his squad-mates, Tech received his mutations in Kaminoan [scientist](/wiki/Scientist "Scientist") [Nala Se](/wiki/Nala_Se "Nala Se")'s [private research laboratory](/wiki/Nala_Se%27s_private_lab "Nala Se's private lab").[[7]](#cite_note-Return_to_Kamino-7)

### The Clone Wars[]

#### The Battle of Anaxes[]

:   "*Experimental unit Clone Force 99. They're defective clones with, uh… desirable mutations.*"
:   ―Commander Cody, on Clone Force 99 moments before they arrived on Anaxes[[9]](#cite_note-The_Bad_Batch-9)

 [![On Anaxes, Tech hacked into the computer system of a Separatist Cyber Center and discovered that Rex's algorithm was being broadcast from the planet Skako Minor.](data:image/gif;base64,R0lGODlhAQABAIABAAAAAP///yH5BAEAAAEALAAAAAABAAEAQAICTAEAOw%3D%3D)](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/starwars/images/2/2f/Bad_Batch_scouting.png/revision/latest?cb=20210507130251) [![On Anaxes, Tech hacked into the computer system of a Separatist Cyber Center and discovered that Rex's algorithm was being broadcast from the planet Skako Minor.](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/starwars/images/2/2f/Bad_Batch_scouting.png/revision/latest/scale-to-width-down/225?cb=20210507130251)](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/starwars/images/2/2f/Bad_Batch_scouting.png/revision/latest?cb=20210507130251) 

On Anaxes, Tech hacked into the computer system of a Separatist Cyber Center and discovered that Rex's algorithm was being broadcast from the planet Skako Minor.

 

Late in the war, Clone Force 99 was sent to [Yalbec Prime](/wiki/Yalbec_Prime "Yalbec Prime") to end an [insurrection](/wiki/Insurrection_on_Yalbec_Prime "Insurrection on Yalbec Prime"). The clones then traveled in their shuttle, the [*Marauder*](/wiki/Marauder_(Omicron-class) "Marauder (Omicron-class)"), to [Anaxes](/wiki/Anaxes "Anaxes"), where the [Republic](/wiki/Galactic_Republic "Galactic Republic") was engaged in a [key campaign](/wiki/Battle_of_Anaxes "Battle of Anaxes") that they were on the losing side of. At the behest of [Clone Commander](/wiki/Clone_Commander "Clone Commander") [Cody](/wiki/Cody "Cody"), Tech and the squad joined [Clone Captain](/wiki/Clone_Captain "Clone Captain") [Rex](/wiki/Rex "Rex"), [Advanced Recon Commando](/wiki/Advanced_Recon_Commando "Advanced Recon Commando") [Jesse](/wiki/Jesse "Jesse"), and [clone medic](/wiki/Clone_medic "Clone medic") [Kix](/wiki/Kix "Kix") on a stealth mission to destroy a [Separatist](/wiki/Confederacy_of_Independent_Systems "Confederacy of Independent Systems") [Cyber Center](/wiki/Cyber_Center "Cyber Center"). There, Tech and Rex discovered that the Separatists had been so successful in countering their strategies because they were accessing [Rex's battle strategy algorithm](/wiki/Rex%27s_battle_strategy_algorithm "Rex's battle strategy algorithm") through [clone trooper](/wiki/Clone_trooper "Clone trooper") [Echo](/wiki/Echo "Echo"), who was being held prisoner on [Skako Minor](/wiki/Skako_Minor "Skako Minor"). They escaped the Cyber Center as droid reinforcements closed in on them.[[9]](#cite_note-The_Bad_Batch-9)

#### Skako Minor[]

##### Rescuing Echo[]

Later, Tech and his squad welcomed Rex and [Jedi General](/wiki/Jedi_General "Jedi General") [Anakin Skywalker](/wiki/Anakin_Skywalker "Anakin Skywalker") aboard the *Marauder* en route to Skako Minor to rescue Echo. After a difficult landing, the clones and Skywalker were attacked by [Poletecs](/wiki/Poletec "Poletec"), the planet's locals, primitive people flying [keeradaks](/wiki/Keeradak "Keeradak"). After the locals took General Skywalker as a prisoner, the clones followed them to their village. Eventually, the [Poletec chief](/wiki/Qin_Yazal "Qin Yazal") cleared up the misunderstanding by ordering a ceasefire and explaining that he did not want the war to spread to his planet. Skywalker declared that the Republic had not brought the war to Skako Minor and that the Separatists were responsible for it.[[11]](#cite_note-A_Distant_Echo-11)

After Tech explained in the Poletecs' language that the Separatists were holding one of their men prisoner in [Purkoll](/wiki/Purkoll "Purkoll"), the chief agreed to provide the clones and Skywalker with scouts to lead them to the city. On their way to Purkoll, Tech noticed that he could no longer trace Echo's signal. As Tech speculated that it could be explained by the atmospheric disturbances, Hunter stated that Echo might be dead and that the signal could be a trap set by the Separatists. When Rex retorted that he was convinced that his friend was still alive, Crosshair declared that he did not blame Rex for leaving Echo for dead at the [Citadel](/wiki/Citadel "Citadel"), as he would have done the same. A fight quickly started, which was however stopped by Skywalker.[[11]](#cite_note-A_Distant_Echo-11)

 [![Echo was found by Rex and Tech and rescued from Skako Minor.](data:image/gif;base64,R0lGODlhAQABAIABAAAAAP///yH5BAEAAAEALAAAAAABAAEAQAICTAEAOw%3D%3D)](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/starwars/images/7/7c/Rex-Echo-Tech-ADistantEcho.png/revision/latest?cb=20230302163026) [![Echo was found by Rex and Tech and rescued from Skako Minor.](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/starwars/images/7/7c/Rex-Echo-Tech-ADistantEcho.png/revision/latest/scale-to-width-down/250?cb=20230302163026)](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/starwars/images/7/7c/Rex-Echo-Tech-ADistantEcho.png/revision/latest?cb=20230302163026) 

Echo was found by Rex and Tech and rescued from Skako Minor.

 

In Purkoll, Tech managed to retrace Echo's signal to the city's [tower](/wiki/Purkoll_Tower_Base "Purkoll Tower Base"). Inside, Clone Force 99, Rex, and Skywalker quickly engaged several [D1-series aerial battle droids](/wiki/D1-series_aerial_battle_droid "D1-series aerial battle droid"). After losing the signal once again, Tech explained that it was only traceable during data transmissions and therefore, the clones had to wait for Echo to transfer more information. After splitting up and searching several rooms, the signal came back and Tech, Wrecker, Hunter, and Rex managed to locate Echo inside a sealed room. However, the clones were interrupted by a transmission from the foreman [Wat Tambor](/wiki/Wat_Tambor "Wat Tambor"), accusing them of having violated the [Techno Union](/wiki/Techno_Union "Techno Union")'s neutrality by invading Purkoll. Tambor then ordered D-wing droids to execute the clones. Joined by Skywalker and Crosshair, the droids were soon destroyed and Tech and Rex entered the room, which turned out to be a large lab. Inside, they found Echo confined within a [stasis chamber](/wiki/Stasis_chamber "Stasis chamber").[[11]](#cite_note-A_Distant_Echo-11)

Tech and Rex found out that Echo was directly connected to the Separatist computer system, which led Tech to try to decrypt his cerebral interface. Indeed, Echo's vital life signs were still dependent on the machine and his body was controlled by computer-generated brain waves, which made him weak, disoriented, and unable to walk. With no reinforcements, the clones and Skywalker were trapped in the laboratory and surrounded by droids. Meanwhile, Wat Tambor decided to send the [Decimator](/wiki/S/D_Decimator_droid "S/D Decimator droid"), a prototype combat droid capable of vaporizing organic life. After Tech found a way to unplug Echo, the latter started regaining consciousness. Before the Decimator could enter the chamber, the clones and Skywalker escaped through the room's top exhaust door. Echo then led the group to a long suspended narrow pipe which led to a building with a landing pad. After he commented on the low chances of survival, especially since the group was surrounded once again by droids on both sides, Tech used recordings he had taken earlier to summon several keeradaks to escape on. Flying on the reptiles, the group was followed by several flying D-wing droids, which eventually crashed into a cliff.[[12]](#cite_note-On_the_Wings_of_Keeradaks-12)

##### Alliance with the Poletecs[]

 [![Tech and his squad fought against the Techno Union droids.](data:image/gif;base64,R0lGODlhAQABAIABAAAAAP///yH5BAEAAAEALAAAAAABAAEAQAICTAEAOw%3D%3D)](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/starwars/images/6/63/Keeradaks-clones-octuptarra.jpg/revision/latest?cb=20210404003823) [![Tech and his squad fought against the Techno Union droids.](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/starwars/images/6/63/Keeradaks-clones-octuptarra.jpg/revision/latest/scale-to-width-down/250?cb=20210404003823)](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/starwars/images/6/63/Keeradaks-clones-octuptarra.jpg/revision/latest?cb=20210404003823) 

Tech and his squad fought against the Techno Union droids.

 

The clones and Skywalker then came back to the Poletec village that had helped them earlier. Tech apologized on their behalf, explaining that they had to flee from Purkoll. However, the group had been followed by droids, which went against the promise they made of not bringing the war to the Poletecs' territory. However, Rex explained that despite being officially neutral, the Techno Union had tried to turn one of their men into a machine and deprived him of his humanity, which meant that the Poletecs had to choose a side too. Indeed, when a force of D-wing droids and two [octuptarra magna tri-droids](/wiki/Octuptarra_tri-droid "Octuptarra tri-droid") arrived in the village, the Poletecs fought alongside the clones and general Skywalker. Although several Poletecs died during the fight, Skywalker, Rex, and Clone Force 99 managed to destroy most droids, the remaining D-wings fleeing back to Wat Tambor. After the Poletec chief declared that they were now allies, the clones and Skywalker left Skako Minor aboard the *Marauder*.[[12]](#cite_note-On_the_Wings_of_Keeradaks-12)

#### A victory for the Republic[]

With Rex's algorithm having been recovered from the Separatists, the Republic increased its chances to retake Anaxes and Clone Force 99 subsequently came back to the planet to assist the war effort. Indeed, Echo's planned to infiltrate Separatist [Admiral](/wiki/Admiral "Admiral") [Trench](/wiki/Trench "Trench")'s [dreadnought](/wiki/Providence-class_Dreadnought "Providence-class Dreadnought") above Anaxes and mislead him by feeding the Separatist systems false strategies. After the plan was approved by [Jedi Master](/wiki/Jedi_Master "Jedi Master") [Mace Windu](/wiki/Mace_Windu "Mace Windu") and Jedi Master [Obi-Wan Kenobi](/wiki/Obi-Wan_Kenobi "Obi-Wan Kenobi"), Clone Force 99, Skywalker, Rex, and Echo departed for their mission aboard the *Marauder.* After transmitting a signal to cloak the ship as a Separatist shuttle, Echo managed to obtain permission to land on the dreadnought. Echo, Tech, Skywalker, and Rex then infiltrated the dreadnought's interface unit. Tech masked Echo's transmission as originating from Skako Minor, which fooled Trench, unaware that the clone had been freed earlier. Trench thus mobilized all his troops to the assembly complex, Echo having claimed that the Republic would not send reinforcements due to their faith in the Jedi. However, when Trench thought that his victory would be imminent, Echo sent feedback pulses to shut down all of the Separatist droids, all fronts consequently falling.[[13]](#cite_note-:0-13)

However, Trench did not entirely rely on the algorithm and therefore activated a [bomb](/wiki/Bomb "Bomb") beneath the assembly complex. Tech thus contacted Master Windu to tell him about the bomb and that they needed help to disarm it, causing Windu to descend into the complex's fusion generator only to realize that the bomb was protected by ray shields. Before the Jedi could transfer the sequence to shut it down, Echo's signal was jammed. Meanwhile, Skywalker, still aboard the dreadnought, located Trench and, after severing his left set of arms, demanded he give the last number needed to deactivate the bomb. After Trench tried to attack the Jedi, Skywalker killed him. Tech, his squad, Rex, and Echo then evacuated from the ship after destroying several droids. Joined by Skywalker, they escaped with the *Marauder*, before blowing up the dreadnought.[[13]](#cite_note-:0-13)

After the battle of Anaxes, Tech and his squad were joined by Echo.

 

After the Republic's victory, Windu, Kenobi, and Skywalker thanked Echo,[[13]](#cite_note-:0-13) who had been promoted to the rank of [Clone Corporal](/wiki/Clone_Corporal "Clone Corporal") for his role in securing the victory,[[14]](#cite_note-USWNE-14) Rex, and the Bad Batch for their service, adding that because of their courage and bravery the Republic's [shipyards](/wiki/Shipyard "Shipyard") would soon be working again. Although Kenobi assured the Batch that there were "[medals](/wiki/Unidentified_medal_(Battle_of_Anaxes) "Unidentified medal (Battle of Anaxes)") coming your way", they declined to accept, with Tech commenting that they were "not really our thing". Before Clone Force 99 left however, Hunter asked Echo if he wanted to become a part of the squad. Echo accepted and joined the Bad Batch, all of whom gave a salute to Rex before departing.[[13]](#cite_note-:0-13)

#### Kaller and Order 66[]

As a member of Clone Force 99, Tech was sent to Kaller to fight under Master Billaba and Padawan Dume.

 

Shortly before the end of the Clone Wars, Clone Force 99 was sent to the [planet](/wiki/Planet "Planet") [Kaller](/wiki/Kaller "Kaller") to serve as reinforcements for Jedi Master [Depa Billaba](/wiki/Depa_Billaba "Depa Billaba") and her [Padawan](/wiki/Padawan "Padawan"), [Caleb Dume](/wiki/Kanan_Jarrus "Kanan Jarrus"). After wiping out all [B1-series battle droids](/wiki/B1-series_battle_droid "B1-series battle droid") present and their [Armored Assault Tanks](/wiki/Armored_Assault_Tank_Mk_I "Armored Assault Tank Mk I"), Tech and his fellow soldiers Hunter, Echo, Crosshair, and Wrecker met with the two Jedi, recommending them to launch a counter-attack since another droid battalion was approaching. Billaba declared that she was impressed by their exploits but asked nevertheless where the reinforcements were. Hunter explained that they had been rerouted to [Coruscant](/wiki/Coruscant "Coruscant") but Tech pointed out that according to his calculations, the clones might not be needed anymore, as the Clone Wars would soon be over. Indeed, Jedi Master Obi-Wan Kenobi had just [engaged](/wiki/Battle_of_Utapau "Battle of Utapau") [General](/wiki/General "General") [Grievous](/wiki/Grievous "Grievous") on [Utapau](/wiki/Utapau "Utapau").[[5]](#cite_note-Aftermath-5)

However, as Clone Force 99 left, [Order 66](/wiki/Order_66 "Order 66") was issued, and Billaba was killed shortly after she told her Padawan to run away. The Bad Batch tried to follow Dume, but he vanished. When Echo wondered what had happened, Tech explained that his comm chatter was issuing an order called "Order 66" even though he did not know what it meant. Indeed, the Bad Batch's [inhibitor chips](/wiki/Behavioral_modification_biochip "Behavioral modification biochip") failed to activate properly, and thus they did not participate in the [Great Jedi Purge](/wiki/Great_Jedi_Purge "Great Jedi Purge"), except for Crosshair who, when asked by Hunter to help him find Dume, tried to kill the young Padawan. As Hunter and Crosshair were after the child, they were contacted via [comlink](/wiki/Comlink "Comlink") by Tech, who told them that all regular clones had received orders to execute all Jedi under the pretext that they had committed [treason](/wiki/Treason "Treason"). Crosshair then spotted Dume and tried to shoot him down despite Hunter's orders not to. Crosshair having fallen unconscious when Dume fought back, Hunter went after him and declared that he was as confused as well about Billaba's execution. Dume refused to listen and ran away, Hunter allowing him to go. Crosshair shortly arrived and asked him what had happened. Hunter lied by declaring that he had killed Dume.[[5]](#cite_note-Aftermath-5)

Acknowledging that Omega was one of them and most importantly was in danger, Hunter decided to come back to Kamino to save her.[[5]](#cite_note-Aftermath-5)

Upon arriving above Kamino, Tech declared that they were not receiving any communication from comscan, which was unusual. Hunter was determined to understand what was happening and Tech landed the *Marauder* on an outdoor landing platform. With the plan of splitting up to find Omega and coming back to the ship, the clones opened the hangar door, only to be surrounded by several shock troopers. Tarkin arrived shortly after and declared that the clones were traitors and had conspired with Saw Gerrera against the Empire resulting in their arrest. The five troopers were marched into a cell protected by a force field. However, Tech declared that their plan did not truly fail, as they immediately noticed that Omega had been sent to the brig too. Hunter asserted that they had come back for her. Crosshair annoyingly asked when Hunter had become sentimental and criticized his leadership. Angry, Crosshair declared that every choice Clone Force 99 had made since Keller was wrong and that as soldiers, they had to follow orders. However, Crosshair was soon led out of the cell by shock troopers.[[5]](#cite_note-Aftermath-5)

While the clones were waiting, Tech realized that their cell was not designed to hold someone like Wrecker, meaning that if he were to punch the correct spot, they could all break free. Echo, Hunter, and Omega stoop up while Tech searched for a weak point in the wall. Tech pointed out the weak spot to Wrecker, who waited for the shock troopers to be distracted to punch it. With a panel dislodged, Omega volunteered to sneak out through the hatch and head to the console to lower the ray shield. After leaving the cell through the ventilation shaft, Omega lowered the shield, which resulted in the clones leaving their cell and knocking out the remaining guards. In the corridors, Tech declared that they needed their gear, to which Omega answered that it had been moved to a hangar.[[5]](#cite_note-Aftermath-5)

Eventually, they decided to go to the planet [Saleucami](/wiki/Saleucami "Saleucami") to visit another [clone deserter](/wiki/Cut_Lawquane "Cut Lawquane"),[[5]](#cite_note-Aftermath-5) whom they had met during the Clone Wars. After arriving on Saleucami, Echo asked about the fugitive they were about to meet and wondered if a deserter could be trusted. Hunter explained that the clone had succeeded in staying off the radar whereas it was not Clone Force 99's specialty. Tech added that running away from the Empire meant that they were also fugitives themselves. After springing a trap, the clones were confronted by Cut Lawquane and his [Twi'lek](/wiki/Twi%27lek "Twi'lek") wife [Suu Lawquane](/wiki/Suu_Lawquane "Suu Lawquane"), who recognized them and led them to their [farm](/wiki/Lawquane%27s_Farm "Lawquane's Farm"). The group discussed the recent events, including Order 66 and the birth of the Galactic Empire. Tech declared that the reason why Order 66 had been efficient was that the clones were programmed to never mention their inhibitor chips. Lawquane explained that he had been visited by Rex the day before and had heard of the chips. When Omega went outside to play with the Lawquane children, [Shaeeah](/wiki/Shaeeah_Lawquane "Shaeeah Lawquane") and [Jek](/wiki/Jek_Lawquane "Jek Lawquane"), Lawquane asked about the young clone and her purpose, to which Hunter replied that she was a defective clone. After Lawquane stated that, just like Kamino, Saleucami was no longer safe, Hunter explained that he planned to travel to a nearby [spaceport](/wiki/Spaceport "Spaceport") with his squad to find off-world transport.[[15]](#cite_note-Cut_and_Run-15)

After landing on the moon, the group encountered a [Sullustan](/wiki/Raspar_Six "Raspar Six") overseeing a refueling depot. After the clones refused that Six scan their ship, the Sullustan offered to skip the step for a bribe, which Wrecker and Tech eventually accepted.[[17]](#cite_note-:1-17)

However, the Sullustan turned out to be working for the [bounty hunter](/wiki/Bounty_hunter "Bounty hunter") [Fennec Shand](/wiki/Fennec_Shand "Fennec Shand"). Informed that Clone Force 99 was on Pantora with Omega, she jumped into hyperspace. While Hunter, Omega, and Echo explored their surroundings, Tech and Wrecker stayed at the landing pad. Tech indeed declared that the ship's [signature key](/wiki/Signature_key "Signature key") was embedded below its rear [parallax inverters](/wiki/Parallax_inverter "Parallax inverter"). After Wrecker reached it, Tech explained that with a slight adjustment, the signature would soon be scrambled. Meanwhile, Shand tried to approach Omega but the situation escalated when her intentions were revealed. Hunter, unable to rescue Omega, contacted Tech, Wrecker, and Echo by [comlink](/wiki/Comlink "Comlink") and explained the situation. Tech tapped into the city's [CCTV system](/wiki/CCTV_system "CCTV system") to find Omega and the bounty hunter and explained that [maintenance droids](/wiki/Maintenance_droid "Maintenance droid") would be needed to repair the ship. Thus, Echo asked several [astromech](/wiki/Astromech_droid "Astromech droid") droids' help, which eventually accepted to repair the ship.[[17]](#cite_note-:1-17)

After going down into the maintenance tunnels, Omega was forced to climb up a ladder and reach the top of a tower to escape Shand. Tech soon intercepted com chatter discussing reports of a child hanging from a tower in the city's lower division and conveyed the news to Hunter, who then stole a [speeder bike](/wiki/Speeder_bike "Speeder bike"). Meanwhile, Shand saved Omega from falling off the tower and dropped her onto a [hovertruck](/wiki/Speeder_truck "Speeder truck"). Inside the hovertruck, Omega raised a level which caused the truck to tilt and the bounty hunter to lose her [blaster](/wiki/Blaster "Blaster") and drop into an [airspeeder](/wiki/Airspeeder "Airspeeder"). Hunter, still after Shand and Omega, blasted at the hijacked airspeeder's engines, causing them to explode. After Hunter caught Omega and planted a [detonator](/wiki/Detonator "Detonator") inside the speeder, Shand jumped out and the clones were able to escape. Back at [Ro Station](/wiki/Ro_Station "Ro Station"), Tech declared that they needed to hurry. The Bad Batch then left Pantora. In space, Hunter explained to Omega that she had been the target of a bounty hunter. Echo explains that a bounty hunter was someone who hunted targets and Tech added that, in this case, she was the target.[[17]](#cite_note-:1-17)

Echo reassured Omega by saying that they would not let anything happen to Muchi, while Tech added that they could earn a decent amount of [credits](/wiki/Galactic_Credit_Standard "Galactic Credit Standard").[[18]](#cite_note-Rampage-18)

After a mission against [Rhokai gang](/wiki/Rhokai_gang_starfighter "Rhokai gang starfighter") [starfighters](/wiki/Starfighter "Starfighter") involving the recovery of a [lizard](/wiki/Lizard "Lizard") named [Ruby](/wiki/Ruby_(lizard) "Ruby (lizard)"), Clone Force 99 found a hooded stranger waiting for them at Cid's Parlor. The man turned out to be Rex, who had supposedly died in action at the end of the Clone Wars. Rex revealed that he had found the squad with the help of the Martez sisters. Tech told Rex that, as defective clones, their inhibitor chips did not function properly during Order 66, except for Crosshair's. Confused and alarmed by Wrecker suddenly complaining about headaches, Rex asked the Bad Batch if they had removed their chips. When Tech confirmed that they never did, Rex slowly reached for his [blaster pistol](/wiki/Blaster_pistol "Blaster pistol"), claiming that the chips were dangerous and that the clones could become out of control without a warning. Before leaving the cantina, Rex told them that he would be in touch to help the group remove their chips.[[20]](#cite_note-Battle_Scars-20)

 [![The moment Wrecker's inhibitor chip activated, he violently attacked Tech.](data:image/gif;base64,R0lGODlhAQABAIABAAAAAP///yH5BAEAAAEALAAAAAABAAEAQAICTAEAOw%3D%3D)](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/starwars/images/4/47/Wrecker_Turns.jpg/revision/latest?cb=20210615071221) [![The moment Wrecker's inhibitor chip activated, he violently attacked Tech.](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/starwars/images/4/47/Wrecker_Turns.jpg/revision/latest/scale-to-width-down/250?cb=20210615071221)](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/starwars/images/4/47/Wrecker_Turns.jpg/revision/latest?cb=20210615071221)

Aboard the *Marauder*, Tech explained that his scanner would soon be finished since he was able to establish a baseline by comparing Rex's brain scan to theirs. Clone Force 99 and Omega then met Rex on the planet [Bracca](/wiki/Bracca "Bracca") so that they could use the medical amenities of a wrecked [Jedi cruiser](/wiki/Unidentified_Venator-class_Star_Destroyer_(Clone_Force_99) "Unidentified Venator-class Star Destroyer (Clone Force 99)") to remove their chips,[[20]](#cite_note-Battle_Scars-20) as Rex did shortly after Order 66.[[21]](#cite_note-Victory_and_Death-21) Rex explained that [Fives](/wiki/Fives "Fives") had tried to [warn him](/wiki/Mission_to_Level_1325 "Mission to Level 1325") about the chips, but that he did not understand at the time. After the procedure was over, Clone Force 99 told Rex that he now knew how to reach them and bid each other farewell. Unbeknownst to them, [scrappers](/wiki/Scrapper_Guild "Scrapper Guild") were watching them through a pair of macrobinoculars and [one](/wiki/Unidentified_scrapper "Unidentified scrapper") asked the Empire to be notified of their presence.[[20]](#cite_note-Battle_Scars-20)

The scrappers having informed the Empire about Clone Force 99 being spotted on Bracca, Commander Crosshair and his [squad](/wiki/Elite_Squad_Trooper "Elite Squad Trooper") were sent to the planet with the order of terminating the clones. Clone Force 99 was spotted and then pursued by Imperials inside the wrecked *Venator*-class Star Destroyer[[22]](#cite_note-Reunion-22) they had previously used to remove their inhibitor chips.[[20]](#cite_note-Battle_Scars-20) Tech tapped into their comms and learned that Crosshair had ordered all squads to push them towards the hangar so that they could capture them. In reality, it was only a ruse used by Crosshair, familiar with the Bad Batch's strategies. Surrounded in the Venator's artillery deck, Crosshair stated that his resentment towards Clone Force 99 came from the fact that they were traitors. Hunter and Omega tried to reason with him, explaining that he was controlled by an inhibitor chip. Nevertheless, Crosshair ordered his troopers to aim for the young clone. Before the squad could target Omega, Tech and Echo activated the nearby [turbolasers](/wiki/Turbolaser "Turbolaser"), which resulted in the ceiling falling and Clone Force 99 having time to escape towards the [ion engines](/wiki/Ion_engine "Ion engine").[[22]](#cite_note-Reunion-22)

Crosshair soon managed to surround the Bad Batch once again. Crosshair's squad started firing up the engines, which would incinerate the clones if they stayed inside. Echo declared that it was impossible for the cruiser to be functional but Tech answered that he had previously restored the ship's main power core, meaning that the engines could indeed be activated. Tech and the rest of the group decided to place explosives all around the engine using what Wrecker had previously salvaged from the starship's [armory](/wiki/Armory "Armory"). After Crosshair ordered [ES-04](/wiki/ES-04 "ES-04") to engage the engines, the ship unleashed flames, knocking off Crosshair's helmet and burning his face.[[22]](#cite_note-Reunion-22)

On their way to the *Marauder*, Hunter and Omega were attacked by [Duros](/wiki/Duros "Duros") bounty hunter [Cad Bane](/wiki/Cad_Bane "Cad Bane"), who shot Hunter in the chest and kidnapped Omega after stunning her. After finding Hunter, Tech, Echo, and Wrecker took him to the ship, where, wounded, he declared that someone had taken Omega and that they needed to rescue her.[[22]](#cite_note-Reunion-22)

Tech stated that the bounty hunter detaining her was most likely long gone and that they would not be able to rescue her if their ship was shot down.[[23]](#cite_note-Bounty_Lost-23)

By consulting an old Republic database, Echo found out that Omega's kidnapper was in fact Cad Bane, the bounty hunter behind the [Senate Hostage Crisis](/wiki/Senate_hostage_crisis "Senate hostage crisis"). Tech concluded that Omega was valuable to the Kaminoans because she had first-generation [DNA](/wiki/DNA "DNA"), making her the only remaining pure genetic replication of Jango Fett, as [Boba](/wiki/Boba_Fett "Boba Fett"), originally named "Alpha", had disappeared during the Clone Wars. The clones realized that the Kaminoans were the ones behind the bounty. Hunter then ordered Tech to contact Scaleback in case she had more information. Meanwhile on [Bora Vio](/wiki/Bora_Vio "Bora Vio"), Omega had managed to escape the cell she was kept in and to use her comm device to contact Clone Force 99. Distraught, Omega said that she did not want to go back to Kamino and end up as an experiment, which Hunter promised would not happen.[[23]](#cite_note-Bounty_Lost-23)

#### Imperial occupation of Raxus Secundus[]

At some point after [Omega's rescue](/wiki/Rescue_of_Omega "Rescue of Omega"), Scaleback tasked Clone Force 99 with the mission of extracting [Senator](/wiki/Senator "Senator") [Avi Singh](/wiki/Avi_Singh "Avi Singh") from [Raxus Secundus](/wiki/Raxus_Secundus "Raxus Secundus"), the former [capital](/wiki/Capital "Capital") of the Confederacy of Independent Systems. When Omega declared that she wanted to come with the squad, Tech declared that with two bounty hunters after her, the young clone needed to keep a low profile, forcing her to stay on Ord Mantell. Aboard the *Marauder*, Tech explained that the coordinates given by Scaleback had them landing outside the [capital](/wiki/Capital "Capital") city of [Raxulon](/wiki/Raxulon "Raxulon"). After the *Marauder* descended into Raxus Secundus' atmosphere, the clones encountered two [Alpha-3 *Nimbus*-class V-wing starfighters](/wiki/Alpha-3_Nimbus-class_V-wing_starfighter "Alpha-3 Nimbus-class V-wing starfighter") who declared that they were flying into restricted airspace. When they requested clearance codes, Tech transmitted the codes that Scaleback had supplied. Echo expressed his disapproval at the idea of helping a separatist, which Tech considered to be irrelevant, as they were not acting in the name of political beliefs but as mercenaries.[[24]](#cite_note-Common_Ground-24)

On Raxus Secundus, the clones were greeted by Singh's droid, [GS-8](/wiki/GS-8 "GS-8") and learned that the senator had been arrested after he spoke against the imperial occupation of the planet. GS-8 led the squad to the [senator's residence](/wiki/Avi_Singh%27s_residence "Avi Singh's residence"), where Tech spotted six exterior guards and multiple heat signatures inside the residence, including four on the subterranean level. After using the droid as a distraction and taking out several clone troopers, Clone Force 99 infiltrated the compound. Inside, Tech tapped into its security system, allowing the clones to keep an eye on the Imperial patrols outside the residence. Ultimately, Hunter sent Wrecker and Echo to clear the upper levels while he and Tech took out the main floor. After another distraction caused by GS-8, Hunter and Tech were able to get rid of the guards and access the residence's lounge, that Imperial [Captain Bragg](/wiki/Bragg "Bragg") was using as an interrogation room. Before the captain could use an [IT-O interrogation unit](/wiki/IT-O_interrogation_droid "IT-O interrogation droid") on Singh, she was stunned by Tech and Hunter. Singh offered to help the clones but Tech revealed that their position had been compromised, an alarm having been triggered. Accompanied by Singh and GS-8, Tech and Hunter were joined by Wrecker and Echo on one of the residence's balconies, where the group spotted an [AT-TE walker](/wiki/All_Terrain_Tactical_Enforcer "All Terrain Tactical Enforcer").[[24]](#cite_note-Common_Ground-24)

After returning from a mission, Clone Force 99 discovered that Ord Mantell and Cid's Parlor had been taken over by the [Devaronian](/wiki/Devaronian "Devaronian") [male](/wiki/Gender "Gender") criminal [Roland Durand](/wiki/Roland_Durand "Roland Durand") from the [Durand crime family](/wiki/Durand_crime_family "Durand crime family") due to the planet's connection to several useful [hyperspace routes](/wiki/Hyperspace_route "Hyperspace route") used by the [Pyke Syndicate](/wiki/Pyke_Syndicate "Pyke Syndicate"). Initially, Tech speculated that Scaleback had been eliminated or forced out, which caused Hunter to believe it was better for the group to leave the planet. However, Scaleback turned out to be alive and blackmailed Clone Force 99 into helping her get her cantina back. She revealed that Durand would soon meet a buyer concerning a shipment of [spice](/wiki/Spice "Spice"). Tech realized that Scaleback wanted the clones to steal the shipment and sabotage the deal between Durand and the Pykes so that they could take care of him.[[27]](#cite_note-Infested-27)

The Pykes confronted Scaleback and demanded that the Bad Batch retrieve the lost spice. To show that this was not a matter up to negotiation, Omega was taken hostage. Clone Force 99 went back to the tunnels with the plan of distracting the irlings so that they could grab the crates without being attacked. Tech threw a [flash bomb](/wiki/Flash_grenade "Flash grenade") into the cavern below the group, which caused a bright explosion of light that scattered the creatures. It gave enough time for the *Marauder* pulley system to retrieve all the spice. The clones met with the Pyke Syndicate again and the conflict was said to be resolved. However, Roland did not have the same luck and had one of his horns cut off as a sign that the Pykes never accept bad deals. Out of gratitude, Scaleback offered to treat the clones and led them to Cid's Parlor.[[27]](#cite_note-Infested-27)

#### A distress call[]

:   "*Look at this. These are not clone troopers.*"

:   ―Tech and Gregor talking about the new Imperial stormtroopers[[28]](#cite_note-War-Mantle-28)

While the *Marauder* was traveling through hyperspace, the Bad Batch received a message from Rex, asking them to rescue the clone [CC-5576](/wiki/Gregor "Gregor") "Gregor", who had sent him a [distress signal](/wiki/Distress_signal "Distress signal"). Tech managed to discover that the distress signal originated from the planet [Daro](/wiki/Daro "Daro"), a terrestrial world in the [Outer Rim Territories](/wiki/Outer_Rim_Territories "Outer Rim Territories") with no known settlements.[[28]](#cite_note-War-Mantle-28)

When Clone Force 99 arrived on Daro, they realized that something was jamming their scanner, which should not be possible, as the planet was supposed to be uninhabited. Tech concluded that the data were no longer accurate. The group observed a shuttle landing at the base of one of the [Daro mountains](/wiki/Imperial_Base_on_Daro "Imperial Base on Daro"), which indicated the location of the settlement where Gregor was kept. Tech explained that the mountain's natural composition made the base nearly impossible to penetrable. Thus, Clone Force 99 understood that this was no longer a simple extraction and that a more complex plan was needed.[[28]](#cite_note-War-Mantle-28)

While Wrecker and Omega stayed aboard the ship in order to provide reinforcement if needed, Tech, Hunter, and Echo broke into the base. Tech tapped into the central database and pinpointed CC-5576's location. Meanwhile, Echo discovered that fifty clone commandos were present at the base, as well as a thousand [TK troopers](/wiki/Stormtrooper "Stormtrooper"), a designation that Tech was not familiar with. Freed from his cell, Gregor explained that the TK-Troopers were human [recruits](/wiki/Project_War-Mantle "Project War-Mantle") that he had been forced to train. Tech sent a signal to Omega and Wrecker so they could come to pick them up. However, while the clones were regaining the *Marauder*, Hunter lost his grip and fell from the ship. Despite Tech not wanting to leave him behind, Hunter insisted that they take themselves to safety. Reluctantly, Clone Force 99 and Gregor jumped into hyperspace, while Hunter was captured by stormtroopers.[[28]](#cite_note-War-Mantle-28)

When Hunter begged him to remove his inhibitor chip, Crosshair revealed that he had already done so a long time ago. #### Count Dooku's war chest[]

##### A dangerous mission[]

Several months after the fall of Tipoca City[[31]](#cite_note-Disney-Star_Wars:_The_Bad_Batch,_Season_2_Press_Kit-31), Clone Force 99, including Tech, was still working for Scaleback. They notably wore new armors, recolored and lacking the number "99" on them.[[32]](#cite_note-32) At some point, Clone Force 99 was offered a mission, which proved itself to be difficult, as it concerned the planet [Serenno](/wiki/Serenno "Serenno"), the homeworld of the late [Count Dooku](/wiki/Dooku "Dooku"), leader of the Confederacy of Independent Systems. The Bad Batch was tasked with stealing one of Dooku's [war chests](/wiki/Dooku%27s_war_chest "Dooku's war chest") from the [former castle](/wiki/Castle_Serenno "Castle Serenno"). Knowing that the Empire would most likely be present on Serenno, Hunter initially refused the mission. However, Tech, Echo, and Wrecker insisted that it would bring them a large amount of money, that they could use to free themselves from their debt to Scaleback. The latter pointed out that inevitably, Ord Mantell would be occupied by the Empire, something that Tech had also concluded.[[30]](#cite_note-Spoils_of_War-30)

On Serenno, Clone Force 99 observed that the planet had been [bombarded](/wiki/Orbital_bombardment "Orbital bombardment") by the Empire. Near a [destroyed Serennian city](/wiki/Bombardment_of_Serenno "Bombardment of Serenno"), Tech spotted several [class four container transports](/wiki/Class_four_container_transport "Class four container transport") departing from the planet and concluded that the group had to be quick if they wanted to steal the war chests. At the local Imperial shipyard, Tech, Echo, Wrecker, and Omega entered the containers while Hunter stayed behind to provide a distraction if needed. When Omega found a chest full of [credits](/wiki/Galactic_Credit_Standard "Galactic Credit Standard"), she asked Tech how someone could possibly be that rich. Tech answered that Dooku acquired these resources from the worlds he had exploited during the Clone Wars.[[30]](#cite_note-Spoils_of_War-30)

The local clone captain, [Captain](/wiki/Captain "Captain") [Wilco](/wiki/Wilco "Wilco"), soon realized that intruders were present at the shipyard and ordered the last remaining cargo ship to depart. As Wrecker ran away from the ship with one of the war chests, Tech, Echo, and Omega stayed behind, trapped in the cargo ship. Tech suggested that the group head to the escape pods but this idea was compromised when the pods were released to prevent the clones from using them. Omega then came up with the idea of ejecting the cargo and escaping the ship in one of them. Tech added that in order to do so, the containers needed to be ejected before leaving the [atmosphere](/wiki/Atmosphere "Atmosphere") of the planet. ##### Escaping Serenno[]

#### The clones' future[]

:   ―Senator Riyo Chuchi to the Bad Batch[[37]](#cite_note-Truth_and_Consequences-37)

Even though Tech and his squad had witnessed the fall of Tipoca City months prior,[[29]](#cite_note-Kamino_Lost-29) the galaxy had been informed that a cataclysmic storm had destroyed the city. Indeed, Vice Admiral Rampart had lied in order to ensure that his [Imperial Defense Recruitment Bill](/wiki/Imperial_Defense_Recruitment_Bill "Imperial Defense Recruitment Bill") would be approved by the [Senate](/wiki/Imperial_Senate "Imperial Senate"). Thus, the clones were contacted by Rex, who worked with Pantora Senator [Riyo Chuchi](/wiki/Riyo_Chuchi "Riyo Chuchi"), and were tasked with recovering a backup copy of the command log stored in Rampart's [*Venator*-class Star Destroyer's](/wiki/Venator-class_Star_Destroyer "Venator-class Star Destroyer") backup data banks, which proved that Kamino had been intentionally destroyed. Upon arriving on [Coruscant](/wiki/Coruscant "Coruscant"), the group decided that Omega would stay with the Senator while the other members of Clone Force 99 and Rex pulled the heist.[[37]](#cite_note-Truth_and_Consequences-37)

 [![Tech, his squad and Rex infiltrated a Venator-class Star Destroyer to recover data showing the destruction of Tipoca City.](data:image/gif;base64,R0lGODlhAQABAIABAAAAAP///yH5BAEAAAEALAAAAAABAAEAQAICTAEAOw%3D%3D)](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/starwars/images/1/13/Vz-114_destroyed.png/revision/latest?cb=20230209193844) [![Tech, his squad and Rex infiltrated a Venator-class Star Destroyer to recover data showing the destruction of Tipoca City.](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/starwars/images/1/13/Vz-114_destroyed.png/revision/latest/scale-to-width-down/235?cb=20230209193844)](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/starwars/images/1/13/Vz-114_destroyed.png/revision/latest?cb=20230209193844)

After Rex and Clone Force 99 infiltrated the Venator, Tech, in the command bridge, found out that the energy conduits were offline and that the data could only be retrieved by rerouting the power, which would inevitably trigger a security breach. Several clone troopers informed of their presence, Wrecker jammed the doors while Tech recovered the file. Rex and Clone Force 99 eventually had to fight several troopers but decided to fire up the Star Destroyer's engines to escape from the ship. The Star Destroyer thus tilted forward which resulted in the frontal support clamp collapsing. Having caused a distraction, the clones escaped with an escape pod and managed to bring the stolen data to Omega, which rushed to the Senate to give it to [Alderaan](/wiki/Alderaan "Alderaan") Senator [Bail Organa](/wiki/Bail_Prestor_Organa "Bail Prestor Organa"), allowing Chuchi to expose Rampart's actions in front of the entire council.[[37]](#cite_note-Truth_and_Consequences-37)

Tech wished him farewell and good luck, while Omega expressed her sorrow at seeing her brother leave the squad.[[37]](#cite_note-Truth_and_Consequences-37)

#### Stuck on Ipsidon[]

##### Cid's mine[]

At some point after Echo left the Batch, Scaleback purchased an [ipsium](/wiki/Ipsium "Ipsium") [mine](/wiki/Cid%27s_mine "Cid's mine") on a desert planet [Ipsidon](/wiki/Ipsidon "Ipsidon"). [Ipsium](/wiki/Ipsium "Ipsium") being a highly valuable [mineral](/wiki/Mineral "Mineral"), Clone Force 99 was tasked with retrieving some from the mine and bringing what they had found to Ord Mantell. Inside the mine, Tech noticed that it was almost empty and that Scaleback had been misled. The little ipsium available being practically out of reach and due to the volatile nature of the mineral, Tech pointed out that Omega would be most likely to [extract](/wiki/Ipsium_mining_tool "Ipsium mining tool") the ipsium, due to her size. However, as the clones managed to retrieve some ipsium, the *Marauder* was stolen by a masked stranger[[6]](#cite_note-The_Crossing-6), who later turned out to be [thief](/wiki/Thief "Thief") and [miner](/wiki/Miner "Miner") [Benni Baro](/wiki/Benni_Baro "Benni Baro").[[38]](#cite_note-Retrieval-38) Both Tech and Hunter reproached Wrecker for not properly guarding the ship. However, Tech checked his scanner and detected a [spaceport](/wiki/Spaceport "Spaceport") 40 [kilometers](/wiki/Kilometer "Kilometer") away from the mine. With no other option left, the clones started walking to the spaceport, in the hope they could get a ship there.[[6]](#cite_note-The_Crossing-6)

A stampede of [Ipsidon bucks](/wiki/Ipsidon_buck "Ipsidon buck") with a thunderous storm soon approached the clones and Tech pointed to another mine entrance they could hide into. While fleeing, Tech inadvertently dropped the canister the group had filled ipsium with early on, causing it to explode after being struck by a thunderbolt. The explosion also resulted in debris blocking the entrance of the mine, trapping the clones inside. Wrecker and Tech bickered about the situation, the latter blaming the former for losing the ship in the first place. Hunter then intervened, explaining that the clones could not afford to fight and had to dig their way out of the mine. While the clones removed as many rocks as they could, Omega tried to locate the *Marauder*, only to realize that its [transponder](/wiki/Transponder "Transponder") was not relaying any signal. Tech pointed out that the thief had been smart enough to disable the transponder and that it was unlikely that they would even see the *Marauder* again. Upset, Omega replied that the ship was their home and that they could not afford to lose it the same way they lost Echo. Not comprehending Omega's reaction, Tech stated that Clone Force 99 had existed before Echo and would survive his departure. Omega then tossed a device towards him and walked away. Confused, Tech declared that he had merely told the truth, to which Hunter replied that Omega already knew it.[[6]](#cite_note-The_Crossing-6)

##### Retrieval of the *Marauder*[]

The clones decided to repair a rusty [speeder bike](/wiki/Speeder_bike "Speeder bike") they had found in the abandoned spaceport. Indeed, Tech had proposed to use it in order to reach a town on the other half of the planet's hemisphere. Omega then remembered that their droid [Gonky](/wiki/Gonky_(Clone_Force_99) "Gonky (Clone Force 99)") was still aboard the *Marauder* and thus could be tracked through his binary reference code, something that Tech described as an ingenious idea. It turned out that their ship was still on the planet, about a hundred kilometers away from the spaceport. After traveling on the now salvaged speeder bike, the clones reached a [town](/wiki/Mokkotown "Mokkotown"), which contained an ipsium mine. Tech's scanner detected several lifeforms in subterranean levels, leading the group to enter the town.[[38]](#cite_note-Retrieval-38)

Clone Force 99 quickly located the thief of the ship, Benni Baro. Ambushing him, the latter revealed that he was working for the gangster [Mokko](/wiki/Mokko "Mokko"), who had taken control of their town from the Techno Union. Baro declared that the ship no longer belonged to the Bad Batch and initially refused to betray his boss, stating that Mokko had done a lot for him. He eventually agreed to lead the clones to their ship only if Mokko were never to learn of it.[[38]](#cite_note-Retrieval-38)

After Tech generated a hologram schematic of the mine, the clones and Baro entered the town, which was protected by a ray shield, through a factory stack. Entering its access hatch, Clone Force 99 witnessed several workers pushing crates of ipsium under the watch of armed droid sentries. When asked about the harsh working conditions, Baro explained that the ipsium was degrading, which had forced Mokko to lower wages. Once they reached the *Marauder*, Tech noticed that its [hyperdrive](/wiki/Hyperdrive "Hyperdrive") was missing. Baro declared that it would not be complicated to fix. As for the ray shield, Baro proposed that Omega follow him to the central tower, where it could be deactivated. Tech then told Omega to transfer the data onto a mini computer so that he could access it remotely. In the tower, Omega managed to collect the data but also found Mokko's profit reports, which indicated that ipsium had never degraded and shown it to Baro. However, he had previously contacted Mokko to tell him about Clone Force 99, in the hope he would earn an additional ration.[[38]](#cite_note-Retrieval-38)

The clones having been caught, Mokko confronted them on a suspension bridge inside the ipsium mine. Tech and Wrecker declared that they were here to get their ship back but Mokko answered that it was now his and warned that if they tried to shoot inside the mine, they could ignite ipsium and cause a massive explosion. When the clones answered that they did not miss when shooting, Mokko brought Omega on a higher suspension bridge and threaten to have her thrown down a chasm. Mokko then asked his workers to take their clones away as he intended to imprison them for ten [years](/wiki/Standard_year "Standard year") and make them work in the mines. However, the gangster was interrupted when Baro appeared with the data Omega has previously found, claiming that Mokko had been lying to them. When the reports were shown to other workers, including the miner [Drake](/wiki/Drake "Drake"), they confronted Mokko. Meanwhile, Hunter rescued Omega. Mokko attempted to defend himself by using droids armed with [electrostaffs](/wiki/Electrostaff "Electrostaff") but Tech and Wrecker quickly deactivated them with their blasters. Losing his balance, Mokko started falling but tried to drag Baro down with him. However, the latter was rescued by the other miners and Mokko eventually fell to his death.[[38]](#cite_note-Retrieval-38)

Later, Baro returned the clones' belongings and declared that now that they were free from Mokko, the workers would be getting an equal amount of profit. After Baro left, Omega told Tech that eventually, the Empire was not the only threat in the galaxy. #### The Zillo Beast[]

When the Bad Batch showed disinterest, Scaleback accepted to raise their cut of the share to 50%. After Hunter initially implied that cutting ties with Scaleback could be an eventuality, Tech reminded the group that the Trandoshan had compromising information about them and that they at least ought to complete this one last mission. However, Clone Force 99 soon realized that Scaleback had only sent coordinates without any ship transponder code or indication of what caused the ship to crash.[[39]](#cite_note-Metamorphosis-39)

After seeing the ship, Tech deduced that the crew had probably perished but Omega disagreed and urged the clones to take a look inside. After exploring the ship and noticing scratch marks, Hunter pointed out that whatever had killed the crew was not human. Thus, Tech decided to go restore the ship's power and check data logs on the bridge. Alone, Tech entered the ship's engine room and reactivated the power. As he was rejoining the group, Tech encountered a [medical droid](/wiki/Unidentified_droid_(Transport_904) "Unidentified droid (Transport 904)") who warned him about the danger before running out of power.[[39]](#cite_note-Metamorphosis-39)

Imperial forces soon arrived above the planet with three [*Venator*-class Star Destroyers](/wiki/Venator-class_Star_Destroyer "Venator-class Star Destroyer"), several [Low Altitude Assault Transport/infantry](/wiki/Low_Altitude_Assault_Transport/infantry "Low Altitude Assault Transport/infantry") [gunships](/wiki/Gunship "Gunship"), and a transport. #### Pabu[]

##### Hidden paradise[]

After [retrieving](/wiki/Shootout_at_Club_Lao_Che "Shootout at Club Lao Che") an old artifact from the criminal [Lanse Crowder](/wiki/Lanse_Crowder "Lanse Crowder") and his associates in [Club Lao Che](/wiki/Club_Lao_Che "Club Lao Che"), Clone Force 99 and Phee Genoa escaped and traveled in hyperspace with the *Marauder*. After Genoa explained that despite Omega's extraordinary soldier skills, she needed to learn more about the world and mix with friends her age, the clones received a transmission from Scaleback, who chastised them for not contacting her for the past twenty rotations. Genoa pointed out that the clones better not make Scaleback one their enemies and concluded that, due to their lack of plans, she could take them to [Pabu](/wiki/Pabu "Pabu"), a tropical planet with a [remote island](/wiki/Pabu_island "Pabu island") that the pirate described as her "home away from home".[[40]](#cite_note-Pabu-40)

Upon arriving on Pabu, Genoa told the clones that most of the island's inhabitants were refugees and that she was actively working on finding remnants of their cultures, stored in the [Archium](/wiki/Archium "Archium"), a structure in [Upper Pabu](/wiki/Upper_Pabu "Upper Pabu") housing artifacts from all around the galaxy. Indeed, Tech had noticed that the artifact that they had found earlier was of no monetary value.[[40]](#cite_note-Pabu-40)

The group soon met [Shep Hazard](/wiki/Shep_Hazard "Shep Hazard"), the [Mayor](/wiki/Mayor "Mayor") of the island and his daughter, [Lyana](/wiki/Lyana_Hazard "Lyana Hazard"), whom Omega quickly befriended. During a walk of the island, Hazard explained that Pabu was a friendly community that [expanded](/wiki/Lower_Pabu "Lower Pabu") over the years. When Omega and Lyana started playing with several simian [moon-yos](/wiki/Moonyo "Moonyo"), Tech pointed out that he had not heard Omega laugh like this for a long time. Shep Hazard explained that the island was a good place to raise a child, to which Hunter asked if the mayor was implying that the clones could stay here permanently. After a feast, Omega and Lyana went to watch the sunset on the latter's motorized [boat](/wiki/Boat "Boat") while the clones stayed with Genoa and Hazard. However, Hunter warned the group that something was coming when he felt the ground shaking and contacted the two children, urging them to return to the shore.[[40]](#cite_note-Pabu-40)

Tech warned that the island was about to suffer from a [sea surge](/wiki/Tsunami "Tsunami"), but Hazard answered that the island had not experienced one in more than three decades. As the residents started taking shelter, Tech advised Hazard to evacuate Lower Pabu, while Hunter declared that he would use the *Marauder* to rescue Omega and Lyana. As the residents evacuated to Upper Pabu, Tech and Genoa manually lowered the rescue ladders to enable more people to take shelter. Meanwhile, Hunter rescued Omega and Lyana while Wrecker and Hazard helped the [last resident](/wiki/Eenta "Eenta") escape.[[40]](#cite_note-Pabu-40)

The ocean waves eventually devastated most of Lower Pabu. Sometime after the [tsunami](/wiki/Tsunami "Tsunami"), Mayor Hazard expressed his relief at evacuating every resident, Tech explained that it would take several rotations to repair what had been destroyed. Eventually, Clone Force 99 accepted to stay on the island to help the citizens rebuild their city.[[40]](#cite_note-Pabu-40)

#### Infiltrating Raven's Peak[]

:   "*We have not always agreed with Crosshair, but he is still our brother. We do not leave our own behind.*"
:   ―Tech, on Clone Force 99's mission to Eriadu[[42]](#cite_note-The_Summit-42)

After Tech found no information on Hemlock, Echo revealed that his network had learned that the doctor would be attending an Imperial summit on Eriadu, Tarkin's homeworld. With the plan of planting a [homing beacon](/wiki/Homing_beacon "Homing beacon") on Hemlock's ship, the clones decided to travel to Tarkin's compound and learn where Crosshair was being detained. However, before leaving Pabu, Genoa came to see Tech, hinting at her feelings for him and asking him not to get involved with any other pirate and mercenary during the mission.[[42]](#cite_note-The_Summit-42)

The clones infiltrated Tarkin's compound, Raven's Peak [in the background].

 

In hyperspace, Tech explained that he had found a suitable location to land on Eriadu. Echo added that the clones would manage to bypass Imperial [cruisers](/wiki/Cruiser "Cruiser") with a fake clearance code he had acquired thanks to one of his contacts. After descending into the mountainous terrain of the planet, the *Marauder* landed near a forest, allowing the clones to have a view of the fortified compound. Clone Force 99 decided to infiltrate the facility via the [rail line](/wiki/Raven%27s_Peak_Sky_Rail "Raven's Peak Sky Rail"), whose sensor platform Tech deactivated using a grappling cable. Fooling the imperial technicians present, the clones reached the compound, killing several TK stormtroopers in the process. In the hangar bay, Tech went with Hunter and Echo to infiltrate the control room, while Wrecker and Omega were tasked with planting the beacon.[[42]](#cite_note-The_Summit-42)

Omega planted the device but ended up being pinned down by stormtroopers, forcing Echo to remotely lift a parked V-wing starfighter and slam it against several cargo boxes to distract the troopers. In the control room, Tech soon started feeling uncomfortable, as he noticed that several systems had been deactivated. Indeed, Hunter and Tech soon realized that the [Partisans](/wiki/Partisans "Partisans"), Saw Gerrera's resistance group, were also present in the compound and were organizing an attack by planting [thermal explosives](/wiki/Thermal_detonator "Thermal detonator").[[42]](#cite_note-The_Summit-42)

Soon after, the clones encountered Gerrera himself, as well as another female Partisan, dressed as stormtroopers. Gerrera reminded them of their [previous encounter](/wiki/Mission_to_Onderon_(Clone_Force_99) "Mission to Onderon (Clone Force 99)") on Onderon and declared that the clones had indeed made their choice concerning their allegiance toward the Empire. He then explained that his plan was to destroy the base and kill the high-ranking Imperials in it, not caring about the gathering intelligence he would destroy in the process. Tech told Gerrera that killing these Imperials would not matter, as they could soon be replaced but Gerrera decided to keep on with the attack. Pursued by troopers, the two groups split up. The Bad Batch reunited and reached the rail line, while the Partisans flew off aboard a shuttle. As Tech overrode the manual controls of the rail line, Gerrera activated the explosives, resulting in the main hangar being destroyed, including Omega's tracking beacon. However, Tarkin had predicted the outcome of the attack and had the facility's conference chamber sealed.[[42]](#cite_note-The_Summit-42)

However, the clones could not prevent their car from being damaged by the starfighters, causing it to hang loose. ### Legacy[]

:   "*Wake up, Wrecker. Clone Force 99 died with Tech. We're not that squad anymore.*"
:   ―Crosshair, regarding Tech's death[[43]](#cite_note-Cavalry-43)

 [![Tech's broken goggles after his fall.](data:image/gif;base64,R0lGODlhAQABAIABAAAAAP///yH5BAEAAAEALAAAAAABAAEAQAICTAEAOw%3D%3D)](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/starwars/images/8/85/Tech_broken_goggle_TBBS2.png/revision/latest?cb=20230330044206) [![Tech's broken goggles after his fall.](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/starwars/images/8/85/Tech_broken_goggle_TBBS2.png/revision/latest/scale-to-width-down/225?cb=20230330044206)](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/starwars/images/8/85/Tech_broken_goggle_TBBS2.png/revision/latest?cb=20230330044206) 

Tech's broken goggles after his fall.

 

Tech's sacrifice allowed Clone Force 99 to get back to the *Marauder* and leave Eriadu. Imperial forces led by Hemlock soon located the wreckage and discovered Tech's broken goggles at the site, the only thing that Hemlock could salvage from him. Following the remaining members of Clone Force 99's escape to Ord Mantell, Omega asked Hunter and Wrecker where Tech was. Hunter replied that Tech had not made it, much to Omega's sadness. Hunter explained the importance of Tech's sacrifice, as Tech had put the needs of the squad ahead of his own life. Soon enough, the Bad Batch was [betrayed](/wiki/Capture_of_Omega "Capture of Omega") by Scaleback and sold out to the Empire, resulting in Omega's capture. When Imperial forces arrived to apprehend the squad, Hemlock returned Tech's goggles to Hunter, mocking his death.[[3]](#cite_note-Plan_99-3) Months later,[[44]](#cite_note-Confined-44) the squad still kept Tech's goggles aboard the *Marauder*.[[45]](#cite_note-Paths_Unknown-45)

When Crosshair and Omega [fled](/wiki/Escape_from_Tantiss "Escape from Tantiss") the [Tantiss Base](/wiki/Tantiss_Base "Tantiss Base") of [Mount Tantiss](/wiki/Mount_Tantiss "Mount Tantiss"), Omega confided to Crosshair that Tech had taught her all the plans Clone Force 99 used, including [Plan 72](/wiki/Plan_72 "Plan 72").[[46]](#cite_note-Shadows_of_Tantiss-46) After Crosshair and Omega regrouped with the rest of the Bad Batch, they provided them with Nala Se's datapad containing information regarding Tantiss, which Echo stated would be difficult to decrypt without Tech.[[47]](#cite_note-TheReturn-47) Phee Genoa later remarked to the Bad Batch—as they were searching for information regarding Hemlock's [m-count](/wiki/Midi-chlorian "Midi-chlorian") research—that finding such information came easier to Tech than it did to her.[[48]](#cite_note-BadTerritory-48)

Eventually, Omega became captive on Tantiss once again. During a [mission](/wiki/Skirmish_on_Wayland "Skirmish on Wayland") to rescue her, Crosshair asserted that Clone Force 99 had died with Tech. Concerned that another member of the squad wouldn't make it out of the base, he volunteered to go in alone. Hunter opposed Crosshair, telling him not to even consider [Plan 99](/wiki/Plan_99 "Plan 99"). Hunter and Wrecker assured Crosshair that Tech knew the risks of going on dangerous missions and that they were still a squad regardless. Eventually, all of Tech's squad managed to survive and rescue Omega, killing Hemlock before they finally settled down peacefully on [Pabu](/wiki/Pabu "Pabu").[[43]](#cite_note-Cavalry-43)

Some time later, when Omega had reached adulthood, she set off to join the [Rebel Alliance](/wiki/Alliance_to_Restore_the_Republic "Alliance to Restore the Republic") as a [pilot](/wiki/Rebel_pilot "Rebel pilot"). Tech's goggles remained with Omega aboard her ship as she left Pabu.[[43]](#cite_note-Cavalry-43)

## Personality and traits[]

:   "*Tech… what was the war like?*"  
    "*It was a primary mission objective comprised of battles on various fronts.*"  
    "*But… what was it like?*"  
    "*Hmm? I just told you.*"
:   ―Omega asking Tech what the Clone Wars were like, and Tech's response[[22]](#cite_note-Reunion-22)

 [![Tech was a master of anything relating to technology.](data:image/gif;base64,R0lGODlhAQABAIABAAAAAP///yH5BAEAAAEALAAAAAABAAEAQAICTAEAOw%3D%3D)](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/starwars/images/d/dd/TechCF99.png/revision/latest?cb=20241218033740) [![Tech was a master of anything relating to technology.](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/starwars/images/d/dd/TechCF99.png/revision/latest/scale-to-width-down/200?cb=20241218033740)](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/starwars/images/d/dd/TechCF99.png/revision/latest?cb=20241218033740) 

Tech was a master of anything relating to technology.

 

While not a physically imposing individual,[[49]](#cite_note-Tech_old_databank-49) Tech's advanced data analysis skills allowed him to solve technical issues at speeds even faster than some droids.[[4]](#cite_note-Tech_Databank-4) As a soldier whose genetic manipulations gave him[[10]](#cite_note-Tech_old-10) an unmatched gift for science and technology,[[49]](#cite_note-Tech_old_databank-49) enabling him to surpass men and machines alike in those fields, he was a valuable member of the [Grand Army of the Republic](/wiki/Grand_Army_of_the_Republic "Grand Army of the Republic"), even if he did not resemble his typical clone siblings, as he appeared leaner, fairer, and younger than his fellow clone troopers.[[10]](#cite_note-Tech_old-10)

Personality-wise, Tech was very talkative and communicative,[[1]](#cite_note-CWCE_JtB-1) earning him his status as the most talkative Bad Batch member.[[4]](#cite_note-Tech_Databank-4) One of his major quirks was his eagerness to dispense trivial knowledge about any given subject without hesitation or reason.[[1]](#cite_note-CWCE_JtB-1) He did this regardless of whether anyone was listening to him, or whether anyone else cared about what he was saying, as Wrecker pointed out at one point. When confronted with a task which demanded his full concentration, he tended to become oblivious to what was going on in his surroundings, unless it was a matter of life and death.[[11]](#cite_note-A_Distant_Echo-11) Tech could often miss the intention behind a question someone asked him, such as when Omega asked him what the [Clone Wars](/wiki/Clone_Wars "Clone Wars") were like.[[22]](#cite_note-Reunion-22) Like the rest of the Bad Batch, Tech became protective of Omega and acted as a mentor for her.

 [![In spite of appearing less imposing than his squadmates, Tech was still an able fighter.](data:image/gif;base64,R0lGODlhAQABAIABAAAAAP///yH5BAEAAAEALAAAAAABAAEAQAICTAEAOw%3D%3D)](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/starwars/images/6/6c/DoWhatWeDo-SWL119.png/revision/latest?cb=20240831164828) [![In spite of appearing less imposing than his squadmates, Tech was still an able fighter.](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/starwars/images/6/6c/DoWhatWeDo-SWL119.png/revision/latest/scale-to-width-down/250?cb=20240831164828)](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/starwars/images/6/6c/DoWhatWeDo-SWL119.png/revision/latest?cb=20240831164828) 

In spite of appearing less imposing than his squadmates, Tech was still an able fighter.

 

He also made an effort to record all sentient sounds he encountered, which Hunter described as a "hobby" of his. On one occasion, this helped the squad, alongside [Jedi Knight](/wiki/Jedi_Knight "Jedi Knight") [Anakin Skywalker](/wiki/Anakin_Skywalker "Anakin Skywalker"), [Rex](/wiki/Rex "Rex"), and [Echo](/wiki/Echo "Echo") escape from an enemy [base](/wiki/Purkoll_Tower_Base "Purkoll Tower Base").[[12]](#cite_note-On_the_Wings_of_Keeradaks-12) Tech was also very calm and non-confrontational, and rarely grew angry. When his squad-mates would argue with each other, he would frequently walk away and not participate. Much like Hunter and unlike his other two teammates, Tech was mostly civil and respectful to his fellow clones.[[1]](#cite_note-CWCE_JtB-1) Sometimes, his pragmatism made him fail to take into account the feelings of other members of his squad, like Omega.[[4]](#cite_note-Tech_Databank-4)

Able to comprehend and notice things easily, Tech occasionally had a habit of unintentionally keeping important information secret, assuming that his teammates would notice it on their own, such as Omega's true nature, which he described as being "obvious," or the clones being programmed through inhibitor chips.[[15]](#cite_note-Cut_and_Run-15)

## Equipment[]

 [![Tech's visor](data:image/gif;base64,R0lGODlhAQABAIABAAAAAP///yH5BAEAAAEALAAAAAABAAEAQAICTAEAOw%3D%3D)](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/starwars/images/8/83/TechVisor-GoH.png/revision/latest?cb=20231001014247) [![Tech's visor](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/starwars/images/8/83/TechVisor-GoH.png/revision/latest?cb=20231001014247)](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/starwars/images/8/83/TechVisor-GoH.png/revision/latest?cb=20231001014247) 

Tech's visor

 

Tech made use of a heavily modified suit of [*Katarn*-class Commando Armor](/wiki/Katarn-class_commando_armor "Katarn-class commando armor") with a unique [helmet](/wiki/Helmet "Helmet"), which held a [specialized visor](/wiki/Data_visor "Data visor") that acted as a computer display.[[1]](#cite_note-CWCE_JtB-1)[[11]](#cite_note-A_Distant_Echo-11) The helmet was equipped with a translation visor that was capable of translating various languages.[[12]](#cite_note-On_the_Wings_of_Keeradaks-12) A computer terminal was installed into the forearm portion of his armor, enabling him to access a number of versatile functions, such as trajectory calculation,[[1]](#cite_note-CWCE_JtB-1)[[11]](#cite_note-A_Distant_Echo-11) translation of alien languages,[[11]](#cite_note-A_Distant_Echo-11) and recording and replaying sound effects.[[12]](#cite_note-On_the_Wings_of_Keeradaks-12) Tech also carried a [datapad](/wiki/Datapad "Datapad") which he used to slice into other technology.[[9]](#cite_note-The_Bad_Batch-9)

Tech's primary weapons were two [DC-17 hand blasters](/wiki/DC-17_repeater_hand_blaster "DC-17 repeater hand blaster"), with which he was very proficient.[[1]](#cite_note-CWCE_JtB-1) During a [mission](/wiki/Mission_to_a_droid_decommissioning_facility "Mission to a droid decommissioning facility") in [Corellia](/wiki/Corellia "Corellia"), he carried a [DC-15A blaster carbine](/wiki/DC-15A_blaster_carbine "DC-15A blaster carbine").[[19]](#cite_note-Decommissioned-19)

## Behind the scenes[]

### Conception and *The Clone Wars*[]

 [![Tech, overseeing the attacking beasts in the unfinished episodes on Kashyyyk.](data:image/gif;base64,R0lGODlhAQABAIABAAAAAP///yH5BAEAAAEALAAAAAABAAEAQAICTAEAOw%3D%3D)](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/starwars/images/1/15/Tech_on_kashyyyk.png/revision/latest?cb=20170608192728) [![Tech, overseeing the attacking beasts in the unfinished episodes on Kashyyyk.](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/starwars/images/1/15/Tech_on_kashyyyk.png/revision/latest/scale-to-width-down/250?cb=20170608192728)](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/starwars/images/1/15/Tech_on_kashyyyk.png/revision/latest?cb=20170608192728) 

Tech, overseeing the attacking beasts in the unfinished episodes on Kashyyyk.

 

[George Lucas](/wiki/George_Lucas "George Lucas") had a very specific vision of the Bad Batch, in that he wanted a version of [The Dirty Dozen](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/The_Dirty_Dozen "wikipedia:The Dirty Dozen") with clones. [Dave Filoni](/wiki/Dave_Filoni "Dave Filoni") sketched their appearances which [Brent Friedman](/wiki/Brent_Friedman "Brent Friedman") and [Matt Michnovetz](/wiki/Matt_Michnovetz "Matt Michnovetz") used to imagine the Bad Batch's voices and personalities. Matt and Brent worked with Dave to flesh out the characters and their story.[[50]](#cite_note-Bad_Batch_Panel-50) Tech was to appear in a four episode arc written by Michnovetz[[51]](#cite_note-BFriedman_Yoda_Kashyyyk_tweet-51) which starred the Bad Batch, now including "Echo", alongside [Yoda](/wiki/Yoda "Yoda") on [Kashyyyk](/wiki/Kashyyyk "Kashyyyk").[[52]](#cite_note-Untold_Clone_Wars_Panel-52)

At the "Untold Clone Wars" panel at [Celebration Anaheim](/wiki/Celebration_Anaheim_(2015) "Celebration Anaheim (2015)"), Dave Filoni talked about the plot of the Kashyyyk arc. He revealed that the characters would ride on "giant ape lizards," that were holy to the [Wookiees](/wiki/Wookiee "Wookiee") in a religious way. In one scene [Tarfful](/wiki/Tarfful "Tarfful") would ask the tree spirits for permission to go into battle against the enemy, which was a [Trandoshan](/wiki/Trandoshan "Trandoshan") and the Separatist Alliance. To get the enemy out of some of their cities they would have to burn some of the trees. The Clones had no problem with it, but it was upsetting to the Wookiees. A part of the arc would be about the Clones and the Wookiees finding a common ground to fight the Separatists. A story reel clip was shown at the panel, which showed the Bad Batch and other Wookiees riding on the "ape lizards" and battle against [kinraths](/wiki/Kinrath/Legends "Kinrath/Legends") from the *[Star Wars: Knights of the Old Republic](/wiki/Star_Wars:_Knights_of_the_Old_Republic "Star Wars: Knights of the Old Republic")* game.[[52]](#cite_note-Untold_Clone_Wars_Panel-52)

Tech in *Star Wars: The Bad Batch* Season 2

 

According to *Bad Batch* producers [Jennifer Corbett](/wiki/Jennifer_Corbett "Jennifer Corbett") and [Brad Rau](/wiki/Brad_Rau "Brad Rau"), Tech's death was a hard decision to reach but was ultimately necessary in order to give a mission into Tarkin's headquarters a realistic weight. They also reasoned it was a noble sacrifice for his family.[[54]](#cite_note-Connections_to_Other-54)

Voice actor [Dee Bradley Baker](/wiki/Dee_Bradley_Baker "Dee Bradley Baker") believed Tech's death was "an amazing finish" and was a moment where Tech, thinking of all the ways to save his family and not even considering how to save himself, came to what Baker thought was "the correct answer" that ensured everything the Bad Batch stood for would survive.[[55]](#cite_note-AmazingFinish-55) Asked to give Tech one final line after his death at a convention, Baker complied and had Tech calm the audience about his passing; in Tech's voice, he reasoned that he had completed his mission by protecting his siblings.[[53]](#cite_note-Tech_final_line-53)

### Contradictions[]

According to *[Star Wars: The Clone Wars: Character Encyclopedia - Join the Battle!](/wiki/Star_Wars:_The_Clone_Wars:_Character_Encyclopedia_-_Join_the_Battle! "Star Wars: The Clone Wars: Character Encyclopedia - Join the Battle!")*, Tech stood 1.93 [meters](/wiki/Meter "Meter")—or 6 [ft](/wiki/Foot "Foot"), 4 inches—tall.[[1]](#cite_note-CWCE_JtB-1) In [2024](/wiki/2024 "2024"), however, the online [StarWars.com](/wiki/StarWars.com "StarWars.com") [Databank](/wiki/Databank_(2014-) "Databank (2014-)") was updated to give Tech the new height of 1.82 [meters](/wiki/Meter "Meter"),[[4]](#cite_note-Tech_Databank-4) whereas previously the Databank had not given any height for the character.[[56]](#cite_note-Tech_2024_old-56) As the 2024-updated Databank is the most recent source, this article assumes it to be correct.
