## Yoda's old Master

## Chronological & political information

### Affiliation(s)

[Jedi Order](/wiki/Jedi_Order "Jedi Order")[[1]](#cite_note-M&A-1)

### Apprentices

[Yoda](/wiki/Yoda "Yoda") ([Padawan](/wiki/Padawan "Padawan"))[[1]](#cite_note-M&A-1)

[[Source]](https://starwars.fandom.com/wiki/Template:Character)

:   "*To live without spears. Without war. For man and mountain. This I can teach you. As my Master once taught me.*"
:   ―Yoda[[2]](#cite_note-SW_30-2)

A **member** of the [Jedi Order](/wiki/Jedi_Order "Jedi Order") took on [Yoda](/wiki/Yoda "Yoda") as a [Padawan](/wiki/Padawan "Padawan") when Yoda was young,[[1]](#cite_note-M&A-1) more than eight hundred [years](/wiki/Standard_year "Standard year") before the [reign](/wiki/Imperial_Era "Imperial Era") of the [Galactic Empire](/wiki/Galactic_Empire "Galactic Empire"). At some point, Yoda's [teacher](/wiki/Teacher "Teacher") told their apprentice an insightful story about two [Jedi](/wiki/Jedi "Jedi") named [K'ungfu](/wiki/K%27ungfu "K'ungfu") and [Chuang](/wiki/Chuang "Chuang"),[[3]](#cite_note-SYWtBaJ-3) and taught him how to live in peace.[[2]](#cite_note-SW_30-2) While Yoda eventually became arguably the greatest Jedi [Grand Master](/wiki/Grand_Master "Grand Master") in [history](/wiki/Timeline_of_galactic_history "Timeline of galactic history"), the information on his Master's identity was lost.[[4]](#cite_note-Helmet_77-4)

During a [mission](/wiki/Mission_to_Vagadarr_Prime "Mission to Vagadarr Prime") to the [planet](/wiki/Planet "Planet")[[2]](#cite_note-SW_30-2) [Vagadarr Prime](/wiki/Vagadarr_Prime "Vagadarr Prime") around the year [40 BBY](/wiki/40_BBY "40 BBY"),[[5]](#cite_note-SWT-5) Yoda swore to pass on his Master's teachings and end the [war](/wiki/War_on_Vagadarr_Prime "War on Vagadarr Prime") between local [human](/wiki/Human "Human") tribes and [giants of living stone](/wiki/Giants_of_living_stone "Giants of living stone"). When Yoda had become the last [Jedi Master](/wiki/Jedi_Master "Jedi Master") in existence, he told his Master's story about K'ungfu and Chuang to [Luke Skywalker](/wiki/Luke_Skywalker "Luke Skywalker") during the latter's [Jedi training](/wiki/Jedi_training "Jedi training") on the planet [Dagobah](/wiki/Dagobah "Dagobah")[[3]](#cite_note-SYWtBaJ-3) in [3 ABY](/wiki/3_ABY "3 ABY").[[5]](#cite_note-SWT-5)

Yoda's old Master was first mentioned, albeit unnamed, in the current *[Star Wars](/wiki/Star_Wars "Star Wars")* [canon](/wiki/Canon "Canon") in *[The Empire Strikes Back: So You Want to Be a Jedi?](/wiki/The_Empire_Strikes_Back:_So_You_Want_to_Be_a_Jedi%3F "The Empire Strikes Back: So You Want to Be a Jedi?")*, a young-readers book which retells the events of[[3]](#cite_note-SYWtBaJ-3) the [1980](/wiki/1980 "1980") film[[7]](#cite_note-Year_By_Year-7) [*Star Wars*: Episode V *The Empire Strikes Back*](/wiki/Star_Wars:_Episode_V_The_Empire_Strikes_Back "Star Wars: Episode V The Empire Strikes Back"). The book was written by [Adam Gidwitz](/wiki/Adam_Gidwitz "Adam Gidwitz") and published in [2015](/wiki/2015 "2015").[[3]](#cite_note-SYWtBaJ-3)

Within the [*Star Wars* Legends](/wiki/Star_Wars_Legends "Star Wars Legends") continuity, Yoda's [Jedi](/wiki/Jedi/Legends "Jedi/Legends") [instructor](/wiki/Teacher/Legends "Teacher/Legends") was [N'Kata Del Gormo](/wiki/N%27Kata_Del_Gormo/Legends "N'Kata Del Gormo/Legends"), a [Hysalrian](/wiki/Hysalrian/Legends "Hysalrian/Legends") [Jedi Master](/wiki/Jedi_Master/Legends "Jedi Master/Legends"),[[8]](#cite_note-DAA-8) according to an [in-universe](/wiki/In-universe "In-universe") legend.[[9]](#cite_note-Leland_Twitter-9) The character was created by [Chuck Hamilton](/wiki/Chuck_Hamilton "Chuck Hamilton"), a *Star Wars* fan, for the backstory of his Hysalrian entry in the "[Design An Alien](/wiki/Design_An_Alien "Design An Alien")" contest held in the *[Star Wars Galaxy Magazine](/wiki/Star_Wars_Galaxy_Magazine "Star Wars Galaxy Magazine")*. Hamilton's entry was given an honorable mention in the [ninth issue](/wiki/Star_Wars_Galaxy_Magazine_9 "Star Wars Galaxy Magazine 9") of the magazine,[[8]](#cite_note-DAA-8) released in [October](/wiki/October "October") [1996](/wiki/1996 "1996").[[10]](#cite_note-SWGM8-10) The [2024](/wiki/2024 "2024") novel *[Mace Windu: The Glass Abyss](/wiki/Mace_Windu:_The_Glass_Abyss "Mace Windu: The Glass Abyss")* by [Steven Barnes](/wiki/Steven_Barnes "Steven Barnes") re-introduces the character of [N'Kata Del Gormo](/wiki/N%27Kata_Del_Gormo "N'Kata Del Gormo") to the current canon as a rumored early instructor of Yoda, albeit not specifying whether they were the latter's Jedi Master.[[11]](#cite_note-Glass_Abyss-11)

According to *Star Wars* veteran [Dave Filoni](/wiki/Dave_Filoni "Dave Filoni"), [Frank Oz](/wiki/Frank_Oz "Frank Oz"),[[6]](#cite_note-Nerdist-6) who provided both the voice and puppet work for Yoda in the [*Star Wars* saga](/wiki/Star_Wars_saga "Star Wars saga"),[[7]](#cite_note-Year_By_Year-7) believed that the character famously [spoke](/wiki/Language "Language") in an unusual, backward manner in honor of his Master.[[6]](#cite_note-Nerdist-6)
