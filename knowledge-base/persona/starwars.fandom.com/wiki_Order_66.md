**Order 66**, also known as **Clone Protocol 66**, **Protocol 66**, or simply "**the Order**," was one of top-secret [contingency orders](/wiki/Executive_Order_Codes_for_the_Grand_Army_of_the_Republic "Executive Order Codes for the Grand Army of the Republic") that identified all [Jedi](/wiki/Jedi "Jedi") as [traitors](/wiki/Treason "Treason") to the [Galactic Republic](/wiki/Galactic_Republic "Galactic Republic") and, therefore, subject to [summary execution](/wiki/Death_penalty "Death penalty") by the [Grand Army of the Republic](/wiki/Grand_Army_of_the_Republic "Grand Army of the Republic"). The order was programmed into the Grand [Army](/wiki/Army "Army") [clone troopers](/wiki/Clone_trooper "Clone trooper") through [behavioral modification biochips](/wiki/Behavioral_modification_biochip "Behavioral modification biochip") implanted in their [brains](/wiki/Brain "Brain") from creation, making it almost impossible for the [clones](/wiki/Cloning "Cloning") to disobey the command to turn against their [Jedi Generals](/wiki/Jedi_General "Jedi General"). The [Kaminoan](/wiki/Kaminoan "Kaminoan") [scientists](/wiki/Scientist "Scientist") who designed the clone [troopers](/wiki/Trooper "Trooper") believed it was only to be used as a contingency protocol against renegade Jedi. In secret, Order 66 was the means by which the [Sith](/wiki/Sith "Sith") intended to bring about the [long-awaited](/wiki/Timeline_of_galactic_history "Timeline of galactic history") fall of the [Jedi Order](/wiki/Jedi_Order "Jedi Order").

During the [Clone Wars](/wiki/Clone_Wars "Clone Wars"), the Sith agenda was progressing relatively unnoticed until a [premature activation](/wiki/Battle_of_Ringo_Vinda "Battle of Ringo Vinda") of Order 66 resulted in the [death](/wiki/Death "Death") of [General](/wiki/General "General") [Tiplar](/wiki/Tiplar "Tiplar") at the hands of [Tup](/wiki/Tup "Tup"), a defective clone whose malfunctioning chip caused him to betray his Jedi [officer](/wiki/Officer "Officer"). The incident led [ARC trooper](/wiki/Advanced_Recon_Commando "Advanced Recon Commando") [Fives](/wiki/Fives "Fives") to conduct his own investigation into the matter, believing the incident to be a [Separatist](/wiki/Confederacy_of_Independent_Systems "Confederacy of Independent Systems") plot against the Republic. On the [galactic](/wiki/Galaxy "Galaxy") [capital](/wiki/Capital "Capital") [Coruscant](/wiki/Coruscant "Coruscant"), however, he learned that the chips conditioned the clones to become hostile against the Jedi. Furthermore, he discovered to an extent [Supreme Chancellor](/wiki/Supreme_Chancellor "Supreme Chancellor") [Sheev Palpatine's](/wiki/Darth_Sidious "Darth Sidious") involvement in the conspiracy and [attempted](/wiki/Escape_from_the_Grand_Republic_Medical_Facility "Escape from the Grand Republic Medical Facility") to [assassinate](/wiki/Assassin "Assassin") him. Fives told what he knew to his Jedi superior [Anakin Skywalker](/wiki/Anakin_Skywalker "Anakin Skywalker"), but before the clone could prove it, he was [killed](/wiki/Mission_to_Level_1325 "Mission to Level 1325") by [clone shock troopers](/wiki/Imperial_shock_trooper "Imperial shock trooper") of the [Coruscant Guard](/wiki/Coruscant_Guard "Coruscant Guard").

The plan continued apace for the remainder of the war, although the [Jedi High Council](/wiki/Jedi_High_Council "Jedi High Council") had grown increasingly wary of the Chancellor's motives. In [19 BBY](/wiki/19_BBY "19 BBY"), a group of [Jedi Masters](/wiki/Jedi_Master "Jedi Master") sought to [arrest Palpatine](/wiki/Duel_in_Palpatine%27s_office "Duel in Palpatine's office") upon learning his true identity as the [Sith Lord](/wiki/Sith_Lord "Sith Lord") [Darth](/wiki/Darth "Darth") Sidious. When their efforts failed, Palpatine used the incident to frame the Jedi Order for treason and, as such, [forcibly compelled](/wiki/Behavioral_modification_biochip "Behavioral modification biochip") clone army to execute their Jedi [commanding officers](/wiki/Commanding_officer "Commanding officer") in accordance with Order 66.

Across [the galaxy](/wiki/The_galaxy "The galaxy"), thousands of Jedi were betrayed and killed by their own [troops](/wiki/Troop "Troop") while the Chancellor's new [apprentice](/wiki/Sith_apprentice "Sith apprentice"), the Sith [Lord](/wiki/Lord "Lord") Darth Vader, formerly Jedi Knight Anakin Skywalker, led the [501st Legion](/wiki/501st_Legion "501st Legion") in a [direct assault](/wiki/Operation:_Knightfall "Operation: Knightfall") on the [Jedi Temple](/wiki/Imperial_Palace "Imperial Palace") of Coruscant. With the exception of a few surviving Jedi, such as [Grand Master](/wiki/Grand_Master "Grand Master") [Yoda](/wiki/Yoda "Yoda"), [Jedi Master](/wiki/Jedi_Master "Jedi Master") [Obi-Wan Kenobi](/wiki/Obi-Wan_Kenobi "Obi-Wan Kenobi"), former [Padawan](/wiki/Padawan "Padawan") [Ahsoka Tano](/wiki/Ahsoka_Tano "Ahsoka Tano"), Padawans [Kanan Jarrus](/wiki/Kanan_Jarrus "Kanan Jarrus") and [Cal Kestis](/wiki/Cal_Kestis "Cal Kestis"), and [Jedi Knight](/wiki/Jedi_Knight "Jedi Knight") [Cere Junda](/wiki/Cere_Junda "Cere Junda"), as well as [Kelleran Beq](/wiki/Kelleran_Beq "Kelleran Beq") and youngling [Grogu](/wiki/Din_Grogu "Din Grogu"), the [Great Jedi Purge](/wiki/Great_Jedi_Purge "Great Jedi Purge") was largely accomplished through the initial activation of Order 66. Even after the initial onslaught, Order 66 still stood, and all Jedi, even people who were merely *suspected* of being Jedi, were to be summarily killed on sight, without requiring evidence or explanation as to why the kill was carried out. Sidious would go on to consolidate his reign as [Emperor](/wiki/Galactic_Emperor "Galactic Emperor") of the [Galactic Empire](/wiki/Galactic_Empire "Galactic Empire"), ruling for [decades](/wiki/Standard_decade "Standard decade") until the prophecy of the [Chosen One](/wiki/Chosen_One "Chosen One") came to pass, marking the end of the Sith and the restoration of balance in [the Force](/wiki/The_Force "The Force") by [4 ABY](/wiki/4_ABY "4 ABY").

## Description

:   "*Under this directive, any and all Jedi leadership must be executed for treason against the Republic. Any soldier that does not comply with the order will also be executed for treason.*"
:   ―Clone Commander Rex, on the implementation of Order 66[[5]](#cite_note-Shattered-5)

 [![Through their bio-chip, a clone could be made to carry out Order 66](data:image/gif;base64,R0lGODlhAQABAIABAAAAAP///yH5BAEAAAEALAAAAAABAAEAQAICTAEAOw%3D%3D)](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/starwars/images/5/5e/CloneInhibitorChip-Fugitive.png/revision/latest?cb=20150410035444) [![Through their bio-chip, a clone could be made to carry out Order 66](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/starwars/images/5/5e/CloneInhibitorChip-Fugitive.png/revision/latest/scale-to-width-down/200?cb=20150410035444)](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/starwars/images/5/5e/CloneInhibitorChip-Fugitive.png/revision/latest?cb=20150410035444) 

Through their bio-chip, a clone could be made to carry out Order 66

 

The [clone troopers](/wiki/Clone_trooper "Clone trooper") of the [Grand Army of the Republic](/wiki/Grand_Army_of_the_Republic "Grand Army of the Republic") were programmed to comply with Clone Protocol 66 during the [cloning](/wiki/Cloning "Cloning") process.[[7]](#cite_note-Fugitive-7) All clones were given a [behavioral modification biochip](/wiki/Behavioral_modification_biochip "Behavioral modification biochip")[[4]](#cite_note-Conspiracy-4) that could be activated by remote order via voice command; [Supreme Chancellor](/wiki/Supreme_Chancellor "Supreme Chancellor") [Sheev Palpatine](/wiki/Darth_Sidious "Darth Sidious"), for example, only needed to tell clones to execute Order 66, and the order was carried out.[[1]](#cite_note-Episode_III-1) The biochip, when activated, would ensure total obedience in a clone, erasing existing biases and beliefs and instilling absolute loyalty to Palpatine. The order also forced the clones to view the [Jedi](/wiki/Jedi "Jedi") as dangerous traitors who needed to be [eliminated](/wiki/Death "Death") at all costs,[[3]](#cite_note-The_Unknown-3) even their own lives.[[8]](#cite_note-Victory_and_Death-8) Officially, the order was part of[[9]](#cite_note-Rise_and_Fall-9) the one hundred and fifty[[10]](#cite_note-Rise-10) [Executive Order Codes for the Grand Army of the Republic](/wiki/Executive_Order_Codes_for_the_Grand_Army_of_the_Republic "Executive Order Codes for the Grand Army of the Republic").[[9]](#cite_note-Rise_and_Fall-9)

In some instances, the clone could resist the will of the chip and hesitate from executing his orders. This act took great effort, and resistance could not be maintained unless the organic chip was removed, which required external assistance in the case of [Clone Commander](/wiki/Clone_Commander "Clone Commander") [Rex](/wiki/Rex "Rex").[[5]](#cite_note-Shattered-5) A few clones were immune to the order, but they were quite rare.[[11]](#cite_note-Battle_Scars-11) [Genetically defective clones](/wiki/Genetically_defective_clone "Genetically defective clone") had a high probability of immunity to the command, due to their mutations interfering with the performance of the inhibitor chip.[[12]](#cite_note-Aftermath-12) However, the chip remained a danger as long as it was still inside a clone's head whether he was immune to the order or not, as outside factors such as head injuries could set it off.[[11]](#cite_note-Battle_Scars-11) Failure to comply with Order 66 was deemed an act of [treason](/wiki/Treason "Treason"); as such, any clone [trooper](/wiki/Trooper "Trooper") who defied the order was also subject to [execution](/wiki/Death_penalty "Death penalty").[[5]](#cite_note-Shattered-5)

## History

### Prelude

#### The Sith in hiding

:   "*It is done, Master. The clone in question is now dead, and the Jedi still suspect nothing.*"  
    "*Good. Destroy the evidence. When the time comes, no one will be able to stop our plan to execute Order 66.*"
:   ―Darth Tyranus and Darth Sidious[[13]](#cite_note-Orders-13)

The [Sith](/wiki/Sith "Sith"), an order of users of the [dark side of the Force](/wiki/Dark_side_of_the_Force "Dark side of the Force"), was formed by fallen members of the [Jedi Order](/wiki/Jedi_Order "Jedi Order") after a war called the [Hundred-Year Darkness](/wiki/Hundred-Year_Darkness "Hundred-Year Darkness").[[14]](#cite_note-SW9-14) Eventually, after many wars, the Jedi defeated and seemingly destroyed the Sith in the final, [decisive conflict](/wiki/Jedi-Sith_War "Jedi-Sith War").[[15]](#cite_note-Episode_I-15) However, one [Sith Lord](/wiki/Sith_Lord "Sith Lord") survived: [Darth Bane](/wiki/Darth_Bane "Darth Bane"). Believing that the Sith's infighting had caused their downfall, Bane created a tradition called the [Rule of Two](/wiki/Rule_of_Two "Rule of Two") where he chose to pass his knowledge on to only one [Sith apprentice](/wiki/Sith_apprentice "Sith apprentice"), who was required to kill their [Sith Master](/wiki/Sith_Master "Sith Master") in order to succeed them.[[16]](#cite_note-Sacrifice-16)

For nearly one thousand [years](/wiki/Standard_year "Standard year"), the Sith thus existed in hiding, plotting their [revenge](/wiki/Grand_Plan "Grand Plan") against the Jedi.[[15]](#cite_note-Episode_I-15) Eventually, a [human](/wiki/Human "Human") [male](/wiki/Gender "Gender") from [Naboo](/wiki/Naboo "Naboo") born Sheev Palpatine[[17]](#cite_note-Tarkin-17) became the apprentice of [Darth Plagueis](/wiki/Darth_Plagueis "Darth Plagueis"), and was dubbed [Darth](/wiki/Darth "Darth") Sidious. He eventually took over as the [Dark Lord of the Sith](/wiki/Dark_Lord_of_the_Sith "Dark Lord of the Sith") after betraying and murdering his master in his [sleep](/wiki/Sleep "Sleep").[[1]](#cite_note-Episode_III-1) In public life, Palpatine became a [politician](/wiki/Politics "Politics"), serving as Naboo's [Senator](/wiki/Senator "Senator") before eventually orchestrating an [invasion](/wiki/Invasion_of_Naboo "Invasion of Naboo") of his [homeworld](/wiki/Homeworld "Homeworld") in order to be elected the Supreme Chancellor of the [Galactic Republic](/wiki/Galactic_Republic "Galactic Republic"), while successfully hiding his true identity, which allowed to to deceive the Senate and Republic.[[15]](#cite_note-Episode_I-15) Sidious planned to fulfill the Sith's revenge plot by destroying the Jedi and transforming the ancient Republic into the [Galactic Empire](/wiki/Galactic_Empire "Galactic Empire"), and he set about engineering the eventual outbreak of the [Clone Wars](/wiki/Clone_Wars "Clone Wars") to do so.[[1]](#cite_note-Episode_III-1)

In [82 BBY](/wiki/82_BBY "82 BBY"),[[18]](#cite_note-Asusto_date-18) the Jedi [Padawan](/wiki/Padawan "Padawan") [Dooku](/wiki/Dooku "Dooku"), on a [mission](/wiki/Mission_to_Asusto "Mission to Asusto") to the [planet](/wiki/Planet "Planet") [Asusto](/wiki/Asusto "Asusto") with [Jedi Master](/wiki/Jedi_Master "Jedi Master") [Lene Kostana](/wiki/Lene_Kostana "Lene Kostana") and her Padawan [Sifo-Dyas](/wiki/Sifo-Dyas "Sifo-Dyas"), had a [Force vision](/wiki/Force_vision "Force vision") of many possible futures during an encounter with the [Presagers of Hakotei](/wiki/Presagers_of_Hakotei "Presagers of Hakotei"), a dark-side-using cult. Among the visions was a possible version of Order 66, with Jedi Master [Jor Aerith](/wiki/Jor_Aerith "Jor Aerith") being killed by Clone [Commander](/wiki/Commander "Commander") [Crane](/wiki/Crane_(clone_trooper) "Crane (clone trooper)"). However, not everything Dooku saw in that vision would come to pass.[[19]](#cite_note-DJL-19) Years later, Dooku left the Jedi Order, before falling to the dark side of [the Force](/wiki/The_Force "The Force") and becoming the Sith [Lord](/wiki/Lord "Lord") Darth Tyranus, apprentice to Sidious.[[20]](#cite_note-Attack_of_the_Clones-20)

The Dark Lords of the Sith orchestrated the Clone Wars, including the implantation of the bio-chips that overrode their host's will with Protocol 66.

 

Around ten years prior to the [outbreak](/wiki/First_Battle_of_Geonosis "First Battle of Geonosis") of the Clone Wars, Jedi Master [Sifo-Dyas](/wiki/Sifo-Dyas "Sifo-Dyas") ordered the clone [army](/wiki/Army "Army") for the Republic,[[20]](#cite_note-Attack_of_the_Clones-20) having had visions of a future conflict. He had a [Behavioral Modification Biochip](/wiki/Behavioral_modification_biochip "Behavioral modification biochip") he wanted instilled in the Clones as a safeguard against rogue Jedi's. However, the Sith took over the project as part of their plot to destroy the Jedi Order, with Dooku, having left the Jedi and become an agent of Sidious, betrayed Sifo-Dyas by having him [murdered](/wiki/Mission_to_Oba_Diah_(Sifo-Dyas) "Mission to Oba Diah (Sifo-Dyas)") by the [Pyke Syndicate](/wiki/Pyke_Syndicate "Pyke Syndicate") and concealing the true circumstances and location of his death from the Jedi.[[21]](#cite_note-The_Lost_One-21) Dooku and Sidious's retooled Dyas's Biochip to contain the Order 66 command, which when activated, would forcibly compel the Clone army to view the Jedi as traitors to the Republic and subsequently execute them. [Count](/wiki/Count "Count") Dooku, posing as a Jedi to the [Kaminoan](/wiki/Kaminoan "Kaminoan") cloners, provided the Kaminoans with a bio-chip to be implanted into the clones during the early phase of their growth period. This chip contained the order the Kaminoans knew as "Clone Protocol 66."[[4]](#cite_note-Conspiracy-4)

The Kaminoans, including [Prime Minister](/wiki/Prime_Minister "Prime Minister") [Lama Su](/wiki/Lama_Su "Lama Su") and [Chief Medical Scientist](/wiki/Chief_Medical_Scientist "Chief Medical Scientist") [Nala Se](/wiki/Nala_Se "Nala Se") were deceived by Dooku into believing Dyas's intention. Dooku, who was only known to the cloners under the alias of his Sith name, also convinced them that it was imperative the existence of the protocol and chips be kept secret from the Jedi, claiming it was what Sifo-Dyas had wanted.[[4]](#cite_note-Conspiracy-4) The clone army's existence became known to the Jedi just before the Clone Wars when [Jedi Knight](/wiki/Jedi_Knight "Jedi Knight") [Obi-Wan Kenobi](/wiki/Obi-Wan_Kenobi "Obi-Wan Kenobi") [tracked](/wiki/Mission_to_Kamino_(Separatist_Crisis) "Mission to Kamino (Separatist Crisis)") [clone template](/wiki/Clone_template "Clone template") [Jango Fett](/wiki/Jango_Fett "Jango Fett") to [Kamino](/wiki/Kamino "Kamino") while investigating an [assassination attempt](/wiki/Plot_to_assassinate_Senator_Amidala "Plot to assassinate Senator Amidala") on [Senator](/wiki/Senator "Senator") [Padmé Amidala](/wiki/Padm%C3%A9_Amidala "Padmé Amidala").[[20]](#cite_note-Attack_of_the_Clones-20) Due to Palpatine's double identity, he could control the war on both ends and deceive both sides.

Early in the war, after the [Ambush on Rugosa](/wiki/Battle_of_Rugosa "Battle of Rugosa"), Jedi [Grand Master](/wiki/Grand_Master "Grand Master") [Yoda](/wiki/Yoda "Yoda"), while [meditating](/wiki/Meditation "Meditation"), had a vision of [Jek](/wiki/Jek "Jek"), one of the clone troopers who had accompanied him to the [moon](/wiki/Moon "Moon"), at the moment Order 66 was issued, sensing Jek's emotions changing from disbelief and regret to a sudden urge to obey. However, Yoda's wariness towards Force visions and the intense disquiet he regularly felt around the clones due to their genetically engineered obedience prevented him from realizing the true import of the brief vision.[[22]](#cite_note-Sharing_the_Same_Face-22)

Prior to the [Battle of Umbara](/wiki/Battle_of_Umbara "Battle of Umbara"), [Jedi General](/wiki/Jedi_General "Jedi General") [Pong Krell](/wiki/Pong_Krell "Pong Krell") had visions of Order 66, the [Great Jedi Purge](/wiki/Great_Jedi_Purge "Great Jedi Purge"), and the rise of the Galactic Empire. Subsequently falling to the dark side, Krell used his visions as justification for attempting to sabotage the battle so he could become Dooku's new apprentice. However, he was captured by the [501st Legion](/wiki/501st_Legion "501st Legion"), who executed him for treason due to the danger that the remaining [Umbaran](/wiki/Umbaran "Umbaran") forces might free him and learn vital military intelligence.[[23]](#cite_note-Carnage_of_Krell-23)

At some point during the Clone Wars, [Eno Cordova](/wiki/Eno_Cordova "Eno Cordova") entered the [Zeffo](/wiki/Zeffo "Zeffo") vault on [Bogano](/wiki/Bogano "Bogano") which allowed him to see a vision of the fall of the Jedi and the subsequent spread of the Dark Side across the galaxy. Cordova then raced back to Coruscant to warn the Jedi Council, presenting them with all of his findings. None of the Council would put faith in his findings however, save for Cordova's old confidant Jocasta Nu. Nu thus entrusted Cordova with a secret [copy](/wiki/Eno_Cordova%27s_holocron "Eno Cordova's holocron") of her list of young Force sensitives procured from the Jedi Archives.[[24]](#cite_note-Fallen_Order-24)

Towards the end of the war, Order 66 was almost prematurely exposed due to a malfunction in the biochip inside clone trooper [Tup](/wiki/Tup "Tup"), resulting in the clone alternating between his regular self and a trance-like state. The latter state caused Tup to betray and shoot Jedi Master [Tiplar](/wiki/Tiplar "Tiplar") during the [Battle of Ringo Vinda](/wiki/Battle_of_Ringo_Vinda "Battle of Ringo Vinda"), killing her instantly. The Republic retreated from their offensive, preparing to study Tup to determine what had happened. Learning what had happened to Tup, Dooku and Sidious feared that he would lead the Jedi to discovering their true plans for the war, including the clone protocol. The [Confederacy of Independent Systems](/wiki/Confederacy_of_Independent_Systems "Confederacy of Independent Systems") tried to kidnap Tup, but the Republic succeeded in bringing him to Kamino for further study.[[3]](#cite_note-The_Unknown-3)

#### Uncovering the evidence

The Kaminoans, trying to hide the protocol's existence, studied Tup and claimed that a virus had caused him to kill [General](/wiki/General "General") Tiplar. [Advanced Recon Commando](/wiki/Advanced_Recon_Commando "Advanced Recon Commando") [Fives](/wiki/Fives "Fives")—Tup's best friend in the 501st—did not believe their claims, discovering the chip inside Tup, although he died after it was removed.[[4]](#cite_note-Conspiracy-4) With the help of the [medical droid](/wiki/Medical_droid "Medical droid") [AZI-3](/wiki/AZI-345211896246498721347 "AZI-345211896246498721347"), Fives also discovered the chip inside his own head, and AZI-3 removed it for study before they both discovered that the chip was inside all of the clones. The Jedi brought Fives to [Coruscant](/wiki/Coruscant "Coruscant") for further examination,[[7]](#cite_note-Fugitive-7) where Fives came face-to-face with Palpatine and discovered that the Chancellor was behind the plot. Palpatine [framed](/wiki/Escape_from_the_Grand_Republic_Medical_Facility "Escape from the Grand Republic Medical Facility") Fives for an [assassination](/wiki/Assassin "Assassin") attempt, discrediting the clone and causing the Jedi and fellow clones to pursue him. Despite knowing the truth, Fives, having been drugged into a state of aggression and paranoia, was unable to convince [Anakin Skywalker](/wiki/Anakin_Skywalker "Anakin Skywalker") or [Clone Captain](/wiki/Clone_Captain "Clone Captain") [Rex](/wiki/Rex "Rex"), who were with him in the [end](/wiki/Mission_to_Level_1325 "Mission to Level 1325"), about the conspiracy or the Supreme Chancellor's involvement before he was killed by the [Coruscant Guard](/wiki/Coruscant_Guard "Coruscant Guard"). The Jedi Order accepted the story that a [parasite](/wiki/Parasite "Parasite") had caused Tup and Fives' behavior, and that the chips were necessary to keep the clones mentally stable.[[13]](#cite_note-Orders-13) Rex was mostly skeptical of Fives' claims about the chips, but, unable to completely dismiss the theory, he filed a confidential grievance report about the chips possibly having a hidden purpose, although he was aware it was likely to be ignored.[[5]](#cite_note-Shattered-5)

Later, the Jedi discovered that the Sith were behind the creation of the Grand Army of the Republic, as Count Dooku—under the name Tyranus—had selected Jango Fett as the [genetic](/wiki/Gene "Gene") template for the clones and had worked with the Kaminoans to create the army after it was ordered by Sifo-Dyas. At the insistence of Grand Master Yoda, who testified to the clones' loyalty and valiant service, the [Jedi High Council](/wiki/Jedi_High_Council "Jedi High Council") chose not to disclose the conspiracy to the public or even the Supreme Chancellor, believing that the Republic would lose the public's trust if it became known that Dooku was involved in the creation of the clone army.[[21]](#cite_note-The_Lost_One-21) Shortly thereafter, Yoda went on a journey to discover the secrets of [immortality](/wiki/Force_spirit "Force spirit"), and, on [Dagobah](/wiki/Dagobah "Dagobah"), saw a vision of what would become Order 66.[[25]](#cite_note-Voices-25) Throughout his journey, Yoda came to accept that the Jedi would likely lose the Clone Wars, though he did not know how. He did, however, believe that the Jedi would still triumph in the long run.[[16]](#cite_note-Sacrifice-16)

After learning from members of the [Grysk](/wiki/Grysk "Grysk") species, which was unknown in most of the galaxy, that the metal [cortosis](/wiki/Cortosis "Cortosis") was resistant to [lightsabers](/wiki/Lightsaber "Lightsaber"), Dooku and Sidious attempted to acquire supplies of the resource from the planet [Mokivj](/wiki/Mokivj "Mokivj") in an [operation](/wiki/Mission_to_Mokivj "Mission to Mokivj") headed by [Duke](/wiki/Duke "Duke") [Solha](/wiki/Solha "Solha"). The Sith hoped to make [clone trooper armor](/wiki/Clone_trooper_armor "Clone trooper armor") containing the metal and supply it to the clones who would carry out Order 66. However, the operation, carried out under the auspices of the Separatists, was foiled by Skywalker and a temporary ally, [Senior Captain](/wiki/Senior_Captain "Senior Captain") [Mitth'raw'nuruodo](/wiki/Mitth%27raw%27nuruodo "Mitth'raw'nuruodo") of the [Chiss Ascendancy](/wiki/Chiss_Ascendancy "Chiss Ascendancy"). Skywalker believed the cortosis [armor](/wiki/Armor "Armor") had been intended for a potential infiltration of the Republic's ranks.[[26]](#cite_note-Thrawn_Alliances-26)

Sometime after the [Battle of Anaxes](/wiki/Battle_of_Anaxes "Battle of Anaxes"),[[27]](#cite_note-Date_Note-27) 501st Legion [clone medic](/wiki/Clone_medic "Clone medic") [Kix](/wiki/Kix "Kix") began to question the details surrounding the event which had led to Tup killing Tiplar. His subsequent investigation led him to discover that Fives had in fact been telling him the truth when he had first told Kix about the Jedi and the clones being framed and that the biochips implanted within the clones could indeed compel them to kill the entire Jedi Order. Before he could share his suspicions, however, he was kidnapped by forces sent by Dooku. He was subsequently tortured, but his denials about having leaked the information failed to convince his captors. He was then brought onto the Separatist [cruiser](/wiki/Cruiser "Cruiser") *[Obrexta III](/wiki/Obrexta_III "Obrexta III")* and inserted into a [cryo-cycle stasis pod](/wiki/Cryo-cycle_stasis_pod "Cryo-cycle stasis pod") for the journey to [Serenno](/wiki/Serenno "Serenno"). As the cruiser was en route, however, it was [ambushed](/wiki/Attack_in_an_unidentified_sector "Attack in an unidentified sector") by Republic forces. After sustaining heavy damage, the [battle droid](/wiki/Battle_droid "Battle droid") [B1-CC14](/wiki/B1-CC14 "B1-CC14") made the decision to jump through [hyperspace](/wiki/Hyperspace "Hyperspace") to a random [sector](/wiki/Sector "Sector") to ensure Kix would stay out of enemy hands. The jump brought them to the desert planet [Ponemah Terminal](/wiki/Ponemah_Terminal "Ponemah Terminal"), with the *Obrexta III* crashing into the planet's dangerous [south polar region](/wiki/Sea_of_Sand "Sea of Sand"), where it would lay undisturbed for [decades](/wiki/Standard_decade "Standard decade").[[28]](#cite_note-Crimson_Corsair-28)

With the secrets of Order 66 secure, Sidious and Dooku continued with their plans during the Clone Wars. By the war's end, public support for the Jedi had weakened due to their management of the conflict,[[29]](#cite_note-Sabotage-29) while Palpatine remained a popular figure. Palpatine covertly betrayed and disposed of Dooku during the [Battle of Coruscant](/wiki/Battle_of_Coruscant "Battle of Coruscant"), and with Kenobi [engaging](/wiki/Battle_of_Utapau "Battle of Utapau") General [Grievous](/wiki/Grievous "Grievous") on [Utapau](/wiki/Utapau "Utapau"), the Jedi hoped the war's end was imminent. However, Palpatine revealed himself to Skywalker as the [Dark Lord of the Sith](/wiki/Dark_Lord_of_the_Sith "Dark Lord of the Sith") in an effort to seduce him to the dark side as his new apprentice. When Skywalker informed Jedi Master [Mace Windu](/wiki/Mace_Windu "Mace Windu") of the Chancellor's identity, Windu assembled a team composed of himself, [Kit Fisto](/wiki/Kit_Fisto "Kit Fisto"), [Agen Kolar](/wiki/Agen_Kolar "Agen Kolar"), and [Saesee Tiin](/wiki/Saesee_Tiin "Saesee Tiin") to arrest Palpatine. Their attempt led to a [lightsaber duel](/wiki/Duel_in_Palpatine%27s_office "Duel in Palpatine's office") in which Palpatine killed all four of the Jedi, and Skywalker betrayed the Jedi and turned to the dark side.[[1]](#cite_note-Episode_III-1)

Palpatine used the duel as a pretext to declare that the Jedi were traitors, ones who had attempted to assassinate him and take control of the Republic for themselves. The Chancellor issued Order 66 to various clone [officers](/wiki/Officer "Officer") across [the galaxy](/wiki/The_galaxy "The galaxy"), which caused the Clones' inhibitor chips to activate and then brainwash them into viewing the Jedi as traitors to the Republic and subsequently executing them, while Skywalker—now the Sith Lord Darth Vader—was [sent](/wiki/Operation:_Knightfall "Operation: Knightfall") to kill the Jedi within the [Jedi Temple](/wiki/Imperial_Palace "Imperial Palace"), thus launching[[1]](#cite_note-Episode_III-1) an unprecedented and galaxy-wide massacre.[[30]](#cite_note-GF_2018-30) Thousands of Jedi throughout the galaxy were betrayed and killed, including [Ki-Adi-Mundi](/wiki/Ki-Adi-Mundi "Ki-Adi-Mundi") on [Mygeeto](/wiki/Mygeeto "Mygeeto"), [Aayla Secura](/wiki/Aayla_Secura "Aayla Secura") on [Felucia](/wiki/Felucia "Felucia"), [Plo Koon](/wiki/Plo_Koon "Plo Koon") in the skies of [Cato Neimoidia](/wiki/Cato_Neimoidia "Cato Neimoidia"), [Stass Allie](/wiki/Stass_Allie "Stass Allie") on [Saleucami](/wiki/Saleucami "Saleucami"),[[1]](#cite_note-Episode_III-1) [Depa Billaba](/wiki/Depa_Billaba "Depa Billaba") on [Kaller](/wiki/Kaller "Kaller"),[[12]](#cite_note-Aftermath-12) [Jaro Tapal](/wiki/Jaro_Tapal "Jaro Tapal") in the [Bracca system](/wiki/Bracca_system "Bracca system"),[[24]](#cite_note-Fallen_Order-24) and even [Jedi younglings](/wiki/Jedi_Initiate "Jedi Initiate").[[1]](#cite_note-Episode_III-1) As part of the execution of Order 66, the elements of the [481st Legion](/wiki/481st_Legion "481st Legion") were sent to the [planet](/wiki/Planet "Planet") [Ilum](/wiki/Ilum "Ilum") to secure the [Jedi Temple](/wiki/Jedi_Temple_(Ilum) "Jedi Temple (Ilum)") there and kill any survivors.[[31]](#cite_note-Nexus_of_Power-31)

Some officers, such as [Clone Marshal Commander](/wiki/Clone_Marshal_Commander "Clone Marshal Commander") [Cody](/wiki/Cody "Cody"), received individual transmissions from Palpatine.[[1]](#cite_note-Episode_III-1) Afterward, the order was broadcast on repeat through the Grand Army's communications channels to ensure that all troopers heard it.[[12]](#cite_note-Aftermath-12) Throughout the duration of the Empire, Order 66 was still active: all known or merely suspected Jedi were to be shot on sight.[[32]](#cite_note-Star_Wars_5-32)

### Spared Jedi

:   "*The clones were given an order—Order 66. The only Jedi who were spared were those who saw through their* lies*. Like you.*"
:   ―The Grand Inquisitor explains to Iskat Akaris how she survived[[33]](#cite_note-Inquisitor:_Rise_of_the_Red_Blade-33)

Aside from Anakin Skywalker, some Jedi were protected from the massacre invoked by Order 66, but under other circumstances. Some Jedi within the Jedi Temple on Coruscant were watched and influenced by spies placed inside the temple by Darth Sidious himself. These spies were assigned to specific Jedi who displayed deviant behavior or who harbored doubts or resentment towards the Jedi Order or the Jedi Council, such as [Iskat Akaris](/wiki/Thirteenth_Sister "Thirteenth Sister"). As shown in her case, once the order was issued and the clones turned on their Jedi officers, the doubtful Jedi, wherever they were or whatever they were doing, were approached by the clones with a hologram of Darth Sidious instead of raised blasters. Sidious offered them a chance to live, so long as they pledged their allegiance to him and revoked their Jedi ways and teachings. The ones who accepted had the chance to become some of the first [Inquisitors](/wiki/Inquisitor "Inquisitor") of the [Inquisitorius](/wiki/Inquisitorius "Inquisitorius"), while those who rejected the offer would share the fate as the rest of their Jedi brothers and sisters.[[33]](#cite_note-Inquisitor:_Rise_of_the_Red_Blade-33)

### Aftermath

#### Aftermath for the Jedi

:   "*The remaining Jedi will be hunted down and defeated!*"
:   ―Darth Sidious[[1]](#cite_note-Episode_III-1)

 [![Enslaved in thought and action after the dispatchment of Order 66, clones of the New Order were celebrated for their part in the eradication of the Jedi.](data:image/gif;base64,R0lGODlhAQABAIABAAAAAP///yH5BAEAAAEALAAAAAABAAEAQAICTAEAOw%3D%3D)](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/starwars/images/3/34/AmeddaJediEradicationCeremony-2017DarthVader1.jpg/revision/latest?cb=20170622194336) [![Enslaved in thought and action after the dispatchment of Order 66, clones of the New Order were celebrated for their part in the eradication of the Jedi.](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/starwars/images/3/34/AmeddaJediEradicationCeremony-2017DarthVader1.jpg/revision/latest/scale-to-width-down/240?cb=20170622194336)](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/starwars/images/3/34/AmeddaJediEradicationCeremony-2017DarthVader1.jpg/revision/latest?cb=20170622194336) 

Enslaved in thought and action after the dispatchment of Order 66, clones of the New Order were celebrated for their part in the eradication of the Jedi.

 

The execution of Order 66 marked the destruction of the Jedi Order. Before the [Galactic Senate](/wiki/Galactic_Senate "Galactic Senate"), Palpatine told the galaxy that the Jedi were traitors who had attempted to kill him and seize power, and the destruction of the Jedi was greeted with applause by the Senate. Having amassed immense central authority during the war, and without the Jedi Order standing in his way, Palpatine [transformed](/wiki/Proclamation_of_the_New_Order "Proclamation of the New Order") the Republic into the Galactic Empire and declared himself [Emperor](/wiki/Galactic_Emperor "Galactic Emperor"). Along with Vader, Palpatine's rule meant that the Sith plan was completed, and the Sith Lords once again ruled the galaxy.[[1]](#cite_note-Episode_III-1)

After surviving Order 66, Obi-Wan Kenobi and Yoda returned to Coruscant with the help of [Senator](/wiki/Senator "Senator") [Bail Organa](/wiki/Bail_Prestor_Organa "Bail Prestor Organa") to undertake a deadly [mission](/wiki/Infiltration_of_the_Jedi_Temple "Infiltration of the Jedi Temple") to infiltrate the Jedi Temple, as Vader had set its communications beacon with a message recalling all surviving Jedi there. After fighting their way inside, Kenobi altered the beacon's programming so it warned Jedi away, while Yoda discovered that Vader had fallen to the dark side.[[1]](#cite_note-Episode_III-1) Kenobi's action managed to save an undetermined number of Jedi survivors.[[37]](#cite_note-TSWB-37)

Nevertheless, few Jedi had survived the onslaught of Order 66.[[1]](#cite_note-Episode_III-1) The Jedi Padawan [Caleb Dume](/wiki/Kanan_Jarrus "Kanan Jarrus") survived the initial attack on his life due to the sacrifice made by his master, Depa Billaba.[[12]](#cite_note-Aftermath-12) Sometime later he was captured by the clones who had killed his master, but avoided death [once more](/wiki/Rescue_of_Caleb_Dume "Rescue of Caleb Dume") due in part to the last-minute intervention of Commander [Grey](/wiki/Grey_(clone_trooper) "Grey (clone trooper)"), who asserted his will and realized that something else had influenced his thoughts when he and his [battalion](/wiki/Depa_Billaba%27s_second_battalion "Depa Billaba's second battalion") had previously turned on Dume and his master. Shortly afterwards, Dume went into hiding and took the name of Kanan Jarrus,[[38]](#cite_note-Kanan_5-38) giving up his identity as a Jedi for fourteen years before returning, with the crew of the *[Ghost](/wiki/Ghost "Ghost")*, to rebel against the Empire.[[39]](#cite_note-KananCowboyVideo-39)

Jedi Master [Quinlan Vos](/wiki/Quinlan_Vos "Quinlan Vos") was presumed to have survived by the Empire, and was a priority target of the purge.[[40]](#cite_note-Dying_Light_I-40) Jedi Knight [Grandea](/wiki/Grandea "Grandea") managed to escape the fate of her fellow Jedi, [Alivar](/wiki/Alivar "Alivar"), by using the [mind trick](/wiki/Mind_trick "Mind trick") to trick the clone troopers into attacking each other. [Jedi Archives](/wiki/Jedi_Archives "Jedi Archives") [librarian](/wiki/Librarian "Librarian") [Jocasta Nu](/wiki/Jocasta_Nu "Jocasta Nu") survived and tried to create a [secret school](/wiki/Jocasta_Nu%27s_cache "Jocasta Nu's cache") to train new Jedi Knights.[[40]](#cite_note-Dying_Light_I-40) Jedi Master [Uvell](/wiki/Uvell "Uvell") also survived and entrusted a ship of Jedi artifacts to [Antron Bach](/wiki/Antron_Bach "Antron Bach") to preserve the Jedi Order.[[47]](#cite_note-TEOH-47) Jedi Knight [Cere Junda](/wiki/Cere_Junda "Cere Junda") survived as well, although her Padawan, [Trilla Suduri](/wiki/Second_Sister "Second Sister"), was captured by the Empire and tortured into becoming the [Inquisitor](/wiki/Inquisitor "Inquisitor") Second Sister.[[48]](#cite_note-Databank_Cere-48) [Grogu](/wiki/Din_Grogu "Din Grogu"), a [youngling](/wiki/Youngling "Youngling") of [Yoda's species](/wiki/Yoda%27s_species "Yoda's species") present at the Jedi Temple during the siege, was rescued, but lost his memories of the exact events due to trauma.[[49]](#cite_note-The_Jedi-49)

#### Aftermath for the clones

:   "*Rumors are, more and more clones have been questioning the Order.*"  
    "*Then they're traitors, like the Jedi.*"
:   ―Commander Cody and commando Crosshair[[51]](#cite_note-Solitary_Clone-51)

Most of the irregular unit [Clone Force 99](/wiki/Clone_Force_99 "Clone Force 99") were immune to the order owing to either genetic mutations or implanted [cybernetics](/wiki/Cybernetics "Cybernetics"), with the exception of [Crosshair](/wiki/Crosshair "Crosshair") — whose chip was amplified by the Empire to enhance its effects on him. The rest of the so-called "Bad Batch" [deserted](/wiki/Escape_from_Kamino "Escape from Kamino") the Empire within [days](/wiki/Standard_day "Standard day") of its formation.[[12]](#cite_note-Aftermath-12) Crosshair would later remove his inhibitor chip but remained loyal to the Empire, believing that his squad had abandoned him to his fate.[[52]](#cite_note-Return_to_Kamino-52) Becoming a fugitive from the Empire, which officially declared him killed in action, Rex[[11]](#cite_note-Battle_Scars-11) eventually met up with Commanders [Wolffe](/wiki/Wolffe "Wolffe") and [Gregor](/wiki/Gregor "Gregor"), who had their chips removed.[[53]](#cite_note-The_Lost_Commanders-53)

Sometime after Order 66, Rex made contact with the Bad Batch and learned they had not removed their chips, as [Tech](/wiki/Tech "Tech") had concluded they were not a danger. However, the persistent [headaches](/wiki/Headache "Headache") [Wrecker](/wiki/Wrecker "Wrecker") had been suffering[[11]](#cite_note-Battle_Scars-11) after recent head injuries[[54]](#cite_note-Decommissioned-54) said otherwise. Rex insisted that the squad meet him on [Bracca](/wiki/Bracca "Bracca") to remove their chips. Reactivating the [medical bay](/wiki/Medical_bay "Medical bay") of a [downed Jedi Cruiser](/wiki/Unidentified_Venator-class_Star_Destroyer_(Clone_Force_99) "Unidentified Venator-class Star Destroyer (Clone Force 99)"), Wrecker was being examined by Tech when his chip activated, while [Hunter](/wiki/Hunter "Hunter") and [Echo](/wiki/Echo "Echo") were telling Rex about how they had witnessed Order 66 being carried out on Kaller. Wrecker subsequently attacked the squad, taking out the adult clones quickly before chasing after the child clone [Omega](/wiki/Omega "Omega"). She attempted to talk him down, but failed, although Wrecker was struggling against the chip. Rex [stunned](/wiki/Stun "Stun") Wrecker in the nick of time, and all of the Batch's chips were subsequently removed, ending the chips' threat to the squad.[[11]](#cite_note-Battle_Scars-11)

Despite the control chips trying to force the clones to obey all orders given in the name of the Empire,[[55]](#cite_note-BB_What's_Next-55) a growing number of clones began to question Order 66[[51]](#cite_note-Solitary_Clone-51) and the Empire's actions, with the chips beginning to wear off as time went by. Despite not being aware of it, Clone Captain "[Howzer](/wiki/Howzer "Howzer")" overcame his chip[[55]](#cite_note-BB_What's_Next-55) and rallied members of his [squad](/wiki/Howzer%27s_squad "Howzer's squad") to reject the Empire as well.[[56]](#cite_note-Rescue_on_Ryloth-56) Commander Cody began to question if the Empire was truly making the galaxy a better place and ultimately abandoned the regime.[[51]](#cite_note-Solitary_Clone-51)

During the Empire's [subjugation](/wiki/Invasion_of_Mon_Cala "Invasion of Mon Cala") of [Mon Cala](/wiki/Mon_Cala "Mon Cala"), surviving Jedi Padawan [Ferren Barr](/wiki/Ferren_Barr "Ferren Barr") hid in [Dac City](/wiki/Dac_City "Dac City") and acted as an advisor to King [Lee-Char](/wiki/Lee-Char "Lee-Char").[[57]](#cite_note-Dark_Lord_14-57) There, he took several acolytes: [Nipaltoo](/wiki/Nipaltoo "Nipaltoo"), [Orvek](/wiki/Orvek "Orvek"), [Rebb](/wiki/Rebb "Rebb"), [Stell](/wiki/Stell "Stell"), [Daren](/wiki/Daren "Daren"), and [Verla](/wiki/Verla "Verla"). Barr was soon confronted by several of the Inquisitors and their [troopers](/wiki/Purge_Trooper "Purge Trooper"), whom he noticed were newer clones who had not yet had their control chips activated. After revealing them as former Jedi, Barr used a [mind trick](/wiki/Mind_trick "Mind trick") to force the troopers to execute Order 66 and attack the Inquisitors. The [Tenth Brother](/wiki/Tenth_Brother "Tenth Brother"), who was formerly the Jedi Master Prosset Dibs,[[58]](#cite_note-Dark_Lord_16-58) was killed in the ensuing firefight, along with the [Ninth Sister](/wiki/Ninth_Sister "Ninth Sister") having her right [leg](/wiki/Limb "Limb") severed due to a betrayal from the [Sixth Brother](/wiki/Sixth_Brother "Sixth Brother") in an attempt to escape.[[6]](#cite_note-Dark_Lord_17-6)

### Legacy

:   "*Our fellow soldiers, the clones, the ones we Jedi fought side by side with, suddenly turned and betrayed us.*"
:   ―Kanan Jarrus, on Order 66[[53]](#cite_note-The_Lost_Commanders-53)

For nearly twenty years, the Empire expanded without any significant Jedi opposition. Approximately five years before the [Battle of Yavin](/wiki/Battle_of_Yavin "Battle of Yavin"), however, surviving Jedi, the few remaining in the galaxy, began to actively aid rebel cells around the galaxy, such as Kanan Jarrus for the [Spectres](/wiki/Spectres "Spectres") and Ahsoka Tano for a larger network of rebel cells. Years later, Kenobi emerged from his exile and began training Luke Skywalker to become a Jedi Knight,[[59]](#cite_note-Episode_IV-59) in the hopes that Luke would defeat Vader and Palpatine.[[60]](#cite_note-Episode_VI-60) Kenobi was [killed](/wiki/Rescue_of_Princess_Leia "Rescue of Princess Leia") by Darth Vader aboard the [Death Star](/wiki/DS-1_Orbital_Battle_Station "DS-1 Orbital Battle Station"),[[59]](#cite_note-Episode_IV-59) leaving Luke to be trained by Yoda, one of the only known surviving Jedi. The two trained on Dagobah before Luke left to confront Vader.[[46]](#cite_note-Episode_V-46) Luke did not return until just before Yoda's death.[[60]](#cite_note-Episode_VI-60)

 [![The redemption of Anakin Skywalker led to the return of the Jedi.](data:image/gif;base64,R0lGODlhAQABAIABAAAAAP///yH5BAEAAAEALAAAAAABAAEAQAICTAEAOw%3D%3D)](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/starwars/images/5/55/JediGhosts-ROTJ.png/revision/latest?cb=20201008104501) [![The redemption of Anakin Skywalker led to the return of the Jedi.](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/starwars/images/5/55/JediGhosts-ROTJ.png/revision/latest/scale-to-width-down/300?cb=20201008104501)](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/starwars/images/5/55/JediGhosts-ROTJ.png/revision/latest?cb=20201008104501) 

The redemption of Anakin Skywalker led to the return of the Jedi.

 

Upon the Jedi Master's death, Luke became the last of the Jedi and set out to confront Vader once more, now having learned the truth that Vader was his father. During the [Battle of Endor](/wiki/Battle_of_Endor "Battle of Endor"), Sidious betrayed Vader by attempting to have Luke kill him and take his placed as Sidious's apprentice, but Luke rebuffed his attempt, and instead successfully redeemed his father, with Anakin killing Sidious. The act of killing the Emperor resulted in Anakin's death, leading to the end of the Sith; through his death, Anakin fulfilled the prophecy of the [Chosen One](/wiki/Chosen_One "Chosen One") and brought balance to the Force. By confronting his father and saving him, Luke became the first new Jedi Knight in a [generation](/wiki/Jedi_Order#A_new_generation "Jedi Order"), bringing about the return of the Jedi.[[60]](#cite_note-Episode_VI-60) Thirty years later, Luke's nephew, [Ben Solo](/wiki/Ben_Solo "Ben Solo"), now known as Kylo Ren, carried out a [similar incident](/wiki/Destruction_of_the_Jedi_Temple "Destruction of the Jedi Temple"), causing Luke to go into exile at the [First Jedi Temple](/wiki/First_Jedi_Temple "First Jedi Temple") on the planet [Ahch-To](/wiki/Ahch-To "Ahch-To").[[61]](#cite_note-Episode_VII-61)

Fifty years after the Clone Wars, Kix was finally awoken during the [Hunt for Dooku's Treasure](/wiki/Hunt_for_Dooku%27s_Treasure "Hunt for Dooku's Treasure"). He had been found by [Sidon Ithano's crew](/wiki/Sidon_Ithano%27s_pirate_crew "Sidon Ithano's pirate crew") and, now unable to save the Republic or Jedi, joined the [pirates](/wiki/Pirate "Pirate").[[28]](#cite_note-Crimson_Corsair-28) In [32 ABY](/wiki/32_ABY "32 ABY"),[[62]](#cite_note-Death_year-62) the former Jedi Padawan [Naq Med](/wiki/Naq_Med "Naq Med") found out that the Jedi were no longer being hunted, which allowed him to finally die peacefully, passing on his lightsaber and robes to his grandson [Karr Nuq Sin](/wiki/Karr_Nuq_Sin "Karr Nuq Sin").[[63]](#cite_note-Force_Collector-63) Sometime following the [Battle of Crait](/wiki/Battle_of_Crait "Battle of Crait")[[64]](#cite_note-GEG-64) in [34 ABY](/wiki/34_ABY "34 ABY"),[[65]](#cite_note-TFA_and_TLJ_Date_Note-65) the pirate [Hondo Ohnaka](/wiki/Hondo_Ohnaka "Hondo Ohnaka") mentioned Order 66 in his book *[Galactic Explorer's Guide](/wiki/Galactic_Explorer%27s_Guide "Galactic Explorer's Guide")*, claiming that the clones had risen up against their Jedi Generals. He recounted that Yoda and Luminara Unduli had survived but that the latter was arrested by the Empire and executed.[[64]](#cite_note-GEG-64)

## Behind the scenes

### Development

:   "*[Order 66] was a part of the story that had been told before, and telling it again was a reminder to the audience of where this all began, where Obi-Wan's journey began. That's why it was in the show.[…]But it was a necessary part of Obi-Wan's story. It belonged in a story about a Jedi who is in hiding, who has to face his past.*"
:   ―Joby Harold[[104]](#cite_note-Vader_terrifying-104)

 [![Order 66 first appeared in Star Wars: Episode III Revenge of the Sith.](data:image/gif;base64,R0lGODlhAQABAIABAAAAAP///yH5BAEAAAEALAAAAAABAAEAQAICTAEAOw%3D%3D)](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/starwars/images/0/07/Order66.png/revision/latest?cb=20140411231155) [![Order 66 first appeared in Star Wars: Episode III Revenge of the Sith.](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/starwars/images/0/07/Order66.png/revision/latest/scale-to-width-down/320?cb=20140411231155)](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/starwars/images/0/07/Order66.png/revision/latest?cb=20140411231155) 

Order 66 first appeared in *Star Wars*: Episode III *Revenge of the Sith*.

 

The events of Order 66, namely the destruction of the Jedi Order, were first mentioned in the [1977](/wiki/1977 "1977") film [*Star Wars*: Episode IV *A New Hope*](/wiki/Star_Wars:_Episode_IV_A_New_Hope "Star Wars: Episode IV A New Hope"), the first installment of the *[Star Wars](/wiki/Star_Wars "Star Wars")* [original trilogy](/wiki/Original_trilogy "Original trilogy").[[59]](#cite_note-Episode_IV-59) The destruction itself was first identified and depicted on-screen in [2005](/wiki/2005 "2005") film [*Star Wars*: Episode III *Revenge of the Sith*](/wiki/Star_Wars:_Episode_III_Revenge_of_the_Sith "Star Wars: Episode III Revenge of the Sith"), the final installment of the [prequel trilogy](/wiki/Prequel_trilogy "Prequel trilogy").[[1]](#cite_note-Episode_III-1)

The backstory of Order 66 was provided in a four-episode arc in [The Lost Missions](/wiki/The_Clone_Wars:_The_Lost_Missions "The Clone Wars: The Lost Missions") of the television series *[Star Wars: The Clone Wars](/wiki/Star_Wars:_The_Clone_Wars "Star Wars: The Clone Wars")*.[[3]](#cite_note-The_Unknown-3)[[4]](#cite_note-Conspiracy-4)[[7]](#cite_note-Fugitive-7)[[13]](#cite_note-Orders-13) An Order 66 flashback scene appeared in the [first](/wiki/Part_I "Part I"),[[68]](#cite_note-Part_I-68) [fifth](/wiki/Part_V "Part V"),[[89]](#cite_note-Part_V-89) and [sixth episodes](/wiki/Part_VI "Part VI") of the *[Star Wars: Obi-Wan Kenobi](/wiki/Star_Wars:_Obi-Wan_Kenobi "Star Wars: Obi-Wan Kenobi")* televisions series.[[105]](#cite_note-Part_VI-105) Writer [Joby Harold](/wiki/Joby_Harold "Joby Harold") wanted the series to begin with Order 66 to remind the audience of where Obi-Wan Kenobi's journey began and to establish that the stakes are high for everyone.[[104]](#cite_note-Vader_terrifying-104)

### Unused and rejected concepts

:   "*I think it was a little more gruesome than you ended up seeing, you know. A lot of young potential Jedi ended up on the cutting room floor.*"
:   ―Ian McDiarmid on the original version of Order 66[[106]](#cite_note-ComicBook.com-106)

 [![Quinlan Vos depicted in a deleted animatic from the earlier versions of the Order 66](data:image/gif;base64,R0lGODlhAQABAIABAAAAAP///yH5BAEAAAEALAAAAAABAAEAQAICTAEAOw%3D%3D)](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/starwars/images/2/22/QuinEp3.jpg/revision/latest?cb=20160420183643) [![Quinlan Vos depicted in a deleted animatic from the earlier versions of the Order 66](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/starwars/images/2/22/QuinEp3.jpg/revision/latest/scale-to-width-down/300?cb=20160420183643)](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/starwars/images/2/22/QuinEp3.jpg/revision/latest?cb=20160420183643) 

Quinlan Vos depicted in a deleted animatic from the earlier versions of the Order 66

 

In the first draft of of *Revenge of the Sith*, dated to [April 13](/wiki/April_13 "April 13"), [2003](/wiki/2003 "2003"), the number of Jedi Knights who were killed was "more extensive" than the ones seen in the finished version of the film, though some animatics for these versions were made.[[107]](#cite_note-TMOSWROTS-107) The following order in this early draft was taken from the book *[The Making of Star Wars Revenge of the Sith](/wiki/The_Making_of_Star_Wars_Revenge_of_the_Sith "The Making of Star Wars Revenge of the Sith")*:

*On Mygeeto, clones blast KI-ADI-MUNDI and SAESEE TIIN. On Saleucami, they kill KIT FISTO, BARRISS OFFEE, and ADI GALLIA. On Kashyyyk, MINA PODIA is gunned down-but YODA saves himself. On Cato Neimoidia, PLO KOON is blasted out of the sky, while an unnamed Jedi lies dead on the planet surface. On Felucia, AAYLA SECURA and QUINLAN VOS are silenced forever.*[[107]](#cite_note-TMOSWROTS-107)

Some other concepts featured in other drafts and versions of *Revenge of the Sith* included [Boba Fett](/wiki/Boba_Fett "Boba Fett") taking part in Order 66 by killing Mace Windu in an attempt to avenge his father Jango's death until [George Lucas](/wiki/George_Lucas "George Lucas") reorganized the entire story and felt that Fett would be "too young" and wasn't an essential part in the story.[[107]](#cite_note-TMOSWROTS-107) For the deaths of Barriss Offee, Luminara Unduli and [Shaak Ti](/wiki/Shaak_Ti "Shaak Ti"), it was planned to use some unused footage of [Nalini Krishan](/wiki/Nalini_Krishan "Nalini Krishan"), [Mary Oyaya](/wiki/Mary_Oyaya "Mary Oyaya") and [Orli Shoshan](/wiki/Orli_Shoshan "Orli Shoshan") from *Attack of the Clones*.[[108]](#cite_note-C.C._Banana-108) In Ti's case, however, Shoshan filmed an earlier death for her in the film's beginning, though both scenes were cut from the finished film.[[107]](#cite_note-TMOSWROTS-107)

[Ian McDiarmid](/wiki/Ian_McDiarmid "Ian McDiarmid"), the actor who played Darth Sidious in the films, stated during a panel appearance at Salt Lake City's FanX convention in [2018](/wiki/2018 "2018") that Order 66 was intended to be more gruesome than in the finished version of the film, but George Lucas toned down the scene from his original vision in order to avoid a harsher rating, and his decision was probably a good idea after all. *Revenge of the Sith* was ultimately rated [PG-13](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Motion_Picture_Association_of_America_film_rating_system "wikipedia:Motion Picture Association of America film rating system"), unlike previous films.[[106]](#cite_note-ComicBook.com-106)

### Non-canon history

Order 66 is mentioned in the non-canon [LEGO](/wiki/LEGO "LEGO") comic "[Ambush in the Temple](/wiki/The_Temple_Ambush "The Temple Ambush")," which featured surviving Jedi Padawans [Mel Acan](/wiki/Mel_Acan "Mel Acan") and [Nena Gallow](/wiki/Nena_Gallow "Nena Gallow"), both of whom had lost their masters to the Great Jedi Purge. Having survived themselves, both Padawans took up residence in a [Jedi Temple](/wiki/Jedi_Temple_(Vinee) "Jedi Temple (Vinee)") on the planet [Vinee](/wiki/Vinee "Vinee"), only to eventually be discovered. Darth Vader, who believed the temple was a perfect hiding spot for those fleeing Order 66 or looking for a spot in hide-and-seek, journeyed to the temple with the Grand Inquisitor, but both Padawans managed to escape their grasp.[[109]](#cite_note-LEGO-109)
