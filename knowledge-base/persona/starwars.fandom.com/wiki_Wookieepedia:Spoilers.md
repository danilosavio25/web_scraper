Pursuant to the rules below, a [spoiler](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Spoiler_(media) "wikipedia:Spoiler (media)") is generally considered a spoiler on Wookieepedia up to and until one month after the official release date of a particular product. Any page with any new information from said source must contain an appropriate spoiler tag until the end of the spoiler period.

Wookieepedia's [Discussions forum](/wiki/Wookieepedia:Discussions "Wookieepedia:Discussions") and [Discord server](/wiki/Wookieepedia:Discord "Wookieepedia:Discord") have their respective rules on spoiler content.

## Spoiler tags

Wookieepedia has several available header templates that serve as formal spoiler tags to indicate that the article contains spoilers from a specific source. Please refer to the [Layout Guide](/wiki/Wookieepedia:Layout_Guide "Wookieepedia:Layout Guide") for the placement of spoiler tags in an article.

### {{[Spoiler](/wiki/Template:Spoiler "Template:Spoiler")}}

:   *{{Spoiler|product's article title|formatted name|time=}}*

The general-purpose spoiler tag, for novels, comics, video games etc. Please refer to the instructions on [Template:Spoiler](/wiki/Template:Spoiler "Template:Spoiler").

### {{[SpoilerFilm](/wiki/Template:SpoilerFilm "Template:SpoilerFilm")}}

:   *{{SpoilerFilm}}*

The spoiler tag to be used during major film releases (such as the [sequel trilogy](/wiki/Sequel_trilogy "Sequel trilogy") and [anthology films](/wiki/Star_Wars_Anthology_Series "Star Wars Anthology Series")) for articles about subjects originating from the film as well as directly related material released around the same time, such as novel adaptations. Articles about subjects that are not original to the film release but nevertheless contain spoilers from them should use {{[Spoiler](/wiki/Template:Spoiler "Template:Spoiler")}} instead. Please refer to the instructions on [Template:SpoilerFilm](/wiki/Template:SpoilerFilm "Template:SpoilerFilm").

Unlike other sources, the spoiler period for major film releases is two months.

:   →See [Wookieepedia:Mofferences/March 6, 2016](/wiki/Wookieepedia:Mofferences/March_6,_2016 "Wookieepedia:Mofferences/March 6, 2016")

### {{[SpoilerTV](/wiki/Template:SpoilerTV "Template:SpoilerTV")}}

:   *{{SpoilerTV|show=series title|episode number}}.*

The spoiler tag for series with episodic installments (such as *[Star Wars: Skeleton Crew](/wiki/Star_Wars:_Skeleton_Crew "Star Wars: Skeleton Crew")* and *[Star Wars: The Bad Batch](/wiki/Star_Wars:_The_Bad_Batch "Star Wars: The Bad Batch")*). When more than one series is releasing simultaneously, the **show** parameter should be used to specify which media the article has spoilers from. Please refer to the instructions on [Template:SpoilerTV](/wiki/Template:SpoilerTV "Template:SpoilerTV").

:   →See [Forum:CT:Adding Tvspoiler to spoiler policy](/wiki/Forum:CT:Adding_Tvspoiler_to_spoiler_policy "Forum:CT:Adding Tvspoiler to spoiler policy")

## Adding new information to articles

Editors should avoid putting spoilers in [edit summaries](https://community.fandom.com/wiki/Help:Edit_summary "Help:Edit summary"). If including spoilers is necessary to be clear, the summary should be prefaced with a warning, e.g. "Andor spoilers: …"

In-universe information from future *Star Wars* products may only be added to articles once the information is released through official outlets, such as [StarWars.com](/wiki/StarWars.com "StarWars.com") press releases or exclusive feature stories in outlets like [*Entertainment Weekly*](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Entertainment_Weekly "wikipedia:Entertainment Weekly") and [*Vanity Fair*](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Vanity_Fair_(magazine) "wikipedia:Vanity Fair (magazine)"), where [Lucasfilm](/wiki/Lucasfilm "Lucasfilm") or an official licensee is expressly providing the information. This specifically disallows information that has been [leaked](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/News_leak "wikipedia:News leak") through unofficial channels. Information divulged in content creator–made reviews may not be added to articles.

Plot details from new *Star Wars* releases like films, television series, and novels may only be added to articles upon a product's U.S. wide-release date. Exceptions include information released through official preview promotional material, such as film trailers and book previews. The following specifics apply:

* **Films**. Information may be added upon midnight [Hawaii–Aleutian Standard Time](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hawaii%E2%80%93Aleutian_Time_Zone "wikipedia:Hawaii–Aleutian Time Zone") (00:00 in [UTC−10:00](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/UTC%E2%88%9210:00 "wikipedia:UTC−10:00")), the latest time zone of a U.S. state, on the day of the U.S. release.
* **Television series**. Information may be added upon a television episode's initial [Eastern Time Zone](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Eastern_Time_Zone "wikipedia:Eastern Time Zone") airtime.
* **Video games**. Information from video games can be added to articles as early as the start of their official early access periods, or upon the day of their standard release otherwise.
* **Other material**. Information for other products like novels and comics may be added upon their day of release.
