***Star Wars: Visions*** is an animated anthology television series[[2]](#cite_note-Release-2) of non-[canonical](/wiki/Canon "Canon")[[8]](#cite_note-Visions_non-canon-8) short films.[[2]](#cite_note-Release-2) Its [initial](/wiki/Star_Wars:_Visions_Volume_1 "Star Wars: Visions Volume 1") release comprised nine [anime](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Anime "wikipedia:Anime")-inspired original short films from various Japanese animation studios that present a different cultural perspective of *[Star Wars](/wiki/Star_Wars "Star Wars")*.[[2]](#cite_note-Release-2) The series premiered on the video streaming service [Disney+](/wiki/Disney%2B "Disney+") on [September 22](/wiki/September_22 "September 22"), [2021](/wiki/2021 "2021").[[2]](#cite_note-Release-2) The novel [*Ronin*](/wiki/Ronin:_A_Visions_Novel "Ronin: A Visions Novel") ties into the short "The Duel."[[9]](#cite_note-Twitter-9) Each short film of *Visions* has a corresponding featurette called *[Star Wars: Visions Filmmaker Focus](/wiki/Star_Wars:_Visions_Filmmaker_Focus "Star Wars: Visions Filmmaker Focus")* that details its making and goes behind the scenes.[[10]](#cite_note-Visions_is_here-10) On [May 29](/wiki/May_29 "May 29"), [2022](/wiki/2022 "2022"), a [second volume](/wiki/Star_Wars:_Visions_Volume_2 "Star Wars: Visions Volume 2") of *Star Wars: Visions* was announced, shifting focus for a showcase of animation studios from around the world.[[11]](#cite_note-SW-SWCA_2022:_6_Highlights_from_the_Star_Wars:_Visions_Panel-11) It debuted on [May 4](/wiki/May_4 "May 4"), [2023](/wiki/2023 "2023").[[5]](#cite_note-Vol2Release-5) On [November 19](/wiki/November_19 "November 19"), [2024](/wiki/2024 "2024"), it was announced a [third volume](/wiki/Star_Wars:_Visions_Volume_3 "Star Wars: Visions Volume 3") of *Star Wars: Visions* will release in [2025](/wiki/2025 "2025"). Volume 3 is intended to return to the series' original focus on the anime genre, with four returning animation studios, along with five new ones.[[12]](#cite_note-Vol3Announcement-12)

## Official description[]

 [![Concept art from the series](data:image/gif;base64,R0lGODlhAQABAIABAAAAAP///yH5BAEAAAEALAAAAAABAAEAQAICTAEAOw%3D%3D)](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/starwars/images/a/a3/Visions_Concept.jpg/revision/latest?cb=20201211082335) [![Concept art from the series](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/starwars/images/a/a3/Visions_Concept.jpg/revision/latest/scale-to-width-down/250?cb=20201211082335)](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/starwars/images/a/a3/Visions_Concept.jpg/revision/latest?cb=20201211082335) 

Concept art from the series

 

*Presenting all-new, creative takes on the galaxy far, far away,* Star Wars: Visions *will be a series of animated short films celebrating* Star Wars *through the lens of the world's best anime creators. The anthology collection will bring fantastic visions from several of the leading Japanese anime studios, offering a fresh and diverse cultural perspective to* Star Wars.[[4]](#cite_note-Announcement-4)

## Continuity[]

:   "*We realized we wanted these to be as authentic as possible to the studios and creators who are making them, made through their unique process, in a medium they're such experts at. So the idea was, this is their vision riffing off all the elements of the* Star Wars *galaxy that inspired them — hopefully to make a really incredible anthology series, unlike anything we've seen before in the* Star Wars *galaxy.*"
:   ―[James Waugh](/wiki/James_Waugh "James Waugh")[[2]](#cite_note-Release-2)

So as to ensure the storytellers had the most freedom possible in developing their *Visions* shorts,[[8]](#cite_note-Visions_non-canon-8) Lucasfilm allowed the shorts to break from the pre-existing *[Star Wars](/wiki/Star_Wars "Star Wars")* timeline.[[2]](#cite_note-Release-2) [Matt Martin](/wiki/Matt_Martin "Matt Martin") of the [Lucasfilm Story Group](/wiki/Lucasfilm_Story_Group "Lucasfilm Story Group") clarified this means all shorts are non-canon. While shorts like "[Tatooine Rhapsody](/wiki/Tatooine_Rhapsody "Tatooine Rhapsody")" could theoretically fit within canon,[[8]](#cite_note-Visions_non-canon-8) no source at this time has brought a *Visions* story into the canonical *Star Wars* timeline.

The world and story of "[The Duel](/wiki/The_Duel_(short_film) "The Duel (short film)")" and [The Ronin](/wiki/The_Ronin "The Ronin") have been expanded in multiple pieces of media. *[Ronin: A Visions Novel](/wiki/Ronin:_A_Visions_Novel "Ronin: A Visions Novel")* (Japanese:『浪人』), written by [Emma Mieko Candon](/wiki/Emma_Mieko_Candon "Emma Mieko Candon"), was published by [Del Rey](/wiki/Del_Rey "Del Rey") on [October 12](/wiki/October_12 "October 12"), 2021[[18]](#cite_note-Ronin_SW.com2-18)[[19]](#cite_note-Novel-19) and two prequel one-shot comic books, written and illustrated by the episode's writer [Takashi Okazaki](/wiki/Takashi_Okazaki "Takashi Okazaki"), [*Visions* 1](/wiki/Visions_1 "Visions 1") and [*Visions – Takashi Okazaki* 1](/wiki/Visions_%E2%80%93_Takashi_Okazaki_1 "Visions – Takashi Okazaki 1"), were published by [Marvel Comics](/wiki/Marvel_Comics "Marvel Comics") on [October 12](/wiki/October_12 "October 12"), [2022](/wiki/2022 "2022")[[20]](#cite_note-Visions-1-20) and [March 20](/wiki/March_20 "March 20"), [2024](/wiki/2024 "2024") respectively.[[21]](#cite_note-Visions-Takashi-Okazaki-1-21)

[*Visions – Peach Momoko* 1](/wiki/Visions_%E2%80%93_Peach_Momoko_1 "Visions – Peach Momoko 1"), an original one-shot comic book written and illustrated by [Peach Momoko](/wiki/Peach_Momoko "Peach Momoko") was published by Marvel Comics in [November 15](/wiki/November_15 "November 15"), [2023](/wiki/2023 "2023").[[22]](#cite_note-peach-momoko-22)

Another original manga, titled *[Star Wars: Visions: Tsukumo](/wiki/Star_Wars:_Visions:_Tsukumo "Star Wars: Visions: Tsukumo")*,[[23]](#cite_note-NYCC_2025-23) written by [Eiichi Shimizu](/wiki/Eiichi_Shimizu "Eiichi Shimizu"), illustrated by [Tomohiro Shimoguchi](/wiki/Tomohiro_Shimoguchi "Tomohiro Shimoguchi"), and published by [Viz Media](/wiki/Viz_Media "Viz Media") was also announced during the [New York Comic Con](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/New_York_Comic_Con "wikipedia:New York Comic Con") on [October 9](/wiki/October_9 "October 9"), [2022](/wiki/2022 "2022").[[24]](#cite_note-NYCC-24)
