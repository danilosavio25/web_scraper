A **Sith apprentice** was a [Force-sensitive](/wiki/Force-sensitive "Force-sensitive") individual trained by a [Sith Master](/wiki/Sith_Master "Sith Master") to use the [dark side of the Force](/wiki/Dark_side_of_the_Force "Dark side of the Force"). The age at which a [Sith](/wiki/Sith "Sith") apprentice began their training varied. [Darth Maul](/wiki/Maul "Maul") became apprenticed to [Darth Sidious](/wiki/Darth_Sidious "Darth Sidious") while still a [child](/wiki/Youngling "Youngling"). [Darth Tyranus](/wiki/Dooku "Dooku") and [Darth Vader](/wiki/Anakin_Skywalker "Anakin Skywalker"), both former members of the [Jedi Order](/wiki/Jedi_Order "Jedi Order"), became Sidious' apprentice, in turn, as adults, with Tyranus being almost seventy years old when he became Sidious' apprentice. Although [Darth Bane](/wiki/Darth_Bane "Darth Bane")'s [Rule of Two](/wiki/Rule_of_Two "Rule of Two") mandated that only two Sith could exist at any given [time](/wiki/Time "Time"), Tyranus broke with the system by training his own successive apprentices—[Asajj Ventress](/wiki/Asajj_Ventress "Asajj Ventress"), [Savage Opress](/wiki/Savage_Opress "Savage Opress"), and [Quinlan Vos](/wiki/Quinlan_Vos "Quinlan Vos")—while still apprenticed to Sidious.

## Description[]

:   "*Become my apprentice. Learn to use the dark side of the Force.*"
:   ―Darth Sidious, to Anakin Skywalker[[4]](#cite_note-Episode_III-4)

 [![As per the hunger for power inherent to the dark side, Sith apprentices longed to overthrow their master to become the master themself.](data:image/gif;base64,R0lGODlhAQABAIABAAAAAP///yH5BAEAAAEALAAAAAABAAEAQAICTAEAOw%3D%3D)](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/starwars/images/4/48/Vader40-AlanQuah.png/revision/latest?cb=20240413001702) [![As per the hunger for power inherent to the dark side, Sith apprentices longed to overthrow their master to become the master themself.](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/starwars/images/4/48/Vader40-AlanQuah.png/revision/latest/scale-to-width-down/250?cb=20240413001702)](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/starwars/images/4/48/Vader40-AlanQuah.png/revision/latest?cb=20240413001702) 

As per the hunger for power inherent to the dark side, Sith apprentices longed to overthrow their master to become the master themself.

 

[Sith Masters](/wiki/Sith_Master "Sith Master") demanded absolute loyalty from their apprentices, but would betray and replace them if they felt stronger potential in another. Younger apprentices made ideal candidates for masters to turn to the [dark side of the Force](/wiki/Dark_side_of_the_Force "Dark side of the Force"), as they lacked the emotional control of a fully fledged [Jedi Knight](/wiki/Jedi_Knight "Jedi Knight"). Inexperienced youth were easier to taint and twist for the purposes of the [Sith](/wiki/Sith "Sith").[[5]](#cite_note-VE-5) Under the [Rule of Two](/wiki/Rule_of_Two "Rule of Two"), it was the duty of a Sith apprentice to one [day](/wiki/Standard_day "Standard day") slay their master and take that place for themself. With that, the newly christened Sith Master would claim an apprentice of their own[[6]](#cite_note-TSWB-6) to pass down the teachings of the Sith.[[7]](#cite_note-Sacrifice-7) Such a model ensured the Sith Order grew in cunning and power with each generation[[6]](#cite_note-TSWB-6) and kept to the shadows,[[4]](#cite_note-Episode_III-4) rather than repeat the infighting created by having too many Sith at one [time](/wiki/Time "Time").[[7]](#cite_note-Sacrifice-7) The apprentice was allowed to train a disposable dark side agent[[8]](#cite_note-Nightsisters-8) identified as a [Sith acolyte](/wiki/Sith_acolyte "Sith acolyte"),[[9]](#cite_note-Dark_Disciple-9) whom they were to supposed to hold back from training as[[8]](#cite_note-Nightsisters-8) a true Sith[[10]](#cite_note-USW_NE-10) so as to ensure only two [Sith Lords](/wiki/Sith_Lord "Sith Lord") existed at one time.[[8]](#cite_note-Nightsisters-8)

If the Sith apprentice failed to overthrow their master, they would most likely be killed in their attempt and, with their failure, be proven unworthy of the Sith lineage, forcing the master to adopt a new apprentice.[[11]](#cite_note-Rise-11) Furthermore, the Sith Master could plot to intercede and replace their apprentice with a more promising student. An outside individual who wanted to claim the Sith apprentice title could attempt to prove themselves by killing the current apprentice.[[12]](#cite_note-DV_48-12) In their effort to overthrow their master, the apprentice might defy the Rule of Two by training an apprentice of their own early—such as by taking their Sith acolyte and training them beyond accepted parameters—in order to have that disciple's aid in overthrowing the master.[[8]](#cite_note-Nightsisters-8)

## History[]

### Always two there are[]

:   "*You have learned much, my apprentice. But if you think that your training is complete, you are as much a fool as the thief you killed.*"
:   ―Darth Sidious, to Darth Maul[[13]](#cite_note-AoR-DM-13)

Following the infighting among the Sith, the surviving [Sith Lord](/wiki/Sith_Lord "Sith Lord") [Darth Bane](/wiki/Darth_Bane "Darth Bane") created the Rule of Two, stating that there shall only be two; a master to embody the power and an apprentice to crave it.[[7]](#cite_note-Sacrifice-7) Bane then took an apprentice, [Darth Zannah](/wiki/Darth_Zannah "Darth Zannah"),[[14]](#cite_note-HC_2-14) who was able to evade the [Jedi Order](/wiki/Jedi_Order "Jedi Order") when they apparently [killed](/wiki/Death "Death") Bane,[[15]](#cite_note-SWEBane-15) although it was rumored that Zannah herself killed Bane in a [lightsaber duel](/wiki/Lightsaber_duel "Lightsaber duel") on [Ambria](/wiki/Ambria "Ambria").[[14]](#cite_note-HC_2-14) Believed extinct by the [Jedi](/wiki/Jedi "Jedi"), the Sith endured and carried on the tradition for over a [millennium](/wiki/Millennium "Millennium").[[16]](#cite_note-Episode_I-16) Over the [centuries](/wiki/Standard_century "Standard century") of secret actions against the Jedi, Sith apprentices had the custom of using special [starships](/wiki/Starship "Starship") known as [Sith Infiltrators](/wiki/Scimitar "Scimitar").[[17]](#cite_note-Complete_Vehicles_2018-17)

 [![Darth Maul was the first Sith apprentice of Darth Sidious.](data:image/gif;base64,R0lGODlhAQABAIABAAAAAP///yH5BAEAAAEALAAAAAABAAEAQAICTAEAOw%3D%3D)](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/starwars/images/7/70/RuleOfTwo-CM.png/revision/latest?cb=20200909233004) [![Darth Maul was the first Sith apprentice of Darth Sidious.](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/starwars/images/7/70/RuleOfTwo-CM.png/revision/latest/scale-to-width-down/250?cb=20200909233004)](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/starwars/images/7/70/RuleOfTwo-CM.png/revision/latest?cb=20200909233004) 

Darth Maul was the first Sith apprentice of Darth Sidious.

 

Sometime before the [Invasion of Naboo](/wiki/Invasion_of_Naboo "Invasion of Naboo"), the Sith [Lord](/wiki/Lord "Lord") [Darth Sidious](/wiki/Darth_Sidious "Darth Sidious") was trained by [Darth Plagueis](/wiki/Darth_Plagueis "Darth Plagueis"). Together they sought to achieve [immortality](/wiki/Immortality "Immortality")[[18]](#cite_note-Tarkin-18) and control [the galaxy](/wiki/The_galaxy "The galaxy") as equal rulers of a new [Sith Empire](/wiki/Sith_Empire "Sith Empire").[[19]](#cite_note-SWEsid-19) However, Sidious betrayed and murdered Plagueis in his [sleep](/wiki/Sleep "Sleep") after learning everything he needed from him.[[4]](#cite_note-Episode_III-4) The Sith and their practices continued through Sidious and his own apprentice, [Darth Maul](/wiki/Maul "Maul"), son of the [Nightsister](/wiki/Nightsisters "Nightsisters") [Mother](/wiki/Mother_(title) "Mother (title)") [Talzin](/wiki/Talzin "Talzin").[[20]](#cite_note-explEPIII-20)

During Maul's training, Sidious brought him to [Malachor](/wiki/Malachor "Malachor"), a [planet](/wiki/Planet "Planet") that had been [scourged](/wiki/Great_Scourge_of_Malachor "Great Scourge of Malachor") by the conflict between the ancient Jedi and Sith. While young Zabrak's hatred for the Jedi was approved by his master, who sensed the power his disciple gained from it, the latter wanted to teach his apprentice to temper his bloodlust with patience, lest reckless activities on [Coruscant](/wiki/Coruscant "Coruscant") revealed the Sith's return to the Jedi Order. The resulting [Force vision](/wiki/Force_vision "Force vision") that Maul experienced taught him that rage alone was not be enough to defeat the Jedi, who held an advantage over the Sith in numbers as well as the trust of the [Galactic Republic](/wiki/Galactic_Republic "Galactic Republic").[[13]](#cite_note-AoR-DM-13)

Sidious replaced Maul with Darth Tyranus, a former Jedi who turned to the dark side of the Force.

 

Although Maul survived[[21]](#cite_note-Witches_of_the_Mist-21) his [battle](/wiki/Battle_of_Naboo "Battle of Naboo") with [Padawan](/wiki/Padawan "Padawan") [Obi-Wan Kenobi](/wiki/Obi-Wan_Kenobi "Obi-Wan Kenobi"),[[16]](#cite_note-Episode_I-16) Sidious moved on with the [Grand Plan](/wiki/Grand_Plan "Grand Plan") and replaced him by taking a new apprentice,[[22]](#cite_note-The_Lawless-22) the [Count of Serenno](/wiki/Count_of_Serenno "Count of Serenno") and former [Jedi Master](/wiki/Jedi_Master "Jedi Master") [Dooku](/wiki/Dooku "Dooku"). Dooku had already been working with Sidious but had yet to fully devote himself to the dark side. Despite his reservations, he believed he was working to make a better galaxy[[23]](#cite_note-The_Sith_Lord-23) and worked under the name "Tyranus." He even eliminated his friend, Jedi Master [Sifo-Dyas](/wiki/Sifo-Dyas "Sifo-Dyas"), taking control of his [clone army](/wiki/Grand_Army_of_the_Republic "Grand Army of the Republic") with Sidious in the process.[[24]](#cite_note-The_Lost_One-24) After the [duel in the Works](/wiki/Duel_in_the_Works "Duel in the Works"),[[23]](#cite_note-The_Sith_Lord-23) Dooku fully submitted to Sidious as his new apprentice, [Darth](/wiki/Darth "Darth") Tyranus, through whom Sidious shaped the [Confederacy of Independent Systems](/wiki/Confederacy_of_Independent_Systems "Confederacy of Independent Systems").[[25]](#cite_note-Riseofthe-25) However, Sidious did not consider Tyranus as a true apprentice, but rather a placeholder and a tool for creating instability.[[26]](#cite_note-Darth_Vader_20-26)

Dooku also recruited the [bounty hunter](/wiki/Bounty_hunter "Bounty hunter") [Jango Fett](/wiki/Jango_Fett "Jango Fett") to be the [clone template](/wiki/Clone_template "Clone template"), and around this time he erased the data on [Kamino](/wiki/Kamino "Kamino") from the [Jedi Archives](/wiki/Jedi_Archives "Jedi Archives"). The Sith Lord further ensured the success of the Grand Plan, allowing the [Separatist Crisis](/wiki/Separatist_Crisis "Separatist Crisis") to escalate into the [Clone Wars](/wiki/Clone_Wars "Clone Wars") before personally delivering the [schematics](/wiki/Death_Star_plans "Death Star plans") for the [ultimate weapon](/wiki/DS-1_Orbital_Battle_Station "DS-1 Orbital Battle Station") to his master on Coruscant.[[27]](#cite_note-Episode_II-27) For three years, the [Count of Serenno](/wiki/Count_of_Serenno "Count of Serenno") led the Confederacy against the Republic. During this time, Tyranus took the [Dathomirian](/wiki/Dathomirian "Dathomirian") [assassin](/wiki/Assassin "Assassin") [Asajj Ventress](/wiki/Asajj_Ventress "Asajj Ventress") under his wing,[[8]](#cite_note-Nightsisters-8) making her his [dark acolyte](/wiki/Dark_Acolyte "Dark Acolyte") in an interpretable violation of the Rule of Two.[[28]](#cite_note-BMF-28) Ventress was indeed the Count's Sith apprentice[[29]](#cite_note-219_Fantasy-29) but could not formally claim the title of Sith Lord.[[30]](#cite_note-HS_5-30)

 [![Asajj Ventress and Savage Opress were trained, in turn, by Tyranus who was still apprenticed to Sidious.](data:image/gif;base64,R0lGODlhAQABAIABAAAAAP///yH5BAEAAAEALAAAAAABAAEAQAICTAEAOw%3D%3D)](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/starwars/images/9/95/VentressSavageDuo-WOTM.jpg/revision/latest?cb=20110924042607) [![Asajj Ventress and Savage Opress were trained, in turn, by Tyranus who was still apprenticed to Sidious.](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/starwars/images/9/95/VentressSavageDuo-WOTM.jpg/revision/latest/scale-to-width-down/250?cb=20110924042607)](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/starwars/images/9/95/VentressSavageDuo-WOTM.jpg/revision/latest?cb=20110924042607) 

Asajj Ventress and Savage Opress were trained, in turn, by Tyranus who was still apprenticed to Sidious.

 

During a [mission](/wiki/Mission_to_Cato_Neimoidia_(Kenobi_and_Skywalker) "Mission to Cato Neimoidia (Kenobi and Skywalker)") on the planet [Cato Neimoidia](/wiki/Cato_Neimoidia "Cato Neimoidia"), Ventress also tried to recruit and kidnap a [Jedi youngling](/wiki/Jedi_Initiate "Jedi Initiate") named [Mill Alibeth](/wiki/Mill_Alibeth "Mill Alibeth"), promising to free her from the ways of the Jedi and ensure she trained under a true master. However, she was forced to escape and abandon her plan to kidnap Alibeth.[[31]](#cite_note-Brotherhood-31) Although Ventress endeavored to prove herself worthy of being his apprentice[[32]](#cite_note-Cloak_of_Darkness-32) and become a true Sith,[[10]](#cite_note-USW_NE-10) Tyranus was forced to discard her during the [Battle of Sullust](/wiki/Battle_of_Sullust "Battle of Sullust") when Sidious grew suspicious of their relationship, having realized that Dooku was training her to be a Sith apprentice[[8]](#cite_note-Nightsisters-8) to ensure he could take Sidious's place under the Rule of Two.[[9]](#cite_note-Dark_Disciple-9)

Dooku subsequently searched for a new apprentice on [Dathomir](/wiki/Dathomir "Dathomir"), Ventress' [homeworld](/wiki/Homeworld "Homeworld"), where he found the [Nightbrother](/wiki/Nightbrothers "Nightbrothers") [Savage Opress](/wiki/Savage_Opress "Savage Opress"), taking him on as his next dark acolyte.[[33]](#cite_note-Ultimate-33) With Opress at his side, Tyranus resumed his plan to eventually overthrow Sidious[[34]](#cite_note-Monster-34) until the [Count](/wiki/Count "Count") discovered that the Zabrak was part of his old pupil's plan to get revenge on him.[[21]](#cite_note-Witches_of_the_Mist-21) He later tried to turn the undercover [Jedi Master](/wiki/Jedi_Master "Jedi Master") [Quinlan Vos](/wiki/Quinlan_Vos "Quinlan Vos") to the dark side, believing they could overthrow Sidious together as master and apprentice. Unlike his other two disciples, the Count told him he would be a proper Dark Lord of the Sith. However, Vos would betray Tyranus after the latter killed his lover Ventress with [Force lightning](/wiki/Force_lightning "Force lightning").[[9]](#cite_note-Dark_Disciple-9)

### Days of Darth Vader[]

#### The Emperor's Fist[]

The Jedi destroyed! The Republic a shattered corpse for me to resurrect as a puppet! And you, Vader—the boy transformed into the man you were destined to be…*"

Tyranus was replaced by Darth Vader, another fallen Jedi, as Sidious' apprentice.

 

Tyranus remained Sidious' apprentice while Opress' training was continued by his brother Maul. Their claim to the mantle of the Sith[[35]](#cite_note-Revival-35)[[36]](#cite_note-Eminence-36) led the true Dark Lord to [confront](/wiki/Battle_of_Mandalore "Battle of Mandalore") his former apprentice, resulting in Opress' death and Maul's capture.[[22]](#cite_note-The_Lawless-22) Sidious ultimately [sacrificed](/wiki/Battle_of_Coruscant "Battle of Coruscant") and betrayed Tyranus in favor of a younger and stronger apprentice who had more potential, [Jedi Knight](/wiki/Jedi_Knight "Jedi Knight") [Anakin Skywalker](/wiki/Anakin_Skywalker "Anakin Skywalker") who was also the [Chosen One](/wiki/Chosen_One "Chosen One") destined to bring balance to [the Force](/wiki/The_Force "The Force") by destroying the Sith. After manipulating Anakin with promises to grant him the power to save his wife and convincing him that the Jedi were holding him back, Sidious managed to turn Skywalker to the dark side. Skywalker pledged his allegiance to Sidious, becoming a Sith apprentice under the name of [Darth](/wiki/Darth "Darth") Vader.[[4]](#cite_note-Episode_III-4)

However, Vader was unable to fulfill his potential after he was severely injured in a [duel](/wiki/Duel_on_Mustafar "Duel on Mustafar") with his former master Obi-Wan Kenobi. They ruled the [Galactic Empire](/wiki/Galactic_Empire "Galactic Empire") for a [generation](/wiki/Imperial_Era "Imperial Era") with Sidious as [Galactic Emperor](/wiki/Galactic_Emperor "Galactic Emperor"),[[37]](#cite_note-Sith_DB-37) while his apprentice, Vader, acted as de facto [Commander-in-Chief](/wiki/Commander-in-Chief "Commander-in-Chief").[[38]](#cite_note-Bust_Collection-38) Before taking on Vader's son [Luke Skywalker](/wiki/Luke_Skywalker "Luke Skywalker") as his apprentice, [Grand Master](/wiki/Grand_Master "Grand Master") [Yoda](/wiki/Yoda "Yoda") entered into the [Cave of Evil](/wiki/Cave_of_Evil "Cave of Evil") on the planet [Dagobah](/wiki/Dagobah "Dagobah"), where he remained in exile, to confront his fears. As part of the vision, an illusion of Sidious mocked Yoda for his failure to see the Sith Grand Plan while he had the chance, heralding Yoda as the greatest of his apprentices. However, through a vision of the boy [Bree Menaren](/wiki/Bree_Menaren "Bree Menaren"), Yoda was reminded that hope always existed and triumphed over the illusions, readying him for his coming mentorship to the young Skywalker.[[39]](#cite_note-Yoda_10-39)

During the rise of the Galactic Empire, Darth Vader enforced his Sith Master's reign over the galaxy.

 

During the [Galactic Civil War](/wiki/Galactic_Civil_War "Galactic Civil War"), Vader tried to convince Skywalker to become his apprentice and rule the galaxy as a [family](/wiki/Skywalker_family "Skywalker family").[[1]](#cite_note-Episode_V-1) After his failure to turn Skywalker to the dark side and a trial on Mustafar, a damaged Vader ventured to Sidious's hidden Sith world of [Exegol](/wiki/Exegol "Exegol"), where Sidious was served by many [Sith cultists](/wiki/Sith_cultist "Sith cultist") and keep secrets like a generically grown force of warriors[[40]](#cite_note-Darth_Vader_11-40) known as the [Tankers](/wiki/Tanker_(Exegol) "Tanker (Exegol)").[[41]](#cite_note-Tankers_tweet-41) Discovering Exegol via a [Wayfinder](/wiki/Wayfinder "Wayfinder") and declaring he was no longer Sidious's apprentice upon arrival, Vader turned on his Sith Master and attempted to hunt down Sidious. Upon confronting the Tankers, Sidious's servant [Ochi of Bestoon](/wiki/Ochi "Ochi"), whom Vader had pressed into his own service, assumed they were more Sith and, therefore, violations of the Rule of Two. Although Sidious teased Vader with the claim that the Tankers could replace him should he fall, Vader rebuked both Ochi and Sidious's remarks. Fighting four Tankers and even falling through the floor, Vader ultimately killed all four. Then faced by a horde of Sith cultists, whom Sidious also heralded as possible replacements for Vader, Vader, used the Force to turn the hordes' own daggers against them, cutting down all.[[40]](#cite_note-Darth_Vader_11-40)

Vader remained loyal to Sidious while various enemies threatened to topple their reign as Master and apprentice.

 

After discovering the [Final Order](/wiki/Final_Order "Final Order") fleet and a massive kyber crystal being harvested by his servants, Vader once more submitted to Sidious upon seeing the great power at his master's disposal: Sidious turned the power of the crystal on Vader, forcing him to bare witness to the "ultimate power" of the dark side. While he experienced a vision of his son telling him it was his destiny to overthrow the Emperor,[[40]](#cite_note-Darth_Vader_11-40) Vader was left with the belief only two Sith could exist, thereby forcing him to hunt down and kill his own son, in order to share in the true power of the Sith[[43]](#cite_note-Darth_Vader_12-43) as Sidious's apprentice; according to Sidious's claims, only as a Sith apprentice filled by pain did Vader have a way to way to share in his ultimate power.[[40]](#cite_note-Darth_Vader_11-40) After a skirmish against Skywalker in the [battle over Jekara](/wiki/Attack_on_the_Executor "Attack on the Executor")[[44]](#cite_note-WBH_5-44) and putting down [Crimson Dawn](/wiki/Crimson_Dawn "Crimson Dawn")'s [war against the Sith](/wiki/War_against_the_Sith "War against the Sith") alongside his master in the [Battle at Amaxine Station](/wiki/Battle_at_Amaxine_Station "Battle at Amaxine Station"),[[45]](#cite_note-HE_5-45) however, Vader would once more betray his master during the [Scourge crisis](/wiki/Scourge_crisis "Scourge crisis"): diving into his hatred for himself to [rediscover his connection to the Force](/wiki/Attack_on_Vader "Attack on Vader") after the [Force Wave](/wiki/Force_Wave "Force Wave") and defeat the [Scourge](/wiki/Scourge "Scourge") threat on Mustafar, Vader rediscovered his hatred for his Sidious[[46]](#cite_note-Darth_Vader_39-46) and ventured to Coruscant to test Sidious's own abilities.[[47]](#cite_note-Darth_Vader_40-47)

While he still recognized Sidious as his master, Vader attacked the Emperor at the [Imperial Palace](/wiki/Imperial_Palace "Imperial Palace") and unleashed the Scourge on Coruscant itself. With Sidious soon shutting down the [Scourge of Coruscant](/wiki/Scourge_of_Coruscant "Scourge of Coruscant") and besting his apprentice, Sidious revealed that, as long as Vader tried to grow his power via hatred, his own power would grow by thriving off his apprentice's hate:[[47]](#cite_note-Darth_Vader_40-47) as the Scourge would reason to Vader, it was impossible to overthrow the Emperor with his hatred as his power source. At that time, the Scourge knew that Vader's greatest desire was to overthrow the master who had humiliated him time and time again. Vader bonded with the Scourge briefly and attempted to create his own version of the virus in the name of fighting his master, yet his efforts failed and he was unable to control it.[[48]](#cite_note-Darth_Vader_41-48) Vader then tried to overthrow his master[[49]](#cite_note-Darth_Vader_42-49) by joining the ranks of the rogue [Schism Imperial](/wiki/Schism_Imperial "Schism Imperial"). Together, they launched an [assault on Exegol](/wiki/Assault_on_Exegol "Assault on Exegol"). Vader also resumed his efforts to turn his son to the dark side as his apprentice.[[50]](#cite_note-Darth_Vader_47-50)

Anakin Skywalker, the man who lived for years as the Sith apprentice Darth Vader, sacrificed himself to save his son from his Sith Master's Force lightning.

 

Vader's schemes[[50]](#cite_note-Darth_Vader_47-50) would fail, however: by the time of the [Battle of Endor](/wiki/Battle_of_Endor "Battle of Endor"), Vader believed it was pointless to resist his master and that he had no choice but to obey the Sith Lord. Additionally, Sidious had similar plans to Vader on Skywalker: he intended to replace Vader with Skywalker.[[2]](#cite_note-Episode_VI-2) Despite the ideals of the Rule of Two, which implied Skywalker would need to be his or the Emperor's apprentice alone if Skywalker was involved at all,[[50]](#cite_note-Darth_Vader_47-50) Vader was under the belief that they would turn Skywalker to the dark side as a third member of their order. Sidious betrayed Vader by attempting to goad Skywalker to kill his father in order to assume his place as a Sith apprentice, but Skywalker resisted the dark side and inspired Vader to revert back to Anakin and [destroy](/wiki/Battle_of_Endor "Battle of Endor") his master at the cost of his life.[[2]](#cite_note-Episode_VI-2) With the presumed destruction of Sidious and the Sith, the now redeemed Anakin brought balance to the Force, and thus fulfilled his destiny as the Chosen One.

Kylo Ren, the grandson of Darth Vader, was secretly trained by Darth Sidious through an artificial strandcast known as Snoke.

 

After returning from death, Sidious conspired to train Vader's grandson, [Kylo Ren](/wiki/Ben_Solo "Ben Solo"), by using the [strandcast](/wiki/Strand-Cast "Strand-Cast") [Snoke](/wiki/Snoke "Snoke") as a proxy master.[[52]](#cite_note-TROS_EE-52) Although Ren was not a Sith, he was a product of Sith teachings as well as [Jedi training](/wiki/Jedi_training "Jedi training").[[53]](#cite_note-Kylo_Ren_DB-53)

It was traditional for a dark side apprentice to kill his master in order to achieve his ascendancy. However, Sidious managed to bypass this custom by having Snoke die in his place.[[6]](#cite_note-TSWB-6) Ren sought to destroy Sidious, who considered himself to be Ren's true master,[[52]](#cite_note-TROS_EE-52) having sworn to never serve another master after Luke Skywalker and Snoke.[[54]](#cite_note-TROS_VD-54) By then, Ren aspired to rule the galaxy as the new [Emperor](/wiki/Emperor "Emperor")[[52]](#cite_note-TROS_EE-52) and Master of the Knights of Ren.[[55]](#cite_note-TROS_JN-55)
