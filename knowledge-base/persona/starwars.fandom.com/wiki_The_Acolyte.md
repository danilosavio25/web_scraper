***The Acolyte*** is a *[Star Wars](/wiki/Star_Wars "Star Wars")* television series developed by [Leslye Headland](/wiki/Leslye_Headland "Leslye Headland") for [Disney+](/wiki/Disney%2B "Disney+"). Headland wrote, executive produced, and served as the series' showrunner. It takes place a century before the events of [*Star Wars*: Episode I *The Phantom Menace*](/wiki/Star_Wars:_Episode_I_The_Phantom_Menace "Star Wars: Episode I The Phantom Menace"), and focuses on the emergence of [dark side powers](/wiki/Dark_side_of_the_Force "Dark side of the Force") in the final [days](/wiki/Standard_day "Standard day") of the [High Republic Era](/wiki/High_Republic_Era "High Republic Era"). *The Acolyte* premiered on [June 4](/wiki/June_4 "June 4"), [2024](/wiki/2024 "2024"), concluded its first season on [July 16](/wiki/July_16 "July 16"), 2024, and was reported as canceled on [August 19](/wiki/August_19 "August 19"), 2024, which was later confirmed by Disney executives. In spite of that ending, details have emerged that the series was intended to run for several seasons.

[Leslye Headland](/wiki/Leslye_Headland "Leslye Headland"), having served as co-creator for the [2019](/wiki/2019 "2019") [*Russian Doll*](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Russian_Doll_(TV_series) "wikipedia:Russian Doll (TV series)") television series, in her words "essentially cold-called [Lucasfilm](/wiki/Lucasfilm "Lucasfilm")"[[13]](#cite_note-NYT_May-13) in 2019[[14]](#cite_note-Inga-14) and pitched what would become *The Acolyte* as a cross between [Disney's](/wiki/The_Walt_Disney_Company "The Walt Disney Company") [2013](/wiki/2013 "2013") film [*Frozen*](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Frozen_(2013_film) "wikipedia:Frozen (2013 film)") and [Quentin Tarantino's](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Quentin_Tarantino "wikipedia:Quentin Tarantino") [2003](/wiki/2003 "2003") film [*Kill Bill*](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kill_Bill:_Volume_1 "wikipedia:Kill Bill: Volume 1").[[13]](#cite_note-NYT_May-13) For the initial pitch to Lucasfilm, Headland worked with her sister Inga Headland to design a [pitch deck](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Presentation_slide "wikipedia:Presentation slide") of imagery paired with story details for the presentation, the cover of which featured a woman in dark clothing cloaked in fog, with the title "Acolyte" displayed above.[[14]](#cite_note-Inga-14) The series' main characters of [Verosha "Osha" Aniseya](/wiki/Verosha_Aniseya "Verosha Aniseya") and [Mae-ho "Mae" Aniseya](/wiki/Mae-ho_Aniseya "Mae-ho Aniseya") were inspired by Headland's relationship with her youngest sister, and Lucasfilm's President [Kathleen Kennedy](/wiki/Kathleen_Kennedy "Kathleen Kennedy") told Headland to fashion her *[Star Wars](/wiki/Star_Wars "Star Wars")* story akin to her "incredibly emotional" play [*Cult of Love*](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cult_of_Love "wikipedia:Cult of Love") about siblinghood. Kennedy responded to Headland's early scripts in 2019: "You've written a great *Star Wars* show. Now go write a Leslye Headland show.*"[[13]](#cite_note-NYT_May-13)*

On [April 22](/wiki/April_22 "April 22"), [2020](/wiki/2020 "2020"), [*Variety*](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Variety_(magazine) "wikipedia:Variety (magazine)") reported that Leslye Headland was writing and serving as showrunner for a *Star Wars* series that would be released on the [Disney+](/wiki/Disney%2B "Disney+") streaming platform. The report noted that the series will be female-centric and take place in a new part of the *Star Wars* timeline.[[15]](#cite_note-Variety-15) An official announcement on [May 4](/wiki/May_4 "May 4"), 2020 on [StarWars.com](/wiki/StarWars.com "StarWars.com") added that Headland will be an executive producer on the project.[[2]](#cite_note-StarWars.com-2) The series's title, logo, and general premise were revealed at a Disney investors' meeting on [December 10](/wiki/December_10 "December 10"), 2020.[[16]](#cite_note-Announcement-16) Lucasfilm's website also listed vice president of film development [Rayne Roberts](/wiki/Rayne_Roberts "Rayne Roberts") as co-developing the series with Headland.[[17]](#cite_note-Rayne-17)

#### Literary and *Star Wars* influences[]

Leslye Headland drew from storytelling in both the [*Star Wars* Legends](/wiki/Star_Wars_Legends "Star Wars Legends") continuity, such as its 1990s roleplaying games as part of what was then known as the "Expanded Universe" beyond the stories of *[Star Wars](/wiki/Star_Wars "Star Wars")* creator [George Lucas](/wiki/George_Lucas "George Lucas"), and the ongoing *[Star Wars: The High Republic](/wiki/Star_Wars:_The_High_Republic "Star Wars: The High Republic")* multimedia project that was launched by Lucasfilm Publishing in 2021. The concept of an acolyte, after which the series is titled, was taken from the Legends continuity; a [Sith Acolyte](/wiki/Sith_acolyte "Sith acolyte") refers to an individual being trained for the position of a [Sith](/wiki/Sith "Sith")'s formal [apprentice](/wiki/Sith_apprentice "Sith apprentice"). Headland included [Vernestra Rwoh](/wiki/Vernestra_Rwoh "Vernestra Rwoh"), who was introduced as a [Jedi](/wiki/Jedi "Jedi") [Padawan](/wiki/Padawan "Padawan") learner in [Justina Ireland](/wiki/Justina_Ireland "Justina Ireland")'s 2021 novel [*A Test of Courage*](/wiki/The_High_Republic:_A_Test_of_Courage "The High Republic: A Test of Courage"), set during the golden age of the [Galactic Republic](/wiki/Galactic_Republic "Galactic Republic")'s [democracy](/wiki/Democracy "Democracy") and the [Jedi Order](/wiki/Jedi_Order "Jedi Order") in the [High Republic Era](/wiki/High_Republic_Era "High Republic Era") in [232 BBY](/wiki/232_BBY "232 BBY"). *The Acolyte* is set a hundred years afterward and stars Rwoh as a [Jedi Master](/wiki/Jedi_Master "Jedi Master"). The series is also set a hundred years before [George Lucas](/wiki/George_Lucas "George Lucas")' [prequel trilogy](/wiki/Prequel_trilogy "Prequel trilogy").[[22]](#cite_note-Vanity_Headland-22)

Headland thought about [Jedi Masters](/wiki/Jedi_Master "Jedi Master") [Mace Windu](/wiki/Mace_Windu "Mace Windu") and [Yoda](/wiki/Yoda "Yoda")'s remarks about the relative dormancy of the [dark side of the Force](/wiki/Dark_side_of_the_Force "Dark side of the Force") in the [1999](/wiki/1999 "1999") prequel film, [*Star Wars*: Episode I *The Phantom Menace*](/wiki/Star_Wars:_Episode_I_The_Phantom_Menace "Star Wars: Episode I The Phantom Menace"), asking who would have been practicing the dark side in the century leading up to the [Sith](/wiki/Sith "Sith")'s return and the rise of the [Galactic Empire](/wiki/Galactic_Empire "Galactic Empire"), as shown in [2002](/wiki/2002 "2002")'s [*Star Wars*: Episode II *Attack of the Clones*](/wiki/Star_Wars:_Episode_II_Attack_of_the_Clones "Star Wars: Episode II Attack of the Clones") and [2005](/wiki/2005 "2005")'s [*Star Wars*: Episode III *Revenge of the Sith*](/wiki/Star_Wars:_Episode_III_Revenge_of_the_Sith "Star Wars: Episode III Revenge of the Sith"). Headland also compares the time of the High Republic in which *The Acolyte* is set to the isolationist tendencies of the [United States](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/United_States "wikipedia:United States") post-[World War I](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/World_War_I "wikipedia:World War I"), particularly in terms of the decay of the High Republic to the time of the [prequel trilogy](/wiki/Prequel_trilogy "Prequel trilogy"), when [Darth Sidious](/wiki/Darth_Sidious "Darth Sidious") infiltrated the [Galactic Senate](/wiki/Galactic_Senate "Galactic Senate").[[22]](#cite_note-Vanity_Headland-22)

In terms of spirituality in *Star Wars*, Headland felt that the concept of the Force as explained by Yoda in *The Empire Strikes Back* strongly resonated with her, as compared to "even" the [English](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/English_people "wikipedia:English people") author [J.R.R. Tolkien](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/J._R._R._Tolkien "wikipedia:J. R. R. Tolkien"), whose [*The Lord of the Rings*](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/The_Lord_of_the_Rings "wikipedia:The Lord of the Rings") fantasy was published in the [1950s](/wiki/1950s "1950s"), or her own [Catholic](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Catholic_Church "wikipedia:Catholic Church") upbringing.[[22]](#cite_note-Vanity_Headland-22) The fantasy aspect of [the galaxy](/wiki/The_galaxy "The galaxy") far, far away also prompted her to compare it to the realm of [Narnia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/The_Chronicles_of_Narnia "wikipedia:The Chronicles of Narnia") created by [C. S. Lewis](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/C._S._Lewis "wikipedia:C. S. Lewis"), a contemporary of Tolkien's. Headland had a tattoo printed on her right hand after Lucasfilm hired her for *The Acolyte*, serving as a reminder of *Star Wars* to herself while making the series—the tattoo shows [Ralph McQuarrie](/wiki/Ralph_McQuarrie "Ralph McQuarrie")'s concept illustration of [Princess](/wiki/Princess "Princess") [Leia Organa](/wiki/Leia_Skywalker_Organa_Solo "Leia Skywalker Organa Solo") for Lucas' [original trilogy](/wiki/Original_trilogy "Original trilogy"), released between [1977](/wiki/1977 "1977") and [1983](/wiki/1983 "1983").[[23]](#cite_note-Headland_trailer_interview-23)

#### Cinematic influences[]

##### Hong Kong Chinese martial arts and Japanese samurai cinema[]

Headland also drew inspiration from her favorite martial arts films. The [Hong Kong](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hong_Kong_action_cinema "wikipedia:Hong Kong action cinema") [Shaw Brothers Studio](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Shaw_Brothers_Studio "wikipedia:Shaw Brothers Studio") film [*Come Drink with Me*](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Come_Drink_with_Me "wikipedia:Come Drink with Me")—directed by [King Hu](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/King_Hu "wikipedia:King Hu"), set in the [Ming era](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ming_dynasty "wikipedia:Ming dynasty") (1368—1644), and released in [1966](/wiki/1960s "1960s") under the [Chinese](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Traditional_Chinese_characters "wikipedia:Traditional Chinese characters") title 大醉俠—features a sequence at an inn that forms the basis of the [Lomi Usqi Noodle Shop](/wiki/Lomi_Usqi_Noodle_Shop "Lomi Usqi Noodle Shop") at the opening of *The Acolyte*'s first episode, "[Lost / Found](/wiki/Lost_/_Found "Lost / Found")."[[24]](#cite_note-Trivia_1-1-24) The fight sequence there between Jedi Master [Indara](/wiki/Indara "Indara") and the assassin [Mae](/wiki/Mae-ho_Aniseya "Mae-ho Aniseya") also pays homage to the martial arts choreography of [*Crouching Tiger, Hidden Dragon*](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Crouching_Tiger,_Hidden_Dragon "wikipedia:Crouching Tiger, Hidden Dragon"), a [2000](/wiki/2000 "2000") [Ang Lee](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ang_Lee "wikipedia:Ang Lee") film titled in Chinese as 臥虎藏龍.[[25]](#cite_note-Trivia_1-8-25)

Aside from [Chinese martial arts films](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Wuxia#Film_and_television "wikipedia:Wuxia"), known as "mouhap" in [Cantonese](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cantonese "wikipedia:Cantonese") and "wuxia" in [Mandarin](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mandarin_Chinese "wikipedia:Mandarin Chinese"), Headland was also inspired by the [Japanese samurai](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Samurai_cinema "wikipedia:Samurai cinema") film [*Yojimbo*](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Yojimbo "wikipedia:Yojimbo"), directed by [Akira Kurosawa](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Akira_Kurosawa "wikipedia:Akira Kurosawa") and released under the [Japanese](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Japanese_language "wikipedia:Japanese language") title 用心棒 in 1961.[[23]](#cite_note-Headland_trailer_interview-23) She explained that *[Star Wars: The Mandalorian](/wiki/Star_Wars:_The_Mandalorian "Star Wars: The Mandalorian")* producer [Jon Favreau](/wiki/Jon_Favreau "Jon Favreau") had mentioned that George Lucas had been influenced by Kurosawa's filmmaking and [Western films](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Western_film "wikipedia:Western film"), and Headland herself based her cinematic influences more strongly on martial arts films such as Shaw Brothers and King Hu productions—naming *Come Drink with Me* alongside King Hu's 1971 film [*A Touch of Zen*](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/A_Touch_of_Zen "wikipedia:A Touch of Zen"), in Chinese titled 俠女—that are "less global and galactic," with deeply personal missions in which the warriors felt "wronged" and had to "make it right."[[22]](#cite_note-Vanity_Headland-22) As a mystery thriller, *The Acolyte* was influenced by Kurosawa's film [*Rashomon*](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Rashomon "wikipedia:Rashomon"), released in [1950](/wiki/1950s "1950s") under the Japanese title 羅生門, for its use of differing interpretations of the same events.[[23]](#cite_note-Headland_trailer_interview-23) Cinematographer Chris Teague additionally cited the 1973 [Toshiya Fujita](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Toshiya_Fujita_(director) "wikipedia:Toshiya Fujita (director)") film [*Lady Snowblood*](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Lady_Snowblood_(film) "wikipedia:Lady Snowblood (film)"), a period drama titled 修羅雪姫 in Japanese, as an ##### Character inspirations[]

Headland compared the story of Mae to *Frozen* and *Kill Bill*, and the character was also inspired by the story of [Ahsoka Tano](/wiki/Ahsoka_Tano "Ahsoka Tano") in the *[Star Wars: The Clone Wars](/wiki/Star_Wars:_The_Clone_Wars "Star Wars: The Clone Wars")* television series' 2013 episode "[The Wrong Jedi](/wiki/The_Wrong_Jedi "The Wrong Jedi")" and the dynamic between [Qi'ra](/wiki/Qi%27ra "Qi'ra") and [Maul](/wiki/Maul "Maul") at the end of the 2016 film *[Solo: A Star Wars Story](/wiki/Solo:_A_Star_Wars_Story "Solo: A Star Wars Story")*.[[27]](#cite_note-Frozen_Bill-27) Amandla Stenberg wrote about the motivations and inner lives of Osha and Mae,[[18]](#cite_note-Turner-Smith_Glamour-18) with separate binders for each twin to inform her approach as a performer and differentiating the characters.[[28]](#cite_note-Stenberg_GMA-28) Indara was inspired by the character [Trinity](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Trinity_(The_Matrix) "wikipedia:Trinity (The Matrix)") from [the Wachowskis'](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/The_Wachowskis "wikipedia:The Wachowskis") [*The Matrix*](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/The_Matrix_(franchise) "wikipedia:The Matrix (franchise)") film franchise (1999—2021), played by [Carrie-Anne Moss](/wiki/Carrie-Anne_Moss "Carrie-Anne Moss"), who Headland also cast as Master Indara.[[29]](#cite_note-Trinity-29) For the first episode, "Lost / Found," Headland described the character's demeanor as both relaxed and controlled; a mix of Lucas' Skywalker saga Jedi character [Obi-Wan Kenobi](/wiki/Obi-Wan_Kenobi "Obi-Wan Kenobi") and the character O-Ren Ishii from *Kill Bill*.[[30]](#cite_note-Trivia_1-7-30)

Mae's accomplice, [Qimir](/wiki/The_Stranger "The Stranger"), is played by [Manny Jacinto](/wiki/Manny_Jacinto "Manny Jacinto") as a scoundrel archetype based on [Johnny Depp](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Johnny_Depp "wikipedia:Johnny Depp")'s performance of [Jack Sparrow](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Jack_Sparrow "wikipedia:Jack Sparrow") in the [*Pirates of the Caribbean*](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pirates_of_the_Caribbean_(film_series) "wikipedia:Pirates of the Caribbean (film series)") film series (2003–2017). Jacinto also compared Qimir's movements to the [silent film](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Silent_film "wikipedia:Silent film") works by [Buster Keaton](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Buster_Keaton "wikipedia:Buster Keaton") and [Charlie Chaplin](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Charlie_Chaplin "wikipedia:Charlie Chaplin") in the early 20th century.[[31]](#cite_note-Jacinto_influences-31) Jacinto appreciated the duality of his character as a dream role for an actor, being able to emphasize the comic qualities of Qimir and then the stillness of the Stranger.[[32]](#cite_note-MJ_duality-32)

The fourth and fifth episodes, respectively titled "[Day](/wiki/Day_(episode) "Day (episode)")" and "[Night](/wiki/Night "Night")," were partly inspired by two [Hollywood](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cinema_of_the_United_States "wikipedia:Cinema of the United States") science fiction films: [Ridley Scott's](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ridley_Scott "wikipedia:Ridley Scott") [1979](/wiki/1979 "1979") [*Alien*](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Alien_(film) "wikipedia:Alien (film)") and [John McTiernan's](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/John_McTiernan "wikipedia:John McTiernan") [1987](/wiki/1987 "1987") [*Predator*](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Predator_(film) "wikipedia:Predator (film)").[[33]](#cite_note-Trivia_5-3-33) Given his dual role as the Stranger, Jacinto was additionally inspired by [Toshiro Mifune's](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Toshiro_Mifune "wikipedia:Toshiro Mifune") performance as the warrior Kikushiyo in 1954's [*Seven Samurai*](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Seven_Samurai "wikipedia:Seven Samurai"), a Kurosawa film named 七人の侍 in Japanese, and [Jackie Chan's](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Jackie_Chan "wikipedia:Jackie Chan") performance as the martial artist [Wong Fei-hung](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Wong_Fei-hung "wikipedia:Wong Fei-hung") in the [Hong Kong](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cinema_of_Hong_Kong "wikipedia:Cinema of Hong Kong") film [*Drunken Master*](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Drunken_Master "wikipedia:Drunken Master"), released as 醉拳 in [1978](/wiki/1978 "1978").[[34]](#cite_note-Windsor-34)

#### Production design[]

:   "*If you look at periods of extended peace in history, you have things like the Renaissance, where art, design, architecture, and clothing are allowed to flourish because the money, resources, and creativity are available to make beautiful things. So, that was part of the design ethos that Kevin took onboard, as well as the costume design. The costumes, the lightsabers, even to some extent the ships, feel less brutal and more designed and finessed. The Jedi's robes have more gold and are more regal. There are more curves and flowing lines in the ships.*"
:   ―Visual effects supervisor Julian Foddy[[35]](#cite_note-Foddy_SW.com-35)

Brendok's mountain retreat was inspired by the 1947 British film [*Black Narcissus*](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Black_Narcissus "wikipedia:Black Narcissus") by [Michael Powell and Emeric Pressburger](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Powell_and_Pressburger "wikipedia:Powell and Pressburger")—set at a nuns' mountain retreat in the [Indian](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/British_Raj "wikipedia:British Raj") [Himalayas](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Himalayas "wikipedia:Himalayas")—coupled with the [Hoover Dam](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hoover_Dam "wikipedia:Hoover Dam") in the United States.[[26]](#cite_note-Crouching_Jedi_Hidden_Sith-26) For Coruscant and its Jedi Temple, production designer [Kevin Jenkins](/wiki/Kevin_Jenkins "Kevin Jenkins") and visual effects supervisor [Julian Foddy](/wiki/Julian_Foddy "Julian Foddy") decided aligning moreso with the prequel trilogy's establishing shots than the ground-level perspective shown in the 2022 television series *[Star Wars: Andor](/wiki/Star_Wars:_Andor "Star Wars: Andor")*, but with distinctive aesthetics echoing the 1971 [William Friedkin](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/William_Friedkin "wikipedia:William Friedkin") film [*The French Connection*](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/The_French_Connection_(film) "wikipedia:The French Connection (film)") and 1970s [New York City](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/New_York_City "wikipedia:New York City"): in particular, a desaturated and photorealistic "retro" atmosphere of haziness.[[36]](#cite_note-Foddy_LF-36)

Jenkins heeded George Lucas' 1977 film [*Star Wars*: Episode IV *A New Hope*'s](/wiki/Star_Wars:_Episode_IV_A_New_Hope "Star Wars: Episode IV A New Hope") lived-in aesthetic, stating that the core of *Star Wars* is [Stanley Kubrick's](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Stanley_Kubrick "wikipedia:Stanley Kubrick") 1968 film [*2001: A Space Odyssey*](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2001:_A_Space_Odyssey "wikipedia:2001: A Space Odyssey") "with dirt"—he learned from the designs of *A New Hope* production crew [John Barry](/wiki/John_Barry "John Barry"), [Norman Reynolds](/wiki/Norman_Reynolds "Norman Reynolds"), and Ralph McQuarrie while cinematographer Chris Teague used anamorphic lenses for *The Acolyte*, with film grain being added to the footage. Jenkins also likened Headland's episodic structure to the cliffhanger-based approach of the [*Flash Gordon*](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Flash_Gordon "wikipedia:Flash Gordon") serials, and further compared the High Republic Era to the height of the [Roman Empire](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Roman_Empire "wikipedia:Roman Empire"), incorporating vivid colors in *The Acolyte*'s production design while maintaining a uniformity of Jedi designs previously shown in *The High Republic* publishing initiative.[[26]](#cite_note-Crouching_Jedi_Hidden_Sith-26) Jenkins said Olega's city was "essentially [Casablanca](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Casablanca "wikipedia:Casablanca") in space" with its blue and red color palette,[[37]](#cite_note-LOL-37) while StarWars.com stated it was inspired by [Marrakesh](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Marrakesh "wikipedia:Marrakesh"), another city in [Morocco](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Morocco "wikipedia:Morocco").[[38]](#cite_note-Trivia_2-1-38)

Costume designer [Jennifer Bryan](/wiki/Jennifer_Bryan "Jennifer Bryan") researched [Trisha Biggar](/wiki/Trisha_Biggar "Trisha Biggar")'s prequel trilogy costumes at the [Lucasfilm Archives](/wiki/Lucasfilm_Archives "Lucasfilm Archives") at [Skywalker Ranch](/wiki/Skywalker_Ranch "Skywalker Ranch"), which particularly informed the clothing of the High Republic Era Jedi along with existing designs, such as High Republic Jedi symbols, from the multimedia project. The color palette of Osha's civilian clothing was informed by the era of the [Vietnam War](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Vietnam_War "wikipedia:Vietnam War") that preceded George Lucas' original trilogy and the *Star Wars* design principle of greeblies and badges. Another distinctive *Star Wars* fashion staple, the poncho, was also incorporated in civilian clothing featured in later episodes. Bryan further took inspiration from warrior cultures from [Asia Minor](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Anatolia "wikipedia:Anatolia"), [East Africa](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/East_Africa "wikipedia:East Africa"), [North Africa](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/North_Africa "wikipedia:North Africa"), and the Roman and [Byzantine Empires](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Byzantine_Empire "wikipedia:Byzantine Empire"): "It needs to look like it's put together from these different historical eras of warriors, but not specific to any one culture." Mae's assassin attire in particular was informed by samurai elements. Bryan also thought that Jodie Turner-Smith's elegance complemented the design made for Mother Aniseya, while [Yord Fandar](/wiki/Yord_Fandar "Yord Fandar")'s fastidiousness and "lustrous drape" called for fabrics slightly different from those used on other Jedi costumes, with his more appearance-conscious attitude reflected in a cloak informed by Roman senators' [togas](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Toga "wikipedia:Toga").[[39]](#cite_note-Costumes-39)

The production gave special thanks to the historic Lucasfilm Archives at Skywalker Ranch and George Lucas' new [Lucas Museum of Narrative Art](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Lucas_Museum_of_Narrative_Art "wikipedia:Lucas Museum of Narrative Art").[[9]](#cite_note-end_credits-9) Leslye Headland emphasized using practical techniques in her direction of the production design, cinematography, and visual effects.[[36]](#cite_note-Foddy_LF-36)

#### Filming in Madeira, Wales, and England[]

:   "*What was really interesting about Wales, especially Southern Wales, is that it had a very raw sort of feeling to it. What also gravitated us to the area was the location of the landing of the [Jedi starship]* Polan*, which became our anchor base. Just behind us in that same valley was this old 18th century dam, this massive black stone brick wall. And nearby was this wonderful forest where the whole floor was a bed of moss growing up the trees that I thought could be where the girls are first introduced. So with the moss forest, the valley vistas and this dam that's singing out to be used, Wales just seemed to tick all the boxes.*"
:   ―Kevin Jenkins[[37]](#cite_note-LOL-37)

Filming began in Europe's autumn in 2022 and lasted until May of 2023.[[36]](#cite_note-Foddy_LF-36) Filming was largely done on location on [Portugal's](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Portugal "wikipedia:Portugal") [Madeira](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Madeira "wikipedia:Madeira") archipelago off the coast of [Africa](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Africa "wikipedia:Africa") and in and around the mountainous forest of the [Bannau Brycheiniog National Park](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Brecon_Beacons_National_Park "wikipedia:Brecon Beacons National Park") (Brecon Beacons) in [Wales](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Wales "wikipedia:Wales"). Interior scenes were shot at [Shinfield Studios](/wiki/Shinfield_Studios "Shinfield Studios") in [Berkshire](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Berkshire "wikipedia:Berkshire"), [England](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/England "wikipedia:England").[[37]](#cite_note-LOL-37) Shooting also took place at the woods of Berkshire's [Englefield Estate](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Englefield_House "wikipedia:Englefield House").[[40]](#cite_note-Locations-40) Manny Jacinto prepared for his role as Qimir in the relative seclusion of the nearby town of [Windsor](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Windsor,_Berkshire "wikipedia:Windsor, Berkshire").[[34]](#cite_note-Windsor-34) Sur Film provided production services in Portugal.[[9]](#cite_note-end_credits-9)

The crew initially used Madeira for the planet [Khofar](/wiki/Khofar "Khofar")'s jungles, but they made use of other parts of the archipelago for scenes set on [Olega](/wiki/Olega "Olega"), [Ueda](/wiki/Ueda "Ueda"), and[[26]](#cite_note-Crouching_Jedi_Hidden_Sith-26) [Bal'demnic](/wiki/Bal%27demnic "Bal'demnic")[[41]](#cite_note-Fry-41) as well.[[26]](#cite_note-Crouching_Jedi_Hidden_Sith-26) Khofar's forest perimeter was shot at Pico do Arieiro and digitally inserted to shots of the Fanal Forest and Poço das Casas. The interior of the jungles was filmed at Shinfield Studios, with practical "mega trees" occupying the soundstage and even some of its stage doors. A short scene of Ueda was shot at Ponta de São Lourenço for its "brown, dusty Western" aesthetic, and the establishing shot of Olega was shot at Pedra D'Eira. Olega's city scenes were shot in Shinfield Studios. The exterior island shots of[[37]](#cite_note-LOL-37) Bal'demnic[[41]](#cite_note-Fry-41) were captured along the northern coastline of western Madeira, and the mouth of Stranger's cave is located at Ribeira de Janela. "Fifteen minutes down the road," the crew found the Porto Moniz Rockpools and shot the Stranger's bathing scene there.[[37]](#cite_note-LOL-37)

#### Post-production in London, Canada, India, Austria, Australia, and California[]

:   "*The musical themes in* The Acolyte *are tied more to an emotion or a point of view or a literary theme than they are to a particular character.*"
:   ―Composer [Michael Abels](/wiki/Michael_Abels "Michael Abels")[[43]](#cite_note-Abels_SW.com-43)

The series was edited by [Miikka Leskinen](/wiki/Miikka_Leskinen "Miikka Leskinen") and [Cheryl Potter](/wiki/Cheryl_Potter "Cheryl Potter") with [Avid](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Avid_Technology "wikipedia:Avid Technology") editing equipment supplied by HireWorks. Sound services were provided by [Skywalker Sound](/wiki/Skywalker_Sound "Skywalker Sound") in [Marin County, California](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Marin_County,_California "wikipedia:Marin County, California"), with Brian Chumney as the supervising sound editor.[[9]](#cite_note-end_credits-9) Visual effects post-production was led by [Industrial Light & Magic](/wiki/Industrial_Light_%26_Magic "Industrial Light & Magic") (ILM), a Lucasfilm subsidiary, with its studios in [London](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/London "wikipedia:London"), England, and [Vancouver](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Vancouver "wikipedia:Vancouver"), [Canada](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Canada "wikipedia:Canada"), extending through to the late end of winter in 2024. ILM's new office in [Mumbai](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mumbai "wikipedia:Mumbai"), [India](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/India "wikipedia:India"), also contributed to the nearly 3000 shots made for *The Acolyte*, marking one of its first completed works.[[36]](#cite_note-Foddy_LF-36) Additional visual effects were made by [Yannix](/wiki/Yannix "Yannix") and SDFX Studios,[[9]](#cite_note-end_credits-9) as well as the London-based Outpost Visual Effects and the Vancouver-based beloFX.[[36]](#cite_note-Foddy_LF-36) Visual effects were also partly produced in [Melbourne](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Melbourne "wikipedia:Melbourne"), [Australia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Australia "wikipedia:Australia"),[[9]](#cite_note-end_credits-9) with Rising Sun Pictures of nearby [Adelaide](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Adelaide "wikipedia:Adelaide") and the [Los Angeles](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Los_Angeles "wikipedia:Los Angeles")–based Luma Pictures having contributed too,[[36]](#cite_note-Foddy_LF-36) as did Hybride of [Ubisoft](/wiki/Ubisoft "Ubisoft")[[9]](#cite_note-end_credits-9) in [Montreal](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Montreal "wikipedia:Montreal"), Canada.[[36]](#cite_note-Foddy_LF-36) Proof Inc. additionally provided visualization services.[[9]](#cite_note-end_credits-9)

Unlike previous productions centered at ILM's headquarters in [San Francisco](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/San_Francisco "wikipedia:San Francisco"), California, ILM London served as the visual effects post-production hub for *The Acolyte*, led by production supervisor Julian Foddy. The visual effects crew worked closely with the creatures' department led by [Neal Scanlan](/wiki/Neal_Scanlan "Neal Scanlan") and editors Leskinen and Potter for the hybrid practical effects and computer graphics shots of the [umbramoths](/wiki/Umbramoth "Umbramoth").[[35]](#cite_note-Foddy_SW.com-35)

The score of *The Acolyte* was composed by Michael Abels with additional music by Miguel Bezanilla, [Cameron Moody](/wiki/Cameron_Moody "Cameron Moody"), and Robert Wallace. The score was recorded at [Synchron Stage Vienna](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Synchron_Stage_Vienna "wikipedia:Synchron Stage Vienna") in [Austria](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Austria "wikipedia:Austria") under the conductor Gottfried Rabl.[[9]](#cite_note-end_credits-9) Abels pointed out that the musical themes were emotion-based rather than character-based, taking the example of the "duality of the characters" Mae and Osha when they are vengeful or hopeful, with the same musical motifs occurring with different characters. Abels added that "You can tell I've had a lot of fun playing with how the Force is used and who uses it and what we think about whether that use of the Force is good or bad." While the score does not foreshadow or connect the music associated with Qimir with the Stranger, when the Stranger is revealed at the end of "Day," the music includes a nod to [Kylo Ren's theme](/wiki/Ben_Solo%27s_theme "Ben Solo's theme"). In keeping with the cinematic echo with *Rashomon*, the two flashback episodes include scenes in which the visuals are the same yet the music is different to accentuate different emotional perspectives.[[43]](#cite_note-Abels_SW.com-43)

#### Production finance[]

:   "The Acolyte *had many strong reviews and did well in the ratings its first week out, but you ultimately opted against a second season. Why didn't you move forward? And can you offer any hint as to how* Skeleton Crew *is doing, or its future?*"  
    "*So as it relates to* Acolyte*, we were happy with our performance, but it wasn't where we needed it to be given the cost structure of that title, quite frankly, to go and make a season two. So that's the reason why we didn't do that.* Skeleton Crew *is in process now, so we'll see. We've seen some growth on that. We’ll see how that goes. As you said, the reviews have been excellent on* Skeleton Crew*, so we'll have to see how that all plays out as it moves forward.*"
:   ―Interviewer Josef Adalian for *Vulture* and Alan Bergman, co-chair of Disney Entertainment[[44]](#cite_note-Vulture-44)

*The Acolyte* was produced with the support of the British Film Commission and the UK Government's High-End Television Tax Relief, the Canadian Film or Video Production Services Tax Credit, the [Province of British Columbia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/British_Columbia "wikipedia:British Columbia") Production Service Tax Credit, [Québec's](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Quebec "wikipedia:Quebec") Production Services Tax Credit, Portugal's Tourism and Cinema Fund, and the Australian Government's PDV Offset tax rebate. Visual effects production in Australia was also supported by [VicScreen](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/VicScreen "wikipedia:VicScreen") in [Victoria](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Victoria_(state) "wikipedia:Victoria (state)") and in [South Australia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/South_Australia "wikipedia:South Australia") with the support of the [South Australian Film Corporation](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/South_Australian_Film_Corporation "wikipedia:South Australian Film Corporation").[[9]](#cite_note-end_credits-9)

While media outlets reported that the series' production cost was around $180 million in [US dollars](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/United_States_dollar "wikipedia:United States dollar"),[[13]](#cite_note-NYT_May-13) Lucasfilm had registered a subsidiary high-end television program production company in [England and Wales](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/England_and_Wales "wikipedia:England and Wales") named Blue Stockings (UK). The company's publicly filed audit covering [March 24](/wiki/March_24 "March 24"), 2021 to [September 24](/wiki/September_24 "September 24") 2023, reports that "the cost was forecasted to be over the production budget" but are approved and fully funded by Lucasfilm as of September 24, 2024, with the production having been certified by the [British Film Institute](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/British_Film_Institute "wikipedia:British Film Institute") in the interim as "British,"[[45]](#cite_note-Audit-45) which would qualify the production for [HM Revenue and Customs](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/HM_Revenue_and_Customs "wikipedia:HM Revenue and Customs") [tax credits](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tax_credit "wikipedia:Tax credit")[[46]](#cite_note-Tax_reliefs-46) for the ultimate parent company, The Walt Disney Company. The report further indicates that UK television production expenditure between those dates totalled £172,320,864 in [British pound sterling](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pound_sterling "wikipedia:Pound sterling"), equivalent to around $230 million US dollars. Based on the UK tax relief scheme, the same filing claims a total of £34,839,012 pounds of expenditure credits for the relevant periods—which would bring Blue Stockings' production cost down to £137,481,852 pounds, equivalent to just under $184 million US dollars.[[45]](#cite_note-Audit-45)

[*Deadline*](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Deadline_Hollywood "wikipedia:Deadline Hollywood") reported that Karyn McCarthy filed a lawsuit against Lucasfilm and its wholly owned subsidiary Blue Stockings (US) in March 2023 in California, alleging a breach of contract and other legal injuries over the role of executive producer for *The Acolyte* in early 2022.[[19]](#cite_note-McCarthy_case-19)

In 2024, Lucasfilm released the series' teaser trailer online on [March 18](/wiki/March_18 "March 18"),[[6]](#cite_note-AcolyteAnnouncement-6) prior to releasing the final trailer on May 4 online[[47]](#cite_note-Final_trailer-47) and in theaters.[[13]](#cite_note-NYT_May-13) Later on the [20th](/wiki/May_20 "May 20") of that month, Lucasfilm Online released a video of Amandla Stenberg playing a new *Star Wars* violin arrangement of "[The Force Theme](/wiki/The_Force_Theme "The Force Theme")" that composer [John Williams](/wiki/John_Williams "John Williams") had written specifically for her,[[48]](#cite_note-Violin-48) with Stenberg proudly performing on her grandfather's violin.[[28]](#cite_note-Stenberg_GMA-28) Consisting of eight episodes, *The Acolyte*'s first two episodes premiered several days later on Disney+ on [June 4](/wiki/June_4 "June 4"), and the final episode was released on [July 16](/wiki/July_16 "July 16").[[13]](#cite_note-NYT_May-13)

Racist and sexist online harassment targeted *The Acolyte*'s cast members[[49]](#cite_note-Star_Wars_fans-49) and [review-bombed](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Review_bomb "wikipedia:Review bomb") the series on ranking websites.[[50]](#cite_note-Forbes_cancel_24-50) On [June 19](/wiki/June_19 "June 19"), the date of "[Juneteenth](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Juneteenth "wikipedia:Juneteenth")" commemorations of the [end of slavery](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/End_of_slavery_in_the_United_States "wikipedia:End of slavery in the United States") in the United States, Amandla Stenberg posted a music video on [Instagram](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Instagram "wikipedia:Instagram") addressing the tired bigotry of discourse around *The Acolyte*. She criticized the transformation of the term "[woke](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Woke "wikipedia:Woke")" for [culture war](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Culture_war "wikipedia:Culture war") and [clickbait](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Clickbait "wikipedia:Clickbait") purposes, including journalists who sacrifice "truth" for selective "metrics and data" that fuel division. Co-stars [Charlie Barnett](/wiki/Charlie_Barnett "Charlie Barnett"), Manny Jacinto, and [Dafne Keen](/wiki/Dafne_Keen "Dafne Keen") commented in support of Stenberg.[[51]](#cite_note-Stenberg_MV-51)

Amandla Stenberg performing the violin solo written for her by John Williams

 

On [August 19](/wiki/August_19 "August 19"), 2024, *Deadline* first reported that the show had been "canceled" and would not have a second season,[[52]](#cite_note-Deadline_cancel-52) and [*Gizmodo*](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gizmodo "wikipedia:Gizmodo") published an updated report that Lucasfilm confirmed to the media outlet that there would not be a second season of *The Acolyte* and that it "had never been officially greenlit."[[53]](#cite_note-Gizmodo_cancel-53) [Twitter](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Twitter "wikipedia:Twitter") owner [Elon Musk](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Elon_Musk "wikipedia:Elon Musk") later reacted to the news on the social media platform to suggest that the series is "woke."[[49]](#cite_note-Star_Wars_fans-49) *The Acolyte* writer Jen Richards criticized the Hollywood industry on Twitter on [August 20](/wiki/August_20 "August 20") for its aversion to "risk" and studio monopolies that only care about immediate financial profits for shareholders rather than art and culture. [John Boyega](/wiki/John_Boyega "John Boyega"), who had played [Finn](/wiki/Finn "Finn") in the [sequel trilogy](/wiki/Sequel_trilogy "Sequel trilogy") and faced racist abuse for his role, acknowledged Amandla Stenberg on an old Instagram post of hers on [August 21](/wiki/August_21 "August 21") amidst a flurry of hateful comments.[[54]](#cite_note-Forbes_cancel_23-54)

In an article titled "Can the Best of Star Wars Survive the Worst of Its Fans?" on [August 23](/wiki/August_23 "August 23"), the [*Rolling Stone*](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Rolling_Stone "wikipedia:Rolling Stone") reported that neither Lucasfilm nor Disney representatives have responded to their requests for comment on the series' discontinuation.[[49]](#cite_note-Star_Wars_fans-49) On the same day, [*Entertainment Weekly*](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Entertainment_Weekly "wikipedia:Entertainment Weekly") reported that Lee Jung-jae was "surprised" by the news and hoped that Disney would invite showrunner Leslye Headland to make a second season of *The Acolyte*,[[55]](#cite_note-EW_cancel-55) and via Instagram's story feature, Rebecca Henderson reposted both the aforementioned *Rolling Stone* article and a fan's tweet calling on "Disney" not to cater to a certain "part of the Star Wars fanbase."[[50]](#cite_note-Forbes_cancel_24-50) Manny Jacinto simply posted one of *The Acolyte*'s closing shots featuring himself as "the Stranger" and Amandla Stenberg as Osha Aniseya with a red heart emoji on Instagram.[[54]](#cite_note-Forbes_cancel_23-54) [Joonas Suotamo](/wiki/Joonas_Suotamo "Joonas Suotamo") likewise posted an image of himself as Kelnacca with a red heart emoji on Instagram on [August 24](/wiki/August_24 "August 24").[[56]](#cite_note-Suotamo-56)

### Cancellation aftermath[]

:   "*I'm going to be transparent and say it’s not a huge shock for me. There has been a rampage of vitriol that we have faced since the show was even announced, when it was still just a concept and no one had even seen it. That's when we started experiencing a rampage of, I would say, hyper-conservative bigotry and vitriol, prejudice, hatred and hateful language towards us.*"
:   ―Amandla Stenberg, on the cancellation of *The Acolyte*[[57]](#cite_note-Stenberg_statement-57)

On [August 28](/wiki/August_28 "August 28"), Stenberg spoke about *The Acolyte*'s cancelation via Instagram stories. The artist said it was "not a huge shock" for them given the "hyper-conservative bigotry and vitriol, prejudice, hatred, and hateful language" with which "the alt-right" targeted the series' creators such as herself—starting when the series was just announced in 2020 through the announcement of Stenberg's casting in 2022 to the series' release in 2024—though she also thanked "everybody who watched it" and appreciated the fans' support, artwork, and theories. They also spoke about how part of processing the vitriol meant being personally vocal in line with her own values despite working under Disney, and called her role in *Star Wars* "an incredible honor and dream" and gave high praise to Lucasfilm, the "vision" of producer Rayne Roberts, and showrunner Leslye Headland: "I will love this experience with her forever." As for the fans, Stenberg wanted to say that they are "deeply loved and appreciated, and it made this job all the worthwhile." Stenberg lastly expressed that "[we all exist in the context in all which we live](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/You_think_you_just_fell_out_of_a_coconut_tree%3F "wikipedia:You think you just fell out of a coconut tree?")," calling on people to vote in the [2024 United States elections](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2024_United_States_elections "wikipedia:2024 United States elections") and also referring to the "hyper divisiveness" of internet discourse around *Star Wars* that they found has been "driven by echo chambers of thought and algorithms that reinforce our biases," and she wanted to "challenge us all and challenge myself to continue questioning what it is that I digest and think critically about what shapes it."[[57]](#cite_note-Stenberg_statement-57)

 [![Nada Maktari's The Acolyte cover for Empire](data:image/gif;base64,R0lGODlhAQABAIABAAAAAP///yH5BAEAAAEALAAAAAABAAEAQAICTAEAOw%3D%3D)](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/starwars/images/a/a1/AcolyteEmpireMaktariCover.jpg/revision/latest?cb=20250417211628) [![Nada Maktari's The Acolyte cover for Empire](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/starwars/images/a/a1/AcolyteEmpireMaktariCover.jpg/revision/latest/scale-to-width-down/250?cb=20250417211628)](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/starwars/images/a/a1/AcolyteEmpireMaktariCover.jpg/revision/latest?cb=20250417211628) 

Nada Maktari's *The Acolyte* cover for *Empire*

 

On [August 29](/wiki/August_29 "August 29"), [Ahmed Best](/wiki/Ahmed_Best "Ahmed Best"), who had faced vicious abuse for his role as [Jar Jar Binks](/wiki/Jar_Jar_Binks "Jar Jar Binks") in the [prequel trilogy](/wiki/Prequel_trilogy "Prequel trilogy"), posted in solidarity with Amandla Stenberg on Instagram.[[58]](#cite_note-Best-58) On [October 2](/wiki/October_2 "October 2"), in an interview with [*Glamour*](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Glamour_(magazine) "wikipedia:Glamour (magazine)") magazine for its Women of the Year Awards, Jodie Turner-Smith praised Amandla Stenberg's dedication to her role and criticized the movie studios for being unsupportive of the actor even while racist abuse runs rampant online. Turner-Smith believed that making a statement against racist abuse would not incur damage to corporate revenue given that people of color, particularly Black people, "make up a very large percentage of buying power." She added that opinions and trends attacking people of color for taking part in intellectual properties started by white people, such as *Star Wars*, would eventually pass even though people such as herself "don't get to fail upwards like a lot of white men."[[18]](#cite_note-Turner-Smith_Glamour-18)

In an interview with [*Vanity Fair*](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Vanity_Fair_(magazine) "wikipedia:Vanity Fair (magazine)") later in November, Lee Jung-jae said that he felt "hurt" and was especially sorry for Leslye Headland by the racist reactions to *The Acolyte*, though he added that he has faith for racism to eventually end. Lee also expressed gratitude for the support of fans and had "strong hopes" for audiences to better appreciate the series as time goes by.[[59]](#cite_note-Vanity_Lee-59) On [December 19](/wiki/December_19 "December 19"), 2024, in an interview with Josef Adalian for the [*Vulture*](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Vulture_(website) "wikipedia:Vulture (website)") news website, Alan Bergman, co-chair of Disney Entertainment, acknowledged in reply to a question about both *The Acolyte* and the *[Star Wars: Skeleton Crew](/wiki/Star_Wars:_Skeleton_Crew "Star Wars: Skeleton Crew")* television series that premiered earlier that month that *The Acolyte* was not renewed for a second season. Despite both series receiving good reviews, Bergman blamed "the cost structure" of *The Acolyte* even though "we were happy with our performance."[[44]](#cite_note-Vulture-44)

### Possibility of a return?[]

 [![Manny Jacinto (pictured in character as "the Stranger") has expressed a great interest in seeing The Acolyte return.](data:image/gif;base64,R0lGODlhAQABAIABAAAAAP///yH5BAEAAAEALAAAAAABAAEAQAICTAEAOw%3D%3D)](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/starwars/images/a/ae/Stranger-SWapp2.png/revision/latest?cb=20251014231916) [![Manny Jacinto (pictured in character as "the Stranger") has expressed a great interest in seeing The Acolyte return.](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/starwars/images/a/ae/Stranger-SWapp2.png/revision/latest/scale-to-width-down/350?cb=20251014231916)](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/starwars/images/a/ae/Stranger-SWapp2.png/revision/latest?cb=20251014231916) 

Manny Jacinto (pictured in character as "the Stranger") has expressed a great interest in seeing *The Acolyte* return.

 

In January 2025, Luminate's end-of-year analysis for 2024 found that *The Acolyte* was viewed for 2.7 billion minutes on Disney+, ranking second only to the 3 billion of the television series [*Percy Jackson and the Olympians*](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Percy_Jackson_and_the_Olympians_(TV_series) "wikipedia:Percy Jackson and the Olympians (TV series)") on the streaming platform.[[60]](#cite_note-Viewership-60) Jecki Lon actress [Dafne Keen](/wiki/Dafne_Keen "Dafne Keen") admitted to [*Screen Rant*](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Screen_Rant "wikipedia:Screen Rant") that the cancellation had been, at least as she saw things, an "overwhelming" matter for all involved. However, Keen also stated she was proud of her work on the series and proud of how Stenberg had handled the cancellation.[[61]](#cite_note-Dafne_Reception-61) Senator [Worus Rayencourt](/wiki/Worus_Rayencourt "Worus Rayencourt") actor [David Harewood](/wiki/David_Harewood "David Harewood") expressed a desire to return to the series in [July](/wiki/July "July") [2025](/wiki/2025 "2025") via his Bluesky account and asked fans to continue pushing for the show's return if they wanted another season.[[62]](#cite_note-Harewood_Reception-62)

The Stranger actor Manny Jacinto has also been vocal about his hope to return to *The Acolyte*. In a late 2024 panel, upon being asked what one of his life goals was, Jacinto stated that he wanted there to be a second season of *The Acolyte* and hoped to appear within it.[[63]](#cite_note-JacintoReturn24-63) In [August](/wiki/August "August") 2025, Jacinto stated that he would love to return to the character of the Stranger, remarking that, "if the fans want it, I'm down and ready to go" while also praising the team behind *The Acolyte* and the greater *Star Wars* franchise.[[64]](#cite_note-JacintoAugust25-64)

## Adaptations and tie-in media[]

 [![The Acolyte: Wayseeker, a 2025 novel by Justina Ireland](data:image/gif;base64,R0lGODlhAQABAIABAAAAAP///yH5BAEAAAEALAAAAAABAAEAQAICTAEAOw%3D%3D)](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/starwars/images/2/28/TheAcolyte-Wayseeker-cover3.jpg/revision/latest?cb=20241114210410) [![The Acolyte: Wayseeker, a 2025 novel by Justina Ireland](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/starwars/images/2/28/TheAcolyte-Wayseeker-cover3.jpg/revision/latest/scale-to-width-down/250?cb=20241114210410)](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/starwars/images/2/28/TheAcolyte-Wayseeker-cover3.jpg/revision/latest?cb=20241114210410) 

*The Acolyte: Wayseeker*, a 2025 novel by Justina Ireland

 

At San Diego Comic Con in July 2024, it was revealed that two tie-in novels of *The Acolyte* would be published in 2025—Justina Ireland's *[The Acolyte: Wayseeker](/wiki/The_Acolyte:_Wayseeker "The Acolyte: Wayseeker")* focusing on Masters Indara and Vernestra Rwoh, and [Tessa Gratton](/wiki/Tessa_Gratton "Tessa Gratton")'s *[The Acolyte: The Crystal Crown](/wiki/The_Acolyte:_The_Crystal_Crown "The Acolyte: The Crystal Crown")* focusing on Padawans Jecki Lon and Yord Fondar.[[65]](#cite_note-Acolyte_novels-65) While initially scheduled for [January 7](/wiki/January_7 "January 7"), 2025,[[66]](#cite_note-EdelweissOld-66) and later delayed, a [visual guide](/wiki/Star_Wars:_The_Acolyte:_The_Visual_Guide "Star Wars: The Acolyte: The Visual Guide") by [Pablo Hidalgo](/wiki/Pablo_Hidalgo "Pablo Hidalgo") for the series was published on [June 17](/wiki/June_17 "June 17"), 2025, by [Dorling Kindersley](/wiki/Dorling_Kindersley "Dorling Kindersley").[[67]](#cite_note-Edelweiss-67) Hidalgo was working on the guide as late as [December](/wiki/December "December") 2024, revealing for those interested in the publishing timeline that his last contribution to the book was [December 22](/wiki/December_22 "December 22") of that year at 10:48 AM.[[68]](#cite_note-PabloDecember-68)

Novels and comics of *The High Republic* have been marketed in relation to the series, with text such as "a century before *The Acolyte*" featuring on their covers.[[69]](#cite_note-Wedding-69) [BioWare](/wiki/BioWare "BioWare")'s [video game](/wiki/Star_Wars_video_games "Star Wars video games") *[Star Wars: The Old Republic](/wiki/Star_Wars:_The_Old_Republic "Star Wars: The Old Republic")*, which has been running since [2011](/wiki/2011 "2011") as part of the [*Star Wars* Legends](/wiki/Star_Wars_Legends "Star Wars Legends") continuity, was updated with player armor in the style of "The Stranger" and an appearance option in the style of Osha and Mae Aniseya[[70]](#cite_note-TOR_gear-70) in collaboration with [Lucasfilm Games](/wiki/Lucasfilm_Games "Lucasfilm Games").[[71]](#cite_note-TOR_letter-71) Following *The Acolyte*'s season finale, the official *Star Wars* books Twitter page urged fans interested in the High Republic Era thanks to the series to check out the time period's multi-media project, beginning with author [Charles Soule](/wiki/Charles_Soule "Charles Soule")'s novel *[The High Republic: Light of the Jedi](/wiki/The_High_Republic:_Light_of_the_Jedi "The High Republic: Light of the Jedi")*.[[72]](#cite_note-Book_Tweet-72)
