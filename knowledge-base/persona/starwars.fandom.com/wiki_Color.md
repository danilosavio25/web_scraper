The Felucian landscape was full of bright colors.

 

The **color** of an object was its property of producing different sensations on the [eye](/wiki/Eye "Eye") as a result of the way it reflected light. The [planet](/wiki/Planet "Planet") of [Felucia](/wiki/Felucia "Felucia") hosted many translucent life-forms that colored the sunlight as its beams penetrated their skin.[[2]](#cite_note-SWEFelu-2) Blue, green,[[3]](#cite_note-SWELightsaberCry-3) red,[[4]](#cite_note-SWELightsaber-4) and yellow were examples of colors.[[5]](#cite_note-SWEGuar-5)

 [![Ahsoka Tano's lightsabers were white, due to her purifying a pair of red kyber crystals that were "bled](data:image/gif;base64,R0lGODlhAQABAIABAAAAAP///yH5BAEAAAEALAAAAAABAAEAQAICTAEAOw%3D%3D)](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/starwars/images/3/38/AhsokaTanoJarKai-CEUEEd.png/revision/latest?cb=20220202130848) [![Ahsoka Tano's lightsabers were white, due to her purifying a pair of red kyber crystals that were "bled](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/starwars/images/3/38/AhsokaTanoJarKai-CEUEEd.png/revision/latest/scale-to-width-down/220?cb=20220202130848)](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/starwars/images/3/38/AhsokaTanoJarKai-CEUEEd.png/revision/latest?cb=20220202130848) 

Ahsoka Tano's lightsabers were white, due to her purifying a pair of red kyber crystals that were "bled."

 

The color of a [Jedi](/wiki/Jedi "Jedi") [lightsaber](/wiki/Lightsaber "Lightsaber")'s blade was defined by its [kyber crystal](/wiki/Kyber_crystal "Kyber crystal"), which was harvested from the [Crystal Cave](/wiki/Crystal_Cave "Crystal Cave") on [Ilum](/wiki/Ilum "Ilum").[[3]](#cite_note-SWELightsaberCry-3) A lightsaber crystal was colorless until it was found by a [Jedi youngling](/wiki/Jedi_Initiate "Jedi Initiate"), where the youngling would then become attuned to the crystal and the crystal would display its color, the color being determined by the youngling's "spiritual alignment."[[7]](#cite_note-The_Gathering_TG-7)[[8]](#cite_note-Ahsoka-8) A Jedi's lightsaber blade was usually green or blue, but other colors were possible, such as [Mace Windu](/wiki/Mace_Windu "Mace Windu")'s [purple-bladed lightsaber](/wiki/Mace_Windu%27s_lightsaber "Mace Windu's lightsaber").[[9]](#cite_note-Databank_Lightsaber-9)[[10]](#cite_note-Ultimate_Star_Wars-10) The [Sith](/wiki/Sith "Sith") often wielded red lightsabers due to a process known as [bleeding](/wiki/Bleeding "Bleeding"), where the user used the [dark side of the Force](/wiki/Dark_side_of_the_Force "Dark side of the Force") to bend a [kyber crystal](/wiki/Kyber_crystal "Kyber crystal") to their will. Additionally Jedi can use the Force to [heal](/wiki/Purification "Purification") them of the dark side presence, causing them to turn white.[[8]](#cite_note-Ahsoka-8)

Specialized [B1-series battle droids](/wiki/B1-series_battle_droid "B1-series battle droid") were distinguished by colored markings on their [armor](/wiki/Armor "Armor"). Blue denoted [pilot droids](/wiki/Pilot_droid "Pilot droid"), red denoted [security droids](/wiki/Security_droid "Security droid"), and yellow denoted command droids.[[11]](#cite_note-Ultimate_Star_Wars_New_Edition-11)

## In culture[]

:   "*Dressed in black. In the armor of the Empire.*"
:   ―[Sabé](/wiki/Sab%C3%A9 "Sabé") after being promoted to [lieutenant commander](/wiki/Lieutenant_Commander "Lieutenant Commander") on [Gabredor III](/wiki/Gabredor_III "Gabredor III")[[12]](#cite_note-DV24-12)

 [![Sheev Palpatine, Supreme Chancellor of the Galactic Republic, favored the color red.](data:image/gif;base64,R0lGODlhAQABAIABAAAAAP///yH5BAEAAAEALAAAAAABAAEAQAICTAEAOw%3D%3D)](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/starwars/images/2/22/Sith_to_be.png/revision/latest?cb=20230517194529) [![Sheev Palpatine, Supreme Chancellor of the Galactic Republic, favored the color red.](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/starwars/images/2/22/Sith_to_be.png/revision/latest/scale-to-width-down/250?cb=20230517194529)](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/starwars/images/2/22/Sith_to_be.png/revision/latest?cb=20230517194529) 

Sheev Palpatine, Supreme Chancellor of the Galactic Republic, favored the color red.

 

In [the galaxy](/wiki/The_galaxy "The galaxy"), colors were often culturally significant. For example, the color red was associated with diplomatic immunity under the [Galactic Republic](/wiki/Galactic_Republic "Galactic Republic"),[[13]](#cite_note-SWJ1-13) warning others not to attack. As the color of ambassadorial relations and neutrality under the Republic, a completely red color scheme such as that of the [*Consular*-class space cruiser](/wiki/Consular-class_light_cruiser "Consular-class light cruiser") *[Radiant VII](/wiki/Radiant_VII "Radiant VII")* indicated a ship came from the [capital](/wiki/Capital "Capital") world of [Coruscant](/wiki/Coruscant "Coruscant"). As the personal favorite color of [Supreme Chancellor](/wiki/Supreme_Chancellor "Supreme Chancellor") [Sheev Palpatine](/wiki/Darth_Sidious "Darth Sidious"), red was co-opted during his chancellery and assigned across Republic vessels at his direction.[[14]](#cite_note-CVNE-14) Dark red was also the main color scheme on Republic ships and vehicles during the Clone Wars, whereas the Separatist ships and vehicles largely used dark blue.[[15]](#cite_note-ROTS-15) However, the color red was sometimes regarded as intense and aggressive. For that reason, the Sith wielded red lightsaber blades that represented their destructive view of [the Force](/wiki/The_Force "The Force").[[9]](#cite_note-Databank_Lightsaber-9) Nevertheless, the members of many Force-worshipping sects native to [Jedha](/wiki/Jedha "Jedha") donned scarlet vestments.[[16]](#cite_note-ROUVG-16)

 [![Clone troopers often painted their armor in accordance with the unit to which they were assigned.](data:image/gif;base64,R0lGODlhAQABAIABAAAAAP///yH5BAEAAAEALAAAAAABAAEAQAICTAEAOw%3D%3D)](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/starwars/images/2/20/JesseKixAnaxes-TBB.png/revision/latest?cb=20200228004613) [![Clone troopers often painted their armor in accordance with the unit to which they were assigned.](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/starwars/images/2/20/JesseKixAnaxes-TBB.png/revision/latest/scale-to-width-down/250?cb=20200228004613)](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/starwars/images/2/20/JesseKixAnaxes-TBB.png/revision/latest?cb=20200228004613) 

Clone troopers often painted their armor in accordance with the unit to which they were assigned.

 

Likewise, the white color of [clone trooper armor](/wiki/Clone_trooper_armor "Clone trooper armor") signified an honorable symbol of defense before becoming skewed into one of a faceless icon of conformity under the [Galactic Empire](/wiki/Galactic_Empire "Galactic Empire").[[17]](#cite_note-TFAVD-17) During the [Clone Wars](/wiki/Clone_Wars "Clone Wars"), the [clone troopers](/wiki/Clone_trooper "Clone trooper") were referred to as "the boys in white."[[18]](#cite_note-Propaganda-18) In the days of the Old Republic, silver was used as a status symbol for royalty, as residents of [Naboo](/wiki/Naboo "Naboo") used [chromium](/wiki/Chromium "Chromium") to cover their [starfighters](/wiki/Starfighter "Starfighter") and [cruisers](/wiki/Cruiser "Cruiser").[[19]](#cite_note-SOTG-19)

All [languages](/wiki/Language "Language") combined, there existed forty billion, eleven million, seven hundred forty-two thousand, nine hundred and eighty-three accepted synonyms for the color blue. In [Galactic Basic Standard](/wiki/Galactic_Basic_Standard "Galactic Basic Standard"), known shades of blue included ao, aqua, azure,[[20]](#cite_note-Dark_Disciple-20) and royal blue.[[21]](#cite_note-Hope_&_Courage-21)

The Galactic Empire favored muted colors of gray, white and black and tried to redesign ships, uniforms, and other government property in this color scheme wherever possible. [Sabine Wren](/wiki/Sabine_Wren "Sabine Wren") was not in favor of this practice and used [graffiti](/wiki/Graffiti "Graffiti") to provide bursts of brighter color.[[1]](#cite_note-newhero-1) In the [Imperial Military](/wiki/Imperial_Military "Imperial Military"), the wearing of white tunics was generally reserved for either [Grand Admirals](/wiki/Grand_Admiral "Grand Admiral") or members of the [Imperial Security Bureau](/wiki/Imperial_Security_Bureau "Imperial Security Bureau").[[22]](#cite_note-Complete_Visual_New-22) Imperial armor was often black,[[12]](#cite_note-DV24-12) as seen on people like [Darth Vader](/wiki/Anakin_Skywalker "Anakin Skywalker"), [TIE pilots](/wiki/TIE_pilot "TIE pilot"),[[23]](#cite_note-ANH-23) members of the [Inquisitorius](/wiki/Inquisitorius "Inquisitorius")[[24]](#cite_note-Kenobi-24) and [Moff](/wiki/Moff "Moff") [Gideon](/wiki/Gideon "Gideon").[[25]](#cite_note-Mando-25)

The [flight suits](/wiki/Flight_suit "Flight suit") worn by the [starfighter pilots](/wiki/Resistance_pilot "Resistance pilot") of the [Resistance](/wiki/Resistance "Resistance") were dyed in a hue known as "interstellar orange."[[17]](#cite_note-TFAVD-17) Each group was also designated by various hues, such as [Red](/wiki/Red_Squadron_(Resistance) "Red Squadron (Resistance)"),[[6]](#cite_note-Episode_7-6) [Green](/wiki/Green_Squadron_(Resistance) "Green Squadron (Resistance)"),[[26]](#cite_note-Episode_8-26) and [Blue](/wiki/Blue_Squadron_(Resistance) "Blue Squadron (Resistance)") Squadrons,[[6]](#cite_note-Episode_7-6) along with their leaders.[[6]](#cite_note-Episode_7-6)[[26]](#cite_note-Episode_8-26)

Podracers were painted in various colors.

 

On [Tatooine](/wiki/Tatooine "Tatooine"), where the sport of [podracing](/wiki/Podracing "Podracing") was common, [racers](/wiki/Podracer_(profession) "Podracer (profession)") wielded various colors on both their [vehicles](/wiki/Podracer "Podracer") and flags.[[27]](#cite_note-Episode_I-27)

In [Tarisian](/wiki/Taris "Taris") culture, the color purple was considered the "color of administration."[[17]](#cite_note-TFAVD-17)

[Caf](/wiki/Caf "Caf") without any [cream](/wiki/Cream "Cream") or [sugar](/wiki/Sugar "Sugar") added was referred to as "black"; [Jedi Master](/wiki/Jedi_Master "Jedi Master") [Obi-Wan Kenobi](/wiki/Obi-Wan_Kenobi "Obi-Wan Kenobi") was known to prefer black caf.[[28]](#cite_note-Brotherhood-28) One form of caf was described as "black and sweet".[[29]](#cite_note-STET!-29)

## In clothing[]

:   "*For what it's worth, you actually do look pretty good in black."  
    "*Huh. *You think so?*"
:   ―A fellow rebel and Luke Skywalker[[30]](#cite_note-Star_Wars_28-30)

When it came to [clothing](/wiki/Clothing "Clothing"), individuals had different preferences when it came to color. For example, when [Luke Skywalker](/wiki/Luke_Skywalker "Luke Skywalker") put on an [Imperial officer](/wiki/Imperial_officer "Imperial officer")'s uniform while on a [mission to Coruscant](/wiki/Mission_to_Coruscant "Mission to Coruscant"), one of his fellow rebels commented that he looked good in black.[[30]](#cite_note-Star_Wars_28-30) After this, Skywalker began wearing black clothing.[[31]](#cite_note-Star_Wars_29-31)

## In biology[]

Not all species saw color the same way; for instance, the [Kaminoans](/wiki/Kaminoan "Kaminoan") lived in dwellings that appeared to be completely white; in reality, the buildings were simply colored with ultraviolet colors invisible to [humans](/wiki/Human "Human").[[32]](#cite_note-AEYNTK-32) Colors that the [Evereni](/wiki/Evereni "Evereni") [Marda Ro](/wiki/Marda_Ro "Marda Ro") had never seen before and that she could not describe existed within [the Veil](/wiki/The_Veil_(Planet_X) "The Veil (Planet X)").[[33]](#cite_note-Path_Ven-33)

## In genetics[]

A human [parent](/wiki/Parent "Parent") could pass on their [eye](/wiki/Eye "Eye") color to their [offspring](/wiki/Reproduction "Reproduction"). [Ben Solo](/wiki/Ben_Solo "Ben Solo") had the same piercing brown eyes as his mother [Leia Organa](/wiki/Leia_Skywalker_Organa_Solo "Leia Skywalker Organa Solo"), who was sure that this feature was inherited from her own birth mother, [Padmé Amidala](/wiki/Padm%C3%A9_Amidala "Padmé Amidala").[[34]](#cite_note-Family_at_War-34)

## Rank[]

Traditionally, the color of the armor on a clone trooper denoted ranks such as [sergeants](/wiki/Clone_Sergeant "Clone Sergeant") and [lieutenants](/wiki/Clone_Lieutenant "Clone Lieutenant"). However, this practice was rescinded as the war progressed, instead being used to signify belonging to a certain division or unit.[[35]](#cite_note-Clone_Trooper_Armor-35)
