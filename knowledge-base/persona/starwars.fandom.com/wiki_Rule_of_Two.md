The **Rule of Two** was a decree for the [Sith](/wiki/Sith "Sith") established by [Sith Lord](/wiki/Sith_Lord "Sith Lord") [Darth Bane](/wiki/Darth_Bane "Darth Bane") so they could operate in secret and eventually get revenge on the [Jedi Order](/wiki/Jedi_Order "Jedi Order") for their near extinction in the [Jedi-Sith War](/wiki/Jedi-Sith_War "Jedi-Sith War"). It was the successor of the [Doctrine of the Dyad](/wiki/Doctrine_of_the_Dyad "Doctrine of the Dyad"), a concept centered on the [Force dyad](/wiki/Force_dyad "Force dyad"), which was a pairing of two [Force-sensitive](/wiki/Force-sensitive "Force-sensitive") beings linked by a unique [Force-bond](/wiki/Force-bond "Force-bond") that was unbreakable. The decree commanded that only two Sith [Lords](/wiki/Lord "Lord") must exist at any given [time](/wiki/Time "Time"): a [master](/wiki/Sith_Master "Sith Master") to represent the power of the [dark side of the Force](/wiki/Dark_side_of_the_Force "Dark side of the Force"), and an [apprentice](/wiki/Sith_apprentice "Sith apprentice") to crave it and train under the master and to one [day](/wiki/Standard_day "Standard day") [fulfill](/wiki/Grand_Plan "Grand Plan") their role. This governed the [Lords of the Sith](/wiki/Dark_Lord_of_the_Sith "Dark Lord of the Sith").

## History[]

### Prelude[]

:   "*There are few secrets of the Force I have yet to master. But our Order's ancient prophecies speak of one fabled manifestation unseen for generations—a dyad. This pairing of two beings strong in the Force, linked together by an unbreakable bond, creates a power as strong as life itself.*"
:   ―Darth Sidious[[4]](#cite_note-SotS-4)

 [![The Doctrine of the Dyad revolved around an unbreakable bond between two beings who were strong with the Force.](data:image/gif;base64,R0lGODlhAQABAIABAAAAAP///yH5BAEAAAEALAAAAAABAAEAQAICTAEAOw%3D%3D)](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/starwars/images/6/6d/Dyad.jpg/revision/latest?cb=20211012225649) [![The Doctrine of the Dyad revolved around an unbreakable bond between two beings who were strong with the Force.](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/starwars/images/6/6d/Dyad.jpg/revision/latest/scale-to-width-down/250?cb=20211012225649)](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/starwars/images/6/6d/Dyad.jpg/revision/latest?cb=20211012225649) 

The Doctrine of the Dyad revolved around an unbreakable bond between two beings who were strong with the Force.

 

The Rule of Two was preceded by the [Doctrine of the Dyad](/wiki/Doctrine_of_the_Dyad "Doctrine of the Dyad"), which was a concept centered on two [Force-sensitive](/wiki/Force-sensitive "Force-sensitive") beings who had a unique [Force-bond](/wiki/Force-bond "Force-bond")—that was unbreakable—which made them one in the Force called a [Force dyad](/wiki/Force_dyad "Force dyad"). Ancient legends referred to the dyad as the source of unique [Force powers](/wiki/Force_power "Force power"), such as [Force healing](/wiki/Force_healing "Force healing"), and the ability to transfer objects through [the Force](/wiki/The_Force "The Force"). The Doctrine of the Dyad was inscribed on the walls of the [Sith Citadel](/wiki/Sith_Citadel "Sith Citadel"),[[4]](#cite_note-SotS-4) an ancient [Sith](/wiki/Sith "Sith") stronghold located on the [planet](/wiki/Planet "Planet") [Exegol](/wiki/Exegol "Exegol"),[[5]](#cite_note-TROS_VD-5) to remind the Sith of the dyad's significance in their quest to attain ultimate power. The [Sith Eternal](/wiki/Sith_Eternal "Sith Eternal"), a [cult](/wiki/Sith_cult "Sith cult") of [Sith loyalists](/wiki/Sith_cultist "Sith cultist"), revered the Doctrine of the Dyad, believing that the dyad symbolized the future of the Sith and was the key to achieving the full potential of the [dark side of the Force](/wiki/Dark_side_of_the_Force "Dark side of the Force").[[4]](#cite_note-SotS-4)

The concept of the Rule of Two, the relationship between a [Sith Master](/wiki/Sith_Master "Sith Master") and a [Sith apprentice](/wiki/Sith_apprentice "Sith apprentice"), was part of ancient Sith ideology since the time of the [Great Scourge of Malachor](/wiki/Great_Scourge_of_Malachor "Great Scourge of Malachor").[[6]](#cite_note-TotA-6) The masters demanded loyalty from their apprentices, but they sometimes betrayed them if they found one with more potential.[[7]](#cite_note-TVE-7) In ancient times, the Sith were often ruled by two Sith while many others existed at the same time under them.[[8]](#cite_note-TROS_JN-8)

### Creation[]

:   "*A millennium ago, the Sith and Jedi were nearly equal in number. But unchecked ambition and infighting turned our Order against itself. Weakened by their own hand, the Sith were nearly wiped from existence by the opportunistic Jedi. But there was a Sith Lord who survived the culling—the great Darth Bane. It was he who reshaped the Sith, creating a new hierarchy to ensure the survival of our Order.*"
:   ―Darth Sidious[[4]](#cite_note-SotS-4)

 [![The Rule of Two was created by Darth Bane, a Sith Lord who survived the destruction of the ancient Sith Order.](data:image/gif;base64,R0lGODlhAQABAIABAAAAAP///yH5BAEAAAEALAAAAAABAAEAQAICTAEAOw%3D%3D)](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/starwars/images/0/05/DarthBane-TheDarkSide.jpg/revision/latest?cb=20240705093659) [![The Rule of Two was created by Darth Bane, a Sith Lord who survived the destruction of the ancient Sith Order.](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/starwars/images/0/05/DarthBane-TheDarkSide.jpg/revision/latest/scale-to-width-down/250?cb=20240705093659)](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/starwars/images/0/05/DarthBane-TheDarkSide.jpg/revision/latest?cb=20240705093659) 

The Rule of Two was created by Darth Bane, a Sith Lord who survived the destruction of the ancient Sith Order.

 

The [Sith](/wiki/Sith "Sith") were the ancient enemies of the [Jedi](/wiki/Jedi "Jedi") for [millennia](/wiki/Millennium "Millennium").[[9]](#cite_note-Helmet-9) Eventually, the Sith's greed for power caused them to turn on themselves,[[1]](#cite_note-Sacrifice-1) which allowed the Jedi to defeat and decimate the weakened Sith a millennium before the [Clone Wars](/wiki/Clone_Wars "Clone Wars").[[10]](#cite_note-USW-10) [Darth Bane](/wiki/Darth_Bane "Darth Bane") emerged from the conflict as the sole surviving Sith [Lord](/wiki/Lord "Lord"). With the Sith in a weakened state,[[1]](#cite_note-Sacrifice-1) the Jedi Order[[11]](#cite_note-SWP-11) [triumphed over the Sith](/wiki/Defeat_of_the_Sith "Defeat of the Sith") on [Ruusan](/wiki/Ruusan "Ruusan").[[9]](#cite_note-Helmet-9) Realizing that the Sith's greed led to their destruction, Bane created the Rule of Two.[[1]](#cite_note-Sacrifice-1) The Sith thenceforth consisted of only two Sith Lords at any given time; the master who embodied the power of the dark side, and the apprentice who craved that power. Bane also created the [Grand Plan](/wiki/Grand_Plan "Grand Plan"), which was a revenge plot against the Jedi. The generations that came after Bane were able to operate in secret for [centuries](/wiki/Standard_century "Standard century"), contributing to Bane's plot that would see the Jedi's destruction and the restoration of Sith rule throughout [the galaxy](/wiki/The_galaxy "The galaxy").[[4]](#cite_note-SotS-4)

The Rule of Two as a philosophy was influenced by Bane's personal experiences as well as the study of ancient [Sith documents](/wiki/Sith_artifact "Sith artifact").[[12]](#cite_note-The_Dark_Side-12) Under the Rule of Two, a Sith apprentice had to kill the Sith Master and take on an apprentice, in order to become the master themselves, which ensured that the Sith grew more powerful and cunning with each generation.[[13]](#cite_note-TSWB-13) On the other hand, the master could train another apprentice, and have them replace and kill their outgoing apprentice.[[14]](#cite_note-Separatist_Rise-14) As a result, the relationship between the master and the apprentice lacked trust, as both constantly searched for signs of weakness in the other.[[15]](#cite_note-Darth_Vader:_Sith_Lord-15)

### Bane and Zannah[]

:   "*Lord Bane's own apprentice struck him down to claim Bane's place. And so began a magnificently vicious cycle. Many Sith apprentices—myself included—have supplanted their masters in order to ascend.*"
:   ―Darth Sidious[[4]](#cite_note-SotS-4)

 [![Bane's death was the start of a tradition in which Sith apprentices completed their training by killing their masters.](data:image/gif;base64,R0lGODlhAQABAIABAAAAAP///yH5BAEAAAEALAAAAAABAAEAQAICTAEAOw%3D%3D)](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/starwars/images/a/a3/SithOrder-SOTJ.jpg/revision/latest?cb=20200113231523) [![Bane's death was the start of a tradition in which Sith apprentices completed their training by killing their masters.](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/starwars/images/a/a3/SithOrder-SOTJ.jpg/revision/latest/scale-to-width-down/250?cb=20200113231523)](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/starwars/images/a/a3/SithOrder-SOTJ.jpg/revision/latest?cb=20200113231523) 

Bane's death was the start of a tradition in which Sith apprentices completed their training by killing their masters.

 

Bane took an apprentice of his own, [Darth Zannah](/wiki/Darth_Zannah "Darth Zannah"),[[16]](#cite_note-HC_2-16) and passed on his knowledge while establishing the new Sith way of pursuing power and revenge against the Jedi through manipulation and subterfuge. The Jedi became aware of Bane's philosophy and Bane was killed.[[17]](#cite_note-Bane-SWE-17) Although historical records weren't accurate, it was considered possible that Bane was [killed](/wiki/Death "Death") by his own apprentice.[[12]](#cite_note-The_Dark_Side-12) The Jedi Order took responsibility for ending what [Jedi Master](/wiki/Jedi_Master "Jedi Master") [Elzar Mann](/wiki/Elzar_Mann "Elzar Mann") described as "the rule of Darth Bane."[[18]](#cite_note-SW_2020_20-18) No matter the case, the Jedi went on to falsely believe that they had destroyed the Sith forever[[17]](#cite_note-Bane-SWE-17) despite rumors that the Sith continued in the shadows.[[19]](#cite_note-dbYBG-19)

However, Bane's plans continued through his apprentice, allowing the Sith to survive secretly for [years](/wiki/Standard_year "Standard year") while planning their revenge against the Jedi Order.[[17]](#cite_note-Bane-SWE-17) [Darth Sidious](/wiki/Darth_Sidious "Darth Sidious") stated in his [book](/wiki/Book "Book"), *[The Secrets of the Sith](/wiki/The_Secrets_of_the_Sith "The Secrets of the Sith")*, that Bane was in fact killed by his apprentice. As a result, a cycle of betrayal and death became affixed to the Rule of Two, in which generations of apprentices fulfilled their training by killing their masters.[[4]](#cite_note-SotS-4) The Rule of Two created a powerful dynamic by limiting the Sith to only one master and one apprentice at a time, although not all Sith followed the decree as some took apprentices of their own while still serving a master.[[20]](#cite_note-VE-20)

### Plagueis and the Stranger[]

The Sith carried out their vendetta for a thousand years, gaining power in secret while the Jedi still believed that the Sith had been destroyed.[[21]](#cite_note-SithSWE-21) In time, a [Dark Lord of the Sith](/wiki/Dark_Lord_of_the_Sith "Dark Lord of the Sith") named [Darth Plagueis](/wiki/Darth_Plagueis "Darth Plagueis")[[22]](#cite_note-Episode_III-22) was trained by the Sith Master [Darth Tenebrous](/wiki/Darth_Tenebrous "Darth Tenebrous")[[23]](#cite_note-BMF_44-23) and worked in the shadows by the late [High Republic Era](/wiki/High_Republic_Era "High Republic Era"). In time, Plagueis took on a human known only as "[the Stranger](/wiki/The_Stranger "The Stranger")" as his[[24]](#cite_note-Leslye_EW-24) Sith[[25]](#cite_note-Stunts-25) apprentice.[[24]](#cite_note-Leslye_EW-24) The Stranger also wanted to take on a [Sith acolyte](/wiki/Sith_acolyte "Sith acolyte") of his own, dreaming of unlocking what he called "the power of two." At first, he recruited the [witch](/wiki/Witch "Witch") [Mae-ho Aniseya](/wiki/Mae-ho_Aniseya "Mae-ho Aniseya") to be his disciple[[26]](#cite_note-Teach_/_Corrupt-26) and [Sith assassin](/wiki/Sith_assassin "Sith assassin"),[[27]](#cite_note-114_Interview-27) but Mae proved to be too focused on revenge for his plans.[[26]](#cite_note-Teach_/_Corrupt-26) Instead, the Stranger found an acolyte in Mae's sister, the former Jedi [Verosha Aniseya](/wiki/Verosha_Aniseya "Verosha Aniseya").[[28]](#cite_note-The_Acolyte-28)

Despite his training by Plagueis,[[24]](#cite_note-Leslye_EW-24) the exact ties the Stranger had to the Sith Order by that time remained unclear. His "power of two" remarks lined up with the Sith traditions outlined by Bane, providing credence to the idea that he was a Sith in the Rule of Two lineage. However, the Stranger's reluctance to call himself a Sith Lord—as he instead took to calling himself someone without a name—and Plagueis' decision to lurk in the shadows near [the Stranger's hideout](/wiki/The_Stranger%27s_cave "The Stranger's cave") cast doubt on that link.[[29]](#cite_note-229POV-29)

### Plagueis and Sidious[]

:   "*Unfortunately, he taught his apprentice everything he knew. Then his apprentice killed him in his sleep.*"
:   ―Darth Sidious, remembering Darth Plagueis[[22]](#cite_note-Episode_III-22)

 [![Darth Sidious was trained by Darth Plagueis, the Sith Lord who sought to conquer death.](data:image/gif;base64,R0lGODlhAQABAIABAAAAAP///yH5BAEAAAEALAAAAAABAAEAQAICTAEAOw%3D%3D)](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/starwars/images/5/52/Sidious_hologram.png/revision/latest?cb=20200921210616) [![Darth Sidious was trained by Darth Plagueis, the Sith Lord who sought to conquer death.](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/starwars/images/5/52/Sidious_hologram.png/revision/latest/scale-to-width-down/250?cb=20200921210616)](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/starwars/images/5/52/Sidious_hologram.png/revision/latest?cb=20200921210616) 

Darth Sidious was trained by Darth Plagueis, the Sith Lord who sought to conquer death.

 

Eventually, Plagueis took Darth Sidious, the Sith persona of [Senator](/wiki/Senator "Senator") Sheev Palpatine of [Naboo](/wiki/Naboo "Naboo"), as his apprentice. They worked for years to coax the final secrets from the dark side of the Force. Sidious called back on the wisdom of Plagueis as a planner and a [prophet](/wiki/Prophet "Prophet"), knowing full well that two Sith were required to enact their means; one to serve as bait for the dark side, the other to be the vessel. Success would allow them the ability to harness the full power of the dark side and rule [immortal](/wiki/Immortality "Immortality") for ten thousand years.[[30]](#cite_note-Tarkin-30) Plagueis attempted to create a Force dyad with Sidious, which was a power as strong as life itself, which he discovered from the Doctrine of the Dyad, which was the predecessor of the Rule of Two. However, Plagueis was unable to form the dyad.[[31]](#cite_note-TROS_Expanded_Edition-31)

### Sidious and Maul[]

:   "*Always two there are. No more, no less. A master and an apprentice.*"  
    "*But which was destroyed? The master or the apprentice?* "
:   ―Yoda, and Mace Windu[[32]](#cite_note-Episode_I-32)

 [![Darth Maul was trained by Sidious, who killed his own master.](data:image/gif;base64,R0lGODlhAQABAIABAAAAAP///yH5BAEAAAEALAAAAAABAAEAQAICTAEAOw%3D%3D)](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/starwars/images/7/70/RuleOfTwo-CM.png/revision/latest?cb=20200909233004) [![Darth Maul was trained by Sidious, who killed his own master.](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/starwars/images/7/70/RuleOfTwo-CM.png/revision/latest/scale-to-width-down/250?cb=20200909233004)](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/starwars/images/7/70/RuleOfTwo-CM.png/revision/latest?cb=20200909233004) 

Darth Maul was trained by Sidious, who killed his own master.

 

Sidious was willing to use anyone he could to advance his plans to achieve ultimate power, even if his methods did not adhere to the Rule of Two perfectly.[[33]](#cite_note-POV229-33) While training under Plagueis, Sidious acquired a [Dathomirian](/wiki/Dathomirian "Dathomirian") [Zabrak](/wiki/Zabrak "Zabrak") named [Maul](/wiki/Maul "Maul") as his pupil.[[34]](#cite_note-dbDSBG-34) He had initially considered [Mother](/wiki/Mother_(title) "Mother (title)") [Talzin](/wiki/Talzin "Talzin"), Maul's mother, to be his Sith apprentice, but took Maul upon realizing his potential.[[35]](#cite_note-Son_of_Dathomir_3-35) Seeing no further use for his master, Sidious betrayed Plagueis by killing him in his [sleep](/wiki/Sleep "Sleep").[[22]](#cite_note-Episode_III-22) However, Sidious always saw Maul as not quite good enough, which he saw as the essential quality that defined Maul's life.[[36]](#cite_note-Hidden_Empire_1-36)

After [killing](/wiki/Skirmish_on_the_moon_of_Drazkel "Skirmish on the moon of Drazkel") his first Jedi, the [Twi'lek](/wiki/Twi%27lek "Twi'lek") [Padawan](/wiki/Padawan "Padawan") [Eldra Kaitis](/wiki/Eldra_Kaitis "Eldra Kaitis"), Darth Maul reflected that she would have made a good Sith apprentice, but that he had to finish her off to prevent his master from discovering his intended betrayal. Concurrently, [Xev Xrexus](/wiki/Xev_Xrexus "Xev Xrexus") from the [Xrexus Cartel](/wiki/Xrexus_Cartel "Xrexus Cartel") theorized the Sith's comeback, leading Maul to kill her[[37]](#cite_note-Darth_Maul_5-37) as Sidious had threatened him with death if they were prematurely discovered.[[38]](#cite_note-Darth_Maul_1-38)

In [32 BBY](/wiki/32_BBY "32 BBY"),[[2]](#cite_note-Timelines-2) during the [Invasion of Naboo](/wiki/Invasion_of_Naboo "Invasion of Naboo") by the [Trade Federation](/wiki/Trade_Federation "Trade Federation"), the Sith finally revealed themselves to the Jedi with Sidious sending Maul to [Tatooine](/wiki/Tatooine "Tatooine") to capture [Queen](/wiki/Monarch_of_Naboo "Monarch of Naboo") [Padmé Amidala](/wiki/Padm%C3%A9_Amidala "Padmé Amidala") and take her back to Naboo so she could sign a peace treaty. The Jedi [Qui-Gon Jinn](/wiki/Qui-Gon_Jinn "Qui-Gon Jinn") [faced](/wiki/Duel_on_Tatooine "Duel on Tatooine") Maul but was able to leave Tatooine with Amidala and his allies, realizing that the Sith had returned. During the [Battle of Naboo](/wiki/Battle_of_Naboo "Battle of Naboo"), which broke the Federation's [blockade](/wiki/Blockade "Blockade"), Maul killed Jinn only to be seemingly killed in turn by Padawan [Obi-Wan Kenobi](/wiki/Obi-Wan_Kenobi "Obi-Wan Kenobi"), leading the Jedi to realize the return of the Sith. During Jinn's [funeral](/wiki/Funeral_of_Qui-Gon_Jinn "Funeral of Qui-Gon Jinn"), both [Yoda](/wiki/Yoda "Yoda") and [Mace Windu](/wiki/Mace_Windu "Mace Windu"), the [Grand Master](/wiki/Grand_Master "Grand Master") and [Master of the Order](/wiki/Master_of_the_Order "Master of the Order") of the Jedi respectively, pondered on who could be the other Sith Lord due to the Rule of Two demanding only two Sith and whether Maul was the master or apprentice of the Sith pair that had just been reduced to one.[[32]](#cite_note-Episode_I-32) Though Maul would later turn out to be alive, Sidious turned his gaze away and lost all interest in him.[[36]](#cite_note-Hidden_Empire_1-36)

### Sidious and Tyranus[]

:   "*Remember, the first and only reality of the Sith. There can only be two. And you are no longer my apprentice. You have been replaced.*"
:   ―Darth Sidious, to Maul[[39]](#cite_note-The_Lawless-39)

 [![Sidious replaced Maul with Count Dooku, a former Jedi Master who adopted the Sith identity of Darth Tyranus.](data:image/gif;base64,R0lGODlhAQABAIABAAAAAP///yH5BAEAAAEALAAAAAABAAEAQAICTAEAOw%3D%3D)](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/starwars/images/a/ac/Sidious_Tyranus.png/revision/latest?cb=20140722221449) [![Sidious replaced Maul with Count Dooku, a former Jedi Master who adopted the Sith identity of Darth Tyranus.](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/starwars/images/a/ac/Sidious_Tyranus.png/revision/latest/scale-to-width-down/250?cb=20140722221449)](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/starwars/images/a/ac/Sidious_Tyranus.png/revision/latest?cb=20140722221449) 

Sidious replaced Maul with Count Dooku, a former Jedi Master who adopted the Sith identity of Darth Tyranus.

 

With Maul presumed dead, Sidious took the former Jedi Master [Count](/wiki/Count "Count") [Dooku](/wiki/Dooku "Dooku") from [House Serenno](/wiki/House_Serenno "House Serenno") as his next apprentice and bestowed him the name Darth Tyranus.[[40]](#cite_note-Episode_II-40) Sidious had actually approached Dooku while still apprenticing Maul, and met with Dooku in a [skyscraper](/wiki/Inquisitorius_Headquarters "Inquisitorius Headquarters") of his after Maul's loss on Naboo to assure him that the deaths of their apprentices were in service of their goals upon being called out for allowing Maul to kill Jinn, whom Dooku felt could have been a great ally to them despite Sidious not thinking the same. Unknown to the Sith, Jedi Master [Yaddle](/wiki/Yaddle "Yaddle") had followed Dooku there, but at Sidious' prompting, Dooku finished Yaddle off to prevent her from alerting the Jedi, officially cutting any ties he still had with the Jedi and becoming Sidious' pupil.[[41]](#cite_note-SW:TotJ-The_Sith_Lord-41) During a [mission on Sullust](/wiki/Mission_on_Sullust "Mission on Sullust"), Dooku considered recruiting the Jedi Knight [Jak'zin](/wiki/Jak%27zin "Jak'zin") as an ally to the Sith cause, but ultimately stopped when Sidious stated that a Sith Master needed only one apprentice and threatened to replace him with the [Togorian](/wiki/Togorian "Togorian") Jedi. Dooku ensured that Jak'zin was killed, though he lamented that his death was a pity.[[42]](#cite_note-AoR_Dooku-42)

In [19 BBY](/wiki/19_BBY "19 BBY"),[[2]](#cite_note-Timelines-2) shortly before the [end](/wiki/Mission_to_Mustafar "Mission to Mustafar") of the Clone Wars, [General](/wiki/General "General") [Grievous](/wiki/Grievous "Grievous") [kidnapped](/wiki/Battle_of_Coruscant "Battle of Coruscant") Palpatine, which was a plan orchestrated by Sidious, in order to bring [Anakin Skywalker](/wiki/Anakin_Skywalker "Anakin Skywalker") to the dark side and replace Dooku as his next apprentice, as Sidious felt it was time to make Anakin his apprentice and that Dooku's purpose had been served. Palpatine's capture led to Kenobi and Skywalker to be dispatched to rescue him aboard the *[Invisible Hand](/wiki/Invisible_Hand "Invisible Hand")*. During a [lightsaber duel](/wiki/Lightsaber_duel "Lightsaber duel") aboard the [ship](/wiki/Starship "Starship"), Skywalker defeated and disarmed Dooku. Sidious then betrayed his apprentice by encouraging Anakin to kill the Sith Lord, paving the way for Skywalker to take the older man's place. After Dooku's death, Sidious and his Jedi rescuers eventually made it back to [Coruscant](/wiki/Coruscant "Coruscant"). Studentless again, Sidious seduced Skywalker to the dark side by [telling](/wiki/Tragedy_of_Darth_Plagueis_the_Wise "Tragedy of Darth Plagueis the Wise") him about Plagueis upon discovering his fears that Amidala, his secret [wife](/wiki/Marriage "Marriage"), would die in [childbirth](/wiki/Reproduction "Reproduction"). Skywalker initially revealed Palpatine's secret to Mace Windu, who then attempted to [arrest the Chancellor](/wiki/Duel_in_Palpatine%27s_office "Duel in Palpatine's office"). Skywalker, however, turned on the Jedi to save Palpatine from being killed, leading to Windu's death.[[22]](#cite_note-Episode_III-22)

No longer a Jedi, Skywalker became Sidious' new apprentice and was anointed as Darth Vader. Sidious issued Order 66,[[22]](#cite_note-Episode_III-22) causing the biochips to activate and brainwash the clone [troopers](/wiki/Trooper "Trooper") into betraying and murdering their [Jedi Generals](/wiki/Jedi_General "Jedi General"),[[57]](#cite_note-Clone_troopers_DB-57) while Vader [purged](/wiki/Operation:_Knightfall "Operation: Knightfall") the [Jedi Temple](/wiki/Imperial_Palace "Imperial Palace") on Coruscant under Sidious's order. No longer requiring the [Separatist Council](/wiki/Executive_Separatist_Council "Executive Separatist Council"), Sidious betrayed them by having Vader [execute them](/wiki/Mission_to_Mustafar "Mission to Mustafar"). Sidious then framed the Jedi for [treason](/wiki/Treason "Treason") and [proclaimed](/wiki/Proclamation_of_the_New_Order "Proclamation of the New Order") himself [Galactic Emperor](/wiki/Galactic_Emperor "Galactic Emperor"), reorganizing the Republic into the [First Galactic Empire](/wiki/Galactic_Empire "Galactic Empire"). Kenobi and Yoda resolved to destroy the Sith, including the former Anakin Skywalker. However, neither Sith Lord was destroyed.[[22]](#cite_note-Episode_III-22)

Vader sustained grievous injuries in his [duel](/wiki/Duel_on_Mustafar "Duel on Mustafar") with Kenobi, and was rebuilt as a [cyborg](/wiki/Cyborg "Cyborg") encased within [life support](/wiki/Life_support "Life support") [armor](/wiki/Darth_Vader%27s_armor "Darth Vader's armor"). Upon learning the existence of [Luke Skywalker](/wiki/Luke_Skywalker "Luke Skywalker"), Vader's secret son and the [Rebel pilot](/wiki/Rebel_pilot "Rebel pilot") who destroyed the [Death Star](/wiki/Death_Star "Death Star"), both made plans to use Luke to overthrow each other. Vader desired for Luke to help him overthrow Sidious, so the two could rule the [galaxy](/wiki/Galaxy "Galaxy"). Sidious, on the other hand, planned to have Luke kill Vader and then replace him as Sidious's apprentice. While trying to recover the Emperor's respect before Cylo's death, Vader became aware of his [parenthood](/wiki/Parent "Parent") after the bounty hunter [Boba Fett](/wiki/Boba_Fett "Boba Fett") [discovered](/wiki/Duel_in_Ben_Kenobi%27s_home "Duel in Ben Kenobi's home") the [pilot](/wiki/Pilot "Pilot")'s name and informed Vader of this.[[62]](#cite_note-Darth_Vader_6-62) Focused on trying to get his son to join him on the dark side, Vader [killed](/wiki/Battle_of_Vrogas_Vas "Battle of Vrogas Vas") Karbin and prevented Luke from being captured on [Vrogas Vas](/wiki/Vrogas_Vas "Vrogas Vas").[[70]](#cite_note-Darth_Vader_15-70)

In [3 ABY](/wiki/3_ABY "3 ABY"),[[2]](#cite_note-Timelines-2) following the [Battle of Hoth](/wiki/Battle_of_Hoth "Battle of Hoth"), Vader communicated with Sidious aboard the *[Executor](/wiki/Executor "Executor")*, as the Emperor had sensed the threat Skywalker posed to the Sith. Promising his master that Skywalker would either choose joining them or be killed by his hand, Vader managed to capture the crew of the *[Millennium Falcon](/wiki/Millennium_Falcon "Millennium Falcon")* on [Cloud City](/wiki/Cloud_City "Cloud City") and manipulated Luke into saving his friends.[[69]](#cite_note-Episode_V-69)

Upon arriving on Cloud City, [Bespin](/wiki/Bespin "Bespin"), Skywalker was unable to hear [Leia Organa's](/wiki/Leia_Skywalker_Organa_Solo "Leia Skywalker Organa Solo") warnings that Vader had set up a trap for him. Skywalker then found Vader and the two engaged in a [duel](/wiki/Duel_on_Cloud_City "Duel on Cloud City"). Vader defeated his son by [severing](/wiki/Dismemberment "Dismemberment") Skywalker's right hand before telling him that he was his father. Vader then offered Skywalker the chance to join him and overthrow the Emperor and bring order to the [galaxy](/wiki/Galaxy "Galaxy"). Skywalker, however, refused and plunged himself into the shaft only to be rescued by the *Falcon*.[[69]](#cite_note-Episode_V-69)

In the aftermath of his failure to bring his son to the dark side, Vader unearthed some secrets about Amidala's death before the Emperor summoned him to the Imperial Palace. Believing that Vader had regressed from a powerful Sith Lord to a grief-stricken widower, Sidious destroyed his apprentice's [cybernetic](/wiki/Cybernetics "Cybernetics") [limbs](/wiki/Limb "Limb") before leaving him on Mustafar as test of Vader's resolve to survive. He also sent his [Sith assassin](/wiki/Sith_assassin "Sith assassin"), [Ochi of Bestoon](/wiki/Ochi "Ochi"), to test Vader's abilities without relying on the Force.[[71]](#cite_note-Darth_Vader_(2020)_6-71)

Vader rebuilt himself, captured Ochi, and [traveled](/wiki/Mission_to_Exegol "Mission to Exegol") to the hidden Sith world of Exegol to discover the Emperor's plans. Finding Sidious there, Vader mocked Sidious' title as his master and declared himself to no longer be his apprentice. Following Sidious into the Sith Citadel, Vader and Ochi discovered the Emperor's creations as well as his [acolytes](/wiki/Sith_cultist "Sith cultist"). Though none of them were Sith, Sidious declared that they all had the potential to replace Vader.[[72]](#cite_note-Darth_Vader_(2020)_11-72)

Vader killed the Emperor's servants, but was ultimately overpowered by Sidious who used a mountain of [kyber crystals](/wiki/Kyber_crystal "Kyber crystal") as a display of the unlimited power in his possession. He discovered the [construction site](/wiki/Shipyards_of_Exegol "Shipyards of Exegol") of a massive [reserve](/wiki/Final_Order "Final Order") of [*Xyston*-class Star Destroyers](/wiki/Xyston-class_Star_Destroyer "Xyston-class Star Destroyer") armed with [planet-destroying cannons](/wiki/Axial_superlaser "Axial superlaser"). He inflicted pain on the kyber, a living entity connected to the Force, in accordance with the traditions of the Sith. As Vader felt the [crystals](/wiki/Crystal "Crystal")' pain through the Force, Sidious issued an ultimatum to him—to share in his power, but as an apprentice once more. Ultimately, Vader returned to his master's side after experiencing a vision of his son, Luke Skywalker, telling him that he could destroy the Emperor.[[72]](#cite_note-Darth_Vader_(2020)_11-72)

#### Questions of loyalty[]

Concluding that there was no room for another and having witnessed the true might and purpose of the Sith, despite knowing that Luke could still be strong, Vader felt that he had no other choice than to eventually kill his son[[73]](#cite_note-Darth_Vader_(2020)_12-73) despite, ultimately, still wishing Skywalker had joined him on Cloud City, just as he still wished Amidala would have joined him on Mustafar.[[74]](#cite_note-Darth_Vader_(2020)_32-74) As Vader continued on and, in time, returned to scheming ways to overthrow his master, he joined the ranks of the [Schism Imperial](/wiki/Schism_Imperial "Schism Imperial") formed by Imperial [Administrator](/wiki/Administrator "Administrator") [Sly Moore](/wiki/Sly_Moore "Sly Moore"),[[75]](#cite_note-Darth_Vader_(2020)_45-75) who, in fact, hoped to destroy both Vader and Skywalker to turn herself into the Emperor's new apprentice.[[76]](#cite_note-Darth_Vader_(2020)_48-76) During a mission to the [Meridional Ice Cap](/wiki/Meridional_Ice_Cap "Meridional Ice Cap") of the planet [Tython](/wiki/Tython "Tython"), Vader encountered a vision of Skywalker, who confessed in the [Martyrium of Frozen Tears](/wiki/Martyrium_of_Frozen_Tears "Martyrium of Frozen Tears") he sometimes thought about joining his father.[[75]](#cite_note-Darth_Vader_(2020)_45-75)

Hearing that,[[75]](#cite_note-Darth_Vader_(2020)_45-75) Vader resumed his scheme to turn his son to the dark side[[77]](#cite_note-Darth_Vader_(2020)_46-77) as his apprentice to overthrow Sidious, something that Sidious became aware of.[[78]](#cite_note-Darth_Vader_(2020)_47-78) To see if his son would destroy him or join his crusade, Vader brought Skywalker's old comrade [Warba Calip](/wiki/Warba_Calip "Warba Calip") into his fold,[[79]](#cite_note-The_Curse-79) only for Calip to betray Vader once she reunited with the young Jedi by not wanting to reveal his location.[[76]](#cite_note-Darth_Vader_(2020)_48-76) [Sabé](/wiki/Sab%C3%A9 "Sabé"), a former [handmaiden](/wiki/Handmaiden "Handmaiden") of Amidala and brief [lieutenant](/wiki/Lieutenant "Lieutenant") to Vader,[[79]](#cite_note-The_Curse-79) joined Calip to see if Skywalker would join the dark side, but he promised Sabé he was not going to repeat his father's mistakes. Vader sensed that Skywalker was not ready to join the dark side but, at that moment, was betrayed by Moore, who revealed her plans to destroy both Vader and Skywalker.[[76]](#cite_note-Darth_Vader_(2020)_48-76)

### Legacy[]

#### Rieve's act[]

Admittedly, we don't really know what this means, but we suspect it means they needed to use two lightsabers for every one lightsaber the Jedi carried. Around [9 ABY](/wiki/9_ABY "9 ABY"),[[86]](#cite_note-9_ABY_Date-86) the [Hunters of the Outer Rim](/wiki/Hunters_of_the_Outer_Rim "Hunters of the Outer Rim") competition would see a Force-sensitive named [Rieve](/wiki/Rieve "Rieve") join its roster under the persona of a Sith Lord. While in private she was on good terms with her fellow [hunters](/wiki/Hunter_(Grand_Arena) "Hunter (Grand Arena)"), her public persona saw use[[87]](#cite_note-BftA-87) in [HoloNet](/wiki/HoloNet "HoloNet")-streamed[[2]](#cite_note-Timelines-2) matches against other competition members.[[88]](#cite_note-Hunters-88) After Rieve won a match against her[[85]](#cite_note-Powers_of_Dark-85) on-stage[[87]](#cite_note-BftA-87) rival,[[89]](#cite_note-First_Edition-89) the Jedi focused droid [J-3DI](/wiki/J-3DI "J-3DI"),[[88]](#cite_note-Hunters-88) [Arena News](/wiki/Arena_News "Arena News") ran a story about her victory and included a reference to the Rule of Two as a fun fact. #### A sole Sith Lord[]

:   "*As the Empire perished, I was born again, my consciousness transferred into a new body on Exegol. Alas, even after years of experimentation, the cloning techniques employed by my acolytes were inadequate. They could not create a vessel capable of containing my unfathomable power.*"
:   ―Darth Sidious[[4]](#cite_note-SotS-4)

 [![Darth Sidious was reborn on Exegol, where he plotted his revenge and the return of the Sith.](data:image/gif;base64,R0lGODlhAQABAIABAAAAAP///yH5BAEAAAEALAAAAAABAAEAQAICTAEAOw%3D%3D)](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/starwars/images/c/c9/Insider197-Palpatineresurrected.png/revision/latest?cb=20231024055024) [![Darth Sidious was reborn on Exegol, where he plotted his revenge and the return of the Sith.](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/starwars/images/c/c9/Insider197-Palpatineresurrected.png/revision/latest/scale-to-width-down/200?cb=20231024055024)](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/starwars/images/c/c9/Insider197-Palpatineresurrected.png/revision/latest?cb=20231024055024) 

Darth Sidious was reborn on Exegol, where he plotted his revenge and the return of the Sith.

 

The deaths of Sidious and Vader ended the Sith lineage that dated back to the creation of the Rule of Two. But while the Sith's destruction was foretold in the Jedi prophecy of the Chosen One, it did not mean the end of the dark side of the Force.[[90]](#cite_note-TLJ_VD-90) Sidious had observed the Rule of Two to the extent that it limited the Sith ranks to a master and an apprentice;[[39]](#cite_note-The_Lawless-39) beyond that, he was unwilling to be replaced and therefore sought to rule the galaxy forever as an immortal Sith Lord. Using the knowledge he gained from his late master, Plagueis, Sidious resurrected himself in a cloned body. The transference was flawed, however, and Sidious found his spirit tethered to a weak, decaying vessel.[[13]](#cite_note-TSWB-13) Sidious regarded the dyad as the key to the ultimate power coveted by all Sith;[[4]](#cite_note-SotS-4) as such, he considered the Rule of Two a "pale imitation", an unworthy but necessary successor to the Doctrine of the dyad.[[31]](#cite_note-TROS_Expanded_Edition-31)[[4]](#cite_note-SotS-4)

Aided by the Sith Eternal, the revived Emperor oversaw the continuation of the [Contingency](/wiki/Contingency "Contingency") from Exegol, where a massive [invasion fleet](/wiki/Final_Order "Final Order") was built to enforce his future [Sith Empire](/wiki/Final_Order "Final Order"). Sidious also sought a new apprentice to train; the grandson of Vader and nephew to Luke Skywalker, [Ben Solo](/wiki/Ben_Solo "Ben Solo")'s strength with the Force was prodigious as a result of his [Skywalker bloodline](/wiki/Skywalker_family "Skywalker family").[[13]](#cite_note-TSWB-13) Through the Force, Sidious secretly communicated with Solo in order to lure him away from the Jedi path, undermining Skywalker's plan to restore the Jedi Order.[[31]](#cite_note-TROS_Expanded_Edition-31) Rather than directly training Solo, he used [Snoke](/wiki/Snoke "Snoke")—an [artificial](/wiki/Bioengineering "Bioengineering") [strandcast](/wiki/Strand-Cast "Strand-Cast") he created and discarded as a vessel for his spirit—through whom he could mold the Skywalker heir into a servant of the dark side.[[13]](#cite_note-TSWB-13) The Sith Eternal were also involved in the creation of Snoke, whom they designed to groom Solo as an heir worthy of claiming the Sith legacy.[[5]](#cite_note-TROS_VD-5)
