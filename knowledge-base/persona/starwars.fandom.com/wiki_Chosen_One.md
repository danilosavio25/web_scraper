The **Chosen One** was the central figure in the [Jedi](/wiki/Jedi "Jedi") prophecy that foretold the coming of the one destined to bring balance to [the Force](/wiki/The_Force "The Force") by destroying the [Sith](/wiki/Sith "Sith"). The Chosen One was [born](/wiki/Reproduction "Reproduction") during the [era](/wiki/Republic_Era "Republic Era") of the [Galactic Republic](/wiki/Galactic_Republic "Galactic Republic"); [Anakin Skywalker](/wiki/Anakin_Skywalker "Anakin Skywalker"), the [son](/wiki/Gender "Gender") of [Shmi Skywalker](/wiki/Shmi_Skywalker_Lars "Shmi Skywalker Lars"), was exceptionally [strong](/wiki/Force-sensitive "Force-sensitive") with [the Force](/wiki/The_Force "The Force"), possessing an unprecedented level of more than twenty-thousand [midi-chlorians](/wiki/Midi-chlorian "Midi-chlorian") in his [blood](/wiki/Blood "Blood"). As a result, Skywalker had the potential to become the most powerful Jedi in [galactic history](/wiki/Timeline_of_galactic_history "Timeline of galactic history"). The [Dark Lord of the Sith](/wiki/Dark_Lord_of_the_Sith "Dark Lord of the Sith") [Darth Sidious](/wiki/Darth_Sidious "Darth Sidious") believed that Skywalker would eventually surpass both himself and [Grand Master](/wiki/Grand_Master "Grand Master") [Yoda](/wiki/Yoda "Yoda"). The [Father](/wiki/Father_(Mortis) "Father (Mortis)"), one of the three [Mortis gods](/wiki/Force_wielder "Force wielder"), would seek to [recruit](/wiki/Mission_to_Mortis "Mission to Mortis") Skywalker as his successor, believing that only the Chosen One had the strength to maintain the balance between his children, the [Son](/wiki/Son "Son") and the [Daughter](/wiki/Daughter "Daughter") in the realm of [Mortis](/wiki/Mortis "Mortis").

Discovered by [Jedi Master](/wiki/Jedi_Master "Jedi Master") [Qui-Gon Jinn](/wiki/Qui-Gon_Jinn "Qui-Gon Jinn") in [32 BBY](/wiki/32_BBY "32 BBY"), Skywalker joined the [Jedi Order](/wiki/Jedi_Order "Jedi Order") and ascended to the rank of [Jedi Knight](/wiki/Jedi_Knight "Jedi Knight") following his [apprenticeship](/wiki/Padawan "Padawan") under [Obi-Wan Kenobi](/wiki/Obi-Wan_Kenobi "Obi-Wan Kenobi"). During the [Clone Wars](/wiki/Clone_Wars "Clone Wars"), however through the manipulations of Darth Sidious, Skywalker [turned](/wiki/Duel_in_Palpatine%27s_office "Duel in Palpatine's office") to the [dark side of the Force](/wiki/Dark_side_of_the_Force "Dark side of the Force") and became the [Sith Lord](/wiki/Sith_Lord "Sith Lord") [Darth](/wiki/Darth "Darth") Vader in [19 BBY](/wiki/19_BBY "19 BBY"). He then participated in the [systematic purge](/wiki/Order_66 "Order 66") that rendered the Jedi virtually [extinct](/wiki/Great_Jedi_Purge "Great Jedi Purge"). With the [Galactic Empire](/wiki/Galactic_Empire "Galactic Empire") [rising to dominate](/wiki/Imperial_Era "Imperial Era") [the galaxy](/wiki/The_galaxy "The galaxy"), Kenobi endeavored to [train](/wiki/Jedi_training "Jedi training") Vader's child, [Luke Skywalker](/wiki/Luke_Skywalker "Luke Skywalker"), believing the prophecy would be fulfilled by the son instead of the [father](/wiki/Parent "Parent"). However, Luke believed that his father could be turned back to the [light side of the Force](/wiki/Light_side_of_the_Force "Light side of the Force"). During the [Battle of Endor](/wiki/Battle_of_Endor "Battle of Endor") in [4 ABY](/wiki/4_ABY "4 ABY"), Vader sacrificed himself by destroying his [Sith Master](/wiki/Sith_Master "Sith Master") in order to save his son. The [deaths](/wiki/Death "Death") of the last [two](/wiki/Rule_of_Two "Rule of Two") Sith [Lords](/wiki/Lord "Lord") fulfilled the Jedi prophecy, restoring balance to the Force. With his destiny complete, the Chosen One [passed into the Force](/wiki/Force_spirit "Force spirit") as Anakin Skywalker.

## History[]

### Origin[]

:   "*A Chosen One shall come, born of no father, and through him will ultimate balance in the Force be restored.*"
:   ―A Jedi prophecy[[6]](#cite_note-Master_&_Apprentice-6)

 [![An ancient Jedi prophecy foretold that the Chosen One would bring balance to the Force by destroying the Sith.](data:image/gif;base64,R0lGODlhAQABAIABAAAAAP///yH5BAEAAAEALAAAAAABAAEAQAICTAEAOw%3D%3D)](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/starwars/images/9/92/AnakinSkywalker-SWTLC.png/revision/latest?cb=20230401012146) [![An ancient Jedi prophecy foretold that the Chosen One would bring balance to the Force by destroying the Sith.](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/starwars/images/9/92/AnakinSkywalker-SWTLC.png/revision/latest/scale-to-width-down/250?cb=20230401012146)](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/starwars/images/9/92/AnakinSkywalker-SWTLC.png/revision/latest?cb=20230401012146) 

An ancient Jedi prophecy foretold that the Chosen One would bring balance to the Force by destroying the Sith.

 

An ancient [Jedi](/wiki/Jedi "Jedi") prophecy[[7]](#cite_note-TSWB-7) made by an ancient [Jedi mystic](/wiki/Jedi_mystic "Jedi mystic") and recorded on a [holocron](/wiki/Holocron "Holocron")[[6]](#cite_note-Master_&_Apprentice-6) foretold that the Chosen One would restore balance to [the Force](/wiki/The_Force "The Force") by destroying the [Sith](/wiki/Sith "Sith").[[4]](#cite_note-Episode_III-4) The prophecy also predicted that the Chosen One would also become one of the strongest [Force-sensitives](/wiki/Force-sensitive "Force-sensitive") in [the galaxy](/wiki/The_galaxy "The galaxy"), rivaling the power of the most legendary champions of the [Jedi Order](/wiki/Jedi_Order "Jedi Order").[[8]](#cite_note-Skywalker:_AFaW-8) The prophesies of the Sith also believed in a coming figure who would be born of the Force. Known to those writings as the [Sith'ari](/wiki/Sith%27ari "Sith'ari"), the Sith foretold such an individual would come forth and hold tremendous power, much like the Jedi believed for the Chosen One. Based on his research, [historian](/wiki/Historian "Historian") [Beaumont Kin](/wiki/Beaumont_Kin "Beaumont Kin") believed the Sith'ari and Chosen One were the same figure.[[9]](#cite_note-Rise_and_Fall-9)

Although the Jedi had long spoken of balance in the Force, there was no consensus on what that meant for the [galaxy](/wiki/Galaxy "Galaxy"). Some people thought that the [light side of the Force](/wiki/Light_side_of_the_Force "Light side of the Force") and the [dark side of the Force](/wiki/Dark_side_of_the_Force "Dark side of the Force") had to be equal in order to create balance, while others believed that the light could be the Force in its natural state, making the dark side the cause of imbalance.[[7]](#cite_note-TSWB-7) In contrast to the Jedi's view of balance, the [Sith Lord](/wiki/Sith_Lord "Sith Lord") [Darth Sidious](/wiki/Darth_Sidious "Darth Sidious") believed that the dark side needed to be free, and the light side suppressed, in order to achieve balance in the Force.[[10]](#cite_note-SOTS-10)

The Sith's greed and desire for power led to infighting among their ranks, resulting in their near-extinction[[11]](#cite_note-Sacrifice-11) and defeat by the Jedi.[[12]](#cite_note-SWP-12) By the [time](/wiki/Time "Time") of the [Invasion of Naboo](/wiki/Invasion_of_Naboo "Invasion of Naboo"), the Jedi Order believed that the Sith had been extinct for a [millennium](/wiki/Millennium "Millennium").[[1]](#cite_note-Episode_I-1) Unbeknownst to the Jedi,[[7]](#cite_note-TSWB-7) the Sith [Lord](/wiki/Lord "Lord") [Darth Bane](/wiki/Darth_Bane "Darth Bane") survived the ancient Sith's destruction and ultimately chose to pass his knowledge to a single apprentice, [Darth Zannah](/wiki/Darth_Zannah "Darth Zannah"),[[13]](#cite_note-HC_2-13) thereby forming the [Rule of Two](/wiki/Rule_of_Two "Rule of Two")[[11]](#cite_note-Sacrifice-11) that would limit the Sith ranks to a [Sith Master](/wiki/Sith_Master "Sith Master") and an [Sith apprentice](/wiki/Sith_apprentice "Sith apprentice") for the next thousand [years](/wiki/Standard_year "Standard year").[[1]](#cite_note-Episode_I-1)

Apart from the Jedi prophecy, the legend of the Chosen One was connected to three ancient [Force wielders](/wiki/Force_wielder "Force wielder")[[14]](#cite_note-Overlords-14)—"the Ones"[[15]](#cite_note-UltimateSW-15) known as the [Father](/wiki/Father_(Mortis) "Father (Mortis)"), the [Son](/wiki/Son "Son"), and the [Daughter](/wiki/Daughter "Daughter")—who resided in the ethereal realm of [Mortis](/wiki/Mortis "Mortis"). The Father maintained balance between the Daughter and the Son, who embodied the light side and the dark side of the Force, respectively. The Father sought to recruit the Chosen One as his successor, believing that only he would have the power to preserve the balance on Mortis in the event of the Father's [death](/wiki/Death "Death").[[16]](#cite_note-Father_Databank-16)

Qui-Gon Jinn discovered Anakin Skywalker, the prophesied Chosen One, during the fall of the Galactic Republic.

 

[Forty-one years](/wiki/41_BBY "41 BBY") before the [Battle of Yavin](/wiki/Battle_of_Yavin "Battle of Yavin"),[[17]](#cite_note-Visual_Guide-17) [Shmi Skywalker](/wiki/Shmi_Skywalker_Lars "Shmi Skywalker Lars") gave [birth](/wiki/Reproduction "Reproduction") to a [son](/wiki/Parent "Parent") named [Anakin Skywalker](/wiki/Anakin_Skywalker "Anakin Skywalker"). Though she could not explain it, there was no father involved in her [child's](/wiki/Youngling "Youngling") conception.[[18]](#cite_note-Shmi_Databank-18) In the first [decade](/wiki/Standard_decade "Standard decade") of young Skywalker's life, both he and his [mother](/wiki/Parent "Parent") lived in [slavery](/wiki/Slavery "Slavery") on the [Outer Rim](/wiki/Outer_Rim_Territories "Outer Rim Territories") [world](/wiki/Planet "Planet") of [Tatooine](/wiki/Tatooine "Tatooine"). Originally the property of [Gardulla Besadii the Elder](/wiki/Gardulla_Besadii_the_Elder "Gardulla Besadii the Elder"), the Skywalkers traded owners after the [Hutt](/wiki/Hutt "Hutt") lost them in a [podracing](/wiki/Podracing "Podracing") bet with the [Toydarian](/wiki/Toydarian "Toydarian") [junk dealer](/wiki/Junk_dealer "Junk dealer") [Watto](/wiki/Watto "Watto"). During his service to [Watto](/wiki/Watto "Watto"), Skywalker befriended [Jedi Master](/wiki/Jedi_Master "Jedi Master") [Qui-Gon Jinn](/wiki/Qui-Gon_Jinn "Qui-Gon Jinn"),[[1]](#cite_note-Episode_I-1) who was known for his fascination with prophecies.[[6]](#cite_note-Master_&_Apprentice-6)

Jinn, having gauged Skywalker's inherent strength in the Force with his own observations, had an analysis made of the boy's exact potential through a [blood](/wiki/DNA "DNA") test. The results confirmed that Skywalker had an unprecedented quantity of [midi-chlorians](/wiki/Midi-chlorian "Midi-chlorian") in his cells; his exact count remained unknown as the readings were off the chart. However, the test did confirm that Skywalker had over twenty thousand midi-chlorians, at least, exceeding the count of every Jedi including [Grand Master](/wiki/Grand_Master "Grand Master") [Yoda](/wiki/Yoda "Yoda"). In addition, the secret of Skywalker's miraculous birth was revealed to Jinn by Shmi.[[1]](#cite_note-Episode_I-1)

 [![Jinn brought Skywalker to the Jedi Temple, determined to see the Chosen One become a Jedi Knight.](data:image/gif;base64,R0lGODlhAQABAIABAAAAAP///yH5BAEAAAEALAAAAAABAAEAQAICTAEAOw%3D%3D)](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/starwars/images/a/ac/High_Council_Chamber.png/revision/latest?cb=20131116223700) [![Jinn brought Skywalker to the Jedi Temple, determined to see the Chosen One become a Jedi Knight.](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/starwars/images/a/ac/High_Council_Chamber.png/revision/latest/scale-to-width-down/250?cb=20131116223700)](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/starwars/images/a/ac/High_Council_Chamber.png/revision/latest?cb=20131116223700) 

Jinn brought Skywalker to the Jedi Temple, determined to see the Chosen One become a Jedi Knight.

 

Faced with such revelations, Jinn came to the conclusion that Skywalker was in fact a vergence in the Force[[1]](#cite_note-Episode_I-1)—the Chosen One destined to bring ultimate balance.[[6]](#cite_note-Master_&_Apprentice-6) After Skywalker was liberated from slavery, he accompanied Jinn to the [Jedi Temple](/wiki/Imperial_Palace "Imperial Palace") on [Coruscant](/wiki/Coruscant "Coruscant"), hoping to begin his [training](/wiki/Jedi_training "Jedi training") as a Jedi. The [Jedi High Council](/wiki/Jedi_High_Council "Jedi High Council") assessed Skywalker's potential with the Force through a series of tests; additionally, they sought to understand his mindset. While his future was clouded in their [Force vision](/wiki/Force_vision "Force vision"), the High Council sensed a great amount of fear within him, including the fear of loss that he felt for his mother who remained a slave on Tatooine. Yoda was concerned that Skywalker's fear would lead him to develop other traits that could lead him to the dark side of the Force, such anger and hate which, in turn, led to suffering. As a result, the High Council declined to authorize Skywalker for training due to his age[[1]](#cite_note-Episode_I-1) and the fear within him.[[19]](#cite_note-Yoda-19)

 [![Following the death of Jinn, Skywalker began his Jedi apprenticeship under Obi-Wan Kenobi.](data:image/gif;base64,R0lGODlhAQABAIABAAAAAP///yH5BAEAAAEALAAAAAABAAEAQAICTAEAOw%3D%3D)](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/starwars/images/3/3d/You_will_be_a_Jedi.png/revision/latest?cb=20240220053910) [![Following the death of Jinn, Skywalker began his Jedi apprenticeship under Obi-Wan Kenobi.](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/starwars/images/3/3d/You_will_be_a_Jedi.png/revision/latest/scale-to-width-down/250?cb=20240220053910)](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/starwars/images/3/3d/You_will_be_a_Jedi.png/revision/latest?cb=20240220053910) 

Following the death of Jinn, Skywalker began his Jedi apprenticeship under Obi-Wan Kenobi.

 

Jinn remained adamant in his belief that Skywalker was prophesied Chosen One. He had intended to train Skywalker himself, but was fatally struck down by the Sith [Lord](/wiki/Lord "Lord") [Darth Maul](/wiki/Maul "Maul") during the [Battle of Naboo](/wiki/Battle_of_Naboo "Battle of Naboo") in [32 BBY](/wiki/32_BBY "32 BBY").[[2]](#cite_note-GA-2) When Jinn died, his last words were spoken in acknowledgment of Skywalker as the Chosen One who would bring balance to the Force. His apprentice, [Padawan](/wiki/Padawan "Padawan") [Obi-Wan Kenobi](/wiki/Obi-Wan_Kenobi "Obi-Wan Kenobi"), promised to train Skywalker. The High Council conferred the rank of [Jedi Knight](/wiki/Jedi_Knight "Jedi Knight") on Kenobi, who defeated the Sith Lord who killed his master, and also permitted him to train Skywalker as his Padawan.[[1]](#cite_note-Episode_I-1) Yoda was the lone dissenter in the High Council's decision to accept Skywalker into the ranks of the Jedi Order.[[8]](#cite_note-Skywalker:_AFaW-8) Regardless of whether the boy was the Chosen One, the Grand Master was certain in the danger that he sensed Skywalker's training would bring. During the [funeral of Qui-Gon Jinn](/wiki/Funeral_of_Qui-Gon_Jinn "Funeral of Qui-Gon Jinn"), Skywalker learned of the High Council's decision to allow Kenobi to train him. Kenobi assured Skywalker that he would become a Jedi.[[1]](#cite_note-Episode_I-1)

#### Path of the Jedi[]

##### Training the Chosen One[]

:   "*If the prophecy is true, your apprentice is the only one who can bring the Force back into balance.*"
:   ―Mace Windu, to Obi-Wan Kenobi[[20]](#cite_note-Episode_II-20)

 [![Already at a young age Skywalker was exceptionally skilled in the Jedi arts.](data:image/gif;base64,R0lGODlhAQABAIABAAAAAP///yH5BAEAAAEALAAAAAABAAEAQAICTAEAOw%3D%3D)](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/starwars/images/c/c2/AoR-AnakinSkywalker-B-textless.png/revision/latest?cb=20190626051005) [![Already at a young age Skywalker was exceptionally skilled in the Jedi arts.](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/starwars/images/c/c2/AoR-AnakinSkywalker-B-textless.png/revision/latest/scale-to-width-down/250?cb=20190626051005)](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/starwars/images/c/c2/AoR-AnakinSkywalker-B-textless.png/revision/latest?cb=20190626051005) 

Already at a young age Skywalker was exceptionally skilled in the Jedi arts.

 

During his apprenticeship under Kenobi, Skywalker struggled to live up to the expectations not only of being a Jedi, but the Chosen One as well. His status as the central figure in an ancient Jedi prophecy made it difficult for him to convince his peers that he was no different from them, which caused him to feel like an outcast. In addition, he grew to idealize the memory of Jinn, whose faith he hoped to vindicate by proving that he was the Chosen One indeed. Although he regarded his Jedi mentor as a father figure in his life, Skywalker believed that Kenobi failed to recognize his true strength in the Force. In time Skywalker became convinced that his power would rival that of the strongest Jedi Masters of his time, including Yoda and [Mace Windu](/wiki/Mace_Windu "Mace Windu"), as foretold in the prophecy of the Chosen One.[[8]](#cite_note-Skywalker:_AFaW-8)

As Skywalker ascended the Jedi ranks, the High Council observed his progress with a degree of concern. Windu commented that Skywalker possessed exceptional skills for his age, Kenobi however felt that his pupil lacked experience and humility. He was concerned that his pupil had developed hubris due to his advanced abilities, a trait which Yoda also perceived in other members of Skywalker's generation as well as the older, more experienced members of the Order.[[20]](#cite_note-Episode_II-20) Although Windu remained skeptical about Skywalker's status as the Chosen One,[[21]](#cite_note-Mace_Windu-21) he was mindful of the prophecy and believed that only Kenobi's Padawan was capable of restoring balance to the Force.[[20]](#cite_note-Episode_II-20)

Another influential figure in Skywalker's life was Sheev Palpatine, a [politician](/wiki/Politics "Politics") from [Naboo](/wiki/Naboo "Naboo"). The public façade of Palpatine masked his true identity as Darth Sidious, who orchestrated the invasion of his [homeworld](/wiki/Homeworld "Homeworld") by the [Trade Federation](/wiki/Trade_Federation "Trade Federation") to ensure his [election](/wiki/Supreme_Chancellor_election,_32_BBY "Supreme Chancellor election, 32 BBY") as [Supreme Chancellor](/wiki/Supreme_Chancellor "Supreme Chancellor") of the [Galactic Republic](/wiki/Galactic_Republic "Galactic Republic").[[8]](#cite_note-Skywalker:_AFaW-8) In Sidious' view, the Force had blessed the Chosen One with power unrivaled by all except for the [Dark Lord of the Sith](/wiki/Dark_Lord_of_the_Sith "Dark Lord of the Sith"). Aware of the Jedi's belief in the Chosen One's destiny, Sidious dismissed the prophecy as an incorrect interpretation of the meaning of balance in the Force.[[10]](#cite_note-SOTS-10) Skywalker's potential for power, combined with his volatile temperament and other traits that conflicted with the [Jedi Code](/wiki/Jedi_Code "Jedi Code"), made him the ideal Sith apprentice to Sidious.[[8]](#cite_note-Skywalker:_AFaW-8)

The Sith Lord used his public identity as Chancellor Palpatine to gain access to Skywalker, creating a friendship that he exploited by undermining the Padawan's commitment to the Jedi Order.[[8]](#cite_note-Skywalker:_AFaW-8) As a result, Skywalker came to harbor doubts about Jedi doctrine.[[10]](#cite_note-SOTS-10) He also grew ambitious and more arrogant due to the influence of Sidious, who assured the young Jedi that he was destined to become greater than all of the Jedi, including Yoda.[[20]](#cite_note-Episode_II-20)

The [Clone Wars](/wiki/Clone_Wars "Clone Wars") began with the [First Battle of Geonosis](/wiki/First_Battle_of_Geonosis "First Battle of Geonosis")[[20]](#cite_note-Episode_II-20) in [22 BBY](/wiki/22_BBY "22 BBY").[[2]](#cite_note-GA-2) Elevated to the status of Jedi Knight, Skywalker and his fellow Jedi became leaders in the newly formed [Grand Army of the Republic](/wiki/Grand_Army_of_the_Republic "Grand Army of the Republic"), adopting the [military rank](/wiki/Military_rank "Military rank") of [Jedi General](/wiki/Jedi_General "Jedi General"). Unlike his peers, Skywalker quickly acclimated to his new role as a [general](/wiki/General "General") in the [Republic Military](/wiki/Republic_Military "Republic Military"). He had struggled to live by the tenets of the Jedi Code despite his desire to overcome emotions that were considered unfit for a Jedi. As a [soldier](/wiki/Soldier "Soldier") on the battlefield, however, he discovered that his power increased with his anger. Although he knowingly violated the Jedi Code more than once during the war, he justified his actions by convincing himself that he was serving the greater good of the Republic. As a result of the war and Chancellor Palpatine's influence, Skywalker began to truly believe he was the Chosen One, and therefore saw himself as the galaxy's savior. Skywalker's acceptance of his new self-perception helped to justify the actions he took that broke with the Jedi way.[[8]](#cite_note-Skywalker:_AFaW-8)

Though a newly promoted Jedi Knight, Skywalker was initially reluctant to train an apprentice despite it being considered one of the Order's greatest traditions. Nevertheless, following the [Battle of Christophsis](/wiki/Battle_of_Christophsis "Battle of Christophsis"), Yoda assigned [Ahsoka Tano](/wiki/Ahsoka_Tano "Ahsoka Tano") as Skywalker's Padawan, believing he was ready to become a teacher. By instructing his own student in the Jedi arts, Yoda hoped that Skywalker would outgrow his inability to sever the emotional attachments that he formed with others.[[22]](#cite_note-TCWfilm-22) It was also Yoda's hope that in gaining a sense of maturity that came with mentoring a young apprentice, Skywalker would finally become the powerful Jedi that the prophecy foretold.[[8]](#cite_note-Skywalker:_AFaW-8)

##### A test on Mortis[]

:   "*You are the Chosen One. You have brought balance to this world. Stay on this path and you will do it again… for the galaxy. But beware… your heart…*"
:   ―The Father, to Anakin Skywalker[[23]](#cite_note-Ghosts_of_Mortis-23)

During the war, Skywalker, Kenobi and Tano were [lured to Mortis](/wiki/Mission_to_Mortis "Mission to Mortis") by the Ones. The Father sought to gauge Skywalker's strength in the Force, ultimately to determine if he really was the Chosen One, and the Son was likewise invested in discovering the Jedi Knight's true potential. Skywalker dismissed the Chosen One as a myth; nevertheless, his ability to force the Father's children into submission convinced the patriarch that Skywalker was indeed the one destined to bring balance to the Force, as only the Chosen One could tame the Son and the Daughter.[[14]](#cite_note-Overlords-14)

 [![In the realm of Mortis, Skywalker endured a series of trials that tested his power as the Chosen One.](data:image/gif;base64,R0lGODlhAQABAIABAAAAAP///yH5BAEAAAEALAAAAAABAAEAQAICTAEAOw%3D%3D)](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/starwars/images/3/32/KneelBeforeZod-Overlords.png/revision/latest?cb=20130524213632) [![In the realm of Mortis, Skywalker endured a series of trials that tested his power as the Chosen One.](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/starwars/images/3/32/KneelBeforeZod-Overlords.png/revision/latest/scale-to-width-down/250?cb=20130524213632)](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/starwars/images/3/32/KneelBeforeZod-Overlords.png/revision/latest?cb=20130524213632) 

In the realm of Mortis, Skywalker endured a series of trials that tested his power as the Chosen One.

 

With his stewardship nearing its end, the Father entreated Skywalker to succeed him in maintaining the balance on Mortis. Skywalker declined the offer, however, and the Father warned him that his selfishness would have repercussions affecting the entire galaxy.[[14]](#cite_note-Overlords-14) The Son set his own plan in motion by abducting Tano, forcing Skywalker to delay his departure in order to rescue his apprentice. Like the Father, the Son was certain that Skywalker was the Chosen One. Trapped in the realm of Mortis by his Father, the Son endeavored to turn the Chosen One to the dark side of the Force, thereby allowing him to escape into the greater galaxy. Skywalker refused under the false impression that the Son wanted him to become a Sith, to which the Son proclaimed that they would destroy the Sith as well as the Jedi.[[24]](#cite_note-Altar_of_Mortis-24)

The Son succeeded in turning Skywalker by revealing the Jedi Knight's fate as a Dark Lord of the Sith through a series of Force visions, which compelled Skywalker to join the Son in the hope of altering the future. Their plan was undone, however, when the Father erased Skywalker's knowledge of the future. Following the destruction of the Ones, Skywalker returned to the galaxy alongside Kenobi and Tano, where they discovered that no time had passed in their absence.[[23]](#cite_note-Ghosts_of_Mortis-23)

During the final [days](/wiki/Standard_day "Standard day") of the Clone Wars, Skywalker was caught in the middle of an increasingly strained relationship between the Jedi High Council and the [Galactic Senate](/wiki/Galactic_Senate "Galactic Senate")'s leader, Chancellor Palpatine. His friendship with the chancellor, as well as his appointment to the High Council's ranks by Palpatine's request, caused the Jedi elders to become more wary of Skywalker. Kenobi's faith did not waver as he was certain that Skywalker was the Chosen One whose destiny was to bring balance to the Force by destroying the Sith. Neither Windu nor Yoda shared his conviction, however, and the Grand Master began to doubt the Jedi's interpretation of the prophecy.[[4]](#cite_note-Episode_III-4)

 [![Sidious' machinations succeeded in turning Skywalker to the dark side, resulting in the creation of Darth Vader.](data:image/gif;base64,R0lGODlhAQABAIABAAAAAP///yH5BAEAAAEALAAAAAABAAEAQAICTAEAOw%3D%3D)](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/starwars/images/4/42/SithTeachings-Convergence.png/revision/latest?cb=20201129074930) [![Sidious' machinations succeeded in turning Skywalker to the dark side, resulting in the creation of Darth Vader.](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/starwars/images/4/42/SithTeachings-Convergence.png/revision/latest/scale-to-width-down/250?cb=20201129074930)](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/starwars/images/4/42/SithTeachings-Convergence.png/revision/latest?cb=20201129074930) 

Sidious' machinations succeeded in turning Skywalker to the dark side, resulting in the creation of Darth Vader.

 

Through the manipulations of Sidious, who revealed his secret identity to the Jedi, Skywalker turned against the Jedi Order and embraced the dark side of the Force as the Sith Lord Darth Vader. His first act as a Sith apprentice was to [attack the Jedi Temple](/wiki/Operation:_Knightfall "Operation: Knightfall")[[10]](#cite_note-SOTS-10) with the [501st Legion](/wiki/501st_Legion "501st Legion"). The Jedi within the Temple were massacred by their former colleague and his [legion](/wiki/Legion "Legion") of [clone troopers](/wiki/Clone_trooper "Clone trooper"). With the exception of a few survivors, the [Great Jedi Purge](/wiki/Great_Jedi_Purge "Great Jedi Purge") succeeded in destroying most of the Jedi Order through [Order 66](/wiki/Order_66 "Order 66").[[4]](#cite_note-Episode_III-4) As a result, Sidious believed that the Chosen One had indeed returned balance to the Force by releasing its dark side through the suppression of the light.[[10]](#cite_note-SOTS-10)

Though wracked by grief over the fall of his former apprentice,[[25]](#cite_note-Obi-Wan_Kenobi-25) Kenobi [confronted Vader](/wiki/Duel_on_Mustafar "Duel on Mustafar") on [Mustafar](/wiki/Mustafar "Mustafar") and emerged as the victor of their [lightsaber duel](/wiki/Lightsaber_duel "Lightsaber duel"). Kenobi exclaimed that Anakin Skywalker was the Chosen One but instead of bringing balance as foretold, he condemned the Force to darkness by joining the Sith. Having said his piece, which Vader replied with utter hatred for his former master, Kenobi left his fallen apprentice to [burn](/wiki/Fire "Fire") on the edge of a [lava](/wiki/Magma "Magma") bend. Vader managed to survive his grievous injuries and was retrieved by Sidious.[[4]](#cite_note-Episode_III-4)

Vader's condition necessitated an extensive [cybernetic reconstruction](/wiki/Cyborg "Cyborg") rendering him more machine than man. His damaged body was encased in a [life-sustaining](/wiki/Life_support "Life support") [suit of armor](/wiki/Darth_Vader%27s_armor "Darth Vader's armor").[[4]](#cite_note-Episode_III-4) Sidious knew that Vader would be weaker than before as a result of his injuries. He therefore tested his apprentice's connection to the dark side by telling Vader that he had caused the death of his [wife](/wiki/Marriage "Marriage"), [Senator](/wiki/Senator "Senator") [Padmé Amidala](/wiki/Padm%C3%A9_Amidala "Padmé Amidala"). The intensity of Vader's anguish and self-hatred revealed that he still possessed great power.[[26]](#cite_note-Sidious_Gallery-26) Vader would henceforth serve as his master's right hand, enforcing the [Emperor's](/wiki/Galactic_Emperor "Galactic Emperor") reign across the [Galactic Empire](/wiki/Galactic_Empire "Galactic Empire").[[4]](#cite_note-Episode_III-4)

With the Emperor's blessing, Darth Vader claimed Mustafar as his own and oversaw the construction of his personal residence, [Fortress Vader](/wiki/Fortress_Vader "Fortress Vader"), with the help of [Darth Momin](/wiki/Darth_Momin "Darth Momin"). Although Momin was long dead by the time of the [Imperial Era](/wiki/Imperial_Era "Imperial Era"), the ancient Sith communicated with his modern counterpart through a [mask](/wiki/Mask_of_Lord_Momin "Mask of Lord Momin") imbued with his dark essence. Having manipulated Vader into constructing the [fortress](/wiki/Fortress "Fortress") as a conduit for the power of the dark side, Momin was able to open a [portal](/wiki/Portal "Portal") in the Force which ultimately facilitated his rebirth. The two Sith Lords [battled](/wiki/Battle_of_Fortress_Vader "Battle of Fortress Vader") each other in a [cave](/wiki/Sith_cave "Sith cave") beneath the fortress, and Momin revealed that he knew about Vader's former persona Anakin Skywalker.[[27]](#cite_note-Dark_Lord_24-27)

The reborn Sith Lord was aware that the Jedi believed Skywalker to be their prophesied Chosen One, and that Vader still considered himself as such despite his fall to the dark side. Scoffing at how Vader could have been gifted with the greatest power in the galaxy, Momin derided his opponent as a broken shadow of his former self. Furthermore, Momin taunted Vader by reminding him of his late wife and how he failed to save her in spite of his potential as the Chosen One. Vader ultimately defeated Momin, resulting in the ancient Sith Lord's second death.[[27]](#cite_note-Dark_Lord_24-27)

Anakin Skywalker's fall from grace affected his former master's view of the prophecy.[[28]](#cite_note-Twin_Suns-28) Whereas Kenobi once believed his apprentice to be the Chosen One,[[4]](#cite_note-Episode_III-4) he became convinced that Vader was incapable of turning from the dark side.[[3]](#cite_note-Episode_VI-3) He therefore placed his hopes in Vader's son, [Luke Skywalker](/wiki/Luke_Skywalker "Luke Skywalker"), whom he watched over during his years in [exile](/wiki/Exile_(punishment) "Exile (punishment)") and [isolation](/wiki/Hermit "Hermit") on Tatooine. In time, Kenobi came to wonder if maybe the prophecy had been misread and Luke was truly the Chosen One, acknowledging that Luke could determine his own destiny.[[8]](#cite_note-Skywalker:_AFaW-8) When [confronted by an old nemesis](/wiki/Mission_to_Tatooine_(Imperial_Era) "Mission to Tatooine (Imperial Era)"), the former Sith Lord Maul, Kenobi brought an end to [decades](/wiki/Standard_decade "Standard decade") of rivalry by striking down the [Dathomirian](/wiki/Dathomirian "Dathomirian"). With his last breath, Maul asked Kenobi if he was protecting the Chosen One, to which the Jedi Master said he was, having come to believe that the prophecy would be fulfilled by Luke instead of Anakin. With that, Maul died believing that the victims of the Sith would be avenged.[[28]](#cite_note-Twin_Suns-28)

Luke Skywalker's instruction in the Jedi arts began during the [Galactic Civil War](/wiki/Galactic_Civil_War "Galactic Civil War"). By then the Jedi were all but extinct as a result of the Great Jedi Purge[[29]](#cite_note-Episode_IV-29) that began with Order 66.[[4]](#cite_note-Episode_III-4) Kenobi served as Luke's first master but was [killed](/wiki/Rescue_of_Princess_Leia "Rescue of Princess Leia") by Vader soon after, cutting short the younger Skywalker's apprenticeship. But in learning the secret to [immortality](/wiki/Immortality "Immortality"), Kenobi was able to [retain his identity in the Force](/wiki/Force_spirit "Force spirit") after death. During the [Battle of Yavin](/wiki/Battle_of_Yavin "Battle of Yavin"), he communed with Luke and told him to trust in the Force. Guided by Kenobi's spirit, Luke saved the [Alliance to Restore the Republic](/wiki/Alliance_to_Restore_the_Republic "Alliance to Restore the Republic") by destroying the [DS-1 Death Star Mobile Battle Station](/wiki/DS-1_Orbital_Battle_Station "DS-1 Orbital Battle Station").[[29]](#cite_note-Episode_IV-29)

Luke's next mentor was Grand Master Yoda who, despite deeming the son as reckless and full of anger like his father, agreed to complete the Jedi hopeful's training in the ways of the Force. Despite the progress that Luke made, he chose to leave [Dagobah](/wiki/Dagobah "Dagobah") before his training was complete. Through the Force, Luke beheld visions of the future that led him to discover that his friends were in danger. Determined to help them, he disregarded Yoda's warning that only a fully-trained Jedi could defeat the Sith. Luke's decision led to his [confrontation with Vader](/wiki/Duel_on_Cloud_City "Duel on Cloud City") at [Cloud City](/wiki/Cloud_City "Cloud City"), where he ultimately learned the truth of his parentage[[30]](#cite_note-Episode_V-30) in [3 ABY](/wiki/3_ABY "3 ABY").[[2]](#cite_note-GA-2)

Vader's failure to save the [Death Star](/wiki/Death_Star "Death Star") caused Sidious to mock him with his title as the Chosen One. The Emperor castigated his apprentice for being the only one who survived the [battle station](/wiki/Battle_station "Battle station")'s destruction. Sidious demeaned Vader's past as the Jedi Order's prophesied Chosen One, calling him "the one chosen to be held responsible" for the Empire's disastrous loss in the [Yavin system](/wiki/Yavin_system "Yavin system"). To this, Vader vowed to correct his mistakes, ignoring the taunts of his master.[[31]](#cite_note-Darth_Vader_1-31) However, Vader was punished for his actions after the [Battle of Hoth](/wiki/Battle_of_Hoth "Battle of Hoth"), having failed to prevent his son's escape on [Bespin](/wiki/Bespin "Bespin"). In addition, he failed to kill [Sabé](/wiki/Sab%C3%A9 "Sabé"), who physically resembled the late Amidala, and ignored his master's orders to report to him on Coruscant. Sidious overpowered Vader with the Force, using his apprentice's own [method of punishment](/wiki/Force_choke "Force choke") to [torture](/wiki/Torture "Torture") him. As Vader struggled in vain to resist, Sidious asked how the Chosen One could be so weak, helpless and fraught with fear when he was destined to bring the Force into balance. The Emperor wondered who chose the Chosen One, and if they could choose another. Sidious compared Vader to a tool that he would discard when it was no longer useful.[[32]](#cite_note-Darth_Vader_2020_6-32)

Having narrowly escaped from his father,[[30]](#cite_note-Episode_V-30) Luke eventually returned to Dagobah, where a dying Yoda confessed that Vader was indeed Luke's father. The revelation that Vader and Anakin were one and the same sowed conflict within Luke, as he was unwilling to kill his own father. Armed with this knowledge, he believed that a remnant of his father's former self still existed within the shell of Vader. Neither Kenobi nor Yoda shared Luke's sentiment, however. Vader was more machine than alive, according to Kenobi, who saw his one-time pupil as a being who was "twisted and evil." Yoda likewise warned Luke that those who started down the path of the dark side would always be dominated by its power. Luke ultimately confronted Vader once more, as his masters told him he must, yet he remained steadfast in his belief that Vader could be redeemed. As the late Jedi Masters predicted, the Emperor used Luke's feelings to his advantage, having lured Luke to the [DS-2 Death Star II Mobile Battle Station](/wiki/DS-2_Death_Star_II_Mobile_Battle_Station "DS-2 Death Star II Mobile Battle Station"), where he sought to turn the young Jedi to the dark side during the [Battle of Endor](/wiki/Battle_of_Endor "Battle of Endor")[[3]](#cite_note-Episode_VI-3) in [4 ABY](/wiki/4_ABY "4 ABY").[[2]](#cite_note-GA-2)

Luke's power increased with his hatred,[[3]](#cite_note-Episode_VI-3) and through his rage[[34]](#cite_note-Luke_DB-34) he defeated his father in a lightsaber duel. Sidious betrayed Vader and attempted to replace him with Luke as his new apprentice.[[3]](#cite_note-Episode_VI-3) However, Luke restrained himself at the last moment and, having defeated Vader and resisted the temptation of the dark side, claimed the mantle of Jedi Knight. His actions confounded the Emperor, who attacked Luke with a barrage of [Force lightning](/wiki/Force_lightning "Force lightning").[[34]](#cite_note-Luke_DB-34) Unable to defend himself, Luke begged his father for help while the Emperor slowly [tortured](/wiki/Torture "Torture") him to death.[[3]](#cite_note-Episode_VI-3) Unwilling to see his son die, Vader gave his life to save Luke, thereby dying as Anakin Skywalker.[[35]](#cite_note-Anakin_Skywalker-35) Anakin ultimately fulfilled his role as the Chosen One[[7]](#cite_note-TSWB-7) and brought balance to the Force,[[36]](#cite_note-Beware_the_Power_of_the_Dark_Side!-36)[[37]](#cite_note-Luke_Skywalker-37) fulfilling Qui-Gon Jinn's belief in the prophecy that foretold Anakin's destiny.[[1]](#cite_note-Episode_I-1)

### Legacy[]

#### Darth Vader's heir[]

:   "*For many years, there was balance and then I saw… Ben. My nephew with that mighty Skywalker blood. And in my hubris, I thought I could train him, I could pass on my strengths.*"
:   ―Luke Skywalker[[38]](#cite_note-Episode_VIII-38)

The Jedi prophecy foretold the destruction of the Sith, but it never predicted the end of darkness.[[39]](#cite_note-TLJ:TVD-39) Although Anakin Skywalker brought the Force back into balance,[[36]](#cite_note-Beware_the_Power_of_the_Dark_Side!-36) the Jedi Order was decimated by his actions as Darth Vader,[[29]](#cite_note-Episode_IV-29) leaving Luke Skywalker as the last of the Jedi.[[3]](#cite_note-Episode_VI-3) Despite the fall of the Sith and the restoration of balance in the Force,[[36]](#cite_note-Beware_the_Power_of_the_Dark_Side!-36) the dark side endured as remnants of the former Galactic Empire reorganized into the [First Order](/wiki/First_Order "First Order") under [Supreme Leader](/wiki/Supreme_Leader "Supreme Leader") [Snoke](/wiki/Snoke "Snoke"),[[40]](#cite_note-Episode_VII-40) a Force-sensitive [being](/wiki/Humanoid "Humanoid") who was strong with the dark side.[[41]](#cite_note-Snoke-41)

 [![Ben Solo was Anakin Skywalker's grandson, but as Kylo Ren he vowed to destroy the Jedi Order, fulfilling the legacy of Darth Vader.](data:image/gif;base64,R0lGODlhAQABAIABAAAAAP///yH5BAEAAAEALAAAAAABAAEAQAICTAEAOw%3D%3D)](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/starwars/images/7/79/Kylo_Ren_Vader_Helmet_Chamber.jpg/revision/latest?cb=20160420020932) [![Ben Solo was Anakin Skywalker's grandson, but as Kylo Ren he vowed to destroy the Jedi Order, fulfilling the legacy of Darth Vader.](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/starwars/images/7/79/Kylo_Ren_Vader_Helmet_Chamber.jpg/revision/latest/scale-to-width-down/250?cb=20160420020932)](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/starwars/images/7/79/Kylo_Ren_Vader_Helmet_Chamber.jpg/revision/latest?cb=20160420020932) 

Ben Solo was Anakin Skywalker's grandson, but as Kylo Ren he vowed to destroy the Jedi Order, fulfilling the legacy of Darth Vader.

 

A new generation of Jedi was trained by Luke, who saw his efforts [destroyed](/wiki/Destruction_of_the_Jedi_Temple "Destruction of the Jedi Temple") by his nephew [Ben Solo](/wiki/Ben_Solo "Ben Solo")'s fall to the dark side of the Force.[[40]](#cite_note-Episode_VII-40) Throughout his youth, Solo struggled with the expectations of being Luke's nephew and the grandson of a prophesied Chosen One.[[42]](#cite_note-TROS_VD-42) As Luke felt, there was a balance in the Force for many years until he saw his nephew.[[38]](#cite_note-Episode_VIII-38) Falling under Snoke's influence, Solo renounced his future as a Jedi and adopted the identity of Kylo Ren, becoming the Supreme Leader's apprentice and a [warlord](/wiki/Warlord "Warlord") of the First Order.[[42]](#cite_note-TROS_VD-42) He also became [master](/wiki/Ren "Ren") of the [Knights of Ren](/wiki/Knights_of_Ren "Knights of Ren"),[[40]](#cite_note-Episode_VII-40)—an elite group of Force warriors that followed Snoke and the ways of the dark side.[[43]](#cite_note-CE-43) Ren saw himself as the rightful heir to the tradition embodied by his fallen grandfather;[[42]](#cite_note-TROS_VD-42) as such, he endeavored to be as strong as Vader.[[44]](#cite_note-Kylo_Ren-44)

Luke disappeared soon after his nephew fell to the dark side,[[34]](#cite_note-Luke_DB-34) leaving only fragments of a [map](/wiki/Map_to_Skywalker "Map to Skywalker") that revealed the [location](/wiki/Ahch-To "Ahch-To") of his self-imposed exile. During the [Cold War](/wiki/Cold_War "Cold War"), Ren's mother, [Princess](/wiki/Princess "Princess") [Leia Organa](/wiki/Leia_Skywalker_Organa_Solo "Leia Skywalker Organa Solo"), endeavored to find her brother and recruit him to fight against the First Order.[[40]](#cite_note-Episode_VII-40) [Lor San Tekka](/wiki/Lor_San_Tekka "Lor San Tekka"), a member of the [Church of the Force](/wiki/Church_of_the_Force "Church of the Force"),[[45]](#cite_note-TFA_VD-45) provided Organa's movement, the [Resistance](/wiki/Resistance "Resistance"), with the last piece of the map to Skywalker.[[40]](#cite_note-Episode_VII-40) San Tekka was a follower of the Jedi religion, despite his lack of Force-sensitivity,[[45]](#cite_note-TFA_VD-45) and believed that the Jedi were needed to maintain balance in the Force.[[40]](#cite_note-Episode_VII-40)

#### Balance preserved[]

Ren, having claimed the position of Supreme Leader by [assassinating](/wiki/Battle_of_Oetchi "Battle of Oetchi") Snoke, tracked the phantom Sith Lord to the distant world of [Exegol](/wiki/Exegol "Exegol"). Though he was determined to eliminate Sidious as a threat to his reign, the former Emperor unveiled a [powerful military](/wiki/Sith_Eternal_military "Sith Eternal military") that would allow the Sith to be reborn and Ren to project First Order might across the galaxy, ruling all of it as Sidious' successor. In return, he urged Ren to kill [Rey](/wiki/Rey_Skywalker "Rey Skywalker"), a Jedi apprentice who was [sired](/wiki/Dathan "Dathan") from Sidious' own [bloodline](/wiki/Palpatine_family "Palpatine family").[[46]](#cite_note-Episode_IX-46)

Ren, however, had grown close to Rey by virtue of their unique [Force-bond](/wiki/Force-bond "Force-bond").[[42]](#cite_note-TROS_VD-42) Having learned that they were each one half of a[[46]](#cite_note-Episode_IX-46) [prophesied](/wiki/Doctrine_of_the_Dyad "Doctrine of the Dyad")[[42]](#cite_note-TROS_VD-42) [Force dyad](/wiki/Force_dyad "Force dyad"), Ren beseeched the former [scavenger](/wiki/Scavenger "Scavenger") to take her place by his side, believing they could kill Rey's grandfather and claim the [Throne of the Sith](/wiki/Throne_of_the_Sith "Throne of the Sith") together. But after Ren [renounced](/wiki/Mission_to_Kef_Bir "Mission to Kef Bir") the dark side, restoring the persona of Ben Solo, Sidious pressed forward by attempting to seduce Rey to the dark side. Using his [new fleet](/wiki/Final_Order "Final Order") to [threaten](/wiki/Battle_of_Exegol "Battle of Exegol") Rey's friends, Sidious almost forced Rey into striking him down, which would allow him to possess her body but for the intervention of Solo, who returned to Exegol to fight at Rey's side.[[46]](#cite_note-Episode_IX-46)

## Behind the scenes[]

### Development[]

:   "*Whether it's Hindu, Chinese or Middle Eastern, all the mythological heroes didn't have fathers. The fathers were the gods.*"
:   ―George Lucas[[48]](#cite_note-48)

The prophecy of the Chosen One, who will bring balance to the Force was introduced in the [1999](/wiki/1999 "1999") film, [*Star Wars*: Episode I *The Phantom Menace*](/wiki/Star_Wars:_Episode_I_The_Phantom_Menace "Star Wars: Episode I The Phantom Menace"), the first installment of the [prequel trilogy](/wiki/Prequel_trilogy "Prequel trilogy"). The Chosen One was revealed to be Anakin Skywalker, who conceived by the Force itself via the mystical one-celled organisms called midi-chlorians [completely without outside influence](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Miraculous_births).[[1]](#cite_note-Episode_I-1)

### Fan debate[]

#### Father or Son? Or Daughter?[]

Though there was some debate among the fans that Anakin Skywalker's son, Luke Skywalker, was actually the Chosen One since he caused his father to destroy Darth Sidious, the debate was settled when George Lucas himself confirmed in an interview that Anakin, even after becoming Darth Vader, was still officially the Chosen One, who brings Balance by ending the Sith rule over the galaxy.[[5]](#cite_note-Featurette-5) *[Darth Vader: Sith Lord](/wiki/Darth_Vader:_Sith_Lord "Darth Vader: Sith Lord")*, a [canon](/wiki/Canon "Canon") reference title, also states explicitly that "Anakin had fulfilled the destiny of the Chosen One."[[49]](#cite_note-Darth_Vader:_Sith_Lord-49)

Nevertheless, based on material Lucas was involved in, the prophecy of the Chosen One was not necessary limited to a single person. In the episode of the Clone Wars TV show titled "[Destiny](/wiki/Destiny_(The_Clone_Wars) "Destiny (The Clone Wars)")," Yoda was told that he will train "who will save the galaxy from a great imbalance", referring to Luke Skywalker.[[50]](#cite_note-Destiny-50) Furthermore, when he discussed his original plans for the [sequel trilogy](/wiki/Sequel_trilogy "Sequel trilogy"), Lucas stated that Princess Leia would become the Chancellor of the restored Republic, elaborating that she "ended up being the Chosen One".[[51]](#cite_note-SWArchives-51)

#### Anakin's birth[]

A popular fan theory also emerged, speculating that Anakin Skywalker may have been actually the result of [Darth Plagueis](/wiki/Darth_Plagueis "Darth Plagueis")' experiments, an ancient Sith, who was allegedly able to create life and defeat death by manipulating midi-chlorians, and whose story was introduced in the [2005](/wiki/2005 "2005") film, [*Star Wars*: Episode III *Revenge of the Sith*](/wiki/Star_Wars:_Episode_III_Revenge_of_the_Sith "Star Wars: Episode III Revenge of the Sith").[[4]](#cite_note-Episode_III-4) The theory also heavily built on a scrapped scene from the early draft of the script for Episode III, which revealed Darth Sidious as Anakin's creator.[[52]](#cite_note-The_Making_of_Star_Wars_Revenge_of_the_Sith-52) The [2012](/wiki/2012 "2012") [*Star Wars* Legends](/wiki/Star_Wars_Legends "Star Wars Legends") novel [*Darth Plagueis*](/wiki/Darth_Plagueis_(novel) "Darth Plagueis (novel)") combined both interpretations of Skywalker's birth stating that [Plagueis](/wiki/Darth_Plagueis/Legends "Darth Plagueis/Legends") was indirectly responsible for [Anakin's](/wiki/Anakin_Skywalker/Legends "Anakin Skywalker/Legends") creation yet [the Force](/wiki/The_Force/Legends "The Force/Legends") conceived Anakin as a way to stop the [Sith](/wiki/Sith/Legends "Sith/Legends").[[53]](#cite_note-DP-53)

George Lucas himself initially said the mystery of the Chosen One's birth is up to the audience to interpret, since it bears no relevance to the outcome of the events.[[5]](#cite_note-Featurette-5) However, in the interview given to Paul Duncan, author of the book *Star Wars Archives: Episodes I-III, 1999-2005* he clearly stated that Anakin was created by the Will of the Force by the midi-chlorians, verifying the circumstances of Anakin's birth proposed in Episode I.[[51]](#cite_note-SWArchives-51)

#### Restoring Balance[]

With the release of the [2019](/wiki/2019 "2019") film, [*Star Wars*: Episode IX *The Rise of Skywalker*](/wiki/Star_Wars:_Episode_IX_The_Rise_of_Skywalker "Star Wars: Episode IX The Rise of Skywalker") and the revelation that Darth Sidious returned from the dead, some fans question whether or not Anakin Skywalker did fulfill the Chosen One prophecy and brought balance to the Force or if the prophecy referred to Rey.[[54]](#cite_note-TROS_Chosen-54) While the prophecy predicted the end of the Sith but not of the dark side of the Force,[[39]](#cite_note-TLJ:TVD-39) Sidious' return demonstrates that the Sith Order wasn't technically destroyed until Rey kills Sidious with the help of all past Jedi, finally settling the ancient conflict of the Jedi and the Sith. However, in the film, Sidious did confirm his death and the Sith's destruction at Anakin's hands as he wanted the Sith to be reborn. Later, Anakin's spirit himself told Rey that he had already brought balance to the Force before and ultimately, Rey prevented the Sith's rebirth to keep the Force balanced.[[46]](#cite_note-Episode_IX-46) In *[The Art of Star Wars: The Rise of Skywalker](/wiki/The_Art_of_Star_Wars:_The_Rise_of_Skywalker "The Art of Star Wars: The Rise of Skywalker")*, art director [Rick Carter](/wiki/Rick_Carter "Rick Carter") expressed his opinion that Rey was the one who fulfilled the prophecy rather than Anakin, stating that while that one would be a Skywalker, it didn't necessarily need to be one by blood.[[55]](#cite_note-Art_of_TROS-55)

In the [2017](/wiki/2017 "2017") film, [*Star Wars*: Episode VIII *The Last Jedi*](/wiki/Star_Wars:_Episode_VIII_The_Last_Jedi "Star Wars: Episode VIII The Last Jedi"), Luke Skywalker notes that there was a balance in the Force for many years until Ben Solo fell to the dark side.[[38]](#cite_note-Episode_VIII-38) It's indeed stated in *The Rise of Skywalker* that Anakin brought balance to the Force at least for a time, as he asks Rey to bring back the balance like he once did.[[46]](#cite_note-Episode_IX-46) The reference book *[Star Wars: The Last Jedi: The Visual Dictionary](/wiki/Star_Wars:_The_Last_Jedi:_The_Visual_Dictionary "Star Wars: The Last Jedi: The Visual Dictionary")* furthermore clarifies that the Chosen One prophecy just foretold the destruction of the Sith, but not of the dark side of the Force itself, explaining the continued existence of dark side users such as Kylo Ren and Snoke.[[39]](#cite_note-TLJ:TVD-39)

According to [Chris Terrio](/wiki/Chris_Terrio "Chris Terrio"), in George Lucas' words, the balance of the Force means that the dark and the light sides of the Force exist together,[[56]](#cite_note-Terrio_misinterpretation-56) Lucas himself said to writers of the *[Star Wars: The Clone Wars](/wiki/Star_Wars:_The_Clone_Wars "Star Wars: The Clone Wars")* TV series that to go to the dark side, an inherent element of individuals that must be overcome through discipline, was to imbalance the Force and referred to Balance as the light keeping the darkness in check, so one won't go to the dark side.[[57]](#cite_note-GL_Force-57)[[51]](#cite_note-SWArchives-51) Terrio stated that Rey has achieved a balance for the current moment,[[56]](#cite_note-Terrio_misinterpretation-56) and [J.J. Abrams](/wiki/J.J._Abrams "J.J. Abrams") affirmed this to be the case in the feature-length documentary "[The Skywalker Legacy](/wiki/The_Skywalker_Legacy "The Skywalker Legacy")", clarifying that the balance in the Force is never permanent and has to be restored from time to time.[[58]](#cite_note-TSL-58)
