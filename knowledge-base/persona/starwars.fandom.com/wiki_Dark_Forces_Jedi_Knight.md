***Dark Forces/Jedi Knight*** is a multimedia project centered on a series of [video games](/wiki/Star_Wars_video_games "Star Wars video games") following the adventures of [Kyle Katarn](/wiki/Kyle_Katarn "Kyle Katarn"), [Mara Jade](/wiki/Mara_Jade_Skywalker "Mara Jade Skywalker") and [Jaden Korr](/wiki/Jaden_Korr "Jaden Korr"). The *Dark Forces* games have multimedia tie-ins, while the *Jedi Knight* games aren't related to any other products.

## History ‡[]

*Dark Forces* debuted with the [first-person shooter](/wiki/Star_Wars_video_games "Star Wars video games") *[Star Wars: Dark Forces](/wiki/Star_Wars:_Dark_Forces "Star Wars: Dark Forces")* in [1995](/wiki/1995 "1995"), by [LucasArts](/wiki/LucasArts "LucasArts"), and introduced the character [Kyle Katarn](/wiki/Kyle_Katarn "Kyle Katarn"). It was followed by a sequel, *[Star Wars: Jedi Knight: Dark Forces II](/wiki/Star_Wars:_Jedi_Knight:_Dark_Forces_II "Star Wars: Jedi Knight: Dark Forces II")*, in [1997](/wiki/1997 "1997"), which saw the *Dark Forces* title slide to a subtitle as the series adopted the new *Jedi Knight* main title, mirroring the evolution of Katarn from a spy operative in the original game to a [Jedi Knight](/wiki/Jedi_Knight/Legends "Jedi Knight/Legends") in the sequel, which featured [lightsabers](/wiki/Lightsaber/Legends "Lightsaber/Legends") and [Force powers](/wiki/Force_power/Legends "Force power/Legends"). It was followed in [1998](/wiki/1998 "1998") by an expansion, *[Star Wars: Jedi Knight: Mysteries of the Sith](/wiki/Star_Wars:_Jedi_Knight:_Mysteries_of_the_Sith "Star Wars: Jedi Knight: Mysteries of the Sith")*, with [Mara Jade](/wiki/Mara_Jade_Skywalker "Mara Jade Skywalker") as the main character.

Between 1997 and 1998, the first two games were adapted by [William C. Dietz](/wiki/William_C._Dietz "William C. Dietz") into [*Star Wars: Dark Forces* novellas](/wiki/Star_Wars:_Dark_Forces_(novellas) "Star Wars: Dark Forces (novellas)"): *[Dark Forces: Soldier for the Empire](/wiki/Dark_Forces:_Soldier_for_the_Empire "Dark Forces: Soldier for the Empire")*, *[Dark Forces: Rebel Agent](/wiki/Dark_Forces:_Rebel_Agent "Dark Forces: Rebel Agent")* and *[Dark Forces: Jedi Knight](/wiki/Dark_Forces:_Jedi_Knight "Dark Forces: Jedi Knight")*. The novellas followed the events of the game as well as providing an extended view of the background of the series.

With a second sequel, *[Star Wars: Jedi Knight II: Jedi Outcast](/wiki/Star_Wars:_Jedi_Knight_II:_Jedi_Outcast "Star Wars: Jedi Knight II: Jedi Outcast")*, the game series abandoned the *Dark Forces* title. Released in [2002](/wiki/2002 "2002"), it was the first time the series departed from internal development at [LucasArts](/wiki/LucasArts "LucasArts") and instead was developed by independent developer [Raven Software](/wiki/Raven_Software "Raven Software"). In [2003](/wiki/2003 "2003"), Raven also released a fourth entry to the series, *[Star Wars: Jedi Knight: Jedi Academy](/wiki/Star_Wars:_Jedi_Knight:_Jedi_Academy "Star Wars: Jedi Knight: Jedi Academy")*, this time focusing on the new character of [Jaden Korr](/wiki/Jaden_Korr "Jaden Korr"). The LucasArts website posted a series of [in-universe](/wiki/In-universe "In-universe") stories, the *[N.R.I. Reports](/wiki/N.R.I._Reports "N.R.I. Reports")*, which provided some background to the then upcoming game.

In [2005](/wiki/2005 "2005"), [roleplaying game](/wiki/Roleplaying_game "Roleplaying game") publisher [Wizards of the Coast](/wiki/Wizards_of_the_Coast "Wizards of the Coast") released two series of web supplements focusing on the *Dark Forces* storyline, written by [Abel G. Peña](/wiki/Abel_G._Pe%C3%B1a "Abel G. Peña") and [Jason Fry](/wiki/Jason_Fry "Jason Fry"). *[The Dark Forces Saga](/wiki/The_Dark_Forces_Saga "The Dark Forces Saga")* provided players with rules and background information for the *[Star Wars Roleplaying Game](/wiki/Star_Wars_Roleplaying_Game "Star Wars Roleplaying Game")*, while *[Kyle Katarn's Tale](/wiki/Kyle_Katarn%27s_Tale "Kyle Katarn's Tale")* did the same for [*Star Wars* Miniatures](/wiki/Star_Wars_Miniatures "Star Wars Miniatures").

Two rejected concepts for future games in the series include *Jedi Knight III: Brink of Darkness* and *Jedi Master*.[[8]](#cite_note-Rogue_Leaders-8) *Jedi Master* was one of the many video-game concepts LucasArts considered for what would become *[Star Wars: The Force Unleashed](/wiki/Star_Wars:_The_Force_Unleashed "Star Wars: The Force Unleashed")*. It was to be a survival horror game—a much darker, more violent, and more mature continuation of the *Jedi Knight* series set during the [Great Jedi Purge](/wiki/Great_Jedi_Purge/Legends "Great Jedi Purge/Legends") immediately following [*Star Wars*: Episode III *Revenge of the Sith*](/wiki/Star_Wars:_Episode_III_Revenge_of_the_Sith "Star Wars: Episode III Revenge of the Sith"). The concept was originally put out to potential consumers to test the demand for both another *Jedi Knight* game and a more mature *Star Wars* game, but both the consumers and the developers felt the concept was too gratuitous and derivative, so it was thrown out.[[9]](#cite_note-Making_of_TFU-9)

On [August 23](/wiki/August_23 "August 23"), [2023](/wiki/2023 "2023") [Nightdive Studios](/wiki/Nightdive_Studios "Nightdive Studios") announced *[Star Wars: Dark Forces Remaster](/wiki/Star_Wars:_Dark_Forces_Remaster "Star Wars: Dark Forces Remaster")*, a remastered version of the original *Star Wars: Dark Forces* video game with enhanced graphics, redone cutscenes, behind-the-scenes content and the demo previously playable at Consumer Electronics Show in 1994. The remastered game was released on [February 28](/wiki/February_28 "February 28"), [2024](/wiki/2024 "2024").[[10]](#cite_note-SW.com-10)

## Continuity[]

 [![Dark Forces logo](data:image/gif;base64,R0lGODlhAQABAIABAAAAAP///yH5BAEAAAEALAAAAAABAAEAQAICTAEAOw%3D%3D)](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/starwars/images/c/c1/DFlogo.jpg/revision/latest?cb=20070318200808) [![Dark Forces logo](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/starwars/images/c/c1/DFlogo.jpg/revision/latest/scale-to-width-down/250?cb=20070318200808)](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/starwars/images/c/c1/DFlogo.jpg/revision/latest?cb=20070318200808) 

*Dark Forces* logo

 

These works were the first video games (years before *[Star Wars: Knights of the Old Republic](/wiki/Star_Wars:_Knights_of_the_Old_Republic "Star Wars: Knights of the Old Republic")*) that had new elements referenced by subsequent [Expanded Universe](/wiki/Star_Wars_Legends "Star Wars Legends") sources and [retcons](/wiki/Retroactive_continuity "Retroactive continuity"), transitioned afterwards to the wider Expanded Universe corpus, thus creating several important and integral concepts in the *Star Wars* continuity and the [galactic history](/wiki/Timeline_of_galactic_history/Legends "Timeline of galactic history/Legends"). Examples include some [Force powers](/wiki/Force_power/Legends "Force power/Legends"), the [dark troopers](/wiki/Dark_Trooper/Legends "Dark Trooper/Legends"), the [Battle of Ruusan](/wiki/Seventh_Battle_of_Ruusan "Seventh Battle of Ruusan"), [Prophets of the Dark Side](/wiki/Prophets_of_the_Dark_Side "Prophets of the Dark Side"), [Dromund Kaas](/wiki/Dromund_Kaas/Legends "Dromund Kaas/Legends"), and the [Reborn](/wiki/Reborn "Reborn").
