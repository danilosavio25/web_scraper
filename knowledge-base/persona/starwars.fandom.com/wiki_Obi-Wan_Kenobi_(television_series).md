***Obi-Wan Kenobi***, marketed as ***Star Wars: Obi-Wan Kenobi***, is a live-action television miniseries developed by [Lucasfilm](/wiki/Lucasfilm "Lucasfilm"). It premiered on [Disney+](/wiki/Disney%2B "Disney+") on [May 26](/wiki/May_26 "May 26"), [2022](/wiki/2022 "2022") and concluded on [June 22](/wiki/June_22 "June 22"), 2022. The series features [Ewan McGregor](/wiki/Ewan_McGregor "Ewan McGregor") and [Hayden Christensen](/wiki/Hayden_Christensen "Hayden Christensen") reprising their roles from the *[Star Wars](/wiki/Star_Wars "Star Wars")* [prequel trilogy](/wiki/Prequel_trilogy "Prequel trilogy"), respectively [Obi-Wan Kenobi](/wiki/Obi-Wan_Kenobi "Obi-Wan Kenobi") and [Darth Vader](/wiki/Anakin_Skywalker "Anakin Skywalker"). It takes place ten years after the events of [*Star Wars*: Episode III *Revenge of the Sith*](/wiki/Star_Wars:_Episode_III_Revenge_of_the_Sith "Star Wars: Episode III Revenge of the Sith") and nine years before the events of [*Star Wars*: Episode IV *A New Hope*](/wiki/Star_Wars:_Episode_IV_A_New_Hope "Star Wars: Episode IV A New Hope").

## Development[]

### As a film[]

:   "*It wasn't always going to be a series, not initially. When we first started talking about, that wasn't really in the cards, but everything's changed so much, so quickly. It's really exciting that it is now. I'm really into the idea to be able to tell the story over several hours instead of just one hit. I think it's going to be quite cool.*"
:   ―Ewan McGregor on how the series evolved from a film[[13]](#cite_note-When_Was_Film-13)

 [![Ewan McGregor first appeared as Obi-Wan Kenobi in .](data:image/gif;base64,R0lGODlhAQABAIABAAAAAP///yH5BAEAAAEALAAAAAABAAEAQAICTAEAOw%3D%3D)](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/starwars/images/a/a4/Obi-Wan_Kenobi_EP1.png/revision/latest?cb=20200516173704) [![Ewan McGregor first appeared as Obi-Wan Kenobi in .](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/starwars/images/a/a4/Obi-Wan_Kenobi_EP1.png/revision/latest/scale-to-width-down/175?cb=20200516173704)](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/starwars/images/a/a4/Obi-Wan_Kenobi_EP1.png/revision/latest?cb=20200516173704) 

Ewan McGregor first appeared as Obi-Wan Kenobi in [1999](/wiki/1999 "1999").

 

In [2013](/wiki/2013 "2013"), news was released that [The Walt Disney Company](/wiki/The_Walt_Disney_Company "The Walt Disney Company")-[Lucasfilm](/wiki/Lucasfilm "Lucasfilm") brand was developing several theatrical pictures separate from the [core, episodic](/wiki/Star_Wars_saga "Star Wars saga") *[Star Wars](/wiki/Star_Wars "Star Wars")* saga, which were later denominated as the *[Star Wars Anthology Series](/wiki/Star_Wars_Anthology_Series "Star Wars Anthology Series")* line.[[14]](#cite_note-SW.com_SW_Spinoffs-14) Talks about a potential Obi-Wan Kenobi-centric movie as part of the Anthology series arose, and [Ewan McGregor](/wiki/Ewan_McGregor "Ewan McGregor"), the actor who played Kenobi in the *Star Wars* [prequel trilogy](/wiki/Prequel_trilogy "Prequel trilogy"), was approached to reprise the role.[[13]](#cite_note-When_Was_Film-13) However, McGregor knew about the project as far back as [2015](/wiki/2015 "2015"),[[15]](#cite_note-CBR-15) and until [2019](/wiki/2019 "2019"), he was forced to withhold information in interviews.[[16]](#cite_note-MensJournal-16)

*[Rogue One: A Star Wars Story](/wiki/Rogue_One:_A_Star_Wars_Story "Rogue One: A Star Wars Story")*, the first installment of the Anthology Series, was released in [December](/wiki/December "December") [2016](/wiki/2016 "2016") and was well received both critically and commercially.[[17]](#cite_note-No_Film-17) In [August](/wiki/August "August") [2017](/wiki/2017 "2017"), sources from [*The Hollywood Reporter*](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/The_Hollywood_Reporter "wikipedia:The Hollywood Reporter") reported that an Anthology film centered on Obi-Wan Kenobi starring McGregor was in development by Lucasfilm, with [*Billy Elliot*](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Billy_Elliot "wikipedia:Billy Elliot") director [Stephen Daldry](/wiki/Stephen_Daldry "Stephen Daldry") in early talks to direct and oversee the project.[[18]](#cite_note-18) [Joel Edgerton](/wiki/Joel_Edgerton "Joel Edgerton"), who portrayed [Owen Lars](/wiki/Owen_Lars "Owen Lars") in the prequel trilogy, had previously expressed interest on reprising his role in a spin-off film about Kenobi.[[19]](#cite_note-19) Once he was hired to helm the film, Daldry contacted [Hossein Amini](/wiki/Hossein_Amini "Hossein Amini") to talk about the possibility of joining the project.[[20]](#cite_note-20)

However, all *Star Wars* stand-alone spin-off films were put on hold following the lower-than-expected box office returns from *[Solo: A Star Wars Story](/wiki/Solo:_A_Star_Wars_Story "Solo: A Star Wars Story")*, the second Anthology film, in [May](/wiki/May "May") [2018](/wiki/2018 "2018").[[17]](#cite_note-No_Film-17) [Four months later](/wiki/September "September"), Disney [CEO](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Chief_executive_officer "wikipedia:Chief executive officer") [Bob Iger](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bob_Iger "wikipedia:Bob Iger") revealed that Lucasfilm had reevaluated its release schedule in the wake of the poor reception of *Solo*.[[21]](#cite_note-21) It was later confirmed that there will be a hiatus on *Star Wars* following the release of [*Star Wars*: Episode IX *The Rise of Skywalker*](/wiki/Star_Wars:_Episode_IX_The_Rise_of_Skywalker "Star Wars: Episode IX The Rise of Skywalker") until [2022](/wiki/2022 "2022").[[22]](#cite_note-22) Despite this, former UK Foreign Secretary [Boris Johnson](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Boris_Johnson "wikipedia:Boris Johnson") was quoted in [November](/wiki/November "November") 2018 as saying that the filmmakers of the Obi-Wan Kenobi film had proposed shooting it in Northern Ireland.[[23]](#cite_note-23) *The Hollywood Reporter* had previously voted it in a 2016 poll as the most wanted Anthology film by fans.[[24]](#cite_note-24)

### As a series[]

:   "*I want to get closer and closer to how Obi-Wan felt while Alec Guinness was playing him. I feel like I'm grayer and nearer him in age, so it'll be easier to do that.*"
:   ―Ewan McGregor on playing Obi-Wan in the series[[16]](#cite_note-MensJournal-16)

[Stuart Beattie](/wiki/Stuart_Beattie "Stuart Beattie") was hired by Lucasfilm to write a script for the Obi-Wan movie.[[25]](#cite_note-Trilogy_Was_Planned-25) McGregor then participated in a costume test for the planned film.[[26]](#cite_note-costume_test-26) However, at some point in development, the film project turned into a television series instead.[[13]](#cite_note-When_Was_Film-13) Beattie's script—the first of a planned trilogy—served as the basis for the the series,[[25]](#cite_note-Trilogy_Was_Planned-25) which was officially announced by McGregor and Lucasfilm President [Kathleen Kennedy](/wiki/Kathleen_Kennedy "Kathleen Kennedy") at the 2019 [D23 Expo](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/D23_(Disney) "wikipedia:D23 (Disney)") after months of rumors and speculation.[[10]](#cite_note-Kenobi_date-10) It takes place ten years after [*Star Wars*: Episode III *Revenge of the Sith*](/wiki/Star_Wars:_Episode_III_Revenge_of_the_Sith "Star Wars: Episode III Revenge of the Sith").[[10]](#cite_note-Kenobi_date-10) During the event, it was also revealed that the episodes had already been written and that production would begin in [2020](/wiki/2020 "2020").[[27]](#cite_note-D23:_Expo_2019-27) Amini and [Deborah Chow](/wiki/Deborah_Chow "Deborah Chow") were later announced as showrunners in [StarWars.com](/wiki/StarWars.com "StarWars.com").[[7]](#cite_note-SW_Deborah-7) Reva, the chief villain of the series, was originally killed off in Beattie's script.[[28]](#cite_note-Killed_Off_Reva-28)

On [January 23](/wiki/January_23 "January 23"), 2020, it was reported that the series had been put on hold due to alleged "script problems."[[29]](#cite_note-29) However, McGregor later stated that these rumors were exaggerations and that production continued as normal. McGregor further stated that while filming was indeed delayed to January [2021](/wiki/2021 "2021"), the series' intended release date would remain unaffected.[[30]](#cite_note-30) On [April 2](/wiki/April_2 "April 2"), 2020, it was reported that [*John Wick: Chapter 3 – Parabellum*](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/John_Wick:_Chapter_3_%E2%80%93_Parabellum "wikipedia:John Wick: Chapter 3 – Parabellum") writer [Joby Harold](/wiki/Joby_Harold "Joby Harold") was hired to write the series, replacing Amini, who left the project back in January.[[31]](#cite_note-31) This was confirmed in 2022, as Kennedy revealed in an interview that she was concerned with the direction of the scripts. She had shut down production in January 2020, and hired Harold to replace Amini.[[2]](#cite_note-EW_cover-2) Despite leaving the project, Amini still received writing and story credits for four episodes of the season.[[6]](#cite_note-Writers_Guild-6)

 [![The original series logo revealed in December 2020](data:image/gif;base64,R0lGODlhAQABAIABAAAAAP///yH5BAEAAAEALAAAAAABAAEAQAICTAEAOw%3D%3D)](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/starwars/images/c/cb/Obi-Wan_Kenobi_series_logo.png/revision/latest?cb=20230724041150) [![The original series logo revealed in December 2020](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/starwars/images/c/cb/Obi-Wan_Kenobi_series_logo.png/revision/latest/scale-to-width-down/250?cb=20230724041150)](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/starwars/images/c/cb/Obi-Wan_Kenobi_series_logo.png/revision/latest?cb=20230724041150) 

The original series logo revealed in December 2020

 

In April 2020, French press release reported that the series was scheduled to debut in 2022.[[32]](#cite_note-French-32) In September 2020, McGregor expected the show to begin filming in spring 2021.[[33]](#cite_note-Spring_2021-33) On [October 9](/wiki/October_9 "October 9"), 2020, Ewan McGregor confirmed while on [BBC's](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/BBC "wikipedia:BBC") [*The Graham Norton Show*](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/The_Graham_Norton_Show "wikipedia:The Graham Norton Show") that filming was scheduled for [March](/wiki/March "March") of 2021.[[34]](#cite_note-GrahamNortonMarch2021-34) On [December 10](/wiki/December_10 "December 10"), 2020, more news on the series broke, including confirmation that the series would take place in [9 BBY](/wiki/9_BBY "9 BBY") and the announcement that [Hayden Christensen](/wiki/Hayden_Christensen "Hayden Christensen") would reprise his role as [Darth Vader](/wiki/Anakin_Skywalker "Anakin Skywalker") for the series. The series' logo was also revealed.[[35]](#cite_note-Title-35)

### Production[]

:   "*There are certain cultural things that we can understand right away, and culturally specific things design-wise. Like, 'Oh, maybe we shouldn't do this, but this?' Things that, intuitively, we pick up. It's been an absolute joy working with Deb because she gets it. She's very smart and knows what she wants. I really enjoy that collaboration because now, I feel like, for the* Obi-Wan *series especially, we have an opportunity to bring a richness to it that hasn't been explored. And I'm working with Chung-hoon* [Chung]*, our director of photography, he's brilliant. He has a wonderful eye.*"
:   ―Doug Chiang[[36]](#cite_note-Doug_Chiang-36)

On [March 29](/wiki/March_29 "March 29"), 2021, Lucasfilm announced the cast list for the series, which includes [Moses Ingram](/wiki/Moses_Ingram "Moses Ingram"), Joel Edgerton, [Bonnie Maree Piesse](/wiki/Bonnie_Maree_Piesse "Bonnie Maree Piesse"), [Kumail Nanjiani](/wiki/Kumail_Nanjiani "Kumail Nanjiani"), [Indira Varma](/wiki/Indira_Varma "Indira Varma"), [Rupert Friend](/wiki/Rupert_Friend "Rupert Friend"), [O'Shea Jackson Jr.](/wiki/O%27Shea_Jackson_Jr. "O'Shea Jackson Jr."), [Sung Kang](/wiki/Sung_Kang "Sung Kang"), [Simone Kessell](/wiki/Simone_Kessell "Simone Kessell"), and [Benny Safdie](/wiki/Benny_Safdie "Benny Safdie"). Filming began in April.[[5]](#cite_note-Cast-5)

In late 2020, the series was said to be produced using "The Volume,"[[37]](#cite_note-EO-Ewan-37) a technology previously used for *[Star Wars: The Mandalorian](/wiki/Star_Wars:_The_Mandalorian "Star Wars: The Mandalorian")*.[[38]](#cite_note-Tech-38) On [May 19](/wiki/May_19 "May 19"), 2021, it was revealed that veteran *Star Wars* concept artist Doug Chiang collaborated with director Deborah Chow on the series, making heavy use of different cultures for new designs. At least six department heads are of Asian American heritage, with [Chung-hoon Chung](/wiki/Chung-hoon_Chung "Chung-hoon Chung") serving as the director of photography.[[36]](#cite_note-Doug_Chiang-36) Filming wrapped on September 19, 2021.[[39]](#cite_note-Wrapped-39) On [February 9](/wiki/February_9 "February 9"), 2022, StarWars.com announced that the series would premiere [May 25](/wiki/May_25 "May 25") of that year.[[40]](#cite_note-Premiere_Announcement-40) The first teaser trailer for the series was released on [March 9](/wiki/March_9 "March 9").[[11]](#cite_note-Teaser-11) It was announced on [March 31](/wiki/March_31 "March 31") that the premiere for the series would be pushed back to [May 27](/wiki/May_27 "May 27"), 2022, with the first two episodes premiering together.[[41]](#cite_note-Premiere_Delayed-41)

On [February 28](/wiki/February_28 "February 28"), 2022, the [Writers Guild of America West](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Writers_Guild_of_America_West "wikipedia:Writers Guild of America West") revealed the final writing credits of the season, which includes the previously unannounced writers Stuart Beattie, [Andrew Stanton](/wiki/Andrew_Stanton "Andrew Stanton"), and [Hannah Friedman](/wiki/Hannah_Friedman "Hannah Friedman") alongside Joby Harold and Hossein Amini.[[6]](#cite_note-Writers_Guild-6) In a post-series interview with *[Star Wars Insider](/wiki/Star_Wars_Insider "Star Wars Insider")*, series composer [Natalie Holt](/wiki/Natalie_Holt "Natalie Holt") stated, "we didn't have very much time on *Obi-Wan Kenobi*, as it was a very compressed process," which she stated created "challenges" for herself and Chow while working on the soundtrack. Holt had been hired as composer in December and needed to be ready for music recording by early March.[[42]](#cite_note-Parallel_Lines-42)

### Music[]

On [February 17](/wiki/February_17 "February 17"), 2022, [*Variety*](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Variety_(magazine) "wikipedia:Variety (magazine)") released an article announcing that composer [John Williams](/wiki/John_Williams "John Williams") would return to the *Star Wars* universe, writing the main theme for the Kenobi character within the show, but that he wouldn't be scoring the series itself.[[43]](#cite_note-Williams-43) On [April 22](/wiki/April_22 "April 22") of that year, it was announced that Natalie Holt would be scoring the series, utilizing Williams' Kenobi theme.[[8]](#cite_note-Natalie_Holt-8) Additional music for the series was written by [William Ross](/wiki/William_Ross "William Ross"), a longtime Williams collaborator, as well as Peter Nickals, Jon Opstad, and Max Aruj.[[9]](#cite_note-obi-wan_end_credits-9)
