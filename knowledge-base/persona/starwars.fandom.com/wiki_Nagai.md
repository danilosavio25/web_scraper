**Nagai** was a [sentient](/wiki/Sentience "Sentience") [humanoid](/wiki/Humanoid "Humanoid") [species](/wiki/Species "Species") hailed from the astronomical object [Nagi](/wiki/Nagi "Nagi"). They had [black](/wiki/Color "Color") [hair](/wiki/Hair "Hair"), two red [eyes](/wiki/Eye "Eye"), and pale white skin, along with pointy [ears](/wiki/Ear "Ear") and unique face markings. The species was shown to be strong-willed and spoke in [Galactic Basic Standard](/wiki/Galactic_Basic_Standard "Galactic Basic Standard").

## Biology and appearance[]

The Nagai were a [sentient](/wiki/Sentience "Sentience") [humanoid](/wiki/Humanoid "Humanoid") [species](/wiki/Species "Species") with [black](/wiki/Color "Color") [hair](/wiki/Hair "Hair"), a pair of red [eyes](/wiki/Eye "Eye"), and pale white skin. They also had distinct pointy [ears](/wiki/Ear "Ear") and face markings unique to each member.[[1]](#cite_note-SW_2-1)

## Society and culture[]

:   "*In* Garlev's *name, we fight! For the Nagai, we fight!*"
:   ―Jesrit, upon rescuing other captured Nagai[[2]](#cite_note-SW_5-2)

Individuals of the Nagai species were shown to be brave and strong-willed, as they took opportunities to rescue one another and rebel for their freedom.[[2]](#cite_note-SW_5-2)[[3]](#cite_note-SW_3-3) They were able communicate verbally with [Galactic Basic Standard](/wiki/Galactic_Basic_Standard "Galactic Basic Standard").[[1]](#cite_note-SW_2-1)

## History[]

:   "*The Zantarrk are manipulators—their leader, Gallana Venk, is a traitor to the Nagai and our ways. She's corrupted us. Taken our children. Our brothers and sisters.*"
:   ―Marntiv, on the Zantarrk[[4]](#cite_note-SW_6-4)

By the [time](/wiki/Time "Time") of the [New Republic Era](/wiki/New_Republic_Era "New Republic Era"), the Nagai had forcibly been removed from their [homeworld](/wiki/Homeworld "Homeworld"),[[1]](#cite_note-SW_2-1) the astronomical object [Nagi](/wiki/Nagi "Nagi"),[[2]](#cite_note-SW_5-2) by outside forces—the [Zantarrk Gang](/wiki/Zantarrk_Gang "Zantarrk Gang")[[4]](#cite_note-SW_6-4) and the [Fenril Consortium](/wiki/Fenril_Consortium "Fenril Consortium")[[5]](#cite_note-SW_4-5)—to work in [labor camps](/wiki/Labor_camp "Labor camp") after the betrayal of the Nagai [Gallana Venk](/wiki/Gallana_Venk "Gallana Venk"), who led the Zantarrk and sold her allegiance to the [Anzati](/wiki/Anzati "Anzati") [Reyna Oskure](/wiki/Reyna_Oskure "Reyna Oskure") and the consortium.[[4]](#cite_note-SW_6-4)

## Nagai in the galaxy[]

:   "*It's not that, Han. I'm not trying to sell these people—they're* my *people. I'm trying to* save *them.*"
:   ―Garlev, to Han Solo[[3]](#cite_note-SW_3-3)

Displaced from their homeworld, the Nagai were forced to do labor work in various [planets](/wiki/Planet "Planet") in the [Fenril sector](/wiki/Fenril_sector "Fenril sector"),[[4]](#cite_note-SW_6-4) including [Kaltrais](/wiki/Kaltrais "Kaltrais")[[2]](#cite_note-SW_5-2) and [Gadrilam](/wiki/Gadrilam "Gadrilam").[[1]](#cite_note-SW_2-1) At some point, in an attempt to get their freedom back, the Nagai started a rebellion against their oppressors. Amongst the rebellion was the Nagai [Garlev](/wiki/Garlev "Garlev"), who worked multiple times with [smugglers](/wiki/Smuggler "Smuggler") [Han Solo](/wiki/Han_Solo "Han Solo") and [Lando Calrissian](/wiki/Landonis_Balthazar_Calrissian "Landonis Balthazar Calrissian") to smuggle his people to safety.[[3]](#cite_note-SW_3-3) Over time, Garlev's bravery convinced other Nagai to follow along and fight for their freedom, including [Marntiv](/wiki/Marntiv "Marntiv")[[4]](#cite_note-SW_6-4) and [Jesrit](/wiki/Jesrit "Jesrit").[[2]](#cite_note-SW_5-2)

[Years](/wiki/Standard_year "Standard year") later, around [6 ABY](/wiki/6_ABY "6 ABY"),[[6]](#cite_note-Date-6) a [Nagai pirate](/wiki/Unidentified_Nagai_pirate "Unidentified Nagai pirate") was confronted by the [Jedi](/wiki/Jedi "Jedi") [Luke Skywalker](/wiki/Luke_Skywalker "Luke Skywalker") and [New Republic pilot](/wiki/New_Republic_pilot "New Republic pilot") [Rynn Zenat](/wiki/Rynn_Zenat "Rynn Zenat") on a planet, following an ambush at the edge of the Fenril sector.[[7]](#cite_note-SW_1-7) The pirate informed Skywalker of a Nagai [smuggler](/wiki/Smuggler "Smuggler") [Ibo Truddispal](/wiki/Ibo_Truddispal "Ibo Truddispal") and his possession of a stolen Jedi artifact, the [Crown of Verity](/wiki/Crown_of_Verity "Crown of Verity"), on Gadrilam. They added that Skywalker would then learn the truth about the Nagai and himself. To cover their trail, the Nagai committed [suicide](/wiki/Death "Death"),[[1]](#cite_note-SW_2-1) injuring Skywalker in the process.[[7]](#cite_note-SW_1-7)

## Behind the scenes[]

Nagai, albeit unidentified, was introduced to current *[Star Wars](/wiki/Star_Wars "Star Wars")* [canon](/wiki/Canon "Canon") in the [first issue](/wiki/Star_Wars_(2025)_1 "Star Wars (2025) 1") of the [2025 iteration](/wiki/Star_Wars_(2025) "Star Wars (2025)") of the flagship [comic book series](/wiki/Star_Wars_comic_books "Star Wars comic books") *Star Wars*,[[7]](#cite_note-SW_1-7) which was written by [Alex Segura](/wiki/Alex_Segura "Alex Segura"), illustrated by [Phil Noto](/wiki/Phil_Noto "Phil Noto"), and published by [Marvel Comics](/wiki/Marvel_Comics "Marvel Comics") on [May 7](/wiki/May_7 "May 7") of that year.[[8]](#cite_note-SW_1_Release-8) The species was later identified as Nagai in the [following issue](/wiki/Star_Wars_(2025)_2 "Star Wars (2025) 2") of the same series also written by Segura.[[1]](#cite_note-SW_2-1) In the [*Star Wars* Legends](/wiki/Star_Wars_Legends "Star Wars Legends") continuity, the species first appeared in the [ninety-first issue](/wiki/Star_Wars_(1977)_91 "Star Wars (1977) 91") of Marvel Comics' [1977 iteration](/wiki/Star_Wars_(1977) "Star Wars (1977)") of the flagship *Star Wars* series, written by [Mary Jo Duffy](/wiki/Mary_Jo_Duffy "Mary Jo Duffy"), illustrated by [Tony Salmons](/wiki/Tony_Salmons "Tony Salmons"), and published in [1984](/wiki/1984 "1984").[[9]](#cite_note-SW_1977_91-9)
