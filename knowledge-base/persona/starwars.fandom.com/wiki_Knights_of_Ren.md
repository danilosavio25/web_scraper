:   ―The Archivist, on the Knights of Ren[[10]](#cite_note-Crimson_Reign_1-10)

The **Knights of Ren** were an enclave of [masked](/wiki/Helmet "Helmet") [dark side](/wiki/Dark_side_of_the_Force "Dark side of the Force") [warriors](/wiki/Soldier "Soldier") that adhered to the ways of the [Ren](/wiki/Ren_(lightsaber) "Ren (lightsaber)"), a [religious](/wiki/Religion "Religion") philosophy epitomized by the [lightsaber](/wiki/Lightsaber "Lightsaber"). A band of [marauders](/wiki/Pirate "Pirate") and [mercenaries](/wiki/Mercenary "Mercenary") from the [Unknown Regions](/wiki/Unknown_Regions "Unknown Regions"), the Knights roamed the [galaxy](/wiki/The_galaxy "The galaxy") causing wanton destruction. Imbued with the power of the [Force](/wiki/The_Force "The Force"), they were devotees of the dark side which they referred to as the "shadow" in addition to their [inherent abilities](/wiki/Force_power "Force power"), the Knights of Ren were armed for [ranged](/wiki/Blaster "Blaster") and [close-quarter combat](/wiki/Melee_weapon "Melee weapon"), making them fearsome opponents in battle.

The Knights of Ren were active during the [Galactic Civil War](/wiki/Galactic_Civil_War "Galactic Civil War") between the [Alliance to Restore the Republic](/wiki/Alliance_to_Restore_the_Republic "Alliance to Restore the Republic") and the [Galactic Empire](/wiki/Galactic_Empire "Galactic Empire"). They were one of the few remaining Force-sensitive groups by the [time](/wiki/Time "Time") of the [Imperial Era](/wiki/Imperial_Era "Imperial Era"), the Empire having [purged](/wiki/Great_Jedi_Purge "Great Jedi Purge") the [galaxy](/wiki/Galaxy "Galaxy") of the [Jedi Order](/wiki/Jedi_Order "Jedi Order") and other Force-based organizations. Their leader, a [warrior](/wiki/Ren_(human) "Ren (human)") known by the name of [Ren](/wiki/Ren "Ren"), personified the Knights' creed. At one point, they were allied with [Lady](/wiki/Lady "Lady") [Qi'ra](/wiki/Qi%27ra "Qi'ra"), the head of [Crimson Dawn](/wiki/Crimson_Dawn "Crimson Dawn"). As such, the Knights supported her goal of reshaping the power structure of the galaxy by [overthrowing](/wiki/War_against_the_Sith "War against the Sith") [Darth Sidious](/wiki/Darth_Sidious "Darth Sidious"), the [Dark Lord of the Sith](/wiki/Dark_Lord_of_the_Sith "Dark Lord of the Sith") and [Galactic Emperor](/wiki/Galactic_Emperor "Galactic Emperor").

After the Empire fell, the Knights of Ren remained active and continued traveling across the galaxy, raiding worlds, hunting for artifacts, and searching for prospective recruits during the [era](/wiki/New_Republic_Era "New Republic Era") of the [New Republic](/wiki/New_Republic "New Republic"). [Ben Solo](/wiki/Ben_Solo "Ben Solo")—the nephew and [Padawan](/wiki/Padawan "Padawan") of [Jedi Master](/wiki/Jedi_Master "Jedi Master") [Luke Skywalker](/wiki/Luke_Skywalker "Luke Skywalker")—[destroyed the new generation](/wiki/Destruction_of_the_Jedi_Temple "Destruction of the Jedi Temple") of [Jedi](/wiki/Jedi "Jedi") and subsequently joined the Knights by [killing](/wiki/Skirmish_on_the_Minemoon "Skirmish on the Minemoon") Ren. Assuming the new name of Kylo Ren, the fallen Jedi became a [warlord](/wiki/Warlord "Warlord") of the [First Order](/wiki/First_Order "First Order") and the apprentice of [Supreme Leader](/wiki/Supreme_Leader "Supreme Leader") [Snoke](/wiki/Snoke "Snoke"). Under Kylo's leadership as master of the Knights of Ren, the Knights of Ren filled the vacuum caused by the [destruction](/wiki/Chosen_One "Chosen One") of the [Sith](/wiki/Sith "Sith"). Unlike the Knights of Ren who lacked training in the ways of the Force, Kylo was the grandson of the [Sith Lord](/wiki/Sith_Lord "Sith Lord") [Darth Vader](/wiki/Anakin_Skywalker "Anakin Skywalker"), and was gifted with the raw power of the [bloodline](/wiki/Skywalker_family "Skywalker family"). Unbeknownst to him, their true loyalty was to Sidious who had been secretly [resurrected](/wiki/Transference "Transference") on the [planet](/wiki/Planet "Planet") [Exegol](/wiki/Exegol "Exegol"). After being betrayed by Kylo under order of Snoke, they eventually fell under the command of [Tava Ren](/wiki/Tava_Ren "Tava Ren").

During the [First Order-Resistance War](/wiki/First_Order-Resistance_War "First Order-Resistance War"), the Knights of Ren once again came under Kylo's rule, becoming his personal [bodyguards](/wiki/Bodyguard "Bodyguard") after he [usurped](/wiki/Battle_of_Oetchi "Battle of Oetchi") Snoke's [rule](/wiki/Dictatorship "Dictatorship") as Supreme Leader. In addition to enforcing Kylo's reign, the marauders served as trackers during the hunt for [Rey](/wiki/Rey_Skywalker "Rey Skywalker"), a Jedi apprentice who fought for the [Resistance](/wiki/Resistance "Resistance") and shared a [unique](/wiki/Force_dyad "Force dyad") [bond](/wiki/Force-bond "Force-bond") with Kylo. Following Ben Solo's return to the [light side of the Force](/wiki/Light_side_of_the_Force "Light side of the Force"), the Knights confronted their former master during the [Battle of Exegol](/wiki/Battle_of_Exegol "Battle of Exegol") and were [slain](/wiki/Death "Death") by the Jedi.

## Description[]

### Overview[]

:   "*Just because we use the shadow doesn't mean the Sith are our* friends*. It's just that the Emperor's been dealing with other problems first—the Jedi, the Nightsisters…we're small fish in comparison. I realize that doesn't fit with how we see ourselves…but that's what it is.*"

An enclave of masked warriors, the Knights of Ren were the elite enforcers of Kylo Ren.

 

The Knights of Ren were an enclave of [masked](/wiki/Helmet "Helmet")[[11]](#cite_note-KOR_DB-11) elite [warriors](/wiki/Soldier "Soldier")[[12]](#cite_note-Rise_of_Skywalker_Little_Golden_Book-12) wielding distinctive weapons for ranged and close-quarters combat, making them the elite and fearsome enforcers of [Kylo Ren's](/wiki/Ben_Solo "Ben Solo") will.[[11]](#cite_note-KOR_DB-11) The Knights hailed from [the galaxy](/wiki/The_galaxy "The galaxy")'s [Unknown Regions](/wiki/Unknown_Regions "Unknown Regions") where their name and reputation inspired [centuries](/wiki/Standard_century "Standard century") of cautionary tales about the dangers lurking in uncharted [space](/wiki/Realspace "Realspace"). Though local folklore warned of the marauders known as the Knights of Ren, the exact origins of their modern incarnation remained mysterious, for they were either directly connected to that ancient tradition or wrapping themselves in the legacy of legends. Nevertheless, the [New Republic-era](/wiki/New_Republic_Era "New Republic Era") Knights of Ren were a force to be reckoned with.[[2]](#cite_note-TROS_VD-2) Their [martial prowess](/wiki/Unarmed_combat "Unarmed combat") was without equal,[[13]](#cite_note-Ultimate_Star_Wars-13) and they thrived in violence and strife.[[2]](#cite_note-TROS_VD-2)

The Knights of Ren were neither [Jedi](/wiki/Jedi "Jedi") nor [Sith](/wiki/Sith "Sith").[[14]](#cite_note-TFA_VD-14) They were imbued with the power of [the Force](/wiki/The_Force "The Force"),[[2]](#cite_note-TROS_VD-2) though not to the extent of the aforementioned Jedi or Sith.[[15]](#cite_note-Soule_Interview-15) Although the Knights of Ren existed alongside the Sith during the [Imperial Era](/wiki/Imperial_Era "Imperial Era"),[[3]](#cite_note-Crimson_Reign_4-3) they persisted past the fall of the Sith[[7]](#cite_note-Episode_VII-7) and, in that capacity, were a new generation of warriors.[[14]](#cite_note-TFA_VD-14) The dark warrior[[16]](#cite_note-Dark_Warrior-16)[[17]](#cite_note-Dark_Warrior_Kylo-17) Kylo Ren, who led the group as the master of the Knights of Ren, was the archetype of their new generation of dark-side devotees that emerged to fill the void left by the Sith's [demise](/wiki/Battle_of_Endor "Battle of Endor").[[14]](#cite_note-TFA_VD-14)

 [![Kylo Ren was the archetype of the Knights of Ren, a new generation of dark-side warriors.](data:image/gif;base64,R0lGODlhAQABAIABAAAAAP///yH5BAEAAAEALAAAAAABAAEAQAICTAEAOw%3D%3D)](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/starwars/images/5/51/Acme-DarkWarriorKyloRen.png/revision/latest?cb=20240415191917) [![Kylo Ren was the archetype of the Knights of Ren, a new generation of dark-side warriors.](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/starwars/images/5/51/Acme-DarkWarriorKyloRen.png/revision/latest/scale-to-width-down/250?cb=20240415191917)](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/starwars/images/5/51/Acme-DarkWarriorKyloRen.png/revision/latest?cb=20240415191917) 

Kylo Ren was the archetype of the Knights of Ren, a new generation of dark-side warriors.

 

The Knights' lack of formal training hindered their connection to the Force,[[2]](#cite_note-TROS_VD-2) whereas Kylo himself had the potential for limitless power,[[18]](#cite_note-Snoke_DB-18) being gifted with the raw strength of his bloodline.[[19]](#cite_note-Episode_VIII-19) Nevertheless, the Knights had a connection to the [dark side of the Force](/wiki/Dark_side_of_the_Force "Dark side of the Force"). As such, other Force-sensitives could sense the cold presence of the dark side when in close proximity to the Knights of Ren.[[20]](#cite_note-Rise_2-20) Despite their untamed state, the Knights were considered comparable to the [Galactic Empire](/wiki/Galactic_Empire "Galactic Empire")'s [Inquisitorius](/wiki/Inquisitorius "Inquisitorius"), a past generation of dark-side wielders and [Jedi hunters](/wiki/Jedi_hunter "Jedi hunter").[[2]](#cite_note-TROS_VD-2)

In combat the dark side surged through the Knights[[2]](#cite_note-TROS_VD-2) despite possessing only slight [Force abilities](/wiki/Force_power "Force power"). [Jedi Master](/wiki/Jedi_Master "Jedi Master") [Luke Skywalker](/wiki/Luke_Skywalker "Luke Skywalker") was unimpressed by their battle skills and basic use of the dark side.[[21]](#cite_note-TSWB-21) Skywalker saw the Knights as a clumsy and untrained group after besting six of them with the Force. He compared their use of the dark side to the way one used a hammer, whereas Skywalker, the last fully trained [Jedi Knight](/wiki/Jedi_Knight "Jedi Knight"), wielded the [light side](/wiki/Light_side_of_the_Force "Light side of the Force") as a blade.[[20]](#cite_note-Rise_2-20) The [Sith Lord](/wiki/Sith_Lord "Sith Lord") [Darth Sidious](/wiki/Darth_Sidious "Darth Sidious") concurred with Skywalker's assessment of the Knights' skills, but he defended them as well, stating that some tasks required a "blunt instrument" rather than a precision based weapon.[[22]](#cite_note-SotS-22)

The Knights were served by [Albrekh](/wiki/Albrekh "Albrekh"),[[2]](#cite_note-TROS_VD-2) an eccentric [Sith alchemist](/wiki/Sith_alchemist "Sith alchemist") and [metalsmith](/wiki/Metalsmith "Metalsmith") known for his[[23]](#cite_note-Albrekh-23) dexterous, steady hands.[[2]](#cite_note-TROS_VD-2) An [alien](/wiki/Alien "Alien")[[6]](#cite_note-Episode_IX-6) whose sensitive [ears](/wiki/Ear "Ear") could tell the heat of metal from the tone it made when struck,[[2]](#cite_note-TROS_VD-2) Albrekh made use of an ancient [Sith forge](/wiki/Sith_forge "Sith forge")[[23]](#cite_note-Albrekh-23) for the purpose of repairing and improving the Knights' battle gear. His additional equipment included a series of four posable arm mechanisms with [magnetic](/wiki/Magnet "Magnet") forceps and reverse-[repulsorlift](/wiki/Repulsorlift "Repulsorlift") [grav](/wiki/Gravity "Gravity")-focused bases. The device was used in conjunction with a compressed air cooling nozzle and a molten [Sarrassian iron](/wiki/Sarrassian_iron "Sarrassian iron") application tool.[[2]](#cite_note-TROS_VD-2)

The *Night Buzzard* was an *Oubliette*-class transport that belonged to the Knights of Ren.

 

The Knights traveled aboard a customized [starship](/wiki/Starship "Starship") known as the *[Night Buzzard](/wiki/Night_Buzzard "Night Buzzard")*. A modified [*Oubliette*-class transport](/wiki/Oubliette-class_transport "Oubliette-class transport") manufactured by [Osseriton Assemblages](/wiki/Osseriton_Assemblages "Osseriton Assemblages"), the *Night Buzzard* was originally a [dungeon](/wiki/Prison "Prison") ship from [Osseriton](/wiki/Osseriton "Osseriton"), a penal world in the Unknown Regions. The Knights appropriated the [transport](/wiki/Transport "Transport") for their personal use and altered its appearance to fit with their dark style. Additionally, they made crude modifications to the vessel's [engine](/wiki/Engine "Engine") systems, generating greater thrust but at a cost to its insulation baffles. This resulted in the *Night Buzzard* spewing a trail of thick, noxious smoke.[[2]](#cite_note-TROS_VD-2)

 [![Although Kylo Ren joined the First Order as Snoke's apprentice, the Knights of Ren remained primarily independent.](data:image/gif;base64,R0lGODlhAQABAIABAAAAAP///yH5BAEAAAEALAAAAAABAAEAQAICTAEAOw%3D%3D)](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/starwars/images/9/99/Episode_VIII_Snoke_and_Kylo_Ren.png/revision/latest?cb=20180727071207) [![Although Kylo Ren joined the First Order as Snoke's apprentice, the Knights of Ren remained primarily independent.](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/starwars/images/9/99/Episode_VIII_Snoke_and_Kylo_Ren.png/revision/latest/scale-to-width-down/250?cb=20180727071207)](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/starwars/images/9/99/Episode_VIII_Snoke_and_Kylo_Ren.png/revision/latest?cb=20180727071207) 

Although Kylo Ren joined the First Order as Snoke's apprentice, the Knights of Ren remained primarily independent.

 

The Knights of Ren became Kylo Ren's most mysterious and deadly servants.[[13]](#cite_note-Ultimate_Star_Wars-13) Despite Kylo's ties to the [First Order](/wiki/First_Order "First Order"), the other knights were not aligned with it, as Kylo and [Supreme Leader](/wiki/Supreme_Leader "Supreme Leader") [Snoke](/wiki/Snoke "Snoke") were the only [Force-sensitive](/wiki/Force-sensitive "Force-sensitive") members of that [regime](/wiki/Politics "Politics").[[24]](#cite_note-TLJ_Expanded_Edition-24) They were an independent, mobile group whose aim was to spread chaos and violence wherever they went.[[21]](#cite_note-TSWB-21) Regardless, Snoke indirectly controlled them for a time as Kylo Ren had submitted to his command; the Supreme Leader had utilized the warriors as a tool to further the dark side within the First Order's battles.[[14]](#cite_note-TFA_VD-14) After Kylo killed Snoke, the Knights appeared more often within the First Order's ranks.[[6]](#cite_note-Episode_IX-6) Where they once traveled the [galaxy](/wiki/Galaxy "Galaxy") as marauders and [mercenaries](/wiki/Mercenary "Mercenary"), the Knights became enforcers of Kylo's [reign](/wiki/Dictatorship "Dictatorship") as the new Supreme Leader. However, only Kylo had the authority to command them, as the Knights would answer to no one else. This singular devotion to one individual created friction between the Knights and the [members](/wiki/First_Order_High_Command "First Order High Command") of the [First Order Supreme Council](/wiki/First_Order_Supreme_Council "First Order Supreme Council").[[2]](#cite_note-TROS_VD-2) After Kylo was redeemed to the light side of the Force and became Ben Solo once more, the Knights did not follow him. In spite of the fact that he had come to view the Knights as his faithful brothers, they had never truly been loyal to their so-called master because,[[5]](#cite_note-TROS_Expanded_Edition-5) while they were not Sith themselves,[[14]](#cite_note-TFA_VD-14) they had become servants of Sidious before he even met them.[[5]](#cite_note-TROS_Expanded_Edition-5)

#### Qualifications for knighthood[]

Joining the Knights of Ren required the death of a worthy target.

 

Membership in the Knights of Ren was gained through ritual sacrifice; the prospective Knight had to choose a "worthy target" to kill. The completion of this rite was referred to as "a good [death](/wiki/Death "Death")."[[22]](#cite_note-SotS-22) Force-sensitivity was another qualification for joining the Knights, who only accepted Force-sensitive [beings](/wiki/Species "Species") due to their inherent ability to connect to the dark side of the Force. Even if a non-Force user was more inclined to their way of thinking than a potential recruit, the Knights would sooner kill them than accept one who could not feel the Force.[[8]](#cite_note-Rise_1-8)

The death requirement for joining the Knights could be either a personal sacrifice, such as the killing of a family member,[[8]](#cite_note-Rise_1-8) or a victory over an opponent in battle.[[25]](#cite_note-Rise_4-25) The Knights also stipulated that the recruit had to willingly kill their target; therefore, they rejected deaths that occurred by accident or through hesitation.[[26]](#cite_note-Rise_3-26) The Knight known as "[Ren](/wiki/Ren_(human) "Ren (human)")" once asked a prospective member to kill his own brother,[[8]](#cite_note-Rise_1-8) and Snoke warned Ben Solo that he would pay a steep price in order to join the Knights of Ren. Eligible candidates who paid that price became part of the group; whereas those who failed would be killed.[[20]](#cite_note-Rise_2-20) Snoke stated that it was rare for others to seek out the Knights of Ren, and that doing so often led to regret.[[26]](#cite_note-Rise_3-26)

#### The marauding knights[]

:   "*Each of the Knights of Ren harbors the shadow of the dark side within them. But unlike the Sith, they are not trained to harness that connection to its fullest potential.*"
:   ―Darth Sidious[[22]](#cite_note-SotS-22)

Under the leadership of Ren, several knights—[Cardo](/wiki/Cardo "Cardo"), [Marinda](/wiki/Marinda "Marinda"), [Vicrul](/wiki/Vicrul "Vicrul"), [Bazzra](/wiki/Bazzra "Bazzra"), [Fyodor](/wiki/Fyodor "Fyodor"), and [Massif](/wiki/Massif "Massif")—served in the group during the [Imperial Era](/wiki/Imperial_Era "Imperial Era").[[3]](#cite_note-Crimson_Reign_4-3) In addition to Ren and later Kylo Ren, the Knights of Ren of the New Republic Era consisted of six members: Vicrul, Cardo, [Ushar](/wiki/Ushar "Ushar"), [Trudgen](/wiki/Trudgen "Trudgen"), [Kuruk](/wiki/Kuruk "Kuruk"), and [Ap'lek](/wiki/Ap%27lek "Ap'lek"). Ren was known for his "viciousness" as a leader.[[2]](#cite_note-TROS_VD-2) After Ren's death, the remaining Knights recognized Kylo as their new master,[[25]](#cite_note-Rise_4-25) with Kylo also taking on the title "Ren"[[6]](#cite_note-Episode_IX-6) like the former masters before him.[[3]](#cite_note-Crimson_Reign_4-3)

 [![As a Knight of Ren, Vicrul adopted several titles, including "reaper of the fallen](data:image/gif;base64,R0lGODlhAQABAIABAAAAAP///yH5BAEAAAEALAAAAAABAAEAQAICTAEAOw%3D%3D)](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/starwars/images/9/90/Vicrul-BountyHunters25.png/revision/latest?cb=20230130031453) [![As a Knight of Ren, Vicrul adopted several titles, including "reaper of the fallen](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/starwars/images/9/90/Vicrul-BountyHunters25.png/revision/latest/scale-to-width-down/250?cb=20230130031453)](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/starwars/images/9/90/Vicrul-BountyHunters25.png/revision/latest?cb=20230130031453) 

As a Knight of Ren, Vicrul adopted several titles, including "reaper of the fallen."

 

Vicrul was the self-styled "harvester," the "reaper of the fallen," and the "targeter of souls." Though he lacked training in the ways of the Force, his connection to the dark side increased with each victim that he killed. He also relied on his hatred to fuel his dark-side powers. Yet with each strike his hate grew, allowing the dark side to surge through him. His latent connection to the Force granted him heightened reflexes and the power to induce fear in his opponents. However, he lacked control over his abilities.[[2]](#cite_note-TROS_VD-2)

Cardo was considered a "walking arsenal"[[27]](#cite_note-Cardo_DB-27) who lived for creating wanton destruction, unlike his fellow Knights who favored precision in their combat styles. Possessing an obsession with weapon modification,[[2]](#cite_note-TROS_VD-2) he spent years tinkering with weapons[[27]](#cite_note-Cardo_DB-27) which made him the Knights' best [armorer](/wiki/Armorer "Armorer").[[2]](#cite_note-TROS_VD-2) As a result, Cardo was responsible for repairing the group's equipment after each mission.[[27]](#cite_note-Cardo_DB-27)

Trudgen was a collector of trophies from fallen conquests. He incorporated said trophies into his existing [armor](/wiki/Armor "Armor"), weapons and equipment, part of which included a fragment of [death trooper armor](/wiki/Death_trooper_armor "Death trooper armor").[[2]](#cite_note-TROS_VD-2) As such, his armor was therefore a record of his victories.[[28]](#cite_note-Trudgen_DB-28)

Ushar was known for torturing his prisoners into begging for mercy, of which he possessed none.

 

Ushar had a reputation for forcing his [prisoners](/wiki/Prisoner "Prisoner") to grovel for mercy. He would take his time with the victims who whimpered for help, believing they deserved a slow punishment. While those who fought back earned his respect, Ushar would kill them all the same, but faster as they had earned a swift death in his view.[[2]](#cite_note-TROS_VD-2)

Kuruk was the most solitary of the Knights. In addition to his role as the group's rifleman, he served as the [pilot](/wiki/Pilot "Pilot") of the *Night Buzzard*. Noted for his precision marksmanship, he also served as the group's primary [sniper](/wiki/Sniper "Sniper") and could be found perched on a high vantage point, covering an assault conducted by his fellow Knights. While Kuruk's helmet was designed to focus his attention on his target, his aim was further sharpened by concentrating on the Force.[[2]](#cite_note-TROS_VD-2)

Ap'lek reveled in deception and strategy, employing feints and misdirection to trap an opponent. In this he differed from the other Knights, who preferred a straightforward and violent approach to eliminating their targets. His nascent Force abilities allowed Ap'lek to peer through the smoke screens that he would create to obscure an enemy's [vision](/wiki/Eye "Eye") and disrupt [sensors](/wiki/Sensor "Sensor"). As a warrior, his preferred lethal weapon was an ancient [Mandalorian](/wiki/Mandalorian "Mandalorian") executioner's [vibro-ax](/wiki/Vibro-ax "Vibro-ax").[[2]](#cite_note-TROS_VD-2)

Prior to the rise of Kylo Ren, the Knights followed the leader who was also known by the name of "[Ren](/wiki/Ren "Ren"),"[[2]](#cite_note-TROS_VD-2) with that man even revealing he had been preceded by [another Knight](/wiki/Ren_(Ren%27s_predecessor) "Ren (Ren's predecessor)") who used that name.[[3]](#cite_note-Crimson_Reign_4-3) Despite being recognized as the master of the Knights,[[25]](#cite_note-Rise_4-25) Ren insisted that the group did not follow him but rather their way of life.[[8]](#cite_note-Rise_1-8) Nevertheless, Ren was the spokesperson of the group, as he would introduce them by name to enemies[[20]](#cite_note-Rise_2-20) and potential allies alike. A devotee of the dark side, he followed the Knights' guiding principle, the Ren, and believed in it on a personal level.[[8]](#cite_note-Rise_1-8) While Ren and his Knights showed no loyalty to Sidious during the Imperial Era,[[3]](#cite_note-Crimson_Reign_4-3) they eventually came to serve under the Sith Lord.[[5]](#cite_note-TROS_Expanded_Edition-5)

Kylo Ren became the master of the Knights of Ren after killing their previous leader.

 

Having rejected the Jedi way, Kylo Ren adopted the title "master of the Knights of Ren"[[7]](#cite_note-Episode_VII-7) after taking control of the group.[[22]](#cite_note-SotS-22) His ability to use the Force granted him many impressive combat skills. Despite his [Jedi training](/wiki/Jedi_training "Jedi training") and knowledge of arcane dark-side lore, Kylo was neither Jedi nor Sith.[[14]](#cite_note-TFA_VD-14) Instead, he personified the teachings of both orders. The combination of Jedi and Sith philosophy created an imbalance within him, however. Though gifted with the raw power of his [grandfather](/wiki/Skywalker_family "Skywalker family"), the Sith [Lord](/wiki/Lord "Lord") [Darth Vader](/wiki/Anakin_Skywalker "Anakin Skywalker"), Kylo struggled to overcome the light side within himself.[[29]](#cite_note-Kylo_Ren_DB-29) Following his ascension as Supreme Leader of the First Order, Kylo called on his Knights to serve as his personal [bodyguards](/wiki/Bodyguard "Bodyguard") in the same way that the [Elite Praetorian Guard](/wiki/Praetorian_Guard "Praetorian Guard") protected his predecessor, Supreme Leader Snoke.[[2]](#cite_note-TROS_VD-2)

### Philosophy[]

:   "*The Ren doesn't stop to worry about what it's burning or the right or wrong of it, or the goals it might achieve. The Ren just is. It lives, and it consumes, and it doesn't apologize. It is its nature and nothing else.*"
:   ―Ren[[8]](#cite_note-Rise_1-8)

 [![The lightsaber called the "Ren" symbolized the leadership over the Knights of Ren.](data:image/gif;base64,R0lGODlhAQABAIABAAAAAP///yH5BAEAAAEALAAAAAABAAEAQAICTAEAOw%3D%3D)](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/starwars/images/d/da/CrimsonReign2-Kirkhamtextless.png/revision/latest?cb=20231119184848) [![The lightsaber called the "Ren" symbolized the leadership over the Knights of Ren.](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/starwars/images/d/da/CrimsonReign2-Kirkhamtextless.png/revision/latest/scale-to-width-down/250?cb=20231119184848)](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/starwars/images/d/da/CrimsonReign2-Kirkhamtextless.png/revision/latest?cb=20231119184848) 

The lightsaber called the "Ren" symbolized the leadership over the Knights of Ren.

 

[Darth](/wiki/Darth "Darth") Sidious stated in his [book](/wiki/Book "Book"), *[The Secrets of the Sith](/wiki/The_Secrets_of_the_Sith "The Secrets of the Sith")*, that the Knights of Ren did not adhere to any code. They were willing to do anything to prevail;[[22]](#cite_note-SotS-22) as such the Knights lived a flexible lifestyle, doing whatever they pleased. They believed that everything they gained was given to them by the dark side. The [Ren](/wiki/Ren_(lightsaber) "Ren (lightsaber)"), a [lightsaber](/wiki/Lightsaber "Lightsaber") possessed by Kylo Ren's predecessor, also known as Ren, symbolized the leadership over the Knights. The nature of the Ren, according to Ren, was that it just existed. It had no conscience, neither acknowledging or expressing concepts such as morality, regret or concern for what it might destroy. Ren believed in this principle on a deep, personal level to the point where he did not consider himself the Knights' leader. All of the Knights, including Ren himself, followed the lightsaber and what it represented in their view.[[8]](#cite_note-Rise_1-8)

Their devotion to the way of the Ren was matched only by their allegiance to the shadow—the dark side of the Force. The Knights did not search for just any Force-sensitive individuals to join the ranks; they wanted those who had a strong affinity for the shadow. This led Ren to consider a mass murderer[[8]](#cite_note-Rise_1-8) and a child[[20]](#cite_note-Rise_2-20) for membership, having sensed the shadow's presence in both of them.[[8]](#cite_note-Rise_1-8)[[20]](#cite_note-Rise_2-20)

The Knights believed that they had a right to take anything and everything they so desired, particularly [Jedi artifacts](/wiki/Jedi_artifact "Jedi artifact"). As such, they held no regard for Luke Skywalker's claim on all properties of the [Jedi Order](/wiki/Jedi_Order "Jedi Order"). Ren's understanding of the [Jedi Code](/wiki/Jedi_Code "Jedi Code") was limited, though he suspected that the ideological differences between his Knights and the Jedi were severe, making coexistence impossible in his view.[[20]](#cite_note-Rise_2-20) Although their ways failed to impress Skywalker, their lawlessness and power appealed to his young apprentice, Ben Solo, who thought of the Knights for years before ultimately leaving the Jedi Order to join their ranks.[[21]](#cite_note-TSWB-21)

#### Armor[]

:   "*The kid looks like trash. An actual pile of garbage. Find him some clothes. I mean… We're the* Knights of Ren*. We have a reputation.*"
:   ―Ren, to Kuruk, on Ben Solo's Jedi attire[[26]](#cite_note-Rise_3-26)

 [![In addition to his signature mask, Kylo Ren wore black robes and padded armor during his time as a Knight of Ren.](data:image/gif;base64,R0lGODlhAQABAIABAAAAAP///yH5BAEAAAEALAAAAAABAAEAQAICTAEAOw%3D%3D)](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/starwars/images/d/d2/Acme-DarkWarrior.png/revision/latest?cb=20240415195913) [![In addition to his signature mask, Kylo Ren wore black robes and padded armor during his time as a Knight of Ren.](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/starwars/images/d/d2/Acme-DarkWarrior.png/revision/latest/scale-to-width-down/300?cb=20240415195913)](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/starwars/images/d/d2/Acme-DarkWarrior.png/revision/latest?cb=20240415195913) 

In addition to his signature mask, Kylo Ren wore black robes and padded armor during his time as a Knight of Ren.

 

The Knights of Ren wore battered, hand-crafted armor that, combined with their weapons and battle helms, added to the aura of dread surrounding them. Being well acquainted with violence and strife, they wore the scars and implements of such on their armor. They appeared intimidatingly unified at first glance, but on closer examination each Knight was distinct, expressing preferences in combat style and tactics. All of their gear was custom, created through a combination of originality and heavy modification upon capture.[[2]](#cite_note-TROS_VD-2)

Ap'lek wore lightweight [plastoid](/wiki/Plastoid "Plastoid") armor beneath his tunic and a [water](/wiki/Water "Water")-shedding oiled [cloak](/wiki/Cloak "Cloak"). His gauntlets and greaves featured raised teeth to assist in parrying and pinning edged attacks. He also wore a [smoke canister](/wiki/Smoke_canister "Smoke canister") on his [belt](/wiki/Belt "Belt"). Cardo's [coat](/wiki/Jacket "Jacket") was [flame](/wiki/Fire "Fire") resistant and made from the hide of [riftiaworm](/wiki/Riftiaworm "Riftiaworm"). His accessories included a [blaster pistol](/wiki/Blaster_pistol "Blaster pistol") [holster](/wiki/Holster "Holster") on his left [leg](/wiki/Limb "Limb"), tri-braced [plasteel](/wiki/Plasteel "Plasteel")-armored greaves, a chest strap to carry [concussion grenades](/wiki/Concussion_grenade "Concussion grenade"), and [plasma](/wiki/Plasma "Plasma") bolt shells on his lower chestplate. Kuruk's [attire](/wiki/Clothing "Clothing") was made of a sound-dampening [susurra-weave](/wiki/Susurra-weave "Susurra-weave") fabric. He had soft-soled [boots](/wiki/Boot "Boot") for silent tread and collar rings to carry his plasma bolt shells. Trudgen's [robes](/wiki/Robe "Robe") consisted of a heavy weave tabard and hood. It was accessorized with armor plates that were taken from his fallen enemies. Ushar wore [ironweave](/wiki/Ironweave "Ironweave") [vambraces](/wiki/Vambrace "Vambrace"), which made for a crushing backhand. Vicrul's coat was made from the skin of an unknown, monstrously large reptile.[[2]](#cite_note-TROS_VD-2)

Like the other Knights,[[6]](#cite_note-Episode_IX-6) Kylo Ren was dressed in [black](/wiki/Color "Color")[[30]](#cite_note-The_Children_from_Tehar-30) robes and armor.[[7]](#cite_note-Episode_VII-7) His cowl was a remnant from his early training, the attached [cape](/wiki/Cape "Cape") was singed from multiple battles, and the robes concealed his [padded armor](/wiki/Padded_armor "Padded armor").[[14]](#cite_note-TFA_VD-14) He later replaced his old cape and cowl with a hoodless cape that was made of static-dampening fabric, grounding electrical interference.[[31]](#cite_note-TLJ_VD-31) After seizing control of the First Order, Ren donned a treated cloak that was flame retardant.[[2]](#cite_note-TROS_VD-2)

#### Helmets[]

:   "*Here, relax. The mask's kind of intense—that's the point—but you and me, maybe we're gonna be friends. Snoke thinks so, anyway.*"
:   ―Ren, to Ben Solo[[26]](#cite_note-Rise_3-26)

 [![The Knights of Ren wore masks and battle helms, equating anonymity with intimidation.](data:image/gif;base64,R0lGODlhAQABAIABAAAAAP///yH5BAEAAAEALAAAAAABAAEAQAICTAEAOw%3D%3D)](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/starwars/images/6/6b/KnightsofRen-TSWB.png/revision/latest?cb=20220417190241) [![The Knights of Ren wore masks and battle helms, equating anonymity with intimidation.](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/starwars/images/6/6b/KnightsofRen-TSWB.png/revision/latest/scale-to-width-down/250?cb=20220417190241)](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/starwars/images/6/6b/KnightsofRen-TSWB.png/revision/latest?cb=20220417190241) 

The Knights of Ren wore masks and battle helms, equating anonymity with intimidation.

 

The Knights concealed their faces beneath ominous masks, echoing the [helmet of Kylo Ren](/wiki/Kylo_Ren%27s_helmet "Kylo Ren's helmet") himself.[[13]](#cite_note-Ultimate_Star_Wars-13) The Knights purposely shielded their features with masks and battle helms, using theatricality to enhance their fearsome appearance and generate greater levels of terror in their opponents. They believed that anonymity equaled intimidation, for their victims would project the most terrifying nightmares into the absent faces of the Knights who struck them down.[[2]](#cite_note-TROS_VD-2) The purpose of their masks predated Kylo, as the Knights had been concealing their faces since the time of Ren, their original leader. In situations where intimidation did not suit their purpose, the Knights would remove their helmets, as Ren did for Solo,[[26]](#cite_note-Rise_3-26) who, later as Kylo, revealed his own face to the [scavenger](/wiki/Scavenger "Scavenger") [Rey](/wiki/Rey_Skywalker "Rey Skywalker").[[7]](#cite_note-Episode_VII-7) [General](/wiki/General "General") [Armitage Hux](/wiki/Armitage_Hux "Armitage Hux") hated that the Knights covered their faces with masks, but speculated it was to hide that they were hideous and scarred beyond recognition.[[5]](#cite_note-TROS_Expanded_Edition-5)

Each helmet was unique to the individual Knight. Cardo's helmet was the most basic of the Knight helms; it was a blast furnace plate molded into a mask and included a neck protector. Ushar's helmet featured an impact crumble left by an opponent, and had [breathing tubes](/wiki/Breathing_tube "Breathing tube") as well as anti-ox filters suggesting that Ushar or his mask was of non-[human](/wiki/Human "Human") origin. Vicrul's case-hardened helmet was made of [pastillion ore](/wiki/Pastillion_ore "Pastillion ore") and featured synth-[leather](/wiki/Leather "Leather") ear flaps to conceal its opening seam.[[2]](#cite_note-TROS_VD-2)

The patchwork helmet of Trudgen, the Knight who collected trophies from his victims, incorporated a fragment of a [death trooper](/wiki/Death_trooper "Death trooper")'s helmet into its design. Kuruk's helmet had a breath screen and vocoder grid as well as blinder flannels designed to focus his vision forward and recesses cut for peripheral line-of-sight. Ap'lek wore a patchwork mask that he created by cobbling together pieces of broken armor. The faceplate was molded into the shape of an unsettling, sinister face of a trickster.[[2]](#cite_note-TROS_VD-2)

Like his fellow Knights, Ren wore a helmet that concealed his features. Red markings adorned the helmet's faceplate.[[8]](#cite_note-Rise_1-8)

 [![Kylo Ren's combat helmet was modeled on the battle gear of the Knights of Ren.](data:image/gif;base64,R0lGODlhAQABAIABAAAAAP///yH5BAEAAAEALAAAAAABAAEAQAICTAEAOw%3D%3D)](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/starwars/images/1/19/KyloRensHelmet-TFA.png/revision/latest?cb=20180325032840) [![Kylo Ren's combat helmet was modeled on the battle gear of the Knights of Ren.](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/starwars/images/1/19/KyloRensHelmet-TFA.png/revision/latest/scale-to-width-down/250?cb=20180325032840)](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/starwars/images/1/19/KyloRensHelmet-TFA.png/revision/latest?cb=20180325032840) 

Kylo Ren's combat helmet was modeled on the battle gear of the Knights of Ren.

 

Kylo Ren's helmet was patterned after the battle gear of the Knights of Ren; it served the twofold purpose of concealing his identity and enhancing his imposing demeanor. Servomotors were attached to the helmet, controlling articulated arms that separated the face mask from the helmet, which allowed Kylo to remove his helmet revealing the uncontained malice in his expression. The [silver](/wiki/Silver "Silver") inlay visor radiated from the eyes as a symbol of power, and the helmet's integrated vocabulator projected Kylo's voice.[[14]](#cite_note-TFA_VD-14) Beneath the mask's theatrics, however, he was still Ben Solo—son of [Han Solo](/wiki/Han_Solo "Han Solo") and [Princess](/wiki/Princess "Princess") [Leia Organa](/wiki/Leia_Skywalker_Organa_Solo "Leia Skywalker Organa Solo").[[32]](#cite_note-Battlefront_II-32) In a fit of rage, Kylo smashed the[[31]](#cite_note-TLJ_VD-31) battered combat helmet[[14]](#cite_note-TFA_VD-14) until nothing but shards remained.[[31]](#cite_note-TLJ_VD-31)

Once free of Snoke's rule, however, Kylo reclaimed his shattered helmet[[2]](#cite_note-TROS_VD-2) and gave the fragments to Albrekh so that it could be reconstructed.[[6]](#cite_note-Episode_IX-6) The eccentric metalsmith[[23]](#cite_note-Albrekh-23) applied his skills in [Sith alchemy](/wiki/Sith_alchemy "Sith alchemy") to fuse the shards back together. The reforged helmet symbolized the breaking and rebuilding of Ren's own identity. It was also stronger than before due to the alchemical lattice of Sarrassian [iron](/wiki/Iron "Iron") that Albrekh painstakingly applied throughout the helmet's cracks. As a side effect, the helmet now featured a webwork of luminous [red](/wiki/Color "Color") Sarrassian iron holding the shards together.[[2]](#cite_note-TROS_VD-2)

Each Knight was armed with a unique and lethally effective weapon, suited to both close-quarter and long range combat.[[13]](#cite_note-Ultimate_Star_Wars-13) Ushar and Ap'lek favored primitive [melee weapons](/wiki/Melee_weapon "Melee weapon"), such as weighted clubs and axes, with crude technological enhancements. The muscle-power of their wielders alone was enough to ensure that these weapons delivered crushing blows. However, with the flick of a switch or the twist of a dial, they took on additional attributes that could make the difference in battle. [Ushar's war club](/wiki/Ushar%27s_war_club "Ushar's war club") had a [kinetite](/wiki/Kinetite "Kinetite") charged end with concussion vector vanes and was held with a firm grip on the blunt, studded end. The [war club](/wiki/War_club "War club") had a studded pummel and a concussion absorption head. Its ability to build up a concussion charge in a [kinetite generator](/wiki/Kinetite_generator "Kinetite generator") could be released in a sudden shockwave. In addition, Ushar carried a [vibromachete](/wiki/Vibro-machete "Vibro-machete") in an inverted sheath on his torso as well as three [thermal detonators](/wiki/Thermal_detonator "Thermal detonator"). [Ap'lek's long ax](/wiki/Ap%27lek%27s_Mandalorian_executioner_vibro-ax "Ap'lek's Mandalorian executioner vibro-ax") was an antiquated [executioner](/wiki/Death_penalty "Death penalty") vibro-ax of Mandalorian origin. The weapon was modified with various upgrades, such as an [ultrasonic generator](/wiki/Ultrasonic_generator "Ultrasonic generator") cable, making it a conduit for ultrasonic energy, and therefore, vibrating its sharpened end to enhance its cutting power through armor and [bone](/wiki/Bone "Bone"). It was also equipped with vibro-insulator plates to protect the handle. The blade was made from [beskar](/wiki/Beskar "Beskar") with gore channels cut into the surface.[[2]](#cite_note-TROS_VD-2)

Vicrul and Trudgen wielded blade weapons, both of which featured ultrasonic vibro-tech settings that increased their lethality to the point where even glancing hits could cause fatal lacerations. The blades were made of crucible [steel](/wiki/Steel "Steel") saturated with [phrik](/wiki/Phrik "Phrik") or beskar filaments, granting them considerable strength and durability. Though a lightsaber could cut through these weapons, it required a greater application of pressure from the wielder.[[2]](#cite_note-TROS_VD-2)

Unlike the other Knights who favored melee weapons for closequarter combat, Kuruk and Cardo brandished [blaster](/wiki/Blaster "Blaster") weaponry—heavily modified and customized to their personal preferences. As such, [Kuruk's blaster rifle](/wiki/Kuruk%27s_blaster_rifle "Kuruk's blaster rifle") and [Cardo's arm cannon](/wiki/Cardo%27s_arm_cannon "Cardo's arm cannon") were unique hodgepodge constructions. The lower barrel was a flammable gel projector. The laser [cannon](/wiki/Cannon "Cannon") featured an air-cooled barrel; its front view had piezoelectric flame projectors flanking the recessed gel spinneret and a forward handgrip and trigger selector. The weapon's bolts had casing to contain the plasma charge and rifling grooves for increased accuracy.[[2]](#cite_note-TROS_VD-2)

## History[]

### Origins[]

:   "*Those who dwell in the Unknown Regions have long whispered legends of the Knights of Ren. This band of marauders was said to have burned their way across worlds, consuming everything in their path. In recent years, an enclave of warriors stepped from the darkness to claim that name—and the fear it instilled—as their own.*"
:   ―Darth Sidious[[22]](#cite_note-SotS-22)

 [![The Knights of Ren took their name from an ancient group of legendary warriors.](data:image/gif;base64,R0lGODlhAQABAIABAAAAAP///yH5BAEAAAEALAAAAAABAAEAQAICTAEAOw%3D%3D)](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/starwars/images/2/23/Knights_of_Ren.png/revision/latest?cb=20160416230650) [![The Knights of Ren took their name from an ancient group of legendary warriors.](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/starwars/images/2/23/Knights_of_Ren.png/revision/latest/scale-to-width-down/250?cb=20160416230650)](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/starwars/images/2/23/Knights_of_Ren.png/revision/latest?cb=20160416230650) 

The Knights of Ren took their name from an ancient group of legendary warriors.

 

The Knights of Ren were marauders who pillaged worlds, leaving a trail of destruction in their wake.[[22]](#cite_note-SotS-22) Their exploits were legendary by the time of the [New Republic](/wiki/New_Republic "New Republic"), particularly among the disparate settlements of the Unknown Regions where the Knights became mythologized over the centuries.[[2]](#cite_note-TROS_VD-2) The name "Knights of Ren" became one synonymous with fear.[[22]](#cite_note-SotS-22) At some stage, a [Knight of Ren](/wiki/Unidentified_Knight_of_Ren "Unidentified Knight of Ren")[[36]](#cite_note-EK-36) traveled to a [Jedi temple](/wiki/Unidentified_Jedi_temple_(Qi%27ra) "Unidentified Jedi temple (Qi'ra)") in search of a Jedi artifact. Instead, however, they would fall to the temple's defenses, leaving their corpse behind to be whittled away over time.[[37]](#cite_note-CC-37) Around [12 BBY](/wiki/12_BBY "12 BBY"),[[38]](#cite_note-12_BBY_Date-38) [Crimson Dawn](/wiki/Crimson_Dawn "Crimson Dawn") member [Qi'ra](/wiki/Qi%27ra "Qi'ra") came across the remains of[[37]](#cite_note-CC-37) the Knight.[[36]](#cite_note-EK-36) She was unsure of to what group the deceased warrior belonged, with her comrade [Trinia](/wiki/Trinia "Trinia") only able to deduce they had belonged to some kind of religious order.[[37]](#cite_note-CC-37)

By the time of the Imperial Era, a group of masked warriors[[39]](#cite_note-WotBH_5-39) emerged claiming to be the fabled Knights of Ren.[[22]](#cite_note-SotS-22) Like their ancient namesakes, the modern Knights of Ren were marauders who acquired their possessions through force, including a prison ship that they confiscated during the [raid on Osseriton](/wiki/Raid_on_Osseriton "Raid on Osseriton"). The Knights had been hired to attack the penal world of Osseriton; they released the prisoners and claimed one of the transports as their own. The ship was modified and christened as the *Night Buzzard*.[[2]](#cite_note-TROS_VD-2) Aside from the Sith, the Knights of Ren were one of the few groups of "dark side practitioners" during the Imperial Era. While the dark side gave them strength,[[10]](#cite_note-Crimson_Reign_1-10) their ability to harness its power was severely limited in comparison to the Sith, largely due to their lack of training or knowledge unlike the more established orders of the Sith or Jedi.[[22]](#cite_note-SotS-22) However, according to "[the Archivist](/wiki/The_Archivist "The Archivist")" the Knights were the only beings whose strength and pride emboldened them to openly contend with the Sith.[[10]](#cite_note-Crimson_Reign_1-10) The Archivist shared what she knew about the Knights of Ren with her benefactor: the then-Lady Qi'ra, who by that time was the leader of Crimson Dawn. Qi'ra decided to use the information to recruit the Knights as part of her plans against the Sith.[[3]](#cite_note-Crimson_Reign_4-3)

### Imperial Era[]

#### Alliance with Crimson Dawn[]

:   "*In a galaxy of countless living, unique beings,* one man *decides how we all live or die. A* Sith*. He will take what he wants from us. Now and forever. He stole your pride, your ambition.*"  
    "*Damn right he did.*"
:   ―Qi'ra and Ren, referring to Darth Sidious[[10]](#cite_note-Crimson_Reign_1-10)

 [![The Knights of Ren joined Crimson Dawn and its allies against the Sith-controlled Galactic Empire.](data:image/gif;base64,R0lGODlhAQABAIABAAAAAP///yH5BAEAAAEALAAAAAABAAEAQAICTAEAOw%3D%3D)](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/starwars/images/6/6d/CrimsonDawnAllies-CrimsonReign1.png/revision/latest?cb=20211209221436) [![The Knights of Ren joined Crimson Dawn and its allies against the Sith-controlled Galactic Empire.](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/starwars/images/6/6d/CrimsonDawnAllies-CrimsonReign1.png/revision/latest/scale-to-width-down/250?cb=20211209221436)](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/starwars/images/6/6d/CrimsonDawnAllies-CrimsonReign1.png/revision/latest?cb=20211209221436) 

The Knights of Ren joined Crimson Dawn and its allies against the Sith-controlled Galactic Empire.

 

During the [Galactic Civil War](/wiki/Galactic_Civil_War "Galactic Civil War") between the [Alliance to Restore the Republic](/wiki/Alliance_to_Restore_the_Republic "Alliance to Restore the Republic") and the Galactic Empire, the Knights of Ren were allies of the Crimson Dawn crime syndicate.[[39]](#cite_note-WotBH_5-39) Seeking to recruit Force-sensitives, Qi'ra approached the Knights and their leader on the planet [Varnak](/wiki/Varnak "Varnak") in the [Mid Rim Territories](/wiki/Mid_Rim_Territories "Mid Rim Territories"), where she proposed an alliance with the goal of ending the Sith hegemony over the galaxy. After the fall of more powerful Force-sensitive groups such as the Jedi Order and the [Nightsisters](/wiki/Nightsisters "Nightsisters"), Ren was certain that the Sith would eliminate the Knights of Ren unless they struck first by following Qi'ra's plan.[[3]](#cite_note-Crimson_Reign_4-3)

Following the [auction for Han Solo](/wiki/Auction_for_Han_Solo "Auction for Han Solo") and the [battle](/wiki/Attack_on_the_Executor "Attack on the Executor") over [Jekara](/wiki/Jekara "Jekara"), the Knights joined Qi'ra and her other allies aboard the Crimson Dawn [flagship](/wiki/Flagship "Flagship"), the [*Vermillion*](/wiki/Vermillion_(Crimson_Dawn) "Vermillion (Crimson Dawn)"), where she told them of how she masterminded a conflict between the Empire and the [Hutt Clan](/wiki/Hutt_Clan "Hutt Clan"). Though she admitted she had hoped Han Solo, then trapped within [carbonite](/wiki/Carbonite "Carbonite"), would be regained by the Rebels, she did not allow their loss—as the [bounty hunter](/wiki/Bounty_hunter "Bounty hunter") [Boba Fett](/wiki/Boba_Fett "Boba Fett") had reclaimed the [smuggler](/wiki/Smuggler "Smuggler") instead—to hinder their plans, telling the Knights and her other allies that the reign of Crimson Dawn was to begin.[[39]](#cite_note-WotBH_5-39)

The Knights of Ren, having survived the Empire's purge of Force-sensitive groups in the galaxy, were utilized by Qi'ra who schemed to overthrow [Dark Lord of the Sith](/wiki/Dark_Lord_of_the_Sith "Dark Lord of the Sith") Darth Sidious and his student Darth Vader. The leader of Crimson Dawn revealed the origins of her organization to her allies, including the Knights of Ren, beginning with her predecessor [Maul](/wiki/Maul "Maul"), whom she learned was a former [Sith apprentice](/wiki/Sith_apprentice "Sith apprentice") of Sidious. Knowing that Sidious was the Sith and real name of [Galactic Emperor](/wiki/Galactic_Emperor "Galactic Emperor") Sheev Palpatine, Qi'ra came to realize that the Sith ruled the galaxy through the Empire. As such, she believed that no other group could grow more powerful as long as the Sith maintained their galactic hegemony. Qi'ra therefore recruited the Knights of Ren, the [Orphans](/wiki/Orphans "Orphans"), [Deathstick](/wiki/Deathstick "Deathstick"), [Ochi](/wiki/Ochi "Ochi") of [Bestoon](/wiki/Bestoon "Bestoon"), and other allies to overthrow the [Emperor](/wiki/Emperor "Emperor") and defeat Vader, convinced that Crimson Dawn and their allies would prosper in a galaxy without the Dark Lord of the Sith or his current student.[[10]](#cite_note-Crimson_Reign_1-10)

#### Infiltrating Fortress Vader[]

##### Journey to Mustafar[]

:   "*This is the biggest job we'll ever pull, no question about it.* Fortress Vader*. We'll be* legends*. I just don't know how we'll do it.*"
:   ―Ren[[10]](#cite_note-Crimson_Reign_1-10)

 [![At the behest of Lady Qi'ra, the Knights of Ren planned to infiltrate Fortress Vader.](data:image/gif;base64,R0lGODlhAQABAIABAAAAAP///yH5BAEAAAEALAAAAAABAAEAQAICTAEAOw%3D%3D)](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/starwars/images/8/81/KnightsOfRenFortressVader-CrimsonReign1.png/revision/latest?cb=20211209215717) [![At the behest of Lady Qi'ra, the Knights of Ren planned to infiltrate Fortress Vader.](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/starwars/images/8/81/KnightsOfRenFortressVader-CrimsonReign1.png/revision/latest/scale-to-width-down/250?cb=20211209215717)](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/starwars/images/8/81/KnightsOfRenFortressVader-CrimsonReign1.png/revision/latest?cb=20211209215717) 

At the behest of Lady Qi'ra, the Knights of Ren planned to infiltrate Fortress Vader.

 

Qi'ra's plan appealed to the Knights of Ren, whose leader, "Ren," concurred with her statement that Sidious had "stolen their pride and ambition." With the Sith and the Empire distracted by a syndicate war that Qi'ra started, the Knights agreed to [steal](/wiki/Heist_on_Vader%27s_Castle "Heist on Vader's Castle")[[10]](#cite_note-Crimson_Reign_1-10) the [Screaming Key](/wiki/Screaming_Key "Screaming Key") from the vaults of [Fortress Vader](/wiki/Fortress_Vader "Fortress Vader") on [Mustafar](/wiki/Mustafar "Mustafar").[[3]](#cite_note-Crimson_Reign_4-3) Their compensation would be anything that they could steal for themselves. Ren accepted the mission, believing that Vader's castle was a site worth looting. Marinda agreed with Ren's assessment of the target's worth, preferring it to their usual activities such as scavenging for kyber crystals in ancient ruins.[[10]](#cite_note-Crimson_Reign_1-10)

Ren and Marinda sparred on the *Night Buzzard* while en route to their destination. Ren confessed to being impressed by Qi'ra's resourcefulness, despite her lack of Force-sensitivity, as she had provided the Knights with access codes along with a map of the fortress. Though they lacked a plan, Ren was confident that his Knights would become living legends for infiltrating Vader's stronghold.[[10]](#cite_note-Crimson_Reign_1-10)

The Knights of Ren landed the *Night Buzzard* in the [Gahenn Plains](/wiki/Gahenn_Plains "Gahenn Plains") of Mustafar and proceeded toward Fortress Vader from the south, avoiding the Imperial garrison on the far side of the fortress. They also used [signal jammers](/wiki/Jammer "Jammer") on their [speeder bikes](/wiki/Speeder_bike "Speeder bike") to evade Imperial [sensors](/wiki/Sensor "Sensor") as they approached the fortress' location. The Knights entered the fortress through an old unguarded entrance that led to the [Sith cave](/wiki/Sith_cave "Sith cave") upon which Vader's castle was built. The entrance to the cave was blocked by [lava](/wiki/Magma "Magma"), but the Knights were able to create an opening with their combined use of telekinesis. They encountered an apparition of the [Mask of Lord Momin](/wiki/Mask_of_Lord_Momin "Mask of Lord Momin") inside the [cave](/wiki/Cave "Cave"). Startled by the sudden movement of [Darth Momin](/wiki/Darth_Momin "Darth Momin")'s mask, the Knight known as Bazzra shot it. Ren however was less concerned about "dead things" haunting a Sith castle, and warned Bazzra to be more concerned about the fortress' occupants who were still alive.[[3]](#cite_note-Crimson_Reign_4-3)

##### Theft of the Screaming Key[]

:   "*And, lest we forget, the theft of the* Screaming Key *from your fortress on Mustafar…*your own stronghold*…by the* Knights of Ren*. Weaklings who can barely use the dark side…and yet somehow they were brazen enough to attack you directly.*"
:   ―Darth Sidious, to Darth Vader[[33]](#cite_note-Crimson_Reign_5-33)

The Knights of Ren successfully infiltrated the castle proper through the cave that was part of its foundation. The route to the castle's vault passed through a storage room where the Knights discovered an [army](/wiki/Army "Army")'s worth of [bacta](/wiki/Bacta "Bacta"). The Knight known as Fyodor took the opportunity to spit in Vader's bacta supply before continuing toward the vault with his companions and their leader.[[3]](#cite_note-Crimson_Reign_4-3)

The heist at Fortress Vader culminated in a lightsaber duel between Ren and the Sith Lord Darth Vader.

 

Ren warned the Knights to exercise caution while passing through the most exposed area on the route. By then, however, they were discovered by Vader's [attendant](/wiki/Aide "Aide"), [Vaneé](/wiki/Vane%C3%A9 "Vaneé") who summoned the [fortress garrison's](/wiki/Fortress_Vader_Imperial_garrison "Fortress Vader Imperial garrison") [lava troopers](/wiki/Volcanic_environment_stormtrooper "Volcanic environment stormtrooper") to kill the intruders. The Knights killed a large number of Imperial troops while Ren moved to complete the mission, using the Force to locate the vault that contained the Screaming Key. Upon acquiring the item, however, the remaining troopers fell back as Vader arrived to deal with the Knights himself. The Knight known as Fyodor was killed by the Dark Lord, causing Ren to order his Knights to retreat. He covered their escape by dueling with Vader who considered his opponent unworthy of wielding a red lightsaber, and his followers nothing more than a "pathetic rabble." Ren nonetheless survived the duel and escaped with his remaining Knights and the Screaming Key.[[3]](#cite_note-Crimson_Reign_4-3)

Vader reported to his master, the Emperor, at the Imperial Palace on Coruscant, in the wake of the Knights' theft of the Screaming Key. Sidious berated his apprentice for not preventing the Knights from stealing the key. He regarded the Knights of Ren as weaklings with a modicum of ability to use the dark side, and was confounded by the brazenness of their attack on Vader within his own stronghold. However, after conferring with [Director](/wiki/Director "Director") [Sliro Barsha](/wiki/Sliro_Barsha "Sliro Barsha") of the [Imperial Security Bureau](/wiki/Imperial_Security_Bureau "Imperial Security Bureau"), Sidious sensed that Crimson Dawn was responsible for certain events in the galaxy, including the Knights' heist on Mustafar as well as the [Syndicate War](/wiki/Syndicate_War "Syndicate War"), the [Grand Hutt Council](/wiki/Grand_Hutt_Council "Grand Hutt Council")'s [attack](/wiki/Attack_on_the_Executor "Attack on the Executor") on the *[Executor](/wiki/Executor "Executor")*, and the [assassination](/wiki/Assassination_of_the_Emperor%27s_Royal_Guard "Assassination of the Emperor's Royal Guard") of the [Emperor's Royal Guards](/wiki/Emperor%27s_Royal_Guard "Emperor's Royal Guard"). The two Sith Lords also deduced that the Knights' benefactor, Lady Qi'ra was mentored by Sidious' former apprentice, Maul, given her knowledge of the [Teräs Käsi](/wiki/Ter%C3%A4s_K%C3%A4si "Teräs Käsi") fighting style.[[33]](#cite_note-Crimson_Reign_5-33)

They would be joined by [Kho Phon Farrus](/wiki/Kho_Phon_Farrus "Kho Phon Farrus"), an expert in [Ascendant](/wiki/Ascendant "Ascendant") cult who was brought in by the Archivist to help studying the cage.[[40]](#cite_note-Hidden_Empire_1-40)

Having submitted to the Sith at the end of the Crimson Dawn war,[[4]](#cite_note-Hidden_Empire_5-4) the Knights of Ren, despite Sidious's supposed demise in the [Battle of Endor](/wiki/Battle_of_Endor "Battle of Endor"), learned of his return to life with his [Sith Eternal](/wiki/Sith_Eternal "Sith Eternal") [cult](/wiki/Sith_cult "Sith cult"). The Knights thus became secret servants of the resurrected Sidious.[[5]](#cite_note-TROS_Expanded_Edition-5) They also became aware of the dark side adept Snoke, a mysterious figure who would sometimes hire their services for a substantial amount of money each time. The Knights, however, did not trust him nor know much about him;[[43]](#cite_note-Legacy_of_Vader_8-43) in secret, Snoke was a genetic [strandcast](/wiki/Strand-Cast "Strand-Cast") and another pawn of Sidious.[[21]](#cite_note-TSWB-21) The knights once sought out [Karrst](/wiki/Karrst "Karrst") and his younger brother [Filin](/wiki/Filin "Filin"), both of whom were wanted for murder by the [Sector Security](/wiki/Sector_Security "Sector Security") [organization](/wiki/Law_enforcement_agency "Law enforcement agency"). Having learned of Karrst through a friend of the Knights, they spent some time looking for the siblings, who were almost captured by [Sector](/wiki/Sector "Sector") Security when the dark gang found them. The Knights immediately descended on the [lawmen](/wiki/Police_officer "Police officer"), pushing them back and establishing a secure perimeter around the brothers. Using their weapons and Force abilities, the six Knights slaughtered the SecSec team while their spokesman, the warrior known as "Ren," approached the would-be prisoners. Ren assessed the situation and concluded that SecSec was determined to apprehend Karrst and Filin, who were responsible for killing at least a hundred people. Though the Knights were also after them, they did not wish to capture or kill them. Instead, they wanted Karrst to join their ranks.[[8]](#cite_note-Rise_1-8)

Karrst was like the Knights, possessing an inherent sensitivity to the Force or "shadow" as the Knights referred to its dark side. Karrst was able to demonstrate his natural ability with [telekinesis](/wiki/Telekinesis "Telekinesis"). His talents impressed Ren and demonstrated what the Knights learned about him from their contact—that Karrst could touch the shadow. Filin pledged his allegiance and that of his brother to the Knights, promising to follow Ren if the Knights helped them to escape from the SecSec forces. However, Ren clarified that it was not he whom the Knights followed, but rather his lightsaber and the way of life that it represented.[[8]](#cite_note-Rise_1-8)

Ren devoted his life to living by the Knights' philosophy; immersing himself in the shadow, he fought its battles, preserved its artifacts, and consumed the power it gave him. He invited Karrst to do the same, but first they required a sacrifice—the murder of his brother. Before Karrst could make his choice, Filin fatally shot his older brother. In doing so, he believed the Knights would let him join their ranks in Karrst's place. While Ren applauded Filin's actions, respecting his willingness to commit fratricide in order to survive, he ultimately killed Filin, who was not [born](/wiki/Reproduction "Reproduction") with a strong connection to the Force. Trudgen noted that the mission did not go as planned, but it was of little consequence to Ren, who believed that their master would eventually find someone else for them to recruit. In the meantime, they sought to cause destruction elsewhere, leaving the ground littered with the corpses of the entire SecSec team and those of the now dead siblings.[[8]](#cite_note-Rise_1-8)

The Knights of Ren [ventured](/wiki/Mission_to_Elphrona "Mission to Elphrona") to the [Outer Rim](/wiki/Outer_Rim_Territories "Outer Rim Territories") [world](/wiki/Planet "Planet") of [Elphrona](/wiki/Elphrona "Elphrona") and found an ancient [Jedi outpost](/wiki/Elphrona_Outpost "Elphrona Outpost") [dating back](/wiki/Timeline_of_galactic_history "Timeline of galactic history") to the time of the [High Republic Era](/wiki/High_Republic_Era "High Republic Era"). The Jedi of the time used it as a storage site, and having never returned to gather their items, it was more of a treasure trove than an outpost. Among its artifacts were [Jedi holocrons](/wiki/Jedi_holocron "Jedi holocron") and [exotic weapons](/wiki/Lightsaber_rifle "Lightsaber rifle"). Ren and his Knights intended to raid the outpost and take everything of value. However, the outpost was also visited by Jedi Master Luke Skywalker, his young [apprentice](/wiki/Padawan "Padawan") Ben Solo, and the [explorer](/wiki/Scout "Scout") [Lor San Tekka](/wiki/Lor_San_Tekka "Lor San Tekka"). In addition to mentoring his nephew in the ways of the Force, Skywalker scoured the galaxy searching for arcane lore that could aid his quest to rebuild the Jedi Order. Shortly after entering the outpost, Skywalker and Solo both sensed the the Knights of Ren emanating with the cold presence of the dark side.[[20]](#cite_note-Rise_2-20)

Skywalker claimed the outpost and its treasures as the property of the Jedi Order, which, at the time, consisted of himself as the last Jedi. Notwithstanding his claim to Jedi artifacts, Skywalker had no intention of allowing them to fall into the Knights' possession, having sensed the dark side in all of them. Ren regarded Skywalker as an old fool, believing that a single Jedi was no match against the combined power of his Knights. When Solo stepped forward, introducing his master and calling him a legend, Ren simply introduced himself and the warriors under his command as the legendary Knights of Ren.[[20]](#cite_note-Rise_2-20)

His warnings ignored, Skywalker instructed his apprentice to protect San Tekka while he personally dealt with Ren's Knights. As the Knights and Skywalker prepared for battle, Ren was musing on how it would almost be a shame to kill the last Jedi, as it would be the first and only Jedi he would ever get to kill. Regardless, he gave the order to attack, causing the Knights to spring into action. With the exception of Ren himself, the other Knights charged in unison, assuring themselves that one man who stood alone against six warriors could not win. Though Skywalker conceded that he was indeed one man, he asserted that he was not alone when "the Force was his ally." Despite their efforts, none of the Knights' attacks connected, as Skywalker either blocked their weapons or evaded them entirely. Knowing they could not defeat him, he used [his green lightsaber](/wiki/Luke_Skywalker%27s_green_lightsaber "Luke Skywalker's green lightsaber") only as a means to defend himself and was content to defeat the Knights but without killing them.[[20]](#cite_note-Rise_2-20)

With the Knights incapacitated and their weapons destroyed, Ren stepped forward to confront Skywalker himself. Ren activated a kill switch on his lightsaber and revealed that it would explode if the weapon left his hand. However, after observing the last Jedi in battle, Ren concluded that the Force was strong with Skywalker and was no longer certain that he could prevail. Unwilling to die for the artifacts inside the outpost, Ren chose to stand down and retreat with his Knights, leaving the Jedi to claim their prize. Before departing, Ren turned his attention to Skywalker's young apprentice. He sensed that, like themselves, Solo had the presence of the shadow within himself. Ren also removed his helmet and threw it at Solo's [feet](/wiki/Foot "Foot"), telling the boy to seek him out when he was ready to join the Knights of Ren.[[20]](#cite_note-Rise_2-20)

After Solo [destroyed](/wiki/Destruction_of_the_Jedi_Temple "Destruction of the Jedi Temple") his uncle's [Jedi temple](/wiki/Jedi_Temple_of_Luke_Skywalker "Jedi Temple of Luke Skywalker"), killing all who were present at the time, he fled from the survivors who confronted him and traveled to a [space station](/wiki/Amaxine_Space_Station "Amaxine Space Station"), where Snoke awaited him with open arms. Solo struggled with feelings of betrayal, believing his uncle tried to murder him in his sleep. That turn of events,[[8]](#cite_note-Rise_1-8) combined with the expectations of being the nephew of the last Jedi and the grandson of a prophesied [Chosen One](/wiki/Chosen_One "Chosen One"),[[2]](#cite_note-TROS_VD-2) left Solo disillusioned with both the Jedi Order and his own [family](/wiki/Solo_family "Solo family").[[8]](#cite_note-Rise_1-8)

Having renouncing the ways of the Jedi, Solo confided in Snoke his interest in joining the Knights of Ren.[[8]](#cite_note-Rise_1-8) Snoke showed his approval and encouraged Solo to seek out the Knights, telling the young boy that he would not have to keep his power in check like he did while training with the Jedi. By joining the Knights, Solo not only saw a chance to learn new things about the Force, but also an opportunity to reinvent himself by adopting a new name.[[20]](#cite_note-Rise_2-20)

Solo asked for Snoke's guidance to locate the Knights of Ren; though the elder Force wielder decided that Solo's first step in his new path should be to find the group on his own, he advised Solo to start where he last encountered the Knights. Snoke also warned Solo that the Knights would exact a heavy price in exchange for admission into their ranks, and that the penalty for failing was death, but Solo was willing to pay any cost in order start his new life.[[20]](#cite_note-Rise_2-20) At the time, however, he was unaware that he would be expected to undergo a particular Sith trial of ascendancy—the sacrifice of someone he cared about.[[2]](#cite_note-TROS_VD-2)

### The Rise of Kylo Ren[]

#### The glory days[]

:   "…glory. *We went where we wanted, did what we wanted.* Took *what we wanted. Credits, artifacts…and* life*. Lots and lots of life. Kylo Ren was real good at that. Good times, for a while. Great times, really. But as the months went on, Kylo started to change. He was never the life of the party, if you know what I mean, but…yeah. He started spending a lot of time alone. We'd see him talking to himself—or maybe just someone we couldn't hear. Even for us, he got…dark.*"
:   ―Cardo[[43]](#cite_note-Legacy_of_Vader_8-43)

Over the course of many months, Ren led his Knights to what they saw as glory. The group continued to act as marauders, claiming credits, artifacts, and many lives. One incident saw the Knights rob a large vault full of credits.[[43]](#cite_note-Legacy_of_Vader_8-43) Ren also adopted the Knights' taste for theatricality, using a shrouded and masked visage to impose fear in the tradition of his ancestor, Darth Vader.[[2]](#cite_note-TROS_VD-2)

 [![Rejecting his former identity, the fallen Jedi Ben Solo adopted the persona of Kylo Ren.](data:image/gif;base64,R0lGODlhAQABAIABAAAAAP///yH5BAEAAAEALAAAAAABAAEAQAICTAEAOw%3D%3D)](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/starwars/images/3/37/Decisive_Blow_SoR.png/revision/latest?cb=20190125052903) [![Rejecting his former identity, the fallen Jedi Ben Solo adopted the persona of Kylo Ren.](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/starwars/images/3/37/Decisive_Blow_SoR.png/revision/latest/scale-to-width-down/250?cb=20190125052903)](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/starwars/images/3/37/Decisive_Blow_SoR.png/revision/latest?cb=20190125052903) 

Rejecting his former identity, the fallen Jedi Ben Solo adopted the persona of Kylo Ren.

 

Kylo Ren became Snoke's apprentice as well as a [warlord](/wiki/Warlord "Warlord") and champion of the First Order. Snoke used his apprentice and the Knights as a tool for the dark side within the First Order.[[14]](#cite_note-TFA_VD-14) Together with General Armitage Hux and [Captain](/wiki/Captain "Captain") [Phasma](/wiki/Phasma "Phasma"), he formed part of the vanguard of First Order leadership which was strikingly young. This new generation of adherents to the [ideals](/wiki/New_Order "New Order") of the old Empire carried a zeal that made up for their lack of experience.[[2]](#cite_note-TROS_VD-2) Ren's rise to power filled the void left by the Sith Order's destruction.[[14]](#cite_note-TFA_VD-14) While the thousand-year Sith lineage of [Darth Bane](/wiki/Darth_Bane "Darth Bane") perished with Vader, the last Sith Lord in the ancient tradition of the [Rule of Two](/wiki/Rule_of_Two "Rule of Two"), the dark side of the Force remained strong in Ren as well as his Master.[[31]](#cite_note-TLJ_VD-31) His modified lightsaber channeled dangerous amounts of power through its cracked kyber crystal.[[2]](#cite_note-TROS_VD-2)

At some point, the Knights of Ren went to the planet Archaidan, a world where the [Emperor’s Royal Guards](/wiki/Emperor%27s_Royal_Guard "Emperor's Royal Guard") used to train, to recruit a former royal guard to join Snoke's [Praetorian Guard](/wiki/Praetorian_Guard "Praetorian Guard"). The Guard refused, resulting in the Knights slaughtering him. [[44]](#cite_note-Path_of_the_Lightsaber_vol_1-44)

Over time, Ren began to act differently, becoming isolated and talking to himself—or someone else unheard—when no one was around. On one occasion, the Knights of Ren partied at a cantina while Ren himself sat back on his own, only for Ren to don his helmet and massacre the other guests. Over time, Ren began to bring his Knights on missions with much clearer purposes, which signaled to the Knights that Ren was acting on behalf of a master—who was in truth Snoke—who needed them to take out particular targets. While the Knights felt as if they were being treated like puppets, they refused to question Ren lest he kill them.[[43]](#cite_note-Legacy_of_Vader_8-43)

Ren was able to kill the warlord, running him through with his lightsaber. Then, sensing a presence the other Knights could not, he stepped forward and approached Snoke, who had arrived in a shuttle with his Praetorian guard and a contingent of stormtroopers.[[43]](#cite_note-Legacy_of_Vader_8-43)

Vicrul began to retort, only for Snoke to strike him down with a blast of Force lighting. Calling the group "the rats of the dark side," Snoke told Ren to be pleased for having brought order to their chaos, but that that chapter was at an end. The Knights fought back, but without Vicrul and Ren, were forced to retreat.[[43]](#cite_note-Legacy_of_Vader_8-43)

In the last days of the [First Order-Resistance War](/wiki/First_Order-Resistance_War "First Order-Resistance War"), the reborn Darth Sidious [broadcasted his message of revenge](/wiki/Palpatine%27s_broadcast "Palpatine's broadcast") throughout the galaxy[[6]](#cite_note-Episode_IX-6) in [35 ABY](/wiki/35_ABY "35 ABY").[[50]](#cite_note-TROS_Date-50) Unwilling to suffer any threat to his reign, Kylo Ren made it his mission to personally investigate the mysterious message.[[6]](#cite_note-Episode_IX-6) He ultimately learned that the former Emperor had in fact returned, despite his demise at the hands of his own Sith apprentice, Kylo's grandfather.[[6]](#cite_note-Episode_IX-6) He also discovered that Sidious' followers had created the [Final Order](/wiki/Final_Order "Final Order"), a [fleet](/wiki/Fleet "Fleet") comprising [planet killer](/wiki/Axial_superlaser "Axial superlaser") [*Xyston*-class Star Destroyers](/wiki/Xyston-class_Star_Destroyer "Xyston-class Star Destroyer") and an [army](/wiki/Sith_Eternal_army "Sith Eternal army") of elite [Sith troopers](/wiki/Sith_trooper "Sith trooper").[[2]](#cite_note-TROS_VD-2) Sidious offered control of the Sith Eternal's military forces to Kylo, which would give the Supreme Leader the resources he needed to complete his conquest of the galaxy. Additionally, the Sith Lord designated Kylo as his successor,[[6]](#cite_note-Episode_IX-6) making the grandson of Vader the heir to the new [Sith Empire](/wiki/Sith_Empire "Sith Empire") formed in the Unknown Regions.[[2]](#cite_note-TROS_VD-2) As a condition, however, Kylo had to kill Rey—thereby destroying the last remnant of the Jedi Order—in order to assume command of the Sith fleet as the new Galactic Emperor.[[6]](#cite_note-Episode_IX-6)

Despite their [militarization](/wiki/Project_Resurrection "Project Resurrection") efforts, the First Order's forces were much smaller than those of their predecessor, the [Imperial Military](/wiki/Imperial_Military "Imperial Military").[[2]](#cite_note-TROS_VD-2) By augmenting their military with the Sith Eternal's [troops](/wiki/Troop "Troop") and [warships](/wiki/Warship "Warship"), the First Order would have the means to rule the galaxy as a true Empire.[[6]](#cite_note-Episode_IX-6)

However, the Supreme Council was agitated by the presence of the Knights of Ren, who were summoned by Kylo to enforce his rule as Supreme Leader. In this regard, they were Kylo's equivalent to the Elite Praetorian Guards who served as Snoke's personal bodyguards. The Knights acknowledged Kylo as the only one with the authority to command them, much to the Supreme Council's displeasure. As a [First Order officer](/wiki/First_Order_officer "First Order officer") who favored protocol and had exacting standards about his [hair](/wiki/Hair "Hair"), General Hux despised the Knights' unkempt appearance and how they treaded dirt into the polished halls of the First Order's [capital ships](/wiki/Capital_ship "Capital ship").[[2]](#cite_note-TROS_VD-2)

Traveling aboard their private transport, the *Night Buzzard*, the Knights and their master arrived on the [*Resurgent*-class Star Destroyer](/wiki/Resurgent-class_Star_Destroyer "Resurgent-class Star Destroyer") [*Steadfast*](/wiki/Steadfast_(Resurgent-class) "Steadfast (Resurgent-class)"), flagship of the First Order leadership.[[2]](#cite_note-TROS_VD-2) Before his meeting with the Supreme Council,[[6]](#cite_note-Episode_IX-6) Kylo gave the fragments of his shattered helmet to Albrekh, who reforged it by using Sith alchemy to hold the shards together. Another objective of the Supreme Leader was to find Rey, a task he personally executed alongside the Knights of Ren. He traveled to the desert planet [Pasaana](/wiki/Pasaana "Pasaana") with his Knights after learning that Rey was there through the the bond that bridged their minds. Sensing Kylo's presence nearby, Rey [confronted](/wiki/Mission_to_Pasaana_(Resistance) "Mission to Pasaana (Resistance)") him while her comrades attempted to escape on the *[Bestoon Legacy](/wiki/Bestoon_Legacy "Bestoon Legacy")*.[[6]](#cite_note-Episode_IX-6) During the Knights' hunt for the scavenger that vexed their master, Ap'lek and Kuruk stalked their prey, an unsuspecting [Chewbacca](/wiki/Chewbacca "Chewbacca"),[[2]](#cite_note-TROS_VD-2) who was subsequently captured by the First Order.[[6]](#cite_note-Episode_IX-6)

Rey and her remaining companions managed to escape from the First Order's forces on Pasaana.[[6]](#cite_note-Episode_IX-6) But while traveling to the [occupied](/wiki/Occupation_of_Kijimi "Occupation of Kijimi") world of [Kijimi](/wiki/Kijimi "Kijimi"), they were once again pursued by Kylo and his Knights, who went to extraordinary lengths in their determination to capture the scavenger. The First Order's forces also arrived on Kijimi and worked in unison with their leader's subordinate warriors, searching for leads on the scavenger and her Resistance allies.[[2]](#cite_note-TROS_VD-2) Unbeknownst to the Knights who combed through the [Thieves' Quarter](/wiki/Thieves%27_Quarter "Thieves' Quarter") of [Kijimi City](/wiki/Kijimi_City "Kijimi City"), their targets mounted a rescue mission to save Chewbacca, who was held prisoner aboard the *Steadfast*.[[6]](#cite_note-Episode_IX-6)

A final duel ensued in the Death Star ruins between the last Jedi and the master of the Knights of Ren.

 

The Force connected Kylo and Rey, but as before neither of them could see the other's surroundings. Kylo urged Rey to tell him where she was, wanting to see her in person, but Rey was not willing to give up her location. As Kylo began revealing what he discovered about Rey's origins, the scavenger attacked him, resulting in a [lightsaber duel](/wiki/Lightsaber_duel "Lightsaber duel") being fought across space and time. During their battle, Vader's mask was teleported to Kylo's location, revealing to the Supreme Leader that Rey was on board his flagship. When he returned to the *Steadfast*, he told Rey that she was in fact the [granddaughter](/wiki/Palpatine_family "Palpatine family") of Darth Sidious. In addition, Kylo had learned that he and Rey were a[[6]](#cite_note-Episode_IX-6) [prophesied](/wiki/Doctrine_of_the_Dyad "Doctrine of the Dyad")[[2]](#cite_note-TROS_VD-2) dyad in the Force. Believing they could overthrow Sidious and claim the [Throne of the Sith](/wiki/Throne_of_the_Sith "Throne of the Sith") together, he tried to convert Rey to the dark side, but once again she refused.[[6]](#cite_note-Episode_IX-6)

 [![Kylo Ren's former self was able to re-emerge after his dark persona ceased to exist.](data:image/gif;base64,R0lGODlhAQABAIABAAAAAP///yH5BAEAAAEALAAAAAABAAEAQAICTAEAOw%3D%3D)](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/starwars/images/6/6c/Kylo_Ren_IX_TROS.jpg/revision/latest?cb=20250210210134) [![Kylo Ren's former self was able to re-emerge after his dark persona ceased to exist.](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/starwars/images/6/6c/Kylo_Ren_IX_TROS.jpg/revision/latest/scale-to-width-down/250?cb=20250210210134)](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/starwars/images/6/6c/Kylo_Ren_IX_TROS.jpg/revision/latest?cb=20250210210134) 

Kylo Ren's former self was able to re-emerge after his dark persona ceased to exist.

 

Kylo would not relent, however. Tracking Rey to [Kef Bir](/wiki/Kef_Bir "Kef Bir"), he found the scavenger in the [Emperor's Throne Room](/wiki/Emperor%27s_Throne_Room "Emperor's Throne Room") aboard the [ruins](/wiki/Death_Star_ruins "Death Star ruins") of the [DS-2 Death Star II Mobile Battle Station](/wiki/DS-2_Death_Star_II_Mobile_Battle_Station "DS-2 Death Star II Mobile Battle Station"). Due to their heritage as the descendants of the last two Sith Lords, Kylo believed that the dark side was part of their nature, making it futile to resist its temptations, though Rey refused as she feared of becoming ruthless like her grandfather. He destroyed the [Emperor's wayfinder](/wiki/Emperor%27s_wayfinder "Emperor's wayfinder") to prevent her from reaching Exegol and confronting Sidious without him, causing Rey to attack Kylo. They dueled across the wreckage of the [battle station](/wiki/Battle_station "Battle station"), but as Kylo gained the upper hand, his dying mother called out to him through the Force. Rey capitalized on Kylo's distraction by wounding him with his own lightsaber, until she too sensed Organa's death across the galaxy. Using [the Force to heal](/wiki/Force_healing "Force healing") Kylo's injury, she confessed that the person she wanted to join was Ben and not Kylo.[[6]](#cite_note-Episode_IX-6)

After Rey's departure, Kylo found himself conversing with a memory of Ben Solo's father, the late Han Solo. Moved by his parents' and Rey's compassion for him, Ben threw Kylo's lightsaber into the [ocean](/wiki/Ocean "Ocean"). The dark Knight known as Kylo Ren was no more and Ben Solo, the last Skywalker, was reborn.[[6]](#cite_note-Episode_IX-6)

 [![During the Battle of Exegol, Ben Solo was confronted by his former followers, the Knights of Ren.](data:image/gif;base64,R0lGODlhAQABAIABAAAAAP///yH5BAEAAAEALAAAAAABAAEAQAICTAEAOw%3D%3D)](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/starwars/images/f/f3/KyloFacesTheKnightsOfRen.png/revision/latest?cb=20200325083206) [![During the Battle of Exegol, Ben Solo was confronted by his former followers, the Knights of Ren.](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/starwars/images/f/f3/KyloFacesTheKnightsOfRen.png/revision/latest/scale-to-width-down/250?cb=20200325083206)](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/starwars/images/f/f3/KyloFacesTheKnightsOfRen.png/revision/latest?cb=20200325083206) 

During the Battle of Exegol, Ben Solo was confronted by his former followers, the Knights of Ren.

 

After the loss of their master, the remaining Knights gathered on Exegol while the Final Order fleet prepared for departure. During the [Battle of Exegol](/wiki/Battle_of_Exegol "Battle of Exegol"), the former Kylo Ren returned to the Sith redoubt but this time as Ben Solo. He entered the [Sith Citadel](/wiki/Sith_Citadel "Sith Citadel") with the intent of protecting Rey from the reborn Emperor, but his path was blocked by the Knights who once followed him.[[6]](#cite_note-Episode_IX-6) Solo initally believed that the Knights were still loyal to him, until he sensed their hatred toward him, leading Solo to realize that their true allegiance was to Darth Sidious. For Solo, who had trained with the Knights whom he regarded as his brothers-in-arms, it was the last breach of faith in a succession of betrayals.[[5]](#cite_note-TROS_Expanded_Edition-5) From the perspective of the Knights, their former master was no longer worthy of leading them; they saw Kylo Ren as having regressed to the persona of Ben Solo, whom they regarded as a weaker version of Ren. As such, the Knights of Ren believed they were capable of defeating Solo.[[51]](#cite_note-Skywalker:_A_Family_at_War-51)

 [![Armed with his grandfather's lightsaber, Solo killed the dark warriors who stood against him, destroying the Knights of Ren.](data:image/gif;base64,R0lGODlhAQABAIABAAAAAP///yH5BAEAAAEALAAAAAABAAEAQAICTAEAOw%3D%3D)](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/starwars/images/0/0a/Death_of_Trudgen.png/revision/latest?cb=20210915004530) [![Armed with his grandfather's lightsaber, Solo killed the dark warriors who stood against him, destroying the Knights of Ren.](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/starwars/images/0/0a/Death_of_Trudgen.png/revision/latest/scale-to-width-down/250?cb=20210915004530)](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/starwars/images/0/0a/Death_of_Trudgen.png/revision/latest?cb=20210915004530) 

Armed with his grandfather's lightsaber, Solo killed the dark warriors who stood against him, destroying the Knights of Ren.

 

Solo struck first using his Force abilities to attack the Knights. However, he was surrounded by the same warriors who had trained alongside him, providing them with knowledge of his fighting style. He was further disadvantaged by his lack of a lightsaber, and for a moment Solo regretted[[5]](#cite_note-TROS_Expanded_Edition-5) discarding his [crossguard lightsaber](/wiki/Crossguard_lightsaber "Crossguard lightsaber") on Kef Bir.[[29]](#cite_note-Kylo_Ren_DB-29) After subduing Solo with their weapons, the Knights drew back to prepare another attack, yet were unaware that Solo was in secret communication with Rey, his other half in the Force dyad. Sensing each other's intentions, Rey passed the Skywalker lightsaber to Solo through their bond. Armed with his grandfather's lightsaber, Solo overpowered his former servants before proceeding to the Dark Lord's [throne room](/wiki/Amphitheater_of_the_Sith_Eternal "Amphitheater of the Sith Eternal") to assist Rey in defeating her grandfather. The resulting deaths of Ap'lek, Cardo, Kuruk, Trudgen, Ushar, and Vicrul marked the end of the Knights of Ren.[[6]](#cite_note-Episode_IX-6)

## Behind the scenes[]

:   "*They're characters that, when we came up with them in* Force Awakens *and had very brief sighting of them, it was something that we had a lot of ideas of sort of further adventures with them, backstory, you know, and all sorts of things that were not the focus of the central story, and never made it into the movie.*"
:   ―J.J. Abrams[[52]](#cite_note-Esquire-52)

 [![Concept art of the Knights of Ren](data:image/gif;base64,R0lGODlhAQABAIABAAAAAP///yH5BAEAAAEALAAAAAABAAEAQAICTAEAOw%3D%3D)](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/starwars/images/2/23/Knights-of-Ren-concept-art.jpg/revision/latest?cb=20160106175246) [![Concept art of the Knights of Ren](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/starwars/images/2/23/Knights-of-Ren-concept-art.jpg/revision/latest/scale-to-width-down/250?cb=20160106175246)](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/starwars/images/2/23/Knights-of-Ren-concept-art.jpg/revision/latest?cb=20160106175246) 

Concept art of the Knights of Ren

 

The Knights of Ren were created for the [2015](/wiki/2015 "2015") film [*Star Wars*: Episode VII *The Force Awakens*](/wiki/Star_Wars:_Episode_VII_The_Force_Awakens "Star Wars: Episode VII The Force Awakens") and appear through Kylo Ren.[[7]](#cite_note-Episode_VII-7) They were based on rejected designs for Kylo Ren.[[53]](#cite_note-Twitter-53) The Knights later appeared in the [2019](/wiki/2019 "2019") film [*Star Wars*: Episode IX *The Rise of Skywalker*](/wiki/Star_Wars:_Episode_IX_The_Rise_of_Skywalker "Star Wars: Episode IX The Rise of Skywalker"), where they were portrayed by [Martin Wilde](/wiki/Martin_Wilde "Martin Wilde"), [Anton Simpson-Tidy](/wiki/Anton_Simpson-Tidy "Anton Simpson-Tidy"), [Lukaz Leong](/wiki/Lukaz_Leong "Lukaz Leong"), [Tom Rodgers](/wiki/Tom_Rodgers "Tom Rodgers"), [Joe Kennard](/wiki/Joe_Kennard "Joe Kennard"), and [Ashley Beck](/wiki/Ashley_Beck "Ashley Beck").[[6]](#cite_note-Episode_IX-6)

During the Director commentary of *The Force Awakens*, director [J.J. Abrams](/wiki/J.J._Abrams "J.J. Abrams") noted that the Knights had a backstory, but it was not revealed at that time.[[54]](#cite_note-Commentary-54) While fleshing out the Knights of Ren for the *[Star Wars: The Rise of Kylo Ren](/wiki/Star_Wars:_The_Rise_of_Kylo_Ren "Star Wars: The Rise of Kylo Ren")* [comic book](/wiki/Star_Wars_comic_books "Star Wars comic books") series, [Charles Soule](/wiki/Charles_Soule "Charles Soule") took inspiration from motorcycle gangs, classic Westerns, and Samurai tales to dramatize some of the lore behind the Knights.[[15]](#cite_note-Soule_Interview-15)

 [![Behind the scenes image of the Knights of Ren in The Rise of Skywalker](data:image/gif;base64,R0lGODlhAQABAIABAAAAAP///yH5BAEAAAEALAAAAAABAAEAQAICTAEAOw%3D%3D)](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/starwars/images/e/e5/KnightsOfRenLeibovitz.jpg/revision/latest?cb=20190523225549) [![Behind the scenes image of the Knights of Ren in The Rise of Skywalker](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/starwars/images/e/e5/KnightsOfRenLeibovitz.jpg/revision/latest/scale-to-width-down/250?cb=20190523225549)](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/starwars/images/e/e5/KnightsOfRenLeibovitz.jpg/revision/latest?cb=20190523225549) 

Behind the scenes image of the Knights of Ren in *The Rise of Skywalker*

 

According to [Rian Johnson](/wiki/Rian_Johnson "Rian Johnson"), writer and director of the [2017](/wiki/2017 "2017") film [*Star Wars*: Episode VIII *The Last Jedi*](/wiki/Star_Wars:_Episode_VIII_The_Last_Jedi "Star Wars: Episode VIII The Last Jedi"), the reason why the group do not appear in the film is that the movie was already full and there was no room for them. Johnson mused that they could have been used in place of Snoke's Elite Praetorian Guards, but it would have been a waste because all the guards had to die and Kylo's connection to the Knights would have added unwanted complications to the scene.[[55]](#cite_note-55)

In May 2019, [*Vanity Fair*](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Vanity_Fair_(magazine) "wikipedia:Vanity Fair (magazine)") revealed that the Knights of Ren would finally appear in *The Rise of Skywalker*. The article also featured a photograph of the Knights during filming at [Jordan](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Jordan "wikipedia:Jordan"), alongside director J.J. Abrams. *Vanity Fair* described the Knights as "masked warriors with specialized weaponry who add an element of chaos to the war between the Resistance and the First Order" and compared their brutish look to those of the characters from the [*Mad Max*](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mad_Max_(franchise) "wikipedia:Mad Max (franchise)") films.[[56]](#cite_note-Vanity_Fair-56) Had *[Star Wars: The Acolyte](/wiki/Star_Wars:_The_Acolyte "Star Wars: The Acolyte")* continued, the crew had ideas to establish the [Sith](/wiki/Sith "Sith") known only as "[the Stranger](/wiki/The_Stranger "The Stranger")" as the first Knight of Ren, with "Ren" even possibly being his real, hidden name.[[57]](#cite_note-Preview-57)
