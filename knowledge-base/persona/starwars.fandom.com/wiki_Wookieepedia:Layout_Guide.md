The **Layout Guide** (**LG**) is one of the core article policies on Wookieepedia. It regulates how Wookieepedia articles should be structured. The [in-universe](/wiki/Wookieepedia:Layout_Guide/In-universe "Wookieepedia:Layout Guide/In-universe") and [out-of-universe Layout Guides](/wiki/Wookieepedia:Layout_Guide/Out-of-universe "Wookieepedia:Layout Guide/Out-of-universe") contain further rules that only apply to one type of article.

For rules regarding the *formatting* of Wookieepedia articles, refer to the [Manual of Style](/wiki/Wookieepedia:Manual_of_Style "Wookieepedia:Manual of Style").

# Article header

## Top template

Example (from [Obi-Wan Kenobi/Legends](/wiki/Obi-Wan_Kenobi/Legends "Obi-Wan Kenobi/Legends")):

```
{{Top|ffa|sprot|mprot}}
```

* The [Top](/wiki/Template:Top "Template:Top") template should be placed at the top of every article. Depending on parameters added to the template, Top will insert navigation symbols and the continuity indicator into the title bar of an article, display tabs linking to an article's counterpart in the alternate continuity, and modify various other aspects of an article, such as its displayed title and the table of contents.
* The following order should be used for parameters on the template:
  1. Article status. These are to show if an article has previously been reviewed and approved as a status article, so should only be used if an article has previously passed a [Featured](/wiki/Wookieepedia:Featured_article_nominations "Wookieepedia:Featured article nominations"), [Good](/wiki/Wookieepedia:Good_article_nominations "Wookieepedia:Good article nominations"), or [Comprehensive article nomination](/wiki/Wookieepedia:Comprehensive_article_nominations "Wookieepedia:Comprehensive article nominations"). These parameters should only be added or modified by members of the [Inquisitorius](/wiki/Wookieepedia:Inquisitorius "Wookieepedia:Inquisitorius"), [AgriCorps](/wiki/Wookieepedia:AgriCorps "Wookieepedia:AgriCorps"), or [EduCorps](/wiki/Wookieepedia:EduCorps "Wookieepedia:EduCorps"). Status parameters are: "fa" (if the article is a [Featured article](/wiki/Wookieepedia:Featured_articles "Wookieepedia:Featured articles")), "ffa" (if the article used to be a Featured article but has since been delisted by the Inquisitorius), "ga" (if the article is a [Good article](/wiki/Wookieepedia:Good_articles "Wookieepedia:Good articles")), "fga" (if the article used to be a Good article but has since been delisted by the AgriCorps), "ca" (if the article is a  [Comprehensive article](/wiki/Wookieepedia:Comprehensive_articles "Wookieepedia:Comprehensive articles")), and "fca" (if the article used to be a Comprehensive article but has since been delisted by the EduCorps). Only the most recent applicable parameter should be used, so if a Good article had its status removed but later became a Featured article, it should only use "featured."
  2. Protection level. These are to show if an article has been [protected](https://community.fandom.com/wiki/Help:Page_protection "w:Help:Page protection") by an [Administrator](/wiki/Wookieepedia:Administrators "Wookieepedia:Administrators"). Articles should only have one parameter for the level of edit protection, although articles can have both edit and move protection, in which case the edit protection should come before the move protection. Protection parameters are: "fprot" ([full protection](/wiki/Wookieepedia:Protection_policy#Full_protection "Wookieepedia:Protection policy")), "sprot" ([semi-protection](/wiki/Wookieepedia:Protection_policy#Semi-protection "Wookieepedia:Protection policy")), "ssprot" ([super-semi-protection](/wiki/Wookieepedia:Protection_policy#Super-semi-protection "Wookieepedia:Protection policy")), and "mprot" ([move protection](/wiki/Wookieepedia:Protection_policy#Move_protection "Wookieepedia:Protection policy")).
  3. Real-world article classification. These parameters are used to denote articles on real-world topics, and articles should only use one of these parameters, placed before the continuity parameters. Parameters are "rwm" for [real-world media](/wiki/Category:Real-world_media "Category:Real-world media"), "rwp" for [real-world people](/wiki/Category:Real-world_people "Category:Real-world people"), "rwc" for [real-world companies](/wiki/Category:Real-world_companies "Category:Real-world companies"), "music" for [real-world music](/wiki/Category:Real-world_music "Category:Real-world music"), and "real" for any real-world concept that does not fit in those more specific categories.
  4. Continuity label. By default, Top displays an article as [canon](/wiki/Canon "Canon"). If an article is non-canon, "noncanon" should be used. If an article is [Legends](/wiki/Star_Wars_Legends "Star Wars Legends"), the Legends banner is automatically displayed if an article has "/Legends" in its title, a canon counterpart with "/Canon" in its title, or if the parameter "canon=" is used in Top. If a Legends article does not fit any of these criteria, "leg" should be used to display the Legends banner. If an article is non-canon in its respective continuity, the article should use "ncc" or "ncl" instead of "can" or "leg" for Canon and Legends articles respectively.
  5. Publishing era parameters. These parameters should be used only on real-world media articles in conjunction with both the "rwm" parameter and the appropriate continuity parameter, and are used to indicate what [official publishing era](/wiki/List_of_publishing_eras "List of publishing eras") the media is set in. For media that is non-narrative in nature but does use a continuity parameter, the "ref" parameter should be used. See [the template documentation](/wiki/Template:Top#Publishing_eras "Template:Top") for the full list of publishing parameters for each continuity.
  6. Continuity tabs. If a subject exists in both continuities under the same name, with those articles only being disambiguated via a "/Legends" or "/Canon" suffix, their tabs will automatically link the two articles together. However, if two counterpart articles have different names, the canon article needs to use "legends=LEGENDS ARTICLE TITLE" and the Legends article "canon=CANON ARTICLE TITLE" to link the articles together.
  7. Title formatting. The title of an article can be formatted through Top; for example, to add italicization. However, Top should not be used to modify the title text itself, only format it. To italicize the entirety of the page's title aside from the parenthetical descriptor, "italics=1" should be used, while "italics2=1" should be used to italicize the entire parenthetical descriptor. If more complex formatting is required, the parameters "title=*FORMATTED* TITLE" and "title2=*FORMATTED* TITLE" should be used accordingly to italicize parts of the title or to lowercase the first word of the full title. "notitle=1" should be used to prevent the automatic shrinking of text inside parentheses on pages where parentheses are part of the in-universe title (e.g. [How to Get Banned (Have a Mind)](/wiki/How_to_Get_Banned_(Have_a_Mind) "How to Get Banned (Have a Mind)")). The title parameters should *not* be used to add additional special characters to the title that are [restricted by technical limitations](/wiki/Wookieepedia:Naming_policy#Be_careful_with_special_characters "Wookieepedia:Naming policy"); the {{[CorrectTitle](/wiki/Template:CorrectTitle "Template:CorrectTitle")}} template must be used instead.
  8. Table of contents. Use "notoc=1" in Top to remove an unwanted table of contents. This is only done in certain circumstances; see the [Table of contents section](#Table_of_contents) below.
  9. Audio. Use "audio=URL" to add a link to an article's audible version on Wookieepedia's [YouTube channel](https://www.youtube.com/@WookOfficial). This should only be used to link videos produced by that channel. Note that the full URL needs to be included.

* All infobox material must be detailed in the article proper, unless exempted by the Layout Guides.
* The name field of the infobox is reserved for the article's title, in accordance with the [Naming policy](/wiki/Wookieepedia:Naming_policy "Wookieepedia:Naming policy") and pursuant to this section's clause on article titles with parenthetical descriptors, and no additional alternate name may be listed in the said field unless specifically exempted by the in-universe or out-of-universe Layout Guides, respectively. Additionally, a clarifying image caption utilizing {{[C](/wiki/Template:C "Template:C")}} can be added into the name field. Captions should not be used as standard, but rather when an infobox image does not make immediately clear how the subject is being depicted.

:   →See [Forum:CT:Alternate names in the infobox](/wiki/Forum:CT:Alternate_names_in_the_infobox "Forum:CT:Alternate names in the infobox") and [Forum:CT:Infobox image captions](/wiki/Forum:CT:Infobox_image_captions "Forum:CT:Infobox image captions")

* Conjecturally titled articles will always include parenthetical descriptors in the infobox name field as standard; parenthetical descriptors will be excluded from the infobox name field for articles with canonical names

:   →See [Wookieepedia:Mofferences/February 12, 2012](/wiki/Wookieepedia:Mofferences/February_12,_2012 "Wookieepedia:Mofferences/February 12, 2012")

* When listing multiple items in a single infobox field, including cascading items, use bullets to offset each item, including the first item listed. Do not use blank indentations.

:   →See [Wookieepedia:Mofferences/June 29, 2014](/wiki/Wookieepedia:Mofferences/June_29,_2014 "Wookieepedia:Mofferences/June 29, 2014")
:   →See [Wookieepedia:Mofferences/March 4, 2018](/wiki/Wookieepedia:Mofferences/March_4,_2018 "Wookieepedia:Mofferences/March 4, 2018")

* Articles should not contain empty infoboxes. An empty infobox is defined as one in which no fields can be filled out other than the name of the subject due to limited information. Such infoboxes should be removed from the article until such time as more information can be added.

:   →See [Forum:CT:Empty infoboxes](/wiki/Forum:CT:Empty_infoboxes "Forum:CT:Empty infoboxes")

* All infobox parameters should be listed on their own line, as should the closing brackets for the infobox as a whole.

:   →See [Forum:CT:Closing braces for templates](/wiki/Forum:CT:Closing_braces_for_templates "Forum:CT:Closing braces for templates")

* {{[ScrollBox](/wiki/Template:ScrollBox "Template:ScrollBox")}} or any other form of scrollbox should not be used for any field in the infobox.

:   →See [Forum:CT:Scroll boxes in infoboxes](/wiki/Forum:CT:Scroll_boxes_in_infoboxes "Forum:CT:Scroll boxes in infoboxes")

### Main image

* Images used in infoboxes will be scaled to 250px (the minimum size to fit the infobox width in desktop view) for all infoboxes except conflict infoboxes (battles, wars, etc.), which use either 300px, 350px, or 400px, depending on specific template size. Larger default sizes are identified on the respective template pages. Images smaller than the default infobox size should not be scaled up.

:   →See [Wookieepedia:Mofferences/June 23, 2013](/wiki/Wookieepedia:Mofferences/June_23,_2013 "Wookieepedia:Mofferences/June 23, 2013")

* Main infobox images shall be at least 400px in width whenever possible. This is done to accommodate the infobox's minimum width on most mobile devices in vertical view.

:   →See [Forum:CT:Infobox image size for mobile skin](/wiki/Forum:CT:Infobox_image_size_for_mobile_skin "Forum:CT:Infobox image size for mobile skin")

## Opening quote

```
 {{Quote|I'm in control. ''I'' make the rules ''now''.|Cad Bane|audio=CadBaneInControl.flac|<ref name="Hostage Crisis">{{TCW|Hostage Crisis}}</ref>}}
```

The opening quote (also known as "lead quote" or "intro quote"), being at the top of an article, should be descriptive of the article subject.

* Opening quotes are subject to the same rules as all other quotes. See the [Quotes section](#Quotes) below.

## Introduction

The introduction ("intro") provides a short summary of the article proper.

* Articles shall only have an introduction section if the article body reaches 165 words in length, not counting the "Behind the scenes" section or any other content (infobox, captions, quotes, etc.). Such articles will then employ sectioning in the article body. However, articles on out-of-universe products, such as books or comics, may still have an introduction even if the article body is less than 165 words long.
* There is no set limit for introduction length, but it should be proportionate to the length of the article.

:   → See [Forum:CT:Amending Layout Guide Introductions policy](/wiki/Forum:CT:Amending_Layout_Guide_Introductions_policy "Forum:CT:Amending Layout Guide Introductions policy")

## Table of contents

A table of contents (TOC) is automatically generated directly above the first heading when an article contains four or more headings.

* The table of contents should be removed if an article on an in-universe topic has no in-universe prose sections (e.g. "History" or "Powers and abilities"), or when an article on a real-world topic has no prose sections at all.
* For in-universe articles, this means articles that have content sections other than "Behind the scenes," such as "History" or "Powers and abilities," should not remove their table of contents, whereas articles whose only prose section is "Behind the scenes" should remove their table of contents.
* Use "notoc=1" in the Top template to remove the table of contents.

:   →See [Forum:CT:Notoc on OOU articles](/wiki/Forum:CT:Notoc_on_OOU_articles "Forum:CT:Notoc on OOU articles")

# Article body

## Sectioning

Example (from [Zek Peiro](/wiki/Zek_Peiro "Zek Peiro")):

```
==Biography==
===Working for the "Kaitis Cartel"===
[...]

===Legacy===
[...]

==Personality and traits==
[...]

==Powers and abilities==
[...]

==Equipment==
[...]

==Behind the scenes==
[...]
```

Sectioning is essential to presentation and navigation. This is accomplished by adding headings and subheadings as appropriate.

* Articles on certain types of subjects, like characters, have established layouts. These can be found in the in-universe and out-of-universe Layout Guides, respectively. While there is no established layout for articles on other types of subjects, it is recommended editors look at existing articles, particularly Status articles, for precedent.
* Headings and subheadings are usually not required for very small articles.
* Section length should be based on the content of the article. Two to three paragraphs of prose per section usually works well, but this is by no means a hard rule.

# Article indexing

## Sources

The Sources section is a bulleted list of officially licensed *Star Wars* material to mention the subject.

* For in-universe articles, list all officially licensed *Star Wars* reference material, typically presented from a non-narrative, "out-of-universe" perspective.
* For out-of-universe articles, list all officially licensed *Star Wars* media to mention the real-world article subject.
* The Sources list is ordered chronologically by real-world publication date
  + List items according to the publisher's officially announced American release date, even though sources frequently hit the open market earlier than their announced release dates. Items may be listed by foreign release dates if there is a considerable gap between the subsequent American release date, such as several weeks or months, or if no American release date exists.
  + If only an item's publication month and/or year are known, place that item at the end of the month or year. Example: If Source A was published on March 15, 2005 and Source B's publication date is only known as March 2005, list Source A first.
    - However, if an item with only a known publication month and/or year is confirmed to be released by another source, place the first item at the date that the second source was released. Example: If Source A was published in December 2018, but Source B was published on December 15, 2018 and includes confirmation that Source A was released, then list Source A as if its publication date was December 15, 2018 and before Source B.
* Listed items include even the smallest mentions of given subjects
* Listed items are italicized by default, except where differentiated in citation templates
  + When listing different editions, the edition follows a comma and is not italicized. Example: [*Galaxy Guide 3: The Empire Strikes Back*, Second Edition](/wiki/Galaxy_Guide_3:_The_Empire_Strikes_Back_(Second_Edition) "Galaxy Guide 3: The Empire Strikes Back (Second Edition)"); [*Starships of the Galaxy*, Saga Edition](/wiki/Starships_of_the_Galaxy_(Saga_Edition) "Starships of the Galaxy (Saga Edition)")
    - An exception to this rule is the first edition. Do not include "First Edition" as part of the listed item's name, as these books were never published under any such denotation, unlike subsequent editions. Example: *[A Guide to the Star Wars Universe](/wiki/A_Guide_to_the_Star_Wars_Universe "A Guide to the Star Wars Universe")*; *[A Guide to the Star Wars Universe, Second Edition, Revised and Expanded](/wiki/A_Guide_to_the_Star_Wars_Universe,_Second_Edition,_Revised_and_Expanded "A Guide to the Star Wars Universe, Second Edition, Revised and Expanded")*; *[A Guide to the Star Wars Universe, Third Edition, Revised and Expanded](/wiki/A_Guide_to_the_Star_Wars_Universe,_Third_Edition,_Revised_and_Expanded "A Guide to the Star Wars Universe, Third Edition, Revised and Expanded")*
* Individual cards from card games should be listed according to the release date of the corresponding set. If an article needs to list multiple cards from a single set, they should be listed in Sources using the {{[CardGameSet](/wiki/Template:CardGameSet "Template:CardGameSet")}} template, which will display the set on its own line followed by the individual cards from that set, sorted alphabetically in a hideable list. If a card game does not have individual sets, then the game itself should be given in the set parameter for the template. The appropriate card game citation template should still be used to list the set and each card within the wrapper template. The citation template for the parent set should use the `parent=1` parameter to indicate its usage in the SourceContents template and to disable the validation logic that requires the department field.

:   :   →See [Forum:CT:Card games in Sources](/wiki/Forum:CT:Card_games_in_Sources "Forum:CT:Card games in Sources"), [Forum:CT:Reference magazines in Sources](/wiki/Forum:CT:Reference_magazines_in_Sources "Forum:CT:Reference magazines in Sources")

* For magazine issues with multiple departments or articles within each issue, each department that mentions the article's topic should be listed as a separate bullet point, and should use the appropriate citation template.
  + If an article's topic is only mentioned or depicted in material included outside of individual departments and articles, such as on the cover or in the Table of Contents, the individual issue should be listed in the Sources list, and should not use the magazine's specialized citation template. (ex: [R1-J5](/wiki/R1-J5 "R1-J5") is pictured above the table of contents in [*Star Wars Insider* 186](/wiki/Star_Wars_Insider_186 "Star Wars Insider 186") but not mentioned or pictured in any of the articles; thus, the magazine should be listed in Sources as [[Star Wars Insider 186|''Star Wars Insider'' 186]].)
  + For [reference magazines](/wiki/Category:Reference_magazines "Category:Reference magazines") such as *[The Official Star Wars Fact File](/wiki/The_Official_Star_Wars_Fact_File "The Official Star Wars Fact File")* and *[Star Wars: Build Your Own R2-D2](/wiki/Star_Wars:_Build_Your_Own_R2-D2 "Star Wars: Build Your Own R2-D2")*, if an article needs to list multiple departments from the same issue, then they should be listed within the {{[SourceContents](/wiki/Template:SourceContents "Template:SourceContents")}} template, which will display the issue on its own line followed by the individual departments from that issue, sorted in release order in a hideable list. The citation template for the individual issue should use the `parent=1` parameter to indicate its usage in the SourceContents template and to disable the validation logic that requires the department field. This template should not be used with fan magazines and other magazines, such as *[Star Wars Insider](/wiki/Star_Wars_Insider "Star Wars Insider")* and the *[Star Wars Adventure Journal](/wiki/Star_Wars_Adventure_Journal "Star Wars Adventure Journal")*.

:   :   →See [Forum:CT:Reference magazines in Sources](/wiki/Forum:CT:Reference_magazines_in_Sources "Forum:CT:Reference magazines in Sources")

## External links

The External links section is a bulleted list of links from websites outside of Wookieepedia. These links are commonly unlicensed and/or unofficial sources of information, such as social media profiles, commercial pages, news articles, fan websites, [Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Main_Page "wikipedia:Main Page") articles, databases, and more.

* The section is called "External links," even if the article only has one external link
* The list must be ordered following a specific hierarchy, with items listed in alphabetic order (primarily following the name of the websites, and secondly the name of individual pages) within each level, and formatted using available [citation templates](/wiki/Category:Internet_citation_templates "Category:Internet citation templates"):

```
1 - Official pages
1.1 - Official website, formatted with {{OfficialSite}}
1.2 - Other, such as StarWars.com profile pages
2 - Social media profiles, including the use of Social media citation templates, as well as {{YouTube}}
3 - Commercial pages, including, but not limited to, the use of Commercial and product listing internet citation templates, as well as other, such as {{Disney}} and {{Marvel}}
4 - Databases
4.1 - Wikipedia
4.2 - Other, including, but not limited to: {{IMDb}}, {{MobyGames}}, other wikis
5 - Other relevant pages
5.1 - Star Wars Blogs and StarWars.com Message Boards, using respectively {{SW}}, {{Blog}} and {{SWBoards}}
5.2 - Articles, interviews, discussion topics, including, but not limited to: {{WebCite}}, {{JCFCite}}, {{Reddit}}), etc.
```

* Wikipedia articles and IMDb pages should be linked with `{{WP|Wikipedia article name}}` for Wikipedia and `{{IMDb|name/xxxxx}}` or `{{IMDb|title/xxxxx}}` for IMDb.
* Unlike other sections of the hierarchy, listings of "other relevant pages" (from non-official websites) in External links should be restricted to only the most critically relevant pages with respect to an article's coverage. Pages used as citations in an article's text that cover only a fraction of the article's subject should not be included. For example, an article only covering the announcement of the participation of a creator to a new work should not be included, unlike an article that primarily covers the creator and their *Star Wars* career. Interviews are considered particularly relevant, and should be systematically listed, as long as they focus on *Star Wars*. These pages should be listed in chronological order of publication, rather than alphabetical order.
  + *Star Wars* Blog posts from the [Pop Culture](http://www.starwars.com/news/category/in-pop-culture); [Quizzes and Polls](http://www.starwars.com/news/category/quizzes); and [Films](http://www.starwars.com/news/category/films) categories are typically prime examples of pages that are not appropriate for the External links. However, any Blog post (featuring the article's subject) that is cited in the course of the article must be listed in the External links.

An index page is an optional subpage that can be created in the Index namespace as Index:PAGENAME for any mainspace article. The index page contains a single list of all media from the article's appearances and sources sections listed in chronological release order with release date and without any accompanying quotes or images. Material from the Notes and references section or External links section may also be included if the material is directly related to the subject of the article, such as autobiographies or podcast interviews. If no release date is available at all this should be stated and the entry included at the end of the list. The date should be referenced, with a Notes and references section at the bottom of the article. The media links should be formatted as in the three aforementioned lists including any media templates and include any additional information templates like {{[1st](/wiki/Template:1st "Template:1st")}} or {{[Po](/wiki/Template:Po "Template:Po")}}.

Separated from the media with an mdash; each entry in the list will then provide information to aid a reader in locating the information on the subject of the article in said piece of media, including page numbers, timestamps, or other locational aids depending on the type of media in question. A list of the formats for this information is suggested below, but the level of detail that a users adds is up to them: someone providing just chapters rather than page numbers is still more likely to help a future reader then adding nothing at all. Likewise, creating an index page does not require you to fill out every source, even if you can only add locational aids for a single piece of media this is still useful. Notes may also be included if necessary for unusual cases, for example, information no longer present on a still live webpage.

It is also important to note that as index pages are totally optional, they will not be considered during the nomination of status articles or for existing status articles. The only requirement for a status article regarding index pages is that if an index page does exist, it is linked to on the mainspace article, similar to the treatment of the {{[Mediacat](/wiki/Template:Mediacat "Template:Mediacat")}} template. The link on the article should use the {{[Indexpage](/wiki/Template:Indexpage "Template:Indexpage")}} template, which should be placed directly below the Appearances section heading. If no Appearances section exists, then it should be placed directly below the Sources heading, and if the article also lacks that then place it directly below the External links section heading.

### Index page media type formats

For any piece of media with multiple page ranges or time stamps, separate them with a comma. For consecutive ranges of pages or time stamps, utilize en dashes. For any piece of media with multiple editions, separate those editions with a semi-colon. For any character appearing throughout the vast majority of the entire media simply "Throughout"

* Film — For each differing edition or service give time range(s) using format provided in the media. Alternatively, scene selection chapters can be provided in place of time stamps for physical media (e.g., [Disney+](/wiki/Disney%2B "Disney+"): 0:43:05–0:46:53, 1:45:20; *[Star Wars: The Complete Saga](/wiki/Star_Wars:_The_Complete_Saga "Star Wars: The Complete Saga")*: Chapter 1; Chapter 12)
* Audiobook — Timestamp or range. Alternatively, chapters may also be used, especially for frequently appearing characters.
* Book — For each differing edition give page or page range (e.g., p. 23, pp. 50–53). If page numbers not given, count starting from first page after inside cover of the book. Alternatively, chapters can be used for frequently appearing (e.g., Chapters 2, 3–4, 6). For Encyclopedia style books with multiple entries per page, the specific entry may also be listed with the page number if necessary (e.g., "Farns Monsbee" p. 124). Note if information is hidden behind a moveable component of the book such as a pull tab or lift-able flap.
* Comic — For each differing edition give page or page range (e.g., p. 23, pp. 50–53).
* Board games and card games — List all relevant cards or other pieces as well as any page ranges in rulebooks (e.g., "Luke Skywalker" card; Game Piece, Rulebook pp. 30–31)
* Video games — Commonly suggested aids include Codex or Database Entry, Level, Coordinates, or Location but given the variable nature of game design other relevant locational aids may be used as needed.
* Webpages — Note timestamps for any relevant videos and slide or page numbers or names for any relevant subpages or embedded galleries and tables. (e.g. Story Gallery slides 8, 11, 13; Trivia Gallery slide 6)
* Toys — Note if the subject is mentioned only on packaging or in an insert (e.g., Jedi Force File p. 3)

# Interactive Maps

An [Interactive Map](https://community.fandom.com/wiki/Help:Interactive_Maps "w:Help:Interactive Maps") is an optional feature that can be created in the Map namespace as Map:NAME. Interactive Maps can be used in a variety of different ways to illustrate information in a visual format. Some common usages include presenting [map markers](/wiki/Map:Map_of_the_galaxy "Map:Map of the galaxy") on a map like the [*Star Wars* Galaxy Map](/wiki/Star_Wars_Galaxy_Map_(poster) "Star Wars Galaxy Map (poster)") or detailing a specific subject's features, such as the [individual components](/wiki/Map:Amidala-SenateGown "Map:Amidala-SenateGown") of [Padmé Amidala](/wiki/Padm%C3%A9_Amidala "Padmé Amidala")'s [Senate gown](/wiki/Senate_gown "Senate gown").

### Guidance

* All Interactive Maps require a [Consensus track](/wiki/Forum:Consensus_track "Forum:Consensus track") to be used on articles. The Consensus track should specify the article(s) the proposed map would be added to, as well as where on the page(s) it would be placed.
  + A Consensus track may also be started to propose adding an already used Interactive Map to one or more articles.
  + An Interactive Map should not immediately be re-presented for a vote without revision following a failed Consensus track, pursuant to the [consensus policy's](/wiki/Wookieepedia:Consensus "Wookieepedia:Consensus") clause on overturning identical consensus resolutions within a certain timeframe without increased voter participation.
  + A Senate Hall may prove helpful for refining a map prior to starting Consensus track to receive suggestions on how to improve it.
  + Interactive Maps may be suggested for deletion via the [Trash compactor](/wiki/Forum:Trash_compactor "Forum:Trash compactor") if the community feels they are not needed to illustrate a topic.
* If placed within an [in-universe](/wiki/In-universe "In-universe") section of an article, an Interactive Map's content must remain separated by continuity pursuant to the [canon policy](/wiki/Wookieepedia:Canon_policy "Wookieepedia:Canon policy"). The continuity restriction does not apply to maps placed in the "Behind the scenes" sections of in-universe articles, or on articles covering [real-world subjects](/wiki/Category:Real-world_articles "Category:Real-world articles").
* Interactive Maps placed on articles should utilize the {{[MapBox](/wiki/Template:MapBox "Template:MapBox")}} template.
* Interactive Maps may be subject to [speedy deletion](/wiki/Wookieepedia:Deletion_policy "Wookieepedia:Deletion policy") under certain circumstances:
  + …if a map remains entirely blank with no map markers added for over a month.
  + …if a map is a duplicate of a pre-existing map.
  + …if a map is created without any canonical basis and only consists of [fanon](/wiki/Wookieepedia:Attribution#Wookieepedia_is_not_the_place_for_fan_fiction "Wookieepedia:Attribution") or [unverifiable claims](/wiki/Wookieepedia:Attribution#No_original_research "Wookieepedia:Attribution").
* It is also important to note that as Interactive Maps are totally optional, they will not be considered during the nomination of status articles or for existing status articles.
* [Images](/wiki/Wookieepedia:Files "Wookieepedia:Files") used as the background of an Interactive Map approved via a Consensus track will be [upload protected](/wiki/Wookieepedia:Protection_policy "Wookieepedia:Protection policy"), as uploading a different resolution of an existing article can significantly alter the placement of a map's coordinate-based markers.
