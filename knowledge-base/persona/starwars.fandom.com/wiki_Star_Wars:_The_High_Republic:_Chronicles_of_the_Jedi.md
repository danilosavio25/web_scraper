***Star Wars: The High Republic: Chronicles of the Jedi***, subtitled "An Illustrated Guide to the Galaxy's Golden Age" and originally announced as ***Star Wars: The High Republic: An Illustrated Archive***, is a [canon](/wiki/Canon "Canon") reference book by [Cole Horton](/wiki/Cole_Horton "Cole Horton"). The book was published by [Insight Editions](/wiki/Insight_Editions "Insight Editions") as part of Phase II of the *[Star Wars: The High Republic](/wiki/Star_Wars:_The_High_Republic "Star Wars: The High Republic")* multimedia project on [February 28](/wiki/February_28 "February 28"), [2023](/wiki/2023 "2023") in the UK and was published on [March 21](/wiki/March_21 "March 21"), 2023 in the US.

## Contents[]

In the introduction, [Jedi Master](/wiki/Jedi_Master "Jedi Master") [Harli Cogra](/wiki/Harli_Cogra "Harli Cogra") explains that his book covers the 150 year period leading up to the [Great Works](/wiki/Great_Works "Great Works") of [Supreme Chancellor](/wiki/Supreme_Chancellor "Supreme Chancellor") [Lina Soh](/wiki/Lina_Soh "Lina Soh"), the [Great Hyperspace Disaster](/wiki/Great_Hyperspace_Disaster "Great Hyperspace Disaster") and the [Galactic Republic](/wiki/Galactic_Republic "Galactic Republic") and [Jedi Order](/wiki/Jedi_Order "Jedi Order")'s conflict with the [Nihil](/wiki/Nihil "Nihil").

### Chapter 1: For Light and Life[]

This chapter gives a background into the history, beliefs and organizational structure of the Jedi during the [High Republic Era](/wiki/High_Republic_Era "High Republic Era"). The first chapter also introduces several members of the [Jedi High Council](/wiki/Jedi_High_Council "Jedi High Council") including [Grand Masters](/wiki/Grand_Master "Grand Master") [Xo Lahru](/wiki/Xo_Lahru "Xo Lahru"), [Pra-Tre Veter](/wiki/Pra-Tre_Veter "Pra-Tre Veter"), [Yoda](/wiki/Yoda "Yoda"), Masters [Ada-Li Carro](/wiki/Ada-Li_Carro "Ada-Li Carro"), [Keaton Murag](/wiki/Keaton_Murag "Keaton Murag"), [Oppo Rancisis](/wiki/Oppo_Rancisis "Oppo Rancisis"), [Yarael Poof](/wiki/Yarael_Poof "Yarael Poof"), [Rana Kant](/wiki/Rana_Kant "Rana Kant"), [Teri Rosason](/wiki/Teri_Rosason "Teri Rosason"), [Soleil Agra](/wiki/Soleil_Agra "Soleil Agra"), Grand Master [Ry Ki-Sakka](/wiki/Ry_Ki-Sakka "Ry Ki-Sakka") and Master [Adampo](/wiki/Adampo "Adampo"). "Light and Life" also talks about the [Grand Temple](/wiki/Imperial_Palace "Imperial Palace"), the [Barash Vow](/wiki/Barash_Vow "Barash Vow") and the [Wayseekers](/wiki/Wayseeker "Wayseeker").

The book also contains a map of [the galaxy](/wiki/The_galaxy "The galaxy") focusing on the [Outer Rim Territories](/wiki/Outer_Rim_Territories "Outer Rim Territories"). Cogra explains that in the past 150 years, the Republic had expanded from its core territories in the [Core Worlds](/wiki/Core_Worlds "Core Worlds") and [Mid Rim Territories](/wiki/Mid_Rim_Territories "Mid Rim Territories") into the Outer Rim during the [Great Hyperspace Rush](/wiki/Great_Hyperspace_Rush "Great Hyperspace Rush") through the development of new [hyperspace](/wiki/Hyperspace "Hyperspace") routes. The map also shows Nihil territory, which covers a thin red band stretching from [Mulita](/wiki/Mulita "Mulita") in the west to [Vint](/wiki/Vint "Vint") in the east.

### Chapter 2: The Jedi of the Frontier[]

This chapter covers the 150 year period leading to the events of the Great Disaster. During that period, the Jedi embarked on a great quest of exploration into the Outer Rim. The chapter also covers hyperspace theory, philosophy and history including the role of the [Graf](/wiki/Graf_family "Graf family") and [San Tekka](/wiki/San_Tekka_clan "San Tekka clan") in hyperspace prospecting. The chapter also covers the joint Republic and Jedi [Pathfinder](/wiki/Republic_Pathfinder "Republic Pathfinder") teams, which played a crucial role in discovering new hyperspace routes. Cogra also talks about the establishment of Jedi frontier outposts, the non-[Force-using](/wiki/The_Force "The Force") [Path of the Open Hand](/wiki/Path_of_the_Open_Hand "Path of the Open Hand") cult, Jedi attempts to mediate peace between the warring planets of [Eiram](/wiki/Eiram "Eiram") and [E'ronoh](/wiki/E%27ronoh "E'ronoh"), the [Battle of Jedha](/wiki/Battle_of_Jedha "Battle of Jedha") and the relationship between the Jedi [Porter Engle](/wiki/Porter_Engle "Porter Engle") and [Barash Silvain](/wiki/Barash_Silvain "Barash Silvain").

### Chapter 3: Beacons of the Republic[]

This chapter talks about the creation of the space station [Starlight Beacon](/wiki/Starlight_Beacon "Starlight Beacon"), which was part of Chancellor Soh's efforts of space stations in the Outer Rim known as beacon. The third chapter also talks about the Great Hyperspace Disaster, which was caused by the Republic freighter *[Legacy Run](/wiki/Legacy_Run "Legacy Run")* colliding with a Nihil ship in hyperspace. The fragments were scattered around the Outer Rim, disrupting interstellar travel and threatening numerous worlds. The third chapter talks about Master [Avar Kriss](/wiki/Avar_Kriss "Avar Kriss")' role in protecting the planet [Hetzal](/wiki/Hetzal_Prime "Hetzal Prime") through the "[Song of the Force](/wiki/Song_of_the_Force "Song of the Force")." The chapter also covers the [Battle of Kur](/wiki/Battle_of_Kur "Battle of Kur"), the dedication of Starlight Beacon and Jedi efforts to evacuate [Dalna](/wiki/Dalna "Dalna") following [groundquakes](/wiki/Groundquake "Groundquake") triggered by the Nihil. Cogra also alludes to the [Night of Sorrow](/wiki/Night_of_Sorrow "Night of Sorrow") which occurred on Dalna 150 years ago but does not explore the subject.

### Chapter 4: Republican Expansion[]

This chapter covers the leadership of Chancellor Lina Soh and the Nihil. The chapter discusses the organizational structure of the Nihil, their tactics and technology. Cogra identifies [Marchion Ro](/wiki/Marchion_Ro "Marchion Ro") as the [Eye of the Nihil](/wiki/Eye_of_the_Nihil "Eye of the Nihil"), the organization's leader. He also covers the Nihil's attack on the [Republic Fair](/wiki/Republic_Fair "Republic Fair") on [Valo](/wiki/Valo "Valo") and the Nihil attack on the [*Elite*-class exploratory vessel](/wiki/Elite-class_vessel "Elite-class vessel") *[Innovator](/wiki/Innovator "Innovator")*. The fourth chapter also profiles several Jedi including [Loden Greatstorm](/wiki/Loden_Greatstorm "Loden Greatstorm"), [Ram Jomaram](/wiki/Ram_Jomaram "Ram Jomaram"), [Bell Zettifar](/wiki/Bell_Zettifar "Bell Zettifar"), [Indeera Stokes](/wiki/Indeera_Stokes "Indeera Stokes"), [Nib Assek](/wiki/Nib_Assek "Nib Assek"), [Mikkel Sutmani](/wiki/Mikkel_Sutmani "Mikkel Sutmani"), [Te'Ami](/wiki/Te%27Ami "Te'Ami"), [Ty Yorrick](/wiki/Ty_Yorrick "Ty Yorrick") and [Elzar Mann](/wiki/Elzar_Mann "Elzar Mann").

### Chapter 5: Growing the Order[]

This chapter talks about the Jedi Order's fleet of [Padawan Academic Cruisers](/wiki/Padawan_Academic_Cruiser "Padawan Academic Cruiser") including the *[Star Hopper](/wiki/Star_Hopper "Star Hopper")*. The chapter profiles the *Star Hopper*'s instructors Grand Master Yoda, Master [Torban Buck](/wiki/Torban_Buck "Torban Buck") and [Padawans](/wiki/Padawan "Padawan") [Lula Talisola](/wiki/Lula_Mrala-Talisola "Lula Mrala-Talisola"), [Zeen Mrala](/wiki/Zeen_Mrala-Talisola "Zeen Mrala-Talisola"), [Qort](/wiki/Qort "Qort"), [Farzala Tarabal](/wiki/Farzala_Tarabal "Farzala Tarabal") and [Bibs](/wiki/Bibs "Bibs"). The fifth chapter also covers several events including the *Star Hopper* Padawans' response to the [Trymant IV disaster](/wiki/Trymant_IV_disaster "Trymant IV disaster") and diplomatic mission to the [Hutts](/wiki/Hutt "Hutt"). The chapter also profiles the prodigy [Jedi Knight](/wiki/Jedi_Knight "Jedi Knight") [Vernestra Rwoh](/wiki/Vernestra_Rwoh "Vernestra Rwoh"), her Padawan [Imri Cantaros](/wiki/Imri_Cantaros "Imri Cantaros") and Padawan [Ady Sun'Zee](/wiki/Ady_Sun%27Zee "Ady Sun'Zee").

### Chapter 6: In Defense of the Light[]

This chapter talks as the Jedi Order's role as peacekeepers in the galaxy. It also contains a lengthy discussion of [lightsabers](/wiki/Lightsaber "Lightsaber"), showcasing the lightsabers of Masters Kriss, Gios, [Orla Jareni](/wiki/Orla_Jareni "Orla Jareni"), [Sskeer](/wiki/Sskeer "Sskeer"), Padawans [Burryaga Agaburry](/wiki/Burryaga_Agaburry "Burryaga Agaburry"), Rwoh and Jedi Knight [Keeve Trennis](/wiki/Keeve_Trennis "Keeve Trennis"). The chapter covers the [Drengir](/wiki/Drengir "Drengir"), the Jedi-Hutt alliance, the [Battle of Grizal](/wiki/Battle_of_Grizal "Battle of Grizal") and touches upon the threat posed by the enigmatic [Nameless](/wiki/Nameless "Nameless").In addition, the chapter profiles several Jedi including [Reath Silas](/wiki/Reath_Silas "Reath Silas"), [Dez Rydan](/wiki/Dez_Rydan "Dez Rydan"), [Cohmac Vitus](/wiki/Cohmac_Vitus "Cohmac Vitus"), [Orla Jareni](/wiki/Orla_Jareni "Orla Jareni"), [Sskeer](/wiki/Sskeer "Sskeer"), Trennis, [Lily Tora-Asi](/wiki/Lily_Tora-Asi "Lily Tora-Asi"), [Ceret](/wiki/Ceret "Ceret"), [Terec](/wiki/Terec "Terec"), [Emerick Caphtor](/wiki/Emerick_Caphtor "Emerick Caphtor"), [Yacek Sparkburn](/wiki/Yacek_Sparkburn "Yacek Sparkburn"), [Nyla Quinn](/wiki/Nyla_Quinn "Nyla Quinn"), and [Lyssa Votz](/wiki/Lyssa_Votz "Lyssa Votz").

### Chapter 7: The Fall of Starlight[]

The chapter begins by discussing Starlight Beacon's role in evacuating the population of [Eiram](/wiki/Eiram "Eiram") following a cyclone. It then discusses several Jedi archivists including Masters [Estala Maru](/wiki/Estala_Maru "Estala Maru") and [OrbaLin](/wiki/OrbaLin "OrbaLin"). The chapter focuses on the events leading to the destruction of Starlight Beacon by the Nihil and its repercussions on the Jedi Order and galaxy. The chapter also lists several Jedi who perished or disappeared during the fall of Starlight including [Obratuk Glii](/wiki/Obratuk_Glii "Obratuk Glii"), [Nooranbakarakana](/wiki/Nooranbakarakana "Nooranbakarakana"), OrbaLin, [Estala Maru](/wiki/Estala_Maru "Estala Maru"), [Stellan Gios](/wiki/Stellan_Gios "Stellan Gios"), [Torban Buck](/wiki/Torban_Buck "Torban Buck"), Burryaga, [Regald Coll](/wiki/Regald_Coll "Regald Coll"), Sskeer, Orla Jareni, [Goonral Monshi](/wiki/Goonral_Monshi "Goonral Monshi"), Lula Talisola, [Farzala Tarabal](/wiki/Farzala_Tarabal "Farzala Tarabal") and [Nib Assek](/wiki/Nib_Assek "Nib Assek").

The chapter also covers Jedi efforts to foil a Nihil plot to steal a fleet of Corellian ships. It ends with a discussion of the enigmatic Marchion Ro and his Nameless creatures. In the afterword, Master Cogra talks about the Jedi High Council recalling all Jedi to the Grand Temple on Coruscant and the formation of a Nihil state known as the [Exclusion Zone](/wiki/Occlusion_Zone "Occlusion Zone"). Cogra ends with a passage extolling the Jedi to fight for [light](/wiki/Light_side_of_the_Force "Light side of the Force") and life.
