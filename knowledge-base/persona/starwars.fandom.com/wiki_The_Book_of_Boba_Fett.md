***Star Wars: The Book of Boba Fett*** is a live-action television series that focuses on the character [Boba Fett](/wiki/Boba_Fett "Boba Fett") taking place after the events of the [second season](/wiki/The_Mandalorian_Season_Two "The Mandalorian Season Two") of *[The Mandalorian](/wiki/Star_Wars:_The_Mandalorian "Star Wars: The Mandalorian")* but before the events of the [third season](/wiki/The_Mandalorian_Season_Three "The Mandalorian Season Three") of *The Mandalorian*. Its first episode was released on [December 29](/wiki/December_29 "December 29"), [2021](/wiki/2021 "2021"). It was first teased, with both its title and release window, in a post-credits sequence of the season two [finale](/wiki/Chapter_16:_The_Rescue "Chapter 16: The Rescue") of *The Mandalorian*.

## Official description[]

*The Book of Boba Fett, a thrilling Star Wars adventure, finds legendary bounty hunter Boba Fett and mercenary Fennec Shand navigating the galaxy's underworld when they return to the sands of Tatooine to stake their claim on the territory once ruled by Jabba the Hutt and his crime syndicate.*[[9]](#cite_note-Premiere_date-9)

## Development[]

[Boba Fett](/wiki/Boba_Fett "Boba Fett") made his live-action return to *Star Wars* in *[Star Wars: The Mandalorian](/wiki/Star_Wars:_The_Mandalorian "Star Wars: The Mandalorian")*'s "[Chapter 14: The Tragedy](/wiki/Chapter_14:_The_Tragedy "Chapter 14: The Tragedy")," an episode directed by [Robert Rodriguez](/wiki/Robert_Rodriguez "Robert Rodriguez"). Series executive producer [Jon Favreau](/wiki/Jon_Favreau "Jon Favreau") originally approached Rodriguez to direct the important episode because he loved Rodriguez's interpretation of the character. He also decided to pitch to Rodriguez and the studio the idea of creating more episodes centering around the character. Although Rodriguez usually avoided franchises, that changed with Boba, as Rodriguez felt that he was an underdeveloped character and that it was as if he was an original character Rodriguez could expand.[[13]](#cite_note-THR-13)

 [![Following the end credits scene of The Mandalorian season two finale, the title was officially made known.](data:image/gif;base64,R0lGODlhAQABAIABAAAAAP///yH5BAEAAAEALAAAAAABAAEAQAICTAEAOw%3D%3D)](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/starwars/images/b/b9/TheBookOfBobaFett-TMCh6.png/revision/latest?cb=20201219031400) [![Following the end credits scene of The Mandalorian season two finale, the title was officially made known.](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/starwars/images/b/b9/TheBookOfBobaFett-TMCh6.png/revision/latest/scale-to-width-down/300?cb=20201219031400)](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/starwars/images/b/b9/TheBookOfBobaFett-TMCh6.png/revision/latest?cb=20201219031400) 

Following the end credits scene of *The Mandalorian* season two finale, the title was officially made known.

 

Reports of a Boba Fett–centered [Disney+](/wiki/Disney%2B "Disney+") miniseries first surfaced online in early [November](/wiki/November "November") [2020](/wiki/2020 "2020"). The reports also mentioned that the series' filming had begun.[[14]](#cite_note-Deadline-14) On [December 10](/wiki/December_10 "December 10"), 2020, [Kathleen Kennedy](/wiki/Kathleen_Kennedy "Kathleen Kennedy") teased that the "next chapter" of *[Star Wars: The Mandalorian](/wiki/Star_Wars:_The_Mandalorian "Star Wars: The Mandalorian")* would arrive on [Christmas](/wiki/December_24 "December 24") of [2021](/wiki/2021 "2021").[[15]](#cite_note-Investor2020-15) Although many fans perceived this to be the [third season](/wiki/The_Mandalorian_Season_Three "The Mandalorian Season Three") of *The Mandalorian*,[[16]](#cite_note-Fans1-16)[[17]](#cite_note-Fans2-17) "[Chapter 16: The Rescue](/wiki/Chapter_16:_The_Rescue "Chapter 16: The Rescue")," the [Season Two](/wiki/The_Mandalorian_Season_Two "The Mandalorian Season Two") finale of *The Mandalorian* released on [December 18](/wiki/December_18 "December 18"), 2020, teased that *The Book of Boba Fett* would be the show to arrive in that timeframe instead.[[18]](#cite_note-Chapter_16-18)

The following [Monday](/wiki/December_21 "December 21"), show producer Jon Favreau appeared on *Good Morning America* on the [ABC Television Network](/wiki/ABC_Television_Network "ABC Television Network"). He clarified that the two were separate shows, with *The Book of Boba Fett* arriving first.[[19]](#cite_note-GMA_YouTube-19) A [StarWars.com](/wiki/StarWars.com "StarWars.com") post later that day reiterated this and stated that *The Mandalorian* Season Three would go into production in 2021.[[1]](#cite_note-Announcement-1) Filming officially wrapped on [June 8](/wiki/June_8 "June 8"), 2021,[[20]](#cite_note-Wrap-20) and the series was still in post-production as of [September](/wiki/September "September") of that year.[[21]](#cite_note-i205-21)

### Music[]

On [September 22](/wiki/September_22 "September 22"), [2021](/wiki/2021 "2021"), it was reported that [Ludwig Göransson](/wiki/Ludwig_G%C3%B6ransson "Ludwig Göransson") would be composing the music for the show.[[22]](#cite_note-Composer-22) The show is scored by [Joseph Shirley](/wiki/Joseph_Shirley "Joseph Shirley"), while Göransson composed the music themes, including the title theme.[[4]](#cite_note-Chapter_1-4) [The soundtrack of the first four episodes](/wiki/The_Book_of_Boba_Fett:_Vol._1_(Chapters_1-4)_(Original_Soundtrack) "The Book of Boba Fett: Vol. 1 (Chapters 1-4) (Original Soundtrack)") was released digitally on [January 21](/wiki/January_21 "January 21"), [2022](/wiki/2022 "2022").[[23]](#cite_note-soundtrack_vol1-23) [The soundtrack of the last three episodes](/wiki/The_Book_of_Boba_Fett:_Vol._2_(Chapters_5-7)_(Original_Soundtrack) "The Book of Boba Fett: Vol. 2 (Chapters 5-7) (Original Soundtrack)") was released digitally on [February 11](/wiki/February_11 "February 11"), 2022.[[24]](#cite_note-soundtrack_vol2-24)

All the parts of the main title theme were sung by Göransson himself. The theme was inspired by Björn Isfält's score to the Swedish 1984 movie *Ronja Rövardotter*.[[25]](#cite_note-Sveriges_Radio-25)
