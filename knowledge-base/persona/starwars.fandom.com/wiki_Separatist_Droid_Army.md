The **Separatist Droid Army**, otherwise referred to as the **CIS Droid Army**, **Separatist Army**, or the **droid army**, served as the ground forces for the [Confederacy of Independent Systems](/wiki/Confederacy_of_Independent_Systems "Confederacy of Independent Systems")' [military](/wiki/Confederacy_military "Confederacy military") during the course of the [Clone Wars](/wiki/Clone_Wars "Clone Wars"). Primarily constructed by the workers of [Baktoid Combat Automata](/wiki/Baktoid_Combat_Automata "Baktoid Combat Automata"), the [droid](/wiki/Droid "Droid") [army](/wiki/Army "Army") was very largest of its kind in [galactic history](/wiki/Timeline_of_galactic_history "Timeline of galactic history"). The army was led by [Kaleesh](/wiki/Kaleesh "Kaleesh") [cyborg](/wiki/Cyborg "Cyborg") [General](/wiki/General "General") [Grievous](/wiki/Grievous "Grievous"), who held the title of [Supreme Martial Commander of the Separatist Droid Armies](/wiki/Supreme_Martial_Commander_of_the_Separatist_Droid_Armies "Supreme Martial Commander of the Separatist Droid Armies"), while also being commanded by Separatist [leader](/wiki/Head_of_State "Head of State") [Count](/wiki/Count "Count") [Dooku](/wiki/Dooku "Dooku"). The Separatist Droid Army was more numerous than the [Grand Army](/wiki/Grand_Army_of_the_Republic "Grand Army of the Republic") of the [Galactic Republic](/wiki/Galactic_Republic "Galactic Republic"). In addition, the droid army received additional reinforcements throughout the war, such as in [21 BBY](/wiki/21_BBY "21 BBY"), when they requested a supplemental amount of three million [battle droids](/wiki/Battle_droid "Battle droid") with financial assistance from the [InterGalactic Banking Clan](/wiki/InterGalactic_Banking_Clan "InterGalactic Banking Clan").

For three [years](/wiki/Standard_year "Standard year"), the Separatist Droid Army, with transportation from the [Confederacy of Independent Systems Navy](/wiki/Confederacy_of_Independent_Systems_Navy "Confederacy of Independent Systems Navy"), engaged in open warfare with the Grand Army of the Republic on many fronts throughout the galaxy, first coming face-to-face on the [planet](/wiki/Planet "Planet") [Geonosis](/wiki/Geonosis "Geonosis"). However, by the third year of the war, the Confederacy and its droid armies had suffered extensive losses to the Republic in the [Outer Rim Sieges](/wiki/Outer_Rim_Sieges "Outer Rim Sieges"), and was later deactivated in totality at the immediate end of the war following the [deaths](/wiki/Death "Death") of the Confederacy's leadership. Despite the Separatist Droid Army's deactivation and the [rise](/wiki/Proclamation_of_the_New_Order "Proclamation of the New Order") of the [Galactic Empire](/wiki/Galactic_Empire "Galactic Empire") under the command of [Galactic Emperor](/wiki/Galactic_Emperor "Galactic Emperor") [Sheev Palpatine](/wiki/Darth_Sidious "Darth Sidious"), who had orchestrated the war, elements of the droid army remained active several years after the [end](/wiki/Mission_to_Mustafar "Mission to Mustafar") of the Clone Wars, although these small groups of [Separatist holdouts](/wiki/Separatist_holdouts "Separatist holdouts"), which were primarily formed from still-active battle droids, were hunted by the Empire following its rise.

## Organization[]

### Administration[]

 [![Grievous served as the Supreme Martial Commander of the Separatist Droid Armies.](data:image/gif;base64,R0lGODlhAQABAIABAAAAAP///yH5BAEAAAEALAAAAAABAAEAQAICTAEAOw%3D%3D)](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/starwars/images/7/7e/Legion-SupremeCommander.png/revision/latest?cb=20250524223213) [![Grievous served as the Supreme Martial Commander of the Separatist Droid Armies.](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/starwars/images/7/7e/Legion-SupremeCommander.png/revision/latest/scale-to-width-down/300?cb=20250524223213)](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/starwars/images/7/7e/Legion-SupremeCommander.png/revision/latest?cb=20250524223213) 

Grievous served as the Supreme Martial Commander of the Separatist Droid Armies.

 

The Separatist Droid Army, otherwise known as the Separatist Army, was commanded by the [Head of State](/wiki/Head_of_State "Head of State") of the [Confederacy of Independent Systems](/wiki/Confederacy_of_Independent_Systems "Confederacy of Independent Systems"), [Count](/wiki/Count "Count") [Dooku](/wiki/Dooku "Dooku"), who was responsible for administering the day-to-day operations of the entire [Confederacy military](/wiki/Confederacy_military "Confederacy military") and thereby the [droid](/wiki/Droid "Droid") [army](/wiki/Army "Army"). In addition, the army was placed under the command of the [Supreme Martial Commander of the Separatist Droid Armies](/wiki/Supreme_Martial_Commander_of_the_Separatist_Droid_Armies "Supreme Martial Commander of the Separatist Droid Armies"), the [Kaleesh](/wiki/Kaleesh "Kaleesh") [cyborg](/wiki/Cyborg "Cyborg") [General](/wiki/General "General") [Grievous](/wiki/Grievous "Grievous"), whose responsibilities were to manage the logistics and tactics for the entire army.[[3]](#cite_note-Rise-3)

Dooku and Grievous were also supplemented by several handpicked generals drawn from the ranks of the Confederacy's member worlds, all of whom served under Grievous's command. Furthermore, while the defense of vital resources and strategic locations from the [Galactic Republic](/wiki/Galactic_Republic "Galactic Republic") fell to the droid army, several of the Confederacy's aligned worlds were known to manage individualized defense.[[3]](#cite_note-Rise-3)

### Order of battle[]

#### Overview[]

 [![ constructed much of the Separatist Droid Army's ground assault vehicles.](data:image/gif;base64,R0lGODlhAQABAIABAAAAAP///yH5BAEAAAEALAAAAAABAAEAQAICTAEAOw%3D%3D)](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/starwars/images/0/01/Baktoid.svg/revision/latest?cb=20250715012116) [![ constructed much of the Separatist Droid Army's ground assault vehicles.](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/starwars/images/0/01/Baktoid.svg/revision/latest/scale-to-width-down/230?cb=20250715012116)](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/starwars/images/0/01/Baktoid.svg/revision/latest?cb=20250715012116) 

[Baktoid Armor Workshop](/wiki/Baktoid_Armor_Workshop "Baktoid Armor Workshop") constructed much of the Separatist Droid Army's ground assault vehicles.

 

In the initial days of the Confederacy, the Separatist Army featured an assortment of forces provided to them from several of the conglomerates that support the Confederacy and its movement. While the army's forces were supplied from the [Trade Federation](/wiki/Trade_Federation "Trade Federation"), the [Techno Union](/wiki/Techno_Union "Techno Union"), the [Commerce Guild](/wiki/Commerce_Guild "Commerce Guild"), the [Corporate Alliance](/wiki/Corporate_Alliance "Corporate Alliance"), the [InterGalactic Banking Clan](/wiki/InterGalactic_Banking_Clan "InterGalactic Banking Clan"), and the [Retail Caucus](/wiki/Retail_Caucus "Retail Caucus"), the uniform army of [B-series battle droids](/wiki/B-series_battle_droid "B-series battle droid")[[3]](#cite_note-Rise-3) and other [battle droid](/wiki/Battle_droid "Battle droid") models were constructed from the workers of several manufacturing companies, including [Baktoid Combat Automata](/wiki/Baktoid_Combat_Automata "Baktoid Combat Automata").[[20]](#cite_note-Ultimate-20)

Furthermore, the public goals of the Confederacy and thereby the Separatist Droid Army were two-fold. The first of its goals were to defend the planets that declared an independent state from the Republic. Second, the Confederacy sought to surround and therefore overwhelm the Core Worlds and deprive it of vital trade from the rest of the galaxy. Moreover, the leaders of the Confederacy possessed a belief that the assurance of proper negotiations were capable through the means of successfully [blockading](/wiki/Blockade "Blockade") a planet of interest, in which would allow the government to conduct the desired negotiations of the government's terms of independence with the Republic from a position of strength.[[3]](#cite_note-Rise-3)

#### Structure[]

 [![The Trade Federation Droid Army was one of many private security forces that supplemented the Separatist Droid Army.](data:image/gif;base64,R0lGODlhAQABAIABAAAAAP///yH5BAEAAAEALAAAAAABAAEAQAICTAEAOw%3D%3D)](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/starwars/images/2/2f/B1_army_and_landing_craft_on_Naboo.png/revision/latest?cb=20190625172408) [![The Trade Federation Droid Army was one of many private security forces that supplemented the Separatist Droid Army.](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/starwars/images/2/2f/B1_army_and_landing_craft_on_Naboo.png/revision/latest/scale-to-width-down/270?cb=20190625172408)](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/starwars/images/2/2f/B1_army_and_landing_craft_on_Naboo.png/revision/latest?cb=20190625172408) 

The Trade Federation Droid Army was one of many private security forces that supplemented the Separatist Droid Army.

 

Although the bulk of the entire Separatist Army[[3]](#cite_note-Rise-3) and the [army infantry corps](/wiki/Army_infantry_corps "Army infantry corps")[[13]](#cite_note-Force_Collection_B1_battle_droid-13) consisted of models from the B-series battle droid line, with defense forces that were often relied on as the front line of defense for their respective worlds. The security defenses of the Confederacy's supporting corporations were also known to provide support to the government's affiliated planets.[[3]](#cite_note-Rise-3)

Made up of billions of battle droids,[[14]](#cite_note-Helmet_Collection_9-14) the Separatists' ground forces comprised the largest amount of droids ever fielded, though not as numerous as the droids on Geonosis. Factional forces were also deployed on personally led special missions by the members of the Confederacy's legislative [Executive Separatist Council](/wiki/Executive_Separatist_Council "Executive Separatist Council") or their trusted allies. These faction-based forces included the [Trade Federation Home Defense Legions](/wiki/Trade_Federation_Home_Defense_Legions "Trade Federation Home Defense Legions")[[3]](#cite_note-Rise-3) and [Trade Federation Droid Army](/wiki/Trade_Federation_Droid_Army "Trade Federation Droid Army"),[[18]](#cite_note-STAP-18) [Techno Union Droid Army](/wiki/Techno_Union_Droid_Army "Techno Union Droid Army"), [Commerce Guild Punitive Security Forces](/wiki/Commerce_Guild_Punitive_Security_Forces "Commerce Guild Punitive Security Forces"), [Corporate Alliance Policy Administration Directorate](/wiki/Corporate_Alliance_Policy_Administration_Directorate "Corporate Alliance Policy Administration Directorate"), [InterGalactic Banking Clan Collections and Security Division](/wiki/Collections_and_Security_Division "Collections and Security Division"),[[3]](#cite_note-Rise-3) and the private [droid army](/wiki/Retail_Caucus_droid_army "Retail Caucus droid army") of the Retail Caucus.[[17]](#cite_note-Front_Lines-17)

## Personnel[]

### Command[]

 [![OOM command battle droids were capable of leading Separatist Droid Army units below regiment strength.](data:image/gif;base64,R0lGODlhAQABAIABAAAAAP///yH5BAEAAAEALAAAAAABAAEAQAICTAEAOw%3D%3D)](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/starwars/images/7/76/224-Ambush.png/revision/latest?cb=20120916023915) [![OOM command battle droids were capable of leading Separatist Droid Army units below regiment strength.](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/starwars/images/7/76/224-Ambush.png/revision/latest/scale-to-width-down/180?cb=20120916023915)](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/starwars/images/7/76/224-Ambush.png/revision/latest?cb=20120916023915) 

OOM command battle droids were capable of leading Separatist Droid Army units below regiment strength.

 

While the Separatist Droid Army maintained a low cost per unit and surpassed the [Grand Army of the Republic](/wiki/Grand_Army_of_the_Republic "Grand Army of the Republic") in their numbers, battle droids possessed limited intelligence and autonomy. However, to supplant their shortfall, Separatist leader Count Dooku courted experienced leaders from the Confederacy's member worlds to command the droid army, all of whom were placed under the command of General Grievous, who served as the leader for the droid army.[[3]](#cite_note-Rise-3)

Such leaders included [Neimoidian](/wiki/Neimoidian "Neimoidian") General [Lok Durd](/wiki/Lok_Durd "Lok Durd"), an overfed arms developer who used his position as a general to initiate field tests for prototype weapons and General [Horn Ambigene](/wiki/Horn_Ambigene "Horn Ambigene"), a rugged individualist who led an anti-Republic [militia](/wiki/Militia "Militia") for decades prior to the outbreak of the [Clone Wars](/wiki/Clone_Wars "Clone Wars"). Another leader included General [Kleeve](/wiki/Kleeve "Kleeve"), a prominent [Devaronian](/wiki/Devaronian "Devaronian") Separatist leader whose talent was dealing in [bounty hunters](/wiki/Bounty_hunter "Bounty hunter") and [mercenaries](/wiki/Mercenary "Mercenary") to complete his objectives. Other [officers](/wiki/Officer "Officer") were General [Oro Dassyne](/wiki/Oro_Dassyne "Oro Dassyne"),[[3]](#cite_note-Rise-3) the [Kerkoiden](/wiki/Kerkoiden "Kerkoiden") General [Whorm Loathsom](/wiki/Whorm_Loathsom "Whorm Loathsom"),[[7]](#cite_note-TCWfilm-7) and the [Karkarodon](/wiki/Karkarodon "Karkarodon") [Commander](/wiki/Commander "Commander") [Riff Tamson](/wiki/Riff_Tamson "Riff Tamson"), who was responsible for leading the droid army on [aquatic](/wiki/Water "Water") planets.[[3]](#cite_note-Rise-3)

However, despite organic leaders being preferred, the Confederacy did not maintain enough competent commanders to lead all their forces. Instead, the government and its forces relied on droid commanders to lead their soldiers. Modified [B1 droid commanders](/wiki/OOM_command_battle_droid "OOM command battle droid"), which bore [yellow](/wiki/Color "Color") markings, were responsible for leading units below the strength of a [regiment](/wiki/Regiment "Regiment"), while probability-obsessed [T-series military strategic analysis and tactics droids](/wiki/T-series_military_strategic_analysis_and_tactics_droid "T-series military strategic analysis and tactics droid") commanded larger units assigned to their missions.[[3]](#cite_note-Rise-3) The Separatist Droid Army also employed [ST-series military strategic analysis and tactics droid](/wiki/ST-series_military_strategic_analysis_and_tactics_droid "ST-series military strategic analysis and tactics droid"), highly intelligent droids who were capable of conducting military operations without direction from a superior officer.[[21]](#cite_note-The_Last_Battle-21) [BX-series droid commandos](/wiki/BX-series_droid_commando "BX-series droid commando") could also serve as commanders[[22]](#cite_note-Outlaws-22) or captains. Captains and other high-ranking commando droids were known to be distinguished from standard BX units by white markings on their plating,[[23]](#cite_note-Encyclopedia-23) although not all BX officers were visibly distant from their fellows in practice.[[22]](#cite_note-Outlaws-22)[[24]](#cite_note-Citadel-24)

In order to openly engage the Grand Army of the Republic, the Separatist Droid Army possessed and wielded a vast array of different weapons throughout its operational history. [E-5 blaster rifles](/wiki/E-5_blaster_rifle "E-5 blaster rifle"), a type of reverse-engineered [blaster rifle](/wiki/Blaster_rifle "Blaster rifle") design from [BlasTech Industries](/wiki/BlasTech_Industries "BlasTech Industries"), was a weapon that proved to be more than sufficient for Separatist droids,[[20]](#cite_note-Ultimate-20) such as standard battle droids and droid commandos,[[47]](#cite_note-Rookies-47) as the weapon generated excessive amounts of heat when in use, which thereby made the E-5 uncomfortable for organic soldiers.[[20]](#cite_note-Ultimate-20)

 [![A B1 battle droid using a J-10 dual blaster cannon.](data:image/gif;base64,R0lGODlhAQABAIABAAAAAP///yH5BAEAAAEALAAAAAABAAEAQAICTAEAOw%3D%3D)](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/starwars/images/7/71/J-10DualBlasterCannon.png/revision/latest?cb=20121004232016) [![A B1 battle droid using a J-10 dual blaster cannon.](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/starwars/images/7/71/J-10DualBlasterCannon.png/revision/latest/scale-to-width-down/270?cb=20121004232016)](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/starwars/images/7/71/J-10DualBlasterCannon.png/revision/latest?cb=20121004232016)

Another weapon that saw frequent use by the droid army were [wrist blasters](/wiki/Wrist_blaster "Wrist blaster"), a model of wrist-mounted [blaster](/wiki/Blaster "Blaster") that was primarily used by B2-series super battle droids,[[1]](#cite_note-Episode_II-1) as well as the [E-5s sniper rifle](/wiki/E-5s_sniper_rifle "E-5s sniper rifle") and [RG-4D](/wiki/RG-4D "RG-4D"), a model of [blaster pistol](/wiki/Blaster_pistol "Blaster pistol") that saw use by the army's B1 droid commanders.[[28]](#cite_note-Battlefront_II-28) Separatist droids also made use of [J-10 dual blaster cannons](/wiki/J-10_dual_blaster_cannon "J-10 dual blaster cannon"), a type of [blaster cannon](/wiki/Blaster_cannon "Blaster cannon") that was essentially a pair of scaled-up reciprocating E-5 blaster rifles. However, the J-10 saw rare deployments throughout the war due to its many drawbacks.[[56]](#cite_note-Gadgets-56) The [DDC-MR6 modular rifle](/wiki/DDC-MR6_modular_rifle "DDC-MR6 modular rifle") was designed for the Separatists but, due to its high cost, only a prototype run of the weapon was used before the Confederacy moved onto cheaper weapons instead.[[57]](#cite_note-SM-57)

To accommodate their blasters, the Separatist Droid Army also incorporated and deployed an array of melee weaponry used by their forces. [Vibroblades](/wiki/Vibroblade "Vibroblade"), a type of bladed [knife](/wiki/Knife "Knife"), often saw use by the army's commando droids, while the [electrostaff](/wiki/Electrostaff "Electrostaff"), a weapon capable of engaging a [lightsaber](/wiki/Lightsaber "Lightsaber"), was typically carried and wielded by IG-100 MagnaGuards.[[2]](#cite_note-Episode_III-2) The droid army also employed a variety of different explosive weaponry, which included the [Sienar Fleet Systems](/wiki/Sienar_Fleet_Systems "Sienar Fleet Systems")-manufactured[[3]](#cite_note-Rise-3) [RPS-6 rocket launcher](/wiki/RPS-6_rocket_launcher "RPS-6 rocket launcher"),[[58]](#cite_note-Lair_of_Grievous-58) the remote operated [thermal annihilator bomb](/wiki/Thermal_annihilator_bomb "Thermal annihilator bomb"),[[39]](#cite_note-Kidnapped-39) [Class-A thermal detonators](/wiki/Class-A_thermal_detonator "Class-A thermal detonator"),[[47]](#cite_note-Rookies-47) [dioxis grenades](/wiki/Dioxis_grenade "Dioxis grenade"),[[59]](#cite_note-BX_Legion-59) and [precision laser darts](/wiki/Vilmarh%27s_Revenge "Vilmarh's Revenge").[[60]](#cite_note-In_Search_of_the_Crystal-60)

Furthermore, the droid army also made use of several different pieces of equipment. Separatist droids, such as OOM command battle droids and oftentimes standard battle droids used [macrobinoculars](/wiki/Macrobinoculars "Macrobinoculars") in order to locate far-away enemies. [Personal energy shields](/wiki/Droid_commando_personal_shield "Droid commando personal shield") were also used within the Separatist Army by its unit of BX-series droid commandos stationed at the [Citadel](/wiki/Citadel "Citadel") on the planet [Lola Sayu](/wiki/Lola_Sayu "Lola Sayu").[[61]](#cite_note-Counterattack-61) Moreover, [comlink booster packs](/wiki/Comlink_booster_pack "Comlink booster pack") were another piece of technological equipment that saw extensive use by the B1 battle droids of the droid army,[[2]](#cite_note-Episode_III-2) while firefighter battle droids, a variant of the B1 battle droid stationed aboard starships of the Confederacy's [naval branch](/wiki/Confederacy_of_Independent_Systems_Navy "Confederacy of Independent Systems Navy"), came equipped with [fire hoses](/wiki/Fire_hose "Fire hose") and enhanced thermal shielding in order to eliminate any and all fires that took place aboard their stationed vessels.[[62]](#cite_note-DM-62)

The droid army also utilized [Defoliator Deployment Tanks](/wiki/Defoliator_Deployment_Tank "Defoliator Deployment Tank"), a modified variant of the Armored Assault Tank created by General Lok Durd that was armed with a [turret](/wiki/Turret "Turret")-mounted [missile launcher](/wiki/Missile_launcher "Missile launcher") designed to fire [defoliator](/wiki/Defoliator "Defoliator") shells.[[4]](#cite_note-Defenders_of_Peace-4)

Another ground assault vehicle used by the Separatist Army was the [super tank](/wiki/Separatist_super-tank_prototype "Separatist super-tank prototype"), an experimental [tank](/wiki/Tank "Tank") equipped with heavy [armor](/wiki/Armor "Armor") and a [ray shield](/wiki/Ray_shield "Ray shield") powerful enough to withstand the firepower of several Republic [AV-7 Anti-vehicle Artillery Cannons](/wiki/AV-7_Anti-vehicle_Artillery_Cannon "AV-7 Anti-vehicle Artillery Cannon") at once.[[64]](#cite_note-Weapons_Factory-64) One other tank in the Separatist Droid Army was the large [seismic tank](/wiki/Seismic_tank "Seismic tank").[[65]](#cite_note-BMF_37-65)

In addition to their plethora of ground assault vehicles, the Separatist Army incorporated several different models of transportation vehicles for the purpose of moving and deploying their troops in high numbers.[[2]](#cite_note-Episode_III-2) Manufactured by [Baktoid Armor Workshop](/wiki/Baktoid_Armor_Workshop "Baktoid Armor Workshop")[[20]](#cite_note-Ultimate-20) and initially used by the Trade Federation Droid Army,[[54]](#cite_note-Episode_I-54) the [Multi-Troop Transport](/wiki/Multi-Troop_Transport "Multi-Troop Transport") was a model of heavy repulsorlift vehicle designed to transport battle droids to the battlefield or cargo, but was also used as both a tank and siege ram.[[3]](#cite_note-Rise-3)

The [Platoon Attack Craft](/wiki/Platoon_Attack_Craft "Platoon Attack Craft"), another transport that initially saw use by the Trade Federation, was a repulsorlift vehicle[[2]](#cite_note-Episode_III-2) capable of carrying many battle droids to a designated area.[[72]](#cite_note-Complete_Vehicles-72) Moreover, the Separatist Army also employed the [XT Beetle Transport](/wiki/XT_Beetle_Transport "XT Beetle Transport"), a model of transport that was originally used by the Trade Federation army and moved on ten pairs of mechanized legs. During the [Separatist Crisis](/wiki/Separatist_Crisis "Separatist Crisis"), Count Dooku's emerging Separatist movement, the Confederacy of Independent Systems, secretly began manufacturing the largest droid army in [galactic history](/wiki/Timeline_of_galactic_history "Timeline of galactic history") on the planet [Geonosis](/wiki/Geonosis "Geonosis")[[1]](#cite_note-Episode_II-1) in stark defiance of Republic laws.[[78]](#cite_note-VE-78) The Separatist movement was supported by the Trade Federation, the Commerce Guild, the Techno Union, the InterGalactic Banking Clan, and the Corporate Alliance. The massive droid army was discovered by [Jedi Knight](/wiki/Jedi_Knight "Jedi Knight") [Obi-Wan Kenobi](/wiki/Obi-Wan_Kenobi "Obi-Wan Kenobi"), whose capture triggered an [attack](/wiki/First_Battle_of_Geonosis "First Battle of Geonosis") by the [Jedi Order](/wiki/Jedi_Order "Jedi Order") and the Galactic Republic's new Grand Army of the Republic, which had been commissioned by [Jedi Master](/wiki/Jedi_Master "Jedi Master") [Sifo-Dyas](/wiki/Sifo-Dyas "Sifo-Dyas"). The outbreak of the Clone Wars played into the hands of [Darth Sidious](/wiki/Darth_Sidious "Darth Sidious"),[[1]](#cite_note-Episode_II-1) who masterminded the conflict while simultaneously serving as [Supreme Chancellor](/wiki/Supreme_Chancellor "Supreme Chancellor") of the Republic.[[2]](#cite_note-Episode_III-2)

#### The campaigns of the final year[]

In [19 BBY](/wiki/19_BBY "19 BBY"),[[19]](#cite_note-Galactic_Atlas-19) elements of the Droid Army under [Admiral](/wiki/Admiral "Admiral") [Trench](/wiki/Trench "Trench") engaged in the [Battle of Ringo Vinda](/wiki/Battle_of_Ringo_Vinda "Battle of Ringo Vinda") Under orders from Dooku they attempted to kidnap clone trooper [Tup](/wiki/Tup "Tup"), whose [inhibitor chip](/wiki/Behavioral_modification_biochip "Behavioral modification biochip") had malfunctioned and caused him to kill Jedi Master [Tiplar](/wiki/Tiplar "Tiplar"). The Separatist was foiled by General Skywalker, [Captain](/wiki/Captain "Captain") Rex, and [ARC trooper](/wiki/Advanced_Recon_Commando "Advanced Recon Commando") [Fives](/wiki/Fives "Fives"), who rescued Tup from Commander [Kraken](/wiki/Kraken "Kraken")'s [shuttle](/wiki/Kraken%27s_Maxillipede_shuttle "Kraken's Maxillipede shuttle").[[37]](#cite_note-The_Unknown-37) After Count Dooku pressured [Rush Clovis](/wiki/Rush_Clovis "Rush Clovis"), the new head of the InterGalactic Banking Clan, to raise interest rates on the Republic, Separatist droid forces attacked Clone Commander [Thorn](/wiki/Thorn "Thorn")'s forces on [Scipio](/wiki/Scipio "Scipio"). When the [Galactic Senate](/wiki/Galactic_Senate "Galactic Senate") voted to [invade](/wiki/Invasion_of_Scipio "Invasion of Scipio") Scipio, Dooku promptly withdrew Separatist forces from the planet. The capture of Scipio gave the Supreme Chancellor control over the banks; placing the galactic economy under the control of the Sith.[[96]](#cite_note-Crisis_at_the_Heart-96)

Later, two of Grievous' IG-100 MagnaGuards assassinated Jedi Master [Tu-Anh](/wiki/Tu-Anh "Tu-Anh") on the neutral world of [Utapau](/wiki/Utapau "Utapau").[[97]](#cite_note-A_Death_on_Utapau-97) While investigating Tu-Anh's death, Master Kenobi and Jedi Knight Skywalker fought the two MagnaGuards and discovered that the Separatists were interested in acquiring a large [kyber crystal](/wiki/Kyber_crystal "Kyber crystal") through several [Sugi](/wiki/Sugi "Sugi") mercenaries led by [Endente](/wiki/Endente "Endente").[[60]](#cite_note-In_Search_of_the_Crystal-60) After Kenobi and Skywalker stole the crystal, General brought some droid forces, including some of his MagnaGuards, to recover the [crystal](/wiki/Crystal "Crystal").[[98]](#cite_note-Crystal_Crisis-98) Despite Grievous evacuating the kyber crystal off world, the Jedi managed to destroy the crystal and the [Separatist supply ship](/wiki/Dual_Hemisphere-Omni_Support_Vessel "Dual Hemisphere-Omni Support Vessel") it was in, the resulting explosion destroying the Separatist fleet around the planet.[[99]](#cite_note-The_Big_Bang-99)

After [escaping](/wiki/Escape_from_the_Spire "Escape from the Spire") from [the Spire](/wiki/Spire_(Stygeon_Prime) "Spire (Stygeon Prime)") prison, [Maul](/wiki/Maul "Maul")'s [Shadow Collective](/wiki/Shadow_Collective "Shadow Collective") inflicted heavy casualties on the Droid Army at the [Battle of Zanbar](/wiki/Battle_of_Zanbar "Battle of Zanbar") before retreating.[[100]](#cite_note-Son_of_Dathomir_1-100) The Shadow Collective later lured Count Dooku and General Grievous into a [trap](/wiki/Battle_on_Ord_Mantell "Battle on Ord Mantell") on [Ord Mantell](/wiki/Ord_Mantell "Ord Mantell"). Separatist ground and space forces were decimated during the battle and the two Separatist leaders were captured by Maul and his allies.[[35]](#cite_note-Son_of_Dathomir_2-35) Maul's Shadow Collective was later [defeated](/wiki/Second_Battle_of_Dathomir "Second Battle of Dathomir") by Separatist forces with the help of [Darth](/wiki/Darth "Darth") Sidious at Dathomir.[[101]](#cite_note-Son_of_Dathomir_3-101)

At some point in 19 BBY,[[102]](#cite_note-YodaTCW-102) the Confederacy made an effort to develop an army of [megadroids](/wiki/Megadroid "Megadroid") under the codename [Operation: Titan](/wiki/Operation:_Titan "Operation: Titan").[[103]](#cite_note-Yoda_7-103) Created to be [superweapons](/wiki/Superweapon "Superweapon") that had the potential to alter the course of the war,[[104]](#cite_note-Bounty_Hunter_42-104) the megadroids were constructed on the small planet of [Golatha](/wiki/Golatha "Golatha") within a [droid factory](/wiki/Golatha_factory "Golatha factory"), which was discovered and destroyed by the Jedi.[[105]](#cite_note-Yoda_8-105) Only one megadroid managed to survive the Clone Wars.[[104]](#cite_note-Bounty_Hunter_42-104)

#### Outer Rim Sieges[]

:   "*Send a message to the ships of the Trade Federation. All droid units must shut down immediately.*"
:   ―Darth Sidious ordering the shutdown of the Separatist's droid forces to Darth Vader[[2]](#cite_note-Episode_III-2)

The Confederacy would find itself pushed into the Outer Rim Territories and besieged by the Republic in the [Outer Rim Sieges](/wiki/Outer_Rim_Sieges "Outer Rim Sieges").[[106]](#cite_note-On_the_Front_Lines-106) By that time, the Republic had found and destroyed most of the Confederacy's droid factories.[[22]](#cite_note-Outlaws-22) Nonetheless, the Separatist Droid Army also fought an extended campaign against the Republic on [Anaxes](/wiki/Anaxes "Anaxes"), a world that held vital Republic [shipyards](/wiki/Shipyard "Shipyard").[[74]](#cite_note-The_Bad_Batch-74) Admiral Trench was aided by the Techno Union's [Foreman](/wiki/Foreman "Foreman") [Wat Tambor](/wiki/Wat_Tambor "Wat Tambor") who used an unconscious clone trooper [Echo](/wiki/Echo "Echo") to provide the Separatists with an algorithm on the Republic's strategies.[[107]](#cite_note-A_Distant_Echo-107) Echo was rescued by [Clone Force 99](/wiki/Clone_Force_99 "Clone Force 99"), General Skywalker, and Rex who fought Tambor's D1-series aerial battle droids and [octuptarra tri-droids](/wiki/Octuptarra_tri-droid "Octuptarra tri-droid") with the help of the [Poletecs](/wiki/Poletec "Poletec") of [Skako Minor](/wiki/Skako_Minor "Skako Minor").[[108]](#cite_note-On_the_Wings_of_Keeradaks-108) With the help of Echo, Skywalker and the clones infiltrated Trench's flagship and disabled his droid army on Anaxes, turning the tide of the battle in favor of the Republic.[[109]](#cite_note-Unfinished_Business-109)

Following the death of Count Dooku at the [Battle of Coruscant](/wiki/Battle_of_Coruscant "Battle of Coruscant"), the Separatist Droid Army battled the Grand Army of the Republic on numerous fronts including [Kashyyyk](/wiki/Kashyyyk "Kashyyyk"), Utapau, Saleucami, [Mygeeto](/wiki/Mygeeto "Mygeeto"), Felucia,[[2]](#cite_note-Episode_III-2) and [Kaller](/wiki/Kaller "Kaller").[[6]](#cite_note-Kanan_1-6) While the Droid Army still numbered in the billions,[[110]](#cite_note-MOF-110) its numbers had, by that time, been severely diminished, greatly diminishing its effectiveness.[[111]](#cite_note-AftermathEp-111) These battles coincided with Palpatine's initiation of [Order 66](/wiki/Order_66 "Order 66"), which led to the [Great Jedi Purge](/wiki/Great_Jedi_Purge "Great Jedi Purge"). Palpatine than transformed the Galactic Republic into the [Galactic Empire](/wiki/Galactic_Empire "Galactic Empire"). Palpatine then sent his new apprentice Anakin Skywalker, who had assumed the persona of Darth Vader, to [execute](/wiki/Mission_to_Mustafar "Mission to Mustafar") the Separatist Council on [Mustafar](/wiki/Mustafar "Mustafar"). Vader also transmitted a signal destroying the Droid Army, ending the Clone Wars.[[2]](#cite_note-Episode_III-2)

 [![General Kalani made peace with former adversaries.](data:image/gif;base64,R0lGODlhAQABAIABAAAAAP///yH5BAEAAAEALAAAAAABAAEAQAICTAEAOw%3D%3D)](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/starwars/images/7/74/Kalani_Rebels.png/revision/latest?cb=20161018210742) [![General Kalani made peace with former adversaries.](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/starwars/images/7/74/Kalani_Rebels.png/revision/latest/scale-to-width-down/205?cb=20161018210742)](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/starwars/images/7/74/Kalani_Rebels.png/revision/latest?cb=20161018210742) 

General Kalani made peace with former adversaries.

 

Despite the destruction of the Separatist Droid Army, its legacy still lived on in battle droids that survived the Clone Wars. After the Clones Wars various [Separatist holdouts](/wiki/Separatist_holdouts "Separatist holdouts") survived and attempted to fight the Empire.[[112]](#cite_note-Tarkin-112) Nevertheless, the Empire ordered all surviving battle droids to be shot on sight[[21]](#cite_note-The_Last_Battle-21) and destroyed.[[113]](#cite_note-Decommissioned-113) Though the IG-series battle droids that had been used the CIS were assigned this fate, the efforts of the [Droid Gotra](/wiki/Droid_Gotra "Droid Gotra") spared many.[[43]](#cite_note-Scum_and_Villainy-43) By the [Imperial Era](/wiki/Imperial_Era "Imperial Era"), the gotra was made up of surviving battle droids[[112]](#cite_note-Tarkin-112) that sought to fight for [droid rights](/wiki/Droid_rights "Droid rights").[[43]](#cite_note-Scum_and_Villainy-43) At some stage, the Tagge Corporation had designed a prototype battle droid to try and secure a contract with the Confederacy, only for TaggeCo to loose out on the contract. Its battle droids, therefore, were left unsold and abandoned in warehouses[[42]](#cite_note-Doctor_Aphra_35-42) while TaggeCo itself would ally with the Empire.[[112]](#cite_note-Tarkin-112)

During the Imperial Era, a group of battle droids under the leadership of General Kalani inhabited the wreckage of a [DH-Omni Support Vessel](/wiki/Unidentified_DH-Omni_Support_Vessel_(Agamar) "Unidentified DH-Omni Support Vessel (Agamar)") on the planet Agamar. Believing the destruction signal was a Republic trick, Kalani had overridden the command signal, preventing the shutdown of his droid forces.[[21]](#cite_note-The_Last_Battle-21) In [2 BBY](/wiki/2_BBY "2 BBY"),[[114]](#cite_note-Date_note-114) he encountered the former Clone Captain Rex, the Jedi Purge survivor [Kanan Jarrus](/wiki/Kanan_Jarrus "Kanan Jarrus"), and his [Padawan](/wiki/Padawan "Padawan") [Ezra Bridger](/wiki/Ezra_Bridger "Ezra Bridger"). Still refusing the way the Clone Wars had ended, Kalani organized a [final battle](/wiki/Mission_to_Agamar_(2_BBY) "Mission to Agamar (2 BBY)") with his prisoners. The rebels managed to break through Kalani's lines and Ezra managed to convince Kalani and Rex to put aside their differences in order to escape Imperial forces. This cooperation informally ended the Clone Wars and allowed Rex and Kalani and his droids to make peace with the past.[[21]](#cite_note-The_Last_Battle-21)

Another holdout during the reign of the Galactic Empire was the [holdout](/wiki/Separatist_holdout_(PM-6986) "Separatist holdout (PM-6986)") on [PM-6986](/wiki/PM-6986 "PM-6986"), led by [JF-86](/wiki/JF-86 "JF-86"). After the Battle for Anaxes and a [cataclysm](/wiki/Destruction_of_Anaxes "Destruction of Anaxes") that destroyed the planet, a droid army survived on a [Separatist stronghold](/wiki/Separatist_stronghold_(PM-6986) "Separatist stronghold (PM-6986)") on what would become the [planetoid](/wiki/Planetoid "Planetoid") PM-6986. Awoken by a Separatist signal that originated [light-years](/wiki/Light-year "Light-year") away, JF-86 proceeded to reactivate the rest of the droid army. Following its last orders, JF-86 sought to destroy the Galactic Senate of the Republic, unaware of the events in the galaxy and the transformation of the Republic into the Empire. JF-86 was not able to carry out its plan however, as it possessed no [starships](/wiki/Starship "Starship") to ferry its troops, and as such, was forced to remain on the planetoid, waiting for an opportunity.[[115]](#cite_note-Dawn-115)
