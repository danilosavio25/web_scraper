**Force spirits**, also known as **Force ghosts**,[[5]](#cite_note-Rise_of_Skywalker_Graphic-5) were [beings](/wiki/Species "Species") who gained [immortality](/wiki/Immortality "Immortality") through the preservation of their consciousness after [death](/wiki/Death "Death"). When lifeforms died, they became one with [the Force](/wiki/The_Force "The Force"). Through special training, however, deceased [Force-sensitives](/wiki/Force-sensitive "Force-sensitive") could retain their identity and, therefore, communicate with the living. By achieving spiritual immortality, a person's physical form would vanish upon their death. This knowledge could be learned only by those who fully embraced the [light side of the Force](/wiki/Light_side_of_the_Force "Light side of the Force"), such as the [Jedi](/wiki/Jedi "Jedi") who lived by the [Jedi Code](/wiki/Jedi_Code "Jedi Code"), a code of selflessness and sacrifice. The followers of the [dark side of the Force](/wiki/Dark_side_of_the_Force "Dark side of the Force"), such as the [Sith](/wiki/Sith "Sith"), were incapable of learning this power, though there were many techniques open to them that offered similar benefits.

Before the [Fall of the Republic](/wiki/Republic_Era "Republic Era"), [Jedi Master](/wiki/Jedi_Master "Jedi Master") [Qui-Gon Jinn](/wiki/Qui-Gon_Jinn "Qui-Gon Jinn") learned the ability to become a Force spirit from the [Shaman of the Whills](/wiki/Shaman_of_the_Whills "Shaman of the Whills"), but was killed by [Darth Maul](/wiki/Maul "Maul") before he could complete his training. As a result, for a time he could only commune with the living as a disembodied voice. Near the [end](/wiki/Order_66 "Order 66") of the [Clone Wars](/wiki/Clone_Wars "Clone Wars"), a conflict in which the [Galactic Republic](/wiki/Galactic_Republic "Galactic Republic") and [Jedi Order](/wiki/Jedi_Order "Jedi Order") were at war with the [Separatists](/wiki/Confederacy_of_Independent_Systems "Confederacy of Independent Systems"), [Grand Master](/wiki/Grand_Master "Grand Master") [Yoda](/wiki/Yoda "Yoda") embarked on a spiritual journey that brought him to the [planets](/wiki/Planet "Planet") [Dagobah](/wiki/Dagobah "Dagobah") and [Moraband](/wiki/Moraband "Moraband")—as well as the [birthplace](/wiki/Wellspring_of_Life "Wellspring of Life") of the [midi-chlorians](/wiki/Midi-chlorian "Midi-chlorian")—and ultimately learned how to retain his identity after death. This ability was also learned by Jedi Master [Obi-Wan Kenobi](/wiki/Obi-Wan_Kenobi "Obi-Wan Kenobi") during the [rise](/wiki/Imperial_Era "Imperial Era") of the [Galactic Empire](/wiki/Galactic_Empire "Galactic Empire"). Qui-Gon subsequently mastered the ability to manifest in an almost solid form. Although [Anakin Skywalker](/wiki/Anakin_Skywalker "Anakin Skywalker")—a fallen [Jedi Knight](/wiki/Jedi_Knight "Jedi Knight") and the [Chosen One](/wiki/Chosen_One "Chosen One") of the Force—never received the training that allowed his mentors to become Force spirits, his [redemption and sacrifice](/wiki/Battle_of_Endor "Battle of Endor") made it possible for his consciousness to be preserved after death by Kenobi and Yoda, much like how his grandson became a spirit in [35 ABY](/wiki/35_ABY "35 ABY"). In [34 ABY](/wiki/34_ABY "34 ABY") and 35 ABY respectively, [Luke Skywalker](/wiki/Luke_Skywalker "Luke Skywalker") and [Leia Organa](/wiki/Leia_Skywalker_Organa_Solo "Leia Skywalker Organa Solo")—Anakin's biological children—attained immortality like their [father](/wiki/Parent "Parent").

## Description[]

:   "*No longer certain that one ever does win a war, I am. For in fighting the battles, the bloodshed, already lost we have. Yet open to us a path remains, that unknown to the Sith is. Through this path, victory we may yet find. Not victory in the Clone Wars, but victory for all time.*"

Through [the light side](/wiki/Light_side_of_the_Force "Light side of the Force") of [the Force](/wiki/The_Force "The Force"), certain beings with special training were able to allow their consciousness to persist after death and appear as Force spirits,[[6]](#cite_note-Episode_V-6) made possible by energy from the [Living Force](/wiki/Living_Force "Living Force") flowing into the [Cosmic Force](/wiki/Cosmic_Force "Cosmic Force").[[4]](#cite_note-Voices-4)

The knowledge was open only to those who followed the light side,[[1]](#cite_note-Sacrifice-1) so sometimes[[7]](#cite_note-Episode_IX-7) only certain individuals, such as [Jedi](/wiki/Jedi "Jedi") and the [Force Priestesses](/wiki/Force_Priestesses "Force Priestesses"), beings who dwelled within the [Wellspring of Life](/wiki/Wellspring_of_Life "Wellspring of Life"), a location said to be the origin of the [midi-chlorians](/wiki/Midi-chlorian "Midi-chlorian"), were able to learn the ability to manifest after death. The dangerous level to become a Force spirit was low, but only possible for a rare few Jedi.[[8]](#cite_note-Expert_Guide-8)

 [![Qui-Gon Jinn appears to Kenobi for the first time as a Force spirit.](data:image/gif;base64,R0lGODlhAQABAIABAAAAAP///yH5BAEAAAEALAAAAAABAAEAQAICTAEAOw%3D%3D)](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/starwars/images/2/29/JinnAppearsToKenobi-OWKpVI.png/revision/latest?cb=20220723192410) [![Qui-Gon Jinn appears to Kenobi for the first time as a Force spirit.](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/starwars/images/2/29/JinnAppearsToKenobi-OWKpVI.png/revision/latest/scale-to-width-down/250?cb=20220723192410)](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/starwars/images/2/29/JinnAppearsToKenobi-OWKpVI.png/revision/latest?cb=20220723192410) 

Qui-Gon Jinn appears to Kenobi for the first time as a Force spirit.

 

The first known Jedi to learn the secret was [Qui-Gon Jinn](/wiki/Qui-Gon_Jinn "Qui-Gon Jinn"), who learned it from a [Shaman of the Whills](/wiki/Shaman_of_the_Whills "Shaman of the Whills").[[9]](#cite_note-Jinn_gallery-9) His training remained incomplete at the time of his death, so he could only manifest as a disembodied voice.[[4]](#cite_note-Voices-4) Nevertheless, Jinn's spirit appeared in a vision to Anakin Skywalker and Obi-Wan Kenobi on Mortis, an incident they wrongly recognized as a true manifestation.[[9]](#cite_note-Jinn_gallery-9) Qui-Gon was later able to complete his training even in death. He became especially determined after Anakin's fall, pushing himself to emerge fully, a feat that took him almost a decade to complete. This later allowed him to appear to [Obi-Wan Kenobi](/wiki/Obi-Wan_Kenobi "Obi-Wan Kenobi") on [Tatooine](/wiki/Tatooine "Tatooine") in an almost corporeal form twenty years after [Order 66](/wiki/Order_66 "Order 66").[[10]](#cite_note-Master_and_Apprentice-10) This secret was later passed on to Obi-Wan Kenobi, Yoda, and [Anakin Skywalker](/wiki/Anakin_Skywalker "Anakin Skywalker").[[11]](#cite_note-Episode_VI-11)

Force spirits were not only able to appear anywhere in the galaxy with a strong presence in the Force, and some were able to even manipulate the Force to similar extents as they could in life. This was demonstrated by Yoda's capability of manipulating weather to create [lightning strikes](/wiki/Force_lightning "Force lightning"). Additionally, he was also shown to be capable of striking Luke Skywalker with his walking stick, demonstrating an ability to physically interact with the living world despite both Yoda and the stick being ostensibly non-corporeal.[[12]](#cite_note-Episode_VIII-12) This kind of power was also shown by the Force spirit of Luke Skywalker himself, who prevented [his father's lightsaber](/wiki/Skywalker_lightsaber "Skywalker lightsaber") from being burned in the crashed [TIE whisper](/wiki/TIE/wi_modified_interceptor "TIE/wi modified interceptor") by Rey, and used [telekinesis](/wiki/Telekinesis "Telekinesis") to raise [*Red Five*](/wiki/Red_Five_(T-65B_X-wing_starfighter) "Red Five (T-65B X-wing starfighter)") from the [waters](/wiki/Water "Water") of [Ahch-To](/wiki/Ahch-To "Ahch-To").[[7]](#cite_note-Episode_IX-7) Anakin and Yoda were seen conjuring objects to aid them, as Yoda, despite dying without it, had his [Gimer stick](/wiki/Gimer_stick "Gimer stick"), and Anakin was able to use his old [lightsaber](/wiki/Lightsaber "Lightsaber"), despite the fact that he died without one and it was still lost at the time, to fight off the Sith wraiths before teleporting Luke back to [Tython](/wiki/Tython "Tython").[[13]](#cite_note-Shadow_of_the_Sith-13)

Obi-Wan Kenobi spoke to Luke Skywalker on Dagobah as a spirit.

 

Upon becoming one with the Force, the physical body of a Jedi vanished from existence either immediately[[14]](#cite_note-Episode_IV-14) or after a short period of time, leaving their [clothes](/wiki/Clothing "Clothing") behind.[[11]](#cite_note-Episode_VI-11) After death, the Jedi's spirit would go to the [Netherworld of the Force](/wiki/Netherworld_of_the_Force "Netherworld of the Force"), another realm of existence.[[15]](#cite_note-TLJA_6-15) Despite not being physically part of a Jedi's body, [cybernetics](/wiki/Cybernetics "Cybernetics") could also vanish if attached to a physical body, as was the case with Luke Skywalker, whose cybernetic hand disappeared alongside his body when he died.[[12]](#cite_note-Episode_VIII-12)

 [![Like his Jedi masters before him, Luke Skywalker was also able to retain his consciousness as a Force spirit.](data:image/gif;base64,R0lGODlhAQABAIABAAAAAP///yH5BAEAAAEALAAAAAABAAEAQAICTAEAOw%3D%3D)](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/starwars/images/c/c5/LukeSkywalkerForceGhost-TROS.png/revision/latest?cb=20210110050551) [![Like his Jedi masters before him, Luke Skywalker was also able to retain his consciousness as a Force spirit.](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/starwars/images/c/c5/LukeSkywalkerForceGhost-TROS.png/revision/latest/scale-to-width-down/250?cb=20210110050551)](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/starwars/images/c/c5/LukeSkywalkerForceGhost-TROS.png/revision/latest?cb=20210110050551) 

Like his Jedi masters before him, Luke Skywalker was also able to retain his consciousness as a Force spirit.

 

Anakin Skywalker appeared on the planet [Mustafar](/wiki/Mustafar "Mustafar"), home to the [castle](/wiki/Fortress_Vader "Fortress Vader") he had used as [Darth](/wiki/Darth "Darth") Vader, to help lead [Lina Graf's crew](/wiki/Lina_Graf%27s_crew "Lina Graf's crew") outside of the fortress. Skywalker made a lightsaber appear in his hands, ensuing its light could be seen by the crew to get them away from the [lava fumes](/wiki/Lava_fume "Lava fume"). Even though she was inside the starship *[Rabbit's Foot](/wiki/Rabbit%27s_Foot "Rabbit's Foot")* when she made the comment, he was also aware of [Lina Graf](/wiki/Lina_Graf "Lina Graf")'s comment that she was sure ghosts did not exist, laughing at the statement as he stood on Mustafar.[[16]](#cite_note-Ghosts_5-16) Skywalker was able to appear as a spirit on [Peridea](/wiki/Peridea "Peridea").[[17]](#cite_note-Ahsoka_VIII-17) Preserving one's consciousness was not technically reserved for members of the Jedi order, as users of the light side not affiliated with the Order could accomplish this feat. One year following her brother's death, [Leia Organa](/wiki/Leia_Skywalker_Organa_Solo "Leia Skywalker Organa Solo") became the only known individual to retain their self-awareness via the light side without technically being a Jedi, and her body did not vanish until the subsequent death of [her son](/wiki/Ben_Solo "Ben Solo") and the simultaneous disappearance of his body. The clothes worn by Leia during her death were no longer sported by her spirit, supplanted by white garments.[[7]](#cite_note-Episode_IX-7)

Luke and Leia appeared to Rey on Tatooine, giving their blessing on her adopting the Skywalker name.

 

In contrast to individuals committed to the light, the [Sith](/wiki/Sith "Sith") were unable to manifest themselves after death in the way of the Jedi, causing them to concentrate on gaining carnal power only.[[1]](#cite_note-Sacrifice-1) However, several possibilities were open to darksiders who wished to endure the demise of their flesh: One Sith, the [Presence](/wiki/Presence "Presence"), was able to preserve part of her conscious essence so long as it was bound to an object like a [Sith holocron](/wiki/Sith_holocron "Sith holocron") in the [Sith temple](/wiki/Malachor_Sith_Temple "Malachor Sith Temple") of [Malachor](/wiki/Malachor "Malachor").[[18]](#cite_note-Twilight_of_the_Apprentice-18) [Darth Momin](/wiki/Darth_Momin "Darth Momin") had part of his consciousness preserved in his [mask](/wiki/Mask_of_Lord_Momin "Mask of Lord Momin")[[19]](#cite_note-Dark_Lord_22-19) until he was crushed to death by [Darth Vader](/wiki/Anakin_Skywalker "Anakin Skywalker") after betraying him.[[20]](#cite_note-Dark_Lord_24-20) Via their mystical power combined with the dark side, the [Nightsisters](/wiki/Nightsisters "Nightsisters") could speak when dead,[[21]](#cite_note-Dark_Disciple-21) and in addition to manifesting themselves in the character of greenish figures under certain circumstances, they were occasionally able to haunt and possess a person invading their rest in peace.[[22]](#cite_note-Visions_and_Voices-22) [Darth Sidious](/wiki/Darth_Sidious "Darth Sidious"), in turn, transferred his soul into a [clone](/wiki/Cloning "Cloning") body shortly prior to the destruction of his original one. In the search of a new vessel to host his consciousness, he futilely endeavored to persuade his granddaughter Rey to slay him, in order for his spirit to pass on to her alongside all the Sith living in him.[[7]](#cite_note-Episode_IX-7)

Albeit unable to manifest themselves as Force spirits, some Jedi could directly address Force-sensitive individuals as voices in their postmortem existence. Having undergone the training overseen by Leia Organa, [Rey](/wiki/Rey_Skywalker "Rey Skywalker") was able to hear the voices of such Jedi who did not use this technique (like Mace Windu) in [35 ABY](/wiki/35_ABY "35 ABY"), just before she slew the Emperor.[[7]](#cite_note-Episode_IX-7)

## Behind the scenes[]

:   "*In the rough draft... Ben explains that... if Vader becomes one with the dark side of the Force, he will lose all identity. If he turns to the good side, he will pass through the Netherworld and in the revised rough draft, Yoda "will rescue him before he becomes one with the Force".*"
:   ―Description about preserving identity after death in Episode VI's rough draft[[24]](#cite_note-SW:TAS-24)

In the rough draft of the [1983](/wiki/1983 "1983") film [*Star Wars*: Episode VI *Return of the Jedi*](/wiki/Star_Wars:_Episode_VI_Return_of_the_Jedi "Star Wars: Episode VI Return of the Jedi"), it was revealed by a resurrected Obi-Wan Kenobi that if Darth Vader became one with the dark side of the Force, he would not be able to retain his identity after death.[[24]](#cite_note-SW:TAS-24) In [George Lucas](/wiki/George_Lucas "George Lucas")'s draft script for the movie, Kenobi and Yoda would have emerged as Force spirits when Palpatine attacked Luke Skywalker with Force lightning to deflect it away from the young Jedi. Later, Anakin, Kenobi, and Yoda would have returned to physical life instead of simply being Force ghosts.[[25]](#cite_note-Rewind_of_the_Jedi-25)

As part of an online discussion, *[Shadow of the Sith](/wiki/Shadow_of_the_Sith "Shadow of the Sith")* author [Adam Christopher](/wiki/Adam_Christopher "Adam Christopher") opined on the influence Force spirits can have on the galaxy of the living. He agreed with an interpretation that Force spirits were akin to "spirit guides". That these guides are at the mercy of the Cosmic Force, allowed to interact with and share information with the living only when the Force sees fit. Referencing his novel, Christopher noted how Anakin needed to use all of his energy to rescue his son, then could do no more because "something" did not wish to see further contact between them at that time.[[26]](#cite_note-Adam_Force_12-26) Christopher also remarked, in response to a suggestion that the Force prevented the Jedi spirits from intervening to keep Rey alive following her defeat of Palpatine,[[27]](#cite_note-Conversation_Kickoff2-27) that Exegol was a receptacle of dark side power that complicated matters with Force spirits.[[28]](#cite_note-Adam_Force_22-28)
