The **Path of the Open Hand**, remembered as "**the faithful**" by the [Ro family](/wiki/Ro_family "Ro family"), was an extremist missionary order and cult that existed on the [planet](/wiki/Planet "Planet") [Dalna](/wiki/Dalna "Dalna") during the [High Republic Era](/wiki/High_Republic_Era "High Republic Era"). Led by [Elecia Zeveron](/wiki/Elecia_Zeveron "Elecia Zeveron"), also known as "[The Mother](/wiki/Mother_(title) "Mother (title)")," who claimed to be a [prophet](/wiki/Prophet "Prophet"), they believed that no one should use [the Force](/wiki/The_Force "The Force"), especially the [Jedi Order](/wiki/Jedi_Order "Jedi Order"). They believed that using the Force was abusing it and would cause [death](/wiki/Death "Death") and disaster throughout [the galaxy](/wiki/The_galaxy "The galaxy").

In [382 BBY](/wiki/382_BBY "382 BBY"), the Path and the Mother came into possession of a living weapon she named the [Leveler](/wiki/Great_Leveler "Great Leveler") that preyed upon [Force-sensitives](/wiki/Force-sensitive "Force-sensitive"), as well as the [Rod of Seasons](/wiki/Rod_of_Seasons "Rod of Seasons"), which could control it. After the creature hatched, she convinced the Path it was an agent of the Force and was doing the Force's will when it killed those who used it. Afterward, the Path, at the direction of its [Guide](/wiki/Guide_of_the_Closed_Fist "Guide of the Closed Fist") [Marda Ro](/wiki/Marda_Ro "Marda Ro"), began to expand their reach by traveling the galaxy in their flagship the *[Gaze Electric](/wiki/Gaze_Electric "Gaze Electric")*, including to the holy [moon](/wiki/Moon "Moon") [Jedha](/wiki/Jedha "Jedha"). At the same time, the Mother manipulated the [Eiram and E'ronoh War](/wiki/Eiram_and_E%27ronoh_War "Eiram and E'ronoh War") in order to come into possession of an Eirami poison and make the [Jedi](/wiki/Jedi "Jedi") out to be villains so that public opinion would be turned against them.

On Jedha, the Path's other leader, [Werth Plouth](/wiki/Werth_Plouth "Werth Plouth"), known as "[The Herald](/wiki/Herald_(title) "Herald (title)")," petitioned for the [Convocation of the Force](/wiki/Convocation_of_the_Force "Convocation of the Force"), a council of different Force-affiliated groups, to be disbanded and for all of its groups to stop use of the Force. After the Convocation rejected his petition, the Herald incited a crowd outside the [Convocation Chambers](/wiki/Convocation_Chambers "Convocation Chambers") against the council and Force-users while [Yana Ro](/wiki/Yana_Ro "Yana Ro"), Marda's cousin, deployed the Leveler into the crowd, the effects of the creature on Force-sensitives turning the enraged group into an all out riot. The riot spread to the [Second Spire](/wiki/Second_Spire "Second Spire"), where a [treaty](/wiki/Eiram%E2%80%93E%27ronoh_peace_treaty "Eiram–E'ronoh peace treaty") between [Eiram](/wiki/Eiram "Eiram") and [E'ronoh](/wiki/E%27ronoh "E'ronoh") was to be signed. This, along with intervention by the [Brotherhood of the Ninth Door](/wiki/Brotherhood_of_the_Ninth_Door "Brotherhood of the Ninth Door") at the direction of the Path, caused both parties to attack each other and began the [Battle of Jedha](/wiki/Battle_of_Jedha "Battle of Jedha"). This had been the Mother's plan, and she attempted to make the Path appear to be the heroes by helping and healing people during the conflict, but many still blamed them and their [healing house](/wiki/Healing_house "Healing house") there was destroyed. In the aftermath, the Mother claimed the Herald had acted alone in causing the conflict and did not represent the Path.

Several different groups of Jedi traveled to Dalna after the battle to investigate the Path. At the same time, infighting began between the Mother and the Herald, but was ended by Marda Ro, who had become even more convinced that using the Force was evil after joining the [mission](/wiki/Mission_to_Planet_X_(Path_of_the_Open_Hand) "Mission to Planet X (Path of the Open Hand)") to the legendary [Planet X](/wiki/Sophros "Sophros") to obtain more of the [Nameless](/wiki/Shrikarai "Shrikarai"), the Leveler's species. She reorganized the Path there into a subgroup called the [Path of the Closed Fist](/wiki/Path_of_the_Closed_Fist "Path of the Closed Fist"), declaring war against the Jedi. In a devastating battle that became known as the [Night of Sorrow](/wiki/Night_of_Sorrow "Night of Sorrow"), the Path then fought the Jedi that had already come to Dalna and many more that arrived for support, killing many of them with the Nameless. However, the Path was defeated by the Jedi with the help of a united Eiram and E'ronoh. After the battle, Marda discovered that the Mother had been lying about her prophetic visions and had been hypocritically using the Force, so Marda killed her using the Leveler. Taking the Path's [flagship](/wiki/Flagship "Flagship"), the *[Gaze Electric](/wiki/Gaze_Electric "Gaze Electric")*, Marda kept her faith but began a new plan to take down the Jedi, founding the [Nihil](/wiki/Nihil "Nihil"). Another descendant of the Path known as the [Elders of the Path](/wiki/Elders_of_the_Path "Elders of the Path") eventually formed, containing Path members that had escaped or survived the Night of Sorrow.

## Description[]

### Philosophy[]

:   "*Clarity and freedom, and harmony with the Force. That is the Path of the Open Hand. The Force is everywhere, in everything, blazing through the particles of the universe, and it is not our place to touch it. We are not above other creatures or things. We are not above the Force. We are it, if we are lucky."  
    “That is why that Jedi shouldn't have moved the flowers.”  
    “He took from the Force. Stole. Abused. Even that alone is wrong, you understand? We do not take. We only hold out our open hands. When you are open, gifts freely given will come to you, as long as you are alive. As long as the Force flows through you.*"
:   ―Marda Ro and Ferali[[1]](#cite_note-POD-1)

The Path of the Open Hand was an extremist[[9]](#cite_note-Reveal-9) [cult](/wiki/Religion "Religion") whose fundamental belief was that no one with [Force-sensitivity](/wiki/Force-sensitive "Force-sensitive") should [use](/wiki/Force_power "Force power") or manipulate [the Force](/wiki/The_Force "The Force"). They believed doing so abused and hurt the Force,[[1]](#cite_note-POD-1) disrupting the balance of [the galaxy](/wiki/The_galaxy "The galaxy") itself.[[10]](#cite_note-Insider_221-10) Furthermore, the Path believed that the Force would always seek to balance itself: in their opinion, if one used the Force to save lives, at least that many lives would be taken away somewhere else in [the galaxy](/wiki/The_galaxy "The galaxy").[[1]](#cite_note-POD-1) In effect, any use of the Force would create unforeseen consequences.[[10]](#cite_note-Insider_221-10) These beliefs developed into inherent [resentment](/wiki/Anti-Jedi_sentiment "Anti-Jedi sentiment") and antagonism towards[[1]](#cite_note-POD-1) the philosophically opposite[[10]](#cite_note-Insider_221-10) [Jedi Order](/wiki/Jedi_Order "Jedi Order"), the most prominent group in the galaxy that used the Force. They believed that the Jedi were the source of much of the misfortune and peril in the galaxy because they thought they used the Force constantly, selfishly, and with abandon and brought it out of balance.[[1]](#cite_note-POD-1)

With that in mind, if a Force-sensitive did join the Path, they would need to suppress their connection to the Force and refuse to call upon it.[[3]](#cite_note-Cataclysm-3) Another critical part of this belief was that no one should have access to [Force artifacts](/wiki/Force_artifact "Force artifact") and weapons or use their powers. Because of this they had a subgroup called the [Children](/wiki/Children_of_the_Open_Hand "Children of the Open Hand") that "liberated" or stole these items to be held by the Path for safekeeping. The other main belief of the cult was that they should provide for each other as the Force would provide for them in return. The members of the cult often supported each other without asking for anything in return but would also willingly give to non-members as well. They referred to the acts and resources that they gave to each other and outside people as 'gifts freely given.'[[1]](#cite_note-POD-1) They often used this tactic to recruit members, giving those that were suffering gifts and support in the hopes that they would join in the cult's comfort and security.[[11]](#cite_note-Convergence-11)

The [Force icon](/wiki/Force_icon "Force icon") was a symbol comprised of three wavy [blue](/wiki/Color "Color") lines that the Path of the Open Hand used to represent the Force. They marked their faces, usually their foreheads, with [brikal shell](/wiki/Brikal_shell "Brikal shell") blue paint in the shape of the icon, wearing it every day. Path members also usually wore plain gray robes with occasional blue or brown accents. The Children wore green and gold robes. The name of the cult stemmed from its gift given freely beliefs, believing that they should always extend and open hand to others, instead of a closed one that took selfishly. Because of this belief, most of its members lived on a communal [compound](/wiki/Path_compound "Path compound") on the [planet](/wiki/Planet "Planet") [Dalna](/wiki/Dalna "Dalna"), where they operated self-sufficiently.[[1]](#cite_note-POD-1)

### Militant transformation[]

:   "*If we leave the Jedi unchecked, their evil will tear through the galaxy like wildfire. The imbalance is real. I've felt it myself, seen what it can do and where it leads. The Force will die, and everything will die with it. Every planet, every moon, every star in the sky. How can we fight that if we continue to fight each other? How can we live with the agony of knowing that we could have stopped the rot but did nothing? The Jedi have come looking for the Path of the Open Hand, but they are too late. That dream has broken. That dream has failed.* We *failed, but no more. We offered them a gift freely given, but they wouldn't take it. Now we offer another gift, a gift they won't be able to refuse. A gift we will deliver here on Dalna and in the galaxy beyond. On Jedha. On Eiram and E'ronoh. On Coruscant itself. They did not heed our warning, and now they will pay the price—the price not of an open hand but a closed fist.*"
:   ―Marda Ro reforms the Path[[2]](#cite_note-PoV-2)

Marda Ro reformed an already radicalized Path of the Open Hand into the openly militant Path of the Closed Fist.

 

In time, the once peaceful Path[[1]](#cite_note-POD-1) would become a militant faction[[3]](#cite_note-Cataclysm-3) under the leadership of [Elecia Zeveron](/wiki/Elecia_Zeveron "Elecia Zeveron")[[2]](#cite_note-PoV-2)—aka "the Mother," who urged her followers to take up arms against the Jedi in the name of the Force[[1]](#cite_note-POD-1)—and [Guide of the Open Hand](/wiki/Guide_of_the_Closed_Fist "Guide of the Closed Fist") [Marda Ro](/wiki/Marda_Ro "Marda Ro"), who would reform the Path into the [Path of the Closed Fist](/wiki/Path_of_the_Closed_Fist "Path of the Closed Fist") when she believed the dream of "an open hand" had failed.[[2]](#cite_note-PoV-2) Older members of the Path became willing to sacrifice themselves as one last "gift given freely" and a living shield, while even the young trained for battle instead of farming. Indeed, whereas the Mother once promised to [Axel Greylark](/wiki/Axel_Greylark "Axel Greylark") that the Path was not a violent people and only detested those who "abused" the Force, an enraged Mother quickly ordered the execution of his mother, [Kyong Greylark](/wiki/Kyong_Greylark "Kyong Greylark"), when his usefulness ran out. [Jedi Knight](/wiki/Jedi_Knight "Jedi Knight") [Gella Nattai](/wiki/Gella_Nattai "Gella Nattai") suspected that people who tried to leave the Path would be executed on the charge of [treason](/wiki/Treason "Treason"). Members of the Path who acted as warriors became known as Path soldiers and Path guards.[[3]](#cite_note-Cataclysm-3)

Even before the formation of the "Closed Fist" under Ro, the Mother's leadership established a militant Path eager to combat the Jedi and their supposedly tyrannical rule over the Force. One pamphlet distributed by the Path in that time spoke of fighting evil and the need to oppose "unnatural" practices, urging fellow Dalnan residents to join the coming battle. As mentally noted by Child member [Yana Ro](/wiki/Yana_Ro "Yana Ro"), the writing made a change to the core Path tenet: while it still mentioned the freedom of the Force, it also made reference to establishing justice and purity within the Force, which Yana took as a sign that the Path was eager to fight. Indeed, the language of "evil" and "tyranny," clearly written in reference to the Jedi Order, concerned her, as did watching the once moderate Elder [Dinube](/wiki/Dinube "Dinube") spread words of condemnation and judgement towards those who would ignore the "plight" suffered by the Force. His words made no reference to the gifts given freely[[2]](#cite_note-PoV-2) the cult had once prided itself upon.[[1]](#cite_note-POD-1)

The Path's internal language also changed, with names like "brothers" and "sister" being applied to cult membership. The Mother also preached that the whole galaxy was in crisis thanks to the Force-usage of the Jedi and those akin to the Order, further claiming that the Republic and [HoloNet](/wiki/HoloNet "HoloNet") could not be trusted. Instead, she urged her followers to stay vigilant and spread the "truth" themselves. Shortly thereafter, Ro formally transformed the cult into the Path of the Closed Fist, urging its membership to protect and liberate the Force together instead of falling to internal division.[[2]](#cite_note-PoV-2) Nonetheless, the Mother's corruption and Ro's rise to power would culminate in the cult breaking apart amid its new militant focus, with the so-called Closed Fist forming in the name of waging war with the Jedi:[[3]](#cite_note-Cataclysm-3) as Ro put it, they would deliver change by force with a closed fist instead of open offerings.[[2]](#cite_note-PoV-2) Losing much of their influence over the cult, the Elders, meanwhile, remained dedicated to the "Open Hand" and abandoned Dalna as Ro's new faction waged its war.[[3]](#cite_note-Cataclysm-3)

### Organization[]

 [![Major leaders of the Path of the Open Hand, from left to right: Guide Marda Ro, "the Mother" Elecia Zeveron, and Herald Werth Plouth](data:image/gif;base64,R0lGODlhAQABAIABAAAAAP///yH5BAEAAAEALAAAAAABAAEAQAICTAEAOw%3D%3D)](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/starwars/images/d/de/Chronicles-PathLeadership.png/revision/latest?cb=20240820200959) [![Major leaders of the Path of the Open Hand, from left to right: Guide Marda Ro, "the Mother" Elecia Zeveron, and Herald Werth Plouth](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/starwars/images/d/de/Chronicles-PathLeadership.png/revision/latest/scale-to-width-down/350?cb=20240820200959)](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/starwars/images/d/de/Chronicles-PathLeadership.png/revision/latest?cb=20240820200959) 

Major leaders of the Path of the Open Hand, from left to right: Guide Marda Ro, "the Mother" Elecia Zeveron, and Herald Werth Plouth

 

The Path of the Open Hand was led by a [Council of Elders](/wiki/Council_of_Elders_(Path_of_the_Open_Hand) "Council of Elders (Path of the Open Hand)") and a [Herald](/wiki/Herald_(title) "Herald (title)"), who presided over the council. [Elders](/wiki/Elder_(Path_of_the_Open_Hand) "Elder (Path of the Open Hand)") were the wisest and usually oldest members of the Path, who helped guide the other members. Eventually, many years after its founding, the Path would get an additional leader in the form of a [Mother](/wiki/Mother_(title) "Mother (title)"), who led the Path with visions of the Force[[1]](#cite_note-POD-1) and managed to secure control of the cult for herself.[[3]](#cite_note-Cataclysm-3) The Mother also founded and presided over a subgroup of the Path called the Children, who traveled across the galaxy to steal [Force artifacts](/wiki/Force_artifact "Force artifact") and bring them back to the [Path compound](/wiki/Path_compound "Path compound"). [Youngling](/wiki/Youngling "Youngling") members of the Path of the Open Hand were called [Littles](/wiki/Littles "Littles") and were watched over by the Elders and members that they chose.[[1]](#cite_note-POD-1) Marda Ro would be named Guide of the Open Hand[[2]](#cite_note-PoV-2) with the Mother's blessing.[[1]](#cite_note-POD-1)

The Path's corruption into the Closed Fist created a degree of strife within its ranks;[[3]](#cite_note-Cataclysm-3) whereas Ro formed the Closed Fist and would be joined the Herald and the Mother, who both clung onto power by joining her effort,[[2]](#cite_note-PoV-2) the Elders of the Open Hand escaped the [Battle of Dalna](/wiki/Night_of_Sorrow "Night of Sorrow"), abandoning the Closed Fist in the name of the Open Hand: as Elder [Yulon Onning](/wiki/Yulon_Onning "Yulon Onning") put it, the chaos of the battle represented something other than the Open Hand he believed in. Indeed, he found that he had lost all power over his supposed followers, although at least one Path guard, a [Sakiyan](/wiki/Sakiyan "Sakiyan") Path member, and a grouping of [shuttles](/wiki/Shuttle "Shuttle") remained at the Elders' disposal. Still, while he hoped to see the Path win the battle, he no longer saw its members as belonging to the same group as he. During the battle, Path [enforcer droids](/wiki/Security_droid "Security droid") also turned on organic Path members, including an old woman who did not wish to fight but instead find her granddaughter. Path members who took part in the battle did so in the name of the Closed Fist according to chants overheard by Axel Greylark, but Kyong Greylark, even after Axel reported on what he had seen amongst its ranks, thought that only some of the Path had morphed into the new group.[[3]](#cite_note-Cataclysm-3)

## History[]

### Origins[]

:   "*We've been here—the Path has been here—for over a hundred years. We began with a single person, grew into a family, and eventually a community. We're self-sustaining, though sometimes trade with Ferdan, and accept gifts.”

Sometime by 483 BBY,[[7]](#cite_note-Date_founded-7) The Path of the Open Hand was founded by a man named [Sachar Rold](/wiki/Sachar_Rold "Sachar Rold"), who had once been a member of the [Guardians of the Whills](/wiki/Guardians_of_the_Whills "Guardians of the Whills") but left them when [his](/wiki/Gender "Gender") belief conflicted with their ways. He was cast out by them and attempted to find himself within the Force. He was led away from his former priesthood by a vision of bright blue light that led him to the planet Dalna, which was free of war and suffering. There, he founded the Path of the Open Hand. The cult developed a [compound](/wiki/Path_compound "Path compound") on the planet, utilizing its caves. They lived self-sufficiently there, with the members supporting each other and living off the land.[[1]](#cite_note-POD-1)

Many years later, a woman[[1]](#cite_note-POD-1) named [Elecia Zeveron](/wiki/Elecia_Zeveron "Elecia Zeveron")[[12]](#cite_note-The_Mother-12) from the [Core Worlds](/wiki/Core_Worlds "Core Worlds") arrived at the Path as a refugee, like the rest of the Path. She was welcomed into and subscribed to the cult and slowly learned their ways. Eventually, Zeveron supposedly had a vision—just like Sachar Rold—of a blue light. However, she claimed the light she saw was in the form of a messenger made of pure Force. She said that it taught her how to 'share life' and listen and be one with the Force, so much so that one day her small garden had bloomed overnight. The Path began to believe that her harmony with the Force was so pure that it flowed out of her and into anyone around her. Because of this and more visions that she appearently continued to have, she was allowed to help lead the Path, despite not qualifying as an Elder. The Path came to believe she had been sent by the Force to guide them.[[1]](#cite_note-POD-1)

Elecia Zeveron, known as the Mother

 

She took the title of "the [Mother](/wiki/Mother_(title) "Mother (title)")" and officially became one of the Path's leaders, taking the position of leadership from "the [Herald](/wiki/Herald_(title) "Herald (title)")," [Werth Plouth](/wiki/Werth_Plouth "Werth Plouth"),[[1]](#cite_note-POD-1) who agreed to let her take control[[2]](#cite_note-PoV-2) and remained their Herald. In time, however, he would desire to take back control of the Path. She worked alongside the [Council of Elders](/wiki/Council_of_Elders_(Path_of_the_Open_Hand) "Council of Elders (Path of the Open Hand)"). Everyone who was part of the Path listened when she spoke, young [Littles](/wiki/Littles "Littles") and Elders alike.[[1]](#cite_note-POD-1) In secret, however, the Mother was a skilled con-artist who freely used the Force herself, using [mind tricks](/wiki/Mind_trick "Mind trick") to get her way and lying about receiving visions because it could guarantee her power. Zeveron's anger stemmed from how her sister, [Oliviah Zeveron](/wiki/Oliviah_Zeveron "Oliviah Zeveron"), had been taken into the Jedi Order as a child, but her own Force abilities were not strong enough for the Jedi to consider her. Insulted, the Mother clung onto the anger she felt towards the Jedi well into her adult life, believing they had not taken her seriously[[2]](#cite_note-PoV-2) and developing a plan to usurp control over the galaxy via the Path[[3]](#cite_note-Cataclysm-3) to enact her revenge.[[2]](#cite_note-PoV-2)

Under her leadership, the cult would greatly grow. The Path would also maintain ties with numerous other groups, including a group of pirates the Mother would provide information about good raiding spots to in return for loyalty[[3]](#cite_note-Cataclysm-3) and wealthy backers whom the Mother had convinced to enrich the Path. Evidence also existed that the Mother used the Path as cover for a smuggling operation, which, in turn, helped to enrich the cult.[[2]](#cite_note-PoV-2) Eventually, the Mother had a vision of a great ship for the Path to travel across the galaxy and spread their message throughout the galaxy. The Path began its construction, and named it the *[Gaze Electric](/wiki/Gaze_Electric "Gaze Electric")*. The Mother founded a subgroup of the Path called the Children, who she presided over. The Children traveled off of Dalna and stole, though they claimed liberated, [Force artifacts](/wiki/Force_artifact "Force artifact") so that they would not allow people to use the Force. They did so by any means necessary, and often resorted to espionage and violence.[[1]](#cite_note-POD-1)

At some point, Axel Greylark, the son of politician [Kyong Greylark](/wiki/Kyong_Greylark "Kyong Greylark") and of the distinguished Greylark family, was cut out of his inheritance after a series of misdeeds. He resorted to smuggling for money and at one point was sent to the Path to smuggle ship parts for the *Gaze Electric* to them for [Ney Madiine](/wiki/Ney_Madiine "Ney Madiine"). He had stayed because they did not worship the Jedi like the rest of the galaxy, having lost his father in an incident that he blamed the Jedi for. At one point he subscribed to their belief that the Force would be free, but eventually fell out of it. However, he stayed with the Path because of the Mother. She had taught him to remember the pain within him every time he would start to forget his father and become the Greylark his mother wanted him to be, eventually had learning to need and use the pain. She eventually sent him back to [Coruscant](/wiki/Coruscant "Coruscant"), close to the [Galactic Senate](/wiki/Galactic_Senate "Galactic Senate") because she realized he'd be more helpful there, but whenever she needed him, he returned, answering her every beck and call.[[11]](#cite_note-Convergence-11)

### Growing influence[]

:   "*Travelers from a system—Dalna, I believe—called themselves the Path of the Open Hand. They offered a better way of life away from all of this, and…away from the influence the Jedi have with the Force. […] Many of the younger ones in the town left with them, but I think their families would have done anything to have them escape the draft.*"
:   ―Iana Percei[[11]](#cite_note-Convergence-11)

Sometime before or duing [382 BBY](/wiki/382_BBY "382 BBY"),[[13]](#cite_note-Expansion_date-13) the Path of the Open Hand expanded their reach to the planets [Eiram](/wiki/Eiram "Eiram") and [E'ronoh](/wiki/E%27ronoh "E'ronoh"), which were in the [Dalnan sector](/wiki/Dalnan_sector "Dalnan sector") along with Dalna, exploiting their [war](/wiki/Eiram_and_E%27ronoh_War "Eiram and E'ronoh War") to recruit people who wanted to escape it, their militaries' drafts, and the poverty and suffering they were experiencing. Many young people joined in the hopes of a better life, and continued to promote the cult on the planets as the Path expanded.[[11]](#cite_note-Convergence-11) They also welcomed and recruited refugees who came to Dalna to escape the war.[[1]](#cite_note-POD-1)

The war between Eiram and E'ronoh provided a degree of chaos that the Path and Mother would be able to thrive within, with the Mother also able to enrich her coffers via the conflict and direct those proceeds to the Path.[[3]](#cite_note-Cataclysm-3) At some point, the Mother, and thus the Path of the Open Hand, also provided aid to the planet Eiram and its Queen, [Adrialla](/wiki/Adrialla "Adrialla"). She requested nothing in return at first, but then asked for her to manufacture poison from the planet's poisonous [Blue scorpions](/wiki/Blue_scorpion "Blue scorpion"). Queen Adrialla did develop the poison, also with the hopes of Eiram using it in the war, but after a failed mission where it failed, she stopped manufacturing it and refused to give any to the Path.[[11]](#cite_note-Convergence-11)

In 382 BBY,[[14]](#cite_note-Date-14) over the course of weeks[[2]](#cite_note-PoV-2) and months,[[15]](#cite_note-THR_10-15) agents working for the Mother were active across the holy moon of [Jedha](/wiki/Jedha "Jedha"), where the Mother worked [Jedha City](/wiki/Jedha_City "Jedha City") to her own ends without being physically present. Her agents stole holy artifacts for sale on the black market in order to fund the Path.[[2]](#cite_note-PoV-2) The greater Path was unaware of the activities on Jedha.[[1]](#cite_note-POD-1)

### Fanning the flames[]

:   "*We tried to help you. Who filled your barrels with grain when Shuraden cut off your trade? Who sent healers to help take care of the injured after E'ronoh raids? Who filled your ships with fuel when the Republic rerouted the hyperlanes?”  
    “The Path of the Open Hand.”  
    “No. The Mother.*"
:   ―Serrena and Queen Adrialla[[11]](#cite_note-Convergence-11)

In 382 BBY,[[14]](#cite_note-Date-14) the Mother tasked [Serrena](/wiki/Serrena "Serrena"), a [Kage](/wiki/Kage "Kage") woman who was a member of her Children, with inserting a program chip into a starfighter that was to be used by [E'ronoh](/wiki/E%27ronoh "E'ronoh")'s [Princeess](/wiki/Prince "Prince") [Xiri A'lbaran](/wiki/Xiri_A%27lbaran "Xiri A'lbaran"), which would take control of the ship and force it to randomly fire and then explode in the hopes of prolonging the [Eiram and E'ronoh War](/wiki/Eiram_and_E%27ronoh_War "Eiram and E'ronoh War"). However, the starfighter was instead used by [Bly Tevin](/wiki/Bly_Tevin "Bly Tevin"), but still caused a [skirmish](/wiki/Skirmish_in_the_Eiram_system "Skirmish in the Eiram system") with [Eiram](/wiki/Eiram "Eiram") that ended up causing A'lbaran to crash land on Eiram, though she survived. Serrena operated out of an abandoned building of the [Brushlands](/wiki/Brushlands "Brushlands") of E'ronoh with her partner, fellow Kage and Path member [Abda](/wiki/Abda "Abda"). Though she had failed, Serrena later intercepted an [EX droid](/wiki/EX_communication_droid "EX communication droid") containing a message of two [Jedi Masters](/wiki/Jedi_Master "Jedi Master"), so she informed the Mother that the Jedi had arrived in the system. However, due to Serrena's failure, the Mother contacted Abda without her knowing inviting her to work for her directly and become one of her Children.[[11]](#cite_note-Convergence-11)

Later, Serrena confronted Queen Adrialla of Eiram aboard the [Galactic Republic](/wiki/Galactic_Republic "Galactic Republic") [Longbeam cruiser](/wiki/Longbeam-class_corvette "Longbeam-class corvette") *[Paxion](/wiki/Paxion "Paxion")*, where a [peace summit](/wiki/Summit_aboard_the_Paxion "Summit aboard the Paxion") was being held to end the war, confronting her about her breaking her deal with the Mother to manufacture poison for them. Adrialla refused to start up production again, even when Serrena threatened that the same thing that had happened to Tevin and Princess A'lbaran could happen to her son, Prince [Phan-tu Zenn](/wiki/Phan-tu_Zenn "Phan-tu Zenn"). Serrena was shot with a [blaster](/wiki/Blaster "Blaster") by Adrialla's wife, [Odelia](/wiki/Odelia "Odelia"), but escaped in an [escape pod](/wiki/Escape_pod "Escape pod") and returned back to Abda.[[11]](#cite_note-Convergence-11)

Meanwhile, Axel Greylark was sent to the system to represent his mother, Kyong Greylark, who was now one of the Republic's [Supreme Chancellors](/wiki/Supreme_Chancellor "Supreme Chancellor"), at the summit. He stopped at a saloon on [Lorta](/wiki/Lorta "Lorta") on his way there, where he was met by [Binnot Ullo](/wiki/Binnot_Ullo "Binnot Ullo"), a member of the Path. Ullo told him that the Mother missed him and cared about him and also had a job for him. When he was reluctant, Ullo told Greylark that it was more than a job, but a purpose. The Mother wanted Greylark to keep track of the Jedi in the system, but he didn't immediately understand why. When he arrived, he found himself assigned to the protection of Princess A'lbaran and Prince Zenn, who had agreed to marry in order to end the war. Using his droid, [QN-1](/wiki/QN-1 "QN-1"), he reported back to the Mother on all of their doings and travels before the weddings, especially on Jedi Knight [Gella Nattai](/wiki/Gella_Nattai "Gella Nattai") who had been assigned to protect the couple along with him and developed an infatuation with. In one E'roni town, Nattai was confronted by a woman named [Kala](/wiki/Kala "Kala") and told her that fighting for peace was useless, and that the Jedi were changing this for the worse out in the galaxy. After shooing her away, Marshal [Iana Percei](/wiki/Iana_Percei "Iana Percei") explained that she was parroting the teachings of the Path of the Open Hand, explaining to Nattai the cult's beliefs as well as arrival and recruitment efforts on E'ronoh.[[11]](#cite_note-Convergence-11)

Even though the Herald expressed that he thought it was a good idea, and Ro insisted that she could guide the Path on Jedha despite distractions and noise, The Mother told her that they didn't want any Path presence on Jedha, and her decision was final. The Mother's decision was likely because of her secret activity via her thieves and other minor agents on Jedha.[[2]](#cite_note-PoV-2) Later, Yana Ro and the rest of the Children were given a new mission, which would be Yana's last. The mother sent them to the planet [Thelj](/wiki/Thelj "Thelj") to an artifact called [Jedi Tears](/wiki/Jedi_Tears "Jedi Tears") from the [Graf family compound](/wiki/Graf_family_compound "Graf family compound") on the planet, Sunshine Dobbs flying them there in his ship. He agreed to take Ro back to the [Path compound](/wiki/Path_compound "Path compound") on [Dalna](/wiki/Dalna "Dalna") so that she could meet with The Mother about the incident.[[1]](#cite_note-POD-1)

Marda and Zink eventually decided to just be friends due to the Jedi's no attachment rule, and the two said their goodbyes when Marda said that she had not been allowed to leave yet. He lamented how strange it was that over all the days he had never gotten to meet the Mother, but Marda just suggested that the Force had kept them apart. After she left, Master Macri and Zink staked out the ship port to see if the Path and the Mother were smuggling in items to Dalna. But when the two only saw Yana and Dobbs come return to the ship dock from their mission, the two Jedi decided that they were going in to the Path compound and searching the caves that night.[[1]](#cite_note-POD-1)

Marda met Yana before she could reach the Mother or the Elders, curious as to how the mission went. Yana explained that the Mother had betrayed her and the Children and gotten Kor killed so that she could get new Children that didn't ask many questions. She insisted that the Mother was a grifter and a liar was wasn't truly following the Force. Marda helped Yana washed up, but refused to believe Yana and maintained her faith in the Mother and in the Force. However, Yana managed to convince her to leave with her after she confronted the Mother, despite Marda's faith not wavering.[[1]](#cite_note-POD-1)

### Dissolution[]

#### Closing the Hand[]

:   "*The Jedi have come looking for the Path of the Open Hand, but they are too late. That dream has broken. That dream has failed.* We *failed, but no more. We offered them a gift freely given, but they wouldn't take it. Now we offer another gift, a gift they won't be able to refuse. A gift we will deliver here on Dalna and in the galaxy beyond. On Jedha. On Eiram and E'ronoh. On Coruscant itself. They did not heed our warning, and now they will pay the price—the price not of an open hand but a closed fist.*"
:   ―Marda Ro[[2]](#cite_note-PoV-2)

After the Mother secretly engineered the [Battle of Jedha](/wiki/Battle_of_Jedha "Battle of Jedha") to disrupt the peace between Eiram and E'ronoh, the Path used the sudden fighting on Jedha as proof of its beliefs: members of the Path alleged that the Jedi Order's use of the Force had been balanced out by the re-opening of the war. The Path also unleashed the Leveler onto the streets of Jedha. After the battle,[[4]](#cite_note-Battle_of_Jedha-4) the Path returned to Dalna, where the Path prepared for a coming battle instead of returning to a life of farming. As such, crops were left to rot where they stood.[[3]](#cite_note-Cataclysm-3) The Jedi connected the violence on Jedha to the Path,[[4]](#cite_note-Battle_of_Jedha-4) with news of the Path's involvement spreading across the stars, to which the Mother responded by claiming the Herald alone was to blame.[[2]](#cite_note-PoV-2) The incident also served to reopen the war between Eiram and E'ronoh, just as the Mother wished. In failing to end the war, the Jedi and Republic had seemingly only brought about more strife, all while the conflict would generate chaos that the Path could use to its benefit.[[3]](#cite_note-Cataclysm-3) While a large degree of anti-Path sentiment was encountered first hand by Marda Ro due to the violence on Jedha,[[2]](#cite_note-PoV-2) the cult still had allies on Dalna.[[3]](#cite_note-Cataclysm-3)

In the Battle of Dalna, the Path, reorganized into the Path of the Closed Fist, warred with Jedi and anti-Path Dalnans. For the battle, the Path was aided by enforcer droids and Nameless[[3]](#cite_note-Cataclysm-3) collected from Marda Ro and Dobbs's [mission to Planet X](/wiki/Mission_to_Planet_X_(Path_of_the_Open_Hand) "Mission to Planet X (Path of the Open Hand)"). The Nameless, however, preyed upon Jedi and Force-sensitive Path members alike, even if those Path members did not even know they had a connection to it or, per their beliefs, refused to use it. Encountering the husked remains of Path members, Marda initially tried to convince herself they were active Force-sensitives hiding in plain sight who deserved their fate, even if deep down she was horrified by what she had unleashed. Meanwhile, the Mother sent Littles [Boolan](/wiki/Boolan "Boolan") and [Utalir](/wiki/Utalir "Utalir") out with [thermal detonators](/wiki/Thermal_detonator "Thermal detonator") to supposedly drop rocks upon the Jedi, although she actually intended to destroy the Path alike. With that, she would have an apparent disturbance created by the Jedi and become a tragic figure. When Marda learned of the Mother's actions from Yana and Boolan, who escaped into the night repeating the Path mantra, Marda officially turned on the woman she had once idealized.[[2]](#cite_note-PoV-2)

#### Breaking the Fist[]

The enforcer droids eventually began cutting down Path members and Jedi alike, while the Elders abandoned the Closed Fist in the name of the Path of the Open Hand.[[3]](#cite_note-Cataclysm-3) Marda, meanwhile, killed a Nameless that nearly feasted upon Utalir and, with the Herald, attacked the Mother, who killed the Herald but was captured by the Jedi. Marda then wiped out the Nameless with the Mother's explosives, but the Leveler managed to survive.[[2]](#cite_note-PoV-2) The battle ended with the arrival of the united militaries of Eiram and E'ronoh, who put down the Path's remaining forces, and the explosion[[3]](#cite_note-Cataclysm-3) set off by Marda, which inadvertently[[2]](#cite_note-PoV-2) collapsed the entire cave network.[[3]](#cite_note-Cataclysm-3) After the battle, Marda executed the Mother for her lies and use of the Force by ordering the Leveler against her. She managed to escape Dalna with the Leveler and the Rod of Seasons aboard the *Gaze Electric*, from which she began of a new plan to free the Force. Meanwhile, Yana made out with the Rod of Daybreak, which she kept hidden from Marda upon realizing her cousin would not abandon the Path's ways.[[2]](#cite_note-PoV-2)

In the aftermath of the Battle of Dalna, remembered by the Dalnans as the Night of Sorrow,[[3]](#cite_note-Cataclysm-3) intelligence that came to Jedi Master [Silandra Sho](/wiki/Silandra_Sho "Silandra Sho") established that the Path was dissolved. According to what Sho learned, the remnants of the Path that were not taken into custody by the Jedi Order returned to the *Gaze Electric*, which then vanished into hyperspace. [Fel Ix](/wiki/Fel_Ix "Fel Ix"), who had been on a mission for the Mother before being held prisoner by the Jedi Padawan [Rooper Nitani](/wiki/Rooper_Nitani "Rooper Nitani"), [Sky Graf](/wiki/Sky_Graf "Sky Graf"), and [Dass Leffbruk](/wiki/Dass_Leffbruk "Dass Leffbruk"), reunited with his [family](/wiki/Fel_Ix%27s_family "Fel Ix's family") on [Batuu](/wiki/Batuu "Batuu"). The family then intended to travel to [Atalia](/wiki/Atalia "Atalia"), where Fel Ix lived before joining the Path.[[16]](#cite_note-Quest_for_Planet_X-16)

### Descendants[]

:   "*You've seen it out there. Seen* them*, with their golden robes and flashing blades. So* resplendent*. So* glorious*. They are guiding us to destruction. The faithful know it, as we knew it on Jedha. As we knew it on Dalna. But the recreants are in the ascendant and the tide can no longer be turned.*"
:   ―Kufa, on the Jedi and the remaining believers of the Path, to Marchion Ro[[17]](#cite_note-The_Rising_Storm-17)

 [![Tromak, a former Path of the Open Hand member who became a leader of the Elders of the Path](data:image/gif;base64,R0lGODlhAQABAIABAAAAAP///yH5BAEAAAEALAAAAAABAAEAQAICTAEAOw%3D%3D)](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/starwars/images/c/c4/Tromak-2021THRA1.jpg/revision/latest?cb=20210204101321) [![Tromak, a former Path of the Open Hand member who became a leader of the Elders of the Path](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/starwars/images/c/c4/Tromak-2021THRA1.jpg/revision/latest/scale-to-width-down/200?cb=20210204101321)](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/starwars/images/c/c4/Tromak-2021THRA1.jpg/revision/latest?cb=20210204101321)

Eventually the Path of the Open Hand split into many descendant groups, with one being the [Elders of the Path](/wiki/Elders_of_the_Path "Elders of the Path"), a group that shared the Path's belief that using the Force was wrong and resided on [Trymant IV](/wiki/Trymant_IV "Trymant IV")[[18]](#cite_note-Chronicles_of_the_Jedi-18) in [232 BBY](/wiki/232_BBY "232 BBY").[[19]](#cite_note-EotP_date-19) [Tromak](/wiki/Tromak "Tromak"), who was one of the Path of the Open Hand's littles[[1]](#cite_note-POD-1) in 382 BBY,[[14]](#cite_note-Date-14) was a member of the Elders of the Path's [Council of Elders](/wiki/Council_of_Elders "Council of Elders")[[20]](#cite_note-THRA_2021_1-20) around 232 BBY.[[19]](#cite_note-EotP_date-19) According to members of the Elders' order, the [Nihil](/wiki/Nihil "Nihil") marauder group had grown out of their order before being cut out.[[21]](#cite_note-RtCT-21) Indeed, the Nihil were guided by members of the [Ro family](/wiki/Ro_family "Ro family"), descendants of Marda Ro.[[22]](#cite_note-Phase_II_reveals-22) A few former Path of the Open Hand members, such as [Alirya](/wiki/Alirya "Alirya"), also had joined Marda's efforts. Marda made it clear her new organization was not to be a religion, however.[[23]](#cite_note-Closed_Fist-23)

When [Kufa](/wiki/Kufa "Kufa"), a member of the Ro family, reunited with her cousin, the [Eye of the Nihil](/wiki/Eye_of_the_Nihil "Eye of the Nihil") [Marchion Ro](/wiki/Marchion_Ro "Marchion Ro"), on [Rystan](/wiki/Rystan "Rystan")[[17]](#cite_note-The_Rising_Storm-17) in [231 BBY](/wiki/231_BBY "231 BBY"),[[24]](#cite_note-231_BBY_Date-24) she remarked that he was returning to "the Path" by meeting with her. Her ship that she had arrived in shared a name with the cult, being named the *[Open Hand](/wiki/Open_Hand "Open Hand")*. He was there with the hope of retrieving the Great Leveler, which was now frozen in the ice in the [The Shrine](/wiki/The_Shrine "The Shrine") beneath [Golamaran](/wiki/Golamaran_ice_flats "Golamaran ice flats"), having been brought there by their ancestors long ago. When he asked, she said that there weren't many of "the faithful" left, which she said was apparent due to the prominence and strength of the Jedi. She reaffirmed the belief of her and the faithful that the Jedi were guiding them to destruction, telling him that they had known it on Jedha and on Dalna, but also admitted defeat and said the Jedi could no longer be stopped. She asked if he was there to bring them hope and 'reopen the Hand' by delivering the message. He insisted that he was not a prophet, but she said that the best prophets found the Path by being lost.[[17]](#cite_note-The_Rising_Storm-17) The fallen Jedi Azlin Rell believed that the Path of the Open Hand lived on, though was just called the Nihil instead, and referred to the Nihil as the Path. The Jedi Knight [Reath Silas](/wiki/Reath_Silas "Reath Silas") was unsure if it was an affectation or if it was some deep insight that Rell had.[[25]](#cite_note-Tears_of_the_Nameless-25)

Mother [Tunenep](/wiki/Tunenep "Tunenep") was a late follower of the Path, who, at an age that exceeded 150 years old, still practiced its teachings during the [Clone Wars](/wiki/Clone_Wars "Clone Wars")[[26]](#cite_note-FotL-26) that raged from [22 BBY](/wiki/22_BBY "22 BBY") and [19 BBY](/wiki/19_BBY "19 BBY"). The war pitted the Republic against the secessionist [Confederacy of Independent Systems](/wiki/Confederacy_of_Independent_Systems "Confederacy of Independent Systems"), with the Jedi called into action as the frontline leaders of the newly-established [Republic Military](/wiki/Republic_Military "Republic Military").[[27]](#cite_note-Timelines-27) Tunenep believed that the Jedi were at fault for the war and that they should be eradicated for the Force to be free. She also taught her faith to [Jesha Cour](/wiki/Jesha_Cour "Jesha Cour"), who she rescued after the [Battle of Amaltanna](/wiki/Battle_of_Amaltanna "Battle of Amaltanna"). Eventually, Tunenep and Cour made their way to Coruscant,[[26]](#cite_note-FotL-26) just as Marda Ro had intended for her Closed Fist,[[2]](#cite_note-PoV-2) but Tunenep was killed during the [Separatists' sudden siege](/wiki/Battle_of_Coruscant "Battle of Coruscant") of the planet[[26]](#cite_note-FotL-26) in 19 BBY. Shortly thereafter, however, the Jedi Order would be all-but destroyed with the execution of [Order 66](/wiki/Order_66 "Order 66") and beginning of the [Great Jedi Purge](/wiki/Great_Jedi_Purge "Great Jedi Purge").[[27]](#cite_note-Timelines-27) In the aftermath, Cour encountered the surviving Jedi Padawan [Ryza Ayre](/wiki/Ryza_Ayre "Ryza Ayre"), whom she sought to poison.[[26]](#cite_note-FotL-26)

## Behind the scenes[]

The Path of the Open Hand cult was created for [Phase II](/wiki/Phase_II:_Quest_of_the_Jedi "Phase II: Quest of the Jedi") of the *[Star Wars: The High Republic](/wiki/Star_Wars:_The_High_Republic "Star Wars: The High Republic")* multimedia project. The cult made its first appearance in the [2022](/wiki/2022 "2022") young-adult novel *[The High Republic: Path of Deceit](/wiki/The_High_Republic:_Path_of_Deceit "The High Republic: Path of Deceit")*[[1]](#cite_note-POD-1) but was first referenced in the [2021](/wiki/2021 "2021") novel *[The High Republic: The Rising Storm](/wiki/The_High_Republic:_The_Rising_Storm "The High Republic: The Rising Storm")* as "the faithful" and simply "The Path."[[17]](#cite_note-The_Rising_Storm-17) Artist [Grant Griffin](/wiki/Grant_Griffin "Grant Griffin") did several rounds of artwork for the "Force cult" characters.[[28]](#cite_note-Grant_Twitter-28)
