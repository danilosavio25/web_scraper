[Wookieepedia](/wiki/Wookieepedia "Wookieepedia")'s **Naming policy** defines how to correctly title mainspace articles. Generally, article naming shall derive from an official [Lucasfilm](/wiki/Lucasfilm "Lucasfilm") source and be easily recognizable for the majority of [English](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/English_language "wikipedia:English language") speakers, with a reasonable minimum of ambiguity.

Please note that for the remainder of this policy page, the lowercase term "canon" refers generally to official *[Star Wars](/wiki/Star_Wars "Star Wars")* information from either [Canon](/wiki/Canon "Canon") or [*Star Wars* Legends](/wiki/Star_Wars_Legends "Star Wars Legends") source material, while the capitalized term "Canon" refers to the official Lucasfilm canon continuity.

## General rules

### Be precise when possible

When naming an article, abide by the following:

1. Use a canon name whenever possible.
   * If a subject has more than one canon name, use the most formal and accurate version provided in sources (example: [Jabba Desilijic Tiure](/wiki/Jabba_Desilijic_Tiure "Jabba Desilijic Tiure") instead of [Jabba the Hutt](/wiki/Jabba_the_Hutt "Jabba the Hutt"); [Mitth'raw'nuruodo](/wiki/Mitth%27raw%27nuruodo/Legends "Mitth'raw'nuruodo/Legends") instead of **Thrawn**; [Alliance to Restore the Republic](/wiki/Alliance_to_Restore_the_Republic "Alliance to Restore the Republic") instead of [Rebel Alliance](/wiki/Rebel_Alliance "Rebel Alliance")).

   :   :   → See [Forum:CT:Article naming conventions](/wiki/Forum:CT:Article_naming_conventions "Forum:CT:Article naming conventions")

   * If the spelling of a subject's name varies between U.S. and U.K. source editions, default to the American-English version of the name (example: [Eirriss Ryloth Defense Tech](/wiki/Eirriss_Ryloth_Defense_Tech "Eirriss Ryloth Defense Tech"), instead of **Eirriss Ryloth Defence Tech**, and [lightsaber](/wiki/Lightsaber "Lightsaber") instead of **lightsabre**)
     + British-English spelling may be used if a subject is spelled as such in a source's U.S. edition (example: [The Most Honourable Guild of Armourers](/wiki/The_Most_Honourable_Guild_of_Armourers "The Most Honourable Guild of Armourers")).

   :   :   → See [Forum:CT:Naming policy: US vs. UK editions](/wiki/Forum:CT:Naming_policy:_US_vs._UK_editions "Forum:CT:Naming policy: US vs. UK editions")

   * Per [Canon policy](/wiki/Wookieepedia:Canon_policy "Wookieepedia:Canon policy"), if a [Canon](/wiki/Canon "Canon") subject is not identified in a canonical source but has been identified in a [LEGO](/wiki/LEGO "LEGO") source, the name provided by the LEGO source may be utilized for the subject until and unless a canonical source provides a different name for the subject, based on a [statement](/wiki/File:PabloHidalgo-LegoName-Twitter.png "File:PabloHidalgo-LegoName-Twitter.png") by [Lucasfilm Story Group](/wiki/Lucasfilm_Story_Group "Lucasfilm Story Group") member [Pablo Hidalgo](/wiki/Pablo_Hidalgo "Pablo Hidalgo") regarding character names from the *[LEGO Star Wars: The Force Awakens](/wiki/LEGO_Star_Wars:_The_Force_Awakens "LEGO Star Wars: The Force Awakens")* video game. If a canonical source later provides the same name as the LEGO source, the canonical source should take precedence as a reference for that name.

   :   :   → See [Forum:CT:LEGO Policy Amendment](/wiki/Forum:CT:LEGO_Policy_Amendment "Forum:CT:LEGO Policy Amendment"), [Forum:CT:Naming Policy LEGO Tweak](/wiki/Forum:CT:Naming_Policy_LEGO_Tweak "Forum:CT:Naming Policy LEGO Tweak")
2. Whenever alternate names are used in canon, use the name under which the subject was known during the later time period (example: [Kanan Jarrus](/wiki/Kanan_Jarrus "Kanan Jarrus") instead of [Caleb Dume](/wiki/Caleb_Dume "Caleb Dume"); *[Rebel Dream](/wiki/Rebel_Dream "Rebel Dream")* instead of ***Tyrant***) or, for characters, at the moment of the character's death (example: [Anakin Skywalker](/wiki/Anakin_Skywalker "Anakin Skywalker") instead of [Darth Vader](/wiki/Darth_Vader "Darth Vader"); [Darth Caedus](/wiki/Darth_Caedus "Darth Caedus") instead of [Jacen Solo](/wiki/Jacen_Solo "Jacen Solo")).
   * Whenever two or more names are used by a character simultaneously (adopted names included), use the most commonly known of those names (example: Both [*Star Wars* Legends](/wiki/Star_Wars_Legends "Star Wars Legends") [Palpatine](/wiki/Palpatine/Legends "Palpatine/Legends") and [Dooku](/wiki/Dooku/Legends "Dooku/Legends") were commonly known under their given names, which they continued to use alongside their [Sith titles](/wiki/Darth/Legends "Darth/Legends"), so those names are preferred for the respective articles). Exceptions may apply to characters who prominently identify as a certain name despite their public persona. A prime example is [Canon](/wiki/Canon "Canon") [Darth Sidious](/wiki/Darth_Sidious "Darth Sidious"), who was publicly known as Palpatine but self-identified as Darth Sidious at the end of his life.

   :   :   → See [Forum:CT:Proposed amendment to the naming policy](/wiki/Forum:CT:Proposed_amendment_to_the_naming_policy "Forum:CT:Proposed amendment to the naming policy")

   * If a non-character subject is known by two or more names across multiple sources with no clear [in-universe](/wiki/In-universe "In-universe") chronological naming precedence, use the name most commonly presented in sources (example: [Golm](/wiki/Golm "Golm") instead of [Bothawui Prime](/wiki/Bothawui_Prime "Bothawui Prime")). Name proliferation may be trumped by a clear and/or consistent intention to rename a subject, in which case the name presented in the most recently published source takes precedence (example: [TIE/sh VIP shuttle](/wiki/TIE/sh_VIP_shuttle "TIE/sh VIP shuttle")).

   :   :   → See [Forum:CT:Naming policy amendments, Part 2](/wiki/Forum:CT:Naming_policy_amendments,_Part_2 "Forum:CT:Naming policy amendments, Part 2")
3. If no canonical name exists, use the in-universe nickname or [callsign](/wiki/Callsign "Callsign") (example: [Shadow Seven](/wiki/Shadow_Seven "Shadow Seven")) and place {{[Nickname](/wiki/Template:Nickname "Template:Nickname")}} at the top of the article.
   * Whenever the character's formal name is known, do not add the nickname to the article title (example: [Obi-Wan Kenobi](/wiki/Obi-Wan_Kenobi "Obi-Wan Kenobi") instead of **Obi-Wan "Ben" Kenobi**).

   :   :   → See [Forum:CT:Naming conventions: Stage names?](/wiki/Forum:CT:Naming_conventions:_Stage_names%3F "Forum:CT:Naming conventions: Stage names?")
4. If no in-universe designation exists, use a conjectural name with {{[Conjecture](/wiki/Template:Conjecture "Template:Conjecture")}} at the top of the article.
   * When using a conjectural name, use "Unidentified" instead of "Unknown" or "Unnamed" (example: [Unidentified Sullustan Jedi](/wiki/Unidentified_Sullustan_Jedi "Unidentified Sullustan Jedi") instead of **Unknown Sullustan Jedi** or **Unnamed Sullustan Jedi**).

   :   :   → See [Forum:CT:Unnamed, unknown, nameless](/wiki/Forum:CT:Unnamed,_unknown,_nameless "Forum:CT:Unnamed, unknown, nameless")

### Lowercase second and subsequent words

Do not capitalize second and subsequent words unless the title is a proper noun (such as a name) or is otherwise always capitalized (example: [Han Solo](/wiki/Han_Solo "Han Solo"), but [*Star Wars* video games](/wiki/Star_Wars_video_games "Star Wars video games")).

Due to the [technical limitations](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Wikipedia:Naming_conventions_(technical_restrictions) "wikipedia:Wikipedia:Naming conventions (technical restrictions)") inherent to the [MediaWiki](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/MediaWiki "wikipedia:MediaWiki") software, the first letter in an article title must always be a capital letter. Ordinarily, this isn't a problem, but it poses an issue when a proper noun's first letter is lowercase (example: [t'landa Til](/wiki/T%27landa_Til "T'landa Til")). The first letter of an internal wikilink need not be capitalized and will direct the reader to the same page (example: [Lightsaber](/wiki/Lightsaber "Lightsaber") or [lightsaber](/wiki/Lightsaber "Lightsaber") can be used interchangeably as needed). Also, the magic word `{{DISPLAYTITLE:name}}` can be used to display the article's name with the first letter in lowercase. (DISPLAYTITLE has advantages over {{[Title](/wiki/Template:Title "Template:Title")}} in that the former is a native MediaWiki function as opposed to a JavaScript hack, takes effect as soon as the page comes up instead of after it finishes loading, and also works in your browser's title bar and history.)

### Singular nouns

Create in-universe article titles using singular nouns unless a subject is always presented in a plural form (example: [Electrobinoculars](/wiki/Electrobinoculars "Electrobinoculars"), [Stuncuffs](/wiki/Stuncuffs "Stuncuffs")). Note that category names follow different pluralization conventions (example: [Category:Black Sun members](/wiki/Category:Black_Sun_members "Category:Black Sun members")).

:   → See [Forum:CT:Naming policy amendments, Part 2](/wiki/Forum:CT:Naming_policy_amendments,_Part_2 "Forum:CT:Naming policy amendments, Part 2")

### Avoid the definite and the indefinite article at the start of the page name

Except in titles of works (example: *[The Approaching Storm](/wiki/The_Approaching_Storm "The Approaching Storm")*, *[The Truce at Bakura](/wiki/The_Truce_at_Bakura "The Truce at Bakura")*) or in official names (example: [The Force](/wiki/The_Force "The Force"), [The Alcazar](/wiki/The_Alcazar "The Alcazar")), avoid the definite ("the") and indefinite ("a"/"an") articles at the beginning of an article title. This applies even if the article subject is usually preceded by the definite article "the" in speech or writing (example: [Death Star](/wiki/Death_Star/Legends "Death Star/Legends") instead of **the Death Star**). However, the following exception may apply:

* If two article subjects, one of which covers a general term and another a specific instance, are competing for the same article title and are usually preceded by the definite article, then "the" *is* to be used at the beginning of the specific instance's article title in order to avoid a parenthetical descriptor (example: [The Grand Inquisitor](/wiki/The_Grand_Inquisitor "The Grand Inquisitor") and [Grand Inquisitor](/wiki/Grand_Inquisitor "Grand Inquisitor"), [The Iteration](/wiki/The_Iteration "The Iteration") and [Iteration](/wiki/Iteration "Iteration"), [The galaxy](/wiki/The_galaxy "The galaxy") and [Galaxy](/wiki/Galaxy "Galaxy")).
  + The preceding exception does not apply for starships and vehicles. Starships and vehicles may only include the definite article at the beginning of the article title if "the" is part of the subject's formal name (example: [*The Last Resort*](/wiki/The_Last_Resort_(starship) "The Last Resort (starship)")).

:   → See [Forum:CT:Naming policy: minor change to definite/indefinite article usage](/wiki/Forum:CT:Naming_policy:_minor_change_to_definite/indefinite_article_usage "Forum:CT:Naming policy: minor change to definite/indefinite article usage"), [Forum:CT:Naming policy amendment, definite/indefinite articles](/wiki/Forum:CT:Naming_policy_amendment,_definite/indefinite_articles "Forum:CT:Naming policy amendment, definite/indefinite articles")

### Prefer spelled-out phrases to abbreviations

Use the full name of the subject instead of its abbreviation (example: [Commission for the Preservation of the New Order](/wiki/Commission_for_the_Preservation_of_the_New_Order "Commission for the Preservation of the New Order") instead of [COMPNOR](/wiki/COMPNOR "COMPNOR")).

### Redirect adjectives to nouns

Adjectives (such as [Imperial](/wiki/Galactic_Empire/Legends "Galactic Empire/Legends")) should redirect to nouns (in this case, [Galactic Empire](/wiki/Galactic_Empire "Galactic Empire")).

### Use English words

Name articles in [English](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/English_language "wikipedia:English language"), and place the native in-universe transliteration at the beginning of the article unless the native form is more commonly used in canon than the English form (example: *[Resol'nare](/wiki/Resol%27nare "Resol'nare")* instead of [Six Actions](/wiki/Six_Actions "Six Actions")).

### Be careful with special characters

Due to the [technical restrictions](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Wikipedia:Naming_conventions_(technical_restrictions) "wikipedia:Wikipedia:Naming conventions (technical restrictions)") inherent to the [MediaWiki](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/MediaWiki "wikipedia:MediaWiki") software, some special characters and text strings cannot be used as part of article titles. For example, neither the piping character (|), the number sign (#), curly brackets ({}), nor square brackets ([]) can be used anywhere in an article title, and the string "Project:" (note the colon) cannot be used at the beginning of a title. See [here](https://mediawiki.org/wiki/Manual:Page_title "mw:Manual:Page title") for a comprehensive list of special characters and strings not permitted in article titles.

Whenever these restrictions force an article to exist at a title different from the subject's actual name (for example, [Project: Shell-cracker](/wiki/Project_Shell-cracker "Project Shell-cracker"), [#2 Warpod](/wiki/2_Warpod "2 Warpod"), [k'Jtari](/wiki/K%27Jtari "K'Jtari")), use the {{[Correct\_title](/wiki/Template:Correct_title "Template:Correct title")}} template on the article in question.

:   → See [Wookieepedia:Mofferences/July 25, 2020](/wiki/Wookieepedia:Mofferences/July_25,_2020 "Wookieepedia:Mofferences/July 25, 2020")

### Disambiguation

All disambiguation pages should include (disambiguation) in their title, and the target title without the parenthetical descriptor should be occupied by either a redirect to the disambiguation page, or an article or redirect that takes priority according to the following rules:

#### In-universe vs. real-world articles

If an in-universe article and a real-world (or out-of-universe) article compete for the same title, the in-universe subject shall take precedence. The real-world article will then disambiguate according to its media or subject type (examples: [Phasma](/wiki/Phasma "Phasma") and [Phasma (novel)](/wiki/Phasma_(novel) "Phasma (novel)"); [Outbound Flight](/wiki/Outbound_Flight "Outbound Flight") and [Outbound Flight (novel)](/wiki/Outbound_Flight_(novel) "Outbound Flight (novel)"); [Blue Shadow Virus](/wiki/Blue_Shadow_Virus "Blue Shadow Virus") and [Blue Shadow Virus (episode)](/wiki/Blue_Shadow_Virus_(episode) "Blue Shadow Virus (episode)"); [David West Reynolds](/wiki/David_West_Reynolds "David West Reynolds") and [David West Reynolds (real person)](/wiki/David_West_Reynolds_(real_person) "David West Reynolds (real person)"); [Shawn Valdez](/wiki/Shawn_Valdez "Shawn Valdez") and [Shawn Valdez (real person)](/wiki/Shawn_Valdez_(real_person) "Shawn Valdez (real person)"))

* Redirects to major in-universe articles will point to their corresponding in-universe subjects rather than serve as the real-world article title (examples: [Ahsoka](/wiki/Ahsoka "Ahsoka") redirects to [Ahsoka Tano](/wiki/Ahsoka_Tano "Ahsoka Tano"), not [Ahsoka (novel)](/wiki/Ahsoka_(novel) "Ahsoka (novel)"); [Kenobi](/wiki/Kenobi "Kenobi") redirects to [Obi-Wan Kenobi](/wiki/Obi-Wan_Kenobi "Obi-Wan Kenobi"), not [Kenobi (novel)](/wiki/Kenobi_(novel) "Kenobi (novel)"); [Thrawn](/wiki/Thrawn "Thrawn") redirects to [Mitth'raw'nuruodo](/wiki/Mitth%27raw%27nuruodo "Mitth'raw'nuruodo"), not [Thrawn (novel)](/wiki/Thrawn_(novel) "Thrawn (novel)"))

:   → See [Forum:CT:Naming policy: IU vs. OOU](/wiki/Forum:CT:Naming_policy:_IU_vs._OOU "Forum:CT:Naming policy: IU vs. OOU")

* As an exception, articles on real-world days, months, years and decades shall take precedence over any in-universe articles competing for the same title (examples: [August 8](/wiki/August_8 "August 8") shall direct to the real-world day, not [August 8 (in-universe)](/wiki/August_8_(in-universe) "August 8 (in-universe)"); [May](/wiki/May "May") should not be a redirect to [Mari Amithest](/wiki/Mari_Amithest "Mari Amithest"), identified as "Jedi Child May" in a movie's script).

:   → See [Forum:CT:Naming policy: real-world dates amendment](/wiki/Forum:CT:Naming_policy:_real-world_dates_amendment "Forum:CT:Naming policy: real-world dates amendment")

#### In-universe vs. in-universe articles

In-universe articles shall disambiguate from one another using a parenthetical descriptor, which must use in-universe terminology (example: [Suba (lieutenant)](/wiki/Suba_(lieutenant) "Suba (lieutenant)"), not **Suba (*The Empire Strikes Back*)**).

* One exception is subjects from the *Star Wars* Legends comic-book series *[The Star Wars](/wiki/The_Star_Wars "The Star Wars")*, a unique story line adapted from [George Lucas](/wiki/George_Lucas "George Lucas")'s [original draft](/wiki/The_Star_Wars_rough_draft "The Star Wars rough draft") for *[Star Wars](/wiki/Star_Wars "Star Wars")*, which is considered non-canon within the Legends continuity. These subjects shall disambiguate via the parenthetical descriptor "*The Star Wars*" (example: [Leia (*The Star Wars*)](/wiki/Leia_(The_Star_Wars) "Leia (The Star Wars)")).

If an article's subject is only referred to in-universe with an "Unidentified" descriptor (example: [Unidentified moon](/wiki/Unidentified_moon "Unidentified moon")) and there are also conjecturally named articles competing for that same page title (example: [Unidentified moon (disambiguation)](/wiki/Unidentified_moon_(disambiguation) "Unidentified moon (disambiguation)")), the former shall take precedence. If there is more than one subject referred to with "Unidentified" in-universe and also conjecturally named articles competing for that same page title, the central title should be a redirect to the corresponding disambiguation page.

#### Disambiguation by continuity

The following section applies exclusively to intra-continuity disambiguation cases. Do not apply these rules to cross-continuity cases.

Articles with identical competing titles should typically be disambiguated via a parenthetical descriptor, and the central title should then be a redirect to the disambiguation page, which should include (disambiguation) in its title. (Example: [Dono](/wiki/Dono "Dono") redirects to [Dono (disambiguation)](/wiki/Dono_(disambiguation) "Dono (disambiguation)"), which serves as a disambiguation page for [Dono (Onderonian)](/wiki/Dono_(Onderonian) "Dono (Onderonian)") and [Dono (Squib)](/wiki/Dono_(Squib) "Dono (Squib)")). The following exceptions apply:

1. Major subjects shall be granted precedence over lesser-known subjects (example: [Battle of Hoth](/wiki/Battle_of_Hoth/Legends "Battle of Hoth/Legends"), *[Executor](/wiki/Executor/Legends "Executor/Legends")*), and disambiguation pages may be created from there as necessary (example: [Battle of Hoth (disambiguation)](/wiki/Battle_of_Hoth_(disambiguation) "Battle of Hoth (disambiguation)"), [Executor (disambiguation)](/wiki/Executor_(disambiguation) "Executor (disambiguation)")).
2. Article subjects with officially confirmed titles shall be granted precedence over conjectural titles (example: [Battle of Corellia](/wiki/Battle_of_Corellia "Battle of Corellia") and [Battle of Corellia (Second Galactic Civil War)](/wiki/Battle_of_Corellia_(Second_Galactic_Civil_War) "Battle of Corellia (Second Galactic Civil War)")).
3. Genuinely named characters shall be granted precedence over impostors or other aliases (example: [Odan](/wiki/Odan "Odan") and [Odan (impostor)](/wiki/Odan_(impostor) "Odan (impostor)")).
4. Precedence shall be granted to articles on planets, species, characters, and starships in that order, pursuant to this section's clause regarding major subjects taking precedence overall. Examples:
   * **Planet**: [Voss](/wiki/Voss "Voss"), [Voss (species)](/wiki/Voss_(species) "Voss (species)"), and [Voss (Imperial officer)](/wiki/Voss_(Imperial_officer) "Voss (Imperial officer)")/[Voss (stormtrooper)](/wiki/Voss_(stormtrooper) "Voss (stormtrooper)"); [Daltarri](/wiki/Daltarri "Daltarri") and [Daltarri (species)](/wiki/Daltarri_(species) "Daltarri (species)")
   * **Species**: [Zeer](/wiki/Zeer "Zeer") and [Zeer (clone trooper)](/wiki/Zeer_(clone_trooper) "Zeer (clone trooper)")/[Zeer (criminal)](/wiki/Zeer_(criminal) "Zeer (criminal)")
   * **Character**: [Anakin Solo](/wiki/Anakin_Solo "Anakin Solo") and [*Anakin Solo* (starship)](/wiki/Anakin_Solo_(starship) "Anakin Solo (starship)")
   * Note the following exceptions:
     + If a species is substantially more significant than its planet namesake, precedence shall be granted to the species and not the planet (example: [Falleen](/wiki/Falleen/Legends "Falleen/Legends") and [Falleen (planet)](/wiki/Falleen_(planet)/Legends "Falleen (planet)/Legends"); [Gand](/wiki/Gand/Legends "Gand/Legends") and [Gand (planet)](/wiki/Gand_(planet)/Legends "Gand (planet)/Legends"))
     + If a given subject is explicitly identified as having inspired the name of another subject, the original namesake shall be granted precedence (example: [Pernicar](/wiki/Pernicar "Pernicar") and [Pernicar (planet)](/wiki/Pernicar_(planet) "Pernicar (planet)")), although this exception may be waived if the inspired subject is a major topic (example: [Endor](/wiki/Endor "Endor") and [Endor (planet)](/wiki/Endor_(planet) "Endor (planet)")).
5. Precedence shall be granted to subjects that can be considered original namesakes for other subjects with competing titles (example: [Dagger](/wiki/Dagger/Legends "Dagger/Legends") and [all other subjects named Dagger](/wiki/Dagger_(disambiguation) "Dagger (disambiguation)")), although this exception may be waived if the namesake's title competes with a major subject (example: *[Tydirium](/wiki/Tydirium/Legends "Tydirium/Legends")* and [Tydirium (ore)](/wiki/Tydirium_(ore) "Tydirium (ore)")).
6. Officially licensed *Star Wars* Legends subjects shall be granted precedence over subjects that were non-canon within the Expanded Universe (example: [Tantive V](/wiki/Tantive_V "Tantive V") and [*Tantive V* (CR90 corvette)](/wiki/Tantive_V_(CR90_corvette) "Tantive V (CR90 corvette)")), as well as subjects from unlicensed sources (example: [Zender](/wiki/Zender "Zender") and [Zender (planet)](/wiki/Zender_(planet) "Zender (planet)")).

Article titles shall avoid the parenthetical descriptor unless there is another article subject with an identical competing title.

#### Disambiguation between continuities

The following section determines how to disambiguate articles with identical competing titles between different continuities, primarily Canon and Legends.

If multiple articles of differing subject matter from both Canon and Legends compete for the same title, precedence shall be granted to the Canon subject, while any Legends articles will disambiguate via parentheses without the need for "/Legends" in the title. A disambiguation page may be created from there as necessary (examples: [Rill](/wiki/Rill "Rill")/[Rill (species)](/wiki/Rill_(species) "Rill (species)") and [Rill (bounty hunter)](/wiki/Rill_(bounty_hunter) "Rill (bounty hunter)"); *[Ravager](/wiki/Ravager "Ravager")* and all other subjects found in [Ravager (disambiguation)](/wiki/Ravager_(disambiguation) "Ravager (disambiguation)").

## Specific rules

### Animals and other organisms

Refer to all creatures of *[Star Wars](/wiki/Star_Wars "Star Wars")* as **creatures** and not animals (example: [Bloodfin (creature)](/wiki/Bloodfin_(creature) "Bloodfin (creature)") instead of **Bloodfin (animal)**).

:   → See [Forum:CT:Creatures vs. animals?](/wiki/Forum:CT:Creatures_vs._animals%3F "Forum:CT:Creatures vs. animals?")

### Canon and Legends

In the instance a Canon article requires the use of an article title currently occupied by a redirect to a Legends article or a Legends disambiguation page, the Canon article should be created at that title, overwriting the redirect or disambiguation page. The Canon article and the Legends version of that article should then use the "legends" and "canon" parameters in the {{Top}} template to correctly link the two article tabs (example: The Canon article shall be [Kitster Banai](/wiki/Kitster_Banai "Kitster Banai"), while the Legends article shall be [Kitster Chanchani Banai](/wiki/Kitster_Chanchani_Banai "Kitster Chanchani Banai")).

Redirects to Canon pages also take priority over Legends subjects with that name (example: [lightsaber crystal](/wiki/Lightsaber_crystal "Lightsaber crystal") redirects to [kyber crystal](/wiki/Kyber_crystal "Kyber crystal") and the Legends article is at [Lightsaber crystal/Legends](/wiki/Lightsaber_crystal/Legends "Lightsaber crystal/Legends")) when the name has been used as an alternate name in a current Canon source.

Redirects that apply to both Canon and Legends versions of an article subject will point to the Canon version by default (example: [Tarkin](/wiki/Tarkin "Tarkin") redirects to [Wilhuff Tarkin](/wiki/Wilhuff_Tarkin "Wilhuff Tarkin"), not [Wilhuff Tarkin/Legends](/wiki/Wilhuff_Tarkin/Legends "Wilhuff Tarkin/Legends")).

:   → See [Forum:CT:Naming policy and titles](/wiki/Forum:CT:Naming_policy_and_titles "Forum:CT:Naming policy and titles"), [Forum:CT:Canon/Legends Default Switch](/wiki/Forum:CT:Canon/Legends_Default_Switch "Forum:CT:Canon/Legends Default Switch"), [Wookieepedia:Mofferences/September 10, 2016](/wiki/Wookieepedia:Mofferences/September_10,_2016 "Wookieepedia:Mofferences/September 10, 2016"), [Forum:CT:Naming policy: The Palpatine Amendment](/wiki/Forum:CT:Naming_policy:_The_Palpatine_Amendment "Forum:CT:Naming policy: The Palpatine Amendment")

### Characters

#### Clone troopers

Use [clone troopers](/wiki/Clone_trooper "Clone trooper")' chosen names/nicknames instead of their numbers in article titles (example: [Fives](/wiki/Fives "Fives") instead of [CT-5555](/wiki/CT-5555 "CT-5555")) in all cases unless it is noted that the clone in question prefers their number, in which case their page name shall be their number. Numbers should also be used if a trooper's name is not known.

:   → See [Forum:CT:Clone trooper names vs. numbers](/wiki/Forum:CT:Clone_trooper_names_vs._numbers "Forum:CT:Clone trooper names vs. numbers"), [Forum:CT:Naming policy update for clone troopers](/wiki/Forum:CT:Naming_policy_update_for_clone_troopers "Forum:CT:Naming policy update for clone troopers"), [Forum:CT:Clone trooper naming update](/wiki/Forum:CT:Clone_trooper_naming_update "Forum:CT:Clone trooper naming update")

#### Hutts

Use a [Hutt](/wiki/Hutt "Hutt")'s full clan name when provided (example: [Jabba Desilijic Tiure](/wiki/Jabba_Desilijic_Tiure "Jabba Desilijic Tiure")). If a Hutt character does not have a clan name, omit the appellation "the Hutt" from the article title (example: [Dorosii](/wiki/Dorosii "Dorosii") instead of [Dorosii the Hutt](/wiki/Dorosii_the_Hutt "Dorosii the Hutt")).

:   → See [Wookieepedia:Mofferences/March 6, 2016](/wiki/Wookieepedia:Mofferences/March_6,_2016 "Wookieepedia:Mofferences/March 6, 2016")

#### Twi'leks

For [Twi'leks](/wiki/Twi%27lek/Legends "Twi'lek/Legends"), use [Galactic Basic Standard](/wiki/Galactic_Basic_Standard "Galactic Basic Standard") names instead of [Twi'leki](/wiki/Twi%27leki/Legends "Twi'leki/Legends") names (example: [Aayla Secura](/wiki/Aayla_Secura/Legends "Aayla Secura/Legends") instead of [Aaylas'ecura](/wiki/Aaylas%27ecura "Aaylas'ecura")).

:   → See [Forum:CT:Twi'lek names](/wiki/Forum:CT:Twi%27lek_names "Forum:CT:Twi'lek names")

#### Unidentified characters

Conjecturally titled character articles shall use "Unidentified" at the beginning of the title unless a title uses a possessive or relationship (example: [Judder Page's father](/wiki/Judder_Page%27s_father "Judder Page's father"), [Meetra Surik's Jedi Master](/wiki/Meetra_Surik%27s_Jedi_Master "Meetra Surik's Jedi Master")).

:   → See [Forum:CT:Naming policy amendments](/wiki/Forum:CT:Naming_policy_amendments "Forum:CT:Naming policy amendments")

### Languages

If a language has been given an official name, then that name should be used as the article's title, with the parenthetical "(language)" if there is a naming conflict (example: [Cerean (language)](/wiki/Cerean_(language) "Cerean (language)") instead of "Cerean language"). If a language has not been given an official name, "[Species] language" should be used as a conjectural title for the article.

:   → See [Forum:CT:Language naming](/wiki/Forum:CT:Language_naming "Forum:CT:Language naming")

### Lists

The list of Xs should be titled as **List of Xs**, rather than **Xs, famous Xs, listing of important Xs, list of noted Xs, list of all Xs,** etc. Before ever creating a list, consider making a category instead: Categories are easier to maintain, and lists are generally discouraged by the [Wookieepedia](/wiki/Wookieepedia "Wookieepedia") community.

### Real-world companies

Articles for real-world companies shall be titled without the inclusion of their [legal status types](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/List_of_legal_entity_types_by_country "wikipedia:List of legal entity types by country"), such as Ltd., Inc., LLC, etc. (examples: [Lucasfilm](/wiki/Lucasfilm "Lucasfilm") not [Lucasfilm Ltd.](/wiki/Lucasfilm_Ltd. "Lucasfilm Ltd."); [Hasbro](/wiki/Hasbro "Hasbro") not [Hasbro Inc.](/wiki/Hasbro_Inc. "Hasbro Inc.")).

:   → See [Forum:CT:Real-world company articles](/wiki/Forum:CT:Real-world_company_articles "Forum:CT:Real-world company articles")

### Real-world people

Articles for real-world people, such as actors and authors, shall be titled according to their actual credited name in a *Star Wars* work, whether that be an abbreviation/stage name (example: [Billy Dee Williams](/wiki/Billy_Dee_Williams "Billy Dee Williams"), not [William December Williams](/wiki/William_December_Williams "William December Williams")) or pseudonym (example: [Jude Watson](/wiki/Jude_Watson "Jude Watson"), not [Judy Blundell](/wiki/Judy_Blundell "Judy Blundell")). Note the following situations and exceptions:

* If a real-world person is credited as two different names between multiple *Star Wars* works, use the most recent credited name and turn the alternate name into a redirect (examples: [Steve Blum](/wiki/Steve_Blum "Steve Blum"), not [Steven Jay Blum](/wiki/Steven_Jay_Blum "Steven Jay Blum"); [Stephen J. Sansweet](/wiki/Stephen_J._Sansweet "Stephen J. Sansweet"), not [Steve Sansweet](/wiki/Steve_Sansweet "Steve Sansweet"); [Stacy Cheregotis](/wiki/Stacy_Cheregotis "Stacy Cheregotis"), not [Stacy Mollema](/wiki/Stacy_Mollema "Stacy Mollema")).
  + If two alternate names are both frequently used between recent sources with no clear preference, use the more formal version (example: [Jonathan W. Rinzler](/wiki/Jonathan_W._Rinzler "Jonathan W. Rinzler"), not [J. W. Rinzler](/wiki/J._W._Rinzler "J. W. Rinzler"))
* Initials in people's names should be formatted with full stops (periods) after them, with a space between the initials (examples: [R. A. Salvatore](/wiki/R._A._Salvatore "R. A. Salvatore"), [A. C. Crispin](/wiki/A._C._Crispin "A. C. Crispin")). The space between the initials may be omitted if the name is routinely formatted without it (example: [J.J. Abrams](/wiki/J.J._Abrams "J.J. Abrams")).
* Article titles may be corrected for obvious misspellings in *Star Wars* credits (examples: [Keira Knightley](/wiki/Keira_Knightley "Keira Knightley"), not [Kiera Knightley](/wiki/Kiera_Knightley "Kiera Knightley"); [Drewe Henley](/wiki/Drewe_Henley "Drewe Henley"), not [Drewe Hemley](/wiki/Drewe_Hemley "Drewe Hemley"))
* Articles on transgender persons shall be titled according to their chosen name. [Deadnaming](https://www.merriam-webster.com/dictionary/deadname) in article titles is not allowed. Credited names shall be turned into redirects.

### Real-world publications

#### General rules

For real-world publications, use the title of the work as the article's name.

##### Languages

* If a source's title is in British English, it must not be converted into American English. *[The Travelling Jindas](/wiki/The_Travelling_Jindas "The Travelling Jindas")* must always be referred to as such.
* If the U.S. and U.K. editions of a title differ, default to the U.S. edition's title (example: [*Star Wars: Rebellion*](/wiki/Star_Wars:_Rebellion_(video_game) "Star Wars: Rebellion (video game)"), instead of *[Star Wars: Supremacy](/wiki/Star_Wars:_Supremacy "Star Wars: Supremacy")*).
  + In this situation, use the U.K. edition's title if the U.K. edition's publication date precedes that of the U.S. edition by a considerable amount of time, such as several weeks or months (examples: *[Star Wars: Galactic Atlas](/wiki/Star_Wars:_Galactic_Atlas "Star Wars: Galactic Atlas")*, instead of *[Star Wars: Galactic Maps: An Illustrated Atlas of the Star Wars Universe](/wiki/Star_Wars:_Galactic_Maps:_An_Illustrated_Atlas_of_the_Star_Wars_Universe "Star Wars: Galactic Maps: An Illustrated Atlas of the Star Wars Universe")*; *[Adventures in Wild Space: The Steal](/wiki/Adventures_in_Wild_Space:_The_Steal "Adventures in Wild Space: The Steal")*, instead of *[Adventures in Wild Space: The Heist](/wiki/Adventures_in_Wild_Space:_The_Heist "Adventures in Wild Space: The Heist")*).
* If a spelling difference exists between the U.S. and U.K. editions of a title, default to the U.S. edition's title (example: *[Art of Coloring: Rogue One: A Star Wars Story](/wiki/Art_of_Coloring:_Rogue_One:_A_Star_Wars_Story "Art of Coloring: Rogue One: A Star Wars Story")*, instead of *[Art of Colouring: Rogue One: A Star Wars Story](/wiki/Art_of_Colouring:_Rogue_One:_A_Star_Wars_Story "Art of Colouring: Rogue One: A Star Wars Story")*).

:   → See [Forum:CT:Naming policy: US vs. UK editions](/wiki/Forum:CT:Naming_policy:_US_vs._UK_editions "Forum:CT:Naming policy: US vs. UK editions")

* For works published exclusively in a language other than English, unless an official source or the author themselves provided an English version of that title, use the title in the original language. Examples:
  + *[Star Wars Resistance: Le Secret de Yeager](/wiki/Star_Wars_Resistance:_Le_Secret_de_Yeager "Star Wars Resistance: Le Secret de Yeager")* in French, instead of *[Star Wars Resistance: Yeager's Secret](/wiki/Star_Wars_Resistance:_Yeager%27s_Secret "Star Wars Resistance: Yeager's Secret")*.
  + *[Star Wars - Das offizielle Magazin](/wiki/Star_Wars_-_Das_offizielle_Magazin "Star Wars - Das offizielle Magazin")* in German, instead of *[Star Wars - The Official Magazine](/wiki/Star_Wars_-_The_Official_Magazine "Star Wars - The Official Magazine")*.
  + *[The Vow of Silver Dawn](/wiki/The_Vow_of_Silver_Dawn "The Vow of Silver Dawn")* (per [Matt Martin](/wiki/File:MattMartin-TheVowofSilverDawn.png "File:MattMartin-TheVowofSilverDawn.png")), instead of *[白银誓约](/wiki/%E7%99%BD%E9%93%B6%E8%AA%93%E7%BA%A6 "白银誓约")* in simplified Chinese.
  + *[Star Wars: The Dark Side](/wiki/Star_Wars:_The_Dark_Side "Star Wars: The Dark Side")* (per [Tricia Barr](/wiki/File:TriciaBarr-DarkSide-February-13-2019.png "File:TriciaBarr-DarkSide-February-13-2019.png")), instead of *[Star Wars: El Lado Oscuro](/wiki/Star_Wars:_El_Lado_Oscuro "Star Wars: El Lado Oscuro")* in Spanish, or *[Star Wars : Le côté obscur](/wiki/Star_Wars_:_Le_c%C3%B4t%C3%A9_obscur "Star Wars : Le côté obscur")* in French.
* However, if a work's original title is in a non-Latin-based script such as [kana](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kana "wikipedia:Kana") or [Chinese characters](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Chinese_characters "wikipedia:Chinese characters"), the [romanized](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Romanization "wikipedia:Romanization") title should be used instead if romanization is possible. (example: Sutā Wōzu No Norimono 100, instead of スター・ウォーズののりもの 100)
* For works that are published exclusively in a language other than English, but are adaptations of an existing work with known titles in both English and that other language, use the English title with a parenthetical disambiguation including the language and media type. (example: [*The Rise of Skywalker* (French junior novelization)](/wiki/The_Rise_of_Skywalker_(French_junior_novelization) "The Rise of Skywalker (French junior novelization)") instead of *[L'ascension de Skywalker](/wiki/L%27ascension_de_Skywalker "L'ascension de Skywalker")* or [*L'ascension de Skywalker* (junior novelization)](/wiki/L%27ascension_de_Skywalker_(junior_novelization) "L'ascension de Skywalker (junior novelization)")).
* A redirect should always be created at the other relevant title (even if the English title has not been provided by an official source).

##### Disambiguation

To disambiguate, add the type of literary work in parentheses, such as "(novel)," "(comic)," or "(short story)." Articles may use "(book)" to disambiguate a non-fiction book. For novelizations, such as [film novelizations](/wiki/Category:Film_novelizations "Category:Film novelizations") or [junior film novelizations](/wiki/Category:Junior_film_novelizations "Category:Junior film novelizations"), use either "(novelization)" or "(junior novelization)" as appropriate.

:   → See [Forum:CT:Naming policy amendments: Books](/wiki/Forum:CT:Naming_policy_amendments:_Books "Forum:CT:Naming policy amendments: Books")

Whenever a book has different editions, list them after a comma when the edition is a part of the formal title, and without italics if it is not formally part of the book's title (example: *[Star Wars: The Roleplaying Game, Second Edition](/wiki/Star_Wars:_The_Roleplaying_Game,_Second_Edition "Star Wars: The Roleplaying Game, Second Edition")*, but [*Galaxy Guide 1: A New Hope*, Second Edition](/wiki/Galaxy_Guide_1:_A_New_Hope_(Second_Edition) "Galaxy Guide 1: A New Hope (Second Edition)")).

:   → See [Forum:CT:How to name publication edition, redux](/wiki/Forum:CT:How_to_name_publication_edition,_redux "Forum:CT:How to name publication edition, redux")

If there is a naming conflict between the title of a work officially translated in another language than English and another original work in that language, the former must be made into a redirect to the English work, while the latter should be provided with a parenthetical disambiguation including the media type (example: *[L'Ascension de Skywalker](/wiki/L%27Ascension_de_Skywalker "L'Ascension de Skywalker")* should serves as a redirect to [*Star Wars*: Episode IX *The Rise of Skywalker*](/wiki/Star_Wars:_Episode_IX_The_Rise_of_Skywalker "Star Wars: Episode IX The Rise of Skywalker"), instead of being the article title for [*L'ascension de Skywalker* (junior novelization)](/wiki/L%27ascension_de_Skywalker_(junior_novelization) "L'ascension de Skywalker (junior novelization)")).

:   → See [Forum:CT:Translation, Articles titles disambiguation](/wiki/Forum:CT:Translation,_Articles_titles_disambiguation "Forum:CT:Translation, Articles titles disambiguation")

To disambiguate for any publication type not covered in the following sections, use the type of literary work, such as "(short story)" (example: [The Tale of the Aiwha Pod (short story)](/wiki/The_Tale_of_the_Aiwha_Pod_(short_story) "The Tale of the Aiwha Pod (short story)"))

#### Comics and magazines

##### Individual comic issues

Do not include the "Star Wars" prefix in the article titles of individual [comic book](/wiki/Star_Wars_comic_books "Star Wars comic books") issues, stories, or original manga volumes. The following exceptions apply:

* Comic issues with "Star Wars"–specific titles (examples: [*Classic Star Wars: Devilworlds* 1](/wiki/Classic_Star_Wars:_Devilworlds_1 "Classic Star Wars: Devilworlds 1"), [*Star Wars Weekly* 1](/wiki/Star_Wars_Weekly_1 "Star Wars Weekly 1"), [*Star Wars Annual* (1977) 3](/wiki/Star_Wars_Annual_(1977)_3 "Star Wars Annual (1977) 3"))
* Comic issues from the following series that follow the "*Star Wars* <issue number>" title format:
  + [Marvel Comics](/wiki/Marvel_Comics "Marvel Comics")' [1977](/wiki/Star_Wars_(1977) "Star Wars (1977)"), [2015](/wiki/Star_Wars_(2015) "Star Wars (2015)"), and [2020](/wiki/Star_Wars_(2020) "Star Wars (2020)") comic series (examples: [*Star Wars* (1977) 1](/wiki/Star_Wars_(1977)_1 "Star Wars (1977) 1"), [*Star Wars* (2015) 1](/wiki/Star_Wars_(2015)_1 "Star Wars (2015) 1"), [*Star Wars* (2020) 1](/wiki/Star_Wars_(2020)_1 "Star Wars (2020) 1"))
  + [Dark Horse's](/wiki/Dark_Horse_Comics "Dark Horse Comics") 2013 [*Star Wars* series](/wiki/Star_Wars_(2013) "Star Wars (2013)") (example: [*Star Wars* (2013) 1](/wiki/Star_Wars_(2013)_1 "Star Wars (2013) 1"))
  + The non-canon series *[The Star Wars](/wiki/The_Star_Wars "The Star Wars")* (example: [*The Star Wars* 1](/wiki/The_Star_Wars_1 "The Star Wars 1"))
* [PhotoComic](/wiki/PhotoComic "PhotoComic") film adaptations (examples: [*Star Wars: The Empire Strikes Back* Cine-Manga](/wiki/Star_Wars:_The_Empire_Strikes_Back_Cine-Manga "Star Wars: The Empire Strikes Back Cine-Manga"), [*Star Wars: Clone Wars* Cine-Manga](/wiki/Star_Wars:_Clone_Wars_Cine-Manga "Star Wars: Clone Wars Cine-Manga"))
* One-shot comic stories published exclusively in trade-paperback format (examples: [*Star Wars: Jango Fett*](/wiki/Star_Wars:_Jango_Fett_(one-shot) "Star Wars: Jango Fett (one-shot)"), *[Star Wars: Zam Wesell](/wiki/Star_Wars:_Zam_Wesell "Star Wars: Zam Wesell")*)
* Non-narrative comic issues that are treated as "Sources" list items (examples: *[Star Wars Handbook 1: X-Wing Rogue Squadron](/wiki/Star_Wars_Handbook_1:_X-Wing_Rogue_Squadron "Star Wars Handbook 1: X-Wing Rogue Squadron")*, [*Star Wars: Legacy* (2006) 0½](/wiki/Star_Wars:_Legacy_(2006)_0%C2%BD "Star Wars: Legacy (2006) 0½"))
* Manga:
  + Manga volumes with "Star Wars"-specific titles (example: [*Star Wars Manga: A New Hope* 1](/wiki/Star_Wars_Manga:_A_New_Hope_1 "Star Wars Manga: A New Hope 1"))
  + Anthology manga volumes (example: *[Star Wars Manga: Black](/wiki/Star_Wars_Manga:_Black "Star Wars Manga: Black")*)
  + Volumes of manga adaptations of another work, such as a webcomic (example: *[Star Wars Leia, Princess of Alderaan, Vol. 1](/wiki/Star_Wars_Leia,_Princess_of_Alderaan,_Vol._1 "Star Wars Leia, Princess of Alderaan, Vol. 1")*)

:   The preceding rule does not apply to comic series, story arcs, trade paperbacks, or any other compendium publication not typically included in an article's Appearances list. These articles shall include "Star Wars" in their titles.

Do not include story names in the article titles of individual comic book issues (examples: [*Star Wars* (1977) 108](/wiki/Star_Wars_(1977)_108 "Star Wars (1977) 108"), not *[Star Wars 108: Forever Crimson](/wiki/Star_Wars_108:_Forever_Crimson "Star Wars 108: Forever Crimson")*; [*Kanan* 1](/wiki/Kanan_1 "Kanan 1"), not *[Kanan 1: The Last Padawan, Part I: Fight](/wiki/Kanan_1:_The_Last_Padawan,_Part_I:_Fight "Kanan 1: The Last Padawan, Part I: Fight")*; [*Lando* 1](/wiki/Lando_1 "Lando 1"), not *[Lando, Part I](/wiki/Lando,_Part_I "Lando, Part I")*).

##### Disambiguation

* To disambiguate comic stories, use "(comic story)" (example: [Sabotage (comic story)](/wiki/Sabotage_(comic_story) "Sabotage (comic story)")).
* To disambiguate comic-book series, use "(comic series)" (example: [*Star Wars: Galaxy's Edge* (comic series)](/wiki/Star_Wars:_Galaxy%27s_Edge_(comic_series) "Star Wars: Galaxy's Edge (comic series)")).
* To disambiguate comic-book collected editions, use "(TPB)" for trade paperbacks and "(HC)" for hardcovers.
* When two or more comic series share the same title, but began publication in different years, the year of each of the series' initial publication should be considered part of the series title, and included in a parenthetical descriptor (example: [*Star Wars: Doctor Aphra* (2016)](/wiki/Star_Wars:_Doctor_Aphra_(2016) "Star Wars: Doctor Aphra (2016)") and [*Star Wars: Doctor Aphra* (2020)](/wiki/Star_Wars:_Doctor_Aphra_(2020) "Star Wars: Doctor Aphra (2020)")). If a comic series name competes with a different piece of *Star Wars* media that was published in the same year as the series began, the comic series takes priority and should use the year parenthetical descriptor.
* The preceding rule also applies to individual issues of the series, and should be applied to *all* issues of the series, regardless of whether there is a competing issue title. A disambiguation page should be created at the root title (example: [*Doctor Aphra* (2016) 12](/wiki/Doctor_Aphra_(2016)_12 "Doctor Aphra (2016) 12") and [*Doctor Aphra* (2020) 12](/wiki/Doctor_Aphra_(2020)_12 "Doctor Aphra (2020) 12"), with a disambiguation page at [Doctor Aphra 12 (disambiguation)](/wiki/Doctor_Aphra_12_(disambiguation) "Doctor Aphra 12 (disambiguation)")).

:   → See [Forum:CT:Naming policy: Comic book collected editions](/wiki/Forum:CT:Naming_policy:_Comic_book_collected_editions "Forum:CT:Naming policy: Comic book collected editions"), [Forum:CT:Naming policy: Canon comic books](/wiki/Forum:CT:Naming_policy:_Canon_comic_books "Forum:CT:Naming policy: Canon comic books"), [Wookieepedia:Mofferences/July 25, 2020](/wiki/Wookieepedia:Mofferences/July_25,_2020 "Wookieepedia:Mofferences/July 25, 2020"), [Forum:CT:Publication Years in Comic Titles](/wiki/Forum:CT:Publication_Years_in_Comic_Titles "Forum:CT:Publication Years in Comic Titles"), ~~[Forum:CT:Comic series titles](/wiki/Forum:CT:Comic_series_titles "Forum:CT:Comic series titles")~~, [Forum:CT:Comic series parantheticals](/wiki/Forum:CT:Comic_series_parantheticals "Forum:CT:Comic series parantheticals"), [Forum:CT:Assorted policy shenanigans](/wiki/Forum:CT:Assorted_policy_shenanigans "Forum:CT:Assorted policy shenanigans")

##### Italics and number sign

* Do not include the number sign (#) in article titles, in-line text, the Appearances/Sources lists, or any other instance when referring to comic books, magazine issues, mangas, or any collections of such. Although some comic book and magazine issues may use the number sign in their published titles, such use of the number sign creates technical and applicable issues for Wookieepedia in certain instances.

:   → See [Forum:CT:Number sign in comic issue titles](/wiki/Forum:CT:Number_sign_in_comic_issue_titles "Forum:CT:Number sign in comic issue titles"), [Wookieepedia:Mofferences/September 10, 2016](/wiki/Wookieepedia:Mofferences/September_10,_2016 "Wookieepedia:Mofferences/September 10, 2016")

* Do not italicize the issue number in the article titles of individual comic books or collections (examples: [*Knights of the Old Republic* 18](/wiki/Knights_of_the_Old_Republic_18 "Knights of the Old Republic 18"), [*Marvel Comics* 1000](/wiki/Marvel_Comics_1000 "Marvel Comics 1000"), [*Doctor Aphra* (2016) 38](/wiki/Doctor_Aphra_(2016)_38 "Doctor Aphra (2016) 38"), [*Star Wars Adventures* (2017) 28](/wiki/Star_Wars_Adventures_(2017)_28 "Star Wars Adventures (2017) 28")).

:   → See [Forum:CT:Italics in Canon comic issues](/wiki/Forum:CT:Italics_in_Canon_comic_issues "Forum:CT:Italics in Canon comic issues"), [Forum:CT:Naming policy: Legends comic books](/wiki/Forum:CT:Naming_policy:_Legends_comic_books "Forum:CT:Naming policy: Legends comic books")

* Do not italicize descriptor words related to publishing info (examples: years and publisher) or format (examples: hardcover, limited edition, or trade paperback)
  + As an exception, publishers should be italicized if their name is normally italicized.
* Do not italicize the following punctuation: periods and parentheses

:   → See [Forum:CT:Comic Policy Cleanup](/wiki/Forum:CT:Comic_Policy_Cleanup "Forum:CT:Comic Policy Cleanup")

#### Novels and books

##### Individual works

Do not include the "Star Wars" prefix in the article titles of novels, junior novels, novellas, eBooks, young-reader stories, storybooks, roleplaying adventure books, or any other print or digital book that would normally be considered an in-universe "[Appearance](/wiki/Wookieepedia:Layout_Guide#Appearances "Wookieepedia:Layout Guide")." The following exceptions apply:

* Film novels and film junior novelizations (examples: [*Star Wars: A New Hope* (novelization)](/wiki/Star_Wars:_A_New_Hope_(novelization) "Star Wars: A New Hope (novelization)"), [*Star Wars*: Episode V *The Empire Strikes Back* (junior novelization)](/wiki/Star_Wars:_Episode_V_The_Empire_Strikes_Back_(junior_novelization) "Star Wars: Episode V The Empire Strikes Back (junior novelization)"), [*Star Wars: The Clone Wars* (novel)](/wiki/Star_Wars:_The_Clone_Wars_(novelization) "Star Wars: The Clone Wars (novelization)"))
* Subjects with "Star Wars"–specific titles (examples: *[Star Wars Galaxies: The Ruins of Dantooine](/wiki/Star_Wars_Galaxies:_The_Ruins_of_Dantooine "Star Wars Galaxies: The Ruins of Dantooine")*, *[Star Wars Missions 1: Assault on Yavin Four](/wiki/Star_Wars_Missions_1:_Assault_on_Yavin_Four "Star Wars Missions 1: Assault on Yavin Four")*, *[Star Wars Journal: The Fight for Justice](/wiki/Star_Wars_Journal:_The_Fight_for_Justice "Star Wars Journal: The Fight for Justice")*, *[Star Wars Adventures: Boba Fett and the Ship of Fear](/wiki/Star_Wars_Adventures:_Boba_Fett_and_the_Ship_of_Fear "Star Wars Adventures: Boba Fett and the Ship of Fear")*, [*LEGO Star Wars: The Yoda Chronicles* (book)](/wiki/LEGO_Star_Wars:_The_Yoda_Chronicles_(book) "LEGO Star Wars: The Yoda Chronicles (book)"))

Applicable books from the *[Star Wars: The Clone Wars](/wiki/Star_Wars:_The_Clone_Wars "Star Wars: The Clone Wars")* and *[Star Wars: The Old Republic](/wiki/Star_Wars:_The_Old_Republic "Star Wars: The Old Republic")* series will be shortened to "*The Clone Wars*" and "*The Old Republic*," respectively (examples: *[The Clone Wars: Wild Space](/wiki/The_Clone_Wars:_Wild_Space "The Clone Wars: Wild Space")* instead of *[Star Wars: The Clone Wars: Wild Space](/wiki/Star_Wars:_The_Clone_Wars:_Wild_Space "Star Wars: The Clone Wars: Wild Space")*, *[The Old Republic: Revan](/wiki/The_Old_Republic:_Revan "The Old Republic: Revan")* instead of *[Star Wars: The Old Republic: Revan](/wiki/Star_Wars:_The_Old_Republic:_Revan "Star Wars: The Old Republic: Revan")*). Applicable books from *[Star Wars Rebels](/wiki/Star_Wars_Rebels "Star Wars Rebels")* will not include the series title.

If the book is part of a series, include the series name as part of the title (examples: *[Galaxy of Fear: Eaten Alive](/wiki/Galaxy_of_Fear:_Eaten_Alive "Galaxy of Fear: Eaten Alive")*, *[Lost Tribe of the Sith: Precipice](/wiki/Lost_Tribe_of_the_Sith:_Precipice "Lost Tribe of the Sith: Precipice")*, *[Fate of the Jedi: Outcast](/wiki/Fate_of_the_Jedi:_Outcast "Fate of the Jedi: Outcast")*). Do not include the series name as part of the title if the series is not typically included in the book's title (examples: *[Heir to the Empire](/wiki/Heir_to_the_Empire "Heir to the Empire")* instead of ***The Thrawn Trilogy: Heir to the Empire***, *[Ambush at Corellia](/wiki/Ambush_at_Corellia "Ambush at Corellia")* instead of ***The Corellian Trilogy: Ambush at Corellia***).

:   → See [Forum:CT:Naming policy: Novels](/wiki/Forum:CT:Naming_policy:_Novels "Forum:CT:Naming policy: Novels")

##### Series

Do include the "Star Wars" prefix in the article titles of novel and book series (examples: *[Star Wars: The Thrawn Trilogy](/wiki/Star_Wars:_The_Thrawn_Trilogy_(hardcover) "Star Wars: The Thrawn Trilogy (hardcover)")*, *[Star Wars: Fate of the Jedi](/wiki/Star_Wars:_Fate_of_the_Jedi "Star Wars: Fate of the Jedi")*, *[Star Wars: The Aftermath Trilogy](/wiki/Star_Wars:_The_Aftermath_Trilogy "Star Wars: The Aftermath Trilogy")*, *[Star Wars: Adventures in Wild Space](/wiki/Star_Wars:_Adventures_in_Wild_Space "Star Wars: Adventures in Wild Space")*).

:   → See [Forum:CT:Naming policy amendments: Books](/wiki/Forum:CT:Naming_policy_amendments:_Books "Forum:CT:Naming policy amendments: Books")

##### Disambiguation

* To disambiguate novels, use "(novel)" (example: [*Death Star* (novel)](/wiki/Death_Star_(novel) "Death Star (novel)")).
  + For novelizations, such as [film novelizations](/wiki/Category:Film_novelizations "Category:Film novelizations") or [junior film novelizations](/wiki/Category:Junior_film_novelizations "Category:Junior film novelizations"), use either "(novelization)" or "(junior novelization)" as appropriate.
* To disambiguate audiobooks, use "(audiobook)" (example: [*Kenobi* (audiobook)](/wiki/Kenobi_(audiobook) "Kenobi (audiobook)")).
  + If multiple audiobooks of the same story compete for the same title, naming precedence shall be granted to the mass-market edition, while all other editions will disambiguate via the publisher name.
  + If both an unabridged and abridged audiobook of the same story exist, use "(unabridged audiobook)" and "(abridged audiobook)" to disambiguate them (example [*Shatterpoint* (unabridged audiobook)](/wiki/Shatterpoint_(unabridged_audiobook) "Shatterpoint (unabridged audiobook)") and [*Shatterpoint* (abridged audiobook)](/wiki/Shatterpoint_(abridged_audiobook) "Shatterpoint (abridged audiobook)"). However, abridged audiobooks should specify "abridged" in parentheses even if no unabridged version exists (example: [*Darksaber* (abridged audiobook)](/wiki/Darksaber_(abridged_audiobook) "Darksaber (abridged audiobook)")).
* Articles may use "(book)" to disambiguate a non-fiction book (example: [*Star Wars: The Magic of Myth* (book)](/wiki/Star_Wars:_The_Magic_of_Myth_(book) "Star Wars: The Magic of Myth (book)")).
* To disambiguate novel series, use "(novel series)" (example: [*Star Wars: X-Wing* (novel series)](/wiki/Star_Wars:_X-Wing_(novel_series) "Star Wars: X-Wing (novel series)")).
* Whenever a book has different editions, list them after a comma when the edition is a part of the formal title, and without italics if it is not formally part of the book's title (example: *[Star Wars: The Roleplaying Game, Second Edition](/wiki/Star_Wars:_The_Roleplaying_Game,_Second_Edition "Star Wars: The Roleplaying Game, Second Edition")*, but [*Galaxy Guide 1: A New Hope*, Second Edition](/wiki/Galaxy_Guide_1:_A_New_Hope_(Second_Edition) "Galaxy Guide 1: A New Hope (Second Edition)")).

:   → See [Wookieepedia:Mofferences/July 25, 2020](/wiki/Wookieepedia:Mofferences/July_25,_2020 "Wookieepedia:Mofferences/July 25, 2020"), [Forum:CT:Assorted policy shenanigans](/wiki/Forum:CT:Assorted_policy_shenanigans "Forum:CT:Assorted policy shenanigans")

#### Video games

To disambiguate [video games](/wiki/Video_game "Video game"), use "(video game)" instead of simply "(game)" (example: [*Star Wars: Shadows of the Empire* (video game)](/wiki/Star_Wars:_Shadows_of_the_Empire_(video_game) "Star Wars: Shadows of the Empire (video game)") instead of ***Star Wars: Shadows of the Empire* (game)**).

:   → See [Forum:CT:Video games](/wiki/Forum:CT:Video_games "Forum:CT:Video games")

### Starships

To disambiguate individual starships, add the ship class in parentheses to the vessel's name.

* The parenthetical descriptor need only specify the abbreviated ship class (example: [*Relentless* (*Pellaeon*-class)](/wiki/Relentless_(Pellaeon-class) "Relentless (Pellaeon-class)")) unless the abbreviated class name applies to multiple ship classes, in which case the full ship class name shall be specified (example: [*Vanguard* (*Defender*-class light corvette)](/wiki/Vanguard_(Defender-class_light_corvette) "Vanguard (Defender-class light corvette)"), because "*Defender*-class" may refer to several different ship classes).
* If a ship class does not include "-class" as part of its name, specify the full class name in parentheses (example: [*Gilder* (GR-75 medium transport)](/wiki/Gilder_(GR-75_medium_transport) "Gilder (GR-75 medium transport)"), [*Mantooine* (CR90 corvette)](/wiki/Mantooine_(CR90_corvette) "Mantooine (CR90 corvette)")).

When using a conjectural name or when the class is unknown, use "starship" instead of simply "ship" (example: [*Carida* (starship)](/wiki/Carida_(starship) "Carida (starship)") instead of **Carida (ship)**). If a ship type is known, use as specific a term as possible (example: [*Aurora* (Star Dreadnought)](/wiki/Aurora_(Star_Dreadnought) "Aurora (Star Dreadnought)"), [*Luminous* (battlecruiser)](/wiki/Luminous_(battlecruiser) "Luminous (battlecruiser)")).

:   → See [Forum:CT:Naming policy amendments, Part 2](/wiki/Forum:CT:Naming_policy_amendments,_Part_2 "Forum:CT:Naming policy amendments, Part 2")

### Star systems and sectors

[Star systems](/wiki/Star_system/Legends "Star system/Legends") and [sectors](/wiki/Sector "Sector") named in *[The Essential Atlas](/wiki/The_Essential_Atlas "The Essential Atlas")*, the [*Star Wars: The Essential Atlas* Online Companion](/wiki/Star_Wars:_The_Essential_Atlas_Online_Companion "Star Wars: The Essential Atlas Online Companion") and/or "[Star Systems of the Galaxy](/wiki/Star_Systems_of_the_Galaxy "Star Systems of the Galaxy")" shall be titled in accordance with these texts, with the understanding that nothing listed in the aforementioned media was intended to override a preexisting name (example: [Drynn system](/wiki/Drynn_system "Drynn system"), listed in the *Atlas* as "Kammia system"), but should be treated as a valid alternate name in-universe per [Jason Fry's](/wiki/File:AtlasEmail-2025-07-31.png "File:AtlasEmail-2025-07-31.png") [comments](https://boards.theforce.net/threads/the-essential-atlas-and-galactic-cartography-official-discussion.21938930/page-139#post-30824201).

Add a conjecture tag to system articles from the aforementioned media if the system is named after a [space station](/wiki/Space_station/Legends "Space station/Legends"); [nebula](/wiki/Nebula/Legends "Nebula/Legends"); [star cluster](/wiki/Star_cluster/Legends "Star cluster/Legends"); rogue [planet](/wiki/Planet/Legends "Planet/Legends"); or any other non-standard, non-planetary astronomical location (example: [Azzameen Station system](/wiki/Azzameen_Station_system "Azzameen Station system"), [Red Nebula system](/wiki/Red_Nebula_system "Red Nebula system")).

:   → See [Forum:CT:Atlas Star Systems: Deviating from our Present Course](/wiki/Forum:CT:Atlas_Star_Systems:_Deviating_from_our_Present_Course "Forum:CT:Atlas Star Systems: Deviating from our Present Course"), [Forum:CT:Naming policy: AST amendment](/wiki/Forum:CT:Naming_policy:_AST_amendment "Forum:CT:Naming policy: AST amendment")

### Titles

Titles are generally **not** considered to be part of a character's name (example: [Dooku](/wiki/Dooku "Dooku") instead of [Count Dooku](/wiki/Count_Dooku "Count Dooku")). The notable exception is the [Sith](/wiki/Sith "Sith") title "**[Darth](/wiki/Darth "Darth")**," which is considered to be part of the [Sith Lord](/wiki/Sith_Lord "Sith Lord")'s name (example: [Darth Sidious](/wiki/Darth_Sidious "Darth Sidious")).

:   → See [Forum:CT:Naming Conventions for Nobility and Royalty](/wiki/Forum:CT:Naming_Conventions_for_Nobility_and_Royalty "Forum:CT:Naming Conventions for Nobility and Royalty")
:   → See [Forum:CT:About those naming conventions...](/wiki/Forum:CT:About_those_naming_conventions... "Forum:CT:About those naming conventions...")

### Years

For Legends [year](/wiki/Standard_year/Legends "Standard year/Legends") articles, omit the comma for years 1000-9999 BBY/ABY (example: [5000 BBY](/wiki/5000_BBY/Legends "5000 BBY/Legends"), not **5,000 BBY**). Do include the comma for all years 10,000+ BBY/ABY.

:   → See [Forum:CT:Commas in year articles](/wiki/Forum:CT:Commas_in_year_articles "Forum:CT:Commas in year articles")
