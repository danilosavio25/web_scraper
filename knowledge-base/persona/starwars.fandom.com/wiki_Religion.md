Religion usually referred to a being's spiritual beliefs and might include some belief in an [afterlife](/wiki/Netherworld_of_the_Force "Netherworld of the Force").[[3]](#cite_note-Episode_III-3) They also might include the belief in a [god](/wiki/God "God") or [the Force](/wiki/The_Force "The Force"), though this was not always the case.[[4]](#cite_note-R1Novel-4) For instance, [Mandalorian](/wiki/Mandalorian "Mandalorian") adherents to the [Way of the Mandalore](/wiki/Way_of_the_Mandalore "Way of the Mandalore") appeared not to show any reverence towards a god or the Force, and instead cherished weaponry, honor, and their armor.[[1]](#cite_note-Chapter_2-1) The people of [Serenno](/wiki/Serenno "Serenno") worshiped seven gods.[[5]](#cite_note-Jedi_Lost-5) The [Central Isopter](/wiki/Central_Isopter "Central Isopter"), on the other hand, worshiped [death](/wiki/Death "Death").[[6]](#cite_note-Star_Wars_41-6) Many religions followed some form of creed or code.[[7]](#cite_note-Chapter_8-7) The [Jedi Order](/wiki/Jedi_Order "Jedi Order") adhered to its [Jedi Code](/wiki/Jedi_Code "Jedi Code"),[[8]](#cite_note-Episode_I-8) while their enemies, the [Sith](/wiki/Sith "Sith"), followed the [Code of the Sith](/wiki/Code_of_the_Sith "Code of the Sith").[[9]](#cite_note-Altar_of_Mortis-9) Likewise, many religions followed a foundational set of scriptures,[[10]](#cite_note-TROSVD-10) such as the [sacred Jedi texts](/wiki/Sacred_Jedi_texts "Sacred Jedi texts") observed by members of the Jedi Order.[[11]](#cite_note-Episode_VIII-11) The [Order of the Esoteric Pulsar](/wiki/Order_of_the_Esoteric_Pulsar "Order of the Esoteric Pulsar") followed the *[Book of Stars](/wiki/Book_of_Stars "Book of Stars")*.[[4]](#cite_note-R1Novel-4)

Central Isopter cultists

 

Whenever a religion fell outside the mainstream, it was often referred to as a "cult." Many of the known cults in [the galaxy](/wiki/The_galaxy "The galaxy") adhered to the [dark side of the Force](/wiki/Dark_side_of_the_Force "Dark side of the Force"), making following them even more stigmatized. In the aftermath of the [Battle of Endor](/wiki/Battle_of_Endor "Battle of Endor"), multiple Sith cults emerged to worship the deposed and presumed deceased [Sith Lord](/wiki/Sith_Lord "Sith Lord") [Darth Sidious](/wiki/Darth_Sidious "Darth Sidious") and his apprentice [Darth Vader](/wiki/Anakin_Skywalker "Anakin Skywalker"). Among these were the the [Acolytes of the Beyond](/wiki/Acolytes_of_the_Beyond "Acolytes of the Beyond"), [Alazmec of Winsit](/wiki/Alazmec_of_Winsit "Alazmec of Winsit"), and the [Sith Eternal](/wiki/Sith_Eternal "Sith Eternal").[[10]](#cite_note-TROSVD-10) Similarly, it was possible for splinter groups to form within religions who disagreed with the orthodox members of their orders. An example of this was the [Ordu Aspectu](/wiki/Ordu_Aspectu "Ordu Aspectu"), a [Jedi](/wiki/Jedi "Jedi") heresy, who sought eternal life through technological means, a pursuit that resulted in open conflict with the Jedi Order.[[12]](#cite_note-Aphra6-12) The orthodox Jedi and Ordu-sect eventually made peace. The [Eternal Rur](/wiki/Eternal_Rur "Eternal Rur") later [destroyed](/wiki/Attack_on_Garn "Attack on Garn") both the breakaway sect and all orthodox Jedi present in the [Citadel of Rur](/wiki/Citadel_of_Rur "Citadel of Rur").[[12]](#cite_note-Aphra6-12)

Many religions had sites scattered throughout the galaxy that were holy or sacred to them. [Jedha City](/wiki/Jedha_City "Jedha City") was a gathering place for a plethora of the galaxy's Force-based religions, including the [Ninn Orthodoxy](/wiki/Ninn_Orthodoxy "Ninn Orthodoxy"), the [Guardians of the Whills](/wiki/Guardians_of_the_Whills "Guardians of the Whills"), the [Phirmists](/wiki/Phirmism "Phirmism"), and the [Zealots of Psusan](/wiki/Zealots_of_Psusan "Zealots of Psusan"), until the city's destruction by the [first Death Star](/wiki/DS-1_Orbital_Battle_Station "DS-1 Orbital Battle Station").[[4]](#cite_note-R1Novel-4) Jedha's importance to various Force religions had led to the establishment of the [Convocation of the Force](/wiki/Convocation_of_the_Force "Convocation of the Force"), which presented common leadership for all non-dark side faiths who joined during the [High Republic Era](/wiki/High_Republic_Era "High Republic Era").[[13]](#cite_note-Peace_and_Unity-13) [Ilum](/wiki/Ilum "Ilum") was a sacred [planet](/wiki/Planet "Planet") for the Jedi Order, where they would harvest [kyber crystals](/wiki/Kyber_crystal "Kyber crystal") for their [lightsabers](/wiki/Lightsaber "Lightsaber");[[14]](#cite_note-The_Gathering-14) and [Moraband](/wiki/Moraband "Moraband") was a dark but sacred world to the Sith and was home to their [burial grounds](/wiki/Valley_of_the_Dark_Lords "Valley of the Dark Lords").[[15]](#cite_note-Sacrifice-15)

## History[]

### Ancient past[]

:   "*Once we were brothers in the Force. But from the Hundred-Year Darkness were born the Sith.*"
:   ―A Jedi[[16]](#cite_note-SW9-16)

At some point in the ancient past, the [Prime Jedi](/wiki/Prime_Jedi "Prime Jedi") created the Jedi Order on [Ahch-To](/wiki/Ahch-To "Ahch-To").[[17]](#cite_note-TLJVD-17) The [sacred Jedi texts](/wiki/Sacred_Jedi_texts "Sacred Jedi texts"), written at the dawn of the Jedi Order, contained some of the earliest religious texts to reference the Force. The *[Rammahgon](/wiki/Rammahgon "Rammahgon")* in particular contained a number of conflicting origin stories for both the [universe](/wiki/Universe "Universe") and the Force.[[10]](#cite_note-TROSVD-10) Soon, the Jedi Order took to the stars, settling on [Ossus](/wiki/Ossus "Ossus") and [Tython](/wiki/Tython "Tython") among other worlds.[[17]](#cite_note-TLJVD-17) At some unknown point, Ahch-To was abandoned by the Jedi and left in the hands of the [Caretakers](/wiki/Caretakers "Caretakers").[[18]](#cite_note-TLJNovel-18)

The ancient enemies of the Jedi, the Sith, were born from a schism in the Jedi Order led by a [rogue Jedi](/wiki/Unidentified_rogue_Jedi "Unidentified rogue Jedi") during the [Hundred-Year Darkness](/wiki/Hundred-Year_Darkness "Hundred-Year Darkness")[[16]](#cite_note-SW9-16) as the rogue Jedi and his followers believed that the true potential of the Force laid in using the [powers](/wiki/Force_power "Force power") of the dark side of the Force.[[19]](#cite_note-Sith-19) Conflict between these two Force religions led to countless wars that resulted in the [Sith Empire](/wiki/Sith_Empire "Sith Empire") in control of the galaxy. Ultimately, however, the Jedi [defeated](/wiki/Jedi-Sith_War "Jedi-Sith War") the Sith and thought them to be extinct.[[20]](#cite_note-Propaganda-20) Afterwards, the [Galactic Republic](/wiki/Galactic_Republic "Galactic Republic") sought to scrub [history](/wiki/Timeline_of_galactic_history "Timeline of galactic history") clean of all mentions of the Sith religion,[[10]](#cite_note-TROSVD-10) even forbidding [protocol droids](/wiki/Protocol_droid "Protocol droid") from translating their [runic language](/wiki/Ur-Kitt%C3%A2t "Ur-Kittât").[[21]](#cite_note-Episode_IX-21) However, the Sith [Lord](/wiki/Lord "Lord") [Darth Bane](/wiki/Darth_Bane "Darth Bane") secretly carried on the Sith religion in the form of the [Rule of Two](/wiki/Rule_of_Two "Rule of Two"), allowing the Sith Order to survive its supposed extinction.[[15]](#cite_note-Sacrifice-15)

In ancient times,[[22]](#cite_note-Aliens_of_the_Galaxy-22) the Jedi once also fought a [series of conflicts](/wiki/Mandalorian-Jedi_War "Mandalorian-Jedi War")[[23]](#cite_note-Ultimate-23) against the Mandalorians,[[24]](#cite_note-Trials_of_the_Darksaber-24) who subscribed to a martial religion.[[25]](#cite_note-Mandalorian-25) The [Mandalorian crusades](/wiki/Mandalorian_crusades "Mandalorian crusades")[[26]](#cite_note-An_Inside_Man_Trivia_Gallery-26) were undertaken by [Mandalorian crusaders](/wiki/Mandalorian_crusaders "Mandalorian crusaders"), which put them at conflict with the Jedi Order.[[27]](#cite_note-Shades_of_Reason_Trivia_Gallery-27) Ultimately, however, the [Mandalorian cataclysm](/wiki/Annihilation "Annihilation") between Mandalorian [warlords](/wiki/Warlord "Warlord") and the Jedi Order[[28]](#cite_note-Creating_Mandalore-28) left the surface of Mandalorian [homeworld](/wiki/Homeworld "Homeworld") of [Mandalore](/wiki/Mandalore "Mandalore")[[29]](#cite_note-Absolutely_Everything_You_Need_to_Know-29) devastated, rendering it uninhabitable outside of [domed cities](/wiki/Domed_city "Domed city").[[30]](#cite_note-Mandalorian_super_commando-30) It was after these conflicts that the Jedi fought against the Sith and thought their ancient order to be extinct.[[31]](#cite_note-Mandalorian_date_note-31)

 [![The Temple of the Kyber, a holy site in the Holy City on Jedha.](data:image/gif;base64,R0lGODlhAQABAIABAAAAAP///yH5BAEAAAEALAAAAAABAAEAQAICTAEAOw%3D%3D)](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/starwars/images/1/15/Temple_of_the_Whills_-_RO_trailer.png/revision/latest?cb=20161207041248) [![The Temple of the Kyber, a holy site in the Holy City on Jedha.](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/starwars/images/1/15/Temple_of_the_Whills_-_RO_trailer.png/revision/latest/scale-to-width-down/180?cb=20161207041248)](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/starwars/images/1/15/Temple_of_the_Whills_-_RO_trailer.png/revision/latest?cb=20161207041248)

The ancient Jedi also fought a religious civil war with a splinter group, the Ordu Aspectu, who disagreed with the orthodox members of the Order over the violence they perceived was perpetrated by the Jedi. Galactic [historians](/wiki/Historian "Historian") differ on what ultimately caused the conflict between the two sects of the Jedi Order, but, in the end, the rogue Jedi [Rur](/wiki/Rur "Rur") and his followers were wiped out by the orthodox Jedi.[[32]](#cite_note-Aphra2-32)

Around the same time, more than 5,000 years before the [Galactic Civil War](/wiki/Galactic_Civil_War "Galactic Civil War"), the holy city of [NiJedha](/wiki/Jedha "Jedha") was built on the [moon](/wiki/Moon "Moon") of [Jedha](/wiki/Jedha "Jedha").[[33]](#cite_note-Atlas-33) The city hosted the [Temple of the Kyber](/wiki/Temple_of_the_Kyber "Temple of the Kyber") and came to be a site of pilgrimage for a plethora of Force religions. The Jedi Order eventually established itself on Jedha, as well, taking up in the Temple of the Kyber.[[4]](#cite_note-R1Novel-4) Despite its religious pluralism, the moon became so associated with the Jedi that it was proposed by historians as a possible location for the [first Jedi temple](/wiki/First_Jedi_Temple "First Jedi Temple").[[17]](#cite_note-TLJVD-17)

### Republic Era[]

#### A Great Peace, yet with continued conflicts[]

:   "*For over a thousand generations the Jedi Knights were the guardians of peace and justice in the Old Republic. Before the dark times. Before the Empire.*"
:   ―Obi-Wan Kenobi[[34]](#cite_note-Episode_IV-34)

With the Sith seemingly defeated, the Jedi established their [main temple](/wiki/Imperial_Palace "Imperial Palace") on the galactic capital of [Coruscant](/wiki/Coruscant "Coruscant"), capping off an ancient [Sith shrine](/wiki/Shrine_in_the_Depths "Shrine in the Depths"), which had also been the site of worship for a number of religious cults throughout the centuries.[[35]](#cite_note-Tarkin-35) A number of religions prospered during the [Republic Era](/wiki/Republic_Era "Republic Era"). Though the Sith were gone, the dark side had its adherents in the form of the [Yacombe](/wiki/Yacombe "Yacombe")[[5]](#cite_note-Jedi_Lost-5) and [apostate](/wiki/Apostate_(religion) "Apostate (religion)") members[[36]](#cite_note-The_High_Republic_2022_7-36) of the [Sorcerers of Tund](/wiki/Sorcerers_of_Tund "Sorcerers of Tund"). Many of their relics came to rest in the [Archive of Forbidden Artifacts](/wiki/Archive_of_Forbidden_Artifacts "Archive of Forbidden Artifacts"), a [Bogan Collection](/wiki/Bogan_Collection "Bogan Collection") in the [Jedi Archives](/wiki/Jedi_Archives "Jedi Archives") on Coruscant.[[5]](#cite_note-Jedi_Lost-5) The Yacombe would later abandon the dark side.[[13]](#cite_note-Peace_and_Unity-13) Religious tension on Jedha eventually boiled over into riots that helped kick off the [Battle of Jedha](/wiki/Battle_of_Jedha "Battle of Jedha"). While the peace that followed the battle was uneasy, the people of Jedha also rallied together to rebuild their home after the conflict.[[37]](#cite_note-Path_of_Vengeance-37)

The [Presagers of Hakotei](/wiki/Presagers_of_Hakotei "Presagers of Hakotei")—a dark side cult that was obsessed with sacrificing living beings in order to receive [visions](/wiki/Force_vision "Force vision") of the future—were also active on the [Outer Rim](/wiki/Outer_Rim_Territories "Outer Rim Territories") planet of [Asusto](/wiki/Asusto "Asusto"), which was strong in the dark side of the Force. The Jedi had long thought the Presagers extinct, but some of the witches survived, and traded Sith artifacts on the [black market](/wiki/Black_market "Black market") in the hopes of luring a powerful Jedi to them. They succeeded in roping in a young [Dooku](/wiki/Dooku "Dooku"), as well as [Jedi Master](/wiki/Jedi_Master "Jedi Master") [Lene Kostana](/wiki/Lene_Kostana "Lene Kostana") and her [Padawan](/wiki/Padawan "Padawan") [Sifo-Dyas](/wiki/Sifo-Dyas "Sifo-Dyas"). Dooku ultimately wiped out the coven, marking his first significant step down the dark path.[[5]](#cite_note-Jedi_Lost-5)

#### Resurgence of the Sith[]

:   "*Once more the Sith will rule the galaxy, and we shall have peace.*"
:   ―Darth Sidious, to Darth Vader[[3]](#cite_note-Episode_III-3)

In [32 BBY](/wiki/32_BBY "32 BBY"), the hidden Sith revealed themselves during the [Invasion of Naboo](/wiki/Invasion_of_Naboo "Invasion of Naboo") when [Darth Maul](/wiki/Maul "Maul") appeared to [Qui-Gon Jinn](/wiki/Qui-Gon_Jinn "Qui-Gon Jinn") on [Tatooine](/wiki/Tatooine "Tatooine"). Though Maul was defeated and thought dead during the [Battle of Naboo](/wiki/Battle_of_Naboo "Battle of Naboo"), the Jedi knew there to be at least one more Sith Lord waiting in the shadows.[[8]](#cite_note-Episode_I-8) This Sith Lord was, unbeknownst to them at the time, the newly elected [Supreme Chancellor](/wiki/Supreme_Chancellor "Supreme Chancellor") of the Republic, Sheev Palpatine, secretly known as [Darth](/wiki/Darth "Darth") Sidious.[[3]](#cite_note-Episode_III-3)

Sidious and his second apprentice [Darth](/wiki/Darth "Darth") Tyranus, the Sith identity of Dooku,[[38]](#cite_note-Episode_II-38) engineered the [Clone Wars](/wiki/Clone_Wars "Clone Wars") to draw the Jedi into a [trap](/wiki/Great_Jedi_Purge "Great Jedi Purge").[[39]](#cite_note-FAW-39) At the end of the war, Sidious declared himself [Emperor](/wiki/Galactic_Emperor "Galactic Emperor") and reorganized the Republic into the [Galactic Empire](/wiki/Galactic_Empire "Galactic Empire"). He then used his new apprentice, [Darth Vader](/wiki/Anakin_Skywalker "Anakin Skywalker"), who had previously [killed](/wiki/Battle_of_Coruscant "Battle of Coruscant") Tyranus, and the [clone troopers](/wiki/Clone_trooper "Clone trooper") of the [Grand Army of the Republic](/wiki/Grand_Army_of_the_Republic "Grand Army of the Republic")[[38]](#cite_note-Episode_II-38) to [extinguish](/wiki/Order_66 "Order 66") the Jedi Order.[[3]](#cite_note-Episode_III-3) The [Nightsisters](/wiki/Nightsisters "Nightsisters") of [Dathomir](/wiki/Dathomir "Dathomir") were also driven to near extinction by Sidious as a result of the war,[[40]](#cite_note-Massacre-40) though a [few](/wiki/Merrin "Merrin") [individuals](/wiki/Shelish "Shelish"),[[41]](#cite_note-Fallen_Order-41)[[42]](#cite_note-Uprising-42) and at least [one clan](/wiki/Nardithi_Nightsisters "Nardithi Nightsisters") survived the purge.[[43]](#cite_note-Ghosts-43)

### Imperial Era[]

:   "*The Jedi are extinct. Their fire has gone out of the universe. You, my friend, are all that's left of their religion.*"

Jedha was ravaged by the Death Star.

 

The Emperor and Darth Vader continued their purge of the Jedi after the formation of the Galactic Empire, forming the [Inquisitorius](/wiki/Inquisitorius "Inquisitorius") to aid in [hunting](/wiki/Jedi_hunter "Jedi hunter") down any survivors.[[44]](#cite_note-Vader_6-44) Sidious also ordered [Project Harvester](/wiki/Project_Harvester "Project Harvester") to gather up the [Force-sensitive](/wiki/Force-sensitive "Force-sensitive") children of the galaxy in the hopes of training them as dark side adherents to his will.[[45]](#cite_note-Harvester-45) Even so, some Jedi survived the purge and went on to join other religions. For instance, [Eeth Koth](/wiki/Eeth_Koth "Eeth Koth"), though he was later murdered by Vader, became a [priest](/wiki/Priest "Priest") in the [Church of the Ganthic Enlightenment](/wiki/Church_of_the_Ganthic_Enlightenment "Church of the Ganthic Enlightenment").[[46]](#cite_note-Vader_19-46) The Jedi teachings also lived on in the form of the [Church of the Force](/wiki/Church_of_the_Force "Church of the Force"), an underground faith of worshipers of the Force that revered the Jedi Order and sought to see the Jedi restored to the galaxy in the wake of the Emperor's purge.[[47]](#cite_note-TFA_VD-47)

However, despite the persecution of the Jedi Order, a number of religions existed and prospered under the Empire. The cult of the Central Isopter was among the more notable. The cult consisted of Force-sensitive members who received visions of great death and went to those sites to revel in the carnage.[[6]](#cite_note-Star_Wars_41-6) They were present at arguably one of the greatest religious catastrophes in galactic history when the holy city on Jedha was destroyed by the [Death Star](/wiki/Death_Star "Death Star").[[48]](#cite_note-Rogue_One-48) The blast eradicated centuries of religious history and destroyed a number of sacred sites.[[4]](#cite_note-R1Novel-4)

In the [Seswenna sector](/wiki/Seswenna_sector "Seswenna sector"), there were over three hundred distinct religious traditions with active practitioners, all of which were officially recognized by their Imperial administrator. [Admiral](/wiki/Admiral "Admiral") [Conan Antonio Motti](/wiki/Conan_Antonio_Motti "Conan Antonio Motti"), a native of the sector, described himself as a "man of faith."[[49]](#cite_note-AIR-49)

In the [Unknown Regions](/wiki/Unknown_Regions "Unknown Regions"), the [Kilji](/wiki/Kilji "Kilji") species followed the [Kilji way of order and enlightenment](/wiki/Kilji_way_of_order_and_enlightenment "Kilji way of order and enlightenment"), a series of ancient dictums which established that being should be "enlightened."[[50]](#cite_note-LE-50)

During the [early rebellion against the Galactic Empire](/wiki/Early_rebellion_against_the_Galactic_Empire "Early rebellion against the Galactic Empire"), evidence of the worship of the [Mortis](/wiki/Mortis "Mortis") [gods](/wiki/Force_wielder "Force wielder") was uncovered at the [Lothal Jedi Temple](/wiki/Lothal_Jedi_Temple "Lothal Jedi Temple"). Darth Sidious was aware of the Mortis deities, suggesting that both the Jedi and the Sith were at one time aware of and potentially worshiped them.[[51]](#cite_note-Wolves_and_a_Door-51) However, the temple was destroyed by Padawan [Ezra Bridger](/wiki/Ezra_Bridger "Ezra Bridger") after Sidious failed to infiltrate the [World Between Worlds](/wiki/World_Between_Worlds "World Between Worlds").[[52]](#cite_note-A_World_Between_Worlds-52)

During the [Galactic Civil War](/wiki/Galactic_Civil_War "Galactic Civil War"), [Jedi Masters](/wiki/Jedi_Master "Jedi Master") [Obi-Wan Kenobi](/wiki/Obi-Wan_Kenobi "Obi-Wan Kenobi") and [Yoda](/wiki/Yoda "Yoda") trained a young [Luke Skywalker](/wiki/Luke_Skywalker "Luke Skywalker") in the ways of the Jedi.[[34]](#cite_note-Episode_IV-34) Though Luke fought in the war for the [Rebel Alliance](/wiki/Alliance_to_Restore_the_Republic "Alliance to Restore the Republic"),[[2]](#cite_note-Episode_V-2) he also scoured the galaxy to recover lost Jedi lore. Much of the surviving records of the Jedi, which weren't obtained or destroyed by the Empire, were hoarded by criminals such as the [Hutt](/wiki/Hutt "Hutt") [crime lord](/wiki/Crime_lord "Crime lord") [Grakkus Jahibakti Tingi](/wiki/Grakkus_Jahibakti_Tingi "Grakkus Jahibakti Tingi").[[53]](#cite_note-Star_Wars_9-53) He also discovered a [school](/wiki/Jocasta_Nu%27s_cache "Jocasta Nu's cache") left behind by Jedi Master [Jocasta Nu](/wiki/Jocasta_Nu "Jocasta Nu"), which contained countless [Jedi holocrons](/wiki/Jedi_holocron "Jedi holocron").[[54]](#cite_note-Darth_Vader_10-54) After completing his [Jedi training](/wiki/Jedi_training "Jedi training"), Luke faced Darth Vader and the Emperor aboard the [DS-2 Death Star II Mobile Battle Station](/wiki/DS-2_Death_Star_II_Mobile_Battle_Station "DS-2 Death Star II Mobile Battle Station") during the [Battle of Endor](/wiki/Battle_of_Endor "Battle of Endor"); Vader ultimately sacrificed himself and destroyed the Emperor, at the cost of his own life, in order to save Luke, his son.[[55]](#cite_note-Episode_VI-55) The returned Anakin Skywalker was thus redeemed in the Force[[56]](#cite_note-Anakin_DB_Bio-56) while the Sith were left extinct[[55]](#cite_note-Episode_VI-55) as foretold by the ancient Jedi prophecy[[57]](#cite_note-TLJ_VD-57) of the [Chosen One](/wiki/Chosen_One "Chosen One").[[3]](#cite_note-Episode_III-3)

### New Republic Era[]

:   "*Like me, they are all that remains of the Jedi religion.*"
:   ―Luke Skywalker, to Rey[[11]](#cite_note-Episode_VIII-11)

After the battle at [Endor](/wiki/Endor "Endor"), Luke Skywalker set out to fulfill his promise to Yoda by restoring the Jedi Order. Alongside his [astromech droid](/wiki/Astromech_droid "Astromech droid")[[58]](#cite_note-R2-D2_DB-58) [R2-D2](/wiki/R2-D2 "R2-D2") and allies such as [Lor San Tekka](/wiki/Lor_San_Tekka "Lor San Tekka"),[[59]](#cite_note-Tekka_Databank-59) Skywalker traveled across the galaxy searching for long-forgotten Jedi lore to help him in his quest to resurrect the Jedi Order.[[60]](#cite_note-Luke_Databank-60) Even so, Skywalker was unsure whether to restore the Jedi Order or if it was best to leave it in the past. Ultimately, however, it would be the volatility and strength of his nephew, [Ben Solo](/wiki/Ben_Solo "Ben Solo"), in the Force and his desperate need for a teacher that convinced Skywalker to go through with restoring the Jedi Order.[[61]](#cite_note-Force_Databank-61) However, Solo was seduced to the dark side of the Force by [Snoke](/wiki/Snoke "Snoke"), becoming Kylo Ren, and the [Jedi Temple of Luke Skywalker](/wiki/Jedi_Temple_of_Luke_Skywalker "Jedi Temple of Luke Skywalker") was [destroyed](/wiki/Destruction_of_the_Jedi_Temple "Destruction of the Jedi Temple") and his students killed. Overwhelmed with guilt and shame, Skywalker exiled himself to the first Jedi Temple at Ahch-To, where, convinced that it was time for the Jedi religion to end, he planned to die, ending the Jedi Order with him as the last of the Jedi.[[11]](#cite_note-Episode_VIII-11)

Skywalker sacrificed himself at the [Battle of Crait](/wiki/Battle_of_Crait "Battle of Crait") to distract Kylo Ren, who had become the new [Supreme Leader](/wiki/Supreme_Leader "Supreme Leader") of the First Order after [assassinating](/wiki/Battle_of_Oetchi "Battle of Oetchi") Snoke with Rey, while Rey, who Skywalker acknowledged as a Jedi, helped the Resistance escape, ensuring that both their cause and the Jedi Order would live on.[[11]](#cite_note-Episode_VIII-11)

The [Sith Eternal](/wiki/Sith_Eternal "Sith Eternal"), a secret group of [Sith cultists](/wiki/Sith_cultist "Sith cultist") that continued the religious beliefs of the Sith after their extinction and sought to resurrect its tradition from [Exegol](/wiki/Exegol "Exegol"), revealed themselves to the galaxy in the [year](/wiki/35_ABY "35 ABY") following the battle at [Crait](/wiki/Crait "Crait"),[[63]](#cite_note-TROS_Date-63) having operated in secrecy for [decades](/wiki/Standard_decade "Standard decade") under their master, a [reborn](/wiki/Cloning "Cloning")[[64]](#cite_note-TROS_EE-64) Darth Sidious. Kylo Ren learned that he was the Sith Eternal's heir to their new Sith Empire, which the Sith cultists planned to establish through the [Final Order](/wiki/Final_Order "Final Order"), but after Ren renounced the dark side and reclaimed his birth identity as Ben Solo, Sidious instead targeted his granddaughter, Rey. Attempting to cultivate her as Empress through a [ritual](/wiki/Transference "Transference") in which he offered himself as a sacrifice to Rey during the [Battle of Exegol](/wiki/Battle_of_Exegol "Battle of Exegol"), Sidious urged his granddaughter to kill him in revenge for having her [mother](/wiki/Miramir "Miramir") and [father](/wiki/Dathan "Dathan") [assassinated](/wiki/Sith_assassin "Sith assassin").[[65]](#cite_note-Rey_Databank-65)

## Behind the scenes[]

:   "*The core thing to pass on is, you know, in talking about religion, is, you know, all religions say one thing—basically—which is love is the secret to the universe, which is compassion, which is love others, take care of others, help each other.*"
:   ―George Lucas[[68]](#cite_note-Happiness_interview-68)

[George Lucas](/wiki/George_Lucas "George Lucas"), the writer of the [original](/wiki/Original_trilogy "Original trilogy") and the [prequel](/wiki/Prequel_trilogy "Prequel trilogy") trilogies, said that, in regards to his *[Star Wars](/wiki/Star_Wars "Star Wars")* films and religion, the core teaching to pass on is that all religions in the real world essentially says one thing: compassion, or love. The Force and the Jedi religion, Lucas stated, were thus based on all religions in the real-world, as he constructed *Star Wars* mainly from [Methodism](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Methodism "wikipedia:Methodism")—(a sect of [Christianity](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Christianity "wikipedia:Christianity"),) [Islam](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Islam "wikipedia:Islam"), and [Judaism](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Judaism "wikipedia:Judaism")—and [Buddhism](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Buddhism "wikipedia:Buddhism"). Based on his beliefs, George Lucas explained, one of the the core values of *Star Wars*, one of the problems of the struggle in *Star Wars*, is about passion against compassion, which is greed against giving and letting go.[[68]](#cite_note-Happiness_interview-68) Compassion is defined by Lucas as joy, an everlasting emotion attained from being selfless[[69]](#cite_note-Happiness_speech-69)—the [light side of the Force](/wiki/Light_side_of_the_Force "Light side of the Force")[[70]](#cite_note-GL_Force-70)—and is one of two components of happiness.[[69]](#cite_note-Happiness_speech-69)
