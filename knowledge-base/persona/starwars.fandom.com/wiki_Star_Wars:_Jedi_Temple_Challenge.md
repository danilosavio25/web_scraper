***Star Wars: Jedi Temple Challenge*** is a children's game show developed by [Lucasfilm](/wiki/Lucasfilm "Lucasfilm"). It debuted on the [*Star Wars* Kids website](/wiki/StarWarsKids.com "StarWarsKids.com") and YouTube channel on [June 10](/wiki/June_10 "June 10"), [2020](/wiki/2020 "2020").[[1]](#cite_note-Premiere-1) The series puts its young contestants through a series of obstacles as part of a [Jedi training](/wiki/Jedi_training "Jedi training") course that tests them on the core [Jedi](/wiki/Jedi "Jedi") principles of strength, knowledge, and bravery. The show is hosted by [Ahmed Best](/wiki/Ahmed_Best "Ahmed Best"), who plays the character of [Jedi Master](/wiki/Jedi_Master "Jedi Master") [Kelleran Beq](/wiki/Kelleran_Beq "Kelleran Beq"), the mentor of the contestants. Best is joined by his droid companion, [AD-3](/wiki/AD-3 "AD-3"), voiced by [Mary Holland](/wiki/Mary_Holland "Mary Holland").[[3]](#cite_note-SW_Jedi_Temple_Challenge-3)

## Development[]

### Conception[]

:   "*I think what's the most important about the representation of someone like Kelleran Beq as far as being a person of color and the leader of this Temple—kind of like the Dean of Jedi—is it breaks down what's possible. It breaks down what you can do, and creates just a brand new world of possibilities out there.*"
:   ―Ahmed Best describing his character Jedi Master Kelleran Beq[[4]](#cite_note-Step_Inside-4)

*Jedi Temple Challenge* was the brainchild of Lucasfilm employees [Steve Blank](/wiki/Steve_Blank "Steve Blank"), [Scott Bromley](/wiki/Scott_Bromley "Scott Bromley"), and Mike Capoferri.[[4]](#cite_note-Step_Inside-4) During an interview on *[The Star Wars Show](/wiki/The_Star_Wars_Show "The Star Wars Show")*, co-star [Ahmed Best](/wiki/Ahmed_Best "Ahmed Best") confirmed that Blank, Bromley, and Capoferri had given him considerable flexibility in developing the main character [Jedi Master](/wiki/Jedi_Master "Jedi Master") [Kelleran Beq](/wiki/Kelleran_Beq "Kelleran Beq") including his history, mannerisms, and [lightsaber](/wiki/Lightsaber "Lightsaber") color. Best confirmed that Master Beq was related to [Achk Med-Beq](/wiki/Achk_Med-Beq "Achk Med-Beq"), whom Best had portrayed during a brief cameo appearance in [*Star Wars:* Episode II *Attack of the Clones*](/wiki/Star_Wars:_Episode_II_Attack_of_the_Clones "Star Wars: Episode II Attack of the Clones").[[5]](#cite_note-SW_Show_27_May_2020-5)

Master Beq's droid companion [AD-3](/wiki/AD-3 "AD-3") was voiced by co-star [Mary Holland](/wiki/Mary_Holland "Mary Holland"), with [Upright Citizens Brigade Theatre](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Upright_Citizens_Brigade_Theatre "wikipedia:Upright Citizens Brigade Theatre") veteran, artist and droid builder [Gordon Tarpley](/wiki/Gordon_Tarpley "Gordon Tarpley") providing the character's live performance.[[4]](#cite_note-Step_Inside-4) Holland described her character's main role as "providing them with someone who isn't so in awe of what they do that they lose all sense of reality and aren't able to consider other perspectives." Best added that AD-3's role was to serve as a friend to Master Beq that could be honest with him "without having any reverential fear or shyness."[[5]](#cite_note-SW_Show_27_May_2020-5)

### Production[]

:   "*Star Wars sets are very specific places unlike any other set you ever step onto. Immediately we all tried to figure out where we were. This could be a moon like Endor! And then the second round is a ship... we immediately try to start piercing the stories of where in the timeline you are, where in the universe you are, and it's one of those things where it just creates its own story as your're standing there.*"
:   ―Ahmed Best describing the sets for *Jedi Temple Challenge*[[4]](#cite_note-Step_Inside-4)

According to *[The Star Wars Show](/wiki/The_Star_Wars_Show "The Star Wars Show")* host [Anthony Carboni](/wiki/Anthony_Carboni "Anthony Carboni") and Lucasfilm writer [Kristin Baver](/wiki/Kristin_Baver "Kristin Baver"), *Jedi Temple Challenge* was modeled after the "big-production" kids' game shows of the 1980s and 1990s.[[5]](#cite_note-SW_Show_27_May_2020-5)[[4]](#cite_note-Step_Inside-4) The series was filmed on a Los Angeles sound stage. Each episode consisted of three rounds, with participants progressing through several obstacle courses in a faux forest to obtain [lightsaber](/wiki/Lightsaber "Lightsaber") parts, undergoing a quiz in a starship set, and enduring through a second set of obstacle courses to obtain [kyber crystals](/wiki/Kyber_crystal "Kyber crystal") from a Jedi Temple.[[4]](#cite_note-Step_Inside-4)

The set for the Jedi spaceship *[Athylia](/wiki/Athylia "Athylia")* was created by production designer Ryan Brett Puckett.[[5]](#cite_note-SW_Show_27_May_2020-5) Puckett incorporated buttons and switches from various *Star Wars* ship sets including the [hyperspace](/wiki/Hyperspace "Hyperspace") levers from the *[Millennium Falcon](/wiki/Millennium_Falcon "Millennium Falcon")* set used during the production of *[Solo: A Star Wars Story](/wiki/Solo:_A_Star_Wars_Story "Solo: A Star Wars Story")*.[[4]](#cite_note-Step_Inside-4) Best observed that the show's sets were designed to the same level of attention as *Star Wars* movie sets. Holland also stated that the show's production design was designed to make the cast feel as though they were stepping onto another planet.[[5]](#cite_note-SW_Show_27_May_2020-5)

Master Beq's droid companions, the protocol droid AD-3 and the astromech droid [LX-R5](/wiki/LX-R5 "LX-R5"), were operated by [Michael McMaster](/wiki/Michael_McMaster "Michael McMaster") and Gordon Tarpley respectively. Tarpley provided AD-3's live performance while Holland provided the droid's live vocals and commentary from a control room in the sound stage. Tarpley and Holland work together to synchronize the former's live-action performance with the latter's voice work.[[4]](#cite_note-Step_Inside-4) The short stories told by AD-3 in each episode were written by the author [Cavan Scott](/wiki/Cavan_Scott "Cavan Scott"),[[6]](#cite_note-Cavan_Tweet-6) who incorporated characters from the YouTube series *[The Star Wars Show](/wiki/The_Star_Wars_Show "The Star Wars Show")* including [mouse droid](/wiki/MSE-6_series_repair_droid "MSE-6 series repair droid") [CH-33Z](/wiki/CH-33Z "CH-33Z") and [Kevin](/wiki/Kevin "Kevin") the [Ewok](/wiki/Ewok "Ewok").[[7]](#cite_note-20_Easter_Eggs-7)

[Sam Witwer](/wiki/Sam_Witwer "Sam Witwer"), who had previously voiced [Maul](/wiki/Maul "Maul") in *[Star Wars: The Clone Wars](/wiki/Star_Wars:_The_Clone_Wars "Star Wars: The Clone Wars")* and *[Star Wars Rebels](/wiki/Star_Wars_Rebels "Star Wars Rebels")*, also voiced a [dark-side](/wiki/Dark_side_of_the_Force "Dark side of the Force") voice that tempted participants in the Jedi Temple round.[[4]](#cite_note-Step_Inside-4) According to sound editor [David W. Collins](/wiki/David_W._Collins "David W. Collins"), Witwer's dark-side voice was designed to move around in both "stereo and surround sound to convey his non-corporeal, spirit-like nature, and make him sound elsewhere, elusive."[[7]](#cite_note-20_Easter_Eggs-7)

The [Padawans](/wiki/Padawan "Padawan")' outfits were based on the costume of Jedi apprentice [Nedriss Narr](/wiki/Nedriss_Narr "Nedriss Narr"), a character from the [Disneyland](/wiki/Disneyland "Disneyland") stage show *[Jedi Training: Trials of the Temple](/wiki/Jedi_Training:_Trials_of_the_Temple "Jedi Training: Trials of the Temple")*. The Padawans' training sabers were also modeled after the Padawans' wooden sabers from *[The Clone Wars](/wiki/Star_Wars:_The_Clone_Wars "Star Wars: The Clone Wars")* episode "[A Test of Strength](/wiki/A_Test_of_Strength "A Test of Strength")."[[7]](#cite_note-20_Easter_Eggs-7)

## Promotion ‡[]

:   "*When they don't move on, the message is go back to the beginning and train more. It's not that you lost and get out of here. There's a lot more lesson in not moving on than moving on. It's no, you can do this. You just have to jump back and train a bit more. So I really like that message.*"
:   ―Ahmed Best discussing the show's message for children, during an interview with Anthony Carboni on *The Star Wars Show*[[5]](#cite_note-SW_Show_27_May_2020-5)

The show's first trailer was released on [May 27](/wiki/May_27 "May 27"), [2020](/wiki/2020 "2020") on the [Star Wars Kids](/wiki/Star_Wars_Kids "Star Wars Kids") YouTube channel and *[The Star Wars Show](/wiki/The_Star_Wars_Show "The Star Wars Show")*.[[8]](#cite_note-SWKidsYouTube-Star_Wars:_Jedi_Temple_Challenge_-_Trailer-8) Co-hosts Ahmed Best and Mary Holland, who played Master Kelleran Beq and his droid companion AD-3, also promoted the show during an interview with *The Star Wars Show* host Anthony Carboni. During the interview, Best confirmed that a key message of the show was motivating children to succeed and that failure could provide a lesson for children to take a step back and train until they were ready to move to the next level.[[5]](#cite_note-SW_Show_27_May_2020-5)

## Release and reception[]

The first two episodes of *Jedi Temple Challenge* were originally going be released on the [*Star Wars* Kids website](/wiki/StarWarsKids.com "StarWarsKids.com") and YouTube channel on [June 3](/wiki/June_3 "June 3"), [2020](/wiki/2020 "2020"). The series consists of ten episodes.[[1]](#cite_note-Premiere-1) However, Best later issued an Instagram post announcing that the series' release had been delayed until [June 10](/wiki/June_10 "June 10") due to the [George Floyd protests](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/George_Floyd_protests "wikipedia:George Floyd protests").[[9]](#cite_note-Ahmed_Instagram-9)[[10]](#cite_note-10) The series was also promoted by [StarWars.com](/wiki/StarWars.com "StarWars.com") staff writer Kristin Baver in an article published on June 10.[[4]](#cite_note-Step_Inside-4)
