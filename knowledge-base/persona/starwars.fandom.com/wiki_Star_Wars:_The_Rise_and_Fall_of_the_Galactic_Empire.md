***Star Wars: The Rise and Fall of the Galactic Empire*** is a reference book authored by Dr. [Chris Kempshall](/wiki/Chris_Kempshall "Chris Kempshall") and published by [Dorling Kindersley](/wiki/Dorling_Kindersley "Dorling Kindersley") on [July 4](/wiki/July_4 "July 4"), [2024](/wiki/2024 "2024") in the [United Kingdom](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/United_Kingdom "wikipedia:United Kingdom") and on [July 9](/wiki/July_9 "July 9") in the [United States](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/United_States "wikipedia:United States"). The book is written from the [in-universe](/wiki/In-universe "In-universe") perspective of [historian](/wiki/Historian "Historian") [Beaumont Kin](/wiki/Beaumont_Kin "Beaumont Kin").[[4]](#cite_note-Beaumont_Tweet-4)

## Publisher's summary[]

***A history of the dark times.***

*"So this is how liberty dies—with thunderous applause." —Senator [Padmé Amidala](/wiki/Padm%C3%A9_Amidala "Padmé Amidala")*

*When [Palpatine](/wiki/Darth_Sidious "Darth Sidious") declared the birth of his [new Empire](/wiki/Galactic_Empire "Galactic Empire"), he expected it would stand for millennia. Instead, it lasted only 24 years. This is the story of how a tyrannical regime rose from the ashes of democracy, ruled the galaxy with an iron fist, and then collapsed into dust.*

*It is a story of war and heroes, of the power of propaganda and the dangers of complacency. But most of all, it is a story of normal people trying to live their lives in the face of a brutal dictatorship.*

*From the ruthlessness of [Darth Vader](/wiki/Anakin_Skywalker "Anakin Skywalker")'s campaigns to the horrors of the [Tarkin Initiative](/wiki/Tarkin_Initiative "Tarkin Initiative"), this book offers fresh new insights into the dark entity at the core of* Star Wars*.*

## Contents[]

### Overview[]

*The Rise and Fall of the Galactic Empire* is written as an [eponymous in-universe book](/wiki/The_Rise_and_Fall_of_the_Galactic_Empire "The Rise and Fall of the Galactic Empire") by [Beaumont Kin](/wiki/Beaumont_Kin "Beaumont Kin"), a former lecturer at the [Lerct Historical Institute](/wiki/Lerct_Historical_Institute "Lerct Historical Institute") and a [captain](/wiki/Captain "Captain") in the [Resistance](/wiki/Resistance "Resistance")'s [Intelligence Division](/wiki/Resistance_Intelligence "Resistance Intelligence"). It was written by Kin in the aftermath of the [Battle of Exegol](/wiki/Battle_of_Exegol "Battle of Exegol") while he was leading the excavation of the [Sith temple](/wiki/Sith_Citadel "Sith Citadel") on [Exegol](/wiki/Exegol "Exegol"). The book uses the ['ABY-BBY' dating system](/wiki/%27ABY-BBY%27_dating_system "'ABY-BBY' dating system") on the rationale that the [Battle of Yavin](/wiki/Battle_of_Yavin "Battle of Yavin") marked a pivotal turning point in the history of the [Galactic Empire](/wiki/Galactic_Empire "Galactic Empire").

*Rise and Fall* is divided into four parts, with 25 chapters. The first part looks at the rise and consolidation of the Empire across the galaxy while the second part focuses on the expansion of Imperial rule across the galaxy. The third part focuses on the battle and campaigns of the [Galactic Civil War](/wiki/Galactic_Civil_War "Galactic Civil War") while the fourth part explores the fall of the Galactic Empire and its continuation via the [First Order](/wiki/First_Order "First Order").

### Part One: Rise and Consolidation[]

The first chapter is a biographical sketch of [Emperor](/wiki/Galactic_Emperor "Galactic Emperor") [Palpatine](/wiki/Darth_Sidious "Darth Sidious"). The chapter covers his [senatorial](/wiki/Senator "Senator") career, rise to [Supreme Chancellor](/wiki/Supreme_Chancellor "Supreme Chancellor") and Emperor, and his secret identity as a [Sith Lord](/wiki/Sith_Lord "Sith Lord"). The second chapter explores how Palpatine engineered the [Clone Wars](/wiki/Clone_Wars "Clone Wars") as a means of building a [large military](/wiki/Republic_Military "Republic Military") and eliminating [threats](/wiki/Mission_to_Mustafar "Mission to Mustafar") to his rule, [particular](/wiki/Great_Jedi_Purge "Great Jedi Purge") the Jedi. The third chapter discusses the consolidation of Imperial control over the [Imperial Senate](/wiki/Imperial_Senate "Imperial Senate"), which had become a rubber stamp by the time of its dissolution following the [Battle of Scarif](/wiki/Battle_of_Scarif "Battle of Scarif").

The fourth chapter looks at the machinery of the Imperial bureaucracy, focusing on [COMPNOR](/wiki/Commission_for_the_Preservation_of_the_New_Order "Commission for the Preservation of the New Order"), the [Imperial Security Bureau](/wiki/Imperial_Security_Bureau "Imperial Security Bureau") and [Imperial Intelligence](/wiki/Imperial_Intelligence "Imperial Intelligence"). The fifth chapter discusses Imperial society, focusing on how the Empire favored the [Core Worlds](/wiki/Core_Worlds "Core Worlds") over the other regions, [Loyalist](/wiki/Loyalist "Loyalist") worlds over [Separatist](/wiki/Confederacy_of_Independent_Systems "Confederacy of Independent Systems") worlds, and pursuing extractive economic policies aimed at expanding the [Imperial Military](/wiki/Imperial_Military "Imperial Military"). The sixth chapter examines the Empire's economic and industrial policies, discussing how the Empire channeled vast resources into its military and the two [Death](/wiki/Death_Star "Death Star") [Star](/wiki/DS-2_Death_Star_II_Mobile_Battle_Station "DS-2 Death Star II Mobile Battle Station") [superweapons](/wiki/Superweapon "Superweapon") at the expense of plundering numerous worlds and their populations including [Cynda](/wiki/Cynda "Cynda"), [Lothal](/wiki/Lothal "Lothal") and [Ilum](/wiki/Ilum "Ilum"). Kin also explores how certain companies such as [Kuat Drive Yards](/wiki/Kuat_Drive_Yards "Kuat Drive Yards") and [Sienar Fleet Systems](/wiki/Sienar_Fleet_Systems "Sienar Fleet Systems") benefitted from Imperial rule.

### Part Two: Expansion and Oppression[]

The second part looks at how the Empire expanded its rule across the galaxy and perpetrated oppressive policies including anti-alien [xenophobia](/wiki/Xenophobia "Xenophobia"), atrocities, and genocide. The seventh chapter discusses the Empire's expansion strategies which included stripping worlds of their [legacy status](/wiki/Legacy_world "Legacy world") on the grounds they collaborated with the Separatists, forced deportations to facilitate the resource exploitations of worlds, and military interventions on worlds such as [Ryloth](/wiki/Ryloth "Ryloth") and [Mimban](/wiki/Mimban "Mimban"). The eighth chapter discusses the structure and machinery of the Imperial Military. Key topics covered in the chapter include the [Imperial Navy](/wiki/Imperial_Navy "Imperial Navy"), [Imperial Army](/wiki/Imperial_Army "Imperial Army"), [Imperial Starfighter Corps](/wiki/Imperial_Starfighter_Corps "Imperial Starfighter Corps"), [Stormtroopers](/wiki/Stormtrooper "Stormtrooper") and [Imperial Academy](/wiki/Imperial_Academy "Imperial Academy") system, which originated in the institutions of the [Galactic Republic](/wiki/Galactic_Republic "Galactic Republic").

The ninth chapter profiles several prominent Imperial political and military figures including [Darth Vader](/wiki/Anakin_Skywalker "Anakin Skywalker"), [Grand Vizier](/wiki/Grand_Vizier "Grand Vizier") [Mas Amedda](/wiki/Mas_Amedda "Mas Amedda"), [Sly Moore](/wiki/Sly_Moore "Sly Moore"), the [Imperial Ruling Council](/wiki/Imperial_Ruling_Council "Imperial Ruling Council"), [Grand Moff](/wiki/Grand_Moff "Grand Moff") [Tarkin](/wiki/Wilhuff_Tarkin "Wilhuff Tarkin"), [Grand Admiral](/wiki/Grand_Admiral "Grand Admiral") [Thrawn](/wiki/Mitth%27raw%27nuruodo "Mitth'raw'nuruodo"), [Grand General](/wiki/Grand_General "Grand General") [Cassio Tagge](/wiki/Cassio_Tagge "Cassio Tagge"), [Admirals](/wiki/Admiral "Admiral") [Kendal Ozzel](/wiki/Kendal_Ozzel "Kendal Ozzel") and [Firmus Piett](/wiki/Firmus_Piett "Firmus Piett"), [General](/wiki/General "General") [Maximilian Veers](/wiki/Maximilian_Veers "Maximilian Veers") and [Moff](/wiki/Moff "Moff") [Tiaan Jerjerrod](/wiki/Tiaan_Jerjerrod "Tiaan Jerjerrod"). Vader is identified as the [Jedi Knight](/wiki/Jedi_Knight "Jedi Knight") [Anakin Skywalker](/wiki/Anakin_Skywalker "Anakin Skywalker") and the father of [Luke Skywalker](/wiki/Luke_Skywalker "Luke Skywalker") and [Leia Organa](/wiki/Leia_Skywalker_Organa_Solo "Leia Skywalker Organa Solo"). The chapter also discusses a clash between the [Tarkin Doctrine](/wiki/Tarkin_Doctrine "Tarkin Doctrine") which favored developing weapons with "great firepower" such as the [Death Star](/wiki/Death_Star "Death Star") and [Star Destroyers](/wiki/Star_Destroyer "Star Destroyer") over Thrawn's doctrine which favored the development of advanced starfighters. While recognizing Thawn's skills as a military tactician, Kin criticizes his targeting of civilians at [Batonn](/wiki/Batonn "Batonn") and [Lothal](/wiki/Lothal "Lothal").

The tenth chapter discusses Emperor Palpatine's efforts to eradicate the Jedi and their legacy. These policies included the destruction of Jedi-themed toys and paraphernalia, [propaganda](/wiki/Propaganda "Propaganda") films such as *[The Last Betrayal](/wiki/The_Last_Betrayal "The Last Betrayal")* and *[Disorders of Magnitude](/wiki/Disorders_of_Magnitude "Disorders of Magnitude")* and the creation of a secretive force of [Jedi hunters](/wiki/Jedi_hunter "Jedi hunter") known as the [Inquisitorius](/wiki/Inquisitorius "Inquisitorius"). Kin attempts to shed light on the Inquisitors based on Imperial records and the accounts of Princess Organa and the [Spectres](/wiki/Spectres "Spectres") rebel cell. Kin observes that the Inquisitors shifted to kidnapping [Force-sensitive](/wiki/Force-sensitive "Force-sensitive") children after the number of Jedi declined. He also ponders whether the sighting of an [Inquisitor](/wiki/Marrok "Marrok") on [Corellia](/wiki/Corellia "Corellia") suggests the survival of the Inquisitorius following the Empire's fall.

The eleventh chapter discusses the Imperial legal system, with Kin arguing that it served the interests of the state rather than Imperial citizens. Kin looks at the [Imperial Legal Code](/wiki/Imperial_Legal_Code "Imperial Legal Code"), which graded crimes from [Class Five infractions](/wiki/Class_Five_infraction "Class Five infraction") such as property damage to [Class One infractions](/wiki/Class_One_Offense "Class One Offense") such as rebellion, sedition, assassinating Imperial officials and piracy. Kin also argues that the Imperial penal system served as pool of forced labor for Imperial industry and the war machine. Kin also briefly mentions Vader's use of bounty hunters and mercenaries and the Empire's tolerance of crime syndicates on the condition that they did not get in their way.

The twelfth chapter looks at prejudice and discrimination in the Empire, focusing on anti-alien xenophobia with some references to [anti-droid sentiment](/wiki/Anti-droid_sentiment "Anti-droid sentiment") and misogyny. Kin argues that the Empire exploited existing tensions between humans and aliens, and anti-droid sentiment that arose from the [Clone Wars](/wiki/Clone_Wars "Clone Wars") as a means of "divide and conquer." He also argues that the Empire promoted the notion that humans were the greatest species in the galaxy. Kin discusses a few case studies of Imperial xenophobic policies including the creation of several non-human control zones on [Coruscant](/wiki/Coruscant "Coruscant"), the imposition of [Galactic Basic Standard](/wiki/Galactic_Basic_Standard "Galactic Basic Standard") on [Talus](/wiki/Talus "Talus")' education system at the expense of aliens, and the [enslavement](/wiki/Slavery "Slavery") of the [Wookiees](/wiki/Wookiee "Wookiee") of [Kashyyyk](/wiki/Kashyyyk "Kashyyyk") on the grounds that the latter supported the Jedi. Kin observes that the reptilian [Trandoshans](/wiki/Trandoshan "Trandoshan") experienced discrimination but were unsympathetic to the plight of the Wookiees due to the long-standing rivalry between the two species. Kin also discusses how anti-alien educational and job discrimination led many aliens to pursue careers as criminals and bounty hunters, perpetuating negative media stereotypes in the Empire.

The thirteenth chapter discusses the Imperial use of atrocities and genocide as a means of suppressing resistance and acquiring the resources of worlds and their inhabitants. Kin discusses several case studies of violent Imperial suppression including [Lothal](/wiki/Lothal "Lothal"), [Aldhani](/wiki/Aldhani "Aldhani"), [Kamino](/wiki/Kamino "Kamino"), [Kashyyyk](/wiki/Kashyyyk "Kashyyyk"), [Wobani](/wiki/Wobani "Wobani"), [Lasan](/wiki/Lasan "Lasan"), [Ghorman](/wiki/Ghorman "Ghorman"), [Mandalore](/wiki/Mandalore "Mandalore") and [Alderaan](/wiki/Alderaan "Alderaan"). Kin describes the [destruction](/wiki/Destruction_of_Alderaan "Destruction of Alderaan") of Alderaan as the culmination of the Empire's intolerance of resistance and disregard of sentient life. Kin ends by discussing efforts by various survivors and scholars including [J0-8O](/wiki/J0-8O "J0-8O"), [Pri'am Q'asl](/wiki/Pri%27am_Q%27asl "Pri'am Q'asl"), [Xugg Twam](/wiki/Xugg_Twam "Xugg Twam"), and [Froh Loeaw](/wiki/Froh_Loeaw "Froh Loeaw") to preserve the memory of Imperial atrocities, as well as efforts by the inhabitants of [Bosph](/wiki/Bosph "Bosph") to "dis-remember" the Galactic Empire.

### Part Three: The Galactic Civil War[]

The fourteenth chapter discusses the opening skirmishes of the [Galactic Civil War](/wiki/Galactic_Civil_War "Galactic Civil War"). While the official history regards Senator [Mon Mothma](/wiki/Mon_Mothma "Mon Mothma")'s denunciation of the Emperor and [call to Rebellion](/wiki/Declaration_of_Rebellion "Declaration of Rebellion") as the foundation of the [Rebel Alliance](/wiki/Alliance_to_Restore_the_Republic "Alliance to Restore the Republic"), Kin seeks to highlight the role of other early rebel figures and movements such as [Luthen Rael](/wiki/Luthen_Rael "Luthen Rael"), [neo-Separatist](/wiki/Neo-Separatist_Coalition "Neo-Separatist Coalition") [Anto Kreegyr](/wiki/Anto_Kreegyr "Anto Kreegyr") and [Saw Gerrera](/wiki/Saw_Gerrera "Saw Gerrera")'s [Partisans](/wiki/Partisans "Partisans"). Kin also argues that heavy-handed Imperial actions on [Ivera X](/wiki/Ivera_X "Ivera X"), [Arieli](/wiki/Arieli "Arieli") and [Dinwa Prime](/wiki/Dinwa_Prime "Dinwa Prime") bolstered resistance.

The fifteenth chapter discusses Imperial fleet and starfighter tactics during the conflict. Kin focuses on the conflict between the "firepower" lobby and critics like Thrawn, who advocated greater investment in the Empire's starfighter capabilities, culminating in the [TIE Defender](/wiki/TIE/d_%22Defender%22_Multi-Role_Starfighter "TIE/d \"Defender\" Multi-Role Starfighter") programme. He argues that the destruction of the [TIE defender factory](/wiki/Imperial_Armory_Complex "Imperial Armory Complex") on Lothal and the resultant loss of funding undermined Thrawn's vision of a more flexible Imperial Navy capable of precision operations. This development allowed the "firepower" lobby to dominate Imperial naval strategy. Kin also critically analyzes Thrawn's tactics during the [Battle of Atollon](/wiki/Battle_of_Atollon "Battle of Atollon"), criticizing Thrawn's decision to appoint [Admiral](/wiki/Admiral "Admiral") [Kassius Konstantine](/wiki/Kassius_Konstantine "Kassius Konstantine") and [Governor](/wiki/Governor "Governor") [Arihnda Pryce](/wiki/Arihnda_Pryce "Arihnda Pryce") to command positions.

The sixteenth chapter covers the [Tarkin Doctrine](/wiki/Tarkin_Doctrine "Tarkin Doctrine"), the [first Death Star](/wiki/DS-1_Orbital_Battle_Station "DS-1 Orbital Battle Station") and the rebel plot to [steal](/wiki/Battle_of_Scarif "Battle of Scarif") the [Death Star plans](/wiki/Death_Star_plans "Death Star plans"). According to Kin, the Tarkin Doctrine was predicated on "ruling through the fear of force rather than force itself." The Death Star was the culmination of the Tarkin Doctrine but its [destruction of Alderaan](/wiki/Destruction_of_Alderaan "Destruction of Alderaan") and the [Battle of Yavin](/wiki/Battle_of_Yavin "Battle of Yavin") undermined its purpose and emboldened the Rebellion.

The seventeenth chapter covers Imperial military operations following the Battle of Yavin. Kin discusses the rivalry between [Grand General](/wiki/Grand_General "Grand General") [Cassio Tagge](/wiki/Cassio_Tagge "Cassio Tagge") and Darth Vader for leadership of the Imperial Military, which culminated in the latter's triumph. Kin also discusses Vader's obsession with finding his son [Luke Skywalker](/wiki/Luke_Skywalker "Luke Skywalker") and recruiting him to the dark side. Kin also covers the fate of the remnants of Thrawn's [Seventh Fleet](/wiki/Seventh_Fleet_(Galactic_Empire) "Seventh Fleet (Galactic Empire)") particularly [Vice Admiral](/wiki/Vice_Admiral "Vice Admiral") [Corf Ferno](/wiki/Corf_Ferno "Corf Ferno") and the development of Imperial special forces units particularly Admiral [Garrick Versio](/wiki/Garrick_Versio "Garrick Versio")'s [Inferno Squad](/wiki/Inferno_Squad "Inferno Squad"), which eliminated the [Dreamers](/wiki/Dreamers "Dreamers"), an offshoot of the Partisans.

The eighteenth chapter focuses on the [Battle of Hoth](/wiki/Battle_of_Hoth "Battle of Hoth") and its impact on Imperial morale. In the years leading up to [Hoth](/wiki/Hoth "Hoth"), the [Joint Chiefs of the Imperial Military](/wiki/Joint_Chiefs_of_the_Imperial_Military "Joint Chiefs of the Imperial Military") had sought to disrupt the Rebel Alliance's ability to supply and sustain its own military war efforts. This had led to a successful Imperial military operation to disrupt Rebel supply lines through the [Derra system](/wiki/Derra_system "Derra system") in the Outer Rim, stripping the Rebellion of their manufacturing abilities. While the Rebel Alliances suffered significant casualties during the Battle of Hoth, Kin argues that Darth Vader's decision to pursue the *[Millennium Falcon](/wiki/Millennium_Falcon "Millennium Falcon")* (due to the mistaken belief that his son Skywalker was aboard the ship) prevented the Empire from hunting down the rest of the [rebel fleet](/wiki/Rebel_Alliance_Navy "Rebel Alliance Navy"). Kin concludes that the failure to destroy the Rebellion undermined Imperial military morale and prolonged the war.

The nineteenth chapter focuses on the [Crimson Dawn](/wiki/Crimson_Dawn "Crimson Dawn") crime syndicate's attempt to [destroy](/wiki/War_against_the_Sith "War against the Sith") the Sith. Drawing upon [The Archivist](/wiki/The_Archivist "The Archivist")'s research, Kin argues that Crimson Dawn's leader [Qi'ra](/wiki/Qi%27ra "Qi'ra") was continuing her predecessor and former [Sith apprentice](/wiki/Sith_apprentice "Sith apprentice") [Maul](/wiki/Maul "Maul")'s conflict with the Sith. Qi'ra's plot involved creating a loose alliance of criminals and recruiting Imperial personnel as sleeper agents. Putting her plans into action, the Crimson Dawn [stirred up](/wiki/Syndicate_War "Syndicate War") trouble in the galactic criminal underworld and activated its Imperial sleeper agents to wage war on multiple fronts. This conflict culminated in a [showdown](/wiki/Battle_at_Amaxine_Station "Battle at Amaxine Station") between Crimson Dawn's forces and the two Sith Lords Palpatine and Vader, which ended with the decimation of Crimson Dawn. Qi'ra vanished from the historical records. Following Crimson Dawn's defeat, the Empire initiated a purge of its armed forces, which robbed the Empire of many experienced soldiers, officers and pilots. This had the effect of reducing morale and encouraging defections to the Rebellion.

The twentieth chapter focuses on the [Battle of Endor](/wiki/Battle_of_Endor "Battle of Endor"). Kin argues that the Empire engineered the Battle of Endor in order to break a stalemate in the [Galactic Civil War](/wiki/Galactic_Civil_War "Galactic Civil War"). The Imperial plan involved using a visit by the Emperor to the [second Death Star](/wiki/DS-2_Death_Star_II_Mobile_Battle_Station "DS-2 Death Star II Mobile Battle Station") to lure the Rebel fleet into a trap consisting of the operational superweapon and elements of the Imperial fleet. Drawing upon Imperial military records and the unpublished memoir *[Hero of the Empire](/wiki/Hero_of_the_Empire "Hero of the Empire")*, Kin found there was significant disagreement among Imperial military commanders including Admirals Piett, [Jhared Montferrat](/wiki/Jhared_Montferrat "Jhared Montferrat"), Versio and Vice-Admiral Ferno over tactics. Kin credits the Rebel victory at Endor to the second Death Star's failure to destroy the Rebel flagship *[Home One](/wiki/Home_One "Home One")*, Imperial ground forces underestimating the threat posed by the native [Ewoks](/wiki/Ewok "Ewok"), the destruction of the Imperial flagship *[Executor](/wiki/Executor "Executor")*, and the Emperor restraining his own military forces due to his obsession with converting Luke Skywalker to the dark side. Ultimately, the Emperor's gambit failed and led to his first death and the beginning of the end for the Empire.

### Part Four: Fall and Continuation[]

The twenty-first chapter deals with the fragmentation of the Empire that occurred after the Battle of Endor and [Operation: Cinder](/wiki/Operation:_Cinder "Operation: Cinder"). Several Imperial officials such as Governor [Ecressys](/wiki/Ecressys "Ecressys"), Moff [Ubrik Adelhard](/wiki/Ubrik_Adelhard "Ubrik Adelhard") established fiefdoms. Other individuals such as [Grand Moff](/wiki/Grand_Moff "Grand Moff") [Plorest](/wiki/Plorest "Plorest") and Admiral [Rolas](/wiki/Rolas "Rolas") proclaimed themselves as Emperor but failed to rally the Empire. Several individuals including [Ederlatth Nataasias Pallopides](/wiki/Ederlatth_Nataasias_Pallopides "Ederlatth Nataasias Pallopides") also claimed to be related to the Emperor. Kin also discusses several worlds targeted by Operation Cinder including [Naboo](/wiki/Naboo "Naboo"), [Vardos](/wiki/Vardos "Vardos"), [Nacronis](/wiki/Nacronis "Nacronis"), [Burnin Konn](/wiki/Burnin_Konn "Burnin Konn") and [Mon Cala](/wiki/Mon_Cala "Mon Cala"). Apart from Mon Cala, most of these targeted worlds were Imperial loyalist worlds. Kin theorizes that the Emperor had no intention of abdicating and had come up with a [posthumous](/wiki/Contingency "Contingency") to punish "disloyal" citizens as a means of purging the Empire of "weakness." Kin speculates that the Emperor wanted a tougher and more ruthless Empire to arise from the ashes of the "Old Empire."

The twenty-second chapter focuses on the events leading up to the pivotal [Battle of Jakku](/wiki/Battle_of_Jakku "Battle of Jakku") including the emergence of Grand Admiral [Rae Sloane](/wiki/Rae_Sloane "Rae Sloane") and the enigmatic shadow master [Gallius Rax](/wiki/Gallius_Rax "Gallius Rax"), the [New Republic](/wiki/New_Republic "New Republic")'s [Project Starhawk](/wiki/Project_Starhawk "Project Starhawk"), the [Battle for Kuat Drive Yards](/wiki/Defense_of_Kuat "Defense of Kuat"), the [Akiva Summit](/wiki/Akiva_Summit "Akiva Summit") and the [attack on Chandrila](/wiki/Attack_on_Chandrila "Attack on Chandrila"). Possessing the Empire's last known remaining Super Star Destroyer *[Ravager](/wiki/Ravager "Ravager")*, Sloane and Rax were able to gain command of most of the Imperial forces. While Grand Vizier Amedda was officially the Emperor's second-in-line, he was merely a powerless figurehead. Feigning peace talks with the New Republic on Chandrila, Rax sent Sloane to meet with [Chancellor](/wiki/Chancellor_(New_Republic) "Chancellor (New Republic)") Mon Mothma during [Liberation Day](/wiki/Liberation_Day_(New_Republic) "Liberation Day (New Republic)"). However, the peace talks were disrupted by several liberated Imperial prisoners, who had been secretly reprogrammed into assassins. Based on his research, Kin is unsure about Sloane's knowledge of the attempted assassination but concedes that she was not in command of the Empire on Chandrila and Jakku.

Kin also covers the Battle of Jakku; covering Rax's military leadership, the space and ground battles between the New Republic and Empire, and New Republic pilot [Norra Wexley](/wiki/Norra_Wexley "Norra Wexley") and Sloane's showdown with Rax. While Rax's motives are unclear, Kin theorizes that the warlord had engineered a showdown on Jakku in order to destroy the assembled Imperial and New Republic forces by using technology within an Imperial facility known as the [Jakku Observatory](/wiki/Jakku_Observatory "Jakku Observatory") to disrupt Jakku's core and destroy the planet. Kin speculates that Sloane and other Imperial personnel and forces who vanished following the Battle of Jakku [fled](/wiki/Unknown_Regions_Imperial_remnant "Unknown Regions Imperial remnant") into the [unknown Regions](/wiki/Unknown_Regions "Unknown Regions"). Following the Imperial defeat on Jakku, Amedda signed the [Galactic Concordance](/wiki/Galactic_Concordance "Galactic Concordance") with the New Republic; ending the Galactic Civil War and ushering New Republic rule over the galaxy.

The twenty-third chapter "Truth and Reconciliation" examines New Republic policies aimed at rebuilding galactic society following the Civil War. Key priorities included delivering justice for victims of Imperial crimes, prosecuting Imperial war criminals, overhauling Imperial legislation to ensure fairness, and placing curbs on executive power to prevent a repeat of the circumstances that led to the rise of the Galactic Empire. Besides helping former prisoners who were liberated from Imperial prison, labor and concentration camps, the New Republic government was faced with the challenge of managing countless former Imperial officials and military personnel. [New Republic Intelligence](/wiki/New_Republic_Intelligence "New Republic Intelligence") developed a system for classifying ex-Imperials on the basis of their value and risk. To aid with reintegration, the [New Republic Amnesty Program](/wiki/New_Republic_Amnesty_Program "New Republic Amnesty Program") was established on Coruscant to help reprogram ex-Imperials.

The twenty-fourth chapter "Complacency and Appeasement" discusses the interwar period between the Galactic Civil War and the [First Order-Resistance War](/wiki/First_Order-Resistance_War "First Order-Resistance War"). Within the New Republic government, a split emerged between the combatants of the Rebel Alliance and politicians who had not been involved in the Civil War, which evolved into the [Populists](/wiki/Populists "Populists") and [Centrists](/wiki/Centrists "Centrists"). The former believed that the best way to maintain peace was through vigilance and were suspicious of ex-Imperials in positions of government. The latter regarded the ex-rebel faction as paranoid warmongers who were unable to abandon their military past.

Kin also critiques the effectiveness of the New Republic's ex-Imperial reintegration programme, pointing out that several "rehabilitated" former Imperials were secretly colluding with [Imperial Remnants](/wiki/Imperial_Remnants "Imperial Remnants") such as the [First Order](/wiki/First_Order "First Order"). The Centrists acted as proxy agents for the [First Order](/wiki/First_Order "First Order"), working to undermine the New Republic government. Kin also criticized Mon Mothma's [Military Disarmament Act](/wiki/Military_Disarmament_Act "Military Disarmament Act") for leaving the New Republic unprepared for the First Order. Kin describes the return of [Grand Admiral](/wiki/Grand_Admiral "Grand Admiral") [Thrawn](/wiki/Mitth%27raw%27nuruodo "Mitth'raw'nuruodo") as turning point in the balance of power between [neo-Imperial](/wiki/Neo-Imperialism "Neo-Imperialism") forces and New Republic elements urging vigilance. He mentions that [University of Coruscant](/wiki/University_of_Coruscant "University of Coruscant") historian [Chalm Plesk](/wiki/Chalm_Plesk "Chalm Plesk")'s [work](/wiki/The_Last_War_Against_the_Galactic_Empire:_On_Deployment_with_the_New_Republic_Fleet "The Last War Against the Galactic Empire: On Deployment with the New Republic Fleet") on the military [campaign](/wiki/Campaign_against_Thrawn%27s_forces "Campaign against Thrawn's forces") waged against Thrawn's forces was still classified by the time of the [Hosnian Cataclysm](/wiki/Hosnian_Cataclysm "Hosnian Cataclysm").

The twenty-fifth chapter focuses on the history of the [First Order](/wiki/First_Order "First Order") and [Final Order](/wiki/Final_Order "Final Order"), the two main successor states to the Galactic Empire. Kin discusses the leadership of the First Order including Grand Admiral [Sloane](/wiki/Sloane "Sloane"), [Commandant](/wiki/Commandant "Commandant") [Brendol Hux](/wiki/Brendol_Hux "Brendol Hux"), [Captain](/wiki/Captain "Captain") [Enric Pryde](/wiki/Enric_Pryde "Enric Pryde") and [General](/wiki/General "General") [Armitage Hux](/wiki/Armitage_Hux "Armitage Hux") but admits that the source material is scarce. Kin also identifies [Supreme Leader](/wiki/Supreme_Leader "Supreme Leader") [Snoke](/wiki/Snoke "Snoke") as a proxy for the resurgent Palpatine and the product of [cloning](/wiki/Cloning "Cloning") experiments. With the financial support of Centrist worlds and the [Corporate Sector Authority](/wiki/Corporate_Sector_Authority "Corporate Sector Authority"), the First Order amassed significant manufacturing and military resources. Kin believes that the First Order's military doctrine was influenced by the Old Empire's "firepower" lobby. Kin also discusses the return of Emperor Palpatine and the events leading to his defeat during the [Battle of Exegol](/wiki/Battle_of_Exegol "Battle of Exegol"), which culminated in the [collapse](/wiki/Uprising_against_the_First_Order "Uprising against the First Order") of both the Final and First Orders.

Kin concludes his book by sharing his personal experiences during the [First Order-Resistance War](/wiki/First_Order-Resistance_War "First Order-Resistance War") and exhorting readers to continue resisting authoritarian and totalitarian regimes such as the Galactic Empire and First Order. He ends by sharing the story of [Maarva Andor](/wiki/Maarva_Carassi_Andor "Maarva Carassi Andor") urging the people of [Ferrix](/wiki/Ferrix "Ferrix") to [fight](/wiki/Ferrix_Riot "Ferrix Riot") the Empire.

## Development[]

### Concept[]

*Star Wars: The Rise and Fall of the Galactic Empire* was written by Doctor [Chris Kempshall](/wiki/Chris_Kempshall "Chris Kempshall").[[1]](#cite_note-DKUS-1) The book was edited by [David Fentiman](/wiki/David_Fentiman "David Fentiman"), and Kempshall received guidance from [Brett Rector](/wiki/Brett_Rector "Brett Rector") and the [Story Group](/wiki/Lucasfilm_Story_Group "Lucasfilm Story Group") at [Lucasfilm](/wiki/Lucasfilm "Lucasfilm"), with additional support from various loved ones and *[Star Wars](/wiki/Star_Wars "Star Wars")* authors,[[5]](#cite_note-Pinned_Tweet-5) such as [Kristin Baver](/wiki/Kristin_Baver "Kristin Baver"), [Amy Richau](/wiki/Amy_Richau "Amy Richau"), [Amy Ratcliffe](/wiki/Amy_Ratcliffe "Amy Ratcliffe"), [Clayton Sandell](/wiki/Clayton_Sandell "Clayton Sandell"), [Adam Christopher](/wiki/Adam_Christopher "Adam Christopher"), [Jason Fry](/wiki/Jason_Fry "Jason Fry"), and [Cole Horton](/wiki/Cole_Horton "Cole Horton").[[6]](#cite_note-Helpers_Tweet-6) Kempshall said that his work was inspired by [William L. Shirer's](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/William_L._Shirer "wikipedia:William L. Shirer") seminal 1960 book [*The Rise and Fall of the Third Reich*](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/The_Rise_and_Fall_of_the_Third_Reich "wikipedia:The Rise and Fall of the Third Reich"), a small bust of *Star Wars*' [Emperor](/wiki/Galactic_Emperor "Galactic Emperor") [Palpatine](/wiki/Darth_Sidious "Darth Sidious"), and [Kristin Baver](/wiki/Kristin_Baver "Kristin Baver")'s [2021](/wiki/2021 "2021") book *[Skywalker: A Family at War](/wiki/Skywalker:_A_Family_at_War "Skywalker: A Family at War")*.[[7]](#cite_note-Holy_trinity_Tweet-7) According to Kempshall, *Rise and Fall* would not have existed without *Skywalker: A Family at War*,[[8]](#cite_note-Kempshall_Skywalker_Family-8) and author [Kristin Baver](/wiki/Kristin_Baver "Kristin Baver") is referenced in *Rise and Fall* as an in-universe [Skywalker family](/wiki/Skywalker_family "Skywalker family") biographer named [Kitrin Braves](/wiki/Kitrin_Braves "Kitrin Braves").[[9]](#cite_note-Kristin_Tweet-9) After the contract for the book was signed, Kempshall had a conversation with [Michael Siglain](/wiki/Michael_Siglain "Michael Siglain") of [Disney–Lucasfilm Press](/wiki/Disney%E2%80%93Lucasfilm_Press "Disney–Lucasfilm Press") at [*Star Wars* Celebration](/wiki/Celebration_Europe_(2023) "Celebration Europe (2023)") in [London](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/London "wikipedia:London") discussing topics such as the book's structure and a sample chapter Siglain had recently read.[[10]](#cite_note-Correction_Tweet-10)

As recounted by [Lucasfilm Story Group](/wiki/Lucasfilm_Story_Group "Lucasfilm Story Group") member [Pablo Hidalgo](/wiki/Pablo_Hidalgo "Pablo Hidalgo"), who worked with Kempshall on the book, the author set out with an ambitious plan.[[11]](#cite_note-PabloCommentary-11) Kempshall, meanwhile, stated that *The Rise and Fall* would be "just a bunch of daft ideas in my head" if the story group and other authorities had not helped him.[[12]](#cite_note-Kempshall_Response-12)

### Connections to then-unreleased material[]

:   "*Did they tell you anything about* [Andor] *season 2 while you were writing?*"  
    "*Yes but nothing more than I needed to know at the time. Which is less than you might think but enough for me to get the right sense and feel of things.*"
:   ―A fan and Chris Kempshall, via Bluesky[[13]](#cite_note-KempshallNeedTo-13)

*Rise and Fall* also ended up connecting well with[[14]](#cite_note-Chris_Tom-14) the then yet-to-be announced *[Star Wars: Reign of the Empire](/wiki/Star_Wars:_Reign_of_the_Empire "Star Wars: Reign of the Empire")* trilogy[[15]](#cite_note-Gizmodo_Reign-15) according to editor [Tom Hoeler](/wiki/Tom_Hoeler "Tom Hoeler").[[14]](#cite_note-Chris_Tom-14) Furthermore, Hoeler noticed details in *Rise and Fall* that he realized would work well with *[Reign of the Empire: Edge of the Abyss](/wiki/Reign_of_the_Empire:_Edge_of_the_Abyss "Reign of the Empire: Edge of the Abyss")*, promising Kempshall that *Rise and Fall* content had been brought up as ideas to be fleshed out in that novel,[[16]](#cite_note-Rise_Inspired-16) much to Kempshall's joy.[[17]](#cite_note-Kempshall_Love_That-17)

As confirmed after the season's premiere, *The Rise and Fall* also contains various nods to the then-upcoming [*Andor* Season 2](/wiki/Andor_Season_2 "Andor Season 2").[[18]](#cite_note-Kempshall_Season2-18) According to Kempshall, he was only told what he "needed to know at the time."[[13]](#cite_note-KempshallNeedTo-13) As confirmed by Kempshall,[[19]](#cite_note-Kempshall_Ghorman-19) the mention of "a significant breakthrough" of resources for the Death Star project[[20]](#cite_note-TRAFOTGE-20) was a reference to the [Ghorman](/wiki/Ghorman "Ghorman")-sourced [kalkite](/wiki/Kalkite "Kalkite")[[21]](#cite_note-Messenger-21) discussed in *Andor* Season 2.[[19]](#cite_note-Kempshall_Ghorman-19) The section also contains a nod to Tarkin actor [Peter Cushing](/wiki/Peter_Cushing "Peter Cushing")[[22]](#cite_note-KempshellCushing-22) being unable to wear his provided [boots](/wiki/Boot "Boot") on the set of [*Star Wars*: Episode IV *A New Hope*](/wiki/Star_Wars:_Episode_IV_A_New_Hope "Star Wars: Episode IV A New Hope")[[23]](#cite_note-Set_Piece-23) by establishing that, in-universe, Tarkin had a great many boots directed to the Death Star.[[20]](#cite_note-TRAFOTGE-20) As fans had asked how he would update the Ghorman passages to reflect information revealed in *Andor* Season 2, Kempshall revealed that—at least as of the then-present of [May](/wiki/May "May"), [2025](/wiki/2025 "2025")—he would actually not change anything[[24]](#cite_note-KempshellUpdate-24) even though it would be possible to write such an update.[[25]](#cite_note-KempshellUpdate2-25) Beyond feeling he could not top what was seen in *Andor*,[[24]](#cite_note-KempshellUpdate-24) he hoped that the text of *The Rise and Fall* already invoked a feeling of sadness and horror, capturing the "awfulness of the Empire's crimes."[[25]](#cite_note-KempshellUpdate2-25)

## Continuity[]

As the conceit of *Star Wars: The Rise and Fall of the Galactic Empire* is that it is an in-universe text written by Beaumont Kin, many continuity errors can be explained away as Kin possessing inaccurate information. For example, Kin makes reference to [Grand Admiral](/wiki/Grand_Admiral "Grand Admiral") [Thrawn's](/wiki/Mitth%27raw%27nuruodo "Mitth'raw'nuruodo") significant civilian casualties at the [Battle of Batonn](/wiki/Batonn_insurgency "Batonn insurgency"). The 2017 novel [*Thrawn*](/wiki/Thrawn_(novel) "Thrawn (novel)") established that the casualties were actually the result of actions taken by [Arihnda Pryce](/wiki/Arihnda_Pryce "Arihnda Pryce"), but the pair conspired to give public credit to Thrawn.[[26]](#cite_note-Thrawn-26) As Kin reports the commonly believed inaccurate version of events, this indicates that Pryce's actions at Batonn did not become public knowledge, even after the fall of the First Order.

* The book states that [General](/wiki/General "General") [Hurst Romodi](/wiki/Hurst_Romodi "Hurst Romodi") [died](/wiki/Death "Death") during the [Battle of Yavin](/wiki/Battle_of_Yavin "Battle of Yavin"), when he in fact participated in the [Battle of Endor](/wiki/Battle_of_Endor "Battle of Endor") per "[To the Last](/wiki/To_the_Last "To the Last")" short story in *[From a Certain Point of View: Return of the Jedi](/wiki/From_a_Certain_Point_of_View:_Return_of_the_Jedi "From a Certain Point of View: Return of the Jedi")*.
* The book states that [Pitina Mar-Mas Voor](/wiki/Pitina_Mar-Mas_Voor "Pitina Mar-Mas Voor") made her final broadcast and was executed by [Darth Vader](/wiki/Anakin_Skywalker "Anakin Skywalker") in 2 BBY. These events actually take place in 2 ABY, per the [*Star Wars: Doctor Aphra*](/wiki/Star_Wars:_Doctor_Aphra_(2016) "Star Wars: Doctor Aphra (2016)") comics.
* The book mistakenly calls [The Grand Inquisitor](/wiki/The_Grand_Inquisitor "The Grand Inquisitor") an [Umbaran](/wiki/Umbaran "Umbaran"), when his species is, in fact, [Pau'an](/wiki/Pau%27an "Pau'an").
* The book states that the [ambush of a rebel convoy at Derra](/wiki/Ambush_of_a_rebel_convoy_at_Derra "Ambush of a rebel convoy at Derra") before the [Battle of Hoth](/wiki/Battle_of_Hoth "Battle of Hoth") involved [Vice Admiral](/wiki/Vice_Admiral "Vice Admiral") [Rae Sloane](/wiki/Rae_Sloane "Rae Sloane") in command of the *[Vigilance](/wiki/Vigilance_(Imperial-class) "Vigilance (Imperial-class)")* and ended with her promotion to [admiral](/wiki/Admiral "Admiral"). However, the short stories "[The Final Order](/wiki/The_Final_Order "The Final Order")"[[27]](#cite_note-Final_Order-27) and "[Lord Vader Will See You Now](/wiki/Lord_Vader_Will_See_You_Now "Lord Vader Will See You Now")" establish she was still a [commodore](/wiki/Commodore "Commodore") in the aftermath of Hoth. "Lord Vader Will See You Now" also ends with her being given command of the *Vigilance* in the post-Hoth period,[[28]](#cite_note-Lord_Vader-28) although the video game *[Star Wars: Squadrons](/wiki/Star_Wars:_Squadrons "Star Wars: Squadrons")* had established she already had a history commanding the vessel to a certain degree by that point.[[29]](#cite_note-Squadrons-29) Additionally, she was still a Vice Admiral during the [Battle of Endor](/wiki/Battle_of_Endor "Battle of Endor") per *[Star Wars Battlefront II](/wiki/Star_Wars_Battlefront_II "Star Wars Battlefront II")* and *[Star Wars Adventures: Destroyer Down](/wiki/Star_Wars_Adventures:_Destroyer_Down "Star Wars Adventures: Destroyer Down")*.
* The book misspells [Morgan Elsbeth](/wiki/Morgan_Elsbeth "Morgan Elsbeth")'s name as "Morgan Elspeth" on one occasion, although subsequent references are spelled correctly.
