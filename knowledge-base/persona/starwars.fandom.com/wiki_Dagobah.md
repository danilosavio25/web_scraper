**Dagobah** was a remote, [unexplored](/wiki/Scout "Scout") [planet](/wiki/Planet "Planet") in the [Dagobah system](/wiki/Dagobah_system "Dagobah system"), and one of the purest places in [the galaxy](/wiki/The_galaxy "The galaxy") within [the Force](/wiki/The_Force "The Force"). A remote world of [swamps](/wiki/Swamp "Swamp") and [forests](/wiki/Forest "Forest"), it served as a refuge for [Jedi](/wiki/Jedi "Jedi") [Grand Master](/wiki/Grand_Master "Grand Master") [Yoda](/wiki/Yoda "Yoda") during his [exile](/wiki/Exile_(punishment) "Exile (punishment)") after the [destruction](/wiki/Order_66 "Order 66") of the [Jedi Order](/wiki/Jedi_Order "Jedi Order"). [Luke Skywalker](/wiki/Luke_Skywalker "Luke Skywalker") received advanced [training](/wiki/Jedi_training "Jedi training") in the ways of the Force under [Jedi Master](/wiki/Jedi_Master "Jedi Master") Yoda on Dagobah, and it was later the place of Yoda's [death](/wiki/Death "Death") and [transformation into the Force](/wiki/Force_spirit "Force spirit").

## Description[]

Dagobah was a [planet](/wiki/Planet "Planet") located in the [Sluis sector](/wiki/Sluis_sector "Sluis sector") of the [Outer Rim Territories](/wiki/Outer_Rim_Territories "Outer Rim Territories").[[1]](#cite_note-Ultimate_Star_Wars-1) It was a [hot](/wiki/Temperature "Temperature") and humid world that was a hotbed of life,[[10]](#cite_note-VE-10) with the [Tash](/wiki/Tash "Tash") [species](/wiki/Species "Species")[[14]](#cite_note-Galaxy's_Edge-14) and numerous creatures—such as [bogwings](/wiki/Bogwing "Bogwing"), [dragonsnakes](/wiki/Dragonsnake "Dragonsnake"), [butcherbugs](/wiki/Butcherbug "Butcherbug"), [sleens](/wiki/Sleen "Sleen"), [vine snakes](/wiki/Vine_snake "Vine snake"), and [swamp slugs](/wiki/Swamp_slug "Swamp slug")[[9]](#cite_note-SWCL-9)—living on world. [Jedi Master](/wiki/Jedi_Master "Jedi Master") [Yoda](/wiki/Yoda "Yoda") would also make a home for himself on Dagobah during his [exile](/wiki/Exile_(punishment) "Exile (punishment)").[[4]](#cite_note-Episode_V-4) Dagobah was a mysterious, mist-shrouded, [swampy](/wiki/Swamp "Swamp") [planet](/wiki/Planet "Planet"), mostly covered in [swamps and bogs](/wiki/Dragonsnake_Bog "Dragonsnake Bog"), interspersed with countless shallow lakes and lagoons and multiple [living caves](/wiki/Gnarltree "Gnarltree"). The continental and oceanic crusts of Dagobah were only vaguely defined, and there was little in the way of [volcanic](/wiki/Volcano "Volcano") activity or earthquakes. Examples of flora included the [lahdia plant](/wiki/Lahdia_plant "Lahdia plant"), the [yoghurt plant](/wiki/Yoghurt_plant "Yoghurt plant"), [gnarltree](/wiki/Gnarltree "Gnarltree"), and [yarum seed](/wiki/Yarum_seed "Yarum seed"). The planet was devoid of any cities.[[9]](#cite_note-SWCL-9)

Among the purest locations in [the entire galaxy](/wiki/The_galaxy "The galaxy") within the energy field known as [the Force](/wiki/The_Force "The Force"),[[18]](#cite_note-Voices-18) it shared a mysterious [time](/wiki/Time "Time")-related quality with such locales as [Mortis](/wiki/Mortis "Mortis") and [Ahch-To](/wiki/Ahch-To "Ahch-To").[[20]](#cite_note-TLJVD-20) The [Cave of Evil](/wiki/Cave_of_Evil "Cave of Evil") was a [vergence](/wiki/Vergence "Vergence") aligned with the [dark side of the Force](/wiki/Dark_side_of_the_Force "Dark side of the Force") on Dagobah.[[21]](#cite_note-Vergence_short_story-21) The Cave was able to expand its influence across Dagobah until Yoda's arrival, which created a powerful presence in the [light side of the Force](/wiki/Light_side_of_the_Force "Light side of the Force") that kept the Cave in check. As such, during the [Imperial Era](/wiki/Imperial_Era "Imperial Era"), Dagobah's ecosystem saw balance until Yoda's passing, which the Cave took advantage of to spread its darkness across Dagobah once more.[[22]](#cite_note-Light_Falls-22)

Dagobah had two seasons, a dry season and a wet season. During the peak of the long dry season (over half an orbit after the rains end) the uplands become too hot for most lifeforms to survive, necessitating any that live there to migrate to the lower lands. Beneath the rapidly leafing canopies of the gnarltrees that emerge from the receding green and thick waters, the once-inundated lowlands revert back to a marshy swamp once again. As the air grows more humid, the gray overcast clouds return and before another quarter orbit passes the planet is far enough away from its sun that the dry season ends and the rains return, again flooding the lowlands and covering the trees in water, making the uplands habitable once more.[[11]](#cite_note-There_is_Another-11)

## History[]

### Republic Era[]

:   "*But again, research. And in an academic paper written during* the High Republic*, I found a description of a world very strong in the Force.*"
:   ―The Archivist[[23]](#cite_note-CR3-23)

During the [High Republic Era](/wiki/High_Republic_Era "High Republic Era"), an [academic paper](/wiki/Unidentified_academic_paper "Unidentified academic paper") was written that described Dagobah as a world strong in the Force.[[23]](#cite_note-CR3-23)

 [![Yoda traveled to Dagobah in 19 BBY at the behest of Qui-Gon Jinn.](data:image/gif;base64,R0lGODlhAQABAIABAAAAAP///yH5BAEAAAEALAAAAAABAAEAQAICTAEAOw%3D%3D)](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/starwars/images/d/dd/Dagobah-Voices.png/revision/latest?cb=20200224064318) [![Yoda traveled to Dagobah in 19 BBY at the behest of Qui-Gon Jinn.](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/starwars/images/d/dd/Dagobah-Voices.png/revision/latest/scale-to-width-down/250?cb=20200224064318)](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/starwars/images/d/dd/Dagobah-Voices.png/revision/latest?cb=20200224064318) 

Yoda traveled to Dagobah in 19 BBY at the behest of Qui-Gon Jinn.

 

During the [Republic Era](/wiki/Republic_Era "Republic Era"), [Halka Four-Den](/wiki/Halka_Four-Den "Halka Four-Den") [Alpha](/wiki/Halka_Four-Den%27s_Alpha_Team "Halka Four-Den's Alpha Team") [Team](/wiki/Alpha_Team "Alpha Team") was dispatched to the [planet](/wiki/Planet "Planet") Dagobah in order to chart and map the extending borders of the [Galactic Republic](/wiki/Galactic_Republic "Galactic Republic") and learn more about it flora and fauna. After three [months](/wiki/Standard_month "Standard month"), Four-Den and her Alpha Team were overwhelmed by diseases, dangerous wildlife, and fast-growing [plants](/wiki/Plant "Plant"). No trace of the team was discovered.[[7]](#cite_note-BMF_20-7)

Near the end of the [Clone Wars](/wiki/Clone_Wars "Clone Wars"), [Jedi](/wiki/Jedi "Jedi") [Grand Master](/wiki/Grand_Master "Grand Master") [Yoda](/wiki/Yoda "Yoda") traveled to the planet Dagobah after hearing the voice of deceased [Jedi Master](/wiki/Jedi_Master "Jedi Master") [Qui-Gon Jinn](/wiki/Qui-Gon_Jinn "Qui-Gon Jinn"). He ventured into the [swamp](/wiki/Swamp "Swamp"), where he communed with Jinn, who appeared manifested as a group of fireflies. He told Yoda of how to communicate with the living after death through the connection between the Living Force and the Cosmic Force. Then, Yoda entered a cave, where he saw a vision of the Jedi fighting the [clones](/wiki/Clone_trooper "Clone trooper") and then the mysterious [Sith Lord](/wiki/Sith_Lord "Sith Lord").[[18]](#cite_note-Voices-18)

### Imperial Era[]

#### Yoda's exile[]

:   "*Into exile I must go. Failed, I have.*"
:   ―Yoda, to Bail Organa[[24]](#cite_note-Episode_III-24)

 [![Yoda lived the planet Dagobah for the rest of his life.](data:image/gif;base64,R0lGODlhAQABAIABAAAAAP///yH5BAEAAAEALAAAAAABAAEAQAICTAEAOw%3D%3D)](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/starwars/images/7/7e/YodaFingerSpark-UP.png/revision/latest?cb=20220102032941) [![Yoda lived the planet Dagobah for the rest of his life.](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/starwars/images/7/7e/YodaFingerSpark-UP.png/revision/latest/scale-to-width-down/200?cb=20220102032941)](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/starwars/images/7/7e/YodaFingerSpark-UP.png/revision/latest?cb=20220102032941) 

Yoda lived the planet Dagobah for the rest of his life.

 

After his failure to defeat the [Sith Lord](/wiki/Sith_Lord "Sith Lord") [Darth Sidious](/wiki/Darth_Sidious "Darth Sidious") following the [formation](/wiki/Declaration_of_a_New_Order "Declaration of a New Order") of the [Galactic Empire](/wiki/Galactic_Empire "Galactic Empire"), Yoda [exiled](/wiki/Exile_(punishment) "Exile (punishment)") himself[[24]](#cite_note-Episode_III-24) to Dagobah.[[4]](#cite_note-Episode_V-4) Using an [E3-standard starship lifeboat](/wiki/E3-standard_starship_lifeboat "E3-standard starship lifeboat"), began his exile where he continued his studies of the Force. The lifeboat served as Yoda's initial shelter but it started to degrade within a year due to Dagobah's [swampy](/wiki/Swamp "Swamp") environment. As the first shelter was slowly consumed by the swamp, Yoda built a new [home](/wiki/Yoda%27s_Hut "Yoda's Hut") for himself.[[25]](#cite_note-USW-25) He positioned it near to the dark side cave but out of range of its influence and would visit every few years.[[26]](#cite_note-FACPOV2-26) However, the Dagobah wet season created a need for the Jedi Master to regularly relocate to the planet's highlands for the duration.[[27]](#cite_note-FACPOV-27)

At some point, former [Sava](/wiki/Sava "Sava") [Madelin Sun](/wiki/The_Archivist "The Archivist") arrived on Dagobah in search of Yoda. After saving her from a [monster](/wiki/Unidentified_Dagobah_creature "Unidentified Dagobah creature"), Yoda used the Force to lead Sun into the dark cave. While inside, she saw visions which dissuaded her from looking for him. She then left the planet as Yoda watched from afar.[[23]](#cite_note-CR3-23)

#### Training ground of Luke Skywalker[]

:   "*You will go to the Dagobah system. There you will learn from Yoda, the Jedi Master who instructed me.*"
:   ―Obi-Wan Kenobi, to Luke Skywalker[[4]](#cite_note-Episode_V-4)

 [![Traveling to Dagobah, Skywalker sought out Grand Master Yoda in order to complete his Jedi training.](data:image/gif;base64,R0lGODlhAQABAIABAAAAAP///yH5BAEAAAEALAAAAAABAAEAQAICTAEAOw%3D%3D)](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/starwars/images/d/dd/Yodalukedagobah.png/revision/latest?cb=20130324024419) [![Traveling to Dagobah, Skywalker sought out Grand Master Yoda in order to complete his Jedi training.](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/starwars/images/d/dd/Yodalukedagobah.png/revision/latest/scale-to-width-down/250?cb=20130324024419)](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/starwars/images/d/dd/Yodalukedagobah.png/revision/latest?cb=20130324024419) 

Traveling to Dagobah, Skywalker sought out Grand Master Yoda in order to complete his Jedi training.

 

[Years](/wiki/Standard_year "Standard year") later, [Luke Skywalker](/wiki/Luke_Skywalker "Luke Skywalker") of the [Alliance to Restore the Republic](/wiki/Alliance_to_Restore_the_Republic "Alliance to Restore the Republic") traveled to Dagobah under the direction of late [Jedi Master](/wiki/Jedi_Master "Jedi Master") [Obi-Wan Kenobi](/wiki/Obi-Wan_Kenobi "Obi-Wan Kenobi") in hopes of finding Yoda and being trained in the ways of the [Jedi Order](/wiki/Jedi_Order "Jedi Order"). Arriving with his astromech [R2-D2](/wiki/R2-D2 "R2-D2"), Skywalker crashed into a bog upon landing and happened upon the Jedi, though he was unaware it was, in fact, Yoda at the time. The old master took Skywalker back to his [hut](/wiki/Yoda%27s_Hut "Yoda's Hut") for dinner, where he revealed his true identity to Skywalker. Initially reluctant to train Skywalker due to his age and lack of patience, Yoda was eventually convinced to teach him by the [Force spirit](/wiki/Force_spirit "Force spirit") of Kenobi.[[4]](#cite_note-Episode_V-4)

One night during Skywalker's training, an Imperial [probe droid](/wiki/Probe_droid "Probe droid") from [Project Swarm](/wiki/Project_Swarm "Project Swarm") arrived on planet. The droid's operator, [Maela](/wiki/Maela "Maela"), initially believed there was no reason to look further into the planet during the unit's patrol until she spotted[[28]](#cite_note-Eyes-28) Skywalker's X-wing in the swamp.[[4]](#cite_note-Episode_V-4) Although she realized the outline of what she saw resembled an X-wing, she had realized the cruelty of the Empire after witnessing the aftermath of the [Battle of Hoth](/wiki/Battle_of_Hoth "Battle of Hoth"), so she deleted the footage before moving on to her next droid.[[28]](#cite_note-Eyes-28)

During his training, Skywalker entered the [Cave of Evil](/wiki/Cave_of_Evil "Cave of Evil") and [confronted](/wiki/Duel_on_Dagobah_(3_ABY) "Duel on Dagobah (3 ABY)") a vision of [Darth Vader](/wiki/Anakin_Skywalker "Anakin Skywalker"), who he struck down, only to see his own face in Vader's [mask](/wiki/Darth_Vader%27s_armor "Darth Vader's armor"). Later, Skywalker ended his training early, however, once he sensed his friends [Han Solo](/wiki/Han_Solo "Han Solo") and [Leia Organa](/wiki/Leia_Skywalker_Organa_Solo "Leia Skywalker Organa Solo") were in danger. Although Yoda and Kenobi attempted to reason with Skywalker to stay, the student left the planet, promising to return and finish his training.[[4]](#cite_note-Episode_V-4)

He would keep his word, and after learning Vader was his father during their [duel on Cloud City](/wiki/Duel_on_Cloud_City "Duel on Cloud City"), saving Leia and Chewbacca from Vader on [Cloud City](/wiki/Cloud_City "Cloud City"), and rescuing Solo from [Jabba the Hutt](/wiki/Jabba_Desilijic_Tiure "Jabba Desilijic Tiure") on [Tatooine](/wiki/Tatooine "Tatooine"), he would return to Dagobah to complete his Jedi training. Upon arriving, he found Yoda sick and dying, who told him that his training was complete, but all he had to do was confront Vader one last time, and the old master became [one with the Force](/wiki/Force_spirit "Force spirit"). The ghost of Kenobi then revealed to Skywalker the truth that Leia was, in fact, a Skywalker and Luke's sister before Luke left the planet to confront Vader and the Emperor in hopes of turning Vader back to the [light side](/wiki/Light_side_of_the_Force "Light side of the Force").[[29]](#cite_note-Episode_VI-29) The Cave of Evil, meanwhile, took advantage of Yoda's death to once again spread its power across Dagobah.[[22]](#cite_note-Light_Falls-22)

Sometime after the [skirmish on the Minemoon](/wiki/Skirmish_on_the_Minemoon "Skirmish on the Minemoon"), [Kylo Ren](/wiki/Ben_Solo "Ben Solo"), nephew of Luke Skywalker, along with his master and [Supreme Leader](/wiki/Supreme_Leader "Supreme Leader") of the [First Order](/wiki/First_Order "First Order"), [Snoke](/wiki/Snoke "Snoke"), came to Dagobah in order to face Ren's darkest fears. In the Cave of Evil, he experienced a vision of his uncle Luke, whom he dueled and killed, and a vision of his parents Leia and Han. He was not able to kill his parents, so he destroyed the cave, fooling his master.[[30]](#cite_note-Age_of_Resistance_Snoke-30)

By [34 ABY](/wiki/34_ABY "34 ABY"),[[31]](#cite_note-34_Date-31) herbs from Dagobah had made their way to [Oga's Cantina](/wiki/Oga%27s_Cantina "Oga's Cantina") on [Batuu](/wiki/Batuu "Batuu"), where they became a popular ingredient in the so-called [Dagobah Slug Slinger](/wiki/Dagobah_Slug_Slinger "Dagobah Slug Slinger") alcoholic beverage.[[14]](#cite_note-Galaxy's_Edge-14) During or after 34 ABY,[[32]](#cite_note-Date-32) the [freighter](/wiki/Freighter "Freighter") *Millennium Falcon* landed on Dagobah. There, the [Wookiee](/wiki/Wookiee "Wookiee") crewmember [Chewbacca](/wiki/Chewbacca "Chewbacca") attempted to take some time to himself to rest aboard the ship, only for the droids [BB-8](/wiki/BB-8 "BB-8") and R2-D2[[33]](#cite_note-Noise-33)—the very same droid who had accompanied Luke Skywalker to Dagobah[[4]](#cite_note-Episode_V-4)—discovered a Kowakian monkey-lizard aboard the ship.[[33]](#cite_note-Noise-33)

## Inhabitants[]

Dagobah was the homeworld of the Tash species, who grew herbs.[[14]](#cite_note-Galaxy's_Edge-14) Jedi Grand Master Yoda made his way to Dagobah and adopted it[[27]](#cite_note-FACPOV-27) as his homeworld during the Imperial Era.[[4]](#cite_note-Episode_V-4) Another being of sorts on Dagobah was the so-called Cave of Evil, a living Force vergence[[26]](#cite_note-FACPOV2-26) strong in the dark side,[[4]](#cite_note-Episode_V-4) but confined to its place on the planet.[[26]](#cite_note-FACPOV2-26)

## Behind the scenes[]

:   "*A small escape pod hurls toward the swamp planet and disappears in the mist. The pod has landed, and YODA descends the ramp, surveying the unfamiliar terrain.*"
:   ―Description of Dagobah's deleted scene from Episode III[[34]](#cite_note-Illustrated-34)

Dagobah first appeared in the [1980](/wiki/1980 "1980") film [*Star Wars*: Episode V *The Empire Strikes Back*](/wiki/Star_Wars:_Episode_V_The_Empire_Strikes_Back "Star Wars: Episode V The Empire Strikes Back"), the second installment of the *[Star Wars](/wiki/Star_Wars "Star Wars")* [original trilogy](/wiki/Original_trilogy "Original trilogy").[[4]](#cite_note-Episode_V-4)

Slated to appear in one scene of the [2005](/wiki/2005 "2005") film, [*Star Wars*: Episode III *Revenge of the Sith*](/wiki/Star_Wars:_Episode_III_Revenge_of_the_Sith "Star Wars: Episode III Revenge of the Sith"), the planet was cut from the final film, leaving it for the DVD release. The scene featured how Yoda started his exile. [George Lucas](/wiki/George_Lucas "George Lucas") removed the scene so that the film would not have "too many endings," despite producer [Rick McCallum](/wiki/Rick_McCallum "Rick McCallum")'s wishes.

In the [2016](/wiki/2016 "2016") reference book [*Star Wars: Complete Locations*](/wiki/Star_Wars:_Complete_Locations_(2016) "Star Wars: Complete Locations (2016)"), an image of Dagobah was mistakenly used in the planet profile of [Kashyyyk](/wiki/Kashyyyk "Kashyyyk"). The book also alleges that no civilization existed on Dagobah,[[9]](#cite_note-SWCL-9) but *[Star Wars: Galaxy's Edge](/wiki/Star_Wars:_Galaxy%27s_Edge "Star Wars: Galaxy's Edge")* later recanonized the Tash species.[[14]](#cite_note-Galaxy's_Edge-14)

The [Kwazel Maw](/wiki/Kwazel_Maw "Kwazel Maw") seen in the *[Star Wars: The Clone Wars](/wiki/Star_Wars:_The_Clone_Wars "Star Wars: The Clone Wars")* episode "[Bombad Jedi](/wiki/Bombad_Jedi "Bombad Jedi")" was based off of an unused design by [Ralph McQuarrie](/wiki/Ralph_McQuarrie "Ralph McQuarrie") for a [swamp](/wiki/Swamp "Swamp") [slug](/wiki/Slug "Slug") creature on Dagobah.[[35]](#cite_note-Bombad_Jedi_Guide-35)

There is a long-running fan debate about how long Luke Skywalker trained on Dagobah.[[36]](#cite_note-20_Easter_Eggs-36) This debate was jokingly referenced in "The Lost Boy," a story told by [Vaneé](/wiki/Vane%C3%A9 "Vaneé"), when Ben Solo asked if Skywalker trained for 20 [minutes](/wiki/Standard_minute "Standard minute") on Dagobah.[[37]](#cite_note-Terrifying_Tales-37)

Lucas' vision for Dagobah started out as a swampy and misty planet that would be similar to the setting of the Sherlock Holmes novel [The Hound of the Baskervilles](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/The_Hound_of_the_Baskervilles), which was in Dartmoor, England.[[38]](#cite_note-Star_Wars:_The_Empire_Strikes_Back:_The_40th_Anniversary_Special_Edition-38)
